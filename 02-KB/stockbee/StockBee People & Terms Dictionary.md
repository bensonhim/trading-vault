## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **EP** | Episodic Pivot — a stock that makes a 4%+ move on high volume due to a catalyst. The core StockBee setup. |
| **EP 9M** | Episodic Pivot with 9 million+ shares traded — the primary setup. Isolates the top ~2% of 12,366 tradeable instruments by volume. |
| **DEP** | Daily Episodic Pivot — a subset of EP 9M that happened in the last 25 days on a fund-quality (1000+) stock, now setting up again. |
| **SOS** | Stock Open Strong — a day trading setup where a stock opens with unusual strength. "SOS supersedes every single thing." |
| **SIPs** | Stock in Play — day trading setup. Any stock with a catalyst and volume that day. Doesn't require SA or market trend. |
| **SA** | Situational Awareness — reading the market environment and adjusting tactics accordingly. "The real game." |
| **NTRT** | Nighttime Is Right Time — scanning aftermarket earnings releases after 4 PM to find next-day candidates. |
| **MTRT** | Morning Time Is Right Time — scanning premarket gaps (4%+ on 100K+ volume) before 9:30 AM to find catalysts. |
| **Sugar Baby** | A home-run setup variant — neglected stock with a mind-blowing catalyst that can move 100%+ in days. |
| **Reversal Bullish** | A setup where you buy a stock that has bottomed and is reversing up. Requires popularity (newsletter/Discord fanboys) + clean pattern. Social arbitrage. |
| **Anticipation** | A setup where you buy before a breakout, expecting it to happen. Worst setup in range-bound markets. |
| **Momentum Burst** | A 3-5 day swing setup. The foundation of Pradeep's method, adapted from Mark Butcher's "Hedge Fund Age" book appendix. |
| **20% Study** | A breadth indicator Pradeep uses. Tracks stocks up/down 20% in a period. Readings >100 = caution/bubble; very low readings = bounce likely. |
| **Trade Factory** | Pradeep's daily process: EP 9M scan → qualify (cat/dog/liquid lava) → SA check → enter → manage stop. Two conveyor belts: home runs (EP/DEP/sugar babies) and singles (momentum burst). |
| **Cat / Dog / Liquid Lava** | EP 9M classification: Cat = real catalyst (earnings, product). Dog = story/fugazi catalyst. Liquid Lava = no catalyst, just volume. Only cat and dog are tradeable. |
| **Fugazi** | A fake/story catalyst. Example: juice company becoming a crypto company. |
| **London Calling** | Pradeep's June 8, 2026 video warning that the market was transitioning to a range-bound chop after the 18% rally in two months. |
| **Kissing the cousin** | Pradeep's metaphor for buying breakouts in a range-bound market despite knowing better. |
| **The Indian skill** | Pradeep's metaphor for situational awareness — the ability to "put your ear to the ground" and sense what's coming by scanning the environment. |
| **Marshmallow test** | Psychology experiment on delayed gratification. Pradeep uses it to explain why self-control predicts trading success. |
| **OPG** | Opening Order — an order type in IBKR that executes at the market open. Used for Day 0 EP entries. |
| **FHP** | First High-Priced breakout — a setup for high-priced stocks. |
