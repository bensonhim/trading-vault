## Core Principle

> *"Sales growth is what is the most important part which drives the stock's growth. Earnings growth is nice to have, but it is not essential."* — [[20. Stock Selection Guide|Stock Selection Strategy Part 2]]

## Institutional Quality = DEP Quality

> *"DEP is more suitable for institutional quality. Institutional quality means something owned by thousand plus funds or 2,500 funds."* — [[16. DEP Guide|DEP Part 10]]

| Quality | Characteristics | Best For | Source |
|---------|----------------|----------|--------|
| **Institutional (1000+ funds)** | Well-covered, predictable, pulls back after gap | DEP, large size | [[16. DEP Guide|DEP Part 10]] |
| **Neglected + hot theme** | Small cap, huge surprise, no pullback | Gap-and-go (NOT DEP) | [[16. DEP Guide|DEP Part 10]] |
| **Story stocks** | No fundamentals, high volatility, big moves | Sugar Babies, short-term burst | [[19. Deep Dive Guide|Deep Dive Part 6]] |

## Key Selection Rules

| Rule | Explanation | Source |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **Sales growth > profitability** | Market reacts to sales acceleration, not earnings alone | [[20. Stock Selection Guide|Stock Selection Strategy Part 2]] |
| **Younger is better** | "The genie is already out of the bottle" on mature growers | [[20. Stock Selection Guide|Stock Selection Strategy Part 2]] |
| **Factor-based approach** | Sales growth primary; fund ownership secondary; price action tertiary | [[20. Stock Selection Guide|Stock Selection Strategy Part 2]] |
| **Working people adaptation** | Focus on institutional quality; skip story stocks and biotechs | [[16. DEP Guide|DEP Part 2]] |
