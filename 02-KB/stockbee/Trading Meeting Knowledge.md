## 1. Market Monitoring Philosophy

### Core Principles

- **Breadth-first, not price-first**: Pradeep relies on market breadth indicators (4% breakouts/breakdowns, quarterly 25%, T2108) to determine regime BEFORE selecting setups.
- **20% Study as primary filter**: When the 20% study is low, it indicates potential for follow-through. High readings warn of froth.
- **Capitulation = generational buy**: Historically, when primary indicator drops below 200, it signals a multi-year bottom.
- **Choppy periods require caution**: After running a "marathon" (strong trend), market typically needs consolidation before the next move.

## 2. Daily Setup Selection Process

### Setup Hierarchy (from sessions)

| Setup | When to Trade | Notes from Meetings |
|-------|---------------|---------------------|
| **EP (Episodic Pivot)** | After earnings/catalyst with volume | Requires genuine catalyst, not just speculation |
| **DEP (Delayed Reaction)** | Post-catalyst pullback with tight consolidation | Favorite setup during choppy periods; needs time to develop |
| **SOS (Start of Swing)** | Breakout on above-average volume | More reliable when breadth confirms |
| **ANT (Anticipation)** | Tight days before breakout | Lower risk, lower conviction |
| **REVERSAL** | Exhaustion/extreme breadth readings | Counter-trend; requires precise timing |
| **WSS (Weak Structure Short)** | Bearish breadth + broken support | Only in bearish or choppy regimes |

### Setup Qualification Criteria

From daily meeting commentary:
- **Category A setup**: Must tick ALL boxes — catalyst, volume, pattern, and breadth alignment.
- **Snow was my #1 idea**: Even with perfect setup, execution matters — entry timing and follow-through.
- **Don't chase extended names**: Dell +221% into catalyst = avoid or size down.
- **Follow-through is everything**: A setup can be perfect but fail without market support.

## 3. Risk Management from Sessions

### Position Sizing

- **Individual risk tolerance drives size**: "If you feel you can take larger risk and don't mind getting chopped, then go and do that thing."
- **Market condition adjusts size**: During choppy periods, reduce size or skip entirely.
- **Stop placement**: Use technical stops (below pivot, below consolidation) rather than arbitrary percentages.

### When to Be Aggressive vs. Cautious

| Regime | Action | Rationale |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Strong breadth + setup | Full size | Alignment of market + stock |
| Choppy/unclear breadth | Small size or watch | Avoid chop |
| After strong run | Cautious / wait | "After running a marathon, can market run another marathon back to back?" |
| Dead cat bounce | Avoid or very small | Reversal setups only — don't chase |

## 4. Catalyst Awareness

### Types Discussed in Meetings

- **Earnings**: Primary catalyst for EP and DEP setups.
- **Guidance upgrades/downgrades**: Secondary catalyst that can extend a move.
- **Sector themes**: AI, data center, prediction markets — sector momentum matters.
- **IPO/news events**: One-off catalysts with limited follow-through potential.

### Catalyst Evaluation

- Is the catalyst "game-changing" or just noise?
- Did the stock already make a big move in anticipation?
- Volume on catalyst day must confirm conviction.

## 5. Psychology & Process

### Key Psychological Principles

- **Process over outcome**: One trade (Snow) stopped out — that doesn't invalidate the process.
- **FOMO management**: "Is it time?" / "I don't want to miss out" — these are danger signals.
- **Discipline in choppy periods**: Sitting on hands is a valid strategy.
- **Individual decision**: Every trader's risk tolerance differs; there is no one-size-fits-all answer.

### Daily Routine (from sessions)

1. **Pre-market**: Check breadth, 20% study, overnight gaps.
2. **Opening hour**: Identify setups from watchlist; assess market tone.
3. **Mid-day**: If no action, step away (gym, lunch) — don't force trades.
4. **Last hour**: Re-assess; this is where follow-through (or fade) becomes clear.
