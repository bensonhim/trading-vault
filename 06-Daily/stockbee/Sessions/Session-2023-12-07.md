# Extended Stocks Get Punished: Below $10B Is the Magic Number

> Historic rally (13% in under a month), but extended stocks are getting punished. Buying is broadening into biotech and small caps. The rule: below $10B market cap, IPO under 10 years, stories make moves. Don't date Arabian women.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish but Extended — Rotation Broadening

> "This move in SPY or QQQ is a historic move. 10% in one month is one of the biggest moves which has ever happened."

Extended stocks pulling back, breakouts on extended stocks failing. But rotation into new stocks is broadening — biotech, small caps, consumer discretionary all attracting buying.

The chorus of people on Twitter calling tops and going to cash? That's a good sign.

> "That's a very good situation to be in. They'll all have FOMO at some stage."

### The $10B Market Cap Rule

In the 20% study, 51 stocks — most have market cap below $10B. Only 34 had market cap above $10B.

> "Given a choice, always look for below $10B capitalization. That's the magic figure."

Palantir example: at its peak, $45B market cap with $1.9B float. What's the probability of that doubling?

### Younger Stocks Make Bigger Moves

- IPO below 10 years = better moves
- Below $10B market cap = better moves
- Stories make big moves
- "Cougar, young stocks make bigger moves"

### The Dating Analogy Continues

> "Should I date Google? After I spent the entire half hour explaining you should go for younger stocks... Should I be dating a cougar who's 90 years old and 500 billion pounds?"

Don't date Arabian women (extended/large-cap stocks). Don't date extended stocks — they'll kick you out.

### Focus List: 9M + Sugar Babies + Combo

- 9M EP combo
- Sugar babies (Rivian, Lucid)
- 80/20 and sell into strength

### Boot Camp Risk Management

Decreased exposure substantially. Taking smaller sizes. Can't actively manage positions during boot camp.
