# Choppy Transition — Lottery Tickets Flying, Breakouts Fading

> The market bounced after a big sell-off, but the bounce is suspect — lottery tickets are flying, breakouts are fading, and the 20% Study screamed extreme. Pradeep's response: tiny size, breakeven stops, and wait for EP 9M / DEP setups only.

---

## AM Session — Situational Awareness Over Setup

### The Bounce Is Suspect

The market rebounded after Friday's sharp sell-off, but Pradeep was not buying it. The 20% Study had reached extreme readings (249 at the prior peak), and historically, every time it spikes that high, a pullback follows within 3–5 days. The bounce was predictable but not trustworthy.

> "After 30% study reaching extreme, that is, like, an almost given. And whenever the study reaches extreme, you can just confirm yourself by going into historical readings."

> "The probability of this just going and keep on making new leg is same as probability of me getting six packs."

### Lottery Tickets = Warning Sign

Stocks like [[INSD]] making 3,660% moves, low-float highly-shorted names flying — these "lottery tickets" appearing en masse is itself a market signal. When absolute garbage stocks make crazy moves, it often precedes a pullback.

> "You see stocks like INSD making 3,660% moves. Based on what? Those are lottery tickets."

> "A lot of tickets flying tends to be a warning for pullbacks."

### Broken Structures Need Time to Repair

Stocks with 3+ EP 9M in the last month are damaged. They need weeks to build tight, readable setups again. Buying the bounce on these is not a strategy.

> "Stocks will need a couple of weeks to repair damage and create tight, tight action setups."

> "If it is not tight, I'm not interested. Tightness — that's the most important thing in a setup. From a risk-reward perspective."

### The Trade Factory Process

Pradeep walked through his entire pre-market workflow:

1. **Start with Sugar Babies** — proven winners, institutional quality names (e.g., [[OKTA]], [[CVNA]], [[IBKR]])
2. **Run EP 9M scan** — the #1 priority setup, sorted by volume
3. **Run Bullish scans** — looking for SOS, continuation, or momentum burst setups
4. **Run Bearish scans** — not for morning trades, but for SA information
5. **Filter everything through SA** — if SA is not bullish, reduce size or skip

> "I have reached my retirement age. I am only trading EP 9M. I am no more interested in anything else. EP 9M is the thing. There is no other setup which can make you as much money as EP 9M."

### The Anticipation Analogy

Pradeep explained why he enters DEP setups before confirmation:

> "If you're married and your wife is coming home at 5:00, and you keep everything ready by 4:00, 4:30, 4:45 — that's anticipation. You start setting things up. As soon as she enters, it's not anticipation."

### DraftKings Is NOT a DEP

A member asked about [[DKNG]] as a DEP. Pradeep was emphatic:

> "If you are calling DraftKings a DEP, you have not seen my video and guide about DEP. There is no game-changing catalyst. The number of people who are into gambling — they exhausted. Growing at 4% now. What catalyst is there?"

> "Fix your understanding of setup. Don't do random things."

### Three Stopped-Out Trades = Market Warning

> "If I get stopped out on three trades in a row, I know it is not me — there is something wrong in the market."

Pradeep was stopped out on [[NVDA]], [[COREWEAVE]] (GLW), and another trade in succession — a signal to back off entirely.

### Situational Awareness Is a Multitude of Factors

SA is not one indicator. It's the totality of observations:

| SA Signal | What It Tells You |
|-----------|-------------------|
| 20% Study extreme (249) | Pullback within 3–5 days |
| T2108 below 20 | Capitulation / generational buy zone |
| Lottery tickets flying | Froth → warning |
| Large-caps moving like story stocks | Unhealthy momentum |
| Breakouts fading same day | No follow-through |
| Low-quality stocks dominating scans | Breadth deterioration |
| Your own trades stopped out | Market is broken for your style |

> "Situational awareness is something which is a part of expertise. Once you become expert in a particular setup, situational awareness automatically comes in. It's like a tennis player looking at weather, wind, opponent's body language."

## Key Takeaways

1. **20% Study at extremes always leads to pullback** — every historical instance confirms this; the question is when, not if
2. **Lottery tickets flying = market warning** — when garbage stocks make 3,000%+ moves, the market is frothy and due for a pullback
3. **EP 9M is the retirement setup** — no other setup makes as much money; focus exclusively on it
4. **Three stopped-out trades = stop trading** — if three consecutive trades fail, the market is broken for your style
5. **Tightness is non-negotiable** — if a chart isn't tight, there's no interest. Broken structures need weeks to repair
6. **Marry a setup** — define it, know it, trade only it. Random stocks from scans are not setups
7. **SA is a multitude of signals** — not one indicator, but the totality of observations from your daily process
8. **Move stops to breakeven ASAP in choppy markets** — small profits or breakeven beats taking losses
9. **DEP requires a game-changing catalyst** — DraftKings with no catalyst is not a DEP; Snow with earnings catalyst is
10. **Choppy periods separate those who respect SA from those who want to trade** — patience is the edge
