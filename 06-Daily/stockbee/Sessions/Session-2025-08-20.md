# Delayed Reaction Week Day 3 — Move Your Stops, Lock In Profit, Three-to-Five-Day Moves, Belief Drives Behavior

**Market pulled back 300+ after yesterday's sell day. T2108 barely moved (50.67→51.14). 20% study leans bearish. Breakouts unlikely to work.** Pradeep's #1 message: **move your stops, lock in profit.** CRWV day 3 — take profit aggressively. Coherent day 3 — move stop to breakeven. KAVA held with conviction (clear catalyst). IBKR locked 80% profit. LRCX added. **WOLF** sold 90% at profit, now setting up as new delayed reaction. **Core theme: three-to-five-day moves are typical — take what the market gives you, then move on.** Deep buyers active but institutional selling in mega-caps continues. Belief drives behavior — new information + reflection = belief change.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness — Cautious, Pullback After 300+ Down Day

> "My essay this morning is cautious as the market pulled back with a 300 plus selling day yesterday. Now we watch to see if there will be follow through to the downside or a bounce. Breakouts unlikely to work."

> "T2108 remained relatively unchanged, 50.67 to 51.14. 20% study now leans bearish with just over a one to one ratio of bearish to bullish stock."

### Delayed Reaction Shorts All Down — Move Your Stops

> "All delayed reaction shots are all down. So what is the thing that you need to do? Move your stops! Move your stops! Not even one person here is thinking."

> "Learn to move the stops. Instead of sitting there."

> "These are all small tactics, and this is what is going to make a difference even if you are trading delayed reaction EP."

### Three-to-Five-Day Moves — Superpower

> "If you understand market structure of three to five day moves and all, that's like a superpower. It's an edge."

> "Typical move in SPY is three to four days and deep buyers come in. So if three to four days deep buyers are going to come in, what is the prudent course of action? You take 80% off."

> "Just moving stop loss is not going to help you. Because if you don't take the profit here, tomorrow it gaps up. This is what happened to me on TNA."

### CRWV Day 3 — Take Profit

> "Core view, third day of this move. You know that you look at it, this is what is most likely to happen after this? It's gonna bounce. So what do you do? Move your stop so aggressively."

> "Or either cut it or move your stop just like any 3 or 4¢ above this because if it is not going to follow through. You don't want to be caught in a counter trend squeeze."

### Coherent Day 3 — Move Stop to Breakeven

> "Coherent. Very nice trade. What was your risk on this day? It never came. Risk never came into play. Next day, you could move your stop to breakeven, just collect your dollars."

### KAVA — Held With Conviction (Catalyst)

> "KAVA might have a potential to make a move like this — 10%, 20%. Now that's the kind of trade with the lowest risk possible."

### IBKR — Locked 80%

### New Positions — LRCX, WOLF Sold Then Re-setup

### Breakouts Not Working — Situational Awareness

> "Are breakouts likely to work? No. And then you see a breakout and you cannot stop yourself from buying the breakout. What is likely to happen? It will fade."

> "Just because there is a setup showing up, the moment you lose situational awareness, your plane is going to crash."

> "This is like saying 'I'm going to fast today' and then the first thing you come to the office and you see a doughnut box and you eat three doughnuts."

### Carvana Day Trade — Amazon News

> "Carvana is stuck in play today. Because it is going to make up and down $20 move in a day. Now if you are a day trader, how difficult is it to capture $2 out of that?"

> "Amazon has been selling cars for the last two years. I just for fun put it in my cart. Nobody contacted me. Nothing happened. It's just a con."

### Not Every Setup Is Delayed Reaction

> "Not everything is a delayed reaction EP. Is there a catalyst? This is just a normal regular breakout. It's just an anticipation setup."

> "Viking Therapeutics as a delayed reaction EP — that's not anywhere close to delayed reaction EP setup."

> "TTD — it didn't trigger in the five days. That means it's not a very weak stock. All of these examples I'm showing you — when did this trigger? On the third day of the weakness. These nuances are the setup."

### Process — Fix One Error Per Day

> "Develop this mentality of singles plus home run. I'm willing to give something like KAVA more room because there is a clear catalyst. But for some of the other things, you take profit and go."

> "Those five, six singles help you finance your next big trade."

> "If you don't have a scan and at 9:30 in the morning you're asking what is this scan — should you be trading also?"

### Selling Continues in Large Caps

> "Larger stocks are still getting sold. Even though deep buyers came in, Amazon, Reddit, HOOD — these are all stocks which are leading the advance. They're still getting sold. Who's selling these stocks? Institutions."

### Short Side Profit Is Ephemeral

> "Lock in profit on the short side. Short side profit can be ephemeral."

> "You can have profit and you go to pee and come back and your buy program has come in and your profit has vanished. It's that brutal."

### Palantir — Relentless Deep Buyers

> "Look at Palantir today. Relentless. $11 move, 7% move. These algorithms know where people are short. They keep buying, they keep buying."

### Apple Short — Starter Position

> "I did take a position in Apple. Very low risk kind of a play. Apple deserves to go down a lot. Just a small position starter."

### Growth Stocks Eventually Go to Toilet

> "Every single growth stock ultimately ends up in the toilet of the market. The best of the management cannot save a company beyond a point."

> "When I started trading, GE was supposed to be the best stock. Jack Welch was considered a god of management. Today what happened?"

### Kirk Kerkorian Story — Deep Buying Until It Doesn't Work

> "Kerkorian had made a big fortune in Las Vegas. He kept buying Ford Motor, saying there is no way Ford can go down. He ended up from billionaire to millionaire. He bought GM also, it went into bankruptcy."

> "Deep buying till it works is a good strategy, but at some stage when it stopped working, all these people will be left holding the bag."

### Breakouts Unlikely to Work

> "I don't think breakouts are likely to work right now. It might change tomorrow if there is a 600+ day or a gap up and go."

> "Even a good stock, good breakout, if the situational awareness says breakouts are not going to work, it will be very difficult. This learning took me three years."

### Anticipation Setups — No

> "Is there any point in looking at anticipation setup here? No. Because anticipation is not likely to work."

### FOMO and Gap Chasing — Belief Change

> "How do you avoid chasing gaps? You make your rule: focus on finding opportunities where my stop is 2.5% or less. If something is gapping up 16%, there is no way it fits into my risk-reward equation."

> "FOMO is primarily a beginner trader problem. They don't believe that if you stay in your lane, you can make money."

### Belief Drives Behavior — The Core Lecture

> "All our problems in trading boil down to beliefs. You have a lot of beliefs."

> "Behavior changes only if belief changes. There is no way. You cannot change your behavior on a long-term basis unless your belief changes."

> "To change belief, what do you need? New information, reflection, or a bad experience. Bad experience changes belief faster than good experience."

> "That's why I shouted at you this morning — it was a stimulus. Strong stimulus to change your belief about locking in profit."

> "The marine instructor technique — they destroy every belief before they build you back. It's the most effective way to change beliefs. They've spent millions researching this."

### News Moves Stocks — Fundamental Belief

> "News moves stocks. This belief, once you form it, is going to change your trading. Most good day traders who make millions — what are they trading? News. They are trading news using charts."

> "You can explain a move as a chart pattern, but is it a chart pattern or is it because of the news that this chart pattern was created?"

### Watch List Reduces FOMO

> "Watch list allows you to reduce FOMO, plus it gives you time to think through trade — where are you going to put stop, what is your condition for taking profit."

> "Komito only operates out of his watch list. He doesn't take a trade which just randomly shows up in a scan."

### 80/20 Rule Again — SPY Example

> "On TNA, I had almost a full account position. I said this is at least three to four days. Next day it gaps up. Stop loss is not going to help. 80/20 taking profit is the strategy."

### Deep Buying vs Real Selling

> "Those days where selling used to start and continue till 4 PM — that has been abolished. Now deep buyers come in and squeeze you."

> "I was part of some groups — they were all discussing buying NVIDIA, buying Palantir. 'This is a scheme by hedge funds to shake us out.' They are all going to buy."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Move your stops** — the #1 message of the day, repeated 10+ times. When shorts are working, aggressively move stops or lock in profit
2. **Three-to-five-day moves** — understanding market structure of 3-5 day moves is a superpower. After day 3, take profit aggressively (80/20)
3. **CRWV day 3** — counter-trend bounce likely, move stop aggressively or take profit. "Three days, good move, take it and thank the madam"
4. **KAVA held** — clear catalyst = more room. No catalyst = take profit quickly
5. **Deep buyers relentless** — algorithms keep buying where shorts are, squeezing profits. Short side profit is ephemeral
6. **IBKR locked 80%** — guidance declining, 80% profit taken, 10¢ stop on remainder
7. **SPY short** — had full position, took 90% profit at peak. "Profit is ephemeral. 6% profit → breakeven close. So frustrating."
8. **WOLF sold and re-setup** — sold 90% at profit, now setting up as new delayed reaction. "Free money → another opportunity"
9. **Breakouts unlikely to work** — situational awareness says no breakouts. "The moment you lose situational awareness, your plane is going to crash"
10. **Carvana day trade** — Amazon car news created $20 range, day traders can capture $2
11. **Apple short started** — small starter, "deserves to go down a lot", low risk
12. **Not everything is delayed reaction** — Viking Therapeutics, Gilead, TTD are NOT delayed reaction setups. Must have catalyst + trigger within 5 days
13. **Belief drives behavior** — new information, reflection, or bad experience changes beliefs. Beliefs change → behavior changes. Bad experience changes belief faster
14. **News moves stocks** — fundamental belief underlying all setups (EP, SHIPS, delayed reaction). Charts show patterns but news creates them
15. **Watch list reduces FOMO** — only trade from watch list, think through stops and profit conditions before entering
16. **Singles + home run mentality** — 5-6 singles finance the next big trade. Don't expect every trade to be a home run
17. **FOMO is a beginner problem** — solution: stay in your lane, focus on setups with ≤2.5% risk
18. **Kerkorian story** — billionaire → millionaire by buying the dip on Ford/GM. Deep buying works until it doesn't
