# Intervention Changes Behavior — Market Tells You It's Bullish, 1100+ Setups Proliferate, Focus on Continuation

April 13 was a tale of two sessions. The AM was quiet, cautious, nothing compelling — extended markets, no clean setups, and a long sermon on behavioral change requiring intervention, not discipline. Then the PM exploded: 1100+ stocks in the combo bullish scan, broad-based buying across the board, setups proliferating everywhere. The market told you it was bullish — Iran didn't matter, the bounce continued, breakouts are following through. The key message: focus on EP 9M (thousands per year) and continuation setups, not parabolic shorting (4-6 per year). Linearity is the #1 qualifying criterion. And above all: behavioral change requires intervention, not willpower.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Short-Term Bearish, Need Pullback

> "A couple of days of consolidation here or a minor pullback is required for good setups to show up. Otherwise, everything looks very extended."

> "Everything is already up three or four or five days from the bottom."

Essay was cautiously bearish, but Pradeep immediately qualified: the Iran conflict doesn't matter, the market never cared, it's just a cyclical dip in a secular bull that started in 2009.

### Breakouts Likely to Work — But Extended

> "Our breakouts likely to work. Yes. ... Selectively I'm willing to do breakouts also because I see the early breakouts are following through."

But everything is extended. Need 2-3 days sideways for fresh setups.

### Process for the Day

> "EP 9M, sugar babies, select breakouts. ... Combo bullish: continuation setup (two links condition) and consolidation breakout (catalyst + volume surge). Combo bearish: weak structure shorts. EP 9M: cats (clear catalyst), dogs (shady story but likely to move), liquid lava (high liquidity, traded daily)."

> "If you're not trading any one of these setups, don't ask me to comment on your setup because your setup is something which you have expertise on. I don't have expertise."

### Start of a Swing — Must Be First Day

> "Start of a swing means a stock which is just starting out. ... If it is second, third day and you're buying third day, don't come and tell me, 'I bought third day and it still worked.' That's your problem."

### Intervention — Not Discipline

This was the dominant theme of the AM session — a full discourse on behavioral change.

> "Behavioral change needs intervention. Everything else — 'be disciplined' — is not going to change this. ... If there is no intervention, you are going to redo the same thing."

> "Discipline is not an intervention. You have to have an intervention."

Examples of interventions:
- **Physical barrier**: Go to the gym at 10:30 AM (physically removes you from the computer)
- **Wire out profits**: Moving money out is a physical intervention
- **Qualifying criteria**: Only trading setups from your scan is an intervention
- **Watch list**: Only trading from your watch list is an intervention
- **Position sizing**: Trading with 5 shares is an intervention
- **Shoplifting analogy**: When California removed the intervention (law), shoplifting increased. Hammurabi was the first to introduce interventions (eye for an eye)

> "If you have kids, how do you change their behavior? Time out. That's intervention."

### Nothing Compelling in AM

> "I don't see anything really which is compelling."

The only candidates:
- **Allo** — Biotech EP, 9/10 on Pradeep's scale (per Gemini), real catalyst (pivotal phase 2 trial results), but NOT closing near high. 42M shares traded.
- **UnitedHealth** — Delayed reaction again, but low volume, "Monday scale of EP = 1 out of 10" due to low volume. "It's a trap."
- **STX** — Continuation setup, best looking pattern, but market needs to confirm
- **Marvel short** — Taken with $1 stop as a day-trade, extremely risky parabolic short

### Linearity — The #1 Qualifying Criterion

> "The thing is that linearity tells you how strong the buying is in the stock. ... I only select stocks with high linearity."

> "Only date people who are well-dressed, no mood swings. ... More predictable action = higher win rate."

ARM = "psychotic" stock (choppy, unpredictable). Don't date stocks with mood swings. Date stocks with tight pants and orderly action.

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### Proliferation of Setups — 1100+ Stocks

> "When the market becomes good, there is a proliferation of setups. ... 1100+ stocks showing up in combo bullish scan."

The PM session was the opposite of AM: everything working, broad-based buying, setups everywhere. The market opened positive and went positive despite weekend Iran fears.

### Market Is Telling You It's Bullish

> "On weekend, people were freaking out — limit down, limit down. ... What happened by the time the market opened? Nothing. ... The market is telling you something. The market is ringing a bell. It is flying an airplane over your head."

> "Breakouts have been working for last two or three days. ... SNDK, you had car, you had so many breakouts following through, and you're saying 'world is falling apart'?"

### 20% Study at 60 — Not a Worry Signal

> "60 is not a reading where you should be worried. ... The only time you should be worried is when 20% study goes above 100. Or when it's below 20. Rest of the time, there is nothing you really need to do."

> "A lot of you people would be better off not even looking at these things like 20% study because you're just getting scared for no reason."

### Asset Allocation Day

> "Today's move looks like asset allocation. ... They're buying a basket without discriminating."

Broad-based buying across sectors — not selective, not stock-specific. This creates opportunities but also means you must be selective about which setup to take.

### EP 9M Prioritization

> "Prioritize by highest volume, highest relative volume. Or prioritize by lower capitalization — they make bigger moves."

> "EP 9M happens thousands of times per year. Parabolic shorting happens 4-5 times per year. Focus on EP 9M."

### Focus on Continuation Setups

> "At this stage of the market, you want to be looking at continuation setups. ... The market has already put a first leg. Now look for continuation."

After the 6+ day bounce, first legs are done. Look for second legs (continuation) or fresh EP 9M with catalyst.

### Don't Worry About Things That Don't Matter

> "People were saying 'limit down, limit down' over the weekend. ... The market opened positive and went positive. ... That tells you something."

The market absorbed the worst geopolitical news and rallied. That's bullish. Stop worrying about Iran.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Behavioral change requires intervention, not discipline** — "Discipline is not an intervention." Physical barriers, position sizing, watch lists, qualifying criteria — these are interventions. Willpower alone fails.
2. **Market told you it's bullish** — Iran fears over the weekend, "limit down" panic, and the market opened positive and rallied. That's the bell ringing over your head.
3. **1100+ setups proliferated in PM** — When the market is good, EP 9M scan alone gives you thousands of opportunities per year.
4. **Focus on EP 9M, not parabolic shorting** — EP 9M happens thousands of times per year. Parabolic shorting happens 4-5 times. Focus where the frequency is.
5. **Linearity is the #1 qualifying criterion** — Only trade stocks with orderly, linear action. Choppy stocks (ARM, SMCI) are "psychotic" — don't date them.
6. **20% study at 60 = neutral** — Only worry above 100 or below 20. Everything in between is noise.
7. **At this market stage, look for continuation setups** — First legs are done. Look for second legs (two links) or fresh EP 9M with catalyst.
8. **Allo was the biotech EP 9M** — 9/10 score, real catalyst, but not closing near high. Position size small (1-2% of account).
9. **Oracle and STX were the best setups** — Oracle EP 9M Liquid Lava, STX continuation. Both with clear catalysts and orderly action.
10. **Asset allocation day** — Broad-based buying, not selective. You must still be selective about which setup to take.
11. **Prioritize by volume and capitalization** — Highest relative volume first, lowest capitalization for bigger moves.
12. **UnitedHealth keeps failing** — Third session mentioning UNH delayed reaction. Low volume keeps disqualifying it. "It's a trap."
13. **Start of a swing = first day only** — Don't buy the 3rd or 4th day. If you missed the start, wait for the next setup.
