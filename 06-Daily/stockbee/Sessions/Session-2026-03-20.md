### Dell = Profit-Taking Lesson

> "For a stock like Dell, which is a very slow mover, 8% gap up — people are going to take profit. That's massive. $98B stock, 8% — some people bought it yesterday and they're getting 16%. They're going to take profit."

**Structural understanding:** Moves on large-cap stocks are 3-5 days. Period. Don't hold expecting more.

### Three-Day Rule — Fundamental Market Law

> "Moves are three days. Three to four days only. That's so fundamental to the way the market operates that it's not going to change in your lifetime."

This is not a suggestion. It's structural. Stair-step moves are 3-4 days. After that, profit-taking.

### Data Feed Disaster

Interactive Brokers certification expired at market open. Couldn't see quotes, P&L, or positions for 20+ minutes. "Heart was in my butt." Lucky that position size was small. Had stops in place. Lesson: always have stops, because data feeds can fail at the worst time.

### Situational Awareness Poster

> "Are breakouts likely to work? The breakouts are not likely to work, and you keep doing it. It only works in movies."

Reminder: in choppy markets, breakouts fade. Don't trade breakouts without catalysts.
