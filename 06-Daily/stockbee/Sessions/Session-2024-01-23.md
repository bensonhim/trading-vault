# Mind Clarity, High-Priced Stocks, and the SMCI Gift That Keeps Giving

Tuesday was about mind clarity. Pradeep was bullish — the market breadth had improved, T2108 was recovering, and he was 100% invested in his 401k again (after a 30-day wash period). But the real lessons were deeper: why high-priced stocks with tight stops move your account faster than low-priced stocks with wide stops, why anticipation has the lowest win rate of all setups, and why watching your PNL (not charts) is how you sell into strength. He also traded SMCI again intraday — bought at $422, sold at $442 in minutes — just by watching his PNL stall.

### The 4% Breakout Thought Experiment

> "If you buy every single 4% breakout at the start of a swing on biotechnology and technology companies, do you think it might make sense?"

Key insight: Range expansion always happens at the **start** of a move. The 20% study shows what worked in the last 5 days, and those moves all started with range expansion.

**Buy at the start of a swing, sell at the start of a swing.** That's the entire game.

- Even if the breakout closes only 60% near the high (not 70%), the 4% breakout at start of swing still works
- The "Finding Buy Points" video explains this — it was so criticized on YouTube that Pradeep took it down

### SIPs: Quality Over Quantity

> "If you can find two good SIPs, that is more than enough rather than posting a laundry list of stocks."

People were posting nonsense SIPs:
- **ENS (3AM)** — press release company, only product is press releases, $1 stock in permanent decline
- **UAL** — guided below consensus, bearish SIPs not bullish
- **Celsius Holdings** — still late to the party
- **Netflix, Meta** — no real SIPs catalyst

**ADM** was still the real SIPs — CFO fraud, front-page news, "earth-shattering" catalyst.

### The FOMO Problem

> "When a stock gaps up 10% and there is no catalyst, and you have not done any work and you buy — that's FOMO."

- 105 emails asking about 401k = zero conviction
- People want answers to questions they haven't earned the right to ask
- "If you don't have conviction, you need explanation for everything"

### IPO 10 and Linear Moves

Stocks IPO'd in the last 10 years make the biggest moves. Always look for:
- **Young stocks** (IPO 10)
- **Linear moves** (linearity = proxy for strong buying)
- **Stories** (stocks with stories make bigger moves than stocks with earnings)

### FSS: Organize Like Crazy

Focus on SIPs, organized like crazy. The daily visualization:
- Always be in control
- Controlled risk taking
- Find and enter early
- Sell into strength
- Think 3 days ahead, 30 days ahead
- Buy the dip when Fed is active
- 80/20 pill

- The market is choppy and struggling

- SMCI was the trade — day trading it intraday for 4-5 trades

### SMCI Intraday Trade: Watching PNL

> "I bought at $422, and I was just watching my PNL. When the PNL started stalling, I sold at $442 something."

The sell-into-strength methodology in practice:
1. Bought SMCI at $422 when it reversed from $15 down
2. PNL went from $1,000 to $4,000 — kept watching
3. Rate of PNL increase started slowing = stall signal
4. Sold into strength at $442

**No chart watching. No five-minute chart. Just watching PNL.**

> "This is an art. Selling into strength is an art."

### Anticipation Setup: Lowest Win Rate

- "Can I take 10 blue pills before doing anticipation? Yes. It still won't change the win rate."

### High-Priced Stocks: The Key to Moving Your Account

> "In one trade, I can move my account 4-5%. It would take 20 trades on high-ADR stocks to do the same thing."

The math:
- **High-ADR stock**: 10% position, 30% move = 3% account move, but wider stop
- **High-priced stock**: 80% position, 5-8% move = 4-6% account move, with $1 stop (half a percent risk)

"I used to move my account 0.5-2% per trade. Now I move it 5-10% per trade on high-priced stocks."

### What Setup to Trade First

> "Stocks in play, 9M — that's the best. But that's not what people want to trade. Everybody wants to trade anticipation."

Priority order for setups:
1. **9M EP / SIPs** — highest return, catalyst-driven
2. **Sugar babies / combo** — singles that build confidence
3. **Anticipation** — lowest win rate, most dependent on market conditions

## Key Takeaways

1. **Buy at the start of a swing, sell at the start of a swing** — the entire game in one sentence
2. **Mind clarity** — know what you're doing and why, or don't trade
3. **High-priced stocks with tight stops > low-priced stocks with wide stops** — $1 stop on a $300+ stock can move your account 4-6% in one trade
4. **SMCI intraday: watch PNL, not charts** — bought $422, sold $442 when PNL stalled. Art, not science
5. **Anticipation has the lowest win rate** — dependent on market conditions and institutional buying; when market is good, 9M works even better
6. **401k signal** — range expansion after 8% correction, 30-day wash period over, 100% invested
7. **4% breakout at start of swing** — thought experiment worth testing: buy every 4% breakout on biotech/tech at start of swing
8. **IPO 10** — stocks in first 10 years make the biggest moves (Shopify, Amazon)
9. **SIPs quality** — two good SIPs > laundry list of junk. ADM was the only real SIPs
10. **ENSC = press release company** — avoid stocks whose only product is press releases and secondaries
11. **CCJ is NOT a sugar baby** — high volume during consolidation = selling, not consolidating
12. **The combination approach** — singles (combo) build confidence and account; home runs (9M/SIPs) move it big
13. **Selling into strength = watching PNL stall rate** — when PNL increase slows, you sell. Not a chart thing, a PNL thing
14. **3M** — delayed reaction potential, negative growth, constant decline, earnings catalyst
15. **FOMO on 10% gaps with no catalyst** — definition of FOMO. Sell into strength, don't buy
16. **Solve your trading problems in 48 hours** — if you don't, they accumulate and compound
