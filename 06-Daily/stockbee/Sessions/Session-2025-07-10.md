# Morning Fade Persists, Airlines Take Off — FEE Fails, Start-of-Swing Dominates, Insiders Sell Until They Don't

Another day of the persistent morning-fade pattern. Pradeep hammered the point: FEE (First Entry Early) has not been an effective strategy. Later entry, wider stops, smaller sizing — that's what's working. Airlines dominated the SIPs (Delta earnings, Hertz breakout, UAL, AL sympathy plays), while MP's 60% premarket gap raised the classic delayed-reaction question. The PM session was a rapid-fire one-minute scan: airlines, Riders, Kimberly, Novo Nordisk — all start-of-swing setups. The AM session featured an extended teaching moment on the "x-ray method" — look at a stock's earnings history before committing to a delayed reaction.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish but Fading Mornings

Judy's essay: bullish, 300+ green reading (2nd consecutive), T2108 at 64.87, 5:1 bullish ratio. Major indices hit record highs, small caps pushed higher, Bitcoin surged to $112K+.

> "Morning strength is fading is the persistent pattern in this market. Yesterday also, you saw that the morning strength got faded."

> "FEE has not been effective strategy. Later entry, wider stops, smaller size has been better."

### The X-Ray Method — Look at the History Before Committing

> "Always look at history of stock. And history tells you a lot."

Pradeep used a medical analogy: if you claim a stock has gold inside, but the x-ray shows stones, don't operate. The "x-ray" is the earnings/sales history — stocks that make big moves have accelerating sales. Without that, you're hoping, not trading.

> "The stocks which make big moves, they have those kind of x-rays. Otherwise, it just doesn't move."

### Story Stocks > Catalyst-Only Stocks

> "Story is better than a real catalyst, because story stocks make bigger moves than stocks with real catalyst."

### Insiders Selling — Doesn't Matter Until It Does

When asked about share offerings and insider selling:

> "They're telling you they don't believe in those moves... It doesn't matter. And then one day it matters."

> "Valuation, insider selling — all these things are not the best timing indicator. But they matter in the long run. In the short term, the market works based on sentiments."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Morning fade is guaranteed** — "almost like any guaranteed" — buy the close, not the open
2. **FEE is dead** — first entry early has not been effective; later entry, wider stops, smaller sizing wins
3. **X-ray method** — look at earnings history before committing to a delayed reaction; stocks without accelerating sales don't make big moves
4. **Story > Catalyst** — story stocks make bigger moves than pure catalyst stocks
5. **Insider selling doesn't matter** — until one day it does
6. **Airlines dominated** — DAL earnings triggered sympathy across the sector (UAL, AL, HTZ, CAR)
7. **Start-of-swing > continuation** — same pattern as previous days
8. **Nike = dumpster** — don't get excited about turnaround plays without the x-ray confirming
9. **"Be slow here, take smaller positions, wider stops"** — Pradeep's parting advice
