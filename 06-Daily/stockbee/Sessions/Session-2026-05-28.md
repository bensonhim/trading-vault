# Revenge of the 20% Study — Simplify Sectors, Respect Tightness, Stop Is Inherent to Setup

**One-sentence overview:** With the 20% Study at 221 (highest in a year), Pradeep simplifies to three sectors, demands tightness before breakout, and demolishes the idea that stop strategy exists independently of setup — your exit is baked into the structure you're trading.

## AM Session — Extended Market, Simplify Everything

### 1. 20% Study Flashing Extreme

The 20% Study hit **221** (his own scan) or **202** (different price cutoff), the **highest reading in approximately a year**. The last time readings were this elevated (~251), a swift pullback followed. Pradeep's plan of action: **wait and watch, preserve capital**, look for [[EP]] 9M and [[DEP]] setups, avoid extended stocks.

> "The highest in probably a year. The last time you had was in May last year. And then that was followed by this quick pullback."

> "Revenge of the 20% study, you have to be prepared for, right, at some stage."

### 2. Sector Simplification — Three Sectors Only

A major process decision: Pradeep analyzed the 20% Study by sector and found that **Technology, Healthcare, and Consumer Cyclical** consistently produce the big moves. Everything else is not worth the time.

> "There is just no point in even going for anything else but consumer cyclical, health care, and technology stock."

> "So I'm going forward only focusing on three sectors. Take health care, consumer cyclical."

The only exception: **Sugar Babies and EP 9M scans remain universe-wide** — no sector filter on those.

### 3. Volume Threshold — 500K Minimum

Analyzing starting volume of breakouts in the 20% Study, Pradeep found that **171 out of 202 stocks** (85%) had breakout volume above 700K. The practical threshold: **500K minimum**, anything below "let others trade that."

> "If it is going to make a move, you see 1,000,000. And that's, like, almost, like, if 20% move you're looking for, then focus on something with 700K."

> "Anything below breaking out below 500,000, let others trade that. It's not even worth your time."

### 4. Tightness Is Required for Explosive Moves

A repeated, emphatic theme: **tight action pre-breakout** is the common characteristic of every stock in the 20% Study.

> "Tightness matters a lot. Tight action, pre breakout. And that's like an almost a common characteristic of everything."

> "Tightness is required for an explosive move. Three tight days."

The memorable analogy:

> "If I was the dictator of the trading world, like my friend Kim Jong Un, I would make it compulsory that if you want to be a trader, to buy the tightest pants possible and wear that while trading."

### 8. Stop Strategy Is Inherent to Setup — The Horse with Camel Legs

This was the philosophical core of the session. Pradeep dismantled the idea that stop/exit strategy can exist independently of setup type.

**Magnitude vs. Duration move:**

> "The biggest problem why traders have problems with stops is the basic understanding between magnitude move and a duration move. People are trying to trade magnitude move with a duration move philosophy."

**Setup-specific exits:**

| Setup | Holding Period | Exit Strategy |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| Momentum burst (SOS/EP) | 3-5 days | Stop at low of day; exit by day 3 if no move |
| EP with catalyst | Until catalyst target | Sell into strength approaching target; aggressive trailing stop |
| Trend following | Weeks-months | [[TI65]] signal — hold until invalidated |
| Oversized position | Variable | 80/20 peel — sell 80% quickly, keep 20% |

> "You cannot separate exit from the setup. Having strategies independent of a setup is really, it's not going to work."

> "If you are trading something which is a three day or five day move, how the fuck it makes sense to put a ten day moving average on it?"

**The "Horse with Camel Legs" analogy:**

> "You want to find a horse. Then you find one elephant's leg, one camel's leg, one monkey's leg, and start saying, okay, this is a horse. And then my horse is not working. Of course not — you're using parts from different animals."

Traders mix EP entry with Minervini exit with Zanger position sizing — the original idea has been butchered.

**On moving averages:**

> "Moving averages are not required because we are not trading a trend following system. If I decide to follow a trend, I would use TI65. But that's not my setup."

### 9. Position Sizing by Stock Quality

> "Nvidia, I can size. Why? Because institutional quality, owned by 5,000 funds."

> "This [speculative] stock can get down 30% in one day. There is a difference between this stock which has bigger capitalization owned by 2,500 funds, versus some high like this."

For [[GILD]], Pradeep sized down because it's a $3-level speculative stock — not suitable for large position. For [[NVDA]] or other institutional-quality names, you can put on real size.

> "How much of my capital can I put in ONDS? Can I buy 100,000 shares? Nobody is stopping me, but is it really prudent?"

### 10. IPOs and Capitalization

> "Most of these companies IPO so late, the insiders have already made money. So it just becomes a way to palm up these stocks to the retail fools who don't understand IPOs."

> "Capitalization is so important. Explosiveness is capitalization."

Big caps like [[SNOW]] at $60B market cap — Pradeep expects modest moves, not explosive ones. Small caps with genuine catalysts are where the big percentage moves happen.

### 11. The Kamasutra vs. Drone Ant Analogy

In a final memorable analogy, Pradeep referenced his video comparing two life strategies:

> "Your whole life is a couple of weeks. By the time you master all the positions of Kamasutra, you're done, man."

The male drone ant lives 2-3 weeks. Trying to master every trading style (Kamasutra) in that timeframe is futile. Pick one approach that matches your life and stick with it.

## Key Takeaways

1. **20% Study at extreme = caution**: Highest reading in a year; last time this happened, a swift pullback followed. Be very selective, not aggressive.
2. **Three sectors only**: Technology, Healthcare, Consumer Cyclical — everything else is "not worth your time." (Sugar Babies and EP 9M remain universe-wide.)
3. **500K volume minimum**: 85% of 20% movers started with 700K+ volume. Below 500K, let others trade it.
4. **Tightness is non-negotiable**: Every stock in the 20% Study showed tight pre-breakout action. No tightness = no explosive move.
5. **Don't chase extended stocks**: Snow at +36% gap is a DEP candidate, not a chase. NVDA reversal didn't follow through — stopped out at breakeven.
6. **Stop is inherent to setup**: You cannot isolate exit strategy from setup. Momentum burst = 3-5 day hold. EP = catalyst target. Trend following = TI65. Don't mix.
7. **Magnitude vs. duration move**: Using a 10-day moving average on a 3-5 day trade makes no logical sense. Match your tools to your timeframe.
8. **Position sizing = stock quality**: Institutional quality (5,000 funds) = can size up. Speculative $3 stock = size down. This is "not rocket science, just plain English."
9. **80/20 peel only when oversizing**: Normal size = hold normally. Oversize = sell 80% quickly, keep 20%. Doing 80/20 on every trade is wrong.
10. **Theme matters**: AI, semiconductors, drones = where money flows. Crypto = dead. Don't fight the market's theme selection.
11. **Capitalization determines explosiveness**: $60B market cap stocks don't make explosive moves. Small caps with catalysts do.
12. **One foot out the door**: In extended markets, trade like you're in a movie theater watching the last scene — ready to exit fast.
