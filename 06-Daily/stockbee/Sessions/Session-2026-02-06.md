# Liquidation Deleveraging, Max Seven Imploding, and Profit from Human Stupidity

> "Our job is to profit from human stupidity and behavior. That's our only job. The story never changes — dot-com, railroads, silver, Bitcoin — it's always the same."

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### SA: Bearish — 700+ Selling Day, Narrative Change

Market down 3%. VIX above 20. Both primary and secondary indicators red. Five-day ratio below 0.5 — aggressive selling. 20% study elevated (bearish >300), signaling potential bounce.

> "Market is down 3% and the freak-out is crazy. Number of things are driving market moves — this is not driven by retail traders. It's hedge funds, cross-country flows, larger bets."

### Narrative Changes Driving the Selloff

1. **Silver/gold parabolic then crash** — people asking "should I buy gold now that it's down?"
2. **Bitcoin becoming "shitcoin"** — 78% government support, still dropping
3. **Software under AI threat** — new theme this week
4. **Max Seven becoming "Magnificent Vendors"** — topping signals on all of them

### Historical Parallel: Dot-Com, Railroads, Every Bubble

> "When dot-com bubble happened, they were spending money like crazy. Cisco, Qualcomm, laying cable. WorldCom saying there'd be so much demand for data pipes. The story never changes. This is no different from railroads in 1900 or dot-com in 2000."

### OPM Deleveraging in Waves

> "The market is one big, highly leveraged bet. You put $1M in an IBKR account and they give you $6M portfolio margin. When the market goes down, all these leveraged bets get closed."

Deleveraging comes in waves:
1. **Smart people** deleverage first
2. **Less smart people** deleverage second
3. **Dumb people and forced sellers** get liquidated last

More deleveraging likely coming, especially in Bitcoin and crypto.

### Google: Next Max Seven Short

> "If you're looking for a short in Max Seven, Google might be the next one to join the party."

### What to Do Now

1. Wait for breadth to improve
2. Be ready for chop to last some time
3. Selling might accelerate (more deleveraging waves)
4. Control risk — that's all you can do
