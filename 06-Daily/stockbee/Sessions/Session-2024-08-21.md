## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Two Types of Traders — Island Dream vs. Grind

**Island Dream**: Looking for one trade to double/triple/40x the account. Low win rate (25%), need 10-30x risk reward. Most beginners fall here.

**Grind**: Finding 0.5-5% increments, thousands of trades, moving account steadily. This is what professional traders actually do.

> "I was also looking for home runs in my first 3-4 years. Then I realized it's a hell of a lot easier to grind out 10%, 20%, 5%, 8% returns and do thousands of trades like this."

### Key Teaching: Citadel Makes 70% of Profits from 1-3 Day Holds

Showed Citadel's strategy slide:
- **Ultra-high turnover**: sub-second (market making)
- **High turnover**: 1-3 days (this is what Pradeep does)
- **Medium turnover**: <1 week
- Low turnover: 1 month+

> "If the world's best hedge fund with 2,500 brilliant people cannot make long-term trading work, who am I to think I'll keep JLo?"

### Key Teaching: Grind Finances the Home Runs

Two-leg strategy:
1. **Grind singles** → generates cash flow (0.5-5% per trade)
2. **Occasionally find a home run** → financed by grind profits

If you don't have the grind, and you put 8% on a home run that doesn't work, in 20 trades you're down 96%.

### Key Teaching: Member Down 250% — Strategy Mismatch

Member shared: up 300% one year, down 50% next, down 20% next, up 300%, etc. Net nowhere. Pradeep's diagnosis: fundamental strategy problem. Chasing island dreams with 25% win rate. Solution: change strategy to grind first, then add home runs.

> "He's not convinced. He's going to tinker around with his island mentality. Instead of tiny bikini, he'll go with big bikini."

### Key Teaching: Christian Kumba Made First Million Grinding

> "Did he make his first million with island dreams? No. Strong structure shorts. That was a grind."

### Key Teaching: Formula Mindset

When Pradeep read the 8-page appendix in a book (likely Darvas), he found a formula. **Once you find a formula, bells ring.** If you see 9M EP and bells don't ring, you don't have formula mindset. You'll keep poking holes instead of making it work.
