# Earnings Season Delays, CVS Short Setup, and Patience on Breakouts

> "10 to 20 delayed reaction trades will come out of this earnings season with potential 20-50% moves. All you need is patience. The bank doesn't care if you make money from singles."

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### SA: Bullish but Fading — Fed Day Caution

Market gapping up but likely to fade. Fed decision today = stay light, mostly cash.

> "Chasing things — if you're doing chasing, you're not even a professional trader."

### Delayed Reaction EPs: The Big Opportunity

Earnings season starting. Multiple stocks setting up as delayed reaction EPs:
- **[[UNH]], [[CVS]], [[HUM]]** — healthcare sector, Medicare proposal catalyst
- **[[TXN]]** (Texas Instruments) — will set up as delayed reaction
- **[[GLW]]** — 100% confidence it sets up (highest volume in 2-3 years)
- **[[USAR]]** — may set up today
- **[[INTL]]** — potential 10-20% downside, but reversed pattern later removed from watch list

> "Potentially 10-20 trades will come out of this earnings season as delayed reaction with potential to make 20-50% moves. All we need is patience."

### Don't Buy 3-4 Days in a Row

> "How can you cure people's propensity to buy a breakout on the third day? It's an incurable disease."

When someone asks about buying TQQQ after 3-4 days up, that's the sell signal.

### TXN: Cyclical Stock Lesson

> "When cyclical stocks announce their worst earnings, the market anticipates a turn and prices it in. When TXN earnings are very good, that's the time to sell TXN."

Cyclical stocks bottom on bad news, top on good news. Don't buy the "good earnings" headline.

### Carvana Imploded

S&P 500 addition — classic sell-the-news. Gapped up then completely imploded.

### GLW Patience

> "GLW had one of the highest volume days in 2-3 years. It's going to set up. Just a question of having patience."

Don't buy the gap — wait for the setup to develop over 5-10 days.

### IWM May Bounce

Small caps pulled back — IWM likely to make a comeback move.

## PM Session (3 PM)

### Market: No Respite — Down All Day

Gap up faded immediately. Never recovered. Classic Fed-day fade pattern.

> "Whenever you see a gap, especially after 3-4 days up, and somebody asks 'is it time to buy TQQQ?' — that's the sell signal."

### Short Side: CVS Is #1

**CVS is the best short candidate** out of the healthcare trio:
- Volume: 44M shares yesterday — highest in 2-3 years
- Didn't bounce back much (unlike UNH which reversed all gains)
- Clear catalyst (government Medicare proposal)

> "CVS will be my number one idea out of the three. Humana just kept going down, never gave you a bounce. UnitedHealth bounce was too strong."

### Delayed Reaction EP: Patience Required

> "You have to have infinite patience to wait for some of the things with negative catalyst. On the long side, if they work, they immediately move. On the short side, you can have a very negative catalyst, then it rallies, negating everything, and THEN it starts going down."

Pattern: Breakdown → Rally (fake recovery) → Range expansion → 20-30% move down.

### Anticipation Setups: Picture Perfect

PM session had multiple picture-perfect anticipation setups. Better than the morning.

> "Two or three picture-perfect ones are there today."

Potential longs: [[DXYZ]], [[EVG]], Parker Hannifin, Rambus, Amgen.

### Stocks Not Working

- **CRDO** — selling off continuously since earnings, no buyers. Weak structure shot, not inspiring confidence.
- **NXPI** — choppy history, gap-down risk. "The moment I look at this, my feeling is it'll gap down next day."

### Retail Turning Down

XRT down 3 days in a row. [[TJX]], [[COST]], [[HD]], [[LOW]] all correcting. Consumer confidence weakening.

---

### Key Takeaways

1. **Earnings season = delayed reaction paradise.** 10-20 trades with 20-50% potential. Patience required.
2. **Fed day = stay light.** Gaps fade, especially after 3-4 up days.
3. **CVS is the #1 short candidate.** Highest volume in 2-3 years, didn't bounce, clear catalyst.
4. **Don't buy breakouts on day 3-4 of a run.** That's the sell signal, not the buy signal.
5. **Cyclical stocks: buy bad earnings, sell good earnings.** TXN lesson.
6. **GLW will set up — be patient.** Highest volume in years, just needs time.
7. **Delayed reaction shorts: breakdown → fake rally → range expansion → profit.** Study hundreds of examples.
8. **Singles market.** Not getting follow-through on breakouts. Trade what the market gives you.
9. **Retail sector turning down.** XRT down 3 days, consumer confidence weakening.
