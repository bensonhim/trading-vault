# New Year Gap-Up: Sugar Babies Shop, SOXL Rules, and Don't Chase the Second Day

> First trading day of 2026 — everything gapping up, sugar babies on a shopping spree, but the golden rule remains: don't buy on a back-to-back gap-up day. Wait for your entry.

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautious Watching

The market monitor showed another red day on New Year's Eve. Primary indicator red, secondary indicators green but deteriorating. The 20% study leaned slightly bearish (1:1 ratio). Small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) continued underperforming.

Pradeep's SA: **cautious watching** — waiting to see if the gap-up attracts profit-taking.

> "Gap up is not an ideal situation because it makes your risk higher. If you chase the gap, you'll have to have a higher stop."

### The Wait-and-Watch Stance

Multiple stocks gapping up made entries difficult. The plan: focus on [[05-Wiki/StockBee/DEP|delayed reaction]] setups and [[05-Wiki/StockBee/Sugar Babies|sugar babies]].

> "I am not going to force anything. There's no need to force."

### How to Find Stocks With Catalysts

Pradeep laid out the methodology clearly:

> "How do you find stock with a catalyst? Either the ones which are in NTRT, MTRT, or the one with the DEP. Sugar babies — they are perennial. They have something going for them."

### SOXL: The Clear Play

When asked which semiconductor stock to trade, Pradeep was direct:

> "If you don't want to buy individual stocks, your best bet is SOXL. When SOXL moves, it can really move. If it gets going, you're talking 25-30%, 100%."

## PM Session (3 PM)

### Sugar Babies on a Shopping Spree

The afternoon confirmed: **EP 9M was raining setups**. The theme was unmistakable.

> "When the 9,000,000 starts raining, that tells you something always. Look at the market breadth — 248 stocks up. Market breadth is telling you that buying is coming into the market."

### The Emerging Themes: AI, Uranium, Ethereum

Three clear themes surfaced:

> "AI, uranium — that's the play. Ethereum. The magnificent seven are going to become the insignificant seven."

|-------|-----------|-----|

### Money Rotating Out of Mega Caps

A critical observation: institutional money is **rotating out of the magnificent seven** into other stocks.

> "Money is rotating out of the max seven into other stocks. That's what the market is telling you today."

### Lower Capitalization = More Explosive

Pradeep emphasized capitalization as a priority filter:

> "Lower capitalization means more explosive. The stock which makes the biggest move is going to be one which has capitalization below a billion. BMNR capitalization is just $119M. CCCX is $820M."

> "Don't go by hypothesis. Trade the setup. It's a lot easier to trade a setup than a hypothesis."

### Don't Trade Hypotheses

A sharp warning about political/narrative trades:

> "Everybody's hypothesis was Trump is there, Bitcoins are going to do well. How well did that work out? What is likely to go up the most? Something which is a theme or something which has earnings with a blow-up kind of catalyst."

> "What works in the market? Lower capitalization."

### Do Your Work: Delayed Reaction Watchlists

> "Focus like crazy on delayed reaction EP, creating a watchlist, coming in with a list of stocks which are likely to break out today. Early in the morning doing some buying. That is all you need to do for this to work."

### Key Takeaways

1. **Don't chase back-to-back gap-ups.** Wait for low-risk entries — DEP, anticipation setups.
2. **SOXL is the cleanest semiconductor play** when the whole sector moves — higher upside than individual large-cap names.
3. **Sugar babies are perennial** — IRWD, CRWV, NBI — they keep coming back.
4. **Themes beat hypotheses.** AI, uranium, and Ethereum are themes. "Trump will make oil great" is a hypothesis — and it failed.
5. **Lower capitalization = more explosive.** Prioritize sub-$1B market cap when choosing among setups.
6. **Money is rotating out of mega caps** into smaller, thematic stocks. This broadens the rally.
7. **Know your entry tactics before the market opens.** If you're asking basic order-type questions at 9:44 AM, you're not ready.
