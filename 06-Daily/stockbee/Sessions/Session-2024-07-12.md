# Huge Breadth Thrust With Small Cap Rotation, Planned Asset Allocation, Breakouts Likely Better Now

Huge breadth thrust with rotation into small caps. Looks like a planned asset allocation — somebody decided to buy small caps on CPI/interest rate signal. Small caps have underperformed since 2021. Question: will it be a sustainable move or one-day phenomenon? Breakouts likely to work much better now. Expect delayed reaction in small caps.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Breadth Thrust + Small Cap Rotation

> "Huge breadth thrust with rotation into small caps. Will there be follow-through buying?"

SA assessment:
- **Planned asset allocation** — Not organic buying, but institutional reallocation on signal
- **Small caps underperformed since 2021** — Nasdaq turbocharged while small caps struggled
- **TQQ selling off** — Technology being sold to fund small cap buying
- **One day doesn't make a trend** — Need follow-through

### Small Cap Profitability Question

> "The small caps are not having profitability. If they don't have profitability and growth, will this lead to a sustainable move?"

Critical question: small caps lack earnings and growth. Without profitability, this could be a one-day phenomenon rather than sustainable rotation.

### Delayed Reaction Expected

> "I expect there'll be some, probably a delayed reaction kind of thing. They might go sideways."

Small caps may consolidate before making a real move. The initial thrust needs follow-through.

### Breakouts Likely Better Now

> "Breakouts likely to work now, much better."

Environment for breakouts has improved after the breadth thrust.

### Key Takeaways

- Breadth thrust into small caps — likely planned asset allocation
- Small caps lack profitability — sustainability questionable
- Expect delayed reaction/sideways before real move
- Breakout environment has improved
- TQQ (tech) being sold, IWM (small caps) being bought — rotation trade
