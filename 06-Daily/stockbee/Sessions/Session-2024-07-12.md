## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Regime Shift: Huge Breadth Thrust into Small Caps

> "Huge breadth thrust with rotation into small caps. Will there be follow-through buying?"

Yesterday was a planned asset allocation — institutions pre-decided to buy small caps on inflation data:

> "Somebody had decided that there is a sign of lowering of inflation, then they are just going to buy the small caps."

The key question: sustainable move or one-day phenomenon? Small caps have underperformed since 2021. They lack profitability and growth, which is why they've struggled.

> "One day doesn't make a trend, but let's see."

### Breakouts Likely to Work — Much Better Environment

> "Breakouts likely to work now. Much better."

The 20% study jumped to 82 stocks — a clear thrust. First legs are getting established, which means **continuation setups** are next:

> "Your 200% attention should be on finding continuation setups in stocks which have made 15% plus move. That is where the continuation setups are going to happen."

### Story Stocks Will Fly When Small Caps Run

> "If the small caps start going, then you will get crazy moves in 9,000,000 breakouts. You'll have crazy moves happening in story stocks. Not immediately, but after a few weeks."

### Fed Wanting to Cut Rates

> "The Fed is itching to get active. The market is at all time high. They want to reduce rate — they'll just add more fuel to the fire."

## 2 PM Meeting

### Managing Existing Positions

> "If you put 25% position, that moves your account by how much? Think about it."

**SOXL/TQQ shorts** — working, still held with wide stops at high of the day.

### The Start of a Swing: The Fundamental Concept

This session hammered home the same principle as July 10:

> "Buy a swing at the start of a swing. Range expansion at the start of a swing. This simple thing — and it's amazing people cannot understand it."

On [[TSM]]: "First leg is 4-5%. What do you expect the second leg to be? 2.5-3%? You want consistent, persistent buying in the first leg."

On [[RDDT]]: "Is this a start of a swing? Yes. Is there persistent continuous buying? Yes. First leg is 49%. This leg can be 20-25%. High-priced stock, first leg is $18. This might make $9-10."

### Singles Pay for Home Runs

> "Singles are so important. You hit a lot of singles, and then once in a while you get a home run, and you need a wider stop, and you put a wider stop. That's the game. By choice."

The SOXL/TQQ trade: wider stop (high of the day), but funded by singles from SOUN and F.

> "I'm perfectly okay to put 8-10% stop on something I think has potential to make 30-40%. The singles pay for that."

### Self-Efficacy: The Real Problem

53 emails asking "Are you still in TQQ?":

> "Nobody has the guts to do this on their own. I told you I'm going to put a stop at high of the day."

> "Having the self-efficacy belief — that's what is required to become a good trader."

### Regime Change and Fat Tails

> "90% of things in the market are based on historical tendencies. But 10% of the time, fat tails happen, and you can never explain why."

> "The top 10 large cap stocks have the highest concentration ever in the history of the stock market."

Breadth-based models break down when 10 stocks drive everything. But the 2-3 day move framework still works:

> "The unique factors might come and go, but this three-day move still remains valid. If there is a catalyst, the stock will make a crazy move. That still remains valid."

### When Small Caps Fly, Crap Runs

> "When small caps fly, some crazy thing is going to fly. Joby Aviation. Some really crazy thing. Fugazi crap is going to run in the next six weeks. And if I'm in the crappiest stock with some crappiest thing showing up in 9,000,000 record, then it will work. It's the regime."
