# Start of Swing Focus — 20% Study Caution, Biotech Non-Gap Breakouts, and the 100% Movers Are Dog Shit

Pradeep opened July 1 with a bullish but cautious read: select breakouts were working, the 20% study was elevated, and the primary focus was EP 9M / delayed-reaction setups. The AM session became a lecture on start-of-swing buying versus chasing day three or four moves, the nature of 100%+ movers (they're always "complete dog shit" stocks with zero revenue), and why SIPs demand your full morning attention while delayed-reaction EP offers more bang for the buck. The PM session shifted to a rotational market — 800+ stocks showing bullish breakouts as money rotated from leaders into beaten-down names, anticipation setups broke out everywhere but follow-through was the question, and Pradeep warned that bottom bounces work after a big market move but often mean "they ran out of good ideas and are going into the junk drawer."

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish, Select Breakouts Working

The market was in a constructive mode with EP 9M and delayed-reaction EP as the primary focuses.

> "If you see 20% study also, you will see that... there are good number of breakouts which have had follow through... especially in biotechnology and technology."

### Start of Swing — The Most Misunderstood Setup

Pradeep stressed that most stocks were making one leg and then going sideways — the hallmark of a start-of-swing market, not a continuation market.

> "Start of a swing, even a start of a swing, as long as the context is right, it can work. And the context is important."

> "Most of the things are start of a swing, because the stocks are making one leg. And once they've made one leg, they are not after that having much follow through."

### SIPs vs. Delayed-Reaction EP — Choose Your Priority

A member asked about managing SIPs and delayed-reaction EP simultaneously. Pradeep was blunt:

> "If you focus on SIPs, you'll not be able to do delayed reaction EP, and delayed reaction EP gives you a lot more bang for your bucks."

> "You cannot chase all the rabbit. You have to decide and prioritize. My priority is delayed reaction EP, most of the time."

### The 100% Movers Are Complete Dog Shit

After scanning the 40%+ and 100%+ daily movers, Pradeep found every single one was a zero-revenue, reverse-split, no-business microcap:

> "If you want to see a 500% move, what you should see is a stock which has just story. No earnings, no sales, no probability of ever making sales or earnings. Something which is completely, 100% unadulterated bullshit."

> "Shorting those stocks after the move has played out is the most reliable setup. They all collapse."

He highlighted member "Lolo Mart" who now exclusively shorts small caps and is consistently hitting five-figure days.

### Offering After Catalyst — Sell First, Ask Questions Later

On secondary offerings (AVAV, etc.):

> "If the market is wildly bullish, then secondaries get bought. Otherwise, it becomes a reason to take profit."

> "Always better to take your profit and run, and then re-enter."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Rotational Market — 800+ Breakouts, Money Flowing into Junk Drawer

The PM session revealed a massive rotation: 861 stocks in the bullish combo scan, beaten-down names starting swings while leaders got sold.

> "There is clearly a rotation happening today. Stocks which are not rallying started rallying, and the stocks which were rallying are getting sold."

> "That's a signature asset allocation on stocks which are not participating in the move."

Pradeep warned this kind of rotation is often deceptive:

> "Bottom bounces at the start of a swing — they tend to work when the market has really made a big move. Many times that also means they ran out of all the other good ideas. They are going into the junk drawer."

### Anticipation Setups Broke Out — But Will They Follow Through?

Many anticipation setups from the morning list had breakouts by afternoon:

> "So many setups which were having an anticipation, they had a breakout today. But let's see if those anticipation setups have follow-through. First day is not a problem. Second day, third day is the problem."

### No Profit Targets — The Sugar Baby Lesson

On profit targets:

> "I don't have a profit target. Stocks move in three to five days. Does the market guarantee you it's going to kiss you 10 times every night? Nothing is guaranteed in setups. You have to dynamically manage the setup."

### Overtrading and the Beginner's Problem

> "Most of the time, the problem for beginner traders is they don't have a setup. Even if they have a setup, they don't have the control. They're overtrading or they cannot take profit. They think every breakout they buy is going to go to the moon."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start of swing is the primary setup** — most stocks are making one leg and going sideways; buy early or don't buy at all
2. **Delayed-reaction EP > SIPs for swing traders** — you can't chase all rabbits; delayed EP gives more size and better risk/reward
3. **100%+ movers are always garbage stocks** — short-side fading after the move is the most reliable trade on these
4. **Offering after catalyst = sell first** — unless the market is wildly bullish, take profits and re-enter later
5. **Day-three-of-swing is where trouble starts** — "the email indicator" fills up when the easy money is already gone
6. **800+ bullish breakouts can mean rotation, not strength** — money flowing into beaten-down names often signals "junk drawer" buying
7. **No profit targets** — dynamically manage; stocks move 3-5 days, sometimes 30%, sometimes 5%
