# Day Four of the Burst — Cautious, Not Excited; Think Three Days Ahead

Pradeep entered the morning with a clear thesis: the rally that started on Thursday was now four days old, the easy money was done, and everybody turning bullish was a contrarian warning sign. "I'm becoming more cautious," he stated, warning members to watch for yesterday's breakouts getting hit — exactly what happened to [[PLTR]], [[QBTS]], and others. The entire AM session was a live demonstration of **situational awareness in action**: he called the fading breakouts before they faded, sold his [[SOXIC]] position at the open, and got stopped out of [[HYLAIN]] for a small profit. The PM session was a comprehensive walkthrough of the **Trade Factory** — the daily repeatable process that generates setups — and a passionate lecture on **knowing your setup definitions** (especially EP 9M and DEP). The day produced essentially zero new long setups and a handful of short candidates, perfectly validating the morning's caution.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Day Four of a Momentum Burst

Pradeep opened by laying out the bear case for aggressive buying:

- The rally started **Thursday** — this was now day four
- Many semiconductor stocks had gapped up pre-market and were **already fading**
- His email indicator was "completely bonkers" — people asking if they should sell their spouse to buy stocks
- The rally was **narrow**: only [[SNDK]], [[MRVL]], [[MU]], and [[SOXIC]] were moving — "the same thing which happened last time before getting hit"

> "I'm becoming more cautious. I want to see the breakouts from yesterday, some of the breakouts, if they start getting hit right at the morning — and this was what happened before."

> "When everybody becomes bullish, the ability to think opposite of where people are thinking."

> "Think three days ahead."

### Yesterday's Breakouts Fading — The Warning Sign

Pradeep specifically identified what to watch before the open, and it played out exactly as predicted:

> "If you see the breakouts from yesterday getting hit early, that is not going to be a good sign. That is exactly how the correction started."

|-------|--------------|------------|

| [[QBTS]] | Failed breakout, stopped people out | "It's failed. It is taking out your stop." |

| [[SNDK]] / [[MRVL]] / [[MU]] | Up 3-4 days, now fading | "All these SNDK and all have faded" |

### Sold Positions, Duct-Taped Hands

Pradeep took decisive action at the open:

- **Sold [[SOXIC]]** at the opening price — "the rally has run its course, and that was the third day and it was gapping up"

- Told members he had "duct-taped" his hands — sitting on hands, not forcing trades

> "I prepared myself today morning saying that today I need to basically tie my hands and move my stop aggressively on anything which is open as much as possible to lock in the gain."

When members asked about buying opportunities, Pradeep's recurring response was to redirect them away from forcing trades:

> "Can I sell my husband and buy SOXIC?" (member email) → "Sell your useless boyfriend, but don't buy SOXIC. Go to Soapland."

> "My situational awareness is cautious, bullish. And I am telling you people, instead of putting your money into the market, you should go to Soapland."

### The Narrow Rally Problem

Only the same semiconductor names were moving — no broad participation — a classic warning sign that the move was running out of fuel.

### Volume and Liquidity — "Motion in the Ocean"

A key teaching of the morning: **stocks making explosive moves trade millions of shares, not thousands.** Pradeep walked through study after study showing 50%+ movers all had volume of 5M+ shares when they made their big moves:

> "I have a small size. Yes. But it's the motion in the ocean which matters."

> "When they decide to go, they just go with large volume. There is no need to go into this really low liquidity play."

> "My stop was low of the day. Always low of the day."

He recommended even considering raising the volume scan threshold to 2M shares, noting that stocks trading 100K-300K shares simply don't make the big moves.

Pradeep pointed to the screen at the end of the AM session:

> "There are clues. My point is there are clues. What you have to do is think through before the market open — this situational awareness — and operate with situational awareness."

### Key Teaching: Know Your Setup Definitions

Pradeep gave an impassioned lesson on **DEP (Delayed Reaction EP)** definition after a member asked about a stock (DICOM) that didn't qualify:

**DEP Requirements:**
1. Stock must show **EP 9M** volume (highest volume in 3 years or life of stock)
2. Stock must have **reacted positively** to the catalyst (earnings, news)
3. Must be the **first setup** after the catalyst — not second or later
4. Should be **institutional quality** (1,000+ funds owning it)

> "Replace that photograph [of your girlfriend] with this photograph. This is what you are looking for in a delayed reaction EP. Stock which reacted positively to the catalyst, EP 9M, and then does something like this. And then this happens. That is delayed reaction EP."

> "If your basic setup understanding is not right, everything which you do after that — does it matter? Tell me."

**DEP is NOT:**
- A stock that gapped up then sold off (that's a failed breakout)
- A second or third setup after catalyst (too late)
- A stock without institutional quality (696 funds ≠ 1,000+ funds)

### Momentum Burst: The Core Belief

> "Stocks move in momentum bursts of three to five days. In three to five days, they make 8 to 20% moves."

> "One day I went to the Princeton Mountain, and then lightning happened. A guy came in and told me, 'Go forth and spread this message to the world.' And it says: stocks move in momentum bursts of three to five days. And since then, life has never been the same again."

> "Understand the grammar of stock moves. Moves happen in three to four days. One, two, three, four. Then there is a small [pullback]. Again, broke out. One, two, three, four."

He walked through chart after chart showing the same pattern: [[MRVL]], [[SMCI]], [[INTC]], [[DELL]], [[LRCX]], [[QCOM]] — all up 3-4 days, now giving back gains.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Day four of a momentum burst = caution, not excitement.** When stocks are up 3-4 days in a row and the easy money has been made, protect profits, don't chase.
2. **Think three days ahead.** By day four, the probability of a pullback or consolidation is high. Ask yourself what happens next, not what happened yesterday.
3. **Yesterday's breakouts fading = correction signal.** When fresh breakouts from yesterday get hit at the open, that was exactly how the last correction started.
4. **Situational awareness is not a black art — it's paying attention.** Pre-think your market thesis before the open. Come in with a plan, not a hope.
5. **Volume = motion in the ocean.** Stocks making 50%+ moves trade millions of shares. Don't waste time on illiquid plays even if your account size is small.
6. **Stop loss = low of the day, always.** Not high of the day (that gets you stopped out repeatedly), but low of the day.
7. **Know your setup definitions cold.** DEP requires: (1) EP 9M volume, (2) positive reaction to catalyst, (3) first setup after catalyst, (4) institutional quality (1,000+ funds). If a stock doesn't meet these, it's not a DEP.
8. **The Trade Factory is a daily process, not a feeling.** Same scans, same sequence, same rules. "You should be able to do this in your sleep, like driving a car."
9. **When the bearish scan has 179 stocks and bullish has almost nothing — that's situational awareness telling you something.** Institutions are selling while retail is telling each other to hold.
10. **Three EP 9M on the short side = knockout punch coming.** Like boxing — three punches, then the fourth is the knockout.
