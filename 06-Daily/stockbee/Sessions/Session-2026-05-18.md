# Situational Awareness Is King — Distribution Day, Breakouts Unlikely

A day where the market's persistent selling made the answer to Pradeep's opening question — *"Are breakouts likely to work?"* — a resounding **no**. Both sessions were dominated by breadth deterioration, EP 9M shorts having follow-through while EP 9M longs stalled, and the central lesson: **SA (situational awareness) is king**. Only [[HIVE]] had a genuine catalyst (Leopold's 13F filing), and even that was better approached as a delayed reaction EP. The 20% study had pulled back from extremes, T2108 was sliding, and inflation/oil narrative was dragging the market lower.

---

## AM Meeting

### The Million-Dollar Question

Pradeep opened with his core daily question:

> "Are breakouts likely to work? Because that's the million dollar question."

The answer was **selectively, at best**. Most breakouts were failing to follow through — breaking out and then squatting or reversing. This wasn't choppy action; it was **persistent selling**.

> "There is selling. Sellers are in control of this tape now for the last couple of days. More selling than buying."

Key breadth numbers: 2,600 stocks up just days ago had deteriorated to ~1,700. The 4% breakout/breakdown ratio was turning red. Primary indicator was approaching bearish territory.

### Setup Hierarchy for This Environment

| Setup | Verdict | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| **EP 9M** | Only with clear catalyst | Most EP 9M longs had zero follow-through |
| **DEP** | Secondary focus | Delayed reaction after catalyst |
| **SIPs** | Tertiary | Day trading strategy, agnostic direction |
| **Continuation** | Questionable | Market not supporting follow-through |
| **Reversal** | Selective | Only in specific oversold conditions |
| **Anticipation** | Unlikely to work | Buying before breakout in this environment = chopped |

> "Your focus should be only on EP 9M or DEP. Secondary focus: Continuation. SIPs. Because now you don't have environment where records are working well. Anticipation unlikely to work here."

### EP 9M Follow-Through: The Smoking Gun

The most powerful argument against buying breakouts came from scanning recent EP 9M setups:

> "None of the stocks which had EP 9M in recent days has had a follow-through. While EP 9M to the downside, like BA or even Tesla, Righetti, they're having follow through."

Out of approximately 194 EP 9M longs in the last 5 days, only **three** had follow-through: [[ENPH]], [[GEN]] (Gen Digital), and [[PANW]] (Palo Alto Networks — but that was 10+ days ago). Meanwhile, EP 9M shorts were consistently working.

### The HIVE Catalyst

[[HIVE]] Digital Technologies was the one stock with a genuine catalyst: Leopold (the OpenAI alumnus whose fund "Situational Awareness" grew from $250M to $18B) had disclosed a position. Pradeep analyzed it:

- Average price ~$2 (3.4M shares on 6.4M volume)
- Low-priced stock = every retail trader would pile in
- Valid EP, but better as **delayed reaction** entry
- Not a high-conviction trade given breadth environment

> "Some famous hedge fund manager or famous investor buying is a valid EP."

### Inflation: The Shark in the Water

Pradeep identified two macro headwinds:

1. **Inflation returning** — market never does well when inflation becomes the narrative
2. **Oil prices** — declining, adding pressure to energy/commodity names

> "There is a shark in the water. And that shark's name is inflation because of high oil prices."

The bond market was signaling rate increases, contradicting the Fed's easing narrative.

### Self-Leadership

A member called Pradeep asking whether to hold a position. His response:

> "In order to become a successful trader, you need setup, you need all these things, time frame, decision. But the most important thing: self-leadership. I don't ask anybody. I just do my thing."

He stressed that buying based on someone else's mention = no conviction = getting chopped.

> "People buy because they don't have their own conviction. Develop confidence. Select setups based on your methodology, not on somebody's mention."

## PM Meeting

### 20% Study as a Permanent Tool

Pradeep opened PM by emphasizing the **20% study**:

> "This is going to happen every time the 20% study goes high. It is not going to happen next day, but it happens always. You should put this in your situational awareness model because it's very simple, but it is a very powerful signal."

Current reading: ~85 (not extreme selling yet, but directionally heading there).

### Trade Factory Walkthrough

For newer members, Pradeep walked through the complete trade factory:

| Category | Expected Move | Timeframe | Setup |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-------|
| **Home runs** | 20-100%+ of account | Weeks | EP 9M, DEP |
| **Singles** | 8-20% or $5-$50 | 3-5 days | Momentum burst, continuation, consolidation breakout, bottom bounce |
| **Anticipation** | Varies | Pre-breakout | Tight days before breakout |
| **Reversals** | Varies | Varies | Exhaustion bounces |

### Scanning Results: Nothing Compelling

The PM scan was barren:

- **EP 9M longs**: [[HIVE]] (catalyst-based, but questionable), [[BAC]] (Bank of America — no catalyst, start of a swing only)
- **Continuation setups**: [[YPF]] (Argentine oil & gas, Iran catalyst), [[DHX]] (low volume, getting chopped)
- **Anticipation**: [[FLYW]], [[VSTS]], [[VELO]] (holding up relatively), [[SMCI]], [[UNH]] (extended), [[MARA]] (too jumpy)
- **Reversal bullish**: [[AMT]] and [[CCI]] worked from Friday; [[UNH]] looked like a minor pullback

> "I just don't see anything which is really terribly exciting. It doesn't make any sense to buy breakouts right now."

### Distribution, Not Orderly Pullback

The key observation repeated across both sessions:

> "Instead of going into orderly consolidation pullback, they're getting hit like this. Stocks are falling out of bed, which makes me think we're going to get some high volatility event."

[[GLW]], [[LRCX]], [[SNDK]] — semis and tech names were breaking down from what should have been tight consolidation. This wasn't normal pullback behavior.

### What Will Resolve This?

Pradeep laid out the resolution framework:

1. **Breadth thrust** — back-to-back strong buying days
2. **EP 9M scan** — will show 8-10-20 opportunities (not the current barren landscape)
3. **Improving breadth** — 4% breakouts overwhelming breakdowns

Until then:

> "SIPs. Stocks in play. If you are doing SIPs, it's both ways. Intraday shorts are working."

### The Anticipation Analogy

When asked about buying anticipation setups in the morning:

> "Anticipation is like your wife is coming home at 6:00. And in anticipation of that, at 4:00, you start making the bath ready, get rose petals, put champagne in the freezer, cook, buy flowers, light candles. That's anticipation. If you see her coming and quickly run to hide your mess and put something in the microwave — that's buying in the morning. That's not anticipation."

> "Buy anticipation toward the close. Otherwise don't do it."

## Key Takeaways

1. **"Are breakouts likely to work?"** — Ask this every morning. Today's answer was **no**. Let it drive every decision.
2. **EP 9M follow-through is the truth-teller** — When long EPs stall and short EPs work, the market is distributing. Only 3 out of ~194 recent EP 9M longs followed through.
3. **SA is king** — Situational awareness over scans, setups, and tips. The market talks daily; listen.
4. **Self-leadership over tip-following** — Buying because Leopold bought HIVE is not conviction. Develop your own methodology.
5. **20% study is a permanent toolbox item** — When extreme, a pullback always follows. Current reading ~85, heading lower.
6. **Distribution ≠ pullback** — Stocks falling out of bed (not orderly consolidation) signals potential high-volatility event ahead.
7. **Inflation/oil is the shark** — The macro narrative has shifted; bonds are pricing in rate increases.
8. **If breakouts won't work, focus on SIPs and select reversals** — SIPs are direction-agnostic; reversals work in choppy environments.
9. **Anticipation = buy near close, not morning** — Entering early in a bad environment = getting chopped.
10. **Wallet stays locked until breadth thrust** — Pradeep explicitly stated he wasn't opening his wallet. "If I at all open my wallet, I will take out $1 and put it."
