## AM Session (9:15 AM)

300+ green day. Five-day ratio back above 2. T2108 at 65.02. 20% study reheating with 4:1 bullish/bearish ratio. But small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) outperformed — IWM new record high. Mega cap tech pulled back with reversal bullish candles.

### Gap Up = Default to Fade

> "What is my first default reaction to a gap up? It's likely to fade. If it doesn't fade, that tells you the market was caught by surprise."

Semis gapping up on TSM guidance, but most already made their moves — not tradable at these levels.

### Market Breadth: Only Valid at Extremes

> "T2108 is only on NYSE stocks. When these indicators were developed, Nasdaq was a speculative small market. NYSE was the real economy."

> "The only valid use of breadth for timing is when breadth is extremely low. Extremely bullish breadth can remain extremely bullish for a long time. Divergence never works as a topping signal."

Book recommendation: *The Complete Guide to Market Breadth Indicators* by Gregory Morris.

### Sector Rotation: Non-Tech Is King

> "Money is rotating into anything which is non-technology. Chemicals, confectioners, candy, specialty finance, home improvement stores, tobacco, oil and gas drilling — do you see any technology in that? No."

Semiconductors ranked #27 in 10-day sector performance. Beaten-down sectors are where the money flows are.

### 20% Moves Not Transitioning

> "Many of the 20% moves are not transitioning into continuation setups. They're just going and reversing."

One-leg moves — chop developing. Don't go in with guns blazing.

### Silver: Watch for Fireworks

> "If you're looking for a SIPs play, watch silver. There might be some fireworks there."
