# Gap and Fade: Sector EP, FOMO Warning, and the Golden Rule

> A sector EP day — AI infrastructure, uranium, and semiconductor all flying — but the golden rule applies: don't buy on the second day of a gap-up. FOMO is a beginner's problem.

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautious, Watching for Profit-Taking

Market monitor had a 400+ green day on Friday. All indicators now green with improved margins. T2108 increased from 46.27 to 50.24. Small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) reversed their downtrend, closing green. Crypto rebounded with Bitcoin above $90K.

But: **another gap-up day, and back-to-back gap-ups tend to fade.**

> "Another gap up — will it attract profit-taking? See? That's the question you should ask when there is a gap up."

### The Sector EP: AI Infrastructure and Uranium

The dominant theme was unmistakable: **a sector EP in AI, semiconductor, and uranium** — while money rotated **out of the magnificent seven**.

> "Sector EP — there was broad-based buying in AI-related stocks, and broad-based selling in software-related stocks. Semiconductor, obviously, you can see very clearly."

> "The real story is: semiconductor, Bitcoin, and AI infrastructure, which includes all the power plays."

### The Magnificent Seven: Party Over?

> "Max seven — they're all in purgatory while the AI and uranium stocks are flying. Money is rotating out of the magnificent seven into other stocks."

### The CVX Trap: Don't Trade Hypotheses

Many members asked about buying oil stocks on the Venezuela news. Pradeep was emphatic:

> "There is a glut of oil all over the world. Why would the oil stock go up if there is a glut of oil in the world market? You're going by hypothesis — trade the setup, not the hypothesis."

> "FOMO is a beginner trader's problem. There is nothing which has dramatically changed. Wait for a delayed reaction EP."

### The Golden Rule: Don't Buy on the Second Day

> "Don't buy on second day. Wait. It's not going to run away. A back-to-back gap-up — what is it likely to do? It's likely to fade. Wait for a low-risk entry."

> "Every single stock which was gapping up has started to fade. Every single one."

### When You're Already In: Protect Profits

For those who bought on Friday and were now sitting on gains:

> "If you didn't expect that things you bought are going to gap up 10%, what do you do? Move your stops, try and lock in some profit. Peel, peel, peel."

> "Unless you buy something based on a catalyst where you're very clear that there is a game-changing catalyst, sell into strength and move your stops."

Reiterating Friday's lesson:

### Key Takeaways

1. **Don't buy on the second day of a gap-up.** The golden rule. Back-to-back gaps fade.
2. **FOMO is a beginner's problem.** Wait for low-risk entries — DEP, anticipation setups.
3. **Trade setups, not hypotheses.** CVX on Venezuela news = hypothesis. AI sector EP = setup.
4. **Sector EPs signal theme rotations.** AI, uranium, semiconductor flying while mega caps sell off.
5. **Money is rotating out of the magnificent seven.** This broadens the rally.
6. **Protect profits when you gap up.** Move stops, peel, don't let gains evaporate.
7. **Build your model book continuously.** Add each successful setup pattern — don't wait for year-end.
8. **Write your daily guide by hand.** Bite-sized, not 20 pages.
9. **Healthcare sector is being positioned into** — start-of-swing setups appearing in medical stocks.
10. **Lower capitalization = more explosive.** Always.
