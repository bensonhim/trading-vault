# Revenge of the 20% Study — Market Transition Day

The 20% Study went extreme and the market stalled — exactly as it always does. Pradeep identified the transition from "let winners run" euphoria to chop, warned against buying breakouts, and spent both sessions hammering the same point: **not every stock is a DEP, and the 20% Study is a forward-looking indicator that always strikes**. Two genuine short-side setups emerged (ZPS, Whirlpool), while the long side offered almost nothing actionable.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### 1. The 20% Study Went Extreme — And the Market Stalled

The core theme of the morning session. The 20% Study had been elevated for days, and Pradeep had been cautious since it crossed the danger zone. Today, the market confirmed what the indicator always signals:

> "It takes some time, but it always strikes. Every time people say 'this time is different.' Yeah. Every time people say this time is different. It is not different."

The 20% Study is a **money flow indicator** — when too many stocks are making 20%+ moves, the market is overextended. Historically, within 5–10 days of going extreme, a pullback follows. This is not a long-term indicator; it's a **short-term forward-looking signal** of money exhaustion.

> "If you can wrap your head around 20% Study, it will save you lot of time in your life. And that is something which is a real edge according to me."

Pradeep calls it the **"kissing your cousin" indicator** — when everyone is euphoric and saying "let winners run," that's the yellow light before the red. He urged members to do a deep dive on the 20% Study going back years:

> "Anybody who's serious about trading, especially if you are swing — this is what you should put in your notebook. The observation about 20% Study, how 20% Study helps you. Because you are going to forget this next time 20% Study goes like this."

### 2. Market Transition: From Marathon to Chop

After an unprecedented 17% move in the S&P 500 in less than 1.5 months, the market is transitioning. The consensus narrative — "let winners run" — is exactly the wrong stance now:

> "What will actually happen here is this will become a better market now for swings. Because a lot of these stocks are going to just make two or three days' move. Sometimes they're just going to make one day big move, and then they are going to help things."

The market faces two resolution paths:
1. **More selling** — deeper pullbacks across extended stocks
2. **Chop** — sideways rotation, death by a thousand cuts

> "Whenever there is choppiness — death by thousand cuts."

### 3. Breadth Deterioration Behind the Scenes

While the index held up, breadth was quietly deteriorating:

- Stocks down 20% increasing (73 on the day)
- Leaders stalling: [[SNDK]] down 12-13%, [[AMD]] stalled, [[INTC]] orderly pullback
- Fresh breakouts not following through ([[GOOG]] sitting, [[AVGO]] stalled)
- Bond yields above 5% — the narrative shifting to **inflation**
- "The other stocks are getting destroyed" — only AI/semi holding up

### 4. EP 9M Downside — First Dip, Don't Chase

Pradeep identified early signs of EP 9M to the downside, but cautioned:

> "You don't chase the first down. You wait for it to deteriorate and then take the easy money."

The pattern is consistent: first EP 9M downside attracts dip buyers. Second time, dip buyers step in again. **Third time is when it starts seriously going down.** Today was the first dip — deep buyers were active on [[NVDA]], [[SNDK]], etc.

> "I'm inclined to believe that we are going to get an EP 9,000,000 party to the downside."

### 5. Quick Trade: Oracle (ORCL)

Pradeep entered [[ORCL]] as an EP 9M trade early in the session — $2 stop, entered around $185, went to $200, then puked back. Small win, quick in and out:

> "Paid for my many years of Great Idea subscription."

### 6. Missed Opportunity: Boeing (BA)

A genuine catalyst — China buying 150 planes as a "lollipop" after Boeing's earnings miss. Pradeep saw the news but didn't connect the dots fast enough:

> "That BA, that was a lollipop. An absolute lollipop, but I didn't really connect the dot yesterday."

### 7. Continuation Setups ≠ Fundamental Catalysts

Pradeep drew a sharp line between structural setups and catalyst-driven setups:

> "Continuation setup is just a market tendency. It has nothing to do with fundamentals. If you try and bungee in fundamentals there, you're going to mess it up."

Continuation setups exploit the **structural tendency** of the market — momentum begets momentum. They work irrespective of earnings, CEO drama, or news.

### 8. Not Everything Is DEP — Catalyst Must Be Game-Changing

This was a major theme across both sessions. Pradeep was frustrated by members calling random stocks DEP:

**FROG** — Not a DEP. Earnings surprise but no game-changing catalyst.

**[[GLW]] (Corning)** — Real DEP. Earnings + huge Meta order. That's a genuine catalyst.

**[[NBIS]]** — Real DEP. $17.4B contract (potential to $19.4B) for a company with $117M revenue. That's game-changing.

**[[MNST]] (Monster)** — Not a DEP. Growth rate declining from 27% to 6% projected. "Same probability as Jennifer Lopez calling you for a date."

> "Outlier earnings is what you're looking for. Outlier earnings — not only outlier earnings, but it is a surprise to the market. This fundamental understanding is what is going to give you those explosive things."

87% of stocks beat earnings this season. By the "good earnings" definition, every stock is a DEP. That's meaningless. **You need outlier earnings that surprise the market.**

### 9. Long-Term Holding Requires Stock Selection Edge

> "Holding long term is a valid strategy, provided you have some reason and way to select stocks. Otherwise, you're going to be getting a lot of stocks that are gonna make 20% moves and then collapse."

Most profitable firms — Citadel, Jane Street, Goldman Sachs — do **swing trading, not long-term holding**. The entire industry has moved to high-frequency and short-term strategies for a reason.

### 10. Cerebras (CBRS) IPO Prediction

> "This is almost guaranteed that CBRS is going to be cut into half. Just wait for a good low-risk setup on it."

High-flying IPOs have a 99% probability of declining. Same pattern as Facebook IPO. Wait for the short setup.

### 11. Anticipation Setups: Only at 3 PM

> "The only time at which you should look at anticipation is at 3 PM. Before that, if you look at it, it's just a waste of time and waste of the process."

[[GOOG]] — looked like a good breakout. Nothing happened.
[[AVGO]] — same pattern. Broke out, stalled.

> "If you have itchy fingers, there are a lot of breakouts. Citadel and Jane Street are looking for you. They will send you a Christmas card."

| Setup | Direction | Catalyst | Pradeep's View |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------------|
| **ZPS** (Zoetis) | Short | Earnings miss + pet spending decline | ✅ Genuine DEP — delayed reaction short |

| Anticipation setups | Long | None | ❌ Lowest win rate, illusion of low risk |

ZPS and Whirlpool have **clear catalysts** — pet spending cuts and housing collapse respectively. Whirlpool is "one of those businesses which cannot really turn around in next four quarters."

### 3. Conviction Must Come From Your Own Research

The ONDS trade became a teaching moment. Pradeep held through chop because he had done his own catalyst research:

> "You cannot create secondhand conviction. You do the research. After your research, based on your analysis, you believe this stock has potential. Then you buy that. Either your judgment is going to be true or not."

People who bought ONDS just because Pradeep mentioned it had **zero conviction** and were "getting antsy" when it chopped. The key insight:

> "In this business, you have to cook your own food and eat it. Otherwise, it doesn't work."

### 4. SNDK Is NOT a Long-Term Hold

> "SNDK is not a stock which is a long-term hold. You are just one year late to that party."

It's a cyclical industry. When the cycle turns, these stocks go down 90%. Referenced the "Kamasutra" video. When Chinese competitors enter memory chips at 1/5 the price, the game is over.

> "You buy it after it has made a thousand percent move, you are going to be the bag holder."

### 5. Anticipation Setups — Lowest Win Rate

> "Most of the time, anticipation setups only work if there is a roaring bull market and everything is melting up. Most other times, you're going to get the illusion that you found a low-risk entry point."

Only look at them at 3 PM. Before that, it's "thinking about dinner at breakfast."

### 6. Using Grok for Catalyst Research

Pradeep uses **Grok** (xAI) for quick premarket catalyst research because it has access to Twitter/X feeds — "Twitter is really human intelligence now." He prompts simply: "What is the bullish/bearish case on [TICKER]?"

He compared Grok favorably to Gemini, which calls every earnings beat an EP and every pullback a DEP — "like an ordinary StockBee member." Despite feeding his entire site into Gemini, it still generates "complete total random" suggestions.

### 7. Procedure in Memory

> "If you have procedure in memory, you can go through 30, 40, 50 stocks in five minutes."

The alternative is asking random questions about random stocks — "What do you think of this? What do you think of that?" — which is not a process, it's a lottery.

### 8. Tesla Short — Potential but Fanboys

Tesla has genuine short catalysts (high interest rates, FSD not approved in China), but the fanboy base keeps buying dips:

> "The only problem with Tesla is whenever it goes down, next day the fanboys come to buy it. That's why I'm avoiding that."

## Key Takeaways

1. **The 20% Study always strikes.** When it goes extreme, a pullback follows within 5–10 days. "This time is different" is always wrong. Document this for conviction.
2. **First dip attracts buyers.** Don't chase the first EP 9M downside. Wait for the second or third — that's when the easy money is made.
3. **Not every stock is a DEP.** A DEP requires a **game-changing catalyst** — outlier earnings that surprise the market, or a transformative contract. 87% of stocks beat earnings this season; that doesn't make them DEPs.
4. **Conviction must be self-generated.** You can't trade on secondhand conviction. Do your own catalyst research, then size accordingly.
5. **Breakouts fail in chop.** After a 17% S&P move in 1.5 months, the market transitions to hit-and-run. Breakouts have low probability.
6. **Cook your own food.** "In this business, you have to cook your own food and eat it. Otherwise, it doesn't work."
7. **Anticipation setups are for 3 PM only.** Looking at them earlier is "thinking about dinner at breakfast" — lowest win rate of all setups.
8. **Cyclical stocks are not long-term holds.** SNDK, memory chips — when the cycle turns, they collapse 90%. Don't buy after a 1000% move.
9. **Use Grok for catalyst research.** Simple prompts, Twitter-fed intelligence. Better than Gemini for premarket analysis.
10. **Procedure in memory = speed.** With a developed process, you can screen 30–50 stocks in 5 minutes. Without it, you're asking random questions.
