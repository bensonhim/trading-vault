# Holiday Week: Continuation Setups Forming, Process Over FOMO

> "Primary break turned bullish last week. Lots of continuation setups setting up. But this rally lacks the institutional FOMO of prior rallies — breakouts are hit and miss. Holiday week: aggressively peel, follow the process, and don't force trades."

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish — But Lacks Institutional FOMO

> "Primary break turned bullish last week. Number of stocks up 50% for the month exceeds stocks down 50%."

But the rally lacks the thrust:

> "Qualitatively, this rally so far lacks the FOMO of other rallies. Stop and go — breakouts work for 3-4 days, then don't, then work again. Not at a level where I want to go 200% on margin."

**Focus**: Continuation setups, 9M EP, delayed reactions, sugar babies, two-linch.

### Holiday Week Caution

Monday-Wednesday only, then Thanksgiving Thursday and half-day Friday. Most trading grinds to a halt by Wednesday. Moves can get exaggerated due to low volume.

> "I'll continue to do FEE and peel aggressively because breakouts aren't doing what I want them to do."

### Situational Awareness: A Daily Practice

> "The question you're trying to answer is simple: Are breakouts working? Are reversals likely to work? How aggressively should I trade? If breakouts are working, do find-and-enter-early. If follow-through is 3-5 days, don't peel on day one."

> "I used to write my own SA every day, check what happened vs. what I said. Do it intensively for 6 weeks, and suddenly it becomes second nature."

### 4% Breakout: Observation → Setup → Money

> "Trading is just observation. From observation, you convert that into a setup. From the setup, you make money."

Reiterated: every 20%+ move starts with a 4% breakout. If you see it enough times, you can take it to the bank.

> "If every time your wife wears a purple dress, you get action — then you rig her wardrobe to only have purple dresses. 4% breakout is the purple dress."

## 11 AM Meeting

> "I had no process, so I was all over the place. Now I solve my trading problem by creating a trade factory — same process day in and day out. If it's 9 AM, I do SA. Same thing every day."

### Continuation Setups Emerging

[[QQQ]], [[SPY]], [[BABA]], [[AAPL]], [[UBER]] — all showing continuation setups. The first leg has formed, now watching for second legs.

### Priority: Higher Price, Higher Liquidity

> "I'm looking for higher-priced stocks, more liquid stocks. I can't get into stocks with 300K daily volume. If I buy 10-15K shares, that's a problem."

When similar setups exist, choose: (1) higher price, (2) higher liquidity, (3) lower capitalization — in that order.

### Margin Use

> "I was on margin in the morning, but did profit booking so I'm not on margin currently. Margin allows me to magnify the return. If I see something, I want to make as much money as possible."
