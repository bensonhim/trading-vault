# Choppy Market, EP 9M Focus, and Write Your Process

Choppy market after a marathon run — EP 9M setups dominated the session, previous trades (NVIDIA, CRWV) failed follow-through and were stopped out, and Pradeep's central message was blunt: **write your process every morning or stop asking the same questions**.

---

## PM Meeting

### Market Context: Choppy After the Marathon

The market was in a choppy transition — not directional enough for clean breakouts. Pradeep noted the broader tone was unclear:

> "It's not really reversal bullish."

> "I really don't see much here which is actionable for me."

Previous positions — [[NVIDIA]] and [[CRWV]] — didn't follow through. Both were stopped out:

> "I'd moved my stop to protect some profit, got stopped out on both of them. So back to cash."

The lesson: after a strong trend run (the "marathon"), the market needs consolidation. Breakouts in this environment have low probability.

### The GLW Stop Mistake

Pradeep admitted a process error on [[GLW]]:

> "I made a silly mistake on GLW. Instead of putting a stop loss, I sent it as a limit order in the morning."

> "I just put, sent it as a limit order in a hurry while trying to adjust. So it just stopped me out."

This is the "next step in trading setups" error — not just identifying the setup, but executing the mechanics correctly. A good setup, botched by order type.

### What's NOT Working

**Reversal bullish**: Pradeep scanned and found nothing actionable.

> "I don't really see anything in reversal bullish. Not at all."

**Extended stocks**: People kept asking about stocks already up 10-20%:

> "If people want to buy some random stock after it has gone up for two weeks, three weeks… they don't have any process in the morning."

> "Then they're asking after a stock is up 10% or 20%, is it still a buy?"

**NVIDIA and CRWV**: The action on these didn't look promising at all — Pradeep was clear they weren't acting right.

### The Dating Analogy (Don't Re-enter Stopped-Out Trades)

On re-entering a trade after being stopped out:

> "Do you go and date the woman who said no to you the first time?"

Don't re-enter. The setup failed. Move on.

### Sugar Babies and Setup Discipline

When asked about a stock that was already extended:

> "What setup is this? It's a Sugar Babies."

> "If Sugar Babies say that, do you want to come today? Do you say no?"

The point: even within Sugar Babies (stocks you can buy from any level), you need rules. When in doubt, don't chase.

### Process Writing: The Central Message

The most important part of this session wasn't a stock pick — it was a repeated, emphatic call to **write your process**:

> "Everyday morning, get up, rewrite your guide."

> "Write the process. Write the process. Write the process. It's a painful thing, but you have to write the process."

> "If you don't write it, it's never going to go into your thick brain."

> "Every day, I come and rewrite this card. Why do I rewrite this card every day? Because whatever you write, you learn."

Specific instructions:

1. **Write rules for every setup**: EP 9M entry rules, DEP 9M entry rules, Sugar Babies rules, where to place stops, how to handle gap-ups.
2. **Handwrite, don't type**: Pradeep cited research showing that physical writing increases retention. Typing doesn't work.
3. **Do it every morning**: 30 minutes each day. This is the "next step" most traders skip.
4. **The profitable traders in the room have whiteboards**: "They have written down on the whiteboard rule for every setup."

> "Those who are really motivated, they will do this. Those who are not motivated, tomorrow morning, they will show up again and say, 'I missed the entry on GLW. Now it is 12 percent and $22. Do you think I should still buy it?'"

> "You can keep crying. Keep crying."

## Key Takeaways

1. **After a marathon, expect chop.** Breakouts have low probability in choppy transition markets. Adjust or sit on hands.
2. **EP 9M was the setup of the day.** Sector clusters (uranium, optics) produced multiple EP 9M breakouts — watch for sector theme alignment.
3. **Order execution matters as much as setup identification.** Pradeep's GLW mistake — limit order instead of stop — cost him the trade. The "next step" is mechanical execution.
4. **Don't re-enter stopped-out trades.** The dating analogy: if she said no, she said no.
5. **Write your process every morning.** Handwrite the rules for each setup — entry, stop, gap-up handling. This is the #1 way to internalize trading rules. Typing doesn't work.
6. **30 minutes of morning writing > hours of asking questions.** The traders who profit have whiteboards with rules. Those who don't write keep asking the same questions.
7. **Nothing actionable in reversal bullish.** When breadth is choppy, reversal setups don't light up.
8. **Liquidity is non-negotiable.** Don't trade wide bid-ask stocks. Period.
