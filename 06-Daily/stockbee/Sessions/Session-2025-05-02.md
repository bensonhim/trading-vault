### 20% Study — Range Expansion Teaching

- 27 stocks on 20% bullish study — all range expansion at start of swing
- "Contrary to what you believe, most stocks will NOT be a gap up"
- "You can completely eliminate gap up from your trading and still find good setups"
- Most 20% movers start from weakness/lows, NOT from consolidation
- "Do you see a consolidation breakout in this entire list? No."
- All starting from weakness — bottom bounce pattern (Hudson Chemical example)

### Capitalization Teaching — The Key to EP Profits

- HIMS made 1000% from IPO, now 7.2B market cap
- "Below 1B capitalization is what you should look for" for biggest moves
- JRVR (900% move): what was starting market cap?
- DOMH (500% move): 28M market cap
- RGLS (500% move): tiny market cap
- CRWV: already 20B — "market is already pricing in a lot"
- "If you want to really make money on EPs, find something with small capitalization"
- Carvana rare case at 50B, Palantir rare case at large cap
- "How many Palantirs are there? It's a unicorn."
- Root: 2B after big move, started at 126M
- "Most of the time, the ones which make the biggest move will be below 1B capitalization"
- CEP: 100M cap before the move — trying to become another MicroStrategy

### Sugar Babies = Leading Indicator

- Sugar babies (speculative stocks) breaking out yesterday and following through today
- WOLF following through nicely
- "Sugar babies act like some sort of leading indicator"
- "When you see sugar babies starting to break out and follow through, typically market tends to do well"
- "Like a barometer of speculative money in the market"
- BUT: "too many sugar babies is a sign the Saudi Sheikh is in town" — melt-up = frothy, last stage before correction

### Earnings Reactions — Brutal, Then Recover

- "One of the things you see this earnings season: good earnings, then sell off, then make moves later"
- Not CEP pattern — delayed reaction after initial sell-off
- Eli Lilly sold off on PBM news, may recover
- O'Reilly: sold off, then immediately recovered
- "Earnings reactions are not as positive as other earnings seasons"
- Many stocks selling off after earnings — strange pattern

### 50% Move Teaching (Continued)

- 50% down moves: 139 stocks down 50% in 25 days
- Persistent selling = key characteristic of 50% down moves
- "Almost invariably there'll be a weak structure short if there is a 50% move"
- "Persistent selling near the high = institutional funds selling = something wrong"
- 3-5 consecutive down days near high = danger signal
- "If you want to short something, better to short something near high than near low"
- Range expansion near 52-week high works better for shorts
