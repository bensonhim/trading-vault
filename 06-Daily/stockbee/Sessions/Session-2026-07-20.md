# The Marshmallow Test — Self-Control, Mean Reversion Bounces, and the Chop Till October

July 20 was a psychology lesson disguised as a trading meeting. The market tried to bounce on low 20% study readings, but Pradeep warned it was a weak, mean-reversion bounce — not a trend resumption — because the bearish readings weren't excessive enough and there was no concurrent confirmation. By the PM session, the bounce had already faded and everything was selling off again. The day's real teaching was about **self-control**: the marshmallow test, Albert Bandura's self-efficacy theory, and Pradeep's observation that traders who lack discipline in the rest of their life will lack it in trading too. He told members who've been struggling for 5-6 years to consider that trading may not be for them. The only recommended setup: SIPs for day trading. Everything else = chop.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### SA: Same as Before — Choppy Range, Secular Bull Intact

> "Long term situational awareness remains same. That is we are in a secular bull market. We started in 2009. 2022 was when ChatGPT was launched, which added more fuel to this secular bull market."

> "This week might chop till October. And then the next earnings season is when we might make a move. ... You can have a choppy period for an extended period of time. ... 2022, full year was like this."

Historical precedent: 2012 also had a full year of chop after a big move. The Scandinavians who started trading after the 2009 rally got wiped out in 2022 by trading breakouts with 10-day moving average stops.

### The 20% Study Bounce — Weak, Not Convincing

The 20% study bullish reading was at 15-18, and the bearish side was only 46 — not excessive enough for a strong bounce.

> "It's not in proportion to the number of stocks down. And it's only 46. ... This works best when you have very high readings of 100+ also going with it. ... Extremely negative breadth is very good for a bounce to happen. We are not really there."

A member asked: last time the 20% study was low, you expected a 3-5 day bounce and it happened. What's different this time?

> "That time, I was very confident. I actually bought SOXL and things like that. Why? Because this [bearish reading] was very high. And this is not very high currently. ... If you have excessive bearishness, you get a good solid three to five days bounce. If it is not there, then it's just a weak bounce which sucks in more people and does nothing."

### The Market Ignored Bad News — That's Information

Friday's Kim Kardashian news (negative catalyst) failed to push the market down. That was information.

> "Yesterday, Kim Kardashian failed to arouse the market. Her sex appeal didn't work. ... If it doesn't sell off, it goes up for a day. ... Whenever there is good information or bad information and the market reacts [or fails to react], that tells you a thing or two."

This is the "Indian skill" — observing what the market does NOT do.

BB also got stopped out. Both were good setups that worked — the chop just meant you had to take profit quickly.

### The Marshmallow Test and Self-Control

The AM session's centerpiece was a deep dive into trading psychology.

> "If you don't have self control in your rest of your life, it is very difficult to have self control in trading. Your trading is a reflection of your inner ability to do self control in rest of your life."

> "Try and go on a diet. Try and reduce weight. Try and go and do exercise every day. That is an example of self control. Nobody is forcing me to go to the gym every day. I pray my trainer to put me through a very difficult workout every day."

**The marshmallow test:**
> "Kids who were in a room, told if they don't eat the marshmallow, they'll get a second marshmallow. The kids who waited — they followed up for 25-30-40 years — they went on to become more successful in life, had less problems, higher confidence. Delayed gratification."

**Albert Bandura's self-efficacy theory:**
> "Self efficacy belief is the foundation of human achievement. Most widely accepted theory of human achievement, by a scientist called Albert Bandura who died a couple of years ago."

**The Zoom call observation:**
> "When I get on a Zoom call with these traders, what is the first thing I notice? They're scrappy. Their appearance looks like a homeless person sitting outside Goldman Sachs office. ... You see their body language. You see how they talk. They just don't have that thing. ... In order to fix your trading, you can do deep dive. You can do all these things. But start by doing 100 burpees."

### Kissing the Cousin

The recurring metaphor for buying breakouts in a choppy market:

> "You tell yourself, I'm not going to kiss my cousin. And the moment you see your cousin, you just start kissing. ... What is the solution? If you can find a solution to that, that is when you will have [success]."

Pradeep shared the origin of his momentum burst method:

## Key Takeaways

1. **Secular bull intact, choppy range till October** — The 18% move in two months is being digested. Historical precedents (2012, 2022) show this can last months.
2. **The 20% study bounce was weak** — Bearish readings at 46, not 100+. Without excessive bearishness, the bounce sucks people in and does nothing.
3. **The market ignoring bad news is information** — Friday's Kim Kardashian catalyst failed to push the market down. That meant short-term selling was exhausted → bounce. But the bounce was weak.
4. **Fading breakouts > buying breakouts** — In a range-bound market, breakouts are more likely to fade than follow through.
5. **Google and BB shorts stopped out** — Good setups, but choppy market means you must take profit quickly. The remaining positions got stopped out.
6. **SIPs is the only recommended setup** — Day trading with catalyst-specific stocks. Focus on tech, biotech, and consumer discretionary (AI/crypto within tech).
7. **Self-control is the #1 predictor** — The marshmallow test, Albert Bandura's self-efficacy theory. If you can't control yourself in life, you can't in trading.
8. **The Zoom call tell** — Traders who are struggling show it in their appearance, body language, and demeanor. Fix your life first.
9. **Dilution risk is everywhere** — Archer Aviation, CLSK, Cipher Mining — all have shelf offerings or serial dilution history. Check before buying any catalyst.
10. **Anticipation is the worst setup here** — Even worse than breakouts. Guaranteed chop.
11. **Infinite patience** — Pradeep has seen this before. He's not tempted. He waits.
12. **If you've been struggling 5-6 years, consider that trading may not be for you** — Not laziness, but some people are not wired for it. Self-leadership is the #1 predictor.
