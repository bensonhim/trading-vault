- 20% study: 2:1 bullish/bearish ratio

- Broad-based selling Monday — Dow waterfall decline, S&P and Nasdaq steep losses
- No catalyst on horizon — "market is approaching low established few weeks ago"
- Stocks that had established first legs last week: all reversed with selling in last 3 days

### No EP Today — Starbucks Missed Opportunity

### 2,500+ Fund Stocks — None in Uptrend

- Walked through list of stocks held by 2,500+ funds (must-own stocks)
- "None of them are in uptrend"
- FAANG stocks barring Netflix — all in purgatory
- Google, Meta, Amazon in "worse purgatory than anything else"
- Coca-Cola only one holding up — "that tells you the market doesn't have any confidence, it knows only thing people are going to do is drink Coke"
- Insurance companies that held up until last week now breaking down (Hartford, CVS)
- "The stocks which have been beaten down are the ones which are going to rally"

### Biotech Emerging Theme — But Questionable

- 20% study dominated by biotech stocks
- "Biotech is where the thing is" — the emerging theme
- But "should we consider swing in biotech? The answer is yes — if there is a good setup"
- Even in this market condition, 12 bullish + 20+ stocks in 20% study
- Most are random stocks — no clear theme beyond biotech

### Don't Get Chopped — Members Down 40%

- Members who loaded up on Meta, Google, Amazon on one-day reversals now asking what to do
- "Every time I loaded up, it has worked on Meta — what can go wrong?" — classic buy-the-dip FOMO
- Members with 40% drawdowns from TQQ, SOXL bought at tops
- "There is not a day goes by when somebody doesn't email me asking if they should put all their account in SOXL"
- "If you're getting chopped, please stop. Don't dig yourself a hole"
- T2108 not being deceptive — it's the external policy factor that's beyond indicators
- "All indicators work under a right regime. If the regime is illogical, if the regime is hell-bent on screwing the economy, there's nothing you can do about it"

### Policy Bear Market — Not Fundamentals

- "This is a bear market because of policy. It is not because of underlying fundamentals"
- FAANG stocks going up for 10 years — "every person is in there"
- "History of market: stocks that do well for 10 years, do they do well the next 10 years? No."
- Since 2009, market only went up — buy-the-dip was the go-to strategy
- "Everybody has been conditioned to just think foolish"
- Morning gap up — likely pressure to produce good news, but doesn't inspire confidence
- Palantir: gave up all gains from morning — classic chop
