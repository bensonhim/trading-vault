# Buy-the-Dip Bounce Day — Trade Factory Deep Dive, SA Framework Teaching, 80/20 and FFM Execution

Day 5 brought a textbook buy-the-dip bounce after 5 consecutive down days, and Pradeep used it to deliver a comprehensive Trade Factory masterclass: the two product lines (EP 9M and Combo LTB), the three superchargers (fee, oversizing, 80/20), and why belief drives behavior. The bounce was expected per SA but faded by afternoon — a choppy, low-conviction environment where only nimble day-trades and 80/20 exits preserved profits. Weak structure shorts proliferated on the anticipation scan, signaling the market shift continues.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM & 11 AM)

### Situational Awareness Is Setup-Specific

> "Are breakouts likely to work? That is the ONLY question. I don't give a damn about the indexes."

Pradeep's core SA framework — each setup has its own essay:

| Setup | SA Condition |
|---|---|
| Stocks in play | Works any day — catalyst drives the move |
| Momentum burst | Primary indicator green on all 4 columns |
| 401k | T2108 below 20 (extreme = single digits = "sell your kidney and buy") |
| Reversals / bottom bounce | Market at extreme oversold, T2108 < 20 |
| Breakouts | Primary green, market trending |
| Weak structure short (WSS) | Works in ANY market condition |
| EP 9M | Works any condition, but magnitude may be lower in pullbacks |
| Shorts | Near turns when primary is red |

The **sequence**: bottom bounce → continuation → consolidation breakout. We are now in the pullback phase where shorts emerge and continuation bounces are short-lived.

### 20% Study — Tactical Decision Tool

> "20% study tells you where the dip buyers are likely to be."

After 3-5 days of selling, dip buyers emerge. That's why aggressive shorting when the market is down 5 days in a row gets you squeezed. The bounce was expected — not because of employment numbers, but because the 20% study was at the lower end.

### 9M Breakdowns = First Signal of Market Change

> "For two months you had zero 9M breakdowns. Suddenly, in the last 20 days, proliferation of breakdowns."

The proliferation of EP 9M breakdowns was the first structural sign the market had shifted from bullish to pullback mode.

### The Trade Factory — Two Product Lines

**Product Line 1: EP 9M (Sugar Babies That Pay You)**

> "Sugar babies which pay you — that's the kind of sugar baby you want."

Big catalyst → volume reflects that → scan for volume threshold (9M+). Then classify:
- **Cat** = high conviction → allows size
- **Dog** = magnitude (200-300% moves) → small size still profits
- **Liquid lava** = allows size with close stops → 5-50%+ account moves in one trade

**Product Line 2: Combo LTB with FHP Focus**

Based on: market moves in momentum bursts of 3-5 days, 8-20% increments. Low threshold breakout = momentum + low volatility → breakouts.

Three long-side setup types:
1. **Continuation** — two legs (look for 2-leg + CV)
2. **Consolidation breakout** — 10+ days, needs volume + catalyst
3. **Bottom bounce** — specific conditions required

> "Coke, Diet Coke, Cherry Coke, Lemon Coke — it's all the same idea. I've taken the EP idea and modified it with 9M EP. It has completely changed the game."

### The Three Superchargers

> "When you add fee, oversizing, and 80/20 — you move your account by 5-50%+ in one trade."

| Supercharger | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Fee** (Fast Entry Early) | Gets you in before the move extends |
| **Oversizing** | 5,000 shares of MRNA, WYNN, BAC — conviction sizing |
| **80/20** | Take 80% profit fast, ride 20% for free |

### Stops: Closer Is Better

> "My belief system is: closer the stop, better it is."

Pradeep's stop evolution: low of day → half of day → half of day or above. Typical stops now: 40¢ to $1. On high-priced stocks, even $1 stops work because the risk/reward ratio is massive.

### Belief Drives Behavior

> "Unless you change your belief, your execution will not change. Profit is an outcome of following a good process."

Reading "Atomic Habits" won't change habits. Belief changes habits. You must believe in your Trade Factory, your process, and your setup-specific SA — otherwise you'll chase other ideas at the first drawdown.

### ANNA3 Mnemonic

> "Remember: the best airline in the world has three As. ANNA3. If there are 4, 5, 6, 10, 12 Anna's — even better. More analyst notes = stronger signal."

Not analyst upgrades/downgrades — **price target raises** specifically. The distinction matters.

## PM Meeting (2 PM)

### Boot Camp Preparation

Pradeep announced boot camp: members must know scans, terminology, and guide section content before attending. No time will be spent on basics during boot camp.

### Bounce Faded — Choppy Afternoon

> "It's a terrible action. I don't have any confidence now that some of these moves will work."

Morning gains evaporated. The bounce was buy-the-dip only — not the start of a new uptrend. Pradeep took profits aggressively on remaining positions and did not recommend holding anything overnight.

### Continuation > Consolidation

> "Continuation breakout is the low-hanging fruit in the market. Consolidation breakouts have terrible win rate."

| Setup Type | Win Rate | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Continuation | Highest | Few people talk about it, orderly pullback then breakout |
| Consolidation | Terrible | "Everybody's eyes on it, everybody is reading the same TA books" |

Key criteria for consolidation breakout: volume + catalyst. Without both, skip it.

### Weak Structure Shorts Proliferating

The anticipation scan showed a flood of weak structure shorts — Affirm, COIN, Lyft, W (Wayfair), SWKS, Inspire Medical, Beyond Meat, LRCX, Rocket, Cargo, Restoration Hardware. This proliferation confirms the market shift.

> "There were no sellers, only buyers. Now you can't find one buyer."

### Velocity-Based Exit

> "You peel when the velocity of the move goes down."

On JPMorgan short and Moderna long: watch PnL velocity. When $2,000 → $2,300 → $2,600 then stalls and starts fading to $2,500 without making a new high — click, go. Don't wait for a reversal. The 80/20 method operationalizes this: take 80% at the first velocity stall.

### Weekend Homework

> "This weekend is when I would spend time looking at consolidation setups for anticipation, because now in the second phase of this market is where you are likely to get some consolidation setups starting to work."

### Position Sizing Question

A member asked about risking only $100 on a high-priced stock that takes up 50% of a $20K account. Pradeep's answer: risk-based, not equity-based. If your stop is 20¢ on a high-priced stock, the position can be large without being reckless — that's how you grow a small account.

### Still Bullish — Market Monitor

> "Am I bullish? I am bullish still. The market monitor is bullish. If the primary indicator is bullish, do you remain bearish?"

Weak structure shorts work in any market condition — including a bullish one. The pullback is a phase within a bullish primary, not a bearish reversal.
