# Gap Up Kills Setups — Sell Into Strength or Get Nothing

First trading Monday of 2025. Market gapped up after Friday's bounce, then faded all day — classic "sell into strength" situation. Pradeep sold 80%+ of every position early (SOXL, CRDO, AVGO, Restoration Hardware) and ended up right. Major teaching session on the **setup framework**: continuation vs consolidation vs bottom bounce, why consolidation has the lowest win rate, and why you must buy at the **start of a swing** — not on day 3, 4, or 5.

### Start of a Swing — The #1 Concept

> "If you cannot understand start of a swing, you are going to have trouble."

- First or second leg = high probability
- Third, fourth, fifth leg = very low probability
- Look at 50% study: most moves complete in 2-3 legs
- **FFM (First Few Moves)** = 2-3 legs, that's the category to trade
- After 3 legs, probability drops dramatically
- "I understand. Lot of you watch cougar porn. But that cougar is only a pawn." — legs 4/5 look attractive but don't work

### Consolidation Breakouts = Lowest Win Rate

- They look beautiful on charts → that's the trap
- "Longer the base, bigger the move" — myth. Most consolidation breakouts fail.
- WGS example: long consolidation, no catalyst, no volume, S&P 500 addition is NOT an EP
- Planet Fitness: consolidation setup — "can it work? yes. but no volume, no catalyst"
- **For consolidation breakout to work**: need volume (tower of volume) AND catalyst
- A stock goes into consolidation FOR A REASON — no buying power, nobody excited
- When new catalyst comes → new buying power → then it moves

### Setup Classification Framework

| Setup Type | What to Look For | Win Rate |
|------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Continuation** | First leg 15%+, pullback 3-10 days, breakout | Best |
| **Consolidation** | Long base, needs volume + catalyst | Lowest |
| **Bottom Bounce** | Rare, from oversold levels | Moderate |
| **Healthcare/Tech** | Can start from any level | Depends on catalyst |
| **High-Priced (FHP)** | Start of swing from any level | Good with tight stops |

### Gap Up = Setup Killer

- "I don't like gaps up" — they kill setups
- Better to take profits on existing positions than force new entries

## PM Meeting — Process, Singles + Home Runs, Fade Day

### The Trade Factory Framework

- Two product lines: **home runs** (10-50%+ moves) and **singles** (0.5-1% moves)
- Why not just home runs? Need 10-25% stops = big drawdowns
- Singles finance the home runs — build buffer, then take big risks
- Singles use 2.5-5% stops; home runs need 10-25% stops
- "If the stop becomes more than 2.5-5%, it doesn't make sense for a single"
- Period: 3-5 days for swing trades

### Setup Types Within Factory

- EP 9M, Delayed EP, Buckets 0/1/2/250 → for finding candidates
- Momentum burst, anticipation, reversals → for hitting singles
- Continuation = primary focus (best win rate)
- Consolidation = happens all the time but **fails a lot**
- Bottom bounce = rare

## Key Takeaways

1. **Gap ups kill setups** — sell into strength on gap-up days, don't chase new entries
2. **Start of a swing is the #1 concept** — buy 1st/2nd leg, avoid 3rd/4th/5th leg
3. **Consolidation breakouts have the lowest win rate** — they look beautiful but fail without volume + catalyst
4. **FFM (First Few Moves) = 2-3 legs** — that's where the money is, not in extended moves
5. **Singles finance home runs** — small gains (0.5-1%) build buffer for 10-25% stop positions
6. **Sell into strength when you have it** — SOXL, CRDO, AVGO, RH all proved this right today
7. **Lululemon = start of swing, Shopify = NOT start of swing** — know the difference
8. **WGS = consolidation with no catalyst/volume** — S&P 500 addition is NOT an EP
9. **Silver/gold stocks in 9M = trap** — "almost like clockwork" they fail
10. **Palantir at $168B cap** — last year's big winners typically fade the next year
11. **AVGO date didn't work out** — bought on catalyst, sold 80%, rest stopped out, no regrets
12. **Restoration Hardware lollipop** — bought Friday 3PM, sold for $14, "I'll do that trade every day"
13. **Process > prediction** — run EP 9M, qualify, classify, execute, repeat
14. **Tinder dating analogy** — swing trading = Tinder (short-term, long-term also OK); position trading = eHarmony
15. **Day traders can make more money** — but Pradeep plays the slower swing game due to account structure and lifestyle
16. **Market analog**: marathon runner can't run another marathon right after — market likely chops before next big move
17. **Canadian companies curse** — RIM/BlackBerry, Shopify all faded after being market leaders
18. **CRDO has no "explosive character"** — good catalyst but doesn't move like it should, $11B cap issue
19. **ZZK breaking news = day trade only** — can't swing trade these opportunities
20. **80/20 rule applied everywhere** — sold 80% of positions into strength, kept 20% runners
