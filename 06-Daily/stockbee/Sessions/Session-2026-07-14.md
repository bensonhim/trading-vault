# IBM Earnings Shock, Small-Cap Short Squeezes, and the Chop Continues — Itchy Fingers Will Get Chopped

The market opened to an IBM earnings catastrophe (-25% gap down) that rippled through the entire software sector. Pradeep used the event as a live case study in catalyst analysis: the Goldman Sachs earnings (boosted by the SpaceX IPO) were a one-time event, CLSK's "6 billion deal" was actually $300M/year starting in 2027, and IBM's 25% gap was already priced in with no further downside likely. The day reinforced the standing message: breakouts don't work in a range-bound market, itchy fingers will get chopped, and the only actionable setups are EP 9M with catalysts relevant to current conditions, SIPs for day trading, and short-side plays on semis. The PM session was a rapid-fire scan of fading breakouts — HP, Google, Blackberry, Citibank — all looked good in the morning and died by afternoon.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Secular Bull Framework Revisited

Pradeep reiterated the long-term view from yesterday: secular bull market, AI trend has 3–5 more years, bubble signs not present (199 IPOs vs. 1,000+ at peaks).

> "When will you know when it will end? With bubble. Watch out for some traditional company becoming an AI company. Procter and Gamble launching a broom with AI built into it. ... IBM will change its name to IBM AI."

### IBM Earnings: Catalyst Analysis Case Study

IBM gapped down 25% on earnings. Pradeep analyzed whether it offered a short-side delayed-reaction EP.

> "25% gap — on the short side, I don't think it's tradeable here. ... I checked in the morning and it was a 7% gap — that was good. But 25% gap, the catalyst is priced in already."

> "IBM CEO is saying clients were distracted due to cybersecurity concerns. ... CEOs say a lot of things. We trade setups, not what CEOs say."

### CLSK: Misleading Headline Catalyst

CLSK announced a "$6 billion deal" that was actually $300M/year starting in 2027.

> "6,000,000,000 was so misleading because the actual was 2027, 300,000,000, and that's it. ... It's like I bought a house on a thirty-year mortgage of a million dollars. Doesn't mean it is worth a million dollars immediately."

### Goldman Sachs: One-Time IPO Boost

> "Why did Goldman Sachs have good earnings? Because of the IPO. Big IPO. SpaceX. Every day they are going to get that kind of IPO? No. So where are they going to find another SpaceX trillion-dollar IPO?"

### Shorting Small Caps: The New Popular Strategy

Pradeep noted the explosion of small-cap shorting as a strategy, driven by the new Market Wizards book.

> "All these people who have made 100 million, 500 million — four of the people, what is their primary strategy? Shorting small cap. ... There are so many communities which are formed for shorting small cap."

This creates cascading short squeezes when small caps start moving pre-market.

> "Once there is a halt or something happens, it just leads to cascading effect, and that is why you're getting some of these crazy moves intraday on these low price stocks."

### Insider Selling as a Red Flag

Pradeep highlighted insider selling data as a critical signal.

> "33 insiders have sold. ... When you have 30+ insider selling, why are the rats deserting the ship? ... The CFO is the guy who's in charge of the engine room, and he can see water coming into the ship."

> "CFO is selling and chief legal officer is selling. If you see that, especially if they do size, that means ... they know something."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Breakouts fade in range-bound markets** — Every morning breakout (HP, Google, Blackberry, Citibank, Apple) faded by afternoon. This is the pattern.
2. **IBM 25% gap was already priced in** — Large gap-downs on earnings are not tradeable as delayed EPs. The 7% pre-market gap was the trade; 25% is too late.
3. **CLSK's "$6B deal" was $300M/year starting 2027** — Always dig past headline numbers. Ask Grok/Gemini for the actual revenue translation.
4. **Goldman Sachs earnings were a one-time IPO boost** — The SpaceX IPO drove GS earnings. Not repeatable.
5. **Insider selling is a red flag** — 30+ insiders selling, especially CFO and chief legal officer, means "the rats are deserting the ship."
6. **Shorting small caps is the new popular strategy** — This creates cascading short squeezes intraday. Day traders can exploit this via SIPs.
7. **Catalyst analysis is a lifetime skill** — Headlines are misleading. Deep analysis (like Milan's approach using AI prompts) gives you an edge.
8. **Dilution risk must be checked** — Ask AI about dilution risk/shelf filings whenever you see a positive catalyst.
9. **Anticipation and reversal bullish don't work in range-bound markets** — Inherent to the setup. Momentum-based setups need momentum.
10. **Itchy fingers get chopped** — The choppy market is the real killer. More accounts are destroyed in range-bound periods than in bear markets.
11. **Hybrid trading is the solution** — Combine day trading (SIPs) with swing trading (EP 9M/DEP) to handle different market regimes.
12. **Don't buy pattern-based breakouts** — Only buy setups with a clear, relevant catalyst. Pattern without context = chop.
