# KLP Day — ADM CFO Fraud, Setup Understanding Crisis, and Sell Into Strength

Monday was a KLP day — the market gapped up, sucked everyone in, then faded. ADM (Archer Daniels Midland) was the only real SIPs — CFO fraud, front-page Bloomberg news, the textbook "bigger than life" catalyst. But the real theme was Pradeep's frustration with poor setup understanding: people buying MARA, Palantir, and Rivian as sugar babies with no setup, putting entire accounts into falling knives. The PM session became a trading problems session focused on the one methodology that separates pros from amateurs: find early, enter early, oversize, sell into strength (80/20).

---

- **20% study expanded rapidly** = room to run, but not a clean breakout environment
- **Two cities market** — tech doing well, small caps getting destroyed
- **Institutional selling every morning** in small caps, algo/day traders stepping in later
- **Bounce expected** but follow-through uncertain

### ADM: The Only SIPs Worth Watching

> "How did I find ADM? It was on the first page. Lot of time, I find my SIPs on the first page of a newspaper."

**ADM = Archer Daniels Midland**
- CFO placed on leave, earnings forecast cut = **fraud catalyst**
- Front page of Bloomberg — "bigger than life" catalyst
- Could be like Mobileye pattern (gap down → bounce) or could short
- Pradeep discussed it with Jay in the morning — Jay didn't have it
- 9M breakdown already on the chart (someone knew something)
- NYSE stock (OPG typically problematic)

### The Setup Understanding Crisis

> "If you don't understand setup, do not trade. Do not trade. You are going to get mugged."

The #1 problem, confirmed by boot camp exercise:
- People putting Ali (yesterday's news) and Celsius Holdings (British guy excited about UK expansion) as SIPs
- Celsius Holdings = "you're so late to the party you won't even get leftovers"
- Ali = yesterday's SIPs, not today's
- Poor setup understanding = competing against traders who know what they're doing

> "Even the gods will not be able to help you."

### SIPs Principle: Look for Bigger Than Life

- ADM stood out like an ad — front page news
- Same as Affirm — front page news on gap-up day
- SIPs = look for something that will attract either buyers OR sellers today
- If a day trader can make $5,000 on it, it's a SIPs

## 11 AM Meeting

Pradeep went long ADM thinking bounce:

### Buying Gaps Without Catalyst = Death

> "So many stocks are gapping up for no reason. Is your brain not thinking why are these gapping up?"

- Gap up **must have a catalyst** or you're selling into strength
- Affirm gapped up Friday WITH catalyst = bought it
- Affirm gapped up Monday with NO reason = didn't buy, sold Friday position
- Buying on second day of swing = KLP day behavior
- "If you're buying on the wrong day, do you deserve to make money?"

### Rivian Sugar Baby = "Drug Addicts in Prison"

### CELH and Rumble: Delayed Reaction Candidates

- **Celsius Holdings (CELH)**: watching for delayed reaction short (17M volume breakdown, decent volume)

### Market Monitor: Don't Front-Run

> "Market monitor during the day is not part of the methodology. Trying to front-run market monitor is where people lose a lot of money."

- Use market monitor at **end of day only**
- Breadth thrust at 10:30 showed only 29 stocks at 4% = not a real thrust

> "Three ways to make money: be first, be smart, or cheat."

| **Oversize** | Put large size into the right trade | Put large size into wrong setup |
| **Sell into strength** | 80/20 — peel at round numbers, keep runner | Hold and hope, let profit turn to loss |
| **Find SIPs daily** | Catalyst > everything | Ignore news, only look at charts |
