# Session — Wednesday, June 10, 2026

**Overview**: After a historic 33% market rally with no sideways consolidation, the market has entered a choppy transition — "London weather" — where breakouts fail and only catalyst-driven setups like EP 9M and DEP survive. Pradeep hammers home: **EP 9M is the only viable setup right now**, and without setup clarity, no amount of discipline saves you.

Even during COVID helicopter money, the market didn't do this. The result: people are **conditioned to buy pullbacks** and do trend following, exactly the wrong instincts for a choppy transition.

- Old leaders (crypto, gold, silver) are not coming back.
- New leaders are emerging — healthcare, biotechnology — visible on the 20% Study.
- Leslie's Pool Company ([[LIII?]]) moved 142% — new things keep appearing.
- The cycle is always the same: new things come in, old leaders die.

> "There is always something new which keeps coming in, and people still get attracted to the old leaders while a new set of leaders continue to emerge."

### Setup Focus: EP 9M and DEP

Pradeep's exclusive focus for this market:

1. **EP 9M** — stocks with volume in the top 1% being bought by institutions
2. **DEP (Delayed Reaction EP)** — post-catalyst pullback with tight consolidation
3. **Sugar Babies** — lower-conviction, wider stop setups

> "EP nine million, DEP, sugar babies — that's my focus. Reversals and charts."

**Why EP 9M works in choppy markets**: These setups work independent of the market because the catalyst gives them a reason to move. They have institutional backing (volume in top 1%) and a clear catalyst.

> "The reason to trade DEP and EP 9M is what? These setups work independent of the market. They have something which can make them work even when the market is wildest and momentum worst."

### EP 9M Deep Dive — Clarity on What Qualifies

The PM session contains Pradeep's most emphatic clarification of what does and does not qualify as a DEP:

**DEP Essential Criteria**:
1. **Institutional quality** — owned by 1,000+ funds
2. **Real catalyst** within the last month
3. **First setup** after that catalyst
4. **Not some flimsy stock** — story stocks with 8 funds don't qualify

> "The DEP, institutional quality. Not some flimsy stock. That's the essential criteria."

**What is NOT a DEP** (correcting a member calling [[RXT]] a DEP):

[[RXT]] at $5.03 is a **story stock** that was on the verge of bankruptcy, then signed an AMD contract. It's not institutional quality (not owned by 1,000+ funds). It's an EP 9M / Sugar Baby — NOT a DEP.

> "He's like telling me that after your kid is three and a half months old, your wife is pregnant. That is not DEP. There is no DEP. This is a breakout. EP 9M. Start of a swing."

**Low-price stock breakout rule**: At the $5 price point, a 20% breakout is the norm. A 4% breakout on a $5 stock won't work. Check the 20% Study and 50% Study to confirm.

> "If you are trading a $5 stock, you want to see a 20% breakout. A 4% breakout on a $5 stock is not going to work."

## Key Takeaways

1. **EP 9M is the only setup for this market**: Regular breakouts have the same probability as "me getting a six pack in the next six months." Only catalyst-driven EP 9M and DEP have a chance in choppy conditions.
2. **DEP requires institutional quality**: Owned by 1,000+ funds, real catalyst within one month, first setup after catalyst. Story stocks like [[RXT]] at $5 are NOT DEP — they're EP 9M / Sugar Baby.
3. **Confirmation costs money**: Enter DEP early based on pre-decided catalyst + tightness. Waiting for confirmation means wider stops and lower reward.
4. **Write down your guide daily**: Pradeep cites Norwegian neuroscientist Audrey van der Meer's research showing handwriting creates brain connectivity patterns that typing cannot.
5. **Low-price stocks need 20%+ breakouts**: At $5 price points, 20% is the norm for successful breakouts. Use the 20% Study to confirm.
6. **Three tries max for DEP**: "If there is a genuine catalyst and I'm convinced, I give it three tries. Three times she says no, then you move on."
7. **Theme is the most powerful catalyst**: Over-extension across AI/semiconductor names creates a theme-level short opportunity — more powerful than any single stock's catalyst.
8. **Perspective management**: See the totality of the setup family (EP → EP 9M → DEP → Sugar Babies → Momentum Burst), not just one piece. Without the total picture, you're a blind man touching one part of the elephant.
