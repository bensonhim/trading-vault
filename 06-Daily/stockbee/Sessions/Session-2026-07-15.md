# 20% Study Says Sleep — Funds Not Buying, Funds Not Selling, and the Chop Grinds On

July 15 was the shortest session of the week — Pradeep had seen enough. The 20% study readings told the whole story: both bullish and bearish readings were middling (25 and 15), meaning funds were neither aggressively buying nor aggressively selling. The market was asleep, digesting the 18% move from two months ago. Breakouts continued to fade, the PM session found nothing actionable in any scan, and Pradeep signed off in five minutes to go to his pool. The core message: this choppy range will persist until the next earnings season (September/October), and the only way to survive it is to stop buying pattern-based breakouts and either develop SIPs/day-trading skills or wait for catalyst-specific EP 9M setups.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### The 20% Study Diagnosis

Pradeep opened with a clear read on the 20% study:

> "These readings are around 25. These readings are around 15. This is supposed to work in the reverse manner. ... It means the funds are not aggressively buying, and the funds are not aggressively selling. When do you make money? You make money when the funds are aggressively buying."

The market made an 18% move in two months and has gone into sleep mode. The readings confirm: no aggressive buying, no aggressive selling — just sideways churn.

### Breakouts Are Inherently a Momentum Strategy

> "Our strategy is basically based on momentum, and momentum works or breakouts work in a momentum market — that is inherent to the setup. ... It is inherent to the setup idea that you trade breakouts when there is a lot of buying coming into the market."

> "Now there is no buying, there is no selling. As a result, you're going to get these kind of situations where [breakouts stall]."

### PENG: A SIP, Not an EP

Members were excited about PENG; Pradeep clarified it was a SIP (Stock in Play), not an EP.

> "Everybody was excited. I was in Oslo that day when PENG showed up. ... It was just a SIPs according to me."

The prediction: PENG would slowly grind down and stall, and the people who bought it would say "breakouts don't work" and move to the next guru.

### What Works in This Market

| Setup | Works? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Pattern-based breakouts | ❌ | No momentum/follow-through |
| EP 9M with clear catalyst | ✅ | Catalyst overcomes market weakness |
| DEP with catalyst | ✅ | Low-risk entry on fund-quality names |
| SIPs for day trading | ✅ | Higher volatility favors day traders |
| Earnings breakouts | ✅ | If genuinely neglected stock |
| Anticipation | ❌ | Inherent to momentum setups; no momentum here |
| Reversal bullish | ❌ | Needs popularity + clean pattern; nothing qualifies |

### Developing SIPs Expertise

> "Just because the market is like this and you suddenly start doing SIPs without having expertise in doing SIPs, more likely you'll be just [chopped]. ... You are in a European country, and breakdancing is not what they appreciate. So suddenly you decide to become a ballroom dancer — is it going to work out? No."

> "All solutions to life should be strategic, not tactical."

## Key Takeaways

1. **20% study says sleep** — Readings at 25/15 mean funds are neither buying nor selling aggressively. No momentum = no breakouts.
2. **Breakouts are inherently a momentum strategy** — They only work when funds are aggressively buying. This is not a market condition problem; it is inherent to the setup.
3. **The chop will persist until next earnings season** — September/October is the likely resolution point. 8–9% pullback may precede a new leg.
4. **PENG was a SIPs, not an EP** — Excitement about PENG was misplaced. It will slowly fade and the buyers will blame breakouts.
5. **Only three setups are actionable here** — EP 9M with relevant catalyst, DEP with catalyst, SIPs for day trading. Everything else = chop.
6. **Don't suddenly become a day trader** — SIPs expertise takes time to develop. "Suddenly deciding to ballroom dance in a breakdancing country" doesn't work.
7. **AHR's 36% gap = priced in** — Plus dilution risk. Risk/reward not favorable.
8. **Shorts slightly more attractive** — Semis breakdown, Lemonade/Dell fading. But even shorts lack follow-through.
9. **Three anticipation setups only** — Faith Therapeutics, PIN, PJN. That's it for the entire scan.
10. **SA took three years to learn** — Even with a setup in hand, it took Pradeep three years to understand situational awareness was the real game.
11. **Strategic, not tactical** — Build skills for the long term. Don't react to each day's market with a new strategy.
12. **Pool > Trading** — When there's nothing to do, go to the pool.
