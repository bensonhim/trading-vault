# Institutions Selling, Retail Bag-Holding, and Organize Like Crazy

> "If you see 22 stocks making new lows per second, who's doing this? Have you ever seen a retail investor come early in the morning and say 'I'm going to sell Palantir today'? This is institutions. If you know institutions are selling, why put your neck there?"

Friday. Broad-based selling. 1,487 stocks in bearish combo vs 127 bullish. The only buyers: **Chinese stocks** ([[BABA]], [[PDD]], [[FUTU]], [[LI]]). Everything else getting hammered. Pradeep's message: **organize like crazy** (OLC), understand **start of a swing** or find another strategy, and when institutions sell — get out of the way. The easy trades: [[XLF]] short, [[FAS]] long (from yesterday), [[DIA]] short, [[TQQ]] short.

## AM Meeting — Cautious, Corrections Happening Under the Surface

### Market: Second Selling Day, Slow Deterioration Near Top

- T2108 dropped from 49.79 to 47.33 — weakness
- Market monitor: second red day, all primary indicators red
- S&P pulled back from ATH, Dow had downside range expansion
- > "Stocks take escalator up and elevator down. 31% move wiped out in two days"
- Slow deterioration near the top — not dramatic, but visible in 15% study
- Many stocks making 15%+ moves, but most from gap-ups that then gave up gains
- Earnings breakouts sitting there, not getting rewarded

### Must-Watch: Very Slim

- IONQ — quantum computing sector, hot theme, high short interest
- MicroStrategy — Bitcoin rallying
- HOOD, HIMS, RDDT — delayed reactions (but RDDT/HOOD had negative reactions, may be shorts)
- DOCS, AVGO, VST — only ones with real catalysts

### 20% Study: Selling Below the Surface

- 41 bullish vs 34 bearish — both sides active, unusual
- > "When T2108 goes below 20, that's a very good time to enter swings. When it goes high, that's when swings start having trouble"
- A lot of selling happening that you don't notice because there are still stocks making moves
- Corrections happening slowly — months of gains wiped in days

### Delayed Reaction: Requires Catalyst and Urgency

- > "Delayed reaction should be something which is setting up after the catalyst, very orderly"
- RDDT and HOOD had negative reactions — those are short setups, not delayed reaction longs
- HIMS also had negative volume reaction

### Key Lesson: Organize Like Crazy (OLC)

- > "Organize like crazy. Everything should be organized"
- Know position size, stop, exit before you ever buy
- Have all information sources set up: Finviz, Trade Ideas, news, earnings, gappers
- Know which stocks are in DJ30, IBD 250, bucket 0/1/2, IPO year
- > "If you are scrambling in the morning looking for short interest, that means you don't have a process"
- FOMO = lack of setup. If your setup says buy on day 1, how can you have FOMO?
- > "Dig 5 feet → 1% survival. Dig 500 feet → 10%. Dig 5,000 feet → 50%. Dig 50,000 feet → 90%"
- Start of a swing: "If you're serious, in 48 hours you can fix that problem"

### Friday Caution + Weekend Risk

- Friday → Monday unknown (tariffs, Elon, etc.)
- > "Think three days ahead"
- Peel positions, don't add risk before weekend

### Momentum Burst: The Lesson That Must Click

- Pradeep frustrated with member not understanding momentum burst
- > "Momentum burst is an event. The stock closes near the high. Range expansion. 9M breakout. Sugar baby. What is the problem?"
- If stock gaps up then gives up the gap = NOT a momentum burst
- If stock doesn't close near high = NOT a start of a swing
- > "If you cannot understand start of a swing and momentum burst, you should not trade setups based on that. Find something else"

### Walmart Is the Economy

- Walmart earnings miss: "significant slowdown in last few weeks"
- People are out of money — 8%+ car loan rates, 7% mortgage rates
- > "Walmart is the economy"
- Walmart = canary in the coal mine, along with Costco breaking down days earlier
- Inflation back, Fed not cutting, Trump adding inflation pressure

### Not "Get Me Out at Any Cost" Selling

- Slow, grinding institutional selling — reducing exposure, not panicking
- 4% breakout count only 171 (should be 500-600 on real capitulation day)
- > "This is institutions reducing their exposure. Not get me out completely"
- But still broad-based: 79% of stocks making new lows

### Good Earnings Not Getting Rewarded = The Party Is Over

- Airbnb, Robinhood, Expedia, Shopify, UPST — all had good earnings, all gave up gains
- > "The party is over for the time being"
- Retail investors are the bag holders now
- Institutions finished distributing to retail

### I Want a Correction

- > "I want a correction. If this develops into a good correction, it will be very good"
- Choppy range-bound = hard to trade. Real correction = clear setups
- T2108 at 38 — needs to go to single digits for a real buying opportunity

### Low Risk Shorts on Continuation Setups

## Key Takeaways

1. **Institutions are selling** — 22 stocks/second making new lows, not retail
2. **Organize like crazy (OLC)** — have all tools, stops, sizes, sources ready before market opens
3. **Chinese stocks only bright spot** — BABA, PDD, Futu, Li; everything else selling
4. **Good earnings not getting rewarded** = distribution complete, retail bag-holding
5. **Walmart is the economy** — significant slowdown, people squeezed by rates
6. **Momentum burst MUST close near high** — if it gaps and fades, it's not a momentum burst
7. **FOMO = lack of setup** — if your setup is defined, how can you have FOMO?
8. **Start of a swing or find another strategy** — this concept is non-negotiable
9. **Low volume selloff, not capitulation** — institutions reducing exposure, not panicking
10. **XLF short / FAS long / DIA short** — the easy sector ETF trades for broad-based selling
11. **I want a correction** — real correction creates better trading conditions than choppy range
