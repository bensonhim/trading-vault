**Markets retreated from Friday's rally — T2108 dropped to 59.41, 20% study at 1:1 (neutral). Buying early still not working.** Pradeep's core message: **"Small edges can be magnified into big edges by sizing, entry tactics, and exit tactics."** **Chewy (CHWY)** was the trade of the day — bought yesterday at $3.58 as reversal bullish, sold 80% at $40.51, keeping 20% free trade. **Viking Therapeutics** was #1 idea — short with catalyst (oral drug failure), but stopped out at breakeven. **SATS/EcoStar** dismissed — just debt restructuring, not game-changing. **Sugar baby** concept introduced: stocks with no earnings but strong story = high volatility, small position, big potential. **Hims & Hers (HIMS)** identified as sugar baby. **Liquidia (LQDA)** best candidate from member presentation — FDA approval catalyst, clean X-ray. **Context is everything** — after 1300+ day, zero follow-through. **Machiavelli lesson**: be fox and lion — suspicious of traps, bold on life-changing setups. **AI for stock research** — Gemini best for catalyst research, simple prompts like "bullish and bearish case on [ticker]." **Deep dive on 20% study** — do 300 stocks to learn what catalysts drive 50%+ moves.

### Small Edge Magnification — Core Lecture

> "The edge may be small. While many people are looking for large edges, they're continually looking for large edges. Large edges are rare."

> "Can you find every day a stock which is going to go up 100%? Small edges can be magnified into big edges by sizing, by entry tactics, by doing things."

> "All these traders, they use a small edge and magnify the small edge by sizing, or they magnify a small edge by doing entry tactics or exit tactics."

> "We had Palantir and Chewy, and I selected Chewy and I didn't select Palantir. Context of a setup matters."

### Viking Therapeutics — #1 Idea

> "Viking Therapeutics was my number one idea today. Because I saw the Eli Lilly news. There is a potential reason for Viking Therapeutics to go down. Disappointing clinical trial results for oral but has other drugs in injectable."

> "Viking Therapeutics, my stop is breakeven. So I'm getting stopped out. Fine. That is perfectly okay with me. Because I was taking a chance that in case it works. If it doesn't work, no money lost."

### Sugar Baby Concept

> "Hims & Hers is a sugar baby. When a sugar baby calls, you take some position. You might take a very small position because she will be very volatile. But that small position can make a big move."

> "All sugar babies, because for whatever reason retail traders are attracted to them, they tend to have a lot more volatility."

> "Rules for trading sugar baby: if sugar baby calls, you take some position. Not full position, small position. That's how you make big money."

### Context Is Everything — After 1300+ Day, Nothing

> "After that big breakout on Friday, the market is just sitting there. There is absolutely no buyers."

> "Context context context. In everything in life, context is everything."

> "If the context is not right, you should not be really aggressive."

> "Tomorrow is Nvidia earnings. Nvidia earnings is a binary event. If investors get disappointed, all the AI tower which has been built will start crumbling."

### Be First, Be Smarter, or Cheat — Margin Call Movie Quote

> "What have I told you since the first day you stepped into my office? There are three ways to make a living in this business. Be first, be smarter, or cheat. I don't cheat. And although I like to think we have some pretty smart people in this building, it sure is a hell of a lot easier to just be first."

> "If you can reflect on that clip and understand it, you will not buy a setup on a second day or third day, or you will really understand the logic of buying at the right time."

### Machiavelli — Fox and Lion

> "But at the same time, if you see something which is such a life-changing setup, then you should be willing to go boldly and take a large position."
