# Death by a Thousand Cuts, Style Drift Warning, Breakouts Look Good Even in Bad Markets

> "A breakout looks exactly the same in a bad market as it does in a good market. The difference is: in a bad market, institutional selling kicks in, buyers disappear, and your breakout fades. This is why professional traders stay out of volatile markets."

Friday confirmed the choppy pattern: intraday rallies followed by fades, institutional sell programs, no follow-through. T2108 dropped to 22.74. Pradeep's core teaching this session: **style drift is the biggest danger** — don't become a day trader just because the market is choppy. FuboTV position remains (~1% of account). The "must watch" list concept is introduced: track high-volume stocks for future 20%+ opportunities. NBIX, Meta, RH, Lulu — all looked like perfect breakouts, all failed. Market conditions are everything.

### Death by a Thousand Cuts: The Core Teaching

> "Breakouts look exactly the same in a good market and a bad market. But when institutional selling starts, buyers disappear. Your stop gets hit over and over. This is death by a thousand cuts."

- When you buy 5-10 breakouts and they all fade, you lose 25% of your account
- Professional traders stay out of volatile markets
- The same setup works differently in bull vs bear conditions
- Lululemon, Restoration Hardware — perfect breakouts that failed because market conditions changed

### Style Drift: The Greatest Danger

> "Once you become a day trader, you cannot go back to swing trading. You start taking profits in 15-20 minutes and can't hold for 3-5 days."

- 98% of traders cannot successfully switch between day trading and swing trading
- Don't switch styles just because the market is choppy
- People who become good at shorting during bear markets get wiped out when the market turns
- "Remain loyal to your style and accept that there are periods when you cannot make money"
- Drawdown is the worst feeling — kills confidence, causes compounding mistakes

## 2 PM Meeting — Breakouts Fail, Style Drift Revisited, Must Watch List Deep Dive

### The Same Breakout Looks Good in Any Market

> "Is NBIX a good breakout? Answer is yes. But the same breakout in a different market condition will go up for 3-5 days because there's buying coming in. Here, there's no buying."

- NBIX: perfect breakout, but market conditions will kill it
- Meta: 9M breakout, third day — faded
- Reddit: "perfect, market leader, RS line making new highs" — then what happened?
- RH (Restoration Hardware): "perfect breakout" — failed
- Lulu: "perfect breakout" — failed
- "I can give you 100 examples like this in the last 5 days"

### Institutional Smart Money Fades Your Breakouts

> "There are people smarter than us in this business. They know you're going to buy this breakout. What's their strategy? To short it. They fade that breakout."

- Breakouts work when money flows into the market continuously
- November was great — money was flowing in
- Since early December, the market changed — selling dominates
- DIA shows clear institutional selling in large stocks

- "I would not be surprised if this stock goes up more than 100%"
- Cap $1.7B → could become $10B
- Playing for **magnitude, not duration**
