# Weak Bounce, LLY SIPs Missed, Focus on Biotech/Tech Sub-$1B

> "Are breakouts likely to work? The question every morning. If the answer is no, what is the probability of an anticipation setup working? 5%. SIPs is the name of the game — it's evergreen."

Day 5 of decline. Market tried to bounce but couldn't convince — "ideal setup for a bounce, but no buyers." T2108 ticked up to 21.99 from 20.41. Eli Lilly earnings miss created the **best SIPs opportunity of the week** and most members missed it because they don't look at short-side SIPs. Pradeep adds LLY to must-watch list for potential $100-200 move. Small cap session announced for 5:30 PM.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### SA: Cautiously Bullish for Short-Term Bounce

- 300+ selling day (3rd consecutive), primary and secondary indicators red
- T2108: 21.99 (up from 20.41 — slight improvement)
- 20% study: bearish 4:1 ratio, but at 20% it's near bounce territory
- 20% study at 20 = both extreme bearish AND extreme bullish readings signal bounces
- "Below 20 is bullish. Bearish approaching 100 is bullish. Extreme readings = bounce"

### Logical Spot for a Bounce — But No Guarantee

- Market down 4 days = mean reversion traders will try their luck
- If it doesn't bounce from here, "that tells you the market is really, really weak"
- Bounce would be short-term (1-3 days), not a trend change
- "Our tendency is to become more bearish after 4 days — that's where you get squeezed"

### The Only Game in Town: Biotech and Tech

> "If you want to simplify your trading, get rid of all the stocks. Focus on biotech and technology stocks. That's all there is."

- 20% study consistently shows biotech and tech — "day in, weeks, months, it doesn't change"
- For small accounts: focus on stocks **below $1B** market cap
- Below $10B is where moves happen; below $1B is even better
- "It's not the size that matters — it's the motion in the ocean you create with the size"

### Bottom Bounce and Reversal Bullish

- Bottom bounce and reversal bullish = possible setup ideas when market turns
- Beaten-down stocks tend to bounce first, NOT the ones that held up best
- "The stocks which held up best might start rallying after 4-6 weeks, by which time the market has already bounced 8-10%"

### Small Cap Session Announced

- Evening session at 5:30 PM on small caps
- Triggered by conversations with day traders in Las Vegas
- Pradeep worked on small cap setups since returning from Vegas
- Focus: how to profit from small caps without blowing up your account

## 2 PM Meeting — Tiny Positions, SIPs Is the Game, Day Trading Opportunity

- Took small position in HOOD — catalyst: projections look good
- Negative: $35.5B market cap — "can it become a $100B company? Never say never, but very difficult mountain to climb"
- "Can it make a 10% move? Yes." — but limited upside potential
- Position sized accordingly: very tiny

### PDYN: Best Delayed Reaction Bullish

- PDYN looks like the best delayed reaction setup
- "Two bouts of big buying" — clear institutional interest
- Low-priced stock, better potential than high-cap names

### CoreMedics: Best Looking Setup

- CoreMedics setting up very nicely
- "Focus on stocks like this — real earnings, not just stories"
- In choppy markets, stocks with real earnings are more likely to work

### Consolidation Breakouts Failing Without Catalyst

- Planet Fitness, WGS, multiple IBD-style breakouts all failing
- "Consolidation breakouts work when there's a very good bull market AND a catalyst"
- Without catalyst, they get hammered back into the range
- "Longer the base, better it is" — that's only true in bull markets

### The Sell-Short-Yesterday's-Breakout Pattern

> "In a bearish market, your easy money is to short something which had a breakout yesterday."

- CEG: someone shorted it and made big money
- HOOD: gapped up, likely to get hammered tomorrow if market opens weak
- "Instead of becoming Robin Hood, it will become neighborhood hood"
- Algorithmic traders fade breakouts systematically — that's how they make money

### LLY SIPs: Keep Watching for 4-5 Days

- LLY: not just a one-day move, potential for follow-through
- Earnings miss creates multi-day opportunity
- Scam (LLY rallied into catalyst 49%, now recovering last 2 days) — but watch list needed
- "Watch list stocks need to be monitored for 2-3 days, then it will only work"

### Selling Not Exhausting — Slow and Dangerous

- "There's not enough selling. The selling is not getting exhausted, so bounces don't stick."
- Slow selling is more dangerous than fast selling
- "Just get over with the selling and then let us go back to our business"

### Style Drift Warning — Day Trading Opportunity

> "This might be your opportunity to become a day trader. High volatility environment. But don't become a day trader permanently."

- Day trading is appropriate for this market
- High volatility = good for day trading
- But swing traders should stay disciplined — tiny positions only
- "If I take a position, the sizes are so small they won't even buy a bracelet for my sugar babies"
