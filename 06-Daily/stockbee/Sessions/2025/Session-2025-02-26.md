# Slow Persistent Selling Persists, Setup Understanding Is Everything, and Formula Beats Intellect

> "Slow selling tends to persist. Panicky sudden selling tends to mean revert. This slow selling makes me think there is more to come. Many of these stocks established the first leg of the move, especially dogs and Fugazi — they will slowly keep going down."

Wednesday. T2108 bounced to 39.22. 20% study near 20 on bullish (potential bounce). VIX above 20 then pulled back. The bounce Pradeep expected arrived — and faded within hours. Selling came right back. This is **slow, persistent, deliberate institutional selling** — not panic. The bounce was a dead cat bounce. Setup understanding was the theme of the day: **range expansion ≠ setup**. You must qualify every setup with context. Pradeep shorted [[Apple]] with a $3 stop on a $240 stock — formula over intellect.

## AM Meeting — Short-Term Bounce Likely, Slow Selling Persists, Qualify Your Setups

### Market: Fifth Day, Bounce Expected But Weak

- T2108 increased from 37.29 to **39.22** — bounce signal
- 20% study: bullish approaching 20, bearish exceeding 100 for second day
- Market monitor: third consecutive red day, primary and secondary still red
- VIX traded above 20 then pulled back
- S&P fell to its lowest close of the year
- Crypto sold off, Bitcoin below 90K
- > "I'm expecting a short term bounce, but will it be tradable? I think this bounce is going to be a weak bounce, and that might attract more selling"

### Slow Selling Persists, Panicky Selling Mean Reverts

- > "Slow persistent selling tends to persist. Panicky sudden selling tends to mean revert"
- This is slow, systematic selling — drip, drip, drip
- Institutions come every morning, sell, stop, sell again
- Not capitulation, not panic — deliberate exposure reduction
- > "When institutions decide to get rid of their 100M shares, they come every day and keep selling"

### Market Character Has Changed Dramatically

- > "Market character has changed dramatically in the last one week"
- Not a single earnings breakout has worked — all gave up gains and went negative
- Not even orderly pullbacks — stocks just reversing and going down
- Need to see real buying (800-900+ breadth day) to confirm bullish turn
- > "You need to see some buying coming in which changes this narrative"

### Reversal Bullish: Entry Timing Is Critical

- Reversal bullish candidates from yesterday (Tapestry, Vistra, Amazon, Palo Alto Networks) all gapped up premarket
- > "The setup was yesterday. There is no setup now"
- Entry for reversal bullish = **end of day, in anticipation**
- If you wait for confirmation (gap up next morning), the trade is over
- > "The opportunity was only to enter at end of day. Now there is no setup"

### Setup Understanding: Range Expansion ≠ Setup

- EP 9M shows MULN with range expansion — but it's a **dog** with no catalyst
- > "It is a range expansion, but it is NOT a setup"
- You must qualify: is it a cat, dog, liquid lava? Is there a catalyst?
- > "Every setup has specific conditions attached to it"
- Momentum burst ≠ setup by itself. Must qualify with context (continuation, consolidation, FSP, etc.)
- > "If you trade random momentum burst, what is likely to happen?"

### Three-Legged Stool Reiterated (Again)

- Setup + Situational Awareness + Process — all must be equal
- > "My essay today: swing setups to bullish side are not likely to work"
- People intellectually understand SA but emotionally want to trade — the chocolate problem
- > "I know I shouldn't eat sweets. But if I see chocolate, I eat five"

### Stocks in Play / Watch

- ENVB, SYTA — dog stocks, lottery tickets only
- Workday — earnings winner, not a clean setup
- SMCI — premarket reaction unconvincing
- Oklo — possible short-term bounce
- AppLovin — short report, but the people who put it out already shorted days ago; they cover, you hold the bag

## 10 AM Meeting — Institutions Selling Early, Don't Short 3rd Day Down, Trade Factory Deep Dive

### Trade Factory: Two Distinct Strategies

| Strategy | Timeframe | Character |
|----------|-----------|-----------|
| **EP 9M / Sugar Babies** | 3-5 days | Island dream — buy, set stop, let it run |
| **SIPs** | Day trade | Grind — every day find something, babysit, get in and out quickly |

- Combination of both is ideal — SIPs grind builds capital for EP home runs
- Anticipation is a **minor product** — don't depend on it
- Reversal setups are **support actors, not main products**

### Institutions Selling Early Again

- > "Not even one buyer. Straight down. This is institutions selling"
- Netflix, Reddit, PLTR — all zero buyers, straight down
- Amazon, Meta — institutions unloading
- > "They come every day and keep selling. Overwhelming everything"
- The selling is **concentrated** in tech/FANG/speculative, not broad-based
- Pharma, defense, consumer staples holding up — money rotating, not leaving

### Don't Short on the Third Day Down

- > "On a third day when the indexes are down, you don't put a short"
- Mean reversion models will kick in — vicious counter-trend bounces
- > "The game is patience here now"
- Wait for bounce, then short again

### 20% Study: Only 11 Stocks Above $10B Market Cap

- > "Out of these 85 stocks, only 11 had capitalization above 10B"
- Lower capitalization = advantage for 20% moves
- Most 20% moves last only 3-5 days then fizzle

### Shorts: Too Late, Wait for Bounce

- Most shorts are already 3-4 days into their move
- > "Shorting late into the move is not a very good idea"
- TQQ straight down with no counter-trend bounce — rare
- Probability of bounce increases after 3 days down
