# New Year Caution: Low-Priced Stocks Dominate as Market Pulls Back

> "Hit singles till at least the first 20%. The worst thing you can do is get into a drawdown at the start of the year and spend months recovering."

First trading day of 2025. Market in pullback mode — slow breadth deterioration, low-priced stocks dominating action, no real selling pressure but no buying urgency either. Pradeep is deliberate and cautious: small size, tight stops, sell into strength. Focus shifts to "cougars" (60+ price, institutional quality) over sugar babies. Only continuation setups working; consolidation breakouts warned against repeatedly.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### SA: Short-Term Cautious, Choppy Market

- Market monitor: 7th Green Day on light volume, primary indicators unchanged, secondary indicators red for 9th consecutive day
- T2108 rose 18.66 → 19.47, 20% study leans bullish despite high-priced stock selling
- Major indices posted losses on New Year's Eve — Nasdaq worst, led by tech
- Crypto stocks retreating, Bitcoin below $100K
- Plan: slow, deliberate risk-taking; hit singles until 20% account growth

### Low-Priced Stocks Dominating Action

- Half of stocks showing 4%+ breakouts are priced below $3
- "When you see really low-priced stocks dominating the action, that typically is a sign near the end of a market or before a correction"
- Dow Jones already corrected ~6%, SPY similar, Nasdaq ~5%, tech only ~3.7%

### 2025 Focus: Cougars Over Sugar Babies

- Shifting from sugar babies/IPOs to "cougars" — stocks priced 60+, institutional quality
- "Size matters. It all boils down to having size."
- Find explosive moves in bucket 0/bucket 1 (39%+ sales growth)
- FFO (find free money) with lowest risk remains primary objective
- Focus areas: Magna 53, Cap 10×10, Niagara 3×2.5

### Consolidation Breakout Warning

> "If your setup understanding is so low, how are you going to be profitable?"

- Question all assumptions — most "held up well" stocks don't dominate 50%+ studies
- Explosive moves start from bottoms, not from stocks "holding up well"
- Consolidation breakouts have very high failure rate without volume + catalyst

## 11 AM Meeting — Setup Clarity, Zim/Marvel Entries, Consolidation Breakout Deep Dive

### Zim Integrated: Continuation Setup (Not Consolidation Breakout)

- Entered Zim and Marvel early as continuation breakouts
- Zim: first leg 17%, then pullback — this is continuation, NOT consolidation
- Pradeep visibly frustrated when members confused the two setups
- "If you are asking this question at 10:00, for your own safety's sake, don't trade"

### Consolidation Breakout: Deep Dive Challenge

> "Challenge anybody who's a fan of consolidation breakout — do a deep dive for 3-6 months and see what happens after breakout. Most go back into range."

- Consolidation breakouts only work at the start of a big bull market or wildly bullish conditions
- They need both volume AND catalyst — without catalyst, they're traps
- Easy to spot, hard to profit from

### Key Positions

- **Marvel (MRVL)**: delayed reaction, clear earnings catalyst — Pradeep's favorite entry of the day
- **Zim (ZIM)**: continuation breakout, entered early, still holding
- **Meta (META)**: large-cap consolidation breakout — but no volume = caution

### "Question All Assumptions"

> "The most important skill in this business is to question all assumptions."

- Stocks that "hold up well" don't dominate 50%+ studies — bottom bouncers do
- Don't believe what books say about consolidation breakouts without verifying
