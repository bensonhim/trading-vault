## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Market Regime: Cautiously Bullish with Deteriorating Margins

> "My essay this morning is cautiously bullish as the market consolidates and absorbs unfolding tariff news. Select breakouts working."

Market monitor had a **400+ selling day** on the 4% study. T2108 pulled back from 69.28 to 62.78. The 20% study still leaned bullish at 5:1, but margins were deteriorating.

### Tariff Theater: Erratic Intraday Volatility

New reciprocal tariffs announced for August 1 across select countries. Pradeep's take:

> "It's erratic. It comes in suddenly at 10:00 when the market is going up... your stocks get hit. And that is the problem."

> "The intraday volatility will kill you if you have a bigger position size."

The algos read news and trade instantly — retail is not in that game. Retail only knows one thing: **BTFD**.

> "What is the one thing which retail knows? Buy the fuck dip, BTFD, right? BTFD and yellow are the only two things they know."

### The Framework Reminder

Pradeep recited the StockBee framework:

> "Controlled risk taking, sellers, SOS. Buy either a start of a swing or send an SOS message to the suicide hotline. Find and enter early. Sell and restraint. Think three days ahead. Look for the first leg setups or second leg setups. Don't buy two-leg, later setups."

### Key Takeaways (AM)

1. **400+ selling day** — T2108 dropped significantly, margins deteriorating
2. **Tariff volatility is algos-first** — retail can't compete with news-driven intraday swings
3. **BTFD is the only retail play** — don't be that trader
4. **Controlled risk, SOS entries** — framework still applies

> "Good news. And the stock cannot hold its gains."

A pattern of breakout, stop out, breakout, stop out — four dates, four failures.
