# Start of a Swing or Get Out — Breakouts Working, Manage Profits, Extended Stocks Failing

**Major shift in situational awareness: "Are breakouts likely to work? YES."** First time in over a week Pradeep says breakouts are working. TNA 9M breakout followed through, small caps rotating. But — **extended stocks near highs are NOT following through.** Yesterday's breakouts (Goldman Sachs, Meta, etc.) just sitting there. Near-high stocks are under pressure. The action is in **fresh starts of swings** and small caps. Pradeep's #1 lesson: **"If you cannot identify start of a swing, you should not be trading. Find another business."** Fat-fingered LNTH short turned into profit ("fat finger became fat wallet"). Mosaic short stopped out for 2¢. Lemonade: sold 80%, moved stop to half of day's range on remaining 20%.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Breakouts NOW Likely to Work — First Yes in Days

> "Are breakouts likely to work? The answer is yes."

> "But I still see on the bearish side, 43 stocks. And a lot of stocks after earnings instead of going up, they're just getting taken to the toilet."

### Manage Existing Positions — Lock In Profits

> "The time to get aggressive was yesterday. I'm not even looking at long at this stage. I'm moving stops, taking profit, locking in profit."

> "When you have it, you take it to the bank."

### Fat Finger to Fat Wallet — LNTH

> "That LNTH mistake, I was sweating in the night. But sometimes the OnlyFans girls are on your side. The fat finger has become a fat wallet."

### Extended Stocks Not Following Through

> "I see lot of these breakouts from yesterday. They're just sitting there. There is no strong buying coming in them."

> "Institutions use breakouts to new highs as a liquidity event because it allows them to sell to fools."

### Small Caps Are Where the Money Is

> "You have to look for something which is small cap which is starting out. The money will be in finding fresh ideas."

> "If you are going to be focusing on stocks like this [extended], they already made their multiple leg move. What is the probability of something like this again making a move?"

### Start of a Swing — THE Fundamental Concept

> "Swing traders make money by buying on start of a swing or in anticipation of a swing. There is no money buying on second and third day."

> "If you cannot identify start of a swing, you should not be even trading. Find some other business."

> "You are trading against some of the smartest people in the business. They are waiting for you to buy on the second and third day."

### Short Side Tricky — Whirlpool Sliced Through Stop

> "Short side is very tricky. Stocks can quickly go up 1 or 2 dollars for no reason. Whirlpool just sliced through my stop and went crazy."

> "I'm not willing to give shorts too much room to run."

### Earnings Don't Drive Price — Context and Spice Do

> "Earnings and sales don't drive price behavior. Only 15 out of 339 stocks have earnings/sales growth of 39+. The spice they add along with the earnings and sales announcement — that is what is important."

> "You cannot make a single factor decision based on earnings figures."

### Kava / CAVA — "North Brunswick Curse"

> "Any restaurant company which opens a restaurant in North Brunswick, their stock goes down. Kava opened just now, and Kava is getting taken to the toilet."

### Delayed Reaction Shorts — Urgency Matters

> "If there is an earnings miss and within five days that stock starts going down, that tends to work very well. When they go sideways for a long time, it can become a risky short."

> "If it is going to go down, you see urgency to sell."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Start of a Swing — Definitive Lesson

> "Start of a swing is the first day of a range expansion which can potentially lead to a 3-4 day move on a swing."

> "Was DNA yesterday a start of a swing? Is there any doubt about it?"

> "Not something which is up three days in a row and you ask 'can I have a Zoom meeting?' Your wife is right. You have no clue what you're doing."

### Yesterday's Breakouts — No Follow-Through

> "Bunch of stocks like Meta, Goldman Sachs — they're just sitting there doing nothing. There is no momentum or follow-through on many of these names."

> "After a 600+ day, you want people to be falling all over themselves to buy. Instead, what do you see? Stocks near highs not making moves."

### Dustbin Stocks Rallying — Short Covering

> "What is showing up in your long scans today? Stocks which are in dustbin. They are near the low and suddenly finding something. I think it's a lot of short covering."

> "These stocks are where they are for a reason — bad earnings, something wrong."

### No Indicator Needed for Start of a Swing

> "If you need an indicator to tell you start of a swing, you're on the wrong path. It takes 20 minutes max to understand."

> "Clarify, reclarify, reclarify. Write it down. Explain it to your dumb boyfriend or girlfriend. If you can articulate it, then it will happen."

### Stop Management — Half Day's Range

> "On a regular momentum burst, like Lemonade, third day of the move, 13-14%. I sold 80% and kept 20%. On the 20%, I moved my stop to half of today's range."

> "On TNA, which is a 9M EP, I'm willing to give it more room. My objective is to make it breakeven first, then go after that."

### Oil and Gas Stocks — Good Follow-Through

> "Oil and gas stocks tend to have very good follow-through. That's typically been my observation most of the time."

### Mosaic — Failed Short, Tinder Date Metaphor

> "If I go on a Tinder date and nothing happens in 15 minutes, I quickly go to the restroom and find my exit."

> "Mosaic — the whole idea of shorting this is it should open lower and keep going lower. Wells Fargo was a good example — open, lower, lower, gaps down. Thank you ma'am. Next one, please."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Breakouts ARE working now** — first "yes" in over a week; TNA, DNA, small caps leading
2. **But extended stocks are NOT following through** — Meta, Goldman Sachs just sitting; near-high stocks under pressure
3. **Start of a swing is THE fundamental concept** — "If you can't identify it, find another business"
4. **Second and third day of a swing = no money** — institutions wait for retail to buy late, then sell to them
5. **Manage profits aggressively** — sell 80% on momentum burst day 3, move stop to half of day's range on remaining 20%
6. **Fat finger → fat wallet** — LNTH accidental short turned profitable
7. **Mosaic short stopped out for 2¢** — "If it has to go down, it should show urgency to sell"
8. **Dustbin stocks rallying = short covering** — near-low stocks popping, not a real rally
9. **Earnings don't drive price** — "Only 15 of 339 stocks have 39%+ growth. The spice they add is what matters."
10. **Short side is tricky** — Whirlpool sliced through stop, 3 shorts stopped out on gap up day
11. **Oil and gas stocks have good follow-through** — observation worth remembering
12. **TNA 9M breakout best trade** — small cap rotation, gave it room to run
13. **No indicator needed for start of a swing** — "20 minutes max to understand. If it takes longer, just not possible."
14. **Fresh small-cap ideas > extended big-cap names** — "The physics of the market: if something has made such a big move, most growth is already priced in"
