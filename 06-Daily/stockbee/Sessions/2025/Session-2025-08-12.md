# Choppy Fade: Breakouts Fail, Shorts Get Squeezed, and Setup Is Everything

> Market gapped up on CPI hopes then faded. Breakouts still not holding. Shorts getting squeezed on the gap up. Only delayed reaction setups and catalyst-driven trades worth considering. Setup clarity is non-negotiable.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautious, Breakouts Unlikely

Judy's essay: cautious, slow deterioration, uncertainty on rate cut timing. Market monitor turned red. T2108 dropped from 44.76 to 43.03. 20% study elevated (~100 stocks) but lacking conviction — just over 1:1 bullish/bearish ratio.

> "Are breakouts likely to work? The answer is no. Delayed reaction EP or delayed reaction EP 9 million is what I would be focused on."

> "Breakdowns are working. Just look at your 20% bearish study. How many times do you get 60 stocks in your bearish study?"

### Circle IPO: SIPs, Not EP

Multiple members excited about [[CIRCL]] (Circle IPO, 53% revenue growth). Pradeep's take: it's a SIPs play at best, not an EP. Most IPOs make a small initial move then decline 40-80%.

> "Circle is likely to go to the toilet. That's the typical pathway of most IPOs."

> "I have nothing which was grabbing me by the neck saying, 'Circle, I should go buy it right away.'"

### Zena Technologies: Neglected SIPs

[[ZENA]] presented as OPG idea — extreme neglect, drone/AI story, 14% short interest. Pradeep acknowledged neglected stocks can make big moves, but warned about "story stocks" that keep changing narratives.

### 20% Study Extreme = Pullback Coming

> "Whenever the 20% study gets very extended, on the corner there'll be some sort of a bandit standing to rob you of the profit."

Market gapping up today, but history says extremes get pulled back. Don't chase the gap.

### Setup Is Everything: The Core Teaching

Extended discussion on why setups matter more than market calls:

> "What we want is a setup. If we have setups, we can make money. If we don't have setups, we can't make money. Fade, Powell, no Powell — that doesn't really matter."

- [[LMND]] was a good setup — entered yesterday, $30K profit at peak, gave back some but the setup was valid
- [[WFC]] was a short setup — it went down. Setup worked regardless of Powell/Trump noise
- Momentum burst patterns work on any timeframe: context + setup = profit

### Nothing Exciting on the Long Side

Pradeep scanned extensively and found no compelling long setups. [[Arista Net]] needs more time. [[SoundHound]] followed through before setting up. Gap up made shorts dangerous.

> "I don't have anything which is in my pocket. I just don't want to chase anything."

### Day Trading Stock Selection: Trade Range and Liquidity

Extended teaching on why low-priced stocks (Tilray, sub-$5) are terrible for day trading:

> "You are trading something which is extremely volatile and then you are complaining that you are getting chopped. That is what those instruments are designed for."

Good day trading stocks need: **$3+ range** and **high volume**. Trade [[CIRCL]], [[META]], [[BMNR]] — not Tilray. SMB prop traders would be all over Circle today.

> "I would not trade anything which has a range of less than $3. Because then I can make $1."

### Palantir: 245x Forward Earnings

> "When the catalyst comes, it will be catastrophic. 40% gap down. That's what will happen."

Not shorting on valuation alone — waiting for a technical catalyst. But keeping it on watch for when the setup arrives.
