### Rate Cuts at All-Time Highs = Bubble Territory

> "Interest rates are typically cut when the market is going down. Not when the market is near all time high, and you have speculative assets making all time highs, and Bitcoins and Ethereum making all time highs."

> "Contextually, the next likely move for the market is a bubble. A massive bubble. And then bubbles are where you can make big money, life changing money."

> "What stocks are going to be benefiting from a bubble? Story stocks. Anything which has no earnings, no sales, and it has a strong story. That is what is going to make the thing."

> "In a bubble market, you trade. You don't reinvest. I almost all the money which I made in COVID time, I kept it. Because I would never believe in any of those stories."

### EP 9,000,000 — How to Find Story Stocks

> "Where are you going to find these stocks? EP nine million, delayed reaction, EP nine million. By basically doing delayed reaction, EP nine million, researching, EP nine million."

> "Whenever you see anything which is trading more than 50 million shares — guaranteed or most probability is going to be a story stock."

> "Don't assume. Don't come with a preconceived notion. Just go by methodology. Look for 9,000,000 breakout. Look for something which does more than 50 million, and then look for an entry."

### Palantir — #1 Delayed Reaction Idea

> "My best compelling setup, must buy kind of setup was Palantir. It was so weak while the market was roaring. Everything was lining up perfectly."

> "If Palantir short works, you're going to look at $10, $20, $30, $40 dollar thing. Ultimately, what happens to retail darlings?"

### AEHR — SIPs Play, Not EP

### Viking Therapeutics — Downgraded

> "Viking Therapeutics is not my number one idea."

### Process, Process, Process — Core Lecture

> "What is the process? In the morning, you can run a couple of processes. You can run the process called SIPs. If you found a candidate using SIPs, you might have found AEHR."

> "Delayed reaction EP — you'd have done your work. You'd have had a watch list. You'd have prioritized your delayed reaction EP with a number one idea, number two, number three, and then you'd have found something like Palantir."

> "Process means having the scan. Process means sorting it by volume. Process means finding start of a swing. Process means finding this thing. Unless you have that, you are not going to find."

> "Become completely obsessed with process. If you cannot articulate your process, that means you don't have a process."

> "The more process oriented you become, the more you'll be in charge of your own trading."

> "Even though Friday was a big buying day, I just couldn't make sense of it. When 1,000+ days happen near the market bottom, that's a bullish signal. But when the market is near all time high, you get a 1,000 signal — I don't even know how to interpret it."

> "Since I started trading, I only seen Fed cut rates when the market is near low. They don't cut rate because the king tells them to cut the rate."

> "What we have seen consistently, persistently is only stocks which have specific catalyst are making moves. Just buying a random pattern-based trade, which most of the time works, in this market condition, hasn't been the most successful trade."

### Catalyst-Based Trades Only
