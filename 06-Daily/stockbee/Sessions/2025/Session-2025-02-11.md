# Setup Not Ticker — Stories Drive 85%, Tight-Tight-Tight Anticipation, and Spin-Off Gold

> "The day you start thinking 'what setup is this?' instead of 'what do you think of this ticker?' — that's when you become a trader. Setup and process, not ticker."

Choppy Tuesday. 79% of stocks making new lows premarket. Market range-bound, news-driven, fading breakouts. But the big lesson today: **Pradeep's complete visualization framework** and a deep dive into **spin-offs** as a hidden gold mine. The answer to "what do you think of this ticker?" is always: **is there a setup?**

3. **Sell into strength** — peel, don't add
4. **Think three days ahead** — weekend risk, gap risk
5. **Buy the dip when Fed is active** — Fed currently on sidelines
6. **Focus on tech, healthcare, consumer discretionary** — biggest moves
7. **STORIES** — the single biggest driver of 50%+ moves, not earnings
8. **Younger stocks** — focus on sub-1B market cap for biggest gains
9. **Linear stocks** — trade linear charts, not choppy ones
10. **Sugar Babies** — speed dial the stocks market loves
11. **Built-in** — institutional ownership matters
12. **Sales stars** — sales growth > profit growth for short-term moves
13. **Organize like crazy** — watch list, scans, NTRT
14. **Stocks in play** — know what's moving and why
15. **Focus on high priced stocks** — longer swings, less volatility for working people
16. **BBT at beginning of big move** — 9M volume breakout
17. **80/20** — peel 80% of position, let 20% run

### 20% Study: Low Price + Low Cap = Biggest Moves

- 63 stocks in 20%+ study
- Less than 10% have market cap above 10B
- More than 50% have market cap below 1B
- > "If you want 20% in a week, focus on stocks below $20 price and below 10B cap"
- Dollar breakout study: high-priced stocks ($60+) make $20+ moves with lower volatility — better for working people

### High-Priced Stock Nuance: Beta Spread

- [[SPGI]] (S&P Global) — $3 spread on a $500+ stock

- Day traders can trade these intraday once spread narrows

- The tighter the consolidation, the bigger the potential explosion
