# Range-Bound Chop, Dating 60-Year-Olds, and Process Over Tickers

> "IBM as a delayed reaction? It's like dating a 62-year-old. What are you going to get? Any action? Focus on something young with a catalyst — at least Affirm has growth."

Range-bound Thursday. Market refuses to sell off, refuses to break out. T2108 dropped from 50.19 to 45.05. Both sides active (47 bullish, 33 bearish in 20% study). Earnings season explosion — more SIPs than you can trade. The dominant lesson: **neglect is the necessary condition for EP**, and **process over tickers**. Every meeting is just "what ticker?" — the methodology matters more.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Neglect is the necessary condition for EP** — stocks rallied 600% into earnings are discovered, not EP. Just SIPs
2. **"Dating 60-year-olds" analogy** — don't chase delayed reactions on old/large stocks (IBM, $229B cap). Focus on young stocks with growth catalysts
3. **Range-bound market chops traders to death** — both sides working, neither side dominant. SIPs is the only reliable strategy
4. **Affirm = best delayed reaction** — controlled risk entry, real catalyst (BNPL), decelerating growth but still 20%+
5. **Process over tickers** — NTRT/MTRT, must-watch list, Briefing.com, WSJ, Bloomberg. The methodology finds the stocks
6. **Parabolic stocks: 80/20 peel and hold** — HIMS 120% in a month. When it reverses, it will be vicious
7. **Short interest sources: Ortex, Finviz, Yahoo Finance** — UPST had 22% short float, CFLT 5.85%
8. **Think three days ahead** — weekend risk, Monday Presidents' Day holiday
9. **SIPs sources: Briefing.com, WSJ "Stocks to Watch", Perplexity AI market hub** — multiple sources for daily SIPs ideas
10. **15 setups available — you only need 2-3** — selectivity over quantity, even in a market with many choices
