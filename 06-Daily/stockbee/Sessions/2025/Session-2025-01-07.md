# Selling Into the Fade, Consolidation Breakouts Are a Trap

> "Why don't consolidation breakouts work? Because everybody wants to buy them. Institutions are slowly distributing — whenever buyers come in, they sell into it. 75% fail."

Market gapped up Monday, faded all day. Tuesday confirmed the fade — institutional selling, breadth deteriorating, combination bearish 764 vs bullish 89. Pradeep's only position: building FuboTV slowly via position sizing (2% of account, no stop). MSTU stopped out. Apogee short worked small. Key teaching: **qualify every setup** like you qualify dates on Tinder — consolidation breakouts look beautiful but 75% fail because institutions distribute into them.

### Consolidation Breakouts: Why 75% Fail

> "Everybody is sold on consolidation breakouts. Why don't they work? Because institutions are slowly distributing — whenever buyers come in, they sell into it."

- Only 20-25% of consolidation breakouts work
- The ones that make big moves start from **bottom** or **continuation**, NOT consolidation
- Studying what works (20% study = millionaire study) vs. what books teach (consolidation breakout)
- This is the most tempting trade in the market — and the lowest probability

### Qualifying Criteria: The Tinder Analogy

- You don't date randomly on Tinder — you set criteria
- Same with stocks: **qualify every setup** before entering
- Continuation: Lynch, Magna 53, cap 10B, EP 9M
- Cat/Dog: start of swing + plausible story
- J&J: good bottom bounce, 2500 funds — but only moves 1-2%, too slow
- BA: no setup, just random

### Linearity and FSS

- Stocks that make big moves have **higher linearity** — orderly setup and move
- Choppy stocks = no aggressive buying
- **FSS**: Focus on Sales for longer-term moves
- Short-term: story stocks dominate (quantum, AI, satellite)
- Long-term (5-10 years): real sales dominate (Nvidia, AMD, Axon, Shopify)
