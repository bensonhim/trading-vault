# Choppy Market — Gun Blazing Hasn't Worked, Delayed Reaction Is the Only Play, IMAT Semiconductor Distribution

**Market chopping relentlessly — breakouts lack follow-through, shorts lack follow-through, only delayed reaction setups with immediate downside work.** Pradeep's situational awareness: "I am not positive on the market ability to have follow through on most breakouts." IMAT (Applied Materials) gapping down with negative growth (-13%), declining fund ownership (4,600 → 4,200), semiconductor distribution day — best delayed reaction short candidate. APLS and AUPH both exited (took profits, continuation setups worked but imperfectly). RNA bought yesterday as anticipation setup, working well. **80/20 rule on TNA would have saved a lot of money.** "Going gun blazing hasn't worked, so I'll just keep my gun at home." ### Gun Blazing Hasn't Worked — Circumspect Approach

> "Semiconductor stocks barring Nvidia and probably AMD have had similar patterns. They're all there in the delayed reaction scan."

### Delayed Reaction Is the Preferred Play

> "Most of them give you a delayed reaction entry. Very few will go straight up, especially with the amount of gap ups which are happening. They all come down."

> "Rather than going in today, getting stopped out and getting frustrated — I'll catch them in delayed reaction EP."

> "Reddit didn't give you a delayed reaction except for one day. Otherwise, all of them give you a delayed reaction entry."

### TNA Debacle — 80/20 Rule Saves Money

> "Every time I don't do 80/20, this year I regret. 80/20 would have still saved you a lot of money."

### Start of a Swing Near the Bottom — What's Working

> "Start of a swing is working more than continuation setup."

### Anticipation Setup — When to Enter

> "When do I enter anticipation setup? I enter in anticipation. RNA, I entered it yesterday in the last two minutes."

> "Anticipation — you buy in anticipation of a reversal. So enter just before the close."

### UnitedHealth and Intel — Too Late to the Party

### Small Cap Shorts Strategy — New Development
