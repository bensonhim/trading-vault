# Delayed Reaction Week — Focus Only on Catalyst-Based EPs, KAVA Stopped Out, Carvana Working, Dow Chemical Template

**Entire week declared "delayed reaction EP only" — no chasing breakouts.** KAVA was #1 idea (highest volume since IPO, guided down, margins under pressure), stopped out after going 50¢ above entry. Front-running destroyed the entry — "if I had not shared KAVA, I would have got a much better entry." **Dow Chemical = #1 template for delayed reaction shorts**: negative growth rate, 2,000 → 1,500 funds declining, 9M volume, clear catalyst. **X-ray distinction: "had good X-ray" vs "has good X-ray"** — declining growth = sell signal. **Capitalization threshold: stocks above $40B rarely continue big moves.** EP definition: must have surprise + neglect. Stock already up 100%+ is NOT an EP. Carvana bought Friday as delayed reaction, working well. ASTS and GDRX both stopped out. 80/20 rule again emphasized. "Marriage vs sugar babies" analogy for delayed reaction vs breakouts.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness — Cautious, Wait-and-See

> "My essay this morning is cautious as the market appears in a wait-and-see posture and is due for a pullback after reaching several all time highs last week."

> "T2,108 decreased from 53.09 to 50.05, signaling weakness. 20% study continues to signal potential pullback — bullish approaching 100, bearish approaching 20."

### This Week: Delayed Reaction EP Only

> "For this entire week, we are going to focus only on one thing. Delayed reaction."

> "So you're not chasing breakouts and things like that. I am going to focus on delayed reaction EP for the morning session. Or if there are no good delayed reaction EP, then anticipation. Those are the two things. Only two things."

### KAVA — #1 Idea, Highest Volume Since IPO

> "KAVA is my number one thing which I'm watching. Highest volume traded since IPO. It guided down, margins are under pressure. There is a negative catalyst."

> "If a short setup has to work, it works in next three to five days. TXN — within five days."

> "I put 50¢ above the high. Around 2% risk. The potential I see here is around 15 to 20%."

### Front-Running Problem

> "The moment you have a list, people knew that KAVA is my number one thing, and some of you started front running. That's why this kind of thing is only one week."

> "I would have got a much better entry on KAVA if I had not said that KAVA is my number one thing."

### Delayed Reaction EP — Deep Dive

> "The whole idea of doing this is to fee. You are stalking a prey. I was watching KAVA for three, four days. Then on Friday, when it showed life, I got into it."

> "When you decide to do delayed reaction, you are going to miss out on breakouts. If you get married, you're gonna miss out on all the sugar babies. If you just chase sugar babies, you're never going to have a stable relationship."

> "I want to get into things with just one or two percent stop. And then the risk reward is in your favor."

### Dow Chemical — #1 Template for Delayed Reaction Shorts

> "How many factors were in your favor when looking at Dow Jones? Highest volume in multi-years. Negative, negative, next four quarters not going to make profit. From 2,013 funds to 1,595. There was an overwhelming case."

> "That is 95% probability play. You want to do it as delayed reaction because then if you have that much confidence and you have done your work — 706M float stock, trades 5-9M shares. You can do size."

### Fund Ownership Declining — Key Short Factor

> "One of the factors which works very well in shorts is if fund ownership is starting to decline. That's a very good factor which almost invariably is one of the factors I look for."

### X-Ray Analysis — "Had Good X-Ray" vs "Has Good X-Ray"

> "There is a difference between it has good X-ray and it had good X-ray. Declining — 46, 27, 19 → 22, 06. Now what is happening? It goes down."

> "Learn to read X-rays because the X-rays tell you a lot."

### EP Definition — Must Have Surprise + Neglect

> "If there is no surprise, then there is no neglect. No neglect. It's not an EP."

> "The whole idea of EP is to find something which is a new thing, which has not been discovered by the market. Something which has not made 100% to 100% move."

### Capitalization Threshold — $40B Ceiling

> "How many stocks can have a capitalization of $40B? It's a very high threshold. Once they reach 30 or 40 billion kind of a mark, nothing good happens."

> "Shopify — amazing quarter after quarter, then it reaches 40B and makes 30-40 times sales and then just prices. This stock had 39% sales growth for five and a half years."

### GoodRx — SIPs Play (Ozempic Catalyst)

> "GoodRx — I thought it was a good enough setup. Small position, 40¢ stop."

> "Ozempic is like the Bitcoin of the pharma industry. It has changed every single element."

### 80/20 Rule — Again

> "Every time I don't do 80/20, this year I regret. 80/20 would have still saved you a lot of money."

> "When they work, they compensate for when they fail. Because when they fail, your risk is so low."

### Process-Driven Trading

> "Create a setup book. Chipotle — missed earnings, slowing sales 6%, margin under pressure. That's the kind of thing you want to see."

> "If you just do blindly, you'll not get anything to work. Get into details."

> "Delayed reaction EP will work if you can select the right stock and differentiate between what is a real catalyst and what is not."

### Small Cap Shorts Strategy

> "I'm working on really small cap shorts as a strategy. Below $1 billion market cap. You can get good long and short in the small cap universe."
