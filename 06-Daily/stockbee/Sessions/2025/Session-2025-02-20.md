## 2 PM Meeting — Financials Hit, XLF Short / FAS Long

### Clear Theme: Financials Getting Hammered

- JPMorgan Chase, Citibank, Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, PayPal — all hit
- Walmart earnings miss triggered the cascade
- > "Financials are the one which are getting hit after the Walmart thing"
- Technology holding up relatively well — selling concentrated in financials

### The Trade: XLF Short or FAS Long

- XLF short = easiest trade, sector ETF, low risk (1.7% stop)
- FAS = triple leveraged long (3x financials), same thesis
- > "I like XLF short better than FAS long because you can put a lot more money in it. It's a slow mover. The stop can be only 1% or 1.7%"
- DIA short = another option if large caps start going down
- Individual shorts: JPM, Citi, MS, GS, PayPal — but XLF is cleaner

### Palantir Should Have Been Shorted Yesterday

- > "The Palantir you should have shorted yesterday"
- Clear breakdown signal that was the pilot fish

### Start of a Swing — The Frustration

- Pradeep's repeated lesson: buy at the **start of a swing**, not mid-move or gap up
- > "I still don't understand why it is so difficult for people to understand start of a swing and buying on start of a swing"
- LA example: someone wanted to buy mid-gap-up — wait for the setup
- > "Just one simple idea, if you can manage to get into your head, will be a game changer"

### Combination Bearish: 564 Stocks

- 564 stocks in combination bearish vs 127 bullish
- 4:1 bearish ratio — overwhelming selling pressure
- Clear financial sector theme in bearish scan

### Pradeep's Posted Picks

- **Bullish**: FAS (triple leveraged financials)
- **Bearish**: XLF short, PM, DIA short
- All sector/theme-based rather than individual stocks

### Key Insight: Retail Trader Advantage
