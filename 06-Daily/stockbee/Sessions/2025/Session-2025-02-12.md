# Thousand Cuts — SA Over Setup, SIPs Over Swings, and the Paradox of Good Sets in Bad Markets

> "A good setup in an unfavorable market — what happens? It doesn't work. That's the paradox. People load up on AG, perfect delayed reaction, catalyst, everything right. They get stopped out. Why? The market."

Choppy Wednesday. CPI higher than expected, reciprocal tariffs pending. Breakouts fading after one day. The dominant lesson: **situational awareness matters more than setup quality**, and in this market, **SIPs (day trades) are the only reliable strategy**. Pradeep traded [[UPST]] with textbook precision — bought at open, peeled 80% at the round number, kept a runner. Meanwhile, members with 20-30% drawdowns from "thousand cuts" learned the hard way.

---

## AM Meeting (Situational Awareness) — Cautious, Selective, SIPs-Focused

### Market: Choppy, News-Driven, Fading Catalysts

- Market monitor had a selling day yesterday, primary indicators red
- T2108 moved from 49.33 to 50.19 (marginal improvement)
- 20% study: bullish ratio dropped from 4:1 to 2:1, bearish side active (26 stocks)
- CPI higher than anticipated, reciprocal tariffs to be signed later today
- > "There is a tussle between bullish and bearish catalyst. It might just be chop."

### Most Catalysts Are Fading After One Day

- Stocks with catalysts are pulling back after one-day moves
- [[CRDO]] had a good catalyst — didn't sustain
- [[EXPE]] had catalyst — pulled back
- Most EP/watch list stocks: catalyst → one day up → fade
- > "This is more a market for delayed reaction than immediate breakout"

### SIPs Are the Strategy

- SIPs work independent of market conditions
- > "SIPs is an evergreen strategy. When breakouts are not working, SIPs is what you focus on"
- Key SIPs for today: [[UPST]], [[DASH]], [[SMCI]], Confluent, Gilead Science
- Categorize SIPs: institutional quality (high-price) vs. speculative (low-price/low-float)
- > "If you have a small account, focus on super low float stocks below $5"

### Premarket: Don't Buy

- Spreads are wide, no stops available, liquidity is a problem
- > "If I do something in premarket, do I have the ability to control my risk? No"
- > "Always be in control and controlled risk-taking is my fundamental motto"

### IBM Delayed Reaction Question — Needs a Real Catalyst

- Member asked about IBM as delayed reaction
- > "Do you see anything in IBM's growth — 1%, 3%, 4%, 6%? What catalyst does IBM have that is so exciting?"
- Delayed reaction requires a genuine catalyst, not just any stock
- > "The day you start thinking 'what setup is this?' instead of 'what do you think of this ticker?' — that's when you become a trader"

## 10 AM Meeting — UPST: The SIPs Masterclass

### UPST: Textbook SIPs Execution

- Bought at 84.65 at 9:31:57, $1 stop, never came into play
- Used "change from open" column in Interactive Brokers to time entry
- > "The moment change from open goes negative and recovers — that's when the checkout is complete, and that's when you buy"
- Target: round number 90 (optimistic), 89 (realistic)
- Played as two-tier: sold 80% around 89, kept small core position for delayed reaction
- Short interest: 22% of float short (17.9M shares)
- > "Why is UPST going crazy? Shorts are covering. Once shorts have covered, what happens?"

### Change From Open: The Key SIPs Timing Tool

- In Interactive Brokers, watch "change from open" dollar column
- When it turns positive after going negative → checkout complete → entry signal
- > "It's not mechanical. You're watching. There is a lot of judgment involved"
- Must have pre-selected the stock (NTRT/MTRT process) — not just random buying

### SIPs Is a 100% Day Trading Strategy

- > "SIPs is a 100% day trading strategy. You just have an idea that this stock is going to make a big move based on the news"
- Sell into strength — if you wait for P&L to drop, you give up gains
- UPST gave up all intraday gains → 80/20 peel saved the trade
- JBM and Jay: the most consistent SIPs traders, they team up and focus exclusively on SIPs
- > "If you don't do SIPs daily, it just doesn't make sense"

### Wall Street Journal: SIPs Source

- > "Wall Street Journal is a good place to get a summary of institutional quality stocks"
- Found DASH, UPST, Confluent from WSJ the night before
- NTRT/MTRT process generates the SIPs list

### Confluent: Potential Delayed Reaction

- 3.1 days short interest, earnings + Databricks partnership news
- But history shows every Confluent earnings has faded
- > "Overwhelming evidence: every single one of these earnings has faded. Play it for just the day"

### No Setups in Bullish Scan — Reflex Rally Signal

- Market turned positive intraday, but scan shows very few tradable setups
- > "If you don't see setups, it tells you there is no real buying conviction. This is just a reflex rally"
- > "Day traders bought the gap down, took profit. Unless real buying comes in, it fades after data"
- Most people are in drawdown — not from one big blow-up

## 2 PM Meeting — The Paradox of Good Setups in Bad Markets

### UPST Afternoon: Shorts Covered, Then What?

- UPST genuine catalyst (earnings beat), but shorts covered → what's left?
- Best strategy: wait for delayed reaction, or play day trade only
- > "If you're convinced UPST has a game-changing catalyst, widen the stop significantly. But is the risk/reward worth it?"

### AG: The Setup vs. SA Paradox

- Member loaded up on [[AG]] — perfect delayed reaction setup, catalyst present
- Put stop at half of day → got stopped out when market gapped down
- > "Perfect setup. Delayed reaction, everything there. Catalyst is there. Why did he get stopped out? It's the market."
- **This is the key lesson**: good setup + unfavorable SA = doesn't work
- > "By the time most people realize SA is important, they've blown up"

### Thousand Cuts: The Real Danger

- > "20-30% drawdowns from thousand cuts — small daily losses that add up"
- None of the drawdowns came from one big blow-up
- > "Every single trader who's been around understands SA. Even proprietary firm day traders making $1-2M last year are struggling now"
- The underlying market conditions changed — same explosive moves are not working

### SIPs Independent of SA — But SA Matters for Swings

- > "SIPs are independent of SA. They work for whatever news is there for that day"
- [[DD]] worked yesterday (day of spin-off news) → SIPs-specific catalyst
- But for swing trades, SA is everything

### Short Interest: Where to Find It

- Ortec (trade ideas platform) shows short interest
- Yahoo Finance, Finviz also have free short interest data
- Example: CFLT 5.85% of float short, 3.52 days to cover
- UPST: 22% of float short, much higher → bigger squeeze potential

### Other Setups Discussed

- [[QMCO]] — intraday EP 9M breakout, missed entry (was at bank), 4.7M float, but already 35% up
- [[CFLT]] — good SIPs, played intraday, but fading pattern
- [[CLS]] — range expansion, but market conditions unfavorable
- [[Booking]] — start of swing, but high-priced stock requires skills and limit orders
- Chinese stocks ([[BABA]], etc.) — starting to follow through, but uncertain

### The Process: NTRT/MTRT Is Non-Negotiable

- > "You can go to a swimming class, watch videos on swimming, but can you really become a swimmer?"
- Must do NTRT/MTRT after market close — that's how stocks like UPST appear on the radar
- Process generates the picks; meetings are just discussion
- > "Every meeting is just about tickers. That's the only thing people are interested in. Not the methodology"

### Limit Orders on High-Spread Stocks

- Never buy at ask on wide-spread stocks
- Put limit order 0.5-1% below ask, wait hours
- Pradeep once took an entire day to fill 100 shares of [[AZO]] — got 10, then 5, then 2, etc.
- > "On high-priced stocks, you have to play with small size"

## Key Takeaways

1. **SA over setup** — "Good setup in unfavorable market = doesn't work." This is the paradox that destroys most traders
2. **SIPs over swings in choppy markets** — when breakouts don't follow through, SIPs (day trades) are the only reliable strategy
3. **Thousand cuts kill accounts** — 20-30% drawdowns come from small daily losses, not one big blow-up
4. **Change from open** — the key SIPs timing tool in Interactive Brokers; buy when it recovers from negative
5. **Round number targets** — UPST target was 90 (optimistic) / 89 (realistic); nearest round number
6. **80/20 peel saves the trade** — UPST gave up all intraday gains, but 80% was already peeled at 89
7. **Short interest matters** — UPST 22% short = squeeze potential; CFLT 5.85% = modest squeeze
8. **Wall Street Journal** — good source for institutional quality SIPs
9. **NTRT/MTRT is non-negotiable** — process generates the picks; watching meetings alone won't make you a trader
10. **Premarket = no control** — wide spreads, no stops, can't manage risk. SIPs entry is at or just after open
