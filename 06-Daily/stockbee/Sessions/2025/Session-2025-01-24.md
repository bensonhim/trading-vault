## 10 AM — EP Framework, Singles Finance Home Runs

### EP 9M Checklist: Magna 53 Capped 10 by 10

- **M**assive acceleration in earnings or sales (prefer sales acceleration — earnings can be manipulated via cost cuts)
- **Gap up** on dramatic catalyst
- **Neglected** — few funds holding, under-covered
- **5**+ days short interest
- **3**+ analyst upgrades on catalyst day
- **Capped** below $10B market cap, IPOs < 10 years

### Singles Finance Home Runs Framework

> "These singles happen more frequently, and they allow you to finance these trades."

- EP requires 20-25% stops — if you put 25% of account on one EP and it fails, you're down 5%
- Singles (0.5-1% gains) happen frequently and let you absorb EP stop-outs
- Pattern-based trades (SIPs): 90% pattern, 10% other criteria → **30% win rate without SA**
- With SA filter: **55-60% win rate** — some periods every trade works
- Delayed EP: same as EP but after a pullback

### Categorizing Trades: Cat, Dog, Liquid Lava

- **Cat**: fundamentally sound catalyst stock
- **Dog**: low-price pump-and-dump (NVA Nasdaq non-compliance up 150% — avoid but acknowledge)
- **Liquid Lava**: NVDA-type — high liquidity, can put large capital to work, moves account 5-10%

### Situational Awareness Is Everything

- "Are breakouts likely to work?" = the only question that matters each morning
- Good setups visible (Waystar, Bank of NY, DDD, Burke) but taking minuscule positions — testing hypotheses only
- Delayed reaction setups also not working — "they're just sitting there, nada"
- Curbing natural instinct to buy good-looking breakouts because fades dominate
- **Framework thinking** > ticker thinking — setup type + SA + process history determines action

### VCP (Volatility Compression Pattern) — Mark Minervini

- Pattern: progressively shallower cups → handle → breakout
- Theory: buyers accumulate at every level, each pullback is shallower because demand increases
- **Key**: Minervini only trades VCP when market is in confirmed bull move — 2-4 weeks after confirmed rally
- Trading VCP breakouts in current choppy environment = heartbreak hotel

### Deep Dive Philosophy (Extended)

> "Every single trader who made it — they may not use the words 'deep dive,' but that's what they did."

- Dan Zanger: obsessively marked every chart
- Mark Minervini: built database of all big winners
- William O'Neil: decades of pattern study
- Day traders' "screen time" = deep dive by another name
- **Supersaturation** = military boot camp principle — overload your brain with patterns until they become second nature
- 50% study internalization: look at enough 50%+ charts and "the charts start talking to you"
- Range expansion principle: stocks move in the direction of range expansion — every chart confirms this
- **Self-leadership**: "There is no problem in your life which you cannot solve if you have self-leadership"

### 80/20 Position Management

- Sell 50-80% of position into strength
- Keep remaining 20% with trailing stop
- This is how you maintain gains in a choppy, fading environment

## Key Takeaways

1. **Singles finance home runs** — frequent small gains absorb EP stop-losses, enabling larger EP positions
2. **SA is the million dollar question** — "Are breakouts likely to work?" asked every morning determines everything
3. **Fades dominate** — even good setups fade intraday; take minuscule positions to test hypotheses
4. **Framework > tickers** — think in setup types + SA + process history, not individual stock names
5. **Deep dive = supersaturation** — overload on patterns until they become second nature; no shortcut
6. **VCP only works in confirmed bull moves** — don't trade consolidation breakouts in choppy markets
7. **Range expansion direction** — stocks move in direction of range expansion; study enough charts and this becomes obvious
