### Anticipation Setup = Edge

Pradeep pointed out that the anticipation setups posted on the website the night before save enormous time — and most members ignore them:

> "First thing I look at in the morning — it saves me so much time from my own work... A lot of information is really available, but nobody uses it."

### Linearity — The Defining Lesson of the Day

When asked about GRR, Pradeep launched into an extended teaching moment:

> "If anything shows volatility, you should avoid it like a plague."

> "Look for linear setups. Is there anybody forcing you to buy stocks like this? No."

The analogy was unforgettable:

> "First husband committed suicide. Second husband jumped out of a car. Third one was pushed. Fourth one drank milk and got food poisoning. And fifth — you think you'll be the one who survives?"

> "When stocks lose linearity, I just don't like them. Sometimes they work. But is it worth your time and effort to get into something as choppy as that?"

> "There are enough opportunities in very linear stocks. Linearity for retail traders is so important."
