### Trade Factory — Home Run vs Singles

> "In the trade factory, there are two types of moves. One is a home run — 20%, 40%, 80% account move. The other is singles — half a percent to 5% per trade. You do hundreds of these and generate cash for when the home run shows up."

> "The home run doesn't show up every day. It shows up rarely. The skills required are also two different things. For home runs, you need analytical skills, understanding of growth stocks, turnaround stocks, story stocks. For singles, it's pattern-based trading."

### Two Ways to Short — Nano Cap + Fund Ownership Decline

> "The most profitable strategy on the short side is nano cap. Stocks with capitalization below $250M. 99% of these stocks don't have any business. They just keep reverse splitting and diluting."

> "The second way is to find institutional quality shorts — stocks owned by 1000+ funds where fund ownership is declining 30-40%. Like Moderna — 2185 funds owned it, now 1300 funds. That's 800 funds selling."

> "Dow is a classic example. 2303 funds owned it at the peak. Since it was added to S&P, it's been in terminal decline. Fund ownership declining by 30-40% per quarter."

> "Stocks which make the best shorts either have declining sales with no hope of change, or they are extremely expensive at the height of mania."

### Vertex Pharmaceuticals — Delayed Reaction Short Thesis
