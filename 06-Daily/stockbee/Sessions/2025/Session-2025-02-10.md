## 10 AM Meeting — Trade Factory Deep Dive

### The Trade Factory: Two Production Lines

| Line | Goal | Setup Types | Key Skill |
|------|------|-------------|------------|
| **Home Runs** | 10-100%+ per trade | EP, EP 9M, Delayed DP, Delayed DP 9M, Sugar Baby | **Catalyst** |
| **Singles** | 0.5-5% per trade | Momentum burst, combination bullish/bearish, anticipation, reversal | **Pattern** |

- EP 9M = most important setup. Plain EP = least important (too rare)
- Process flow: **SA (situational awareness) → NTRT/MTRT → SIPs/EP 9M → Watch list + Breakouts**

### 50% Study: Stories Drive 85% of Big Moves

- Of 178 stocks that made 50%+ moves in 40 days, **only 15% had earnings catalyst**
- > "What drives stocks to big moves in the short run? Stories. Stories drive them"
- Top 25 biggest winners: less than 10% had earnings/sales growth
- Short-term = stories + pattern. Long-term (5 years) = earnings/sales matter
- **This answers the question once and for all** — focus on EP 9M and sugar babies

### Consolidation Breakout = 25% Win Rate

- Deep dive on consolidation breakouts: only 25% work
- The ones that work have high volume or a catalyst
- > "You went on a date and she kicked you in the balls. Next time she took your wig. Next time your underwear went flying"
- Don't waste time on consolidations — focus on continuation and start-of-swing setups

### Buying on Day 3-4 = FOMO = 90% Failure Rate

- APPS up 69% in 3 days — no setup left, no delay, no entry point
- > "If you're buying on the third day after a 69% move, what is the probability you'll still be a trader in 2-3 years?"
- Boston Scientific — up 6 days, $155B market cap, 1.5B float — can't make 20-30% moves
- > "Do you expect a 50-year-old overweight person to run 100 meters?"

### Four Types of Problems (Revisited)

1. **Setup problem** — wrong setup (e.g., consolidation breakout)
2. **Process problem** — right setup, wrong execution
3. **Situational awareness** — trading in choppy market as if trending
4. **Trader's problem** — analysis paralysis, overthinking

### "Good Enough Is Good Enough"

- Don't waste years fighting TC2000, Briefing, MarketSmith — they work well enough
- > "Good enough is very good enough. Is it stopping you from making money? No? Then just run with it"
- Solve your problems once — don't revisit them
- EP series starts at 5PM today for next few days (Platinum members only)
