## 10 AM Meeting — Trade Factory and Buying Right

### The Trade Factory

Two production lines:

| Line | Goal | Setup Types |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Home Runs** | 10-100%+ account moves | EP, EP 9M, Delayed DP, Delayed DP 9M, Sugar Baby |
| **Singles** | 0.5-5% account moves | Momentum burst, combination bullish/bearish, reversal, anticipation |

- **EP 9M is the single most important setup** — least important is plain EP (too rare to rely on alone)
- Singles finance the home runs

### The Fundamental Lesson: Buy Right

> "Just one thing if you can do in trading — it might change your trading: buy at the start of a swing"

- Buying on day 3 or 4 of a move = FOMO = 90% chance of getting hurt
- > "FOMO. It's a promo. Form one, confirmation bias. By third or fourth day, you see it worked, so you can't control yourself"
- The answer is always in the process — if you understand the trade factory, you know how long to hold Palantir

### Four Types of Trading Problems

1. **Setup problem** — wrong setup selection
2. **Process problem** — right setup, wrong execution (e.g., bought LAES but sold for pennies instead of 17%)
3. **Situational awareness problem** — trading in a choppy market as if it's trending
4. **Trader's problem** — analysis paralysis, overthinking, never executing

> "The only difference between successful and unsuccessful traders is that successful ones have found some workable solution to their trading problem. Not perfect — workable."

### Sugar Baby = Setup, Don't Question It

- Sugar baby is a setup, not a stock to analyze
- > "If a sugar baby shows up in a scan, do you really go and ask 100 questions? No"
- [[MSTR]] CEO "certified crazy" — but that doesn't stop you from making 100% on the setup
- Market has fallen in love with certain stocks — who are you to question it?

### Google/Meta: The Turnpike Problem

- Google's core business = turnpike (low CapEx, high profit) — build once, collect toll forever
- Now they're saying they'll invest $50-70B to make $5B — the opposite of turnpike
- When Meta did this: stock dropped 77%. When Amazon did this: stock dropped 55%
- > "These companies are making massive bets. In 3-4 quarters, they'll be dead money"

### Market Since Mid-December = Chop Fest

- November was great for breakouts. Since mid-December: deterioration
- Only 3-4 good breakout days since then
- Solutions: day trade, scalp, sit on hands, or develop new setups
