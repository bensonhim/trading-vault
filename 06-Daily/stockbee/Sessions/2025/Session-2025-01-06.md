# Gap Up, Sell Into Strength, Start of Swing Is Everything

> "Good trading is about following a process and doing the same thing again and again, improving every day. Every time, you must be cognizant of situational awareness."

Monday gap up after Friday's bounce. Pradeep sells aggressively into strength — SOXL 11% profit, AVGO 80/20, CRDO 80%, RH "lollipop" profit. Market fades all day after morning pop. Key teaching: **start of a swing** is the only valid entry. Third/fourth legs have very low probability. Consolidation breakouts have the lowest win rate. Gap ups kill setups — take profits first, re-enter later.

### Sell Into Strength: The Professional's Move

> "What professional traders do is sell into strength. What immature traders want to do is hold."

- Gap up = sell into strength immediately
- Morning scramble: took profits before seeking new entries
- Missed Google entry because taking profits was higher priority

### Start of a Swing: The Essential Concept

> "If you can just understand one concept — start of a swing — it might help you a lot."

- **Continuation setup**: first leg 15%+, pullback 3-10 days, then breakout
- **Consolidation setup**: looks beautiful but lowest win rate — most go back into range
- **Bottom bounce**: rare but powerful when conditions align
- Third/fourth leg = low probability; first/second leg = where money is made

### Cat, Dog, and Liquid Lava Framework

- **Cat**: stock with clearly identifiable catalyst (earnings, sales, management change) — real, tangible
- **Dog**: story stock — "we're going to launch batteries on Mars, power your sex toys" — speculative nonsense
- **Liquid Lava**: trades 9M+ every day, just goes up — high-volume momentum plays

### WGS: Consolidation Breakout Case Study

- Has a catalyst (bucket zero) but no volume

- S&P 500 inclusion is NOT an EP catalyst — funds already positioned

## 2 PM Meeting — Trade Factory Deep Dive, Singles Finance Home Runs, Fading Day

### Trade Factory Framework: Singles Finance Home Runs

- **Home runs/mini home runs**: 10-50%+ moves, require 10-25% stops, need size
- **Singles**: 0.5-1% account moves, 2.5-5% stops
- Singles finance the home runs — they provide the cushion for large-risk positions
- "If you put 150% of account with a 10% stop, you'll have big drawdowns — that's not a good strategy"

### Market Fades: Gap Up Profits Preserved

3. **Consolidation breakout = lowest win rate** — looks beautiful, fails most of the time
4. **Singles finance home runs** — 0.5-1% singles with tight stops fund the 10-25% risk positions
5. **Cat/Dog/Liquid Lava** — classify your SIPs before sizing
6. **SOXL 11%, AVGO $80/share, RH lollipop** — take profits early in the year
7. **Cap below 10B + IPO within 10 years** — where biggest moves come from
8. **20% study is your millionaire study** — study what works, not what books say
9. **Bottom stocks bounce harder** — not stocks that "held up well"
10. **Gap up kills setups** — wait for entries, don't chase
11. **Lulu > Shopify** — start of swing vs. third day up
