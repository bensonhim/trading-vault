# Raining Setups on the Short Side, Earnings Are One-Day Wonders, and Don't Overtrade the Chop

> "It's raining setups on the short side after a long time. Every earnings breakout is a one-day wonder. Good earnings are not getting rewarded — that's why the market is going down."

Thursday. T2108 dropped to 37.83. No capitulation — just drip, drip, drip selling. Pradeep's essay: cautiously bullish for a short-term bounce, but remained bearish overall. The bounce that arrived was weak and faded. Earnings breakouts died in one day. Delayed reaction bearish setups are everywhere. > "Too much foreplay is not good. I want to see bang, bang, bang selling — then relief rally." Instead: slow simmer. No meetings tomorrow (Friday).

---

## AM Meeting — Cautiously Bearish, News Is the Edge, Delayed Reaction Focus

### Market: T2108 Down, Bounce Expected But Weak

- T2108 decreased from 39.22 to **37.83** — still elevated, not capitulation
- 20% study: 5:1 bearish-to-bullish ratio — short-term bounce signal
- Market monitor: slight green on 4% indicator, but primary/secondary still red
- Yesterday's bounce was very weak
- > "If you get a very weak bounce, there will be more likelihood of a down move"
- Stocks that were holding up also started breaking down
- > "Remain bearish until something really big, buying comes in"

### News = Massive Edge, Most People Ignore It

- > "News remains one of the greatest edges in the market"
- Most people don't look at catalysts — they look at charts and moving averages only
- 50% study showed stocks with news made the biggest moves
- > "Whoever spends time in understanding catalyst has an edge"
- XNET (China tech/cloud) — news-driven, but higher float = less explosive than low-float names

### Earnings Breakouts = One-Day Wonders

- This earnings season's theme: **good earnings are not getting rewarded**
- Snow, Root, AVGO, CRDO, DocuSign — all one-day wonders then fade
- > "Shorting good earnings was the trade"
- 50% study: only 11 stocks above $10B market cap made 50%+ moves — small cap = edge
- After news exhausts, stocks give up gains

### Delayed Reaction Bearish = Primary Trade Focus

- Lots of breakdowns happened — now setting up for second leg
- > "After the first leg, there will be a second leg — that is where your delayed short you're waiting for"
- HIMS, Carvana, JPMorgan, Robinhood, Hertz — delayed reaction candidates
- > "I would focus on delayed reaction here because there are a lot of breakdowns"

### Bearish Moves Are Highly Volatile

- > "The defining characteristic of a bearish move is that it is going to be highly volatile intraday"
- Most bearish moves are short duration
- Need wider stops for shorts — intraday whipsaws take out stops then continue down
- IWM example: went down, recovered all the way (taking out stop), then went back down
- > "You need a wider stop to make money in shorts"

### Don't Overtrade

- > "Highly selective. If you see a good setup, take it. Otherwise, you can get chopped"
- SIPs only for day trades — no follow-through for swing
- Slow, deliberate risk taking on A+ quality setups only

## 10 AM Meeting — No Panic Selling, Retail Buying the Dip, Delayed Reaction Setups Everywhere

- Not capitulation — institutions methodically reducing exposure

- > "Yoga class — everybody talking about buying Palantir, it's a bargain"

### Earnings Winners Die Fast

- Snow, Root, AVGO, CRDO, DocuSign — all earnings winners that became one-day trades
- > "Shorting good earnings was the trade"
- Market looking beyond this quarter's earnings — narrative has changed

### Weak Structure Shorts Everywhere

- AMD, PLTR, NFLX, Reddit, CDNS, Constellation Energy — all becoming weak structure shorts
- > "So many of these are becoming weak structure shorts"
- Intraday volatility = biggest risk for shorts (stops get taken out)
- > "The risk here is intraday bounce — the biggest risk to taking shorts here"

### Delayed Reaction Bearish = Focus

- HIMS, JPMorgan, Robinhood, Hertz, Carvana — delayed reaction setups forming
- > "Delayed reaction bearish is where I would focus"
- PayPal breakdown → delayed reaction forming
- Google earnings flop → now setting up as delayed reaction

### Don't Overtrade This Market

- > "Lot of people will end up overtrading in this period. If you overtrade with no clear direction, you'll be a complete mess by the time the market turns"
- Long setups look good (Vertex Pharma, MA) but probability of follow-through is low
- > "What is the probability of breakouts working here? We don't know"
