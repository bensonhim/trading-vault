# Think in Setups, Not Stocks — Volume Is the Golden Test, FOMO Is the Enemy, Sell the Gap Up

The defining lesson of July 15 was **setup thinking over stock thinking**. Pradeep received 30+ emails asking "should I buy NVDA/MP?" — proof that members think in stocks, not setups. MP was a good setup yesterday (delayed reaction), but NOT a setup today. NVIDIA gapped up on China news — classic "sell the gap up" scenario. The golden test for any catalyst: **volume**. If the market takes the news seriously, volume screams. No volume = no conviction. Government policy catalysts (like MP's rare earth executive order) tend to be durable — First Solar went up 600% on German renewable policy. A new theme emerged: Chinese stocks (BABA, BIDU, JD). Pradeep is heading to Germany — no meetings Wed-Fri.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish but Likely to Fade the Gap

Shankar presented (Judy absent): Very bullish, semis surging on NVIDIA China news, nuclear/crypto themes. But semis overextended (18% above 200 SMA). Pradeep's override:

> "When positive news comes in after a stock has made a big move and expectations were already there, fade the gap at least in the short run."

> "It's a selective market focused on stocks with immediate catalyst and reason to go up. If you just buy a pretty looking chart or a breakout, probability of it working is relatively low."

### Think in Setups, Not Stocks — The Core Lesson

> "MP was a good setup yesterday. Delayed reaction. Is it a good setup now? No. Simple as that."

> "The biggest problem for traders is they don't believe in setups or they don't have setups. If you think in setups, you will have 25-year longevity in this game. If you don't, you'll be sometime lucky, and most of the time unlucky."

> "No setup, no buy. FOMO goes away. Overtrading goes away. So many things go away."

### The 30 Emails Prove the Point

> "Since morning, 33 emails asking me about MP or NVIDIA — 'Should I sell my girlfriend? Should I sell my wife? Buy NVIDIA!' What does this demonstrate? That they should not be trading."

### Volume = Golden Test of Catalyst Validity

> "If there is a genuine catalyst, there is a very simple test — the market takes it seriously. It screams volume. Money will follow."

> "MP, on the day of the catalyst, traded 86 million shares. The highest number ever. That is why that catalyst was powerful. That is why I was looking for a delayed reaction."

> "How many news are released in a day? A lot. But it doesn't really matter. The news which can attract buyer volume is what you are interested in."

### Government Policy Catalysts Are Durable

> "MP is a government policy change catalyst. Government policy changes tend to be durable because once the government changes the policy, it remains for 10, 20, 30 years."

> "First Solar went up 600% when the German government changed policy requiring renewable energy. Government policy."

### NVIDIA = Sell the News

> "People are so bullish on NVIDIA. Someone was banging the table on NVIDIA this morning. What happened? Straight down from the open. The institutions are not fools. They are selling into strength. Who's selling? Funds. Algorithms. Quantitative traders who have studied market behavior."

### The Worm Confirmed the Fade

> "The red worm was on top from the start. Breakouts are likely to fade when the worm is in the 20+ territory."

3. **Government policy catalysts are durable** — First Solar (600% move), MP (rare earth executive order)
4. **Sell the gap up** — when positive news hits after a big run, institutions distribute to retail

6. **9M breakdowns > breakouts** — tactical shift confirmed from Jul 14
7. **China theme emerging** — BABA, BIDU, JD dominating breakout lists
