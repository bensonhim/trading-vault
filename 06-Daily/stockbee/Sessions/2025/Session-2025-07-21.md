# Melt-Up Cracks, Earnings Reality Check — Delayed Reaction Is the Play, Catalyst Still King, Kohl's Is a Joke

Back from Germany, Pradeep found a market where the melt-up phase has hit a wall. High-flyers (Netflix, MicroStrategy, Red Cat, QuantumScape) were breaking down one by one. The key message: delayed reaction is the primary strategy now, because stocks have rallied into earnings and need "beyond extraordinary" results to keep going. The best earning plays are neglected stocks, not extended ones. Meta was the standout — a reversal bullish setup discussed at the Frankfurt boot camp that gapped up $8 on earnings. The PM session was brief: AC (short), Blue Owl (delayed reaction), SoundHound (9M breakdown), Johnson & Johnson (delayed reaction bullish), and ARRY (start of swing in tech) were the handful of setups.

Judy's essay: cautiously bullish, T2108 dropped from 57.98 to 55.61, 20% study approaching extreme levels. Small caps pulled back with range expansion to downside.

Pradeep's override:

> "The 20% study is approaching extreme levels, but we have seen in this market it tends to go even more extreme. At the same time, you see a lot of stocks breaking down."

> "Slowly, slowly, the breadth is deteriorating. But with a smaller breadth, the market can still keep going up."

### Earnings Season = Delayed Reaction Focus

> "Your best bet in earnings is always something which has been neglected for at least a month or two, and then there is a surprisingly good earnings."

> "Everything is priced in. Every stock has rallied into earnings. They need to pull a big rabbit out of their hat for them to keep going."
