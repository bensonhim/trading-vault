# Tariff Day Volatility — Get Popcorn, Not Positions

> "Buy the biggest box of diapers from Costco and be ready. There will be volatility and there will be blood. Only make sure that it's not your blood."

Trump's 25% tariffs on Canada/Mexico and 10% on China sent markets gap-down Monday. Pradeep's stance: protect capital, wait for delayed reactions, don't be a hero. The day turned into a masterclass on **setup clarity** — when members call AbbVie and T "delayed reaction EPs," it exposes the #1 fixable problem in trading.

---

## AM Meeting (Situational Awareness) — Drama Chapter One

### Tariff Shock and the Popcorn Strategy

- 25% tariffs on Canada/Mexico, 10% on China confirmed Feb 1
- T2108 declined from 50.6 to 45.23, 20% study leans bearish
- VIX spiked; all indices sold off into close + after-hours
- Crypto sold off with the broader market
- > "This is one chapter — the first chapter of the drama, and there'll be several chapters"

### Government Policy Change EP

- If tariffs stick, this is a **government policy change EP** — these tend to be enduring
- But Trump is unpredictable: "Nobody knows what is in Trump's mind"
- If market reaction is very negative, he may "declare victory" and reverse → whipsaw
- > "If some stock is going to be negatively affected, it will show up in your 9M EP with a lot of volume"

### Controlled Risk Taking

- > "Protect yourself. Don't be a hero here. Controlled risk taking."
- Don't let political opinions influence trading
- Best approach: watch and wait like a bird, pick up worms when safe, go back
- Focus on triple ETFs — high correlation, everything moves together
- For swing: wait for volatility to die down, then look for delayed reactions
- For day trade: possible, but only with controlled size

## Key Takeaways

1. **Government policy EPs are real but unpredictable** — tariffs create volatility, not clarity. Wait for delayed reactions, don't front-run policy
2. **Protect capital first** — "controlled risk taking, always win control" on tariff/volatility days
3. **Setup clarity is the #1 fixable problem** — if you're calling AbbVie a delayed reaction EP, you've lost the plot. Write your own setup guides
4. **EP + 9M + momentum burst** — the boot camp presenters who made triple digits all traded these three setups. Differentiator: size and clarity
5. **Chinese stocks may benefit** from tariff reshuffling — watch for delayed reaction setups there
6. **Volatility is good for day traders, bad for swing traders** — wait for volatility to compress before swing entries
7. **Don't confuse a gap-down reversal with a bottom bounce** — different setups, different risk profiles
