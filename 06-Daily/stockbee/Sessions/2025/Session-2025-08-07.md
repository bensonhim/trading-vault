# Gap and Crap: Russia-Ukraine News Fades, Breakouts Fail, and SA Is Your Religion

> Market gapped up on Russia-Ukraine peace news — then faded all day. Breakouts got chopped. Only [[DUOL]] (SIPs) and [[NBIS]] (delayed reaction) worked. The lesson: situational awareness is your religion, or you get chopped.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Gap Up on Flimsy News: The Trade Is to Fade

> "The gap is happening because, apparently, the war between Russia and Ukraine is going to get over. When did the market start caring about this war?"

Pradeep's immediate reaction: **the gap may fade**. Semi tariff exemptions also fueling the gap, but no conviction on breakouts.

> "Breakouts are not likely to work. Some select breakouts always work. My focus remains delayed reaction, 9M EP with immediate catalyst."

### Bearish Follow-Through Still Dominant

40 stocks showing bearish follow-through on the scan. Much better follow-through on the short side than the long side. Slow deterioration continues but no "get me out" urgency.

### Duolingo: The SIPs Masterclass

Pradeep bought [[DUOL]] in after-hours at ~$394 after seeing earnings. Key insights:

- Analyzed past reactions, short interest, projected $450-500 target
- Entered after-hours, sold 80% near the top within minutes
- Remaining 20% stopped out with slippage — fast market reality

> "SIPs is not something I like doing because it requires you to be really paying attention and making quick decisions."

### NBIS: High-Capitalization Caution

> "I would definitely look at it as a delayed reaction kind of setup. But the float is 235M, capitalization is 13B — may not make a fast move."

Decided to treat it as SIPs (quick in/out), not EP (hold). Stock went to $70 instantly then faded — validating the decision.

### Situational Awareness: Your Religion or Your Roller Coaster

Powerful extended analogy: trading without SA is like a roller coaster — up, down, up, down. With SA, it's wait-go-wait-go.

> "I had taken my account up almost doubled, tripled my account and I took it down. I felt like shit. Never going to happen again. Then I created this whole situational awareness model."

> "If your belief is situational awareness is for fools, then you will buy any random breakout anytime. It's a question of belief."

### 71% of Stocks Making New Lows by End of Session

Overwhelming evidence of broad-based selling. Gap completely faded. Only [[LMND]] held gains as gap-and-go.

## 2 PM Meeting

### Gap and Crap Confirmed: No Buyers

> "71% of stocks making new lows for the day. Except for Shankar, I don't think anybody is going long this market."

Everything that gapped up faded. [[HOOD]] faded, [[AIRBNB]] faded, [[SOXL]] faded. The Russia-Ukraine news created a trap for breakout buyers.

### The Jesse Livermore Warning

> "You are standing on a rail track and you see an impending train coming. Do you sit there and start drinking water, or do you just run away?"

Broad-based institutional selling visible. Not retail — systematic selling at elevated levels.

### Short Candidates Identified

[[ENX]], [[AI]], [[TXN]], [[DIA]], [[ROKU]], [[SPXL]], [[DIS]], [[WFC]], [[PAY]] — all actionable shorts.

> "If you don't want to listen to situational awareness, you're going to get chopped."

### Market Rotation Not Yet Convincing

Even stocks with good catalysts ([[DOCN]], [[AAPL]]) getting hit. Gap-up news created more short setups than long opportunities.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Gap and crap** — news-driven gaps in a deteriorating market fade; don't buy breakouts
2. **SIPs require execution skill** — pre-planning, after-hours entry, quick exits; not for beginners
3. **NBIS: high cap = delayed reaction, not EP** — 13B market cap limits fast moves
4. **Situational awareness is your religion** — without it, you're on a roller coaster
5. **71% stocks making new lows** — institutional selling, not retail
6. **Shorts still working** — semiconductor breakdown theme intact, delayed reaction shorts favored
