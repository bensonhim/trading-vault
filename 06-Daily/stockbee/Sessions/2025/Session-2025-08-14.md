# Immediate Follow-Through Is Key — Delayed Reaction Shorts Must Show Weakness Fast, Lemonade Complete, 20% Study at 157

**20% study at 157 — extremely elevated, historically signals pullback.** Pradeep's major research finding: **for delayed reaction shorts to work, they must show immediate follow-through within 3 days.** If they go sideways, it's a trap. "The one which worked — they immediately start going down. Third day, they're down. That's the best one." Lemonade fully exited at ~44% profit (bought $51.76, sold 80% at $56.83, remaining at $55). TNA stop moved to breakeven. DNA stopped out at breakeven. Market rotation: **"They are selling the winners and buying the losers."** Near-high stocks not following through. Dustbin stocks rallying = short covering. **Write your guide** — Pradeep demands 25+ examples per setup type, handwritten.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Breakouts Working — But 20% Study at 157, Extremely Frothy

> "20% study is at 157. The levels which are elevated like this have a tendency in the next 5-10 days of leading to a pullback."

> "Breakouts are working, yes. But the best time to trade is also when things are elevated. You have to trade, but with one leg in the exit door."

### Rotation: Selling Winners, Buying Losers

> "They are selling the winners and they're buying the losers. These stocks are where they are for a reason — bad earnings, something wrong. It's just short covering."

> "Near-high stocks are going down. Near-high stocks are not really making a move. The stocks which are extended — they are not really attracting buyers."

### Start of a Swing — 2025 Is the Year of Start of a Swing

> "So far, 2025 is a year where start of a swing is working more than continuation setup."

> "If you cannot identify start of a swing, you should not be even trading. Find some other business."

### Earnings Don't Drive Price — Context and "Spice" Do

> "Only 15 stocks out of 339 have earnings/sales growth of 39+. Earnings and sales don't drive price behavior. The spice they add along with the earnings announcement — that is what is important."

> "Monday.com — earnings were not bad, 38% growth, 24% sales. Why did the stock sell off? Because they said margins are under pressure. Context matters."

### Short Side — Urgency to Sell

> "If there is an earnings miss and within five days that stock starts going down, that tends to work very well. When they go sideways for a long time, it can become a risky short."

> "If it is going to go down, you see urgency to sell."

### Manage Existing Positions — Yesterday Was the Time

> "The time to get aggressive was yesterday. I'm not even looking at long at this stage. I'm just moving stops, taking profit, locking in profit."

### CAVA / Kava — North Brunswick Curse Returns

> "Any restaurant company which opens in North Brunswick, their stock goes down. Kava is getting taken to the toilet."

### Neglect Is Critical for SIPs

> "The neglect part is so important. Reddit had neglect of almost six months. That's why Reddit did so well. Lemonade was neglected before it moved."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Schizophrenic Market — Be Selective

> "When it just looks like the market is going to overcome its choppiness, it's back. Two days ago, inflation was not a problem. Now today, inflation is a big problem."

> "If the market is schizophrenic, you have to look for only the best opportunities and not go for B and C class setups."

### Breakouts NOT Following Through Again

> "Most of the breakouts that day, which look good — Goldman Sachs, Meta — they didn't really follow through. They're just sitting there doing nothing."

> "Yesterday's 600+ day, you want people falling all over themselves to buy. Instead, near-high stocks are under pressure."

### Delayed Reaction Shorts — Key Research Finding

> "The one which worked — they immediately start going down. Third day, they're down. That's the best one."

> "If they go sideways for more than three days, it tends to not really follow through. That's a trap."

> "The weak stocks — they just don't even try to rally. Dow Chemical: third day, it starts going down and never looked back. Chipotle: third day, it never looked back."

### Immediate Follow-Through = Best Shorts

> "Study hundreds of examples and you will come to the same conclusion: if you want to make money on delayed reaction EP, look for the ones which immediately have follow-through."

> "After the gap down, if there is no buyer, that's the best sign. They are so weak they can't even bounce for one or two days."

### Write Your Guide — 25 Examples Per Setup

> "Write your own guide — handwritten guide with at least 25 examples of start of a swing. If you're going to trade delayed reaction EP, 25 examples guide."

> "For my first five years, every day in the morning, I used to rewrite my guides. I had hundreds of booklets with 100-500 examples."

### Five-Star Trades — Confluence of Catalysts

> "Dow Jones had 9M breakdown. I researched it at night: negative growth rate, making losses for next 3-4 quarters, 2,000 funds to 1,500. Everything was lining up. That's a five-star trade."

> "ENPH — Trump is negative on solar, 2,300 funds to 1,283, margins getting squeezed. Multiple catalysts = higher conviction = larger position size."

### Catalyst-Based vs Pattern-Based — Two Paths

> "If you want to trade singles (pattern-based), you need to be a pattern expert. If you want to trade home runs (catalyst-based), you need to be a catalyst expert."

> "Both can make money. Catalyst-based are better because you have higher conviction."

### Lemonade — Complete Trade

> "Lemonade, I bought at $51.76 as a delayed reaction. Sold 80% at $56.83. Made my stop at $55 on the remaining. I'm happy with that. It did 44%. Most of the catalyst gets priced in within a few days."

### Chipotle / Restaurant Stocks — Party Is Over

> "Chipotle — terrible earnings, long way to go. Restaurant stocks, the party is over."

> "When you get rid of illegal immigrants, what's going to happen to the restaurant business?"

### TNA — Home Run Trade Management

> "If you took TNA as a swing trade, you would have taken profit at 80% and moved your stop. If you took it as a home run, you move your stop to breakeven and let it run."

> "To hit home runs, you need a personality. A lot of time, they'll go up 20% and just end up going down."

### Short Side Nuance — Gap Down Next Day Is Best

> "On shorts, a lot of the action happens very fast. You can have $15 profit and by the time your stop gets hit, you get a $4-5 bad fill."

> "For shorts, the best setup is: 9M breakdown, next day it starts going down. That's what you want. Not something that goes sideways for a week."
