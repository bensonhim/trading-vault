# No Setups Day — Choppiness After AI Marathon

The market delivered a day with zero viable setups — after a marathon AI-driven run, semiconductor and mega-cap names pulled back but not with enough conviction for either side. Pradeep's entire thesis was simple: if you don't see a setup, you don't trade. Both sessions reinforced that discipline beats desperation.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Cautiously Bullish with Deteriorating Margins

The morning essay set the tone: cautiously bullish, but the 20% study was over 100 (overheating), primary and secondary indicators were green with "deteriorating margins signaling weakness," and the market monitor had a 400+ red day. The S&P and Nasdaq were at record highs, but underneath, non-technology stocks were already in pullback mode.

> "And it's a bull market driven by AI as a team. Short term, lots of AI team stocks are extended and were able to pull back or stalling."

### The November 2020 Parallel

Pradeep drew a direct comparison to the **November 2020 – February 2021** period, where the 20% study stayed elevated for months. That was the COVID meme-stock era (GME, AMC, stocks that "don't even exist now"). The key difference this time: AI has fundamental backing, but the quantitative pattern of extended breadth is the same.

> "The closest parallel to this is November '21 to February 2022, where 20% study remained elevated."

### IPO Count as the Real Bubble Indicator

One of the session's most important concepts: **IPO count** is the genuine bubble signal, not price levels. During the COVID peak, ~1,200 SPACs IPO'd in 3 months. Pre-COVID, ~5,000 stocks traded; it spiked to 7,000. That was the bubble. Currently, IPO volume is not at bubble levels.

> "When do IPOs come in the market? When it is easy to raise money, when people are crazy. When the IPO madness starts, then you can claim that there is a bubble."

### Broken Breadth Indicators

Every traditional breadth indicator has stopped working reliably in the last five years. The "50% in a month" indicator (stocks up 50% in a month above 20) used to be a near-certain correction signal. Now it stays elevated for months. The sheer volume of money flowing into the market has changed the velocity of moves.

> "Every single market breadth indicator, which used to work very well, keeps breaking in the last five years."

### Earnings Season: Shorts Working, DEPs Not Appearing

This earnings season produced numerous short-side opportunities (Whirlpool, Netflix, Lululemon, Goodyear Tire) — many went straight down without offering DEP entries. The shorts are working, but the classic DEP setup is absent.

> "Goodyear Tire also from earnings straight down. I was thinking that Goodyear Tire will give a delayed reaction. Just went straight down."

> "A lot of them, they start winding down after earnings. There is no even opportunity for delayed reaction. Straight down."

### SIPs vs EP: QUBT as a Cautionary Example

[[QUBT]] was highlighted as a textbook **SIPs (Stocks in Play)**, NOT an EP. The stock rallied 44% into earnings, but at $288K revenue, it's pure speculation. The critical distinction:

| Criteria | SIPs | EP |
|----------|------|----|
| Catalyst | News/hype driven | Genuine earnings catalyst |
| Neglect | No — already discovered | Yes — market hasn't noticed |
| Volume | Spike on speculation | Sustained institutional buying |
| Pattern | Gap up, no base | Breakout from consolidation |

> "If you don't understand the difference between SIPs and EP, then you're going to hold a lot of SIPs as EP."

> "At 10 AM, if you're asking 'what is EP?', there is a problem. Where is the neglect?"

### SMR: A Failed EP Example

SMR was dissected as a **failed EP** — sloppy action, multiple failed breakout attempts. Each time the stock tried to break out, it failed. The lesson: **look for clean breakouts**, not repeated failures.

> "You went on a date. Nothing happened. You got excited. Underwear flying. Third time you go. I look for clean breakouts. When there is a clean breakout, it's much easier."

### RocketLab: The Clean Setup Contrast

RocketLab was the model setup: catalyst, sideways consolidation, then clean breakout = 44% in two days. This is what EP traders should wait for.

> "The catalyst was good. There was a sideways move. That's why in just two days, the stock made 45%. That's what you want to see — cleaner breakout."

### Position Management: Respecting Stops

All open positions were stopped out — NVIDIA, TXN, RocketLab. Pradeep had moved stops aggressively because these were extended moves. The key principle:

> "You had to respect your stops."

> "I had moved my stop very aggressively because it was 40% in two days. I had taken profit yesterday and moved the stop that got hit."

### The Algorithm Momentum Problem

Pradeep identified a structural market issue: quantitative/trend-following algorithms pile into the same momentum stocks. When all funds own the same names, exits get blocked, and the result is a stampede.

> "Every quant trader just keeps buying momentum stock, and then all the funds are in the same stocks, and then the exit gets blocked. They take a blood bath. That's called the stampede."

### Deep Buyers vs. Setup Discipline

Deep buyers came in aggressively in the morning but started fading by mid-session. Despite the buying interest, no EP 9M setups appeared.

> "Will I be a buyer of EP 9M? Yes. If it is a good setup. If it is a good setup, yes."

> "I don't see anything which I would get excited on long or short side at the moment."

### Sector Divergence: Non-Technology Already Correcting

Underneath the AI narrative, financials and non-tech names were already in pullback: JPMorgan, Home Depot, Bank of America, PNC, Wells Fargo, Citigroup. The divergence was clear — AI held up, everything else deteriorated.

> "The non-technology stocks are all already in pullback mode. Financials, JPMorgan Chase, things like that."

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### Zero Setups — The Whole Message

The PM session reinforced the morning thesis with even more clarity. Running scans produced nothing actionable. The only scan hit was [[KO]] (Coca-Cola), which Pradeep classified as **liquid lava** — highly liquid, institutionally owned, but unlikely to produce meaningful moves.

> "I don't see anything which I think is viable. If you look at things, as I said in the morning, everything was put on very close stops, and I got stopped out. I don't see any reason to get into anything currently because I don't see setups."

### Coca-Cola: Liquid Lava Classification

[[KO]] was the only breakout candidate, but Pradeep immediately dismissed it as a setup worth trading:

> "Coca-Cola, which is breaking out. It's owned by 2,500 funds. It's a boring stock which is not going to make big moves. That's liquid lava."

> "It's called liquid lava. It's a liquid stock which trades that kind of volume every day. That's a 1.94% dollar breakout. Know your setup, know your setup, know your setup."

### Setup Clarity Is the Foundation

The PM session's core message, repeated in multiple forms:

> "Setup clarity is the foundation for being successful."

> "If I don't see a setup, I don't see a setup."

> "The answer is always wait for a setup. Do you see a viable setup? If you don't see a viable setup, just wait."

### Healthcare "Rotation" — Dismissed

When asked about healthcare rotation, Pradeep pointed out that the stocks people called "rotating into" had already been going up for a month. That's not rotation — that's late discovery.

> "Is that what rotation is called? This is where this stock started going up. After that, UnitedHealth earnings. This is not rotation. If you are talking about this is rotation, I think you are on the wrong track."

### No Short Setups Either

Semi stocks weren't breaking down on high volume — just "pinprick" selling. Not enough conviction for shorts.

> "There are no real breakdowns at the moment. It's just pinprick kind of selling. They might transition into a sideways kind of move."

> "I don't think it is time to go aggressively short the semiconductor because there is not enough selling."

### Retail Fully Loaded, Institutions Taking Profit

> "I can assure you that mom and pop is not selling. Every single email I have, every single call I'm getting from people I had not been in touch with, is 'I'm fully loaded. What do you think?' This is the institution taking money off. Retail is fully committed."

### After-Hours Stops: Recipe for Disaster

Pradeep gave a clear warning about stops that trigger in after-hours:

> "If you are putting stops which move in after hours, you are going to get chopped. That's a recipe for disaster."

### Choppiness Is Expected After a Marathon

The day's price action — no conviction on either side — was exactly what Pradeep expected after an extended run. The transition could go either way: more selling, deep buyers return and push higher, or sideways chop.

> "After the choppiness, you will see some setups, and new setups. At the moment, do you see a setup? No. But everybody wants to press their luck."

## Key Takeaways

1. **No setup = no trade.** This was the day's entire thesis, stated in both sessions. If your scan produces nothing, you sit on your hands.
2. **Know the difference between SIPs and EP.** QUBT is a SIPs (speculation, no neglect, no base), not an EP. Confusing the two leads to holding garbage.
3. **IPO count is the real bubble indicator.** When IPOs surge (2000+ in months), that's a bubble. Currently not at that level.
4. **Clean breakouts only.** SMR's multiple failed EPs illustrate why repeated failures are not a setup. RocketLab's clean consolidation-to-breakout is the model.
5. **Liquid lava stocks (KO) don't make you money.** High institutional ownership + low volatility = boring, not actionable.
6. **Respect your stops.** All three open positions (NVIDIA, TXN, RocketLab) were stopped out. Moved aggressively, but part of the design.
7. **Broken breadth indicators.** The 50%/month and 20% study signals that used to reliably call corrections now stay elevated for months. The market's velocity has changed.
8. **Algorithm crowding creates stampede risk.** When all quant funds pile into the same momentum stocks, exits get blocked. This is a structural feature of the current market.
9. **Retail is fully loaded, institutions are taking profit.** When people who've never asked about the market start calling to say they're "fully loaded," that's a contrarian signal.
10. **After-hours stops = disaster.** Only active stops during market hours; after-hours gaps will chop you out.
