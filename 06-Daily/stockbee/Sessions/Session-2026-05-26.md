# Think in Setups, Not Stocks

> "The day you stop thinking in stocks and start thinking in setups, a lot of things will happen."

Three consecutive 300+ breadth days signal a **trending market**, but the 20% Study nearing 99 flashes yellow — overheating likely ahead. Pradeep drives home one message above all: **trade setups, not stocks**. Anticipation traps abound after a gap-up open, and the decision-making framework — not FOMO — must govern every entry.

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### Market Context: Trending but Overheating

- **Bullish breadth thrust**: 3rd consecutive 300+ day (500 → 385 → 339)
- **20% Study approaching 99**: Yellow light — euphoric readings typically lead to a dip within days
- **Gap up after 3 up days**: "Invitation for profit taking" — many stocks opened higher then faded
- **Iran "deal" at 95%**: Pradeep's analogy — "It's like negotiating with Jennifer Lopez for a date; 95% agreed, only 5% remaining — she has to say yes"

> "Gap after three days of moves is always an invitation for profit taking."

### Primary Focus: EP 9M, DEP, Sugar Babies

Pradeep's plan: **slow and deliberate risk-taking** on A+ quality EP 9M and delayed reaction setups, selling into strength.

Key video references (which few members watched):
- How to trade delayed reactions
- EP 9M family
- Sugar Baby scan and process

> "A lot of you want to make money, and some have dreams — I'm just going to have a job and spend half an hour and make money. It is possible — but only if you have detailed understanding of what you are doing."

### The Machiavelli Lesson: Be Both Lion and Fox

Pradeep references *The Prince* by Niccolò Machiavelli — the prince must be both **lion** (courage) and **fox** (trap awareness):

> "Lions have courage, but they cannot avoid traps. Fox knows all the traps, but doesn't have courage. You have to avoid all the traps."

**Market traps identified**:
- Anticipation setups = "lady boy" — looks attractive, traps you
- Bull flags after extended moves = institutions use gap-ups as liquidity events
- Consolidation breakouts without volume = heartbreak hotel

> "Anticipation setups are such a tease. They call you: come, come, come here. And then you see a very beautiful girl, and you get very excited, and then you find that's a lady boy."

### EP 9M = Elephant Footprints (Institutional Buying)

The most important concept of the session: **EP 9M reveals where institutions are aggressive**.

- 244M shares traded on a single stock = institutional signature
- You don't need a paid service to find institutional buying — your EP 9M scan tells you
- When institutional quality stocks (5000+ funds) show EP 9M, that's the clearest signal

> "Nothing is going to tell you institutional buying like EP 9M. But we don't want to believe that. We want some service which will tell us this is institutional buying."

### AVGO: The Third-Time Trap

[[AVGO]] was the most discussed stock — a large-cap sugar baby owned by 5000+ funds, but with **3 failed breakout attempts**:

> "One time, she kicked you in the balls. Second time, she threw your underwear. Third time, she almost threw you from the 3rd floor. And fourth time, you say — here is a rose, and I'm here again."

Pradeep wants to see **volume surge** before entering AVGO. Decision framework > FOMO.

### Carvana: Sugar Baby Working

[[CVNA]] was identified Friday as a sugar baby. It's working well — Pradeep holds a full position (not 80/20) because it's a high-priced, clean setup.

> "If you follow the methodology, what is Carvana? Carvana is a sugar baby. It showed up in your scan. Very good setup."

### Biotech EP = One-Day Wonders

> "If you study biotech EP 9M, almost all will be one-day wonders. Everything happens on the first or second day, and then that's it."

## PM Session (3 PM)

### Think in Setups, Not Stocks

The PM session reinforces the AM's core message with laser focus:

> "My number one advice for anybody who wants to make money is to think in setups, not stocks."

> "You are trading a setup. You are not trading a stock."

- Is there a setup? → Get excited
- No setup? → Don't get excited, don't discuss
- Random stock questions during trading hours = "asking on the production line where to put this screw"

### Wire: The Clean Setup Example

[[WIRE]] was used as a teaching example of what a proper setup looks like:
- Catalyst: AI + earnings
- EP 9M volume present
- Range expansion (start of swing)
- Clean entry, no ambiguity

> "How difficult is it to understand this? This is the start of a swing."

### Trade Factory Process

Pradeep walked through his full trade factory scan sequence:
1. **EP 9M** → categorize into liquid lava, dogs, and cat
2. **Combination bullish** → filter by volume (1M+ shares traded)
3. **Sugar Babies** → look for continuation or pullback entries
4. **Reversal bullish** → sorted by volume, independent of market regime
5. **Anticipation watchlist** → last, and with extreme caution

> "Everything comes from the trade factory. Nothing which I do doesn't come from the trade factory."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Think in setups, not stocks** — this is the #1 rule repeated across both sessions
2. **20% Study at 99 = yellow light** — euphoric readings precede dips; manage size accordingly
3. **Gap up after 3 up days = profit-taking invitation** — don't chase the open
4. **EP 9M reveals institutional buying** — the "elephant footprints" signal; no paid service needed
5. **Anticipation setups are traps** — "lady boy" analogy: looks attractive, but will hurt you
6. **Consolidation breakouts need C+V** — catalyst AND volume; without volume, it's heartbreak hotel
7. **Multiple failed breakouts = skepticism** — AVGO failed 3 times; wait for volume surge
8. **Sugar babies work both ways** — they give happiness, but when they dump, they make you miserable
9. **Decision framework > FOMO** — create thumb rules, stay within your framework
10. **Reversal bullish is regime-independent** — works in any market condition
11. **Biotech EP = one-day wonders** — almost no follow-through after day 1-2
12. **Trade factory is the process** — scans → setup identification → consideration set → decision → execution
