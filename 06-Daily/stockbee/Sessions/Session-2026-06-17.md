# Situational Awareness, Setup Clarity, and the Four-Factor Fix

Pradeep used a choppy, post-marathon market day to deliver a masterclass on situational awareness (SA), setup qualification, and — in the PM session — a deeply original framework for changing trading behavior through belief revision. The market was transitioning from a strong thrust into range-bound chop; breakouts were failing, momentum leaders were fading, and sugar babies were catching cold. Only two EP 9M setups passed the filter: **T** (solar/AI theme) and **BULL** (three consecutive EP 9M). The rest of the day was teaching: how to read the market's language, how to identify a real continuation setup, and how to change your beliefs when your trading isn't working.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: The Single Question

Pradeep opened with the core framework: SA answers one question — **"Are breakouts likely to work for the next three to five days?"** If you can't answer with conviction, you don't trade breakouts.

> "Whatever can make you answer this question is what your situational awareness should be based on."

SA is not one indicator — it's a **confluence of factors**: the 20% Study, 4% breakouts/breakdowns, follow-through on your own trades, and whether yesterday's EP 9M stocks are holding or breaking down.

### The Market Read: Transition to Chop

The key observation was that stocks which had made 3–5 day moves from Thursday were not transitioning into orderly low-volume pullbacks. Instead, they were showing **momentum bursts to the downside** — some with 9% breakdowns.

> "If they had transitioned into low volume consolidation pullback, I would have said another leg. But what has happened? Stock after stock — what is happening here? They are not transitioning into orderly."

This is the tell: when breakouts from prior days **fail to consolidate sideways** and instead reverse violently, the market regime has shifted from trending to choppy/range-bound.

### Sugar Babies as Leading Indicators

> "Whenever you see the sugar babies starting to have a cold or a flu, that means the market is going to have COVID."

On Thursday, sugar babies were standing at attention. By Monday/Tuesday, they were fading. This is early information — the market's high-beta names are losing sponsorship before the indices confirm weakness.

### The Three-to-Five Day Cycle

Pradeep referenced his weekend "London Calling" video where he anticipated the current transition:

> "I was expecting that they will only work for three to five days. Because the context of the market was different. Here, the context was the market had made a crazy move."

After a marathon (18% in one month), the market needs to rest. The analogy: **how many times can you run a marathon back-to-back?**

### Focus: EP, DEP, Selective

> "My focus will remain on EP, DEP, but I'll be more selective. I'm not going to take my wallet out. I'm going to hide my wallet."

The only acceptable setups today: **EP 9M with genuine catalyst**, DEP, and maybe sugar babies — but only if something "grabs me by the neck."

### The OODA Loop

Pradeep shared that "situational awareness" as a concept came from his time at DHL/FedEx — it's from the airline industry. The **OODA loop** (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) was developed for fighter pilots. 99% of plane crashes happen not from mechanical failure but from the pilot losing situational awareness. Same in trading.

### Continuation Setup Teaching

Pradeep showed a textbook continuation setup and contrasted it with what someone was calling a "continuation":

> "This is what the continuation setup looks like. First leg was 18%. Orderly three day pullback and breaking out. It meets every definition. Now tell me — by what imagination do you see THIS looking like that?"

The key characteristics of a real continuation: **linearity**, **tight pullback**, **clear breakout**. Without these, it's not a continuation — it's random noise.

### Catalyst Research Process

> "Organize like crazy, do the work, put the catalyst."

Pradeep demonstrated his daily process: when a stock appears in EP 9M, he immediately researches the catalyst (via Finviz), puts it in his notes, and then never has to research it again when it shows up later as a DEP or continuation. This one-minute-per-stock habit compounds over time.

### CPC Story — Focus on Setup, Not Macro

> "PCB typed CPI as a ticker. He said, 'I can't short CPI.' That guy makes millions of dollars."

The point: don't get distracted by macro data (CPI, Fed meetings). Focus on setups and SA.

Pradeep reinforced the morning's read: many of the recent stories are done. The market made a marathon move; now it consolidates or pulls back.

The "three EP 9M in a row" rule: when a stock prints EP 9M three times in ten days, it's likely also a sugar baby, and the market is telling you something significant.

### Male Drone Ant vs. 64 Kamasutra Positions

> "I believe in being a male drone ant rather than practicing 64 Kamasutra positions."

The male drone ant has one purpose — execute it well. Pradeep's one purpose: **trade for magnitude**, not duration. Pick one or two high-conviction setups and hit them hard, instead of spreading across dozens of mediocre trades.

### Setup Qualification: Who Will Buy After You?

> "Just because stock shows up in the scan, it is not a setup. Setup has specific conditions, and you also want to see: who is likely to buy it? Are there enough people lining up to buy after this?"

Before entering any breakout, ask: **"Who can I palm this off to on day 2, 3, 4?"** If there's no clear pool of future buyers, skip it.

### Anticipation Setup — Don't Be That Person

A member asked about Intel as an anticipation setup. Pradeep was emphatic:

> "Do you see Brad Pitt there? This is NOT an anticipation setup. This is nowhere close to an anticipation setup."

He showed a textbook anticipation setup: tight consolidation, multiple T-days, then breakout. The contrast was stark — Intel had none of those characteristics.

> "Substitute your girlfriend's photo with this chart. This is what you want to find."

### The Four-Factor Model: Why Trades Fail

Pradeep introduced a diagnostic framework for when trades don't work:

| Factor | Problem | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Setup** | Wrong setup inherently | Buying [[HOOD]] on day 5 of a swing — not an SOS |
| **Process** | Right setup, wrong execution | Bought [[FRMI]] with conviction, sold after 3 days of no move — then it took off |
| **SA** | Perfect setup, wrong market regime | Everything aligns but the market is in chop mode |
| **Trader** | Psychology/behavior problem | Selling too early, not following stops, FOMO entries |

> "If it is a trader problem, you fire the trader and get a new trader."

### The Belief Change Framework (Extended Teaching)

This was the most significant PM segment. Pradeep laid out a complete model for behavioral change:

**New Information → New Insight → Reflection → Changed Belief → Changed Behavior**

He used several analogies:

1. **Bloodletting** — For centuries, people believed bleeding cured diseases. New scientific discovery (germ theory) changed beliefs, which changed medical practice.
2. **Smoking** — In the 60s/70s, everyone smoked. New information (cancer link) changed beliefs, which changed behavior.
3. **Personal story** — During COVID, he was overweight, reflected, hired a trainer the next day. Reflection → action.
4. **Moving to New York** — If you grow up in a small Alabama town and move to NYC, your beliefs either shatter or you run back home. New environment = fastest belief change.

> "Beliefs change people's behavior. In order to change beliefs, you need new information and new insight. Then you reflect on it. And that changes your belief."

### Practical Application: EP 9M Gap vs. Non-Gap

A member asked about [[QBTS]] — a sugar baby with EP 9M that faded in the last hour. Pradeep used this to illustrate belief change:

> "In EP 9M, it is my observation — I prefer non-gap or small gap. When there is a gap, there has to be a really dramatic reason for it."

His advice: don't take this on faith. **Do a deep dive** — go back one month, compare gap vs. non-gap EP 9M outcomes. Study hundreds of examples. The evidence will change your belief, which will change your behavior.

### The 20% Study: Why and How

When asked how to identify which 20% movers to trade, Pradeep explained the study's purpose:

> "The objective of the 20% Study is to understand HOW 20% moves happen."

Study the moves. How did they start? With a 4% breakout. Was there a fresh catalyst? Was there high volume? Then **classify**: which patterns are easily identifiable at the start? Which are not? Discard the ones you can't identify in real-time. Create templates from the ones you can. That's how setup clarity develops.

> "I printed out 40,000 charts of stocks that made 25% moves in a month. I sorted them into piles — this looks like what I want to trade, this doesn't. And I made templates."

### Surprise Is Eternal

> "There is always going to be surprise. Businesses get surprises. Countries get surprises. The EP 9M is a filter that tells you this surprise is big — focus on this surprise."

Even in a choppy/range-bound market, catalysts create independent moves. The methodology (EP 9M, DEP, SIPs) is designed to catch these surprises regardless of market direction.

### Stop Placement Advice

> "If you are getting continuously hit with half-of-the-day stops, keep your stop at low of the day. Adjust your position size accordingly."

If you're getting stopped out repeatedly, investigate: Is it a setup problem? A stock selection problem? Sometimes it's just bad luck (like [[ONDS]]). But if everything is stopping you out, go back and examine what's wrong with your selection.

## Key Takeaways

1. **SA answers one question**: "Are breakouts likely to work?" Build a multi-factor model to answer it — don't rely on a single indicator.
2. **After a marathon, expect chop**: When 3–5 day movers show momentum bursts to downside instead of orderly pullbacks, the regime has shifted. Reduce size or sit out.
3. **Sugar babies are the canary**: When they catch a cold, the market gets COVID.
4. **Buy Start of Swing, not day five**: The SOS concept is non-negotiable. If you're buying on the 4th or 5th day of a swing, you're entering a "male drone ant" trade with no purpose.
5. **Three EP 9M in a row = market knows something**: This is a high-conviction signal. The stock is also likely a sugar baby.
6. **Qualify every setup**: Catalyst + who will buy after you + SOS confirmation. Without all three, it's not a trade.
7. **Prefer non-gap or small-gap EP 9M**: Gap ups without dramatic catalyst tend to fade. Do the deep dive — study hundreds of examples.
8. **The Four-Factor Diagnostic**: When a trade fails, investigate which of the four (Setup, Process, SA, Trader) went wrong. Don't blame the market.
9. **Change beliefs through evidence**: New Information → Insight → Reflection → Changed Belief → Changed Behavior. Don't take Pradeep's word — do the deep dive yourself.
10. **Process compounds**: OLC (Organize Like Crazy). Research catalysts when they appear, log them, never re-research. One minute per stock, daily, builds a compounding knowledge base.
11. **Surprise is eternal**: Regardless of market regime, catalysts create independent moves. EP 9M/DEP methodology catches these surprises.
12. **20% Study purpose**: Not to find any 20% mover, but to understand HOW they start — what preceded them, what made them identifiable, and which templates to build for real-time recognition.
