### OPG Is a TACTIC, Not a Setup

> "OPG is a tactic. You can do OPG like crazy and lose your money because you're just buying random stock as OPG. OPG — we do it on a stock which has a real catalyst."

Critical distinction: OPG is the execution method, not the thesis. Without a real catalyst, OPG is random gambling.

### CRCL — Best Trade of the Year

> "That CRCL, I played it with CIRCA which was 87%, 90%, 100% move. That was the best trade of the year."

Someone asked about CRCL as a delayed EP when it was already up 64%. Pradeep's response:

> "Saying CRCL is a DEP is like saying 'I just found out we are pregnant and the kid is in first grade.'"

The catalyst was earlier. The entry was earlier. You missed it — don't chase.

### Love at First Sight — Quality Setups Only

> "There should be no doubt that this is a good quality setup when you see it, in one glance. Love at first sight."

Pradeep refuses to get involved in setups where confidence isn't overwhelming. BMNR? Five failed breakouts. Low quality. Skip. Focus where evidence is overwhelming.

> "I want to be involved where there is overwhelming evidence in every factor. After that, if the setup doesn't work, that's just the nature of the game. But I controlled what I could control."

### Market Doesn't Take War Seriously

> "If the market took the war seriously, we'd be down 20. The market is just ignoring the war or doesn't care about it."

Liquidity thesis remains: COVID money never withdrawn, tax cuts add more. Money flows to stocks regardless of geopolitics.

### Focus on Two Things Only

1. **Biotech** — only sector with real follow-through
2. **Stocks with recent catalyst** — earnings, insider buys, clinical trials

Everything else: secondary reminder, not actionable.

### The ONE Indicator People Get Wrong

> "There's only one indicator which you are getting wrong, and that indicator is situational awareness."

Multiple traders told Pradeep they're getting chopped. His answer is always the same: the environment doesn't support breakouts, so stop buying them. As soon as the bell rings, situational awareness goes out the window.

### Having a Fat Coach

> "It's like having a fat coach who tells you to eat less, exercise more, and who can't get up."

Don't be the person who says "don't get chopped" and then chops themselves.

### Coupang — $136M Insider Buy, But…

> "TTD also had a big insider buy and it didn't work. Nike had all insiders buying at the same time — one day wonder, then kept going down. Sell the news."

Insider buying is a catalyst, not a guarantee. Check whether the market is reacting positively.

### Are Breakouts Likely to Work?

> "If you can answer the question 'are breakouts likely to work for next 3-5 days' with confidence, then buy. If not, look at intraday opportunities like SNDK or Adobe."

The answer today: NO. So don't buy breakouts. Look at intraday only, or walk away.

### Walk Away From the Computer

> "I go work out. I do things. I open the swimming pool. I grill. I assemble furniture. I finish taxes. I have HEG meetings. If you sit there, you're sitting in a casino. You feel like pressing some button."

The only way to not trade in a choppy market is physical distance from the screen.

### Process — Keep Doing the Work Even When Not Buying

Scanning, building watchlists, doing anticipation scans — keep the skill sharp even when not executing. Don't stop the process just because you're not buying.
