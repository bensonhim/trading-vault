# Microsoft Meltdown, Gold Parabolic, and Singles Market

> "Everything happened in the first 15 days of the year. After that, transitioning into a choppy market. When breakouts have no follow-through and shorts have no follow-through — that's choppiness. Focus on singles, SIPs, and intraday."

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### SA: Choppy Market — Negative Breadth Day

Gold and silver in parabolic mode. Breadth turned choppy — one or two positive days followed by selling. Earnings in full swing but positive news getting sold. 237 bearish vs 86 bullish stocks at open.

> "Focus on singles, SIPs, and intraday. Drop size during choppy periods. Take profit quickly."

### Key SIPs and Setups

- **[[CVS]]** — primary DEP short candidate (Medicare catalyst, watching for delayed reaction)
- **[[JOB]]** — short side (plane not flying, but gap-down shorts are tricky)
- **[[CRVS]]** — phase 1 trial stock, already 112% move — what's left?
- **[[NIO]]** — Chinese stock, only name showing premarket
- **[[META]]** — gapping up on earnings, day trade only. Better as delayed reaction EP

> "With large stocks like Meta, most of the time it becomes good as a delayed reaction EP. Much better to wait."

### Schwab Blocking Tickers — Pump and Dump

Bloomberg article explains why Schwab blocks ~200 tickers — pump and dump schemes forcing broker risk management. Both longs and shorts can lose money when the exchange halts trading.

> "That's why active traders use Interactive Brokers or Centerpoint."

### CRDO: Quality of Setup Matters

Since earnings, CRDO kept selling without one buyer. Weak structure shot, not inspiring confidence. Compare with APLD which went sideways after earnings — that's a better setup quality.

### Don't Buy Three Days Up (Again)

Trader wanted to buy SNDK — but tomorrow is earnings. Pradeep: "Sell your wife, sell your kids, and buy SNDK. Tomorrow is earnings. Buying in front of earnings is the dumbest thing."

### Oil & Gas E&P: Know Your Industry

CNX (coal/oil play) — "good revenue" doesn't matter. E&P stocks are cyclical. More production + lower oil prices = more losses. Sales figures only drive healthcare and tech. Consumer discretionary needs margins, not just sales.

> "Educate yourself on the industry. E&P stocks move in anticipation. They're cyclical."

## PM Session (3 PM)

### Microsoft Creates Panic

Microsoft earnings triggered a selloff. Market dropped straight down, then program trading recovered half. VIX collapsed back. Not a complete washout — market recovered.

> "Wasn't prepared for this kind of day. Didn't profit as much as I should have from AGQ."

### AGQ: The Missed Six-Figure Trade

Pradeep shorted AGQ intraday multiple times but missed the optimal entry. If he'd shorted right out of the gate, it would have been a six-figure day. Still profitable, but not the maximum.

Shorted at $268.83, covered at $263.84. Shorted again at $270.85. AGQ moves $4-6 in the time it takes to blink.

> "If you don't know what you're doing, it's terrible. $3-4 moves happen before you can blink."

### Gold/Silver: Parabolic Blow-Off Warning

> "The last time this happened was 2008-2009. When the party gets over on silver, it can be really ugly. 48% down in one week. Same narrative, same everything."

AGQ, SLV, KGC, GDX, MicroStrategy — all precious metals/bitcoin plays in parabolic mode. The setup is reversal bearish / exertion. Best case: 30-40% short in one day. Worst case: gaps up 10%.

> "I'm not taking it overnight because I don't have suicidal tendencies. But if you have high risk tolerance, this is the short."

### Shorting Gap-Downs Is Tricky

> "Shorting a gap-down is always tricky. Most of the time, you're better off waiting. No matter how negative the catalyst, dip buyers step in. Boeing door fell off mid-flight — still, next day buyers stepped in. Then it went down 50%."

Pattern: Gap down → dip buyers → brief rally → then the real move down.

### Tesla: Not a Weak Structure Shot

Tesla doesn't meet Niagara (3 days down) or Weak (3 by 2.5) criteria. Too many retail bulls will buy the dip. Pradeep wouldn't go near it for shorting.

> "Legendary amount of people bullish on Tesla. Next day, they all go buy. I wouldn't go anywhere close to it."

### CVS Still #1 Short

CVS remains the best short candidate from healthcare. Highest volume in 2-3 years, clear Medicare catalyst, didn't bounce like UNH.

### Carvana: Delayed Reaction Candidate

After S&P 500 addition pump-and-dump, Carvana is setting up as a delayed reaction short. Completely imploded after the index addition.

### Linde (LIN) Breaking Out

Linde breaking out — good fourth-leg move. Institutional quality, high price breakout.

### Delayed Reaction EP: The Bread and Butter

After market close, Pradeep goes through earnings to find delayed reaction setups. This is the core process — not macro calls, not predicting recessions.

> "Don't clutter your mind with macro. You buy this setup or short that setup. Which makes money? Trade the setup. Everything in trading is probability."

### Key Takeaways

1. **Choppy market confirmed.** Breakouts AND shorts lack follow-through. Singles and SIPs only.
2. **Microsoft earnings triggered selloff.** Market recovered half on program buying. Not a full washout.
3. **AGQ/SLV parabolic = blow-off top risk.** 2008-2009 parallel: 48% down in one week. Reversal bearish exertion setup.
4. **Shorting gap-downs is tricky.** Dip buyers always step in first, even with catastrophic catalysts. Wait for the real move.
5. **CVS remains #1 short candidate.** Highest volume in years, didn't bounce, Medicare catalyst.
6. **Sales growth only matters for healthcare and tech.** E&P and cyclical stocks need different analysis. Consumer discretionary needs margins.
7. **Don't buy in front of earnings.** SNDK lesson — tomorrow is earnings, buying today is gambling.
8. **Tesla: not a short setup.** Too many retail bulls, doesn't meet weak structure criteria.
9. **Meta: day trade only, then wait for delayed reaction.** Large stocks gap up then fade.
10. **Trade the setup, not the macro narrative.** Probability over prediction.
