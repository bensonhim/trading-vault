# The Party Is Over: Breakouts Failing, SA Rules the Day

> "The party is over. Most of the good part of the move is over. The whole thing is to reset for it to make a move." Three days of selling, breakouts failing, and only disciplined 80/20 trades work.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness Deep Dive: The Most Important Skill

Pradeep devoted the entire morning session to situational awareness — calling it the single most important skill for traders:

> "Every trader who survives the market for a few years realizes the importance of SA. The moment you realize, realization itself can change your life."

> "I can guarantee you 200% — you are going to blow up. You're not going to make money, and you're not going to keep money unless you develop situational awareness."

### Market Monitor: Three Days of Selling

Market Monitor context:
- Three days of selling = breakouts unlikely to work
- 20% study at 16 (bullish territory, but short-term pullback)
- When any Market Monitor column is red, breakouts are not likely to work

> "I was so aggressive during the initial phase of this bull move. I started winding down my risk on the first selling hint."

### The Phase Transition: Key Teaching

> "When the market bounces, the stocks which are oversold are the ones which make the move. Every single time. Stocks which held up near 52-week highs don't even make a move."

> "The biggest moving stocks were down 60%, 70%, 80%, 90% — those are the ones which made the biggest move. This happens every time."

**Phase framework**:
1. **First 6-10 weeks**: Oversold bounces dominate (already happened)
2. **Transition**: Extended stocks get hit, consolidation breakouts start forming
3. **Second phase**: Consolidation breakouts work (starting to form now)

### Current Setup Priority

> "Setups likely to work: shorts near 52-week high on extended stocks. 9,000,000 breakdowns and delayed reactions."

### The Bottom Setup: Capturing the First Leg

> "This year, I'm going to spend a lot of time trying to capture the first leg of these moves, because that's where the money is. The first leg was 80%."

### The 80/20 and Oversizing Philosophy

On QuantumScape member asking about holding:

> "You take 80% profit. But this is where the island mentality comes in — 'But what if I sell and it keeps going up?' Solve this problem once and for all."

On Soros and oversizing:

> "George Soros had only 30% win rate. But why did he make money? Because when he had a trade which was good, he was sizing up like crazy. Sizing up like crazy — that's the key to making money."

### JPMorgan Short: Trade Completed

> "JPMorgan — I got out of that trade yesterday itself. I made $5,000 on that trade. The remaining got stopped out."

### Fix Yourself First

> "If you really want to make money trading — first, fix yourself. Make a rule saying you're not going to buy if situational awareness is bearish. You write your SA as bearish, and then next day you go buy 16 breakouts. Why the fuck are you doing situational awareness?"

> "Once and for all, decide whether you're going to do 80/20 or not. Make the call and stick to it."

On CSX (5th leg breakout):

> "You want my opinion on CSX, which is first leg, second leg, third leg, fourth leg — this will be a fifth leg? Aren't you better off going to the mall and distributing your money?"

1. **The party is over** — easy money phase of 50% in two months is done

5. **Oversold stocks make the biggest moves** — near-52-week-high conventional wisdom is wrong for first 6-10 weeks
