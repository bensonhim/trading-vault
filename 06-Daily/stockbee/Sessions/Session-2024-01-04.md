# Day 4 of Pullback — Oversold Bounces Only Play, Breakouts Failing, 80/20 and Mind Clarity Deep Dive

Day 4 reinforced everything from the prior sessions with even stronger conviction: breakouts are failing, situational awareness demands extreme selectivity, and the only plays working are oversold bounces and day-trade setups. Pradeep spent significant time on the philosophy of 80/20, oversizing, and mind clarity — declaring that journaling without a setup and situational awareness framework is useless. The market was choppy, morning bounces faded, and Pradeep was playing very small.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM & 11 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Deep Teaching

> "For a trader, the most important skill is basically situational awareness once they found the setup. If you don't have situational awareness, you're going to be out of this game sooner or later."

> "I can guarantee you 200% that you are going to blow up. You're going to lose money."

Pradeep explained the full framework behind Market Monitor, created in 2002 and since copied widely. The core insight: Market Monitor identifies periods when breakouts are likely to work and possible bottoms. When Market Monitor columns turn red, breakouts are unlikely to work. Period.

### Three Days of Selling = Bounce Likely

> "Three days of selling plus 20% study sets up for likely buy-the-dip kind of a rally here in short term."

The expectation was a technical bounce — not a trend resumption. This is buy-the-dip territory only suitable for day traders or very nimble swing traders using 80/20.

### What's Working vs. Not Working

| Setup | Works? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Oversold bounces (BTD) | ✅ | Mobileye, Mara — gap-down stocks bouncing for one day |
| SIPs (day trade) | ✅ | Day-trade only; QuantumScape was the example |
| EP 9M (short) | ⚠️ | JPMorgan short worked yesterday; day 3+ diminishing returns |
| Weak structure shorts | ⏳ | Starting to show up; Coin, Wayfair, Chewy setting up |
| Breakouts (long) | ❌ | "Our breakouts are unlikely to work" — explicit call |
| Anticipation (long) | ❌ | "Hell, no" — choppy, no follow-through |
| Pattern breakouts | ❌ | Third, fourth, fifth leg stocks — CSX was a 5th leg |

### The Oversold Bounce vs. Breakout Cycle

> "After the market has already made a big move... what worked was the stocks which are oversold. Stocks which are holding up near high don't even make a move."

Pradeep's key cycle teaching:
1. **First phase (Nov 2 rally)**: Oversold stocks bounce hardest
2. **Transition**: Extended stocks start reversing, 9M breakdowns appear
3. **Second phase (coming)**: Consolidation breakouts start forming in 6–10 weeks
4. **Throughout**: Different sugar babies work in each phase

### 80/20 Is Position Sizing, Not Just Exits

> "When I do eighty twenty, what am I doing? I'm oversizing the position."

Deep explanation of 80/20 as a complete trading method:
- **Oversize the entry** — 85% of account in JPMorgan (5,000 shares)
- **Take 80% off quickly** — Leaves original-sized swing position
- **The math** — JPMorgan: $171.73 entry, $172 stop = 27¢ risk = 0.13% of account
- **Profit locked** — 80% off at profit; remaining 20% is a free ride
- **Even Cigna** — Good breakout that gave up all gains; 80/20 still made it profitable

> "You don't need balls to do FFFM. You need skills."

### The Mind Clarity Framework

> "The most important thing which determines whether you are going to be profitable or not — mind clarity. If you can achieve mind clarity, then everything will work. If you don't achieve mind clarity, nothing will work."

Pradeep's prescription for fixing trading problems (6 weeks):
1. **Deep dive** — Study your setup thoroughly
2. **Reflection** — Write your own guide
3. **Fine-tune daily** — Refine the guide every day
4. **Procedural memory** — Eventually it becomes effortless

On journaling: "98% of traders do journaling. If that was the key, they'd all be profitable. The problem is they don't have a setup or situational awareness."

### Soros and Oversizing

> "George Soros had only 30% win rate. But why did he make money? Because size. When he had a trade which was the good one, he was sizing up like crazy."

All successful traders converge on the same method: oversize when conditions favor, sell into strength, and don't trade when conditions don't favor.

### JPMorgan Trade Outcome

Short JPMorgan from $171.73, took 80% off at $170.53. Remaining 20% stopped out at breakeven. Total profit: ~$5,000 on a $1M account for one day. Small risk, outsized return — FFFM in action.

### Cigna Outcome

Yesterday's best setup (Cigna breakout) gave up all gains today. But 80/20 made it profitable — took 80% off early, remaining 20% stopped out with profit retained.

## PM Meeting (2 PM)

### Morning Bounce Faded

> "There are no buyers in this market."

The morning bounce (buy-the-dip setup) faded by afternoon. Healthcare, oil & gas, and other rotation plays all gave up gains. The bounce was purely technical — short covering after 3 days of selling.

> "All these people get excited saying, oh, sector rotation is happening. Oil and gas sector! What happened to them?"

### Breakouts Failing Everywhere

Pradeep walked through the Trade Factory systematically:
- **EP 9M bullish**: Technical bounces, not breakouts — AMD, COIN, QuantumScape bouncing but fading
- **Combination bullish**: 199 stocks — too many, most are third/fourth leg breakouts that won't work
- **Combination bearish**: 56 stocks — more interesting, selling spreading
- **Anticipation**: "Not even one good setup I see here"
- **Weak structure shorts**: Starting to appear — Coin, Wayfair, Chewy, Ouster

### QuantumScape: Case Study in Greed vs. Discipline

> "QuantumScape is up 27-30%. Is it better to just do 80/20 aggressively? Obviously, you take profit. You take 80% off."

QuantumScape was a 9M/SIPs play up 26%+ in one day. Multiple members wanted to buy or hold. Pradeep's answer: 80/20, take profit, move on. The "what if it goes higher" mentality is the exact island mentality problem.

### Trade Web (TW)

Only stock with a genuine catalyst: reported December ADV of $1.46T, up 43% YoY. News came at 7:48 AM. Pradeep identified it as a consolidation breakout with higher volume. One of the few setups he considered.

### LQDA

> "That's a nice breakout."

LQDA was identified as the best breakout setup of the day — lower limb pullback then breaking out. But even this came with the caveat that breakouts are unlikely to work in current conditions.

### Weak Structure Shorts Emerging

The sell-off is spreading to more sectors. New shorts starting to set up:
- **Coin (COIN)** — weak structure short setting up
- **Wayfair (W)** — "very nice weak structure shot setting up" but has support levels
- **Chewy** — weak structure short
- **Ouster** — weak structure short
- **Capital One Financial** — exhaustion setup but not worth it
- **Nvidia** — starting to hesitate

### The Party Is Over

> "The party is over. The good part of the move, most of the good part of the move is over."

> "The whole thing is to reset for it to make a move. I would start on the weekend looking at consolidation breakouts."

Pradeep's forward-looking call: the easy money phase is done. Now we need to wait for:
1. More pullback (likely)
2. Consolidation patterns to form (2-6 weeks)
3. New setups to emerge from those consolidations

### 401(k) and Market Monitor

Pradeep explained how he uses Market Monitor for his 401(k):
- Bought TQQQ/SQQQ aggressively when Market Monitor signaled bottom (Nov 2)
- Sold when his 401(k) hit 50% — a non-leveraged mutual fund making 50% in under two months was "too good to be true"
- "When something is moving fast, move your stops faster"
- T2108 signal: buy when below 20, sell when overextended
- Current T2108 is nowhere near 20 — not a buy signal for 401(k)

### Market Monitor: Don't Refine What Works

> "If I change the model, all my 25 years of accumulated understanding has gone through the roof."

Someone asked about refining Market Monitor. Pradeep's answer: don't change what works. The model correctly called the November bottom and the current pullback. Fine-tuning indicators is a hobby, not a path to profitability.

### Darvas Context

Pradeep gave important context on Nicolas Darvas: the period when Darvas made $2M, the market went up 57% in 14 months — one of the best bull markets ever. Without that context, people blindly apply "buy at new highs" and lose money.
