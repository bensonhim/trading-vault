### IPO Warning: The Jay Ritter Research

> "This is the inevitable feature of IPOs most of the time."

### The "Three Dates" Analogy for Dead Positions

On selling [[GS]]:

> "You went on three dates. Nothing happened. Why do you want to keep sitting there?"

The analogy: if a stock isn't moving after three days in your favor, it's dead weight. Move capital to something that's moving.

### Selling Into Strength

On [[DXYZ]]:

> "The moment it gapped up that much, I moved my stop because that is too much money for just two days. Remaining 20%, give it room with a stop. If this is violated, not worth holding."

Standard exit: trim 80% on massive gap, keep 20% with a trailing stop below the box low (Darvas-style).

### Setup Clarity: The Core Teaching

The PM session delivered a **definitive teaching on continuation vs. consolidation setups** — the most important concept of the day:

#### Continuation Setup

| Feature | Criteria |
|---------|----------|
| Structure | First leg → short pause (less than 10 days) → breakout |
| Pause | Orderly, low volume, narrow/negative range days |
| Buying | Persistent, consistent buying in the first leg |
| Breakout | Follows directly from the pause |
| No catalyst needed | Momentum carries through |

> "This is what a continuation setup looks like. First leg where there is consistent, persistent buying. After that, it pauses. This is a tight day, narrow day, or negative day. Then there is a breakout."

#### Consolidation Setup

| Feature | Criteria |
|---------|----------|
| Structure | Big move → long sideways churn (10+ days) → breakout attempt |
| Problem | Buyers have no urgency — stock goes sideways for weeks |
| Breakout | Often fails — "they just squat" |
| Requires | **Catalyst + Volume (C+V)** — both must be present |
| Without C+V | Breakout reverts into range — don't trade it |

> "In a consolidation, stock makes a big move, then goes sideways for more than ten days. What it means is that the buyers have no hurry to get into it. In order for it to actually make a move, what is required is a fresh catalyst and a volume surge. Now does it have those two criteria?"

> "Most consolidation breakouts, they just go back into the range. Nothing happened. This is why we don't want to be trading consolidation breakouts unless there is a catalyst and volume."

#### The Marriage Analogy

> "When you got married, when you're young, every day you're looking for action. Now you just sit there. There is no action going to be. Same thing happens — more than ten days, it keeps going sideways."

**Weekend homework assignment**: Write one page on what a continuation setup is, and one page on what a consolidation setup is. Know the difference. "If you can get this clarity, lifelong learning."

### The Psychology-vs-Setup Problem

A crucial teaching using a member's recurring AMD mistake:

> "What do traders say when their trade goes wrong? 'It is my psychology which is a problem.' Psychology cannot solve a wrong setup understanding problem. By making it into a psychology problem, you are never finding the root cause."

**The real issue**: The member was buying breakouts in the afternoon when momentum had faded. Morning setups worked because momentum existed; afternoon setups failed because the same chart pattern had no follow-through.

**Solution**: Don't trade in the afternoon, OR find a setup that works in low-momentum environments. It's a **setup problem**, not a psychology problem.

> "Unless you revisit that and answer that question, some intervention has to happen. You have to come to a realization. Just saying 'next week I'll do better' — every alcoholic says the same thing."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Breadth thrust confirmed** — 300+ breakouts, 20% study bullish 2:1. The signal was three days ago when sugar babies all fired simultaneously. That was the entry day.

2. **Sugar babies are a forward-looking indicator** — they lead at market turns, both up and down. When they line up en masse, act immediately.

3. **Know the difference between continuation and consolidation setups** — continuation: short pause (under 10 days), no catalyst needed, momentum carries. Consolidation: long churn (10+ days), requires C+V (catalyst + volume), otherwise breakout fails.

4. **Third day = danger zone** — don't buy stocks on their third day of a move. "If you are finding EP every day, it is 100% confirmed that you missed your appointment with your psychologist."

5. **It's a setup problem, not a psychology problem** — if your afternoon trades keep failing, the setup doesn't work in that environment. Change the setup, not your mindset.

6. **IPOs are a fool's gambit** — retail allocation = nobody wants it. Referenced Jay Ritter's research: most IPOs decline after offering.

7. **Sell into strength, trim aggressively** — when a stock is up 50%+ in two days, take 80% off the table. Trail the remaining 20% with a tight stop.

8. **Sugar Babies process is now formalized** — added as a fourth scan tab, will be part of 3 PM meetings going forward.

9. **"Three dates" rule for dead positions** — if a stock hasn't moved in three days of holding, it's not going to. Move the capital.

10. **Market phase matters** — we are in a transitional/choppy-to-trending phase. Only EP (rare), EP 9M, DEP, and Sugar Babies work reliably here.
