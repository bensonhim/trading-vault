# Short-Term Bearish — 80/20 Engineering, Win Rate vs Drawdown, Finding Your Style

First session after the MLK long weekend. Pradeep declared **short-term bearish** — breadth has been deteriorating for 8 of 10 days, and he's not buying marginal breakouts. The day was dominated by two deep teachings: (1) a full 80/20 engineering breakdown including position sizing on BA, and (2) finding your trading style based on personality — low drawdown vs big wins. The BA short continued working with a second gap down. He canceled the rest of the week's meetings out of frustration with people ignoring situational awareness.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Short-Term Bearish

> "As far as I'm concerned, short term bearish. Breadth deterioration continues. Every day they come and sell."

SA for Tuesday:
- **Working**: 9M breakdowns, delayed reaction shorts, select longs (with extreme selectivity)
- **Not working**: Most breakouts — will fade, chop you out
- **Context**: 8 of last 10 days were selling. Systematic distribution under the surface
- **Large caps still holding** — but at some stage they'll join the party
- **Actionable**: Focus on shorts, be extremely selective on longs

### BA Short Update: Free Money Keeps Giving

BA gapped down again (second consecutive gap down). Pradeep took 80% off at the open, leaving 20% with a raised stop:

> "My entry was so good because my entry was in the first five minutes. Even better money."

Still holding the 20% runner with stop moved above entry. Two gaps down = the trade did exactly what it was supposed to do.

### Goldman Sachs Earnings: Not a Setup

Someone asked about shorting GS after earnings miss. Pradeep's analogy:

> "If your wife gives you 100 kisses, is that a daily phenomenon or something special? There might be good news. But if she gives you two kisses or no kiss, is that unusual? Goldman Sachs earnings — is there a surprise? A big surprise? No. Then why do you think it'll react in a big way?"

No surprise = no edge = no trade. Post-earnings drift only works with a **big** surprise to either side.

### 4% Breakout Reinforcement

Continued from yesterday's deep dive:
- 90%+ of stocks making 100%+ moves in a year start from lows, not highs
- "If it has to work, it works. The stop never comes into play."
- Stocks that don't break out 4% — entering early at 1-2% doesn't help because those stocks never get escape velocity

### Position Sizing Philosophy

> "My default philosophy is to oversize a position. Oversize and then take 80% off. When I took 80% off, the 20% which is still there is still a quarter million position."

## 11 AM Meeting

### Catalyst vs Pattern: Two Different Games

Pradeep drew a sharp distinction between two types of trades:

| Trade Type | Objective | Account Impact | Basis |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Catalyst (9M EP)** | Move account 50-100% | Large | Story + volume = genuine catalyst |
| **Pattern (combo/LTV)** | Move account 0.5-5% | Small | Continuation, consolidation, bottom bounce |

> "If there is a stock with a catalyst — a story catalyst — and it does 50M shares, do I need a two-inch pattern to buy it? I'm buying based on the catalyst. Why is this so difficult to understand?"

### The Frustration: Ignoring Situational Awareness

People kept asking about marginal breakout setups (Arista Networks, ShockWave, etc.) despite the bearish SA:

> "I'm canceling all meetings starting from this meeting till the end of the week because this is just a waste of time. Every single time I'm telling you, situational awareness that I'm bearish. Select breakouts are not going to work. You can see it in your own trading."

Key point: Someone sent him a complete trading plan stating they'd "only trade with situational awareness" — then immediately asked about buying breakouts in a bearish environment. Creating a trading plan is easy. Executing it is the process problem.

### UVXY Bouncing = Warning

### Nothing Actionable on Long Side

## PM Meeting (2 PM)

### The 80/20 Engineering Deep Dive (EJ's Question)

This was the most detailed teaching on 80/20 position management and style finding.

**BA as a worked example:**
- Entry: ~$225, 5,000 shares = ~$1.1M position (on a $1M account, that's oversized)
- 80/20: Take 80% profit → left with 20% (~$200K position)
- Then another 80/20 on the remaining 20%
- Result: BA moved the account by more than 5% in a single trade
- "Find 20 trades like BA and you can double your account"

**The two paths:**

| Path | Win Rate | Drawdown | Requirements | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **High win rate** | 60-80% | Low | F&F, 80/20, SA, high-priced stocks, find & enter early | Pradeep's style |
| **Low win rate** | 25-30% | High (50%+) | Catalyst selection, analytical skill, wider stops, let runners run | KQ's style |

> "Can you make money with 30% win rate? Yes. But can everybody make money with 30% win rate? No. KQ is a unique personality who doesn't mind 50% drawdown. Most people here are not capable of handling 50% drawdown."

**Engineering high win rate:**
1. Find & enter early (first 5 minutes)
2. 80/20 profit management
3. Situational awareness (don't trade breakouts in bearish markets)
4. High-priced, liquid stocks (less volatile)
5. Selective entries — only trades with clear catalysts

**The $1 stop on a $3 stock problem:**
- If you put a $1 stop on a $3 stock, that's 33% risk
- "You are not going to be around in this business for more than one year"
- Low of the day or half of the day is the correct stop method
- Calculate position size from risk: $100 risk ÷ $0.41 risk per share = 243 shares

**Warren Buffett analogy:**
> "If you offer $1 billion to Warren Buffett to become a growth investor, would he? No. It's not his belief. He's a value investor fundamentally. Same thing — you have to know your personality."

### Finding Your Style

Pradeep's style:
- **Hates drawdowns** — that's his #1 constraint
- Engineers everything for high win rate and low drawdowns
- F&F, 80/20, SA, high-priced stocks, large size only when confident
- In Nov-Dec: aggressive, on margin, portfolio margin
- In Jan: cautious, selective, only high-probability setups

> "I'm always thinking: how can I increase my win rate? Because if I can increase the win rate, then I can go big — ballsy moves — large size on the good trades, because confidence comes from high win rate."

### EPAM: The Only Losing Trade

EPAM was entered as a good setup but stopped out immediately at market open. Even the best setups fail sometimes — that's why you have stops and high win rate to absorb the losses.

### Combo: 88 Bullish vs 355 Bearish

The scan numbers told the whole story. Nothing to do on the long side unless you want to get chopped.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Short-term bearish confirmed** — 8 of 10 days selling, systematic distribution, don't buy marginal breakouts
2. **SA > Setup, always** — If your SA says bearish and you still buy breakouts, you're wasting everyone's time including yours
3. **80/20 solves two problems** — (1) high win rate, (2) locks in profit when stock fades. Both work regardless of position size
4. **Oversize then 80/20** — Enter large, take 80% off quickly, let the 20% run with raised stops. This is how you move the account 5%+ in one trade
5. **Win rate vs drawdown = personality choice** — You can make money with 30% win rate IF you tolerate 50% drawdowns. Most people can't. Know thyself
6. **Catalyst vs pattern = two different games** — 9M EP is for moving your account 50-100% (catalyst). Combo/LTV is for 0.5-5% (pattern). Don't confuse them
7. **No surprise = no edge** — Goldman Sachs earnings: no outlier catalyst, no trade. Post-earnings drift requires BIG surprise
8. **$1 stop on a $3 stock = 33% risk = account death** — Use low of day or half of day, then calculate position size from dollar risk
9. **UVXY bouncing = correction warning** — Back-to-back UVXY bounces signal increasing probability of market pullback
10. **BA short = template executed twice** — Second gap down, 80% taken off at open, 20% runner with raised stop. Free money continues
11. **Process over plan** — Writing a trading plan is easy. Following it when SA says "don't trade" is the actual test
12. **Large caps joining the party soon** — After mega-caps' big rally, they're due for pullback. When they join the selling, the correction accelerates
13. **Only EPAM was a losing trade** — Good setup, stopped out immediately. That's why you have stops and high win rate
14. **88 bullish vs 355 bearish** — The numbers don't lie. Nothing actionable on long side
15. **Finding your style = answering personality questions** — What drawdown can you tolerate? What win rate do you need? Engineer everything toward your constraints
