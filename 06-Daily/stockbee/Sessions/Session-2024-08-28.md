# Understand the Environment, Don't Repeat Failing Approaches, If Early Entries Fail 3-4 Times Stop Doing Them

Core lesson: understand the environment. If early entries haven't worked for 3-4 days, stop doing them. If breakouts aren't working, stop buying breakouts. Adapt to what IS working — don't repeat what's failing. The dog analogy: if a dog bites you 3 times, don't approach it a 4th time.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Understand the Environment

> "If you're buying early and last three, four days, it hasn't worked — what are you going to be doing? Same thing in trading."

SA assessment:
- **Understand the environment** — Why isn't it working?
- **Don't repeat failing approaches** — If early entries fail 3-4 days, stop
- **If breakouts aren't working** — Stop buying breakouts
- **Adapt** — The market tells you what works

### The Dog Analogy

> "First time you approach the dog, it barks. Second time, it bites. Third time, it catches you. Fourth time, are you going to approach the dog?"

Same in trading. If a setup fails 3-4 times, stop doing it. Don't keep approaching the barking dog.

### Key Takeaways

- Understand the environment — why isn't your approach working?
- If early entries fail 3-4 days, stop doing early entries
- If breakouts aren't working, stop buying breakouts
- Adapt to what IS working in this environment
- The dog analogy: stop approaching the dog that bites you
