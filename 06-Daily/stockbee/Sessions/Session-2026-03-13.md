# Don't Buy the Cookies — Range Expansion Follow-Through, Institutional Gap-Downs Bounce, NBIS Delayed EP, SIPs Work in Any Market

Day 10. Range expansion follow-through — selling didn't bounce back all day yesterday. Market bounced early Friday but faded. **Adobe was the intraday masterclass** — institutional quality stock gapped down on CEO exit, bounced $7+ from open. **NBIS delayed EP** — catalyst now in play after earlier SOS. ### Adobe — Institutional Gap-Down Masterclass

**The trade:** Adobe gapped down on CEO (founder) exit + AI concerns. Pradeep called it as an intraday play, NOT an OPG.

> "Adobe is institutional quality. 5,000+ funds own it. Gap down on institutional quality stocks — they tend to recover."

Adobe bounced $7+ from open. Multiple intraday opportunities. Key insight: study hundreds of institutional gap-downs to develop confidence in this pattern.

**How to trade gap-downs on institutional stocks:**
1. They tend to gap down then bounce
2. Buy after turnaround (when change goes positive), not at open
3. Give wider stops than normal
4. It's a day trade, not a swing — worst is already priced in
5. Multi-day bounce likely (Adobe could bounce 3-5 days)

**Corvus vs Adobe — Not the same:**
- Corvus: losses, no profitability, market doesn't believe they'll turn profitable
- Adobe: profitable company, earnings beat, CEO exit is temporary overhang
- "Is that the same situation? No."

### SIPs — Works Independent of Market Conditions

> "SIPs is a setup which works independent of market conditions."

Market conditions don't matter for SIPs. Bumble yesterday = 40%+ move regardless of chop. The methodology identifies catalysts, not patterns.

### The Cookie Trap — Why Good Breakouts Look Good in Bad Markets

> "Even in a bad market, a good breakout is going to look good. But what happens after a good breakout if it is a bad market? It just sits there."

That's the trap. The breakout looks tempting. You buy. It sits. Then 10-14 breakouts do this and you take cut after cut after cut. **Situational awareness tells you when NOT to buy breakouts.**

> "You see cookies, you see ice cream, everything — and then you go there. And it's a trap."

### Dicks — Acquisition, Not Organic Earnings

Critical nuance: always check if earnings growth is organic or acquisition-driven. Dicks = acquisition. Not a real turnaround.
