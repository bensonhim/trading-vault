# Holiday Eve Drift — Bullish but Quiet, Trade Ideas Demo, and 100% Movers Are Still Dog Shit

July 3 was a shortened session ahead of the July 4th holiday. Pradeep's situational awareness was bullish — the 20% study showed a healthy 6:1 bullish-to-bearish ratio, the T2108 had risen to 67.05, and the S&P closed at a record high. But the day was going to be dead after the first 15 minutes. Pradeep used the slow morning to demo his Trade Ideas (the "Worm") real-time scanner, showing how it catches momentum stocks before TC2000. He reiterated the dog-shit lesson from two days prior, discussed the upcoming Market Wizards book, and warned about lifestyle creep destroying trader motivation after big wins. The market was green but quiet — nothing worth chasing.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish, Quiet

Judy's essay: bullish, 400+ green readings for a second straight day, T2108 at 67.05, 6:1 bullish-to-bearish ratio, S&P at record highs. Shortened session with 1 PM close.

> "Most of the action will happen the first fifteen, twenty minutes. After that, it will be almost dead because most people would be already on their holiday."

### The 20% Study — No Man's Land

> "The 20% study is around 63. It's not extreme. And the bearish side is 12. So there is not much on the short side. Almost everything is on the long side."

### Trade Ideas Demo — The Worm

Pradeep showed the "Worm" (Trade Ideas real-time scanner) for the first time in a session:

> "You will find a breakout much earlier here than even in TC2000 many times. Stocks with momentum tend to go up — when a stock shows up here, it has made 4% new highs in a few minutes."

> "My rule of thumb is if I'm going to use any tool, it should pay off at least fifty to a hundred times its price."

The Worm caught CRWV and BTOG early — the latter being a classic "complete dog shit" 100% mover.

### Lifestyle Creep Kills Trader Motivation

A discussion about account size and motivation led to a classic Pradeep tangent:

> "You get a boat. You get a sugar baby. You lose motivation. You build a house."

Referencing the upcoming Market Wizards book and trader "KQ":

> "Once you make $100 million, do you really need to trade like this? Unless you want to be doing money management."

### Nothing Worth Buying

After scanning EP 9M, bullish combo, and bearish scans:

> "Nothing here even remotely looks interesting."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Holiday sessions are dead** — all action in first 15 minutes, then standstill
2. **Trade Ideas / Worm catches momentum before TC2000** — useful for active traders, not for everyone
3. **100% movers are always garbage** — the same lesson repeated: zero-revenue microcaps make the crazy moves, short-side fading is the reliable trade
