# Buyers Return, Tariff Ignored — SIPs Cyclicity, Start of Swing Dominates, and the Internet Antenna Saga

The market shrugged off tariff drama — Pradeep noted a Wall Street Journal front-page article revealing the tariff letters were just a gimmick to save face. The T2108 bounced back to 64.29, the 20% study remained 5:1 bullish, and select breakouts were working. But the key observation was cyclicity: "every shit stock flies, then every shit stock gaps up and fades." Start-of-swing setups dominated while continuation setups failed. Pradeep spent the AM session dealing with internet issues (T-Mobile home internet + Waveform antenna solution) and the PM session finding almost nothing compelling — only a handful of possibilities including General Dynamics, TIGR, Carvana, and some oil/gas names. The AM session also featured a member (Manesh) reflecting on improving his SIP process by focusing on earnings plays and avoiding low-price biotechs.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Buyers Back, Tariff Ignored

Judy's essay: cautiously bullish, 300+ green reading, T2108 at 64.29, 5:1 bullish ratio. Tariff updates were absorbed by the market.

> "The tariff was just a gimmick. Behind the scenes, it was just an idea to shake out as much as possible. This was just to delay and shake out."

### SIPs Cyclicity — Everything Flies, Then Everything Fades

> "There is a period when every single catalyst stock is going to work, and then there is a period where every single catalyst stock is going to gap up and fade. That's the subtle hint you have to be constantly vigilant about."

### Start of Swing > Continuation

> "Start-of-swing setups are more common than other things. Very few continuation setups are working."

### Manesh's SIP Journey Improvement

Manesh reported progress: focusing on earnings plays, narrowing his list from 20-30 stocks, and learning to avoid low-price biotechs. Pradeep acknowledged the improvement but warned about premarket chasing:

> "Another mistake which I've realized from my trading is entering stocks premarket. Sometimes you chase them into premarket and then at the open they gap down."

## Key Takeaways

1. **SIPs have cyclicity** — periods where everything works, then periods where everything fades
2. **Start-of-swing > continuation** — the dominant pattern in this market
3. **Tariff drama is theater** — WSJ revealed it was a face-saving gimmick
4. **Late-stage breakouts are dangerous** — "you should not get very excited about them"
5. **Morning strength fading is a persistent pattern** — buy the close, not the open
6. **RxRx and Toast** — avoid stocks with repeated failed breakouts; they're "cluster fuckers"
7. **DraftKings has a hidden catalyst** — the gambling loss cap in the big bill benefits the house
8. **Extreme selectivity required** — only a handful of names worth watching on this day
