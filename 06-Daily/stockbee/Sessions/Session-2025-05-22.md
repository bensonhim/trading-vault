### Major Teaching: Stocks Have 1-2 Legs, 3rd Leg Is Extremely Rare

- Studied 20-40 years of history: stocks making big moves typically do so in **1-2 legs**
- **3rd leg is "extremely rare"** — almost never happens
- "Stocks are like men. How many times do men score in the night?"
- After 2 legs (first leg, second leg gap up), the setup often looks perfect but **fails**
- "It looks like it's going to break out, then doesn't have catalyst or buyers willing to pay higher price"
- Many current stocks already have 2-3 legs — **natural pullback candidates**
- Extended stocks near 52-week highs with 2-3 legs = vulnerable to 5-10% pullback
- Examples: Uber already pulling back, Netflix likely next, IDXX straight up
- 50%+ study confirms: stocks making 100-300% moves typically do it in 1-2 legs, very rarely 3
- "This is the nature of the market which hasn't changed in 25 years"

### Short-Side Framework

- First dip gets bought — "the first dip always attracts buyers"
- Need **continuation setup on indexes** for shorts to work

- Stocks after 2-3 legs near 52-week highs = short watch list
- "Prefer stocks that have run up and are breaking down, not stocks near lows breaking down"
