# Choppy Range, Shorts Dominate, UNH Best Short, Trade What You See

> "Why can't you trade what you see in front of you? What do you see? Microsoft short, SPY short, UnitedHealth short. Why do all these fantasy scenarios? Who knows these things? I certainly don't."

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### SA: Cautious — Tariff Uncertainty, Supreme Court Ruling

Primary indicators green, most secondary red. 20% study bullish (4:1 ratio). VIX retreated. Indices rebounded after choppy session Friday — disappointing economic data, geopolitical tensions, Supreme Court tariff ruling.

> "Choppy sideways action with select breakout and breakdown working. It's a very selective market, trading in a tight range near the high. If this breaks, go aggressive. If this breaks down, go aggressive. In between, you'll get chopped."

### Supreme Court Ruling: More Uncertainty, No Resolution

> "The Supreme Court decision came in Friday. The market didn't really react at all. Positive catalyst, market just yawns. But it has led to more uncertainty because it hasn't resolved anything."

### Pre-Market: Mixed Signals

Up pre-market: ACLX, VRT, EHAB, Legion, FBIO, SST, KBR, EIBRX, LLY, DPZ, ATSD
Down pre-market: NVO, AMR, SLN, ABJT, INMD, SCI, OKLO, ROX

### SIPs Watch: Eli Lilly, D, Novo Nordisk

- **Eli Lilly** — positive catalyst, large cap, want to see how it closes
- **Novo Nordisk** — 16M shares pre-market, choppy to trade
- **D (Dominion)** — downgraded, down $10

### Not Much to Play With

> "There are just not much to play with here. It's all choppy."

## PM Session (3 PM)

### Breadth: Selling Accelerates

EP 9M volume: $1.2B+. 6.87 vs 2.01 — not a 300+ day but selling present. Most things fading after early buying. Small caps also selling off.

> "Will there be follow-through? That has been the problem. Down days followed by up days, up days followed by down days. When I'm not sure, I am cautious."

### This Is January 2 in Reverse (For Shorts)

> "It looks like January 2 on the short side. So many stocks on the short side that it will be like one of those days. Shorts don't need volume to work."

1,300 stocks on short side. At 3M volume cutoff, still 536 candidates.

### AI Narrative Has Flipped

> "Suddenly the narrative changed from AI being bullish for the stock market to AI being bearish for the stock market. Every day somebody says this industry will be wiped out by AI. I read somewhere that DoorDash will be wiped out by AI. How? AI is going to deliver your restaurant food?"

> "What AI will not kill: Instagram influencers and OnlyFans. Who wants to see AI-generated OnlyFans?"

### Long Side: Slim Pickings

Institutional quality longs:
- **HSY** (Hershey) — nice setup
- **DGX** — nice setup
- **AMGN** — institutional quality
- **UHS** (Universal Health Services) — institutional quality
- **ALVN**, **BII**, **AVR**, **CVX**, **TSLA**

Money rotating into biotech.

> "Still need medicine to cure your depression because AI has taken over your job."

### Short Side: Overwhelming Choice

Best shorts:
- **SPY / QQQ / TQQ** — market itself, low risk (1.21%), institutional quality
- **UNH** — best risk/reward, 2.13% risk, negative government policy catalyst, highest volume ever traded
- **HUM** — similar to UNH, works too
- **MSFT** — "maybe the next Oracle" trajectory
- **TSLA** — fanboy stock, skeptical, level players may come in
- **WFC** — 4% risk
- **BAC** — good short
- **W**, **CITI**, **AMZN**, **LVS**, **LYFT**, **VFC**, **DOCS**

> "Based on what stocks are showing up in your scan, I would go into SPY or QQQ. Those are the ones which are the market itself. Institutional quality, 5,000+ fund ownership."

### UNH: The Best Short Setup

> "UnitedHealth had a negative government policy change, and then it is going down. Highest volume ever traded. That's the one that looks most attractive from a risk/reward perspective."

### Don't Trade What You Think, Trade What You See

> "When I start understanding things like that, I will retire from trading. Who knows these things? I don't even claim to understand all these things. I just see AVR medical instruments, probability of going up 3-5 days. Go and buy. That's the way to look at it."

### Breakouts Not Working

> "For the last 10-15 days, very few breakouts have had straightforward follow-through. It's not just one day's problem. Why get involved in things where I don't have an edge?"

Wouldn't even take reversal bullish setups today. Nothing remotely interesting on the reversal side.

---

### Key Takeaways

1. **Choppy range-bound market.** No follow-through for 10-15 days. Cautious stance.
2. **Shorts dominate.** 1,300 stocks on short side. Like January 2 in reverse.
3. **UNH = best short.** Negative government catalyst, highest volume, 2.13% risk.
4. **SPY/QQQ/TQQ shorts** — market itself, institutional quality, low risk.
5. **AI narrative has flipped.** From "AI bullish" to "AI will destroy everything" — same hysteria, different direction.
6. **Trade what you see.** Don't build fantasy macro scenarios. See setup, take trade.
7. **Breakouts not working.** 10-15 days of failed follow-through. Avoid long side unless institutional quality.
8. **Money rotating into biotech.** AMGN, UHS showing up.
9. **Shorts don't need volume to work.** Different from longs.
10. **Supreme Court tariff ruling = more uncertainty, no resolution.** Market yawned at it.
