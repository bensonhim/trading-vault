# Semiconductor Top Forming — Range-Bound Fade, No Fresh Setups, and Pradeep's Norway Sign-Off

Pradeep's last session before his Norway vacation was a masterclass in reading market deterioration. He opened by warning that the Friday–Tuesday rally was three days old, the 20% study had reached caution territory, and the semiconductor complex was showing the classic signature of a top forming: tight consolidations that tempt breakout traders, then fail. By mid-morning the warning became reality — semis broke down, his ARM and Intel positions were stopped out, and breadth from the prior day proved to be rotation into beaten-down names rather than genuine trend strength. The PM session confirmed the distribution: QQQ printed a range-expansion down day, leading stocks were straight down, and there was nothing worth buying. The day also featured Pradeep's recurring rants on consolidation-breakout temptation, social-arbitrage reversal bullish, the superiority of EP 9M over plain EP, and a pointed warning about prop-firm scams. He signed off for a week in Norway and told the room to either follow the process or accept that trading might not be for them.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: The Top-Forming Signature in Semiconductors

Pradeep opened with a clear warning: semiconductors were not behaving like leaders anymore.

> "Semi stocks, they are showing the kind of action you see just before top forming. ... Every time the down moves don't follow through, up moves don't follow through. That's typically where distribution is happening from the bigger player. The bigger players are checking out of this hotel, but we get tempted because we are suckers."

The pattern he described is a long, tight consolidation that looks like a coiled spring but is actually institutional distribution in slow motion.

> "Whenever you break that rule [buying consolidation breakouts without catalyst/volume], you always end up in heartbreak hotel."

### The 20% Study Is Extended

The breadth indicator was already flashing caution.

> "The 20% study... it's greater than 100. That's like the ... we already are in that territory of caution. So we are already extended."

> "Typically leads to some sort of a pullback."

### Rotation into Beaten-Down Names Is a Trap

Pradeep noted that yesterday's breadth was good, but it was breadth into beaten-down stocks — not leadership.

> "That rotation can be very misleading. When the market is distributing, sometimes you see it for a couple of days, this rotation into this beaten down stocks. But then, again, they get hit very hard because they are beaten down, or they're in downtrend for a reason."

### Focus on Clear Catalysts

The only setups he wanted were EP 9M, DEP, and sugar babies with a clear reason to go up.

> "Focus on stock with clear reason to go up. Just a pattern-based setup may not work very well. Good example: NNBR from yesterday has a reason. Catalyst. Or BLZ that I just closed. That had a reason."

### Institutional Quality and DEP

Pradeep again explained that DEP is a subset of EP 9M, and it requires fund-quality names.

> "DEP is a subset of EP 9M. Every DEP is also an EP 9M, which has happened in the last twenty five days. But not every EP 9M can become a DEP."

> "The first requirement for DEP is fund quality. Thousand plus what? So if it is not thousand plus part, it's just normal."

### Position Sizing and Prop-Firm Scams

A member asked about prop firms. Pradeep warned most are scams.

> "Prop firms are complete scam. I would not recommend anybody unless you're going to a prop firm like SMB Capital. ... SMB, Trillium — these are genuine, real firms."

On sizing:

> "Most of the successful initial success I have seen in recent years: they are doing only institutional quality, and the second reason is size. They can do size because they're doing institutional quality."

### Stop Discipline and "Trim the Bushes"

Pradeep got stopped out of ARM and Intel but emphasized that taking profit and moving stops is the right behavior.

> "Always keep trimming the bushes. ... Taking profit always never hurts."

> "If a girl says no to you, do you go on a second date or a third date? If it had to go, it would have gone by now. ... Once you got stopped out, there is nothing to do. Look for the next setup."

### Do the Guide

Pradeep returned to the low guide-completion rate.

> "Only 37% of the people do guide, and everybody who's profitable has done guide multiple times, so that tells you everything."

> "My epitaph is definitely going to say that he died valiantly trying to convince people to buy start of a swing."

### Marry a Setup, Not a Stock

Pradeep's closing AM advice:

> "If at all you want to marry, my advice is, don't marry. But if at all you want to marry, marry a setup, not a stock. And then have a situational awareness specific to that setup."

> "There is no global situational awareness. Situational awareness is specific to your setup."

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### The Correction Has Started

Pradeep opened the PM session with the verdict: the leading sector is rolling over.

> "When the leading sector starts rolling over, all other things join. There is nothing like one sector falls and everything else holds. When buyers step aside, it is going to be spreading to everything else."

QQQ had printed a range-expansion down day.

> "QQQ. That's a range expansion. Start of a swing [down]. That's how typically corrections start. ... Gradual one time deep bars, two time, third time is the charm or fourth time is the charm. So slow deterioration."

### Institutions Used Breakout Liquidity to Distribute

The PM repeated the AM theme with more evidence.

> "The institutions were distributing here. ... Every time these stocks try to break out, it's the same thing which has been happening. ... They break it, suck in all the retail crowd, and then sell."

> "Many of the retail crowd would still not sell because they're waiting for their twenty day moving average, fifty day moving average to be violated."

### No Actionable Longs

Pradeep ran the full trade factory and found almost nothing.

> "I don't see anything which is like actionable. ... There is just no qualified setup which matches anything which looks like a good setup."

> "I looked at every single thing under the sun in delayed reaction watch list, and there is nothing I can see."

### Reversal Bullish: Still Requires Popularity and a Clean Pattern

He showed what a real reversal bullish looks like (clean, egg-shaped bottom) and contrasted it with today's weak candidates.

> "Reversal bullish, you want at least a positive head to the candle. ... Just because it shows up in the scan is not a thing."

> "Who's going to buy it? Show me one person on Twitter who's excited, who runs a newsletter service or a Discord. They're talking about Target, and they are dying to get into Target tomorrow?"

### Sell Into Strength or Be Forced to Sell Into Weakness

Pradeep's core risk-management message:

> "Don't sell into strength, then you're forced to sell into weakness."

> "The time to sell was yesterday. At the moment those breakout failed, it's just like a dump and go move."

### 20% Study and Quarterly Indicator

A member asked about the quarterly monitor still being green. Pradeep's response:

> "Please sell all your everything jewelry. ... By the time the quarterly indicator turns red, you will have lost your wife and everything and your gold also. In market, you see a sign of smoke, you run. You don't wait for the engine fire."

> "The revenge of the 20% study. It went to 100 plus and boom, it came in."

### Final Message: Process or Quit

Pradeep closed with a harsh but clear message:

> "Every trade will come from our trade factory. Nothing beyond that. Every trade needs to be qualified, and it needs to have a context and qualification. The moment you start loosening your standards, the game is over."

> "If you cannot do this, I would say that just accept that trading is not for you and find something else to do."

And then he signed off for Norway and the swimming pool.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Semiconductor top forming** — Tight consolidations, failed breakouts, and no follow-through are classic signs of institutional distribution. Pradeep warned this was happening in the AM; the PM confirmed it.
2. **Leading sector rollover drags everything** — When semis/AI trade rolls over, the rest of the market follows. Don't expect isolated strength.
3. **20% study above 100 was the warning** — Extended breadth readings preceded the pullback, just as they have before.
4. **Rotation into beaten-down names is a distribution trap** — Good breadth into gold, silver, banks, and old-economy names often occurs just before a correction.
5. **There was almost nothing to buy** — EP 9M, DEP, delayed-reaction EP, anticipation, and reversal bullish all came up empty.
6. **Consolidation breakouts need catalyst + volume** — Without "C and V," they are heartbreak hotel. Pradeep learned this the hard way over ten years.
7. **DEP is a subset of EP 9M, not a separate thing** — Every DEP is an EP 9M from the last 25 days, but not every EP 9M is a DEP. DEP requires fund quality (thousand-plus names).
8. **Reversal bullish requires popularity and a clean pattern** — The setup is social arbitrage. Target and weak charts fail the filter.
9. **Sell into strength or be forced to sell into weakness** — Trimming winners and moving stops preserves gains. Waiting for moving-average violations costs money.
10. **Most prop firms are scams** — SMB Capital and Trillium were named as legitimate exceptions.
11. **Do the guide** — The profitability correlate remains completing the guide multiple times.
12. **Situational awareness is setup-specific** — There is no generic "market essay." EP 9M, reversal bullish, and continuation each have their own context.
13. **If you can't follow the process, trading may not be for you** — Pradeep's final line before vacation.
