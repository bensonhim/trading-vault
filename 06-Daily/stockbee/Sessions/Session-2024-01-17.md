## 11 AM Meeting

### The Eating Utensil Analogy: How Do You Move Your Account?

This was the core teaching of the day.

> "Some people eat with a spoon. Some with a fork. Some with chopsticks. Some with their hands. Ultimately, what is the objective? To get food in your mouth."

Two ways to move your account:

| Method | Account Impact | How |
|---|---|---|
| **9M EP (catalyst)** | 50-300% per trade | Cat/dog/liquid lava with genuine catalyst, large size |
| **Combo/LTV (pattern)** | 0.5-5% per trade | Continuation/consolidation, or high-priced stocks with size |

Size = leverage. High-priced stocks allow large capital with small risk.

**BA as example:**
- Entry ~$225, 5,000 shares
- That's ~$1.1M position (oversized by design)
- 80/20: take 80% profit, leave 20% running
- Result: moved account by 5%+ in one trade
- "Find 20 trades like BA and you can double your account"

### Finding Your Style: The Personality Match

Pradeep drew explicit comparisons between trading styles:

| Trader | Style | Win Rate | Drawdown | Key Trait |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pradeep** | F&F, 80/20, SA, high-priced, delayed reaction | High | Low (<10%) | Hates drawdowns, wants high win rate |
| **KQ (Oliver Kell)** | Catalyst-based, let winners run | ~30% | 50%+ | Tolerates massive drawdowns for big wins |
| **Minervini** | VCP consolidation breakout, trend template | Moderate | Moderate | Buys near 52-week highs only |
| **Dan Zenger** | High-priced stocks, larger stops | Variable | Variable | Piles into positions |

> "Can you make money with 30% win rate? Yes. But can everybody make money with 30% win rate? No. KQ is a unique personality who doesn't mind 50% drawdown. Most people here are not capable of handling 50% drawdown."

**Pradeep's style constraints:**
- Hates drawdowns — #1 priority
- Engineers everything for high win rate: F&F, 80/20, SA, high-priced stocks
- Oversizes positions, takes 80% off quickly, lets 20% run
- In Nov-Dec: aggressive (portfolio margin). In Jan: cautious, selective

**The KQ followers problem:**
> "All the people trying to become another KQ — how many have become another KQ? Many of them are going broke. They come asking for a discount on membership because they're already down 50%."

### Renaissance Capital and Citadel: Why Swing Trading Wins

> "The owner of Renaissance Capital was asked: 'If you have 800 PhDs and know how the market works, why are you not doing position trading? Why are you doing few seconds to few days holding?' His answer: 'If there is a thousand page novel and the first word is New York, can you predict the next 3-5 words? Yes. Can you predict the remaining thousand pages? No.'"

Citadel's strategy breakdown:
- Market making / HFT / stat arb: seconds to days → **where they make most money**
- Medium term equity: less than 1 week holding
- Long-only multi-asset: more than 1 month
- Most money made in <1 month holding

> "Everybody here wants to be a position trader. And they [Citadel/Renaissance] make billions doing this [swing trading]."

### Market Monitor Reading: The First Two Columns

A member asked which columns in the Market Monitor matter for breakouts:

> "The first column and second column. 4% breakouts and breakdowns. If both are green, breakouts work. If one of them is red, breakouts do not work. Simple."

The yellow columns (25% quarterly) are NOT the signal. The first two columns (4% breakout/breakdown) are what matter.

## PM Meeting (2 PM)

### Trade Factory: 9M EP Finances Combo

> "9M EP is designed to find large moves that move your account in large increments. Combo will move your account by 0.5-2%, occasionally 5% if you do size. The idea is: combo allows you to finance 9M EP."

The portfolio structure:
- **9M EP** → 50-300% per trade (wider stops, larger risk, catalyst-based)
- **Combo** → 0.5-5% per trade (finances the 9M EP trades, builds account for size)
- **Anticipation** → tries to anticipate combo bullish setups (lowest probability)

### Market: Slow Deterioration, Not Capitulation

Key distinction:
- **Current market**: Slow, systematic, drip-drip selling. Institutions reducing risk gradually. Not panic selling.
- **Real selling (capitulation)**: 500-600+ distribution days back to back, "get me out at any cost" selling. That's when you load up on shorts like TQQQ and make 10%+ in a day.
- **Current is torture by a thousand cuts** — choppy, not dramatic

> "The small caps are already broken. The large caps are still holding up. At some stage, they will join the party. When they read the riot act, they don't only take the books. They take the chairs. And they also take the piano player."

### Minervini's Trend Template vs Our Approach

Pradeep explained why Minervini's style is fundamentally different:
- **Minervini's trend template**: Stock must be above 150-day MA, 200-day MA rising, 50-day MA > 200-day MA, price > 30% above 52-week low, within 25% of 52-week high
- **Our continuation setup**: Can be near lows, bottom-of-barrel, not meeting any of Minervini's criteria
- **Minervini buys consolidation breakouts near highs** — we buy continuation breakouts and 9M EPs near lows
- These are **completely different setups** — different entry points, different timing, different conditions

> "You have to understand the nitty-gritties of people's setup. Unless you understand that, you'll try to apply someone else's method to your style and it won't work."

## Key Takeaways

1. **SA > everything** — If your SA says bearish and you still buy breakouts, you're wasting everyone's time including yours
2. **Different styles for different personalities** — Pradeep (high win rate, low drawdown) vs KQ (low win rate, big wins) vs Minervini (VCP near highs) — you must match style to personality
3. **The eating utensil analogy** — Fork, spoon, chopsticks, hands — all just get food in your mouth. Different methods, same objective: move your account 1-5% per trade
4. **Size = leverage** — High-priced stocks + small stop = large position = meaningful account movement with controlled risk
5. **BA short = 5%+ account mover** — Oversized entry, 80/20 profit management, zero risk on remaining 20%. This is the template trade for the whole month
6. **Combo finances 9M EP** — Small wins from combo build the capital for large-size 9M EP trades
7. **Slow deterioration, not capitulation** — Drip-drip selling, not panic. Institutions reducing risk. When real selling comes (600+ distribution days), load up on shorts
8. **T2108 at 50, heading lower** — Below 20 = bounce territory. Below 10 = very bullish. Currently in no-man's-land
9. **Renaissance and Citadel make billions in swing trading** — Holding periods of seconds to days. Position trading is extremely difficult. Swing trading is what works
10. **Minervini's approach is fundamentally different** — Trend template, consolidation breakouts near highs. Our continuation/9M EP approach buys near lows. Don't mix methods
11. **Delayed reaction = best current setup** — Lower risk entry, genuine catalyst, clear structure. Mobileye, XPO, BA all templates
12. **Market Monitor: first two columns** — If both 4% breakout and breakdown columns are green, breakouts work. If one is red, they don't. Simple.
13. **95% of people never find their potential** — The deep dive exercise separates those who will from those who won't
14. **XOP, ServiceNow, weak structure shorts** — These are setting up. Semis rolling over. Apple looking shaky.
15. **Nothing actionable on long side** — Be patient. Wait for setups. Don't force trades in a deteriorating market
