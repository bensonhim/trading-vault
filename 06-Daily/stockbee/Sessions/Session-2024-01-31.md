# The Must-Buy Setup and Why Your Paradigm Is Killing You

FOMC Wednesday. Powell (the stock, not the Fed chair) made 50%+ in one day — the most compelling SIPs/EP setup of the week. Yet many members couldn't recognize it. The core teaching: **your mental paradigm is your biggest enemy.** Opening range high, ATR filters, and old chart patterns are preventing you from seeing what's obvious. Meanwhile, the market has fundamentally changed — moves are faster, consolidations are shorter, and classical patterns are dying. Pradeep drew on Joel Barker's paradigm book: the Romans built chariot-width roads, and for thousands of years after chariots disappeared, people still built roads to chariot width. Your paradigm is your chariot-width road.

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: FOMC Day — Choppy but Bullish

- Bullish but cautious — FOMC can change everything
- Gap down might be bought, but be careful
- Market extended on technology side
- **What's working**: SIPs, delayed reaction to SIPs, FHP (high-priced stocks)
- **What's NOT working**: Classical sugar babies, regular breakouts
- Many setups having 2-day moves then reversing — adjust risk accordingly

### ⚡ Market Speed Has Fundamentally Changed

> "If a stock makes a 100% move, how much time does it take? Less than two months. Most of the time."

- 100%+ moves happen in <2 months — this is the new reality
- 50% moves happen in <10 days
- **12-week cup-with-handle is eternity** — classical patterns are dying
- Shorter consolidations are the new normal
- Longer consolidations = institutions lost interest or are distributing
- Nasdaq made a massive move with hardly any consolidation
- Algorithmic trading and quant funds compress multi-week moves into days
- **Mental models need updating** — old patterns don't work in the new market

### 20% Study: Selective Buying, No Significant Selling

- Only 23 stocks up 20%+ in regular hours — not extreme buying
- All have catalysts — these will set up for delayed reaction
- Bearish side: barely any stocks down 20%+ — no selling pressure
- Use the 20% study as a study tool — what happened after first leg? Did it follow through?

### Key Visualization Reminder

- Controlled risk taking, find and enter early, sell into strength
- T3 — think three days ahead
- Sectors making biggest moves: technology, healthcare, consumer discretionary
- **Stocks with stories make bigger moves** — this is not new
- Linear moves = continuous persistent buying = institutional conviction
- To find sector moves early: run combination scan — all moves start with 4% breakout

## 11 AM Meeting — Powell: The Must-Buy Setup That Separated the Clarity from the Clueless

### ⚡ POWELL (POWL): The Defining Trade of the Week

> "If you have a must buy, you sell your wife, your husband, your kids, your horse, your dog, and you buy."

- **Earnings**: $1.98 vs $0.84 consensus — more than double
- **Float**: 9.6M shares
- **Market cap**: $946M (tiny)
- **Past moves**: 40%, 46%, 50% on previous earnings
- **Neglect**: Nobody was paying attention — no analyst coverage
- **Result**: 50%+ move in ONE DAY (entire move priced in by 9:30-10:00 AM)
- **Short interest**: Only 2.53% — NOT required for a compelling setup (Magna is must-have, 53 is optional)

### Why People Missed It: The Opening Range High Paradigm

> "Some of you are addicted to opening range high. When you have such a compelling setup, you want to get in. You want to get in."

- Members waiting for opening range high = missed the entire move
- By the time ORH triggered, half the move was gone
- **OPG (opening price guarantee) is the only valid entry for compelling setups like this**
- Higher float stocks may need ORH, but neglected low-float stocks with catalyst = OPG immediately
- The 2.53% short interest shouldn't have been a filter — **Magna is mandatory, 53/cap/short interest are additional filters**
- "Even God cannot help you" if you can't see this setup

### The Powell Anatomy: What Made It Compelling

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Earnings beat | $1.98 vs $0.84 consensus (138% beat) |
| Float | 9.6M shares |
| Market cap | $946M |
| Neglect | 5+ months of sideways, no analyst coverage |
| Past earnings | 40%, 46%, 50% moves |
| Growth | >1000% revenue growth |
| Gap up | 17% premarket → projected 50%+ total move |
| FSS | 58 funds (low, but growing fast) |
| Short interest | 2.53% (low, but not needed for this type of setup) |

### Catalyst Changes Everything — ATR Is Useless

- ATR is backward-looking — a stock with low ATR will have HIGH ATR when a catalyst hits
- Don't filter out stocks based on ATR
- Catalyst = the thing that matters, not historical volatility

### SIPs Priority Hierarchy

1. **EP** — Powell-type setups, rare but transformative
2. **SIPs** — daily opportunity, smaller but consistent
3. **9M breakout** — when no EP or SIPs available
4. **Sugar babies** — when market favors them (currently NOT working)
5. **Country in trouble** — Z category, lowest priority

### ⚡ Tenacity vs Persistence — The Mindset Shift

> "Persistence is just mindless — you keep hitting your head against the wall. Tenacity is you fall down, you change your tactics, and try again."

- **Persistence** = doing the same thing over and over, expecting different results
- **Tenacity** = adapting your tactics after each failure, trying again differently
- SIPs requires tenacity — daily practice, refine selection, adjust entry/exit
- "If you stick to mastering SIPs for 3-6 months with 5 shares, you'll figure it out"
- The opportunity exists every day — you need the methodology (the "straw") to extract it

### Paradigm Shift: Joel Barker's Lesson

- Romans built roads to chariot width → for thousands of years after chariots, people still built chariot-width roads
- Bicycle seats = horse saddles → uncomfortable for centuries because of paradigm
- **Your paradigm (opening range high, ATR filters, chart-only analysis) is your chariot-width road**
- People stuck in paradigms can't see what's obvious to those who've had the eureka moment
- Some members will never get it — "I cannot do anything about it"
- Those who DO get it (DG from Bulgaria, member who found PXLW) — their trading changes forever

### OPG vs ORH vs MOO

- **OPG** (Opening Price Guarantee) = market on open order = fills at opening price
- **ORH** (Opening Range High) = wait for opening range to establish, then buy breakout
- **MOO** (Market On Open) = same as OPG — it's a market order type
- For compelling neglected SIPs like Powell, OPG is the only valid entry
- Higher float SIPs may need ORH
- Not all brokers support OPG — Fidelity does, IBKR does, Webull doesn't, Robinhood doesn't
- If you can't do OPG, premarket buy is also valid

### What to Do When You Can't Do SIPs (Day Job, IRA, etc.)
