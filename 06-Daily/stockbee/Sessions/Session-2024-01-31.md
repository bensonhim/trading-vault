## 11 AM Meeting — The Powell Trade: Must-Buy Setup, Neglect + Low Float + Low Cap

### The Powell 50% Move: Anatomy of a Must-Buy

- [[Powell]]: earnings beat $1.98 vs 84¢ consensus (more than double)
- Float: 9.6M shares, cap: $946M
- Last earnings: 38-49% moves — this time even bigger catalyst
- Neglected for months — nobody paying attention
- Made 50% in under 40 minutes — entire EP priced in one day
- "If you didn't get in, you'll regret it for the rest of your life"

### Must-Buy Framework

> "Must buy = 100% confidence the stock will move 50%+ based on compelling evidence."

- Three tiers: **must buy** (Powell, SMCI), **good** (second-tier SIPs), **okay** (tertiary)
- People putting Microsoft, AMD, Google in SIPs list — that's not must-buy
- **Neglect + low float + low cap = explosive potential** — Microsoft can't double in a day
- Short float of 2.53% is **optional** (Magna 53 criteria) — the **Magna part is mandatory**
- People overthinking opening range high on a 9.6M float stock — "your butler won't wait for you"

### Opening Range High Trap

- On stocks like Powell, opening range high means you've already missed 30%+
- For neglected, low-float, low-cap stocks: **OPG (Opening Price Guarantee) is the only valid entry**
- OPG = market order at opening price — you get filled at open
- People emailing "I had it on my list but waited for ORH" — that's the paradigm trap

### Paradigm Shift: Joel Barker's Lesson

> "When the Romans built roads, they built them chariot-width. For thousands of years after, even railways were built chariot-width. People get caught in a paradigm."

- Opening range high = your paradigm, your chariot-width road
- SIPs = a new paradigm many can't accept
- "Unlearning is very difficult" — you must erase old mental models
- Bicycle seats are uncomfortable because they copied horse saddles — paradigm stuck

### Creativity vs. Discipline (Reinforced)

- Discipline without creativity = hitting head against wall (persistence)
- **Tenacity** = fall down, change tactics, try again (the Marathi word "chikati")
- Persistence = dumb people strategy; tenacity = smart people strategy
- Start with 5 shares, prove the concept, then scale

### The SIPs Quality Lesson

- Powell had ALL the characteristics: neglect, low float, low cap, prior explosive moves, blowout catalyst
- "Don't you see the numbers? They're staring at you"
- Member who identified Powell but didn't execute: "I was looking at the wrong criteria (short float ratio) instead of the mandatory criteria (Magna)"
- **Mandatory**: float, capitalization, prior move history, catalyst quality
- **Optional**: short interest (Magna 53), analyst coverage

### Delayed Reaction Update

- [[Cisco]]: delayed reaction + neglected + buyback + short interest
- [[GM]]: delayed reaction, but $48B cap — too big for big move
- [[Johnson Controls International|JCI]]: missed earnings, went down first, then up — delayed reaction complex
- [[BA]]: catalyst but institutions not selling — hard to short even with bad news
- Long side = prices in fast; short side = takes longer to price in
