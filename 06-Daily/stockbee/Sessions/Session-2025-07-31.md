# Correction Starting, Shorts Working — SOXL/TXN Shorts Clean, Breakouts Failing, Delayed Reaction Is the Play

T2108 plunged from 57.73 to 49.79, 5-day ratio below 1 (weak buying), 20% study at 1:1. Pradeep's verdict after returning from holiday: **a correction is starting**. Breakouts are failing, stocks that rallied into earnings are selling off, and semiconductor stocks are rolling over one by one. The cleanest trades were on the short side: SOXL (9M breakdown, range expansion after 5 legs) and TXN (perfect delayed reaction short with 0.97% risk). Pradeep's key lesson: "Think setups. Don't think 'or think things.' Just buy a setup." APLD was the only long standout (exploring strategic options = they want to sell themselves). Meta and Microsoft gapped up but were too extended — classic delayed reaction candidates, not chase candidates.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish but Deteriorating

Judy's essay: bullish, but 400+ selling day, T2108 dropped to 49.79 (weakness), 5-day ratio below 1, 20% study at 1:1. Small caps continued pullback, Dow and S&P closed red after record highs, Nasdaq modest gain.

Pradeep's assessment after returning from holiday:

> "I see more breakdowns after earnings than breakouts. There is a distribution happening. A lot of these stocks rallied into earnings — they rallied 50% or more, and for that to continue, they need something completely crazy."

> "Story stocks are giving up their gains. QuantumScape is over. Kohl's is in the toilet. American Eagle is under pressure."

> "Three consecutive days of selling. The 20% study is declining — it's almost equal now."

### Delayed Reaction = Preferred Setup for Extended Stocks

> "Your best bet on larger stocks like Carvana and Meta is always delayed reaction. They gap up $17 on earnings, and where are you going to put a stop? You don't know."

> "Delayed reaction is my preferred setup on many of the larger stocks which tend to have pullbacks after one day. They offer you an opportunity to get in with a good entry."

### APLD = Catalyst Story (Strategic Options = They Want to Sell)

> "Applied Digital — they want to sell themselves, they said. Strategic options is a euphemism for 'we're looking for somebody to buy us.' Same as saying 'I'm a mean reversion trader' when you're really a pullback trader."

### Don't Buy Extended Stocks After Earnings

> "28% stock has rallied into earnings. Your best bet is always when there is a surprise, and the stock is not rallying into that surprise."

> "Somebody called me excited about Microsoft and Meta, wanting to put a big position in them. I told them no — that's all priced in."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Correction Is Starting — Shorts Are Working

> "Most of the money I have made in the last couple of days has been on the short side. Shorts are working very well, especially semiconductor shorts."

> "Do I think correction is starting? Yes."

> "Retail is all in. Retail is taking home equity loans to invest in the market. Who is selling? Institutional selling."

> "Breakouts are not really sticking. I'm not really interested in long setups."

### SOXL Short = Clear Range Expansion

> "SOXL — this is such a clear range expansion after five legs. When SOXL gets going, it can be a 25-50% swing. Risk is $0.50 to $1. I'm willing to take that risk."

> "9M works independent of the market. 30 million shares sold. One of the highest volume ever. You don't even need situational awareness."

### TXN Short = Perfect Delayed Reaction (Yesterday)

> "Yesterday's TXN — this was a perfect delayed reaction. Your risk was 0.97%. You can put in a lot more with that kind of risk."

> "TXN is not a stock retail investors go near. It's owned by 2,500 funds. Who is selling? Institutional."

> "I just put a short there. Entry at $188.95. And it just did this." (shows the chart going down)

### Semiconductors Rolling Over

> "Every single semiconductor except Nvidia is coming out with earnings and going down. ASML guiding down, ARM guiding down, Qualcomm down."

> "If things are so great, why is ASML guiding down? Why are all these semiconductor stocks going down?"

> "The best earnings in cyclical stocks come near the cycle top."

### Think Setups, Not Narratives

> "Think setups. Don't think 'or think things.' Just buy a setup."

> "In order to do this, you should have seen 10,000, 50,000 examples of range expansion. When you see it, involuntarily your hand goes and puts in 5,000 shares of TXN short."

> "People are hopping from one setup to another. If you float from one setup to another, you don't acquire deep enough understanding of even one thing."

### Coursera = Technical Setup, No Catalyst

> "Coursera — impressive result? 8%, 5%, 5% sales growth, which is slower than their last four quarters. Does it make sense to get excited?"

> "As a technical pullback, yes, the setup might work. But what is the reason why I should get excited about Coursera?"

### IPO Advice

> "If you bought Meta on IPO, it took you two years to break even. Most IPOs that people are excited about — they go down."

> "The best time to get an IPO? Pre-IPO allocation. If you're dating the CEO."
