## 11 AM Meeting — Process Orientation Masterclass

### 4% Breakout = Escape Velocity

The defining teaching of this session:

> "I looked at every single stock which made a 25% move in a month. The only common thing was a 4% breakout. You could not explain it by any other thing."

Key principles:
- **4% is the signal** — there must be escape velocity
- **Stocks that don't break out 4% don't make the move** — they just don't go
- **Even at 4%, the move still continues** — "by the time it's 4%, it's too late" is wrong
- **The move starts from consolidation or bottom** — that's the structure to study
- **If it has to work, the stop never comes into play** — if it doesn't, even a wide stop gets hit

> "If you just focus on 4% breakout and do a damn good job of managing risk, you will make money. Period."

### Study 20%: Structure of Moves

Only 27 stocks making 20%+ (down from 40 a few days ago). Key lessons from studying structure:

### Process Orientation: Toyota Analogy

The defining quote of the session:

> "Toyota did not invent the car. It invented a **process** to make quality cars and beat the pants of others. What works in the market — momentum, EP, stocks in play, breakouts, pullback, volatility cycles, small cap, shots — is well known. You don't need to invent the car. What you need is a **process like Toyota** for trading a breakout."

Daily process:
1. **Pre-market**: Stocks in play (briefing.com, fly, SIPs, Anna 3)
2. **9 AM**: SA → visualization → trade factory
3. **Market open**: Enter trades (OPG or first 5 minutes)
4. **By 10 AM**: Most process done — in trades, stops set
5. **11 AM**: Combo scan → find remaining setups
6. **2 PM**: Anticipation (not today — long weekend)
7. **End of day**: Take profits, adjust stops, prepare for next day

> "By 10 AM, most of my process is done. I'm in the stocks I want to be in. Everything after that is taking profit, raising stops."
