# Choppy Market, Slim Pickings: Only MOMO Meets the Bar

> In a market where select breakouts are the only game and most scans turn up empty, MOMO is the single setup that meets the criteria — everything else is either too extended, too low-volume, or too choppy to touch.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Choppy, Selective Breakouts Only

Pradeep opened with a clear regime call:

> "We continue to be in a choppy kind of environment where select breakouts are working."

The focus: **9,000,000** and **delayed reaction** setups. Earnings season starting would add volatility and news-driven movement.

### The 4% Breakout: The Unit That Doesn't Change

Reviewing the 20% study (at only 20 — few breakouts happening):

> "Things don't change as much as people keep saying, oh, market keep changing. But, like, in that one unit of 4% breakout continues to be the unit which tends to, which shows up at the beginning of a breakout miraculously."

Every working breakout in the scan started with a 4% breakout or range expansion:

| Stock | Setup | Catalyst |
|-------|-------|----------|
| CAAS | Breakout | 4% breakout start |
| HIRE | Breakout | 4% breakout start |
| WOLF | Breakout | Range expansion |
| ANNE | Breakout | 4% breakout |
| JMI | Breakout | 4% breakout |

### Built-in: Small Cap, Low Price, Sector Concentration

The structural filters held:

> "Capitalization of all these stocks is below $10 billion. Most of them are below $10 billion."

> "Most of the big moves happen below $20."

> "The stocks which make the big moves are not necessarily the stocks which are already going up. Most of the moves start from the lows."

Key sector bias:

> "All these stocks — the ones which work — come from biotechnology or information technology. Healthcare, technology tends to dominate the most."

### Short Side: One-in-Three

Seven shorts to 21 longs — a 1:3 ratio. Range expansion to the downside starts the move. Dollar breakdowns show the same pattern at $100+ price levels.

## 10 AM Meeting (Trade Factory)

### Scanning Results: Barren

The trade factory scan for 9,000,000 record combo plus LTV turned up very little:

- [[TSLA]] — already up multiple days, too late
- [[NVDA]] — same, too extended
- [[SGC]] — biotechnology, up 294%, risk too high unless bought on early morning news; wait for delayed reaction setup

> "So I would wait for this to set up as delayed reaction because it's a biotechnology kind of a play. Typically, they'll raise capital and all and might work as a delayed reaction."

### Combination Scan: 25 Bullish, 41 Bearish — But Nothing Clean

Starting with bullish, sorted by volume, looking for **start of a swing** — range expansion at the beginning, not after 2-3 days of movement:

- Most candidates: insufficient volume, not a clean start-of-swing
- **MOMO** — the only viable setup: persistent buying → orderly pullback → breakout, low price, $1.2B market cap

> "Consolidation, too long. How much consolidation is allowed? One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine days. So it's less than ten days."

### Bearish Side: No Shorts Either

Intel, UnitedHealth, Autodesk, Netflix, MongoDB, ServiceNow — all at low volume, not interesting for shorts:

> "At this volume levels, I'm not even interested in these stocks."

No actionable short setups found.

### RXRX: A Delayed Reaction That Didn't Work

> "This was a good delayed reaction. Did it work? No. So there is no urgency to sell."

A delayed reaction can be correctly identified and still fail — the lack of urgency means no forced exit, but the lesson is clear: even good setups whiff in this environment.

### The Bottom Line

> "Am I going to go and put all my money into this? No. But that's the only setup which is looking... that meets the criteria."

**One setup. Choppy market. Size down.**

## Key Takeaways

1. **Choppy, selective market** — only select breakouts working; 20% study at 20 (very few breakouts)
2. **4% breakout remains the universal unit** — every working breakout started with one; this doesn't change across regimes
3. **Small cap + low price + biotech/tech sector** — the structural filters that keep working (below $10B cap, below $20 price)
4. **Moves start from lows, not highs** — don't chase stocks already going up
5. **Earnings season adds volatility** — focus on 9,000,000 and delayed reaction setups
6. **MOMO was the only viable trade** — persistent buying → orderly pullback → breakout, under 10 days consolidation
7. **Delayed reactions can fail** — RXRX was a good delayed reaction that didn't work; no urgency to sell means no forced exit
8. **Most of the market is untradeable** — volume too low, moves too extended, setups too unclear
9. **Short side is thin** — 7 shorts to 21 longs (1:3 ratio), no actionable shorts in the scan
10. **Size down in choppy markets** — one marginal setup does not justify full position
