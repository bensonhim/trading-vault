## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bearish, Choppy Range

> "High intraday volatility is likely. If you are not a day trader, wait for VIX to crash."

Bearish regime. Choppy range forming. VIX very high — stops can get hit easily. For swing traders, this is a dangerous environment. Day traders should be minting money.

### Key Teaching: 20% Study — Short Near Highs, Not Lows

> "If you have to short something, there is no point in shorting something which is a weak stock. You should always short near the high."

The 20% study is mean-reverting: too many shorts = bullish. But the structural lesson: stocks near 52-week highs make the best shorts. Weak stocks are already sold out — "anybody who has to sell already sold." The 4% breakdown appears at the START of every 20%+ down move.

> "The magical indicator called 4% breakdown tends to appear at the start of a 20% plus down move."

Context matters: 4% breakdown in a bull market won't work. It works at the start of a swing, after range expansion, with 733 days of selling, with large-cap range expansion to the downside.

### Key Teaching: 60% Drawdowns — No Setup, No Stops, Leverage

Three members with 60%+ drawdowns. Common thread: no setup, no stops, small accounts using maximum leverage.

> "If you don't have a setup, it is never going to work. In a bull market, you can get away without having a setup and not having stops."

Regime change is what kills traders. They're slow to realize it's not the same game. NVDA trader kept buying every dip and doubling down — wiped out.

> "Every penny which I have made, I kept during COVID and added to that. They were just lucky. You don't want to be lucky."

### Positions Update

All three shorts from yesterday stopped out:
- **MA** — stopped out for small profit ($5 buffer evaporated)
- **MRK** — stopped out for breakeven
- **CBOE** — remaining 20% stopped at breakeven
- **0% invested** by end of AM meeting

> "If they are to work, they should work immediately. They don't want to work? I'm fine. Worst case is zero profit."

### Visualization Reinforced

Always be controlled. Find and enter early. Sell into strength. Think 3 days ahead. Think 30 days ahead. BTFD when Fed is active. Process orientation.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Don't Micromanage Swing Trades on 1-Min Charts

> "If you have three, four positions, then how many are you going to look at? I want to make money with least amount of effort."

If a setup is right, it should work without micromanaging on sub-daily charts. The signal is more than enough. Day traders should look at sub-second charts; swing traders should not.

### Key Teaching: ADR Is a Backward-Looking Indicator

> "ADR tells you average daily range. But you're selecting a stock after its 20-day ADR has gone high, when its ADR was low when it was setting up. By that time, the move might be over."

Stocks selected by momentum (TI65) all have high ADR — but that's AFTER the move started. Low ADR stocks breaking out with 9M EP catalyst will have high ADR on the breakout day — you miss the move waiting for ADR to confirm.

### Trade Factory: Zero Actionable Setups

Ran complete trade factory (9M EP, combo bullish/bearish, LTV, anticipation, reversal). Found nothing worth taking.

> "The fact that we are going through this and we are not even finding anything, just one or two, tells me that the market is not yet ready for a move."

Apple as a delayed reaction? Gapped down, tricky to chart. Not worth it.

### Market Outlook: Big Opportunity Ahead

> "I see a big opportunity in the market now. The market has corrected. Out of every market correction, you can really make easy money. As long as you don't get chopped during this phase."

V-shaped recovery probability: ~10%. Most likely: choppy range for multiple weeks. The turn will be obvious — 1000-1500+ stocks at 4% breakout breadth thrust. Current count: only 113.

> "When the market turns, you'll see a helicopter flying over your head with a banner."

### CBOE Position Closed

Took 80% profit yesterday (+$7.75). Remaining 20% stopped at breakeven. Volatility play complete.

### Key Teaching: Anticipation Means Buying BEFORE Confirmation

> "The idea behind anticipation is to buy before the confirmation comes in, so that your risk is low."

If you wait for weeks-of-bar confirmation, it becomes a breakout — then why call it anticipation? Anticipation is about low-risk early entry. Same logic as buying SIPs/EP using OPG orders instead of waiting for opening range breakout (which can be 8% by itself).

### Key Teaching: Exhaustion Setup (RBA Global Question)

Member asked about RBA Global as a bottom bounce. Pradeep clarified:

> "For a bottom bounce, requirements: 3 days down, strong close, T2108 below 20, index component."

RBA is NOT an index component of S&P 500 (only S&P 400). It's an exhaustion setup — selling exhausts, then a turn. Made money on it? Yes. Replicable? Not as a systematic methodology trade.
