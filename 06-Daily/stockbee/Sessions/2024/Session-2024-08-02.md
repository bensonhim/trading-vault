# Process Day: Coffee Shop Analogy, 4% Breakout Is Structural, Marry Your Setup

> Day 2 of market sell-off. 68% stocks making new lows. TNA short carried with breakeven stop. EQIX single closed for +$18 profit. Key teaching: "Find something structural in the market — 4% breakout is like coffee. People buy coffee every day. It's inherent to the nature of the market." If you can make 5% per trade 40 times a year, that's enough. The complete framework: momentum burst is your coffee shop. Stock selection (buckets, funds, sugar babies, IPO 10, bill 10, FSS, FHP) is what you sell. Delayed reaction, anticipation, VCP are all variations. Marry a setup. Marry a timeframe. No setup = no future. Top 3 trader problems: no setup, no situational awareness, process errors (not selling into strength, no stops, taking too many trades).

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Regime: Bearish — Attempted Rally in Small Caps Failed

> "Situational awareness: bearish. Attempted rally in small caps failed. Larger stocks already in downtrend. Selective breakouts will still work once the fast pace of selling is over."

Gap down expected. BTFD traders will try to buy the dip, but institutional selling continues.

### 20% Study: Range Expansion Is Structural

> "Find something structural in the market where you can make 5% per trade. If you can do that 40 times a year, it's more than enough."

**Coffee shop analogy**: Want to become a millionaire in America? Open a coffee shop. Coffee costs 20¢ to make, sells for $1.50. Multiply by 1,000 cups × 365 days. Simple, structural, inherent to human nature.

> "4% breakout is like coffee. It is inherent to the nature of the market. Whenever there is a 4% breakout, the stock tends to move in the direction of that breakout."

- 4% breakout → stock goes up 24-27% in 2-3 days (multiple examples shown)
- 4% breakout → stock goes up 35% in 5 days (another example)
- Sometimes 4% breakouts fail — "that's the nature of the market"
- **The question is not "will it work every time?" but "is this structural enough to build a process around?"**

### Key Teaching: Buy Swing at Start of Swing (Repeated 3rd Day)

> "If you cannot identify the start of a swing, the game is over. It's the single most difficult lesson for traders."

Same core message as July 30 and 31. Examples shown: every 20%+ move starts with a visible range expansion. Buying on day 2 or 3 is a compromise with 97% probability of capital wipeout.

### Key Teaching: Become Process-Oriented

> "This game is about making and keeping profit. Become process-oriented."

Boot camp member up 42% in 2 months → he's taking singles, keeping profits, and **taking breaks**. Another member up 20% then gave it all back → didn't sell into strength, went big on next trade.

**Process = daily routine:**
- Wake up, same time
- Run the same scans in the same order
- Same watchlists, same tools, same monitors
- Same setup criteria, same entry, same exit (80/20)
- Don't change the formula every month

> "If you run a coffee shop, do you change your coffee every day? No. Same equipment, same water, same cup."

### Key Teaching: Marry a Setup

> "If you don't have a setup, rest of the things don't matter. Marry a setup. Marry a timeframe."

Top 3 trader problems:
1. **No setup** — the #1 issue
2. **No situational awareness** — buying breakouts on selling days
3. **Process errors** — not selling into strength, no stops, taking too many trades, not giving trades room

Even if you find a setup, most people don't stick with it for more than a few months. "If it doesn't work on two trades, they're out."

### Key Teaching: Don't Ask for "More Details"

> "There is no guide for singles because singles is not a setup. Momentum burst is the setup. Singles are what you get from the setup."

Repeated frustration with members asking for "more details" on range expansion and delayed reaction. The concept is simple: range expansion → direction. Delayed reaction = EP with a pullback. "Just start making the coffee."

## 10 AM Meeting

### Trade Factory: Very Few Actionable Setups

Combo bullish: 33 stocks. Combo bearish: far more. Situational awareness: breakouts not likely to work. "I have zero threshold for getting chopped."

### Tesla Short Carried

Only position held from yesterday. Stop moved to breakeven. "The bullet should keep going down." If it reverses, out immediately.

### Controlled Risk Taking: Don't Buy Breakouts in This Market

> "Is it a good setup? Yes. Am I going to get into it? No. Let it go without me."

Multiple good-looking setups (McDonald's, BGC Global, Bright Horizons) — all range expansions, all start of swing. But situational awareness says breakouts will fade. Discipline: don't take trades that break your rules.

> "I know it's dangerous to go in this market. I'm not a day trader. Why will I take a long?"

### Key Teaching: Abhimanyu Story — Know Your Exit

> "In Indian mythology, Abhimanyu knows how to enter a war formation, but he doesn't know how to exit. He enters successfully. What happens to him? He gets killed."

Know where you're going to exit before you enter. 3-5 day momentum burst = sell into strength. Don't hold a Timas beyond its move.

### Key Teaching: Size Matters (Dan Zanger Example)

> "The secret of big returns is size."

Dan Zanger made 27,000% returns. His secret: putting full account size on every trade, trading sub-$10M float small caps. Mark Minervini won the US Investing Championship using oversized positions and 80/20 selling.

> "Even on a single, I put in size. EQIX — 800 shares, $800 stock, in a million dollar account. That's what moves accounts."

But: **size is the last thing to crack**. First: setup confidence. Second: situational awareness. Third: process discipline. Only then: size.

### Key Teaching: Friday Shorts — Think 3 Days Ahead

> "On a Friday, I go short with big positions, and I wake up Monday and everything gaps up. Takes out my stop."

Short side requires extreme tactical skill. Wait for counter-trend rallies, then short those. Don't short aggressively on day 2 of selling — probability of Monday gap-up bounce is high.

### Key Teaching: Range Expansion Is Anatomy

> "Understanding range expansion is like wanting to become a doctor without understanding anatomy and physiology. You can become a voodoo doctor, but not a real one."

If you don't understand range expansion and start of swing, you cannot be a swing trader. It's that fundamental.

### Member Problems Diagnosed

- **Taking 10+ trades/day** — too many, most will fail in this market
- **Finding 5-6 EPs per day** — "They're wasting their time. Real EPs: max 3-5 per year."
- **ARDX** — not a real EP, members arguing it is because they found it

### Tool Discussion: Don't Chase New Tools

> "I have 16 emails from people pitching me their tools. Do I even respond? No. My trade factory works like a charm. Why would I break something that's not broken?"

Freeze your tools. MarketSmith, Briefing, The Fly, Trade Ideas — total cost <$5,000/year, less than 1% of trading capital.

### Key Teaching: Don't Take Trades That Break Your Rules

> "If I see situational awareness this red, I'm not going to go in. It's like a hurricane — surfers die during hurricane season because they can't control themselves."

Red = red light. Green = green light. Don't overthink it.

### Trading Problems Session

Members asked to share their problems. Common themes:

1. **No setup** — the #1 issue. "99% of the time, they just try to justify some angle."
2. **No conviction** — "If you don't have conviction in your setup, you'll flirt from setup to setup."
3. **Process errors** — not selling into strength, no stops, taking too many trades, not giving trades room
4. **"I'm not making money"** — not a specific problem. Is it entry? Exit? Size? Situational awareness?
5. **Selling problem** — "Sell 80%, keep 20%. Move stop on remaining 20%. Sell in tranches."

### Key Teaching: EP Fill Strategy

> "If I expect an EP to make 50%+ and it makes 40% in one day, I will fill because I know it's not sustainable. But my objective on EPs is to hold for full potential."

EP = home run approach. Give it room to run. Singles = sell into strength approach.

### Key Teaching: 4% Breakout Selection

When there are multiple 4% breakout candidates:
1. Select the one with **lowest market capitalization**
2. Select the one with **best earnings quality**
