### Two Factories: Singles vs Home Runs

> "There are two separate factories. This is a factory for home runs, and this is a factory for singles. Completely different way of trading."

**Home runs (9M EP):** Give room to run. Don't move stop up too early. 20%+ moves possible.

**Singles:** Peel aggressively. Position size 25-50% of account. Target 2.5% stop or less. Aim for 5-10% move → moves account 2.5-5%.

> "People underappreciate 3-5 day trades where you can move your account in that increment."

### Position Sizing Logic

- **Singles:** 25-70% of account, 2.5% stop or less
- **9M EP/Home runs:** 50-100% of account when risk is near-zero (like TNA entry)
- **Small caps need 10-15% stops** — adjust size down or use margin for singles on same account
- **High-priced stocks** → can get 1-2% stops → allows bigger position sizing
- **2,500/5,000 fund stocks** → can do size, move account 4-5% per single

### Small Cap vs High Priced: Risk-Reward

> "Why do people blow up? Wider stop and 3-4 trades don't work. The wider stop is what blows up people. You put a 2.5% stop on small caps, you're going to get stopped out."

Small caps are "sexy" but require 10-15% stops. High-priced stocks with 1-2% stops allow safer, larger positions. Singles on high-priced names with fund backing (2,500+) can move your account meaningfully.
