# News Drives Moves: 49/77 Stocks, Define Your Setup or Find Another Job

> Last day before vacation. **20% study reveals: 49 out of 77 stocks had news catalysts at the start of their move.** Less than half were in IBD 250. News + neglect is what drives big moves, not earnings/sales screens. Market gapping up but set up for a **fade** — Pradeep called it in the morning. Core teaching hammered repeatedly: **define your setup, or find another job.** Start of a swing is non-negotiable. Consolidation breakouts are the most tempting traps. Only 1 anticipation idea found all day. Going on vacation after this meeting.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### 20% Study Deep Dive: News Is What Drives Moves

> "Out of 77 stocks, 49 had news at the beginning. News is what is the most important thing."

Quick dirty analysis of every stock in the 20% up study: **49 out of 77 had news catalysts** — earnings, buyouts, CEO changes, sector news. Not random breakouts. Not chart patterns. News.

### Key Teaching: News + Neglect = What Moves Markets

The stocks that moved 20%+ were: news-driven (earnings, buyouts, catalysts) and neglected (small caps, IPO10, low institutional ownership). IBD 250 stocks? Only 30 out of 77. **Less than half.** So much for "stocks with earnings and sales make the biggest moves."

### Key Teaching: Create Insight Through Observation

> "William O'Neil learned by studying past winners. He was trading a longer-term method. If you're trading a 20% move, RS line, ADR, dollar volume — none of that matters. When there's a new catalyst, anything works."

Do your own 20% study. Mark each stock: news or no news? Earnings or random? Small cap or large? This creates insight. Insight creates edge.

### Key Teaching: Market Set Up for a Fade

> "I think the market is ideally set for a fade. Everyone is chasing, FOMO is at a sell."

Gap up morning = fade likely. Sit on hands or take small positions. Context matters.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Self-Efficacy Belief = #1 Success Determinant

> "Self-efficacy belief is the foundation of success in life. Without it, nothing happens."

People who succeed: find solutions to problems. People who don't: sit and think. 12-year-old kid wrote email asking about 9M EP. That's self-efficacy. People with CA, CMT, CFA still can't trade — they have analysis but no formula approach.

### Key Teaching: Analysis Doesn't Make Money — Formula Does

> "Analysis doesn't make you money. What makes you money is a formula approach."

Someone spent 18 years perfecting technical analysis, then told Pradeep his momentum burst method was wrong. Chart reading, double bottoms, moving averages — none of it makes money without a process.

### Key Teaching: Hired Someone Who Lost $150K, Now Makes $50K/Month

Same story: same setups (9M EP, combo, SIPs). Process orientation. Taking profits quickly. Putting stops. Going to work after first hour. No secret sauce.

### Key Teaching: 49 Position — Bought Day 1, Stopped Out

Pradeep bought 49 on day 1 start-of-swing. Got stopped out. The correct entry was day 2. **Buying on day 1 of swing = common mistake.** Start of swing matters — you need to be at the START, not chasing.

### Key Teaching: Lumen — 2nd Day of Swing, Up 20%+

Lumen not selected because it's the 2nd day of a swing, already up 20%+. Can't put a 2.5% stop. Would need wider risk. Not worth it at this stage.

### Key Teaching: Define Your Setup — Platinum Blondes vs Redheads

> "If you decide you only date platinum blondes, then don't date redheads. Define your setup."

Same analogy from yesterday, reinforced. Define your criteria. Stick to it. If you dilute, you end up with "Walmart models" and wonder why your trading sucks.

### Key Teaching: High-Priced Stocks Can Start from Any Level

Berkshire Hathaway, Nvidia, etc. — high-priced stocks don't need a base. They can start a swing from any level. That's why FHP (focus on high-priced) is a qualifier.

## 2 PM Meeting (3 PM)

### Key Teaching: Start of a Swing — Non-Negotiable

> "If you're a swing trader and you're not buying at the start of a swing, just find another job."

Buying on day 2, day 3, day 4 = chasing. Start of swing = day 1. That's where the probability is highest. Everything after is extended risk.

### Key Teaching: Consolidation Breakouts = Heartbreak Hotel

> "They look so nice because there's a long consolidation, they're breaking out, and then you get into it, and then nothing happens."

Consolidation breakouts are the most TEMPTING traps. Why? Because they look perfect. Clean breakout from a base. But without a catalyst and volume surge, they fail. Over and over.

Need: catalyst + volume surge + start of a swing. Without all three, it's heartbreak hotel.

### Key Teaching: Screen Time Makes You Good

> "Screen time is what is going to make you a good trader. A lot of screen time."

Go through the 15%+ study daily. Mark where you should have bought, where you should have sold. This is how you internalize pattern recognition. No shortcut.

### Key Teaching: Prioritize, Prioritize, Prioritize

From the trade factory, you might get 60+ candidates. First pass: eliminate everything that doesn't meet your criteria. Second pass: prioritize by quality. Third pass: select only the best 2-3 setups. That's how you trade with focus.

### Key Teaching: 15% Study Shows What's Working Now

Carvana was a consolidation breakout yesterday — no catalyst, no volume surge. Result: it's a trap. Compare to Axon, Bristol Myers Squibb — 9M EP with catalyst and volume. Those work.
