# Selective Rally Begins: Pullback Looms as Six-Week Surge Exhausts Itself

> After one of the most relentless six-week rallies in market history, short-term pullback is the highest probability play — the broad-based rally is over, and only selective setups will work going forward.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Short-Term Pullback, Long-Term Bullish

Pradeep opened 2024 with clear dual-timeframe awareness:

> "My situational awareness — short term, likely to be a pullback. Because the market is extended. Most of the stocks are extended."

> "Long term, bullish. Short term, pullback likely."

The critical context: the market had done in six weeks what typically takes 18 months, with only 2-3 negative days in the entire rally.

### The Phase Shift: Broad Rally → Selective Rally

> "Now we'll move into the second phase, which is very selective rally. In the last one and a half months, it didn't matter — everything was going up. Selective rally is what is likely to happen."

The key implication: **9,000,000 delayed reaction** and **9,000,000 breakdown** become the primary setups. Broad-based buying is over.

### SIPs for 2024

Pradeep laid out his structural framework for the year:

| Concept | Rule |
|---------|------|
| **IPO 10** | Stocks make biggest moves in first 10 years of IPO |
| **Built-in** | Biggest movers have market cap below $10B |
| **Stories** | 95% of the 100 biggest moves each year will be story-driven (Buystuff) |
| **Stocks in Play** | Fresh news = catalyst; builds methods around this |
| **Linearity** | Proxy for strong buying; avoid choppy stocks |
| **Plastics** | Healthcare, Technology, Consumer Discretionary make biggest moves |

> "If you build your methods around stocks in play, IPO 10, built-in — you are more likely to make money."

### The Extension Problem

> "There are 200,278 stocks up 25% in a quarter, only 470 are down. That kind of excessive strength is not sustainable."

Market correction comes via **price** (pullback) or **time** (sideways chop). Both are likely here.

### The Delayed Reaction Lesson

> "Delayed reaction means a delayed reaction. If your wife tells you to wash the dishes and you wash it after three days — that's not a good delayed reaction."

**Key point**: A delayed reaction setup is the market's delayed response to a catalyst. The entry window is narrow — once the stock has already moved, it's too late.

### Don't Chase Extended Stocks

On Moderna (up 13%):

> "You want me to select a stock which is up 13% now? What is your risk reward on a stock like this if you buy now?"

> "If you want to short something, short something which has gone up a lot. People were in love with MARA — that's where it happens."

## 2 PM Meeting

### Linearity: The First Filter

> "The first thing which you should be looking at whenever you're looking at any setup is — linearity. Because linearity will directly affect your stop."

Pradeep's blunt assessment on choppy stocks:

> "If you trade stocks like ExxonMobil, Bristol-Myers Squibb — with very close, choppy stops — what do you think your win rate will be? Very low."

> "The more choppier stock you trade, the more choppier your equity curve is going to be."

### Market Snapshot: Selective, Not Broad

> "Even though the red worm was going up, we have more than 300 stocks over 4% today. So where is the pullback? The pullback is in select stocks."

Overall: 60% stocks making new highs, 40% making new lows — more buying than selling, but rotation is active.

### Best Setups Summary

| Setup Type | Stocks | Quality |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Bullish** | [[CI]] (Cigna), [[ABBV]], [[CDIO]] | Best of day |

Pradeep tightened his anticipation scan filters:

> "I want to spend less time trading and more times giving out tickets to North Korea."
