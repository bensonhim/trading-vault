# Selling Day: TNA Stopped Out, EQIX Single +$18, Be First or Be Smarter

> Fed's July 31 dovish signal reversed overnight — broad-based institutional selling hit the market. TNA's 9M breakout from yesterday was a "KLPD" (let down) — stopped out at breakeven, no emotions, move on. EQIX range expansion from yesterday worked perfectly as a single: +$18. Key teaching: "Buy a swing at the start of a swing" — the single most difficult lesson for traders. The complete framework: stock selection (buckets, funds, sugar babies, IPO 10, bill 10, FSS, FHP) × setup type (momentum burst = breakout/bottom/continuation/consolidation, delayed reaction, anticipation). All are variations of the same core concept. "When they read the world, they take the whores, the Johns, and the piano player."

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Regime: Bullish but Choppy → Sell-Off Day

Situational awareness started "bullish but choppy" but rapidly deteriorated into broad-based selling. 80%+ stocks making new lows by end of day.

### Delayed Reaction: Deep Dive

> "A delayed reaction is just a setup where there is a catalyst, and you wait for it to offer a lower-risk entry."

Stocks overreact on earnings day → pull back for 3-5 days → then offer a great entry. Focus on **bucket 0 and bucket 1** stocks (already have a catalyst = earnings reaction).

Mobileye example: bad earnings → overreaction → short the delayed reaction pullback. Same pattern on the long side.

### Complete Framework: One Slide That Explains Everything

> "I can summarize all my trading into this one slide."

**Stock Selection Strategies** (why buy):
- Buckets (growth-based)
- Funds (ownership-based: 5,000/2,500/2,000 funds)
- Sugar Babies (persistent 50%+ swing tendency)
- IPO 10 (younger companies = bigger moves)
- Bill 10 (market cap below $10B)
- FSS (Find Sales Star: 39%+ growth)
- FHP (Find High Priced: for singles with 1% risk)

**Setup Types** (how to buy):
- Momentum burst (breakout from bottom, continuation, consolidation — all same concept)
- Delayed reaction (momentum burst after a pullback from catalyst)
- Anticipation (pre-catalyst entry — low win rate)

> "It's like having a Coke and you create vanilla Coke, Coke Zero, Cherry Coke. They're essentially the same thing."

For buckets and funds: buy **any** range expansion at the start of a swing (bottom, continuation, consolidation — doesn't matter). For sugar babies: buy from any level without asking questions.

### Key Teaching: Don't Marry Stocks

> "100 out of 100 times, I would buy TNA [at that setup]. It was a damn good setup. It didn't work. Shit happens. Cut your losses and move on."

TNA reversed after yesterday's Fed rally. Stopped out at breakeven. Zero emotions. "If it didn't work, it didn't work. On to the next one."

### Visualization

Always be controlled, controlled risk taking, find and enter early, sell into strength, think 3 days ahead, BTFD when Fed is active, plastics, stories, younger stocks, linearity, sugar babies (some new ones emerging — ISPS), IPO 10/Bill 10, FSS, FHP, BDE, 80/20, fill position, FFM.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Trade Factory: Almost Nothing Actionable

Combo bullish: 45 stocks. Combo bearish: 340 stocks. Overwhelming selling pressure. "I don't see anything which is really standing out."

### Key Teaching: Be First, Be Smarter, or Cheat

> "What have I told you since the first day you stepped into my office? There are three ways to make a living in this business. Be first, be smarter, or cheat."

Margin Call quote. If you wait for end-of-day confirmation to buy an EP, "you're not even trading the setup." The money is all in being first.

### Singles: Pure Pattern Recognition

> "Singles is all pattern-based. There is no brain involved. I don't even know what Equinix does. I just saw the pattern. It's up $18 today."

EQIX bought yesterday as range expansion, sold into strength today for +$18. Pure pattern trade — no fundamental analysis needed.

To develop pattern recognition muscle: **deep dives / screen time**. "Instead of reading books on trading, spend a thousand hours of screen time."

### Key Teaching: Buy Swing at Start of Swing

> "If you're buying on the second or third day, you're late to the party. The most difficult lesson for traders to understand: buy a swing at the start of a swing."

Same teaching from yesterday, reinforced even more strongly. Buying extended setups has 97% probability of wiping out capital over time.

### R (Aralez) — The Only Long Idea

Sugar Baby called → you answer. Small position only because price point is too low to move accounts meaningfully (would need 200,000 shares). "I'm not that crazy."

### TNA KLPD: The Lesson

> "Once KLPD happened, the trade was over. If you're still thinking this is going to work, you're a very romantic person."

KLPD = "KhLPD" (Hindi slang for letdown). The moment TNA reversed from yesterday's breakout, the trade was done. No praying, no calling people, no hoping. Close the trade.

### Key Teaching: Protect Trading Capital

> "I see my trading capital as the biggest advantage I have. I don't want to lose it under any circumstances. If I protect my capital, there is another day."

On selling days, sit in cash. Don't fight institutional selling. "Elephants are trampling the ground — do I want to go out there? The answer is no."

### Max (MAX) — Member Question

Member excited about 800% EPS surprise. Pradeep dug deeper: earnings included one-time items, sales growth was from acquisition, FTC investigation ongoing. "I can see things which raise red flags on this page itself." Not a real catalyst.

### CCJ Short — Possible but Not Compelling

Uranium stock down 13% already, choppy. Risk/reward not favorable for shorts on a down day when you'd need a 5%+ stop. "Not really something I'm going to get into."

## 2 PM Meeting

### The Selling Accelerates

> "The Fed day has acted as a catalyst for reversal. Previously, Fed acted as a positive catalyst. Now it's acted as a negative catalyst."

80%+ stocks making new lows. Triple ATLs dominating 9M scan = broad-based selling. Program selling visible on charts — pause, sell, pause, sell.

### Who's Selling?

> "Everybody I know is buying. They're buying TNA, they're buying some stock. Who's selling? The big guys."

Institutional selling. Retail is bottom-fishing while institutions are liquidating.

### The "Piano Player" Quote

> "When they raid the whorehouse, they take the whores, the Johns, and the piano player."

On days like this, everything sells — good stocks and bad stocks alike.

### Short Side: Story Stocks Collapse

Stocks that went up on narratives (NVAX, story stocks) are now collapsing. Short setups emerging but market already down 13%+ on many — risk/reward unfavorable.

- [[TZA]] — ultra-short ETF, +10% (day trade only)
- [[CCJ]] — possible short but too extended
- Short ETFs dominating 9M scan

### Key Teaching: Don't Argue With the Market

> "The market is telling you everything you need to know. Market reaction is what counts."

Don't argue about whether the sell-off is "real" or temporary. Accept it and find setups (short or wait for long).

### EQIX: The Successful Single

> "Equinix was such a nice setup. $18 profit. Stop never came into play. Made my money."

Bought yesterday as range expansion on a high-priced stock. Sold into strength today. "Higher the price, better" for dollar breakouts — $30-70 moves on $800+ stocks vs $2-3 moves on cheap stocks.

### Key Teaching: Buy Swing at Start of Swing (Reinforced AGAIN)

> "The single most difficult lesson for traders to understand: buy a swing at the start of a swing."

Showed chart of a member asking about buying a stock that's already up 3 days. "97% probability that if you keep doing this, you'll wipe out your capital." FOMO = buying from emotion, not rational mind.

### Fill Position + Move Stop to Breakeven

> "My first objective, once I get into a trade, is to try and move my stop to breakeven. Because if I get to breakeven, the worst is I'm going to lose $0. Best is I'm going to make money."

Then 80/20 rule: sell 80% quickly, keep 20% for runners. These tactics engineer a high win rate.

### Anticipation Setups: NOT Today

> "I'm not going to be buying any anticipation setups here."

Market too weak. Only looking for concrete setups — singles or shorts.

### Protect Capital, Come Back Tomorrow

> "Enjoy your summer. Go to the swimming pool, spend time on the beach, then come back."

Sit in cash. The market will set up again. Our advantage: we're not moving billions.
