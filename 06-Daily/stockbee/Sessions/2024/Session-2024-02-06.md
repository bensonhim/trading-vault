# Breakouts Overwhelmingly Not Working, Catalyst Is King, and Surface Area of Luck

> "Breakouts are not working. That is the overwhelming theme you need to be aware of."

Choppy, narrow rally with massive under-the-hood deterioration. Breakouts failing, shorts struggling, only select biotechs and SIPs working. Pradeep delivers a blunt assessment: sit out, go short, or trade SIPs — but don't buy breakouts. Key lecture on catalyst as the single most important edge, and why low-price stocks demand wider stops.

## 11 AM Meeting — Breakouts Overwhelmingly Not Working, Execution Is Art

### Overwhelming Theme: Breakouts Not Working

> "The overwhelming theme which you need to be aware of is that breakouts are not working."

- IWM in a funk = small stocks not working
- [[AAPL]] not in good shape, [[META]] giving up gains (not a surprise — does this every time)
- Only [[NVDA]] and [[SMCI]] are rockets
- Breakout traders: sit out or switch to different setup

### Can't Build a Career on Outliers

> "How many SMCI are you going to find in a year? You cannot build your trading career on outliers."

- [[SMCI]] = perfect setup, but these come once or twice a year
- Build on what happens thousands of times — singles, momentum burst, 9M
- MPA members missed Nov-Dec rally because consolidation setups appeared late in the move

### Execution Is Art, Not Formula

> "This is an art. Everybody is looking for a perfect answer. There is no perfect answer."

- Pradeep entered [[SMCI]] at 1 minute — watched it till last second, entered when it started recovering
- No OPG because no clear stop level — watched and timed entry
- Beta spread matters for position sizing, not for entry timing
- Each situation is unique — no "optimal strategy" template

### Market Context and Forward Guidance

- Market is forward-looking — rewards forward guidance over past earnings
- Cyclical stocks (airlines, commodities, shipping): buy when they're "in shit"
- [[ULCC]]: 5.1-day short interest, but one-day wonder with no follow-through guarantee
- Economy strong, inflation coming down — but market needs to consolidate first

### Low-Price Stocks = Wider Stops

- Low-price stocks have inherently higher volatility
- Need 7-10% stops, not 2.5%
- Can't put full position size → takes more trades to move account
- "Everybody is attracted to low price stock, but they don't realize low price stocks have inherently higher volatility"

## 2 PM Meeting — Delayed Reaction Fading, Catalyst Creates Charts, Surface Area of Luck

### Delayed Reaction: Dropping from Daily Process

- Delayed reaction scan has ~400 stocks — too many to review daily
- Pradeep: "This is the last session where I'll do this delayed reaction"
- Will use delayed reaction in after-hours watch-list mode only
- Going back to 9M + combo + bearish scans for daily process
- Key insight: the good delayed-reaction plays are the ones you identify ahead of time, not real-time

### Catalyst Creates Charts

> "Catalyst creates charts. Catalyst is very important."

- [[Altimmune]]: nothing happening, then catalyst arrives → stock just keeps going
- If starting over, "the number one thing I would focus on is catalyst"
- Catalyst as an edge "is never going to go away"
- Small caps fade because their businesses are terrible — that's structural, not cyclical
- Same on short side: catalyst drives the move

### Consolidation Breakouts Everywhere, Waiting for Breadth Thrust

- Dozens of stocks setting up for consolidation breakout ([[GS]], [[HD]], [[CDW]], [[ABBV]], many more)
- "One day there'll be a market breadth thrust — that day, all these consolidation breakouts will break out same day"
- But not happening this month
- 1400%+ days won't work in this market — need bigger breadth thrust
- VCP patterns show up late in bull markets (Mark Miner's style)

### Watch Lists and Trading Styles

- No golden rule — watch lists work if they're small (25-30 stocks max)
- Different setups for different market phases:
  - Continuation setups → early in rally
  - Consolidation setups → late in rally
  - SIPs → always works (but requires execution skill)
- "Every setup works under certain conditions"

### Surface Area of Luck

> "I use the surface area of luck philosophy to get lucky first."

- Pradeep joined every service in first 2 years — Dan Zanger, MPA, everything
- Spent a lot of money but had a breakthrough
- "A lot of this is luck. If you meet the right person or join the right service at the right time, you save a lot of time."
- Luck is not random — increase surface area by being in more places, trying more things
- Largest number of new StockBee members coming from MPA (most aggressively promoted service currently)

### Market Outlook

- "Easy part of this rally is done. It will take choppiness."
- Range-bound for some time
- Market priced in rate cuts in 6 weeks — now consolidating
- Economy fundamentally strong — jobs everywhere except IT/Wall Street
- When small companies start posting growth again → sustained rally for 2-3 years
