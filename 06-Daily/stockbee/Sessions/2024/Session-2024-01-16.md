### 9M EP vs Combo LTV — Different Setups

9M EP is catalyst-based (story, earnings, event). Combo LTV is pattern-based (continuation, consolidation, bottom bounce). They serve different purposes:

| Setup | Purpose | Account Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 9M EP (catalyst) | Move account 50-100% | Big winners |
| Combo LTV (pattern) | Move account 1-5% | Consistent small wins |

> "This is catalyst based, this is pattern based. Why people are confused about this simple thing?"

### Finding Your Style — The Deep Dive

A member asked about 80/20 and position sizing. This became a 30-minute masterclass:

**High win rate vs low win rate — engineer your style:**

| Approach | Win Rate | Drawdown | Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| EG style (SA + find & enter early + 80/20) | High | Low | Process discipline, high-priced stocks, close stops |
| KQ style (catalyst + let winners run) | ~30% | 50%+ | Catalyst selection, wide stops, analytical skill, emotional tolerance |

> "Can you make money with 30% win rate? Yes. But can everybody make money with 30% win rate? No. KQ is a unique personality who doesn't mind 50% drawdown."

**80/20 increases win rate regardless of position size:**

1. Lock in 80% profit → high win rate
2. If stock fades after profit → you've locked gains
3. High win rate → confidence → can put on large size

> "I don't want drawdowns. That is my number one thing. I've engineered everything to have low drawdowns. Find and enter early. Situational awareness. 80/20. High-priced stocks. Liquid shorts."

### BA — Single Trade Account Mover

> "BA moved the account by more than 5%. Find 20 trades like BA and you can double your account."

### Short Side — Be Selective Too

- BA short: clear catalyst, large volume, delayed reaction
- DAL short: worked
- Spirit Aero (BA supplier): same catalyst category

### Low-Price Stock Stops

On a $4 stock with $1 stop = 25% risk. That's unworkable.

> "If you're trading with 33% stop risk, you are not going to be around in this business for more than one year."

Stop placement: low of day or half of day. Dollar risk determines position size, not the other way around.
