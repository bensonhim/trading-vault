# Pullback in a Bull Market, NVIDIA Short for Valentine's, and Know Your Setups

> "Bull markets don't just top with one sudden act. There is always a gradual erosion of leadership."

Day after CPI selloff. Pradeep's read: overreaction, not a top. 9M breakdowns = zero. Leaders ([[NVDA]], [[SMCI]]) didn't even get scratched. He shorts [[NVDA]] for Valentine's Day money (weak structure shot), scores on [[MARA]] (40% in three days), and day-trades [[DTSS]] and [[UBER]]. The lesson: run your trade factory, know your setups, and don't let one red day destroy your process.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM) — Overreaction, Not a Top

### CPI Day Was Just One Day

- CPI data released 12 times a year — one bad day cannot define your strategy
- Before CPI, small caps had strong buying, breakouts working, breadth thrust
- "Will there be follow-through selling?" — that's the key question, not "is the top in?"

### Leaders Didn't Get Scratched

> "What happened to NVIDIA yesterday? Spent rest of the day recovering. What happened to SMCI? Actually went UP."

- The market leaders (AI/semis/crypto) didn't suffer real damage
- 9M breakdown scan: almost nothing near highs
- Stocks that are down (Walgreens, UPS, etc.) had their own problems
- "This is just overreaction. Market was due for a pullback"

### The First Phase of the Bull Is Over — Consolidations Setting Up

- Many small caps made first leg, now consolidating
- MDT/MDP scans show consolidation patterns everywhere
- These consolidations need catalyst to break out
- When they start breaking down instead → THEN worry about market top

### Will I Buy Breakouts? Yes — Selectively, in Hot Themes

- Biotech, technology, AI/semi/crypto — still the themes
- Random stocks won't work; you must be in hot themes
- "Will it be easy to trade? No. Will there be tradable opportunities? Yes."
- 20% study: biotech and tech dominating
- Pradeep has not changed his 401(k) allocation — bull market thesis intact

### Visualization (Unchanged)

- Control risk, find and enter early, sell into strength, T-3, T-30
- Builtin, IPO-10, sugar babies, linear stocks, stories stocks
- SIPs, FHP, BBT, anticipation (2T/3T, 80/20)
