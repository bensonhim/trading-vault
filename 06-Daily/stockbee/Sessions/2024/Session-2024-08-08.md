## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bearish, Choppy, Day 4 of Bounce

> "In a highly volatile bearish market, from day to day, you can have these kinds of moves. As a result, you can get disoriented. Before you realize, you can have big losses."

Day gap-ups followed by fade — typical high-volatility bearish market behavior. Breakouts fade. Shorts get stopped out on gap-ups. Close stops seldom work in high volatility.

### Key Teaching: Situational Awareness Is Higher-Order Skill

> "Most of the people who blow up, blow up because they have no situational awareness. They go heavy at the wrong time, either on the wrong side or long side."

If you understand situational awareness, you operate with a filter on top of your trading. Aggressive when setups align, sit on hands when they don't. 95% of the time market conditions are fine — it's only 5-10% that are bad. Avoid those and you'll have fewer drawdowns, higher win rate.

### Key Teaching: 20% Study — The ONLY Path to 20%+ Moves

> "If you want to find a 20% move in a week, there is only one way: breakouts and news. Those are the two paths to becoming a profitable trader."

Study the 167 stocks in the 20% down study. What triggers a 20% move? How does it start? How long does it last? The conclusion: **focus on breakouts and news.** This saves you enormous time — you won't waste time trying to make anticipation work or random setups work.

> "Listening doesn't make you a good trader. Finding out something which works and arriving at conclusion makes you a good trader."

### Visualization

Always be controlled. Find and enter early. Sell into strength. Think 3 days ahead. Think 30 days ahead. BTFD when Fed is active. Plastics. Stories. Sugar babies (Lumen = new sugar baby, others look sick). FFM.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Sugar Baby + 9M = Two Things That Still Work

> "9M works based on catalyst. Lumen had immediate catalyst. That's why it worked for 3 days."

9M EP and sugar babies are the only setups with high probability in this market. Lumen: 307M shares = sugar baby call. Always take it.

### Key Teaching: Don't Buy Random Breakouts — Qualify Them

> "Are we buying a random breakout? No. You buy a setup. Just because there are 700 stocks in the scan, there may be just one or two setups. Not only that, you also don't buy it if your situational awareness is bearish."

Scans are only candidates. Like a fisherman throwing a net and then measuring the fish — keep the 18-inch ones, throw back the rest. Every successful trader qualifies their breakouts.

### Key Teaching: ALAB — Unforced Error

Member put 35% of account on ALAB thinking it was an EP. But ALAB didn't react positively to earnings in after-hours (was down 2%). How can it be an EP if the stock doesn't gap up? The second condition of EP (gap up) was not met.

> "Unforced errors. There is no need to go into that at all."

### Key Teaching: X-Ray Analysis — Acceleration Matters

Look at declining revenue stocks vs. accelerating revenue stocks. ALAB: 62%, 57%, 51%, 48%, 39%, 35%, 29%, 27%, 26% — **decelerating**. Shark Ninja: 19%, 8%, 7%, 8% — **accelerating**. You want acceleration, not deceleration.

> "When Nvidia was going up, sales were accelerating. That's why NVIDIA made a move. Now it's decelerating."

### Trade Factory: Almost Nothing Actionable

Every other setup either didn't qualify or had wrong context:
- Palantir — delayed reaction, but 11% risk required in choppy market = no
- Shopify — earnings, faded after gap up, not confident
- UnitedHealth — nice anticipation, but market conditions wrong
- RTX — faded, not working
- Exact Sciences — delayed reaction, not confident it follows through

## 2 PM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Qualifying Criteria — The Fishing Net Analogy

> "Scans are only candidates. It's like you throw a fishing net, and then the fisherman measures the fish. If the fish is 18 inches, they keep it. If it is less, they just throw it back into the sea."

Context + qualifying criteria = what every successful trader does. Darvas had 8 qualifying criteria (all-time high, infant industry, best earnings, low capitalization, high volume surge, box breakout). He wasn't buying random boxes.

### Key Teaching: Context Matters — Dating Analogy

> "You want to date a woman and then you go to a funeral and start flirting with the newly single wife. Is that likely to work? Is the context right?"

Every setup has a context. If context is wrong, the setup won't work regardless of how good it looks.

### PDD — Worked for 3 Days Because Context Was Right

PDD was bucket 1, high-priced, Chinese market catalyst. That's why it worked despite bearish market. Context was right.

### Only One Qualified Setup Found

After scanning ~328 stocks through trade factory, only **one** met all qualifying criteria. Most were random breakouts in a choppy market that would get chopped.

### Key Teaching: Don't Shift Styles

> "If I suddenly shift my style and start doing mean reversion — do I have accumulated experience? It would take me 3-5 years to perfect. This has taken me 24 years."

Mean reversion works for some (MHP, quant traders), but they use multiple systems simultaneously. Don't shift styles just because your style isn't producing trades right now.

### Key Teaching: Exhaustion Bars Needed for Reversal Bullish

For reversal bullish setups to work, you need real selling with exhaustion bars. Current market has no real exhaustion — just random gaps and gap downs. Mini exhaustion at best. Need T2108 at single digits + real panic selling for proper reversals.

### Key Teaching: FOMO Is Beginner Trader's Problem

> "FOMO is going to be a problem for all beginner traders. They don't see the full market cycle. They think they'll miss out."

Over-trading, FOMO, no setup, no situational awareness, wanting to trade multiple strategies — all beginner problems.

### Key Teaching: Shorts Get Whipsawed in High Volatility

> "Market volatility is not good for shorting also, because then everything gaps up and you get stopped out, and then the stock starts going down."

High volatility = high correlation. Everything moves together. Shorts get stopped out on gap-ups, then stocks resume decline. Not ideal for swing shorts either.

### Market Outlook

> "Choppy period might last for weeks. A lot of stocks might still have second leg down. I'm going on vacation next week."

Volatility still at historical highs. Breakouts not likely to work. Need volatility to collapse completely for proper setups. Taking vacation because market conditions are unfavorable.
