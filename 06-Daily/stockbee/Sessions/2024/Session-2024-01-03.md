20% study at 17 = bullish territory. The pullback is a correction within a bull market, not a bear market.

### The Most Important Lesson: Oversold Beats Near-High

Pradeep delivered a pivotal teaching on market phases:

> "Every single time the market bounces, the stocks which are oversold are the ones which make the move. You can go back and look at this for the last ten years."

> "Then why are people continuing to tell you to focus on stocks near 52-week highs? [...] Most of the stocks which make the move are below 200-day moving average."

**Phase transition**: First 6-10 weeks of a bull market = oversold bounces dominate. After that = consolidation breakouts form.

> "Consolidation setups will start forming now. In the first phase, oversold stocks bounced. Second phase will be when you get consolidation breakouts."

## 2 PM Meeting

### Breakouts Are Failing

[[CI]] (Cigna) — good breakout yesterday, gave up all gains today. The 80/20 tactic saved profits.

> "That's why 80/20 is such an important part of the equation. That saves your bacon. Especially when you're doing swing trading."

### The 80/20 Tactic Explained in Detail

Pradeep detailed the math:

| Trade | Entry | Stop | Risk | 80% Exit | Result |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [[JPM]] short | $171.73 | $172.00 | 0.13% of account | $170.53 | Made ~$1.20/share on 80% |
| [[ABBV]] long | ~$156 | — | 0.5% of account | Took profit at ~$57 | 80% booked |
| [[CI]] long | — | Breakeven | — | Stopped on 20% | Small profit retained |

> "When I do 80/20, I'm oversizing the position. In a $1M account, 5,000 shares of JPMorgan at $171.73 — that's close to 85% of the account. When I took out 80%, I'm left with the original swing position I would have put in."
