## 2 PM Meeting

### Afternoon: Fading Continues, Zero Confidence

> "I have zero confidence currently in this market."

Red worm back on top. Most morning breakouts faded. Poor man's worm starting to fade. This is NOT the time to go aggressive.

### Key Teaching: Overconfidence Cycle

> "When you feel like God's gift to trading, that's when you should reduce your risk to very low."

A member (DG) asked about getting aggressive after Russell pullback — Pradeep explained the classic trap:

1. Breakouts work well → confidence soars → increase risk → market changes → drawdown
2. Solution: Never exceed 2% overnight risk. Reset account regularly (like PCB resets to $35-45K monthly)
3. "When you're doing really well, that's when you should think you're doing really bad"

> "PCB resets his account to $35-45K every month. Sometimes he makes $100K from $35K. People say he should be a billionaire. That's not how it works."

### Reliable Singles Factory — Focus for Choppy Markets

In choppy markets, switch to the **singles factory**:
- Use 2,500/5,000 fund scans for high-priced stocks with tight stops (1-2%)
- Each single moves account 4-5%
- SIPs for day traders — same stock can be traded intraday multiple times

### HIMS Closed

Closed HIMS position after 3 days — not making further move. Better opportunities for capital elsewhere.

### BDE (Buy the Dip) Timing Question

> "There's no simple answer. When there is range expansion in the indexes, doing the BDE (buy the dip) works much better. No range expansion in SPY right now — it's setup-dependent."

### Viking Therapeutics: Catalyst but No Follow-Through

Clear catalyst (weight loss drug), but last 3 times the same catalyst appeared, stock did nothing after the initial pop. Would wait for delayed reaction setup instead of chasing.

### Friday Guidance

- Choppy market likely to persist
- SIPs and selective singles are the right approach
- Zero confidence for aggressive swing positions
- If selling accelerates, cascading stops will hit everything again
