### 9M EP Is THE Best Setup

> "9M EP is my most important methodology, even better than classical EP. When 9M works, it goes 50%, 100%, 300%. The expectancy is different."

HIMS has history of 78% and 47% moves on 9M breakouts. CLF is bucket zero + 9M — both can make big moves.

> "Every time I break my absolute rule — buy on first day of swing — something happens. This is not a guideline. It's a RULE."

### Singles vs Home Runs: Two Different Factories

> "There are two separate factories. Home runs factory and singles factory. Completely different way of trading."

**Home runs (9M EP):** 8-10% stops, give room to run, 50-300% potential. Position size can be 50-100% of account when risk is near-zero.

**Singles:** 2.5% stops or less, peel aggressively, 5-10% moves, position size 25-70% of account. Hits of 2.5% per trade move account meaningfully.

> "People who struggle are trying to hit home runs. I want to hit lots of singles. Singles finance the home runs."

### Write Your Own Guide

> "If you want to understand the setup, write a guide. Write your guide for 9M. If you write it and post it on timeline, you will understand what you're talking about. Listening is passive."

### Tesla Short: Cult Stock

Tesla short taken at $222. Small position because Elon can pull rabbits out. Cult personality makes it unpredictable. 9M breakdown on earnings miss, but fanboys still buying.

### Position Sizing Deep Dive

- **Singles on high-priced stocks with 2,500+ fund ownership:** Can put 80% of account, 1-2% stops, moves account 4-5% per single
- **Singles on small caps:** Need 10-15% stops, adjust size down to 5-10% of account, moves account 1-2%
- **9M EP with zero risk (like TNA):** Can put 50-100% of account
- **MARA sugar baby example:** One trader made 90% of his year's profit just trading MARA swings

> "Why do people blow up? Wider stops and 3-4 trades don't work. Small cap with 2.5% stop = you're going to get stopped out. Win rate drops to 25% or less."

### Key Teachings

**On holding 9M EP trades:**
> "If you keep taking small profits, you'll never have a trade with a large profit. Some trades you have to give room to run. TNA — zero risk, best case 40-60%. Why would you sell for a small profit?"

**On 9M volume vs dollar volume:**
> "I use share volume, not dollar volume. Dollar volume cuts off stocks coming from lows. Volume shows participation. 9M shares is 9M shares regardless of price."
