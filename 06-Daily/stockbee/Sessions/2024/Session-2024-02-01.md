# NXT Red Flags, Process Mastery, and Delayed Reaction Precision

> "Don't call everything EP. If you call everything game-changing, you're going to be in trouble. EP is extremely rare — you're lucky to find two to five ideas in a year."

Post-FOMC day. Choppy, range-bound action. Short side is the only attractive side. Pradeep dissects NXT as a cautionary tale — 87M shares spun off from Flex, institutional holders ready to sell — and delivers an extended lecture on process mastery, reflection, and ranking your ideas.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM) — NXT Research Critique, Controlled Risk, Don't Call Everything EP

### SA: Choppy Range-Bound, Short Side Attractive

- Choppy range-bound action likely post-FOMC
- Correction of smaller magnitude or range-bound action more probable than straight down
- Long-term bullish — Powell signaled rate cuts coming
- Short side looks more attractive right now

### NXT: The Victoria's Secret Model Who Isn't

> "Before sending me an email claiming this is a Victoria's Secret model, just have a look at her."

- [[NXT]]: float discrepancy — 23M reported vs 121M actual
- 87M shares were spun off from Flex on December 31 to institutional shareholders
- Those institutions got shares for free — a 25-30% gap up = massive selling pressure
- Top-line numbers excellent, but "you have to see the red flags also"
- Not a dilution — it's a spin-off — but institutional holders will sell into strength
- "If I see a red flag, I'm not going to go anywhere"

### EP Requires Analytical Skills

> "You make money in EP if you are willing to peel the layers and go into details."

- EP is extremely rare — lucky to find 2-5 ideas per year
- Calling everything EP = guaranteed trouble
- Research beyond top-line numbers: float changes, shareholder composition, catalyst quality
- [[SOFR]]: float went from 110M to 927M — similar red flag pattern
- Controlled risk taking: "knowing what I know, if I go big on something like this, it doesn't make sense"

### SIPs and Risk Management

- SIPs exist, but how you play them matters — strategize with lowest risk possible
- [[Align]]: good setup but $4 spread = risk whipping
- Want to find entry points with less than 0.5% risk
- If that risk level isn't available, wait

## 2 PM Meeting — Process Mastery, Reflection, Ranking Ideas

### Process Mastery = Four Pillars

> "Process mastery consists of knowing the setups, knowing the scans, knowing the quality parameters, and execution skills."

1. **Setups** — EP, SIPs, 9M, delayed reaction, etc.
2. **Scans** — which scans to run, how to filter
3. **Quality parameters** — what makes a good vs. poor setup
4. **Execution** — when to enter, when to exit

### Process Loop + Quality Loop

- Daily process loop: run scans → identify setups → quality filter → execute → reflect
- Quality loop: set standards (like "only 18+ year whiskey") → enforce them
- If something isn't working, **fix the process** — don't externalize the problem
- "Reflect on it, fix the process, come back, do a better job"

### SIPs Quality Control

> "If you have five girls interested in you, rank them one, two, three."

- Rank SIPs ideas — focus only on #1 or #2
- [[Align]] was not a top-2 idea — negative catalyst quality, gapping up too much on 6% growth
- Quality filters for SIPs:
  - **Short interest >5 days** → automatic consideration
  - **Low float (<10M) + catalyst** → automatic qualification
  - **100%+ earnings/sales growth** → automatic qualification
  - FinWiz (free) has short interest data

### Watch List = 2-3 Stocks Maximum

> "If you have thirty, fifty stocks on your watch list, you are doing it wrong."

- Maximum 2-3 stocks on watch list at any time
- Better to do a damn good job on 3 stocks than a bad job on 600

### Delayed Reaction: Best with Urgency

- Best delayed reactions happen in 2-3 days (not weeks)
- If it takes too long, "the fun is gone"
- Ideal: catalyst → 2-3 day pause → immediate reaction
- [[KTX]], [[NKTX]], [[ARQT]], [[Fastenal]] = good delayed reaction setups
- [[Columbia Bank]] = ideal delayed reaction short (immediate after catalyst)

### NXT Post-Mortem

- NXT faded as predicted (morning analysis validated)
- Buying came in later, but fading concern was correct
- Sugar baby context better than breakout context in choppy market

### Process Decisions to Reduce Overwhelm

- "I'm going to focus only on 9M shorts and delayed reaction shorts with 1M+ volume"
- Focus on continuation breakouts + 9M — skip consolidation breakouts (high failure rate)
- Start eliminating things: "I don't have to do everything"
- Free up energy by narrowing focus

### Reflection Framework

> "The best thing you can do to become a good trader is to spend time reflecting."

- Reflect → fix process → come back tomorrow → do better
- Activities that foster reflection: walks, hikes, bike rides, kayaking — alone time
- Activities that kill reflection: football games, drinking with friends, social media bitching
- "If your personal life is in a mess, your trading will be in a mess"
- Fix process problems in 24-48 hours, not weeks
- Don't join the "bitching gang" — history is written by doers, not complainers
