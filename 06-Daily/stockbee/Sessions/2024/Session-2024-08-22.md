> **Setup hierarchy: 9M EP is the bread and butter. Anticipation and reversal are seductive but least important — "the Chinese girl texting you."** ERIE (9M breakout + catalyst) made money progressively selling 80% before halts. Story stocks season arrived — big stocks stalled, stories flying. Market showing first signs of selling (red worm on top). **Shamelessly copy what works first, improvise later.** If you had to drop one setup, drop anticipation.

### Key Teaching: Prioritize by Low NC and High Price

> "Lower the NC better it is... The one which is $100 plus with the low NC, and that's the one which I like."

Priority order for anticipation:
1. **SkyWater (SKYT)** — NC 0.03, $100+, 5000+ funds — #1 priority

Lower market cap = more explosive potential. Apple (3.5T) might go $1-2; SkyWater at $480M can go 10% in a day.

### Key Teaching: If Buying Near Close, Set Stop Next Morning

> "If you are buying in last two minutes, do you need a stop? You put a stop today morning. You don't put a stop like any other."

Don't set stops overnight for anticipation entries. Set them in the morning before market opens. For already-profitable positions, move stop above entry (breakeven plus).

## 2 PM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Setup Hierarchy — Drop Anticipation First

> "If I had to drop a setup from my process, I would drop anticipation... The real money is in 9M EP or combo LTV. That's the bread and butter."

Setup importance ranking:
1. **9M EP** — most important, most money
2. **Combo + LTV** — singles, consistent
3. **Some reversal / Free money** — bonus, not core
4. **Anticipation** — least important, most seductive

### Key Teaching: "Anticipation Is the Chinese Girl Texting You"

> "Some Chinese girl will contact [an 80-year-old]... He first put in 30K, then 50K... by the time his daughter found out, he'd given 1.6 million... Anticipation and reversal is the Chinese girl texting you, saying hey, you can make a lot of money."

Anticipation and reversal are seductive but unreliable. They work only in specific market conditions, require excellent execution, and attract beginners who should be focusing on 9M EP.

### Key Teaching: Shamelessly Copy First

> "I copied 100%... shamelessly copy completely... China copies Fitbit... First, copy and make it work, then do your improvisation."

Dunkin' Donut franchise analogy: you don't improvise on day one. Copy the proven system, make money, then improve.

Same for trading: copy 9M EP, make it work, then add anticipation later.

### Key Teaching: Market Context — First Signs of Selling

- Range expansion on downside appearing
- Red worm all over (combination bearish 437 vs bullish 55)
- Breakouts fading (Move, MicroStrategy, AMT all stopped out)
- But not aggressive shorting — need 300-500 down day for confidence
- Microsoft short taken with 1.78% risk (earnings not great + range expansion)

### Key Teaching: Story Stock Position Sizing

> "I can't put 50% of my account on a stock which is $3... I have around a 5% position."

Story stocks = 5% max position size. Too illiquid for large positions. That's why Pradeep focuses on 5000+ funds stocks — they allow size.

### Anticipation — #1 Idea: Lumen

> "This is my number one idea. If it closes like this, I'll definitely be a buyer."

Lumen setting up as high tide flag — if it closes within 3-4¢ of current level, buy near close. Everyone watching this setup. #1 priority over all other anticipation candidates.

Other anticipation candidates: Green Dot (not bad), Under Armour, Uber (only stock holding up while market sells).

### Key Teaching: 9M Threshold — 9M Is the Sweet Spot

- Lowering to 7M or 5M gives more setups but more noise
- 9M provides enough opportunities in a year
- More setups ≠ better — "it's always a compromise"
- "9M gives you more than enough opportunities in a year, even in a month"
