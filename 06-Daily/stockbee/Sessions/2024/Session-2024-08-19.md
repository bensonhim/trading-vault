# Number One Problem: No Setup — Write It Down or Miracles Don't Happen

> **Number one problem traders have: no setup.** If you can't write it down, it doesn't exist. "Thoughts and prayers" is what people ask for when they have no setup. Four reasons a trade fails: setup error, process error, market conditions, trader problem. Most failures are trader problems. Market bounced V-shaped but cautious — small caps never participated. **Zim and EXCL were the two actionable setups** — EXCL failed by end of day. We are in a young trend; setup ideas will keep working. Boot camp this weekend.

### Key Teaching: "What Are Your Thoughts?" = No Setup

> "Whenever somebody asks for my thoughts, my prayers go to him. I pray that Jesus finds him a way to understand setups."

Asking "what are your thoughts on this stock?" is a symptom of **no setup clarity**. If you had a defined setup, you'd know immediately — does it fit or not? No thoughts needed.

> "If you start doing random things, you get random results."

### Key Teaching: Four Reasons a Trade Doesn't Work

| # | Reason | Example |
|---|--------|---------|
| 1 | **Setup error** | Buying on day 3 instead of day 1 of swing |
| 2 | **Process error** | Wrong stop placement, forgot to sell into strength, wrong position size |
| 3 | **Market conditions** | Setup and process right, but market not conducive to your style |
| 4 | **Trader problem** | No setup, no process, no situational awareness, can't follow own rules |

> "Most of the problems people have are trader problems."

### Key Teaching: Write Down Your Setup

> "If you cannot write down your setup, it is never going to happen. Miracles don't happen if you cannot write down your setup."

Write it down. When you write it, you see the gaps in your understanding. Without writing, you're flying on vague understanding.

### Key Teaching: Bucket Zero and Bucket One = Real Catalyst

Delayed reactions should only be looked for in **bucket zero or bucket one stocks** (real earnings and sales catalyst) or EG 100 or IBD 250 or 2,500+ funds. Not on random stocks.

### Key Teaching: Magna Is the Most Important Criterion

> "Magna is the most important. Everything else is optional."

53 Cap 10 by 10 — Magna (earnings acceleration) is essential. The rest is nice-to-have.

### Key Teaching: Dedication to a Setup = 6 Months Minimum

Pradeep is working on a setup idea for 6 months. Member who consistently does daily work and sends it = "100% will make it." Out of 100 people who start, 2-3 follow through for more than a couple days.

### Key Teaching: Stop Asking for Thoughts, Find Your Setup

> "People don't want to have a setup. They just want you to tell them what stocks to buy."

Wolf was a sugar baby on **Friday**, not today. Calling it a sugar baby on Monday = buying an extended setup. Number one problem since 2005, across 5,000+ people: **no defined setup**.

## 2 PM Meeting

### EXCL Failed — Breakouts That Don't Hold

EXCL looked great at 10 AM. By 2 PM, it gave up all gains. If a breakout fails, it fails. No excuses.

### SIPs Discussion: Day Trading Strategy

> "Most SIPs are one-day phenomena."

SIPs = day trading strategy. They pop and give back gains same day. Not suitable for holding overnight or for small accounts trying to compound. Small accounts should focus on **high-conviction continuation momentum bursts** instead.

### Key Teaching: Day Traders Make More Initially but Burn Out

> "All the day traders I know, the good ones, they want to become swing traders after they made money."

Day trading is mentally taxing. Burnout rate is very high. Initial years: day traders make more. Long term: lifestyle forces transition to swing trading.

### Key Teaching: Small Account Growth

> "You have to have high conviction ideas and put all your account in one idea, then do more research."

One idea at a time. Full conviction. Then research the next.
