### The Sugar Baby Lesson: Two Different Businesses

> "EP 9M is for moving your account in increments of 20%, 50%, 100%, 300%. Two-linch, billing — those are for 0.5% to 5% increments. These are two different businesses."

- **Business 1**: Two-linch, billing, momentum burst, weak structure short — small incremental profits (0.5–5%)
- **Business 2**: EP 9M, sugar babies — big moves (20%, 50%, 100%, 300%)
- PepsiCo analogy: drinks business ≠ snacks business. Different strategies for different businesses
- If you peel a sugar baby for 1%, you will **never** capture a 100% move
- Give sugar babies **room** — 8% stops are normal for a stock that can move 200%
- CLSK: "This is just the second or third day of the move. It can go to 30, 40"

### CLSK: The Case Study

- Bought CLSK before boot camp on the 9M breakout day
- Put low-of-the-day as stop because of boot camp (couldn't babysit)
- "9M so you give it more room to run"
- Not peeling — letting it run because it's a sugar baby in a hot theme
- Only reason to sell: if it starts moving in gaps (like MARA did previously)
- CLSK has done 60% swings before — 12% is nothing in context

### APP: Delayed Reaction

- [[APP]]: delayed reaction to earnings, 9M breakout
- Earnings was good — genuine catalyst

### How to Enter Sugar Babies

- "As it shows up in my scan, I'm going to buy"
- Use "stocks with maximum momentum" scan — when a sugar baby appears, buy immediately
- Don't wait for trend lines or percentage breakouts on sugar babies
- Stop: $0.30–0.40 below entry, then move to breakeven

### Thematic EPs Do Very Well

- When there's a clear theme (Bitcoin today), thematic EPs outperform
- "The theme is Bitcoins" — everything crypto-related is working
- Focus where the money is flowing, not on random setups outside the theme
