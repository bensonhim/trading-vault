## 2 PM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Singles + Home Runs = Smooth Equity Curve

> "If you put 25% on a swing and it goes up 10%, your account moves 2.5%. If it goes up 20%, you get 5%. Sometimes you just have to settle for whatever it will give."

Singles move 0.5-5%. Home runs move 10-100%. Combination of both creates a smooth equity curve. Operating with 8-20% stops creates volatile equity curve. Operating with 0.5-5% stops (singles methodology) creates smoother curve.

> "Are there enough opportunities to hit singles in a year using momentum burst? Yes. Are there enough home run opportunities? EP classical gives only 3-5. But EP 9M gives 100-300."

### Key Teaching: Anticipation Is Hardest Setup

> "The most difficult setup to make work is anticipation. But it is the setup which, for whatever reason, everybody wants to become an anticipation trader. It's hell of a lot easier to make a breakout setup work."

People get 50% drawdowns trying anticipation after anticipation. Death by a thousand cuts. Cycle repeats. Start with breakouts, make those work first.

### Key Teaching: Copy Shamelessly

> "Find something which works and make it work. I found something that worked for somebody, took that idea, refined it, made it work, never looked back."

Good ideas travel. EP, buying breakout, selling into strength — they have legs. That's why so many people use them. Don't reinvent the wheel.

### Key Teaching: Shorting Small Caps Has an Edge

> "Shorting small caps is a profitable strategy. Most small caps are fugazi — they can only do one or two quarters of good sales. They don't have staying power."

But 9M EP is a bigger edge, more scalable, less risky, more money on the long side. Don't need to trade all edges.

### Key Teaching: Short Early, Don't Short Late

> "Early in the correction, shorting makes sense. Later in the correction, probability of getting chopped is extremely high."

All the weak structure shorts might work IF market cooperates. But risk of whipsaw from late-day program buying is real.

> "My biggest worry is that there might be another leg down, but the market might be too choppy. Most of the damage has been done. If I take shorts aggressively here and a buy program comes in, I'm scrambling."

### Key Teaching: Day Trading Environment

High volatility is ideal for day trading. Day traders should be minting money. Stocks in play + range expansion + fade setups = bread and butter. For swing traders: patience, wait for setups.

### Key Teaching: Make Breakouts Work

> "People have made millions trading breakouts. It's an age-old technique. Find a way to make breakouts work for you. That's the simplest advice."

Breakouts or pullbacks — those are the two basic methods. Reversals are just types of pullbacks. Start with breakouts.

### 401k / Patience
