## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Selling Halted Temporarily, Not Over

> "The fast selling phase, for the time being, has been halted."

Past selling stopped, but that doesn't mean correction is over. VIX still elevated. Gap-down after yesterday's rally — typical bearish market behavior. Lateral/choppy action likely for days.

Historical pattern: after initial selling, you get 3-5 day bounce, then another leg down. Probability favors more downside, not a final bottom.

### Key Teaching: Select Breakouts Always Work

> "Select breakouts always work. Select breakouts always work. Select breakouts always work."

Repeated 3 times for emphasis. The market always offers select breakouts. Even in bearish markets, 20% study shows 25 stocks on bullish side vs 5 on bearish side. But most of the bullish ones are **news or earnings driven**, not random breakouts.

### Key Teaching: 20% Study — News/Catalyst Is What Drives Moves

Most stocks in the 20% up study moved on catalysts: earnings breakouts (Axon, SWIM, XPEL), buyouts (Hamilton, Castle), news (Compass Secure, Inogen). Not random breakouts.

> "If there is a story, the stock will go up no matter what."

### Key Teaching: Younger Setups Will Form Now

Everything has been reset. Stocks beaten down will come back with catalysts. IPO10 stocks prominent in the 20% study — younger setups forming.

### Key Teaching: Linear vs Choppy Setups

Choose stocks with linearity — clean breakouts that follow through. Avoid choppy stocks even though they can be traded.

> "Can you make money trading something choppy? Yes. But do you have to trade choppy stocks?"

Google, Lumen — linear. Other stocks — all over the place. Choose linearity.

### Key Teaching: 401k — Wait for T2108 Below 20

> "Whenever T2108 has gone below 20, always made money in 401k."

Don't try to catch small turns. Wait for T2108 below 20 (or at least below 40), then buy big. Infinite patience for the right signal. Don't sit through drawdowns.

### Key Teaching: SIPs Move on Individual Catalyst, Not WORM

> "SIPs are going to move based on its individual catalyst. It is not related to the WORM."

The WORM (market direction) matters for swing trades, not SIPs. SIPs move on their own catalyst regardless of market.

### Key Teaching: Don't Buy Random Breakouts — Context Matters

Breakout without gap is easier to trade (known stop). Breakout with gap requires wider risk. In choppy market, breakouts fade. Know your situational awareness before entering.

### Key Teaching: Without Gap vs With Gap

> "If you can find a breakout without a gap, the probability is much higher."

Without gap: orderly, clear stop. With gap: wider risk, less certainty.

### Key Teaching: 9M EP vs Combo vs LTV — Know Your Setup

- **9M EP**: works independent of situational awareness IF there's a catalyst
- **Combo**: momentum burst — buying one day of momentum leading to continuation
- **LTV**: low risk entry on already-established momentum

Don't confuse them. Each requires different context.

### Key Teaching: Write Your Own Guide

> "If you write your own guide for 9M EP in your own words, that's how you gain clarity."

Member example: handwritten guide with examples, in his own language. That's understanding. If you can't write it simply, you don't understand it.

Multiple member guides exist on the members' method page. Study them, then write your own.

### Key Teaching: Conscious Decision Not to Rush

> "There is a conscious decision currently which I have made not to rush into a trade because I see the gap happening right away."

In choppy/volatile market, FOMO into gaps = getting stopped out. Sit on hands.
