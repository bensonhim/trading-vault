# The Dog That Bites — Stop Repeating Failing Patterns

> **If a dog barks at you, then bites you, then catches you by the balls — do you approach it a fourth time? Same with failing patterns.** If buying early hasn't worked for 3-4 days, stop doing it. Situational awareness means reading the environment and controlling your own actions. AVAV and VRAX were today's SIPs — same setup category, but AVAV (catalyst legs) = hold longer, VRAX (data rate) = take money and run. **Dynamic decision-making, not mechanical rules.**

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Key Analogy: The Dog and the Dishwasher

> "First time you approach the dog, it barks. Second time, it bites you. Third time, it catches you by your balls. What are you going to do the fourth time?"

Same pattern in trading: if buying early hasn't worked for 3-4 days, stop. If breakouts aren't working, stop buying breakouts. Don't repeat failing patterns 60 more times.

> "First time you stack the dishwasher, she shouts at you. Second time, she doesn't talk to you for two days. Third time, your underwear is flying. Fourth time — are you going anywhere near that dishwasher?"

### Key Teaching: Situational Awareness = Control Over Your Own Actions

Reading the market environment is not optional. Every successful trader does it — even day traders. If you can't read the market, you'll have problems. It's a continuous process, not a one-day lesson.

### Key Teaching: Breakouts Work in Cycles

When breakouts don't work, everyone says "breakouts don't work." On day 11, everyone becomes a breakout trader with large size. This is the nature of the market. Accept it.

### Key Teaching: Higher-Priced Stocks Doing Better

> "The higher price stocks have done much better, and they have much better follow through than the stocks which are at a lower price."

From the 20% study: high-priced stocks are outperforming. Costco had very good follow through. Shift focus accordingly.

### Key Teaching: FEE Not Working — Focus on Catalyst-Based Setups

Current environment: breakouts fading, FEE not working. Pradeep focused on:
- 9M breakout (technology + biotech)
- Catalyst-based setups
- Delayed reactions
- PRCT bought as delayed reaction (bucket zero, catalyst)

### Key Teaching: Always Sell Into Strength

> "Anything that you're buying, if you sell into strength or try and protect your profit, it is likely that you are going to be in a better position."

### Key Teaching: New Scan — Bullish Combo Plastic

Created a new scan: technology + healthcare (biotech) sectors combined. After studying 20%, many breakouts start from biotech/tech. Process innovation based on daily study.

### Key Teaching: EP 3M Threshold

Members are using EP 1M or 3M to get into 9M breakouts even earlier. Pradeep prefers 3M over 1M — 1M can get overwhelming with noise.

### Key Teaching: Relative Volume Is Useless Without Absolute Volume

> "Relative volume is useless. You can have a stock with 10K shares trading at 30K and it'll show relative volume. Unless you have an absolute volume filter with it, relative volume is of no use."

Dollar volume misses the best 9M breakouts because they have no volume before the breakout. ADR has the same problem.

### Key Teaching: Think 3 Days Ahead

Monday is a holiday. Political uncertainty + Fed rate decision coming. VTFD: when Fed is supportive, keep buying.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Trade: AVAV vs VRAX — Same Category, Different Risk Profile

Both were SIPs today found via NTRT/MTRT process. But the dynamic decision framework says:

- **AVAV** — catalyst-based, potential for 20-30%+ more → hold longer, don't aggressively take profit
- **VRAX** — data rate (no real catalyst legs) → take money and run, move stop aggressively

> "If early, can it potentially run? Above can potentially run. Verax — is it the same situation? No. You have to move your stop aggressively, take the money, and run."

### Key Teaching: Dynamic Decision Framework

> "Trading is a dynamic decision making model. At the same setup, how you trade is very important."

Same setup, different entry time = completely different trade. Early entry has different profit potential than morning entry. This is what makes trading extremely challenging for new people AND fascinating for experienced traders.

### Key Teaching: Hold Two Conflicting Ideas

> "In spite of the fact that the ESE is bearish — breakouts are typically not working — I'm not going to do FEE, but there is also clear catalyst-based NTRT/MTRT kind of play for which the ESE doesn't matter."

This frustrates new people: "don't trade" but also "I'm still going for catalyst trades." The ability to hold both ideas simultaneously is what separates skilled traders.

### Key Teaching: Screen Time — The Only Path

> "There is only one secret, and that is hundreds of hours of screen time. Screen time."

No course, no boot camp, no one whispering sweet nothings in your ear can replace screen time. Every day Pradeep studies the 20% and 50% scans — why did each stock move, what was the catalyst, where did it start from. This is obsessive, repetitive pattern recognition.

> "I just waste my time studying these stocks. Why? So I can figure out what makes the stock go up. It's hundreds of thousands of hours of study."

### Key Teaching: Setup Choice Is Like Dating

> "Trading is no different than dating. I made a choice: I'm going to trade setups with a persistent first leg, orderly pullback <10 days, then breakout. That's my oat milk. You might choose something else — that's your choice. Neither is wrong."

Don't ask Pradeep about setups outside his framework — he can't help you. Like asking a heart surgeon about urology. His framework: first leg + orderly pullback + breakout. Period.

### Key Teaching: 80/20 Is a Savior for Singles

> "80/20 is like a Jesus for short-term traders. Especially for hitting singles."

On anticipation setups, 100% of the time Pradeep wants to do 80/20 (sell 80% into the pop, keep 20% runner).

### Key Teaching: Cookie on the Table

> "There's a cookie on the table. Am I going to eat it? No. Fitness first. If I want six-pack abs, I can't eat the cookie."

He knows breakouts are available. He knows the cookie is there. But situational awareness tells him most breakouts aren't working. Discipline = not eating the cookie.

### Key Teaching: Know Why Every 9M Breakout Exists

> "If you don't know why a stock had a 9M breakout in the last 5-10 days, that's a process failure."

You should know whether it's a catalyst, a story, or a sugar baby. VRAX had a catalyst. Peloton had no catalyst — it's a sugar baby. Redfin — why the 9M? You should know.

### Key Teaching: Only Take Setups With Confidence

Sold Rivian — stopped out because it didn't work after entry. Didn't take XPEV (Chinese stock — no confidence). Only entered setups with conviction.

### Key Teaching: Continuous Feedback Loop

Even good setups fading today → more cautious, smaller size. The feedback loop is continuous: refine situational awareness, refine setup understanding, refine entry decisions.

> "When the process doesn't work, you need to take it personally, then you'll improve. If you externalize the problem, you never improve."

### Key Teaching: Self-Learning Is Non-Negotiable

> "All the traders who make it in this business — they are self-learners. This is like entrepreneurship."

You can shorten the learning curve with boot camp, but you cannot outsource the learning itself. Nobody can do it for you.

2. **Same setup, different entry = completely different trade** — AVAV (catalyst, hold) vs VRAX (data rate, take money and run)
3. **Hold two conflicting ideas** — ESE bearish AND still take catalyst trades
4. **Screen time is the only path** — no shortcut, no substitute, hundreds of thousands of hours
5. **Setup choice is personal** — like choosing oat milk over cow's milk. Neither wrong, but know your choice
6. **80/20 is a savior for singles** — sell 80% into pop, keep 20% runner, especially on anticipation
7. **Know why every 9M breakout exists** — if you can't explain it, that's a process failure
8. **Higher-priced stocks outperforming** — 20% study confirms better follow-through
9. **FEE not working** — focus on 9M breakout, catalyst-based, delayed reactions
10. **Sell into strength** — protect profits, especially in choppy environments
11. **Cookie on the table analogy** — breakouts exist, but situational awareness says don't eat the cookie
12. **Continuous feedback loop** — refine situational awareness, setup understanding, and entry decisions daily
13. **Self-learning is non-negotiable** — all successful traders are self-learners
14. **When the process doesn't work, take it personally** — then you'll improve. Externalizing = never improving
