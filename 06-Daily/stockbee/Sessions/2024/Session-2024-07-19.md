### 20% Study Context: At Start of Bull Market, Don't Fade High Readings

20% study dropped from 212 to ~29. But at the start of a bull market, these readings can keep going up to 300-400. Don't short based on oscillator overbought readings at the start of a trend.

### Consolidation Breakout Failure Rate

Key lesson on why [[COIN]] and [[HOOD]] didn't work:

> "Consolidation breakouts have a high failure rate unless there is a game-changing catalyst. COIN had Trump catalyst but no extraordinary volume. By third day, if it's not going, you know it's not going to work."

Sold COIN and HOOD on Thursday morning (9:30–9:45 AM) because they weren't performing. This is procedural memory — by third day, consolidation breakouts that don't move should be exited.

### Deep Buying: The Play for Today

> "Today is a perfect setup for deep buying. If things are gapping down, what do you think is the trade? It's a dip."

Large caps may see dip-buyers stepping in. The money rotating out of large caps into small caps is a secular trend, not a 3-day trade.

### Trade Ideas Software ("The Worm")

Real-time situational awareness tool: shows buying vs selling pressure. Red worm = sellers in control. Green worm = buyers stepping in.

> "If I'm bullish on the market, I'm not going to jump into things. The worm was showing sellers dominating. So I purposefully decided not to do F&E today."

### Day Trading vs Swing Trading: Choose One

> "Either you become a day trader or you become a swing trader. Each requires 3–4 years to become good at it. Unless you're lucky and find a site like this with ready-made setups."
