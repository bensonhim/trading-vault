## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Trade: Build-A-Bear (BBW) — The Trade Nobody Saw

BBW was a SIPs play that nobody posted. Earnings: 65¢ vs consensus, came in at $6.46. The real drivers:

1. **10-day+ short interest** — the #1 driver of explosive SIPs moves
2. **Low float** — most 100-300% movers have floats under 5M shares
3. **Reverse split** — many of the biggest movers had reverse splits recently

> "Nobody posted BBW. Why didn't anybody think BBW was a setup? The number one driver of big moves on SIPs is high short interest."

### Key Teaching: How to Actually Deep Dive on SIPs

**The deep dive framework that most people skip:**

Priority order for SIPs:
1. **10-day+ short interest** — first priority, biggest driver of explosive moves
2. **Float under 5M** — lower float = more explosive
3. **Reverse split in last 1-3 months** — common characteristic of 200-300% movers
4. **Real EP or turnaround** — genuine catalyst

> "Somebody did 10 deep dives on SIPs/EP and still not making money. I asked: what are your learnings? They started talking about opening range breakout. Those are TACTICS. Your larger findings aren't right — what's the point of going on a 1-minute chart if you don't know that high short interest + low float + reverse split is what drives these moves?"

### Key Teaching: Calendar (CAL) — 9M Liquid Lava

Calendar setting up as 9M EP. Low threshold breakout. Recently had buying coming in. Not a bad setup — liquid lava situation.

### Key Teaching: Slow Deliberate — Don't Rush

> "Slow deliberate. Don't rush in. The market has traded multiple times in the last couple of days. Be very clear — wait for the best setup to show."

### Key Teaching: Affirm Already Made Its Move

> "It's already made that kind of a move. So what is left? I would wait for a delayed reaction kind of a setup there."

30% gap = most of the move is done. Wait for the pullback and setup.

### Key Teaching: No Breadth Thrust = No FEE

Market monitor shows no sustained buying. After a 700-day, you get nothing the next day. You need a 300-500 day breadth thrust to confirm buying. Until then, no FEE.

> "You want to see a 2% range expansion day on SPY, and then if you buy, I think a lot of breakouts will work. Right now it's just sideways."

### Key Teaching: Extended First Legs Don't Work

> "I think five to six days is best. When you get 10-12 day first legs, the probability of the breakout not working is very high."

Stocks with 10-12 day first legs before breakout = low probability. 5-6 day first leg is the sweet spot.

### Key Teaching: PayPal — Extended First Leg, Likely Fade

PayPal's first leg was too long (11 days). Probability says it fades after breakout.

### Key Teaching: HPQ (HP Inc.) — Maybe the One Idea

> "HPQ — that looks good to me. These kind of things tend to have good success ratio."

Reversal bar + catalyst. One of the few things that looked actionable.

### Key Teaching: Write Your Guide

> "Out of all the people I spoke to this month one-on-one, my number one question: do you have a written guide? They don't say no — they give explanations about work conditions, girlfriend problems, etc. But the answer is: is there a guide? Yes or no?"

Most traders don't have a written guide. Without writing it, retention is zero. Pradeep rewrote his guide every week for years.

### Key Teaching: If Stopped Out 3 Times, Stop Trading

> "Three times in a row, you got stopped out — so don't do FEE. Just don't keep doing that for 60 more trades."

If something isn't working, stop and reevaluate. Don't compound the error.

### Key Teaching: First 10 Years, One Setup Only

> "For the first ten years of my trading career, I was trading only one setup. 4% breakout. Nothing else. When a stock goes up, goes sideways, then breaks out."

One idea. One setup. Master it before adding more.

### Key Teaching: 6% Drawdown = Stop and Reevaluate

Pradeep's personal rule: if he hits 6% drawdown, he stops trading and reevaluates everything.

### Key Teaching: Don't Ask for One-on-One

> "I might have to institute a fee for one-on-one because people don't follow through. The best thing is boot camp. And if you have a question, ask it in these meetings — everybody benefits."

6-month waiting list for one-on-ones. Just ask in the meetings.
