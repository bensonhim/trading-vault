# SA Is the Single Most Important Skill, Setups vs Discrete Information, and Singles Over Home Runs

> "Situational awareness is the single most important concept in trading. The moment you learn that, your life will be different."

Choppy market, breakouts failing, only biotech and SIPs working. Pradeep delivers a defining lecture on the difference between a **setup** (complete end-to-end trading method) and **discrete information** (useless without context), and why mastering SA + one setup beats chasing home runs.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM) — Choppy Market, Biotech Only, SIPs Working

### SA: Choppy, Only Biotech Working

- Choppy market except for stocks in play and select large-cap tech/biotech
- Most stocks not having follow-through
- **Only biotech breakouts working** — 20% study dominated by biotech names
- SIPs, delayed SIPs, 9M biotech breakouts = what's working
- Breakouts failing — don't buy random breakouts in this market
- Shorts not working either

### 17 Criteria = Dating Site with 22 Questions

> "Someone emailed me: 'I added 17 criteria to improve my win rate. When I applied all 17, nothing showed up.' Obviously — you created 17 criteria."

- Don't overcomplicate SIPs — all you need to know: **is there a catalyst, and will it move today?**
- Institutional ownership doesn't matter for SIPs — 99% institutional-owned stocks still move on catalyst
- Simple things are very difficult for people to accept

### Singles Over Home Runs

> "How many SMCI are you going to find in a year? If you build your trading around finding the next SMCI, most of the time you'll be eating ramen noodles."

- Singles strategy (9M, sugar babies) = consistent money
- Home run (EP like SMCI) = rare, can't depend on it
- Hit singles → when a home run comes, you're positioned
- 9M gives many more trade opportunities per year than EP hunting

### Visualization: Controlled Risk

- SA = most important skill — if breakouts not working, don't buy breakouts
- T-3, find and enter early, sell into strength
- BTFD (buy the dip when Fed is active)
- Stories stocks, younger stocks, lower cap = bigger moves
- SIPs + FHP focus
- FFFM: find lowest stop possible, trade works instantly

## 11 AM Meeting — Setup vs Discrete Information, SA Overrides Everything

### Breakouts Not Working — Overwhelming Evidence

- SA readings around 90 — clear selling pressure
- "Even God is not going to help you if you buy breakouts here"
- Buying breakouts when SA says no = going into a burning house

### The Defining Lecture: Setup vs Discrete Information

> "A setup is a complete end-to-end trading method. Discrete information (institutional ownership, float, chart patterns) is just an ingredient — not a recipe."

- **Setup** = complete formula: scan → identify → enter → stop → exit → position size
- **Discrete information** = institutional ownership, float, chart pattern — useless without setup context
- People collect discrete information like "avocado is good for you" — avocado is not a recipe
- "Buy setups, not stocks" — [[SMCI]]: if day-trading, it doesn't matter if extended; what's the setup?
- Institutional ownership percentage is irrelevant for SIPs — what matters is the catalyst for that day
- Number of funds matters more than percentage for EP (not for SIPs)

### SA + Setup = 100%

> "If you find a setup, you've found 50% of the puzzle. The other 50% is situational awareness. Both must be in place."

- Setup without SA = random trades that lose money
- SA without setup = knowledge but no execution framework
- Both together = money-making system
- USIC champion who made big money during COVID, then lost 70% of capital — no SA concept
- "If you cannot understand SA and setup, please find another job"

### SIPs Simplified: Catalyst Is All That Matters

- [[Meta]]: catalyst day = party. That's it. One day.
- [[APD]]: missed earnings → short it. Simple.
- [[Estée Lauder]]: bad earnings, gapping up → fade it. Simple.
- For SIPs, the only questions: is there a catalyst? Is it going to move today? Where's the party?
- Don't need 17 criteria, don't need institutional ownership analysis
- Catalyst + entry + tight stop + sell into strength = SIPs setup complete

### NYSE OPG Order Warning

- Reiterated: NYSE stocks have manual open, market makers won't fill orders they'd lose money on
- Use market orders after open for less-liquid names, not OPG

### Momentum Burst Finances EP

> "Master momentum burst so that it can finance your EP trade."

- Momentum burst = the setup that saves you money in choppy markets
- When SA says breakouts won't work, momentum burst tells you to stay out
- Recommended for active traders: master **momentum burst + 9M EP**
