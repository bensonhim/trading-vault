# Sell Into Strength: The ABBB Lesson, Setup Discipline, and When to Step Back

> Rotation confirmed — money flowing out of large caps into small caps. ABBB sold 80% at 8% profit in under an hour. The lesson: sell into strength, hit singles, and when your setups tell you nothing is actionable, do nothing.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Regime: Rotation Confirmed — Small Caps In, Large Caps Out

> "I see big rotation into small caps. Your scan showed that SOXL and TQQ were not the place to be — TNA was."

Continuation setups will form in coming days. The time for bottom bounces is over.

### Stories Fly in Bull Markets — But Not Real Earnings

> "Locafy, LGVN, QuantumScape — they're just complete Fugazi stories. Sound Hound AI, Lucid Motors, loss-making useless companies making 51% moves. Stocks with real earnings? Not making moves."

Short-term: stories > fundamentals. This is the great paradox.

### Non-Negotiables

> "Buy young setups. Buy at start of swing. Hit singles. Sell into strength. These are non-negotiable."

If a position goes 30% in your favor, **take profit**. Don't let it come back and hit your stop.

### Range Expansion: The Core of Everything

> "The whole idea — whether continuation, bottom bounce, 9M record, or consolidation breakout — is range expansion. If you don't understand range expansion, the whole trading doesn't make sense."

### 9M Breakout: The Best Setup Working

Last 10 days: most big movers are 9M records. Volume + range expansion = institutional buying.

### Stock Selection: 2,500 Fund Ownership

> "I got into ABBB instantly because I know it's owned by 2,500 funds. The buying that comes in is institutional."

For singles: prefer stocks owned by 5,000 or 2,500 funds — can do size, tight stops, reliable moves.

> "Can I put all my account in Microsoft short? What is the risk? 1–2%. Hit singles. Hit singles."

The advantage of high-priced, fund-owned stocks: minimal risk per trade, can do large size.

## 10 AM Meeting (Trade Factory)

### Procedural Memory: The Core Skill

> "Procedural memory is our ability to perform a process. It doesn't involve brains. It happens automatically."

Trade factory is pre-thought. Run scan → see setup → execute. No analysis during market hours.

### ABBB: 80% Sold at 8% Profit

ABBB bought yesterday at $58.66. Went up 8%+ in under an hour.

> "I sold 80% into strength. Free money. 8% for holding overnight."

Moved stop to breakeven-plus on remaining 20%. Got stopped out on the 20% later. Total: a single.

### The 80/20 Rule in Practice

When ABBB spiked 9% in the morning, Pradeep sold 80% immediately:

> "Professional traders are sharks. They have figured this out. If you get 9% in less than half an hour, you offload. Then who's left to buy?"

The people buying at 9% up are the suckers — they're buying where professionals are selling.

### Anticipation Setups: Why They Fail for Beginners

ABBB was an anticipation setup. Pradeep bought day before. When it broke out:

1. Professionals who bought early sold into strength (80/20)
2. Late buyers bought at 8–9% up
3. Selling pressure + stops triggered = reversal

> "Why does anticipation have the lowest win rate? Because people chase. Everyone who does anticipation has it on their watch list. Late people come in, professionals start taking profit."

### Is There a Setup? The Million-Dollar Question

> "I buy setups. If a setup meets my definition, I buy it. If there is no setup, there is no setup. You don't need to force a trade."

Walked through several stocks: CAT (no setup), IMAX (no setup — consolidation without volume or catalyst), Estée Lauder (4th leg short — wrong setup, wrong time).

> "You want to pick up chicks? You go in a bar. First thing — is she available? Is it start of a swing? If she's got a ring on her hand, there's no point wasting your time."

## 2 PM Meeting

### Market Sell-Off: Trump Interview Impact

Trump interview questioned rate cuts → market sold off. Crypto reversed hard. But:

> "I'm not going to become bearish just because one day is negative. The money is still on the long side."

### SOXL/TQQ Shorts: Managed Correctly

> "SOXL made the same amount that TNA made. TNA made 20% move. I missed 23% move on TNA, but SOXL also made the same."

80/20 rule applied to shorts too — sold 80% when it was working, trailed 20%.

### CRISPR: Stopped Out at Breakeven-Plus

Moved stop to breakeven-plus. Got hit. No loss. That's the game.

### Anticipation: Why It Has the Lowest Win Rate

Extended explanation of why anticipation setups fail:

1. Professional traders buy early (day before)
2. In the morning, smart money adds
3. By 10 AM, who's left to buy? The chasers
4. Chasers buy, professionals sell into their buying
5. Stock reverses, chasers get stopped out

> "Professional traders are sharks. After getting hit multiple times, they figured this out. But if you're a newcomer, do you know this? No. You're a sucker."

The fix: **Buy the day before** (anticipation entry), sell 80% into the morning spike, keep 20% with tight stop.

### When Not to Trade

> "I'm not likely to take an anticipation setup today. Why? Because what I see doesn't inspire confidence that it will work tomorrow."

Market sold off with big reversals — not normal consolidation. Continuation setups need more time.

> "I don't like the action I see. The big reversals shouldn't be happening this way."

### Weak Structure Shorts: Wrong Time, Wrong Place

Someone wanted to short stocks at 10 AM:

> "At 10 AM, you're looking for shorts? That's a mountain-sized problem. You don't know how to prioritize. Even if a weak structure short works, when should you short it? 3:00–3:30 PM, not 10 AM."

### The Psychology of Losses

> "If you're getting affected by losses, you have to redesign your entire strategy. The tactics — selling into strength, hitting singles — are designed to prevent big losses."

Pradeep's own evolution: "I used to have mood swings. Now it doesn't matter. The SOXL/TQQ profits are still there. Plus, the trade factory will find singles next week too."

### The 23% Stop Problem

On IBP (up 23% already):

> "Can you make money with a 23% stop? Yes. But try putting a 23% stop and come back after six months to tell us whether you're still alive. The probability of surviving six months with 23% stops is very low."

Pradeep's preference: 1–2.5% stops on high-priced fund-owned stocks. Size > wide stops.

### Key Anticipation Setups for Next Day

Only 1–2 good setups after review: CORT Therapeutics (biotech), maybe a few others. Most need more consolidation time.

> "I'll go through the 2,500 fund list tonight and make a decision on how to play next week."

### DJT: No Marriage

> "DJT is not a stock I'm going to marry. I found it, it showed up, I got stopped out. That's what it is."

Only remaining position: Intel, holding by a thread.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Rotation confirmed** — money flowing from large caps to small caps; TNA > SOXL/TQQ
2. **Sell into strength** — ABBB sold 80% at 8% profit in under an hour. Free money
3. **80/20 rule** — when a position spikes, sell 80%, keep 20% with trailing stop
4. **Anticipation has lowest win rate** — professionals buy early, sell into chasers. Fix: buy day before
5. **"Is there a setup?" is the million-dollar question** — if no setup, no trade. Don't force it
6. **Stories fly in bull markets** — Fugazi stocks (Locafy, SOUN, JOBY) making 51% moves. Fundamentals don't matter short-term
7. **Range expansion is the core of everything** — 9M = range expansion + volume; momentum burst = range expansion; continuation = range expansion after consolidation
8. **Stock selection matters** — 2,500/5,000 fund ownership = institutional buying = can do size with tight stops
9. **Weak structure shorts at 10 AM = wrong priority** — look for longs in the morning; shorts belong at 3:00–3:30 PM if at all
10. **Singles finance everything** — the SOXL/TQQ profits fund the next round of singles
11. **Know your setup or don't trade** — CAT has no setup, IMAX has no setup (no volume, no catalyst). Don't buy things that aren't setups
12. **When market action doesn't inspire confidence, step back** — today's big reversals are not normal; reduce exposure, wait for better setups
