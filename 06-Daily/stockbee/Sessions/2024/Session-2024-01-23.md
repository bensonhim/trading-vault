## 2 PM Meeting — Trading Problems: Selling Into Strength, High-Priced Stocks, Anticipation

### Trade Factory: Choppy Market, Not Much Actionable

- Red worm dominating, green worm weak
- "Hardly any stocks making moves. 67% declining"
- Only a few short setups (weak structure): [[TNDM]], [[FWRD]], maybe [[TNDM]] (tandem diabetes)
- Long side: [[ACAD]] (consolidation, low price), [[COOE]] ($202, earnings 2/24, nice setup)
- Anticipation: mostly needs more time, nothing actionable today

### Selling Into Strength = Art, Not Formula

> "You're watching your PNL. When the PNL stalls, you just take the money. That IS selling into strength."

- No magic formula (not "sell at 2R" or "sell when ADR > 10MA")
- Watch PNL, not charts
- SMCI intraday: PNL went from $0 to batshit crazy → stalled → sold
- "Selling into strength is an art. It comes with experience."
- Procedural memory develops over 10, 15, 30 trades

### Don't Trade Your PNL? Wrong Advice

- "People say don't trade your PNL — that's terrible advice"
- If PNL goes batshit crazy rapidly, that IS the signal to start peeling
- If short position goes batshit crazy, take profit immediately

### Anticipation Has Lowest Win Rate

- "The fascination with anticipation setups is like the fascination with Kim Kardashian"
- Anticipation depends on market conditions AND other fools buying after you
- When market is good, 9M EP works better AND makes more money
- "Why are you stuck on a strategy which has the lowest win rate of all strategies?"
- If you must trade anticipation: wider stop, longer hold — but that defeats the purpose

### High-Priced Stocks = Account Movers

| Approach | Stop Size | Position Size | Stock Move | Account Move |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| High ADR stocks | 10% | 10% of account | 30% | 3% |
| High-priced stocks | 0.5% | 80% of account | 5% | 4% |

> "I'd rather move my account by 5-10% in one trade on a high-priced stock than by 0.5-2% on a high-ADR stock."

- High-priced stocks allow tight stops → oversized positions → bigger account moves
- This is why Pradeep trades [[SMCI]], [[ADM]], not penny stocks
- "The game is about how much you move your account, not the percentage move of the stock"

### 48-Hour Rule for Trading Problems

> "Solve your trading problem in 48 hours. If you're not solving it in 48 hours, the trading problem will just accumulate."

### Consolidation Breakout Requirements

- Pullback/consolidation < 10 days
- Two links + CV (catalyst + volume)
- Bottom bounce: T2108 below 20
- "Anything above 10 days is a reset, not a consolidation"
