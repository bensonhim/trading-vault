### The Algorithmic Trading Explanation: Why Moves Happen in One Day

**Critical insight — how quant/algo funds operate:**

- Algo models calculate: "Based on this catalyst, stock should go up 33%"
- They keep buying at every percentage until the stock reaches their target
- Once it hits 33%, they sell everything — done for the day
- Whether that 33% happens in one day or two weeks, they don't care
- Result: **moves that used to take weeks now happen in one day**

> "If the catalyst was supposed to make 30%, the algorithm keeps buying until 30%, then dumps. Whether it happens in one day or two weeks, they don't care."

- This is why [[SMCI]] made a 60% move in days — algos priced it in immediately
- This is why [[IBM]] went up 12% in one day — algos calculated it was worth 12% and bought relentlessly
- Implication: **enter early (FFFM), sell before they sell, don't hold past the modeled move**
- Old EP strategy: stock gaps up 4-5%, then goes up 25-30% over two weeks
- Now: stock gaps up 15%, makes 30% in one day, then nothing — algos priced it all in
