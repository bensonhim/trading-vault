## 11 AM Meeting — "Let's Discuss Which Breakouts to Buy" (Sarcastic)

### 9M EP vs Combo LTV — The Architecture

| Setup | Objective | Account Move | Stop Width |
|---|---|---|---|
| 9M EP (catalyst) | Move account 50-300% | Large increment | 5-10% |
| Combo LTV (pattern) | Move account 0.5-5% | Small increment | <2.5% |
| Combo finances 9M | Build capital for occasional big trade | Consistent wins | Tight |

**Size = Leverage = Alpha:**

> "High priced stocks allow large capital with small risk. With 1% stop on a $200 stock, you can put big capital to work. Size is alpha. That's what hedge funds do."

### The Food Analogy

> "Some people eat with a spoon. Some with chopsticks. Some with their hand. The objective is the same — get food in your mouth."

Same with trading: the objective is to move your account 1-5% while controlling risk. Whatever method suits your personality.

### Find Your Style — Deep Masterclass

**High win rate approach (EG style):**
1. Find & enter early
2. Situational awareness
3. 80/20 profit-taking
4. High-priced stocks (less volatile, allows big size with small stop)
5. Liquid shorts only
6. Result: low drawdowns, high confidence → can put on large size

> "I don't want drawdowns. That is my number one thing. I've engineered everything to have low drawdowns."

**Low win rate approach (KQ style):**
1. Catalyst selection
2. Analytical skills
3. Wider stops
4. Let winners run
5. Result: 30% win rate, 50%+ drawdown tolerance required

> "All the Swedish guys trying to become another KQ — how many of them have succeeded? Many are going broke. They come to me asking for a discount on membership because they're already down 50%."

**The equation:**
- Want high win rate → find & enter early + 80/20 + SA + high-priced stocks
- Want big wins → catalyst + buy right + wider stops + analytical skill
- Can't have both without the personality to match

**BA as proof:**

> "BA moved the account by more than 5%. Find 20 trades like BA and you can double your account."

### SA Is the Most Critical Skill

> "The most critical skill in this business is situational awareness. Without it, you're not going to make it."

Mark Minervini has a timing model (different name, same concept). Dan Zenger has one. Every survivor has one. It's been copied across Twitter — Pradeep originated it.

### Different Styles for Different Setups

- **Minervini**: consolidation breakouts, trend template (150-day MA rising, 200-day MA rising, stock within 25% of high, up 30% from low) — enters much later
- **StockBee**: continuation breakouts, 9M EP, delayed reaction — enters earlier
- These are **different setups** requiring **different conditions**

> "You have to understand the nitty-gritties of people's setups. Otherwise you'll confuse VCP with continuation."

### Why Swing Trading Is Proven

**Citadel's strategy** (most successful hedge fund):
- Market making, HFT, stat arb: 1-3 days holding
- Medium-term equity: <1 week
- Most profit from **<1 month holding**

**Renaissance Capital** (Jim Simons):
- Average holding: few seconds to few days
- Performance > Warren Buffett

**The novel analogy:**
> "If the first word of a 1,000-page novel is 'New York' — can you predict the next 3-5 words? Yes. Can you predict what the remaining 1,000 pages are about? No."

That's why swing trading works — predict 3-5 days, not 1,000 days.

### Risk Profile Changes With Age

> "When I was young, I was reckless — 10-15% stops, all account in. Now I put large capital on BA but my stop was less than 1%. Same risk, different execution."

> "I'm more risk averse now. I only trade when money is lying there."
