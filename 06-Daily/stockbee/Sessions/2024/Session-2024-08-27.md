# The Anticipation Game — Front-Run or Get Front-Run

> **Eli Lilly was the #1 anticipation idea from Friday. It opened up 8%, then faded all day. The pattern: newsletter writers buy before publishing, algorithms front-run the breakout, retail buys the pop, smart money sells into it.** Anticipation is a front-run setup — buy at 3:58, sell when it pops. "If you're finding 5-6 setups a day, you're on the wrong track." **Write your guide. Only 3 people posted after boot camp.** Market choppy — be extremely selective. Only need 2-3 trades per month for 15-20% moves.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Key Teaching: Avoid Overtrading — Be Deliberate

> "If your early entries are not working and it has not worked for last 2-3 days, and you're again and again going into an early entry, then it's never going to work."

If something fails 3-4 times, stop doing it. Don't repeat failing patterns. Finding 8-10 setups per day = wrong track.

Technology and biotechnology 9M breakouts dominating: CCRM, Decor, MDX, Redfin. These are still the bread and butter.

### Key Teaching: 84% of Stocks Making New Lows

Friday's breath thrust brought buying, but Monday morning 84% of stocks opening at new lows. Look for deep buyers to step in. Not bearish, but not aggressive buying either.

### Boot Camp Reflection

Only 3-4 people posted their learnings after boot camp. Writing your guide in your own words is essential for retention.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Trade: Eli Lilly — Anticipation Proves the Pattern

LLY was the #1 anticipation idea from Friday. It opened up ~$20, then faded all day. This is THE anticipation pattern:

1. Newsletter writers identify the setup
2. They buy BEFORE they publish
3. Algorithms know retail will buy the breakout
4. The pop happens in first 5-20 minutes
5. Smart money sells into the pop
6. Retail is left holding the bag

> "If you're buying it now, you're too late to the party. The only valid entry was at the open or a day earlier."

### Key Teaching: Premarket Is Noise

> "Don't even pay attention to premarket. Premarket is noise. Especially for anticipation setups, ignore premarket."

LLY went down to $9.18 in premarket — meaningless. Wait for market open.

### Key Teaching: Don't Move Stops Too Early

Member moved stop on LLY too tight (below the open) and got stopped out on a small wick. **Give stocks room to run**, especially with catalysts.

> "You're too early to move the stop. There is a catalyst for the stock. You should give it room to run."

### Key Teaching: Volume Confirmation NOT Required for High-Priced Stocks

Member asked about volume confirmation on LLY. Pradeep was emphatic:

> "For a high-priced stock, is volume confirmation required? No. If you wait for volume confirmation, I can guarantee you'll be late to the party. Especially for anticipation setups."

### Key Teaching: Anticipation = Lowest Win Rate, Most Seductive

> "The most difficult setup to trade with the lowest win rate is anticipation. And which setup is everybody most fascinated with? Anticipation. Because it's the sexiest setup."

### Key Teaching: Writing Your Guide = Retention

> "Every week, I used to rewrite my guide. Why? Mind clarity. So you're very clear."

Without writing your own guide, every session is one-way communication. Retention = zero. Write it, reflect, internalize.

## 3 PM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Anticipation Is a Front-Run Setup — This Is the Core Insight

**The full explanation of how anticipation works:**

> "There are at least 500K newsletters. Out of those, 300K would have had Eli Lilly as their #1 pick. The newsletter writers have already bought it before they publish. When it breaks out, everyone wants to buy it. The day traders sell in the first 5-20 minutes. The algorithms know this."

**The game:**
- Identify anticipation setup (like CWEN)
- Buy at 3:58 PM day before
- Next morning, it pops 4-8% in first candle
- Sell into the pop (the people buying are the ones who saw it in newsletters)
- If you try to buy in the morning, your entry = the exit price of smart money

> "This is going to be the nature of anticipation setup. This is not going to change whether you like it or not."

### Key Teaching: Very Few Anticipation Setups Become Swing Trades

> "Very few anticipation setups are going to go on to become a swing trade. A lot of them are just going to be 5-10 minutes."

Most anticipation = pop and fade. Design your strategy accordingly: buy day before, sell into the pop.

### Key Teaching: CWEN (Clearway Energy) — Tomorrow's Anticipation Example

CWEN setting up as anticipation. NC is tiny. If bought at 3:58:
- Next morning opens positive
- First candle = 4-8%
- That's where you sell
- If you try buying in the morning, you're buying at the smart money's exit price

### Key Teaching: Goosehead (GUZET) — Friday's #1 Idea Proved the Pattern

Pradeep highlighted GUZET on Friday as #1 anticipation idea. It popped Monday morning (the Guzet day), then faded. Classic anticipation behavior.

### Key Teaching: "Jennifer Lopez Has Had Six Marriages"

> "People are attracted to Jennifer Lopez. She's had six marriages. The seventh guy thinks he'll be the lucky one. There are so many other single women who never married."

Anticipation is JLo. You keep marrying it thinking it'll be different this time. It won't.

### Key Teaching: Delayed Reaction Requires a REAL Catalyst

Member asked about ENR (Energizer) as delayed reaction. Pradeep rejected it:

> "If you call every gap up a delayed reaction... What is a delayed reaction? There should be a real catalyst. Estee Lauder had bad earnings, guided down. That's a real catalyst for a delayed reaction. ENR has 0% sales, -1%, -9%. What makes you excited?"

### Key Teaching: You Only Need 2-3 Trades Per Month

> "For September, I just need to find two trades that will go up 20%+ or one to three trades that will go up 15%+."

Be extremely selective. Don't take 60 trades. Take 2-3 A-grade setups.

### Key Teaching: Small Account = More Selective, Not More Trades

> "If you have a small account, you should be MORE selective, not less. Waiting for the best conditions."

Small account ≠ more trades. Small account = fewer, better trades.

### Key Teaching: Sugar Babies Work Both Ways

> "All real-life sugar babies, if you think they're exclusive to you, you're a fool. They go both ways. You can short a sugar baby and it works very well."

### Key Teaching: Process Error vs. Setup Error

> "Not selling Eli Lilly when it's up $18-20 is a process error. It's also a failure to understand the setup characteristic."

The setup (anticipation) is designed to pop and fade. Not understanding this and holding for more = process error, not setup error.
