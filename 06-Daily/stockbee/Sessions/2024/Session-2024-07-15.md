# Breadth Thrust Follow-Through: Continuation Setups, Sugar Babies, and the Size Game

> After the big breadth thrust, continuation setups are proliferating — RDDT delivered a 5% single, SOXL/TQQ shorts are working, and the real lesson is: know your setups like a coffee shop knows its menu, or don't trade at all.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Regime: Bullish, Breadth Thrust, Continuation Setups

> "There's a big breadth thrust right on it. So unless there is a negation of that thrust, it is likely to last."

Money flowed into small caps. The setup that will work: **continuation setups** — first legs have been established, now look for second legs.

> "Your 200% attention should be on finding continuation setups in stocks which have made 15% plus move."

### 20% Study: Factor Analysis — The Victoria's Secret Model Approach

Pradeep's most detailed lesson on factor analysis:

> "If you are made in charge of selecting Victoria's Secret models, what is the first thing you'll do? Study the most successful Victoria's Secret models. Find out their characteristics — height, leg length, figure. Then look for the same thing in the current market."

Build a profile of successful 20% moves:
- What drove them? (News, earnings, stories)
- What was their capitalization? (Most below $10B)
- What was the volume on breakout day?
- What sector? (Biotech, technology dominate)

> "Your objective should be to try and capture two or three good 20% moves in a week. If you can capture two or three good moves of 20% in a week, and you put 25% on the trade, how much will your account move by? 5%."

### Be a Sniper, Not a Sprayer

> "Be like a sniper. You have a lot of options, but select the best one. And if they don't really work — you know the ones which work. They just follow through. They just go up next day. They just don't come back."

### Focus on a Strategy, Master It

> "You have a choice of strategies. Each one of them is valid. Make your decision and don't worry about others."

Valid strategies: momentum burst (9,000,000), EP, SIPs, top 25, anticipation (harder, lower win rate). But pick ONE and go deep.

### Know Your Setups: The Coffee Shop Analogy

> "If you start a coffee shop, are you going to ask 'What is filter coffee? What is a cappuccino?' You would not run a business like that. But trading — that is how people run it."

Every setup has criteria. Consolidation breakout needs volume + catalyst. If it doesn't meet criteria, don't buy it.

### RDDT Trade Management: A Teaching Case

Bought at $73, moved stop up when it faded yesterday, stopped out at $76.79 for 5%.

> "If I don't lose money, that's a win. And this is where, sometimes, if you're an active trader, you're paying attention. When it went up in the morning, I immediately moved my stop."

### Sugar Baby Lesson: When the Call Comes, Answer

On COIN and CIPHER (Bitcoin sugar babies):

> "If you really have a sugar baby and she calls you — 'Hey, honey, can you come home?' — what do you say? You go."

> "MARA — four times I got stopped out, and the day I didn't take it, it worked. The sugar baby called and I didn't answer the call."

MARA was up 22%. The cost of not answering sugar baby calls.

### [[BILL]] Holding: The Best Setup of the Day

> "Continuation setup — persistent continuous buying, very low volume during correction, start of a swing. It's not a third day of a swing."

Pradeep bought BILL with a tight stop, then moved stop to breakeven after it went up $1. Worst case: breakeven. Best case: continuation for several days.

> "Please do not buy BILL. I just gave you an example. Don't buy BILL."

He said this because 50+ emails would come asking "Are you still in BILL?"

### SOUN Trade: Done, Moved On

> "Somebody sent me an email — 'You sold SOUN, and now it is up.' By design, you know, the past moves of SOUN are 2-3 days. That's the trade."

### Progressive Exposure Myth

Extended monologue on why progressive exposure doesn't work for momentum burst trading:

> "Dan Zanger made his money by going all in, on margin. If you're trading three-day momentum bursts, where the fuck are you going to have progressive exposure? You shoot all your load here, put all your dollars, and see what happens."

> "The people who made big money — they put all their account, and on margin, in one trade. Then after they make it, they start giving advice about risk management."

> "It's the size which matters. Ultimately, this game is about size."

### Key Distinctions

- **Singles** = 1-5% account moves, tight stops, high probability
- **Home runs** = wider stops (8-10%), lower frequency, but 20-50% potential
- **Singles pay for home runs** — you can take the SOXL/TQQ risk because singles fund it

## Key Takeaways

1. **Breadth thrust follow-through** — continuation setups proliferating, money flowing into small caps
2. **Factor analysis** — study what drives 20% moves (cap, sector, volume, catalyst) and build a profile like selecting Victoria's Secret models
3. **Know your setups or don't trade** — every setup has criteria; if it doesn't meet them, it's a random momentum burst
4. **COIN is the best play** — sugar baby, Bitcoin thematic catalyst, automatic buy at start of swing
5. **RDDT managed correctly** — bought at $73, moved stop up, stopped out at $76.79 for 5% single
6. **SOXL/TQQ shorts still working** — wider stops funded by singles
7. **Singles pay for home runs** — hit lots of singles, then take calculated risk on bigger setups
8. **Sugar baby rule** — when a sugar baby calls, answer. MARA: 4 stops, then the one you don't take works 22%
9. **Index additions have no edge** — institutional money front-runs them
10. **Size is what matters** — the game is about sizing correctly for the setup type
11. **Progressive exposure is for trend following, not momentum bursts** — on 3-5 day trades, you either commit or you don't
12. **Start of a swing is non-negotiable** — BILL was the only clean start-of-swing setup in the session
