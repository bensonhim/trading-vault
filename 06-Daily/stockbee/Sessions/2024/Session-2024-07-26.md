## 10 AM Meeting

### SIPs Execution: Speed Is Everything

> "09:30:17 I executed the trades, and by 09:33-09:34, I was out. Within four or five minutes. That's the skill."

Pradeep executed SIPs on NSC (Norfolk Southern), 3M, Coursera, HHS using **basket orders** via Interactive Brokers. Entered at market open, closed within minutes for profits.

> "If you have four positions open, how much quick thinking and reflex action is required? See HHS is not working — kill it. This is up $3 — kill it, take profit. That's the skill."

### SIPs Is a Day Trading Strategy

> "SIPs is a day trading strategy most of the time. You need very good execution skill."

- Seven guides on SIPs on the site (Lolo Math recommended)
- Use basket orders (IB TWS) — one button, 4-5 stocks at once
- Close manually, don't automate exits
- Size down in choppy markets — "half single is still a single"
- NSC: entered 9:30:17, out 9:33:57
- 3M: up $4 before could set stops, sold for profit
- Coursera: everyone chased it, but Newell Rubbermaid was actually better SIP

### SIPs Stop Loss: 1-2% Below Entry

> "Put a stop 1-2% below your entry. But most of the time on SIPs, by the time you enter, the stock is already up — you sell when you have profit."

Problem: market orders mean you don't know exact entry. Solution: enter fast, sell faster.

### High-Priced Stocks: 1% Risk, 8%+ Returns

> "You can take 1% risk on AutoZone and it makes 8%. Tell me which low-price stock you can get in with 1% risk and make 8%?"

- [[AutoZone]]: 100 shares, made $10,000+ on a single
- [[O'Reilly]]: very high win rate on breakouts, but wide spread — use limit orders
- BlackRock ($800): 1.53% risk, 4% return in 4 days = excellent risk/reward
- "It's not the size that matters. The motion you create in the ocean is what matters."

### AutoZone: Why I Didn't Buy on the Sell-Off Day

> "I saw AutoZone every day. I was tempted. But on that day, SPY was falling apart. Market was selling off. That's the only reason I didn't buy — I bought it next day instead."

Key lesson: situational awareness matters. A-category setup + bad market = skip. Same setup next day + better market = take it.

### Money Still Rotating Into Healthcare and Value

[[IBB]], [[LABU]], [[VTV]], [[IWD]] (+4.74%), Berkshire Hathaway — all showing money rotating into value and healthcare. This is the trade: value and healthcare.

### Breakouts Fading — Size Down, Not Out

[[Service Corp]] — nice continuation setup but fading. [[Vertex Pharma]] — good breakout yesterday, faded. [[ADP]] — 2,500 funds, nice breakout, chopped by index selling.
