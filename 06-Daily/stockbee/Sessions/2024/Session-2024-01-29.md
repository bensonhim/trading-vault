# Selective Market, Eureka Moments, and the Trade Factory: Singles Finance Home Runs

> "It's not hard work that builds traders — it's the Eureka moment. One flash insight and your life changes. You can do 10 deep dives and still not make money. But one insight? That changes everything."

## 11 AM Meeting — Trade Factory Process, Cat/Dog/Liquid Lava, Context Matters

### Trade Factory: Two Halves That Work Together

**Singles (9M EP, SIPs, sugar babies) → finance the home runs (EP, large size)**

- If you only do home runs without singles, you won't have sufficient conviction or position size
- SIPs and NTRT/MTRT entries allow you to enter setups with close stops
- This is the process, not random picks

### Cat, Dog, Liquid Lava Framework

- **Cat** = stock with clear catalyst capable of 50%+ move (institutional quality)
- **Dog** = stock with dodgy catalyst, story-driven ("car that runs on water")
- **Liquid lava** = stock that trades every day, volatile, can make big moves
- For combo LTV: looking for continuation setups (2 legs+) with catalyst and volume
- Bottom bounce: only trade when conditions are ripe (not now)

### [[SoFi]]: Sugar Baby with Dilution Problem

- 627M float — keeps diluting, insiders selling
- "They will miss no opportunity to dilute"
- Good for day trade, but they'll announce dilution after earnings
- Pradeep didn't even get in despite it being flagged
- Context matters: understanding WHY a stock behaves the way it does

### [[Affirm]]: Context-Driven Selection

- Pradeep read credit article ("credit is back") and IBD feature on Affirm
- That context made him choose Affirm for delayed reaction
- "I read that article and said: Affirm is the one. Makes sense."
- This is the kind of qualitative judgment you can't put in a guide

### [[QuantumScape]]: 100% Unadulterated Dog

- Speculative stock that makes crazy moves for no reason
- "Even from time to time, it can make crazy moves"
- Don't oversize on speculative names

### Size Only With Conviction: [[SMCI]] Example

- When all stars align (AI catalyst, institutional buying, earnings acceleration), oversize
- "How many stocks are you going to find where all the stars line up? Study SMCI as an example."
- Every Fidelity, Vanguard, mutual fund piled into SMCI that day
- That's the kind of SIPs where you put 100% of your account

### The Eureka Moment (Reinforced from Previous Day)

- Flash insight, not gradual accumulation
