# Drink Water, Not FOMO — Bounce Likely, But Buy Nothing Without a Setup

Thursday was a masterclass in doing nothing. Pradeep called the market bullish for a short-term bounce (20% study at extreme lows) but found zero quality setups. The market gapped up, members FOMO'd into gaps, and got stopped out. Pradeep's answer: drink cold water and do 20 pushups. The day's core teaching — buy a setup or don't buy at all — was reinforced by watching every gap fade by noon. He also revealed the origin story of the Market Monitor: built in 2002-2003 specifically to solve his own FOMO problem after getting chopped for three years.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish for a Bounce

> "I'm bullish long term but cautious short term. The market has gone down several days in a row. A bounce is likely. But I'm going to wait for the bounce to come before I buy anything."

SA for Thursday:
- **Bullish for a short-term tradable bounce** (1-3 days)
- The **20% study** is at very low levels — historically, this signals a bounce
- **Breadth still deteriorating** — that's why it's "short term" bullish, not "back to the races"
- **Watch mode today** — don't chase anything
- The 20% study is a **platinum member** indicator

### What to Look For on a Bounce

Stocks that have been sold down recently, especially:
- **Sugar babies** that pulled back
- **AIRM** — example of a stock that went out
- Run the trade factory and let the setups come to you

### Visualization Reminder

Always be in control → controlled risk taking → P (find and enter early) → sell into strength → T3A (think 3 days ahead) → think 30 days ahead

> "For 30 days ahead, I would say we are just going to chop around here. Buy the dip when the Fed is active."

### Key Principles Reiterated

- **Stories > earnings** — stocks with stories make bigger moves (DWSE example)
- **Cougar** — watch cougar videos for linearity patterns
- **Sugar babies** — don't lose your sugar babies
- **FHP** — focus on high-priced stocks
- **80/20** — big bang volume at start of move, then 80/20 profit management
- **FSM** — the objective of all trading

## 11 AM Meeting

### The FOMO Lesson: Don't Buy Gaps Without Catalysts

> "A lot of you people had FOMO this morning. What did he do? He just went and bought. Without thinking."

The gap up created FOMO. Members bought NVDA, AVGO, Palo Alto Networks at the open. All faded. Pradeep drank water instead.

**Why not buy gaps:**
- Gap ups without a **game-changing catalyst** (NTRT/MTRT) fade
- Taiwan Semi — gapped up, but what catalyst makes it a big move? Nothing
- The only reason to buy a gap up is if it has a genuine catalyst that meets 9M EP criteria

> "I see a lot of gaps in the morning. My first reaction is 'oh no.' I was bullish, but I didn't want a gap up. A gap is not what I am interested in. I want to look for a setup."

### Catalyst Quality — Magna 53 Cap 10×10

The guide section has a specific module (Magna 53 Cap 10×10) that teaches catalyst quality. Study it.

### Don't Date Drama Queens

> "Some stocks are like some people — very excitable, always difficult to deal with. It's best not to get involved with those people."

Choppy stocks = drama queens. Avoid them.

The trade factory is designed to:

### BA Update: Stopped Out, But Watching

> "BA has bounced back. I got stopped out on my remaining position, but I'm going to watch this stock because it will set up again and make another move down at some stage."

BA thesis remains intact — just needs to re-set up.

### The Widow Analogy: Don't Date Stocks That Kill Their Partners

> "You're on a hinge, and you meet a girl. Her first husband — she ran him over with a car. Second husband — she poisoned. Third — she pushed him off a cliff. And you want to date her. Is the problem with the girl or with you?"

Breakouts have been failing for days. Good breakouts look good even in bad markets — they just don't work. The outcome is different. Knowing this and still buying breakouts is the problem.

### The Chop Cycle: Pradeep's Personal Story

> "I was a beginner trader. In the first three years, I used to run up my account, give back all the gains, start over. When it happened the third time, I was really frustrated. I said: I need to either find a solution to this problem or I need to stop trading."

The solution: **Market Monitor**, developed 2002-2003, started publishing 2007. A systematic SA framework that tells you when breakouts are likely to work and when they're not.

> "The moment I sat down and said I need to find a solution, the problems disappear. Most people don't want to deal with that problem."

### The Behavioral Change Framework

1. **Recognize the problem** — FOMO, overtrading, style drift
2. **Confront it** — like a doctor telling you high blood pressure = die or change
3. **Make a conscious decision** — operate from SA or become a day trader
4. **Find your solution** — Market Monitor was Pradeep's; yours may be different
5. **The problem disappears** — but then you have a different set of problems

> "You keep trading in unfavorable conditions, lose confidence in your setup, switch to a new style. The market changes. If you had stuck with your method, you'd have made tons of money. But by the time you perfect the new thing, the market phase has changed."

- **Delta Airlines** — Pradeep watching for a second trade with real size

### Short Side: Cautious

Market down 5-6 days = bounce risk. Taking fresh shorts here risks getting squeezed by a relief rally. Ideally wait for a 1-2 day bounce, then short the setup.

### Sugar Babies Don't Care About Market

> "Sugar babies are a different category. When she calls, you pick up. There is a wife, there is a mistress, and there is a sugar baby. You don't ask questions."

### Reactive vs Anticipative Trading

| Style | Trader | Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Reactive** | Pradeep | Combination scan → something shows up → buy setup |
| **Anticipative** | Minervini, O'Neil | Watch list only → wait for breakout from watch list |

Pradeep's problem with watch lists: what you're watching may not break out while other things do. His solution: move anticipation entries to 3:58 PM.

> "There is no perfect solution to all trading problems. You have to find a workable compromise and live with it."
