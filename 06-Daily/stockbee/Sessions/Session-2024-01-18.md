# Short-Term Bullish Bounce — But Buy Setups, Not FOMO Gaps

> "Buy a setup. Only buy a setup. Was there a setup on NVIDIA today? No. FOMO is beginner traders' problem. The worst thing you can do is remain at the beginner stage and have FOMO."

SA flipped to short-term bullish — 20% study at low levels signals a 1-3 day bounce likely. But Pradeep bought nothing all day. Gap ups without catalyst = fade. The bounce was unconvincing. The lesson: buy setups, not FOMO.

## 11 AM Meeting — FOMO Is the Enemy

### Gap Up Day, Nothing to Buy

Market gapped up. People bought in FOMO. Pradeep bought **nothing**.

> "If there is a gap up, I'm not buying. The only reason I'll buy a gap up is if it is NTRT, MTRT, or stocks in play. Without a catalyst, I'm not buying a gap up."

- Combination bullish: only 46 stocks (vs 200+ bearish)
- Most breakouts faded after gap up
- AMD/NVIDIA: 3rd day of bounce, too late, gap up
- Apple: analyst upgrade (not a catalyst)
- Taiwan Semi: choppy past behavior, gap up = pass

### Buy a Setup, Period

> "Buy a setup. Only buy a setup. Was there a setup on NVIDIA today? No. It's day 3. It's FOMO."

The only acceptable setups today:
- [[DBX]] (Dropbox) — good continuation setup, best of the weak options
- [[AVGO]] — looked good but gap up made entry risky
- [[NSC]] (Norfolk Southern) — activist catalyst, consolidation breakout

### Cold Water on Your Head

> "When there is a gap up, you should drink cold water. Or better, put the cold water on your head to cool you down before going in."

### NTRT/MTRT = Catalyst Framework

- NTRT (Negative Time Reaction) = stock misses earnings, gaps down
- MTRT (Multiple Time Reaction) = stock beats earnings, gaps up
- Only buy gap ups with these catalysts
- Analyst upgrade is NOT a catalyst

> "Analyst upgrade by itself is not a catalyst. It's just a gap up."

### Trading Problems Session

**Problem: Can't keep hands still in choppy markets**

> "Change happens only if you get kicked or someone shows you a mirror. Sit down and confront the problem. The day I sat down and said I need to find a solution — the problem disappeared."

Pradeep's solution was the **market monitor** (created 2002-2003):
- Filter: only buy breakouts when primary indicators are bullish
- Only 4 columns initially
- This solved his boom-bust cycle problem

**Problem: Gap up mind clarity**

- Policy decision: don't trade gaps unless you know WHY the gap happened (NTRT/MTRT)
- Simplest solution: prefer non-gap setups

**Problem: Watch list vs reactive trading**

- Reactive traders (like Pradeep): respond to combo scan in real-time
- Anticipated traders (like Minervini): only trade from watch list
- Both are valid — make a conscious decision and stick with it
- "Not dating redheads" analogy — conscious choice, nothing wrong with redheads

**Problem: Position sizing**

- Large position size is a personality type
- Some people can't handle it
- 80/20 works regardless of size — it increases win rate

### Good Breakout vs Bad Breakout

> "A good breakout looks good even in a bad market. But a good breakout in a good market works like a charm, and even a good breakout in a bad market just doesn't work. They look the same."

The visual is identical. The only difference is SA. This is why SA matters — it's the filter that determines outcome.
