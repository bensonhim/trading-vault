### Two Phases of Selling — Critical Framework

1. **Fast selling phase** — Institutions decide to reduce exposure (e.g., by 30%), sell aggressively. No buyers step in. 500-800+ selling days, continuous. Example: COVID crash, early 2023. "Get me out at any cost"
2. **Slow selling phase** — After fast selling, stock-specific reasons drive continued decline. QBTS, IONQ, RGTI, LAES entering slow death — will keep selling until down 90%. Moderna, chemical companies = slow death. Interest rate pressure hits small caps, homebuilders.

- Currently in slow selling, not fast selling — no 500-800 selling days
- Correlation in bearish markets: everything moves together. Good longs get wiped by market sells, good shorts get wiped by intraday buy programs
- **Monday bounce after 4-day decline = reflex buying, not tradable rally**

### Breakouts NOT Likely to Work — Ask One Question
