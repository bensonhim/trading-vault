# Death by Thousand Cuts: No Buyers, Food Distribution Leading, XLP Short Signal

> "This is the kind of market which can lead you to death by thousand cuts. Even if the market is bad, a good setup is going to look good. But what happens after that? The breakout doesn't work."

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Market Regime: Bearish / Choppy — Death by Thousand Cuts

Pradeep's essay: bearish. Market monitor red day. Primary indicators green, secondary red. 20% study leans bearish (2:1 bearish to bullish). VIX pulled back, major indices choppy. Crypto/software selling.

> "By the time you realize there is something wrong in the market, you're basically down 20% drawdown and then your head is messed up, and when the real move starts, by that time you are in no mood to trade."

### Food Distribution Leading = Risk-Off

Defensive sectors leading is bearish signal:

> "When is the last time you saw food distribution leading to a new bull market? When food distribution starts going up, it is a sign that the market is in risk-off mode."

XLP already on third leg — trade is over. Insurance sector also selling. No buyers in technology names ([[META]], [[MSFT]], [[GOOGL]] all stepped on by an elephant).

### Circular Logic of Support Levels

The market bounces because everyone draws the same lines:

> "You have this bunch of people who draw these lines, and their lines tell them that this is the level. So they buy. But the buying has to stick. It's a self-fulfilling prophecy — till it breaks."

Three down days → gap down → people buy → bounce. Repeat. Until it breaks.

### XLP Short: Third Leg Exhaustion

XLP short flagged yesterday as exhaustion play:

> "Every time XLP has made a move like this, it has been followed by a down move. This trade is already over. This is third leg."

XLP gapping down this morning. If it stays below half of the day, stop at high of day — low risk short setup.

### Sector Focus Question

Member asked about focusing on sectors:

### Suicide Indicator / Email Indicator

Pradeep's contrarian sentiment gauge is bearish — not enough fear:

> "I have 40 emails where people are asking, should they put all their money in MAG7? What could go wrong with MAG7? I don't have one email from someone saying they've lost 80% of their account. When I start getting those emails, I become bullish."

### When Will the Turn Come?

> "Every turn in the market starts with numbers which will scream buy buy buy buy. It will be loud and clear — 1,500+ stocks at 4% plus. You'll have 50 EP 9M opportunities. Your scan will have more setups than your money."

### Situational Awareness: The Higher-Order Skill

> "If you are going to be trading breakouts, you have to be trading breakouts in a market in which breakouts are likely to work. If you are going to be trading pullbacks, you need to be trading pullbacks in a market in which pullbacks are likely to work. Situational awareness is non-negotiable."

> "This is a higher order skill. Most people don't even survive to get this higher order skill. They blow up before that."

### Casino Analogy: The Greatest Fool Theory

> "You go into a casino — green lights, red lights, blue lights — you just want to gamble. I put $20 in, walked away with $525. Cash out immediately. Nobody chases you. People cannot control their emotion."

The market opens and people chase — greatest fool theory in action.

### FSLY Analysis: Catalyst Already Priced In

> "Can you look at an essay-plus setup? When essay is in your favor is when you trade. Make money with the least risk possible."
