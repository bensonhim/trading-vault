# Young Momentum, Sugar Babies Calling, and the 20% Study Bounce

After four consecutive red days pushed the 20% Study to extreme bearish readings, Pradeep identified a high-probability short-term bounce — but stressed that this is a **tradable bounce, not a trend reversal**. The session's central argument: focus on **young momentum** (stocks just beginning their move) rather than chasing old momentum (extended names), and use the confluence of extreme breadth readings + sugar baby signals to time entries. The market obliged with a late-day rally, but Pradeep was eating lunch when it happened — reinforcing his rule: don't chase, trust your process, and let the trade factory produce tomorrow's setups.

### The Young Momentum Thesis

This was the session's dominant theme — Pradeep returned to it repeatedly:

> "Look for young momentum. Not old momentum. In every culture, there is a value to youth. Same in the stock market."

> "After EP 9,000,000, look for young momentum — not something like ABNB, which has already made a big move."

> "All these stocks called 'true market leaders' — they are called that after momentum has already peaked out. By the time you find them, the momentum has peaked out."

- **Old momentum** = stocks that already made big moves (e.g., Oklo, names near 52-week highs that have topped out)
- **Young momentum** = stocks just beginning their move, showing up on 20% Study, momentum scans, bullish combo
- **How to find it**: Use the 20% Study to see where new momentum is emerging; use momentum tabs sorted by 10-day momentum

### Singles, Mini Home Runs, and Home Runs

Pradeep laid out the account-growth framework clearly:

> "You need a combination of home runs, mini home runs, and singles to make money in the market. People who only try to do home runs will have very high drawdowns."

- **Singles**: 0.5%–5% account move per trade; target 8–20% stock move; most frequent
- **Mini home runs**: ~10% account move on a 25% position that moves 40%
- **Home runs**: Occasional; need tight stops (2–2.5%); funded by singles profits
- **Peeling strategy**: Oversize to 75% of account, peel at 10% profit, let remainder ride

> "If I put a 8% stop on a home run, it is only possible if I can make singles where I can put 2%, 2.5%, or 1% stop."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Focus on young momentum, not old momentum** — Stocks near 52-week highs that have already peaked are "old momentum." Use the 20% Study and momentum scans to find stocks just beginning their move.

2. **Extreme 20% Study readings = contrarian bounce signal** — When bearish readings hit 100+, a short-term bounce of 2–3 days is highly probable. But it doesn't guarantee a trend reversal. "Don't start kissing your cousin yet."

3. **EP 9M and DEP work independently of market conditions** — These catalyst-driven setups don't require bullish breadth to succeed. They have a "reason to go up."

4. **Sugar babies all calling on the same day = market confirmation** — When multiple high-beta, catalyst stocks break out simultaneously, it confirms the bounce signal from breadth indicators.

5. **Don't chase extended entries** — If SNDK is already 10% above your ideal entry, the stop is too wide. Wait for a better entry or buy a non-extended alternative like Intel or TNA.

6. **Think in setups, not stocks** — "Setup selection, setup selection, think in setups, develop procedural memory for setups." Don't ask "what about SpaceX?" — ask "what setup is there?"

7. **Buy the start of a swing, not the third day** — Stitches Fix was the first day of a swing on the day before; buying the third day is like going back to a girlfriend who kicked you out two days in a row.

8. **Singles fund home runs** — 0.5%–5% account moves from singles (8–20% stock moves) are more frequent and build skill. Use peel-into-strength: oversize to 75%, peel at 10%, let remainder ride.

9. **Intraday volatility = danger zone** — In choppy markets, early entries get faded. Real moves start later. "If you don't want to get chopped, don't trade in this market condition."

10. **Build your watch list at night** — The PM meeting confirmed that stocks on the morning watch list (Dell, Intel, SNDK) were the ones that broke out. "Do the DEP/SB watch list at night instead of spending time talking to your trad wife."

11. **Marry a setup** — "Nothing is going to happen in your trading life unless you marry a setup." Start with one setup (EP 9M), master it, then add others. The principles remain the same.

12. **Higher education matters** — Pradeep's MBA from JBIMS transformed his life; don't listen to people who went to Ivy League schools telling you education is useless.
