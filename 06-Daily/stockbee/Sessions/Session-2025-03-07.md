# DeepSeek + Walmart Dual Catalyst, Slow Horror Movie Selling, AVGO Earnings

Bearish essay unchanged. 400+ selling day. T2108 at 25.70. Two big forces driving the decline: DeepSeek (changed the AI narrative, stocks never recovered) and Walmart (consumer slowing down). Tariff uncertainty adds daily volatility but is not the primary driver. "This is a slow-moving horror movie." AVGO earnings winner with 77% AI revenue growth. SIPs and controlled risk taking. Duration bearish market possible if Trump policy changes continue.

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bearish, 400+ Selling Day

- 400+ selling day yesterday
- T2108 decreased from 28.43 to 25.70
- 10-day ratio + primary/secondary indicators red with deteriorating margins
- 20% study leans bearish (just over 1:1 bearish to bullish)
- VIX jumped. All markets ended negative after tariff news
- TNA and IWM pulled back. Crypto declined

### Key Themes

**Two Big Forces Driving the Decline**

> "DeepSeek has been 100 times more powerful than Trump."

1. **DeepSeek** changed the AI narrative. NVDA gapped down 16% that day and never recovered. 30+ AI models released since. AI becoming a commodity game. Companies questioning massive capex.

2. **Walmart earnings miss** was the signal that the consumer is slowing down. Walmart is the economy. 70-80% of people shop there. The Walmart earnings miss gave a "bloody blow" to an already weak market.

3. **Earnings reaction failure** was happening even before DeepSeek. Good earnings were not getting rewarded. Stocks went up 1-2 days then faded.

**Duration Bearish Market Possible**

> "Can a 14-18 month bearish market where the market goes down 20-30% happen? Yes. The factors exist."

- Policy changes (tariffs, government cuts) can drive duration bearish markets
- Reducing government spending = reducing buying power = bearish
- T2108 needs to go below 20 or even into single digits for real bottom

**AVGO (Broadcom) Earnings Winner**

- Record Q1 revenue, AI revenue up 77% YoY
- Bucket one stock, high short interest
- But fading from premarket levels. Day trade opportunity only
- Gap, AVGO, Starbucks (SBUX layoffs), DoorDash (lawsuit, pocketing tips)

**SIPs and Controlled Risk Taking**

- SIPs always work. They have a specific catalyst for that day
- Find free money setups. Peel positions
- High intraday volatility and news-driven sudden moves make swing trading with close stops difficult
- Need wider stops or lower size

**Slow-Moving Horror Movie**

> "You want T2108 to go down 40-50% in one day. Otherwise it's just Chinese torture."

- Gradual selling, not capitulation
- Stocks near highs are breaking down slowly (Netflix, Arista, PayPal, CDNS)
- Technology and biotechnology sectors dominate the downside
- More stocks joining the bearish party every day
