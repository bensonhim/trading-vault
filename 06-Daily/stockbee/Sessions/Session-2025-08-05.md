# Stalemate Day — Shorts Setting Up, Lemonade the Only Long, Fix Your Setup Clarity

T2108 bounced to 43.9 (from 36.95), 20% study equal-weighted at 1:1, market monitor had 300+ green reading — but Pradeep's verdict remains unchanged: **"Are breakouts likely to work? No."** Market is in a stalemate between institutional selling and deep buyers. The only actionable long: **Lemonade** (9M EP, delayed reaction candidate). Everything else is on the short side — TXN, LRCX, SOXL, JCI, Spotify, CRM, AMAT all setting up as delayed reaction shorts. Many perfect big-structure shorts have earnings in 2-3 days (problematic). XOM short working — 3rd day, take profit. Trump announced semiconductor and pharma tariffs incoming. Dominant message: **write a setup guide, build procedural memory, get mind clarity**.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautious, Reflex Bounce

Judy's essay: cautious, yesterday's bounce was quiet on headlines. T2108 increased to 43.9, 20% study equal-weighted 1:1, VIX retreated. Major indices rallied on buy-the-dip enthusiasm. Small caps (TNA, IWM) outperformed. Plan: slow and deliberate risk-taking on A+ quality EP 9M and delayed reaction setups.

Pradeep's assessment:

> "Are breakouts likely to work? No. Follow-through will be a problem."

> "The market is having a weak sell-off today. Yesterday was a bounce. Ideal scenario: small bounce, then continues to sell off. Worst case: chop zone."

### Delayed Reaction Shorts = Primary Play (Again)

> "My number one focus is delayed reaction EP. Most of my watch list is delayed reaction, and most of them are shorts."

> "TXN will give us another opportunity to short. LRCX — these all reacted negatively to earnings, and they're rallying a little bit. The template for this kind of play is Wells Fargo after earnings — it rallied, then the short was right there."

### The Template: Wells Fargo Pattern

> "The template for this kind of play is: stock reacts negatively to earnings, bounces for a few days, then offers the short entry. That's exactly what's happening on LRCX, TXN, JCI."

### Write a Setup Guide — Build Procedural Memory

> "If you do not have a written guide for your own setup, especially if you are new, you're just going to be wasting your time again and again."

> "Every day, I used to write a guide for my own trading. I rewrote it every day so I could remember before the market opened."

> "If you're asking me 'is this a 9M EP?' every day, it means you don't understand the setup. If you understand the setup, you can answer your own question."

### Mind Clarity = Key to Profitability

> "Mind clarity about setup is going to come when you write down a guide for yourself saying 'this is what weak structure short is.' Show up for boot camp."

> "I think one of the biggest problems I see — people buying breakouts that are not working. Have you heard of something called market monitor? Selling, selling, selling, selling. What do you think? Your breakouts are going to work?"

### Don't Short Early — Deep Buyers Still Present

> "There is a structural tendency in the market. Anything which is down, there are deep buyers who try to buy it. So you don't want to be caught shorting something early."

### Lemonade = Best Long Candidate

> "Lemonade — I said this would become 9M. Definitely a good setup. High short interest, 7 days to cover, X-ray $36 to $48. Definitely something to keep an eye on for delayed reaction."

> "I'm more inclined towards delayed reaction nowadays because it allows me a much better entry. I don't know where to put a stop on a stock that just took off."
