# Back from Norway — Secular Bull Intact, Choppy Range Persists, and Breakouts Are a Trap

Pradeep returned from a week in Norway to find the market exactly where he left it: choppy, range-bound, and full of members with itchy fingers buying breakouts that were destined to fail. The AM session framed the big picture: the secular bull market that started in 2009 is intact (no bubble signs — IPO counts are 199 vs. 1,000+ at bubble peaks, Fed is not aggressively hiking), but the 18% index move in two months has created a sideways digestion phase that could last until the next earnings season. The PM session reinforced that breakouts in a range-bound market are a recipe for chopped accounts, the short side (semis, oil/gas plays) looked slightly more attractive, and Pradeep repeatedly warned against forcing trades. His key advice: ease back in slowly after a break, focus on EP 9M with catalysts relevant to current conditions (oil/Iran), and don't trade pattern-based breakouts.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Secular Bull Market Framework

Pradeep opened with the long-term view: the secular bull market that started in 2009 is intact.

> "Secular bull markets last anywhere from fifteen to twenty years, even more. ... Secular bull markets end with bubble: 800 to 1,000+ IPOs in a year or less, plus the Fed raising rates to cool the economy. Both of these are not currently happening."

The AI secular trend is only two years old — likely has another three to five years to run. The current choppy range is just digestion after an 18% move in two months.

> "18% bull market happened in two months. So after that, you are just in a choppy range, and this chop is likely to continue — probably not this earnings season, it will probably resolve next earnings season."

### 20% Study Confirms Range-Bound

The 20% study hit low readings (27) and the market bounced, but there was no strong breadth thrust.

> "The funds are not aggressively buying, and the funds are not aggressively selling. ... You make money when the funds are aggressively buying."

### Easing Back In After Holiday

Pradeep's approach after a break: slow and deliberate, not gun blazing.

> "Whenever I come back from any holiday, I don't go gun blazing because if you go guns blazing, you're not really completely in tune with the market. ... You have to ease into trading when you come back."

### Breakouts in a Choppy Market Are a Trap

The core AM warning: breakout traders will get chopped in this environment.

> "This is the period where most of the traders who are breakout traders are going to just chop their account because they don't have the ability to not trade a breakout in a market where breakouts are not working."

> "There is very little edge in trading breakouts most of the time. There is an edge when the conditions are perfect. That time, it works like a charm."

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### Short Side Slightly More Attractive

With the market range-bound, Pradeep noted the short side had slightly better setups.

> "If you are looking for something, obviously short side is the one which is under today. So SNDK, SOXL."

But he cautioned that range-bound action can gap up and chop you out.

### Catalyst Must Match Market Conditions

Pradeep emphasized that catalysts only work if they are relevant to the current environment.

> "When COVID-19 happened, most stocks were going down, but the stocks which had a catalyst specific to COVID-19 — mask providers, vaccine providers — that was the catalyst specific to that time period. So do you have a catalyst specific to this time period?"

### The 17-Services Problem

A member who had been through 17 trading services and was still struggling asked if he should sign up for StockBee.

> "Please don't sign up because if you have gone through 17 services and you are still struggling, it is not that there is anything that you should be able to figure out why you're struggling. That is more important than signing up for another service."

> "You don't want to inherit somebody who's gone through 17 services — according to me has a problem which is not related to setups at all."

### Self-Sabotage Pattern

Pradeep described the recurring pattern of traders who make money then destroy it in one bad day.

> "They will listen to you for six weeks. And then one day, in one hour, everything goes out of the window, and they just go and kill themselves."

> "A surgeon here who was a member — making money, and one day a trade went against him, and he would just go and do a thousand cuts. Eight, nine times, he has done the same thing: making a million and losing it in three to four days."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Secular bull market is intact** — No bubble signs (IPO counts 199 vs. 1,000+ at peaks, Fed not hiking). AI trend has 3–5 more years.
2. **18% in two months created a choppy digestion** — Range-bound action likely persists until next earnings season (September/October).
3. **Breakouts are a trap in range-bound markets** — Momentum/breakout strategies only work when funds are aggressively buying. Currently they are neither buying nor selling aggressively.
4. **Ease back in after a break** — Don't go gun blazing after a holiday. Slow and deliberate.
5. **Catalysts must match market conditions** — Iran/oil is the current relevant catalyst. COVID mask/vaccine stocks were the 2020 catalyst. Generic catalysts don't work.
6. **Short side slightly more attractive** — Semis (SNDK, SOXL) and Groupon breakdown looked better than longs.
7. **17 services = not a setup problem** — If someone has been through 17 services and still struggles, the problem is deeper than setups.
8. **Self-sabotage is unsolvable from outside** — You cannot fix someone who makes money for six weeks then destroys it in one hour.
9. **London Calling video was the warning** — Pradeep flagged this environment weeks before it happened.
10. **Norway is beautiful** — Fjords, friluftsliv, and frozen pizza. Recommended.
