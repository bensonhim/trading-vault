## 11 AM Meeting — The Party Metaphor: SIPs Is a Hookup

### ⚡ Meta = The Only Party in Town

> "Where is the day trade party likely to be today? Meta. That's it."

- Meta had massive earnings beat — the ONLY game in town
- Decks (Deckers) also good but less liquid
- Amazon also a party but smaller move
- Clorox = "55-plus community playing bingo, talking about appendicitis and diabetes" — NOT the party
- SIPs selection = choosing which party to attend — go to the biggest party

### SIPs vs EP: The Dating Metaphor (Core Teaching)

> "SIPs is a hookup. It's just a one night stand. Sometimes a few hours. Take your money and go."

| | SIPs | EP |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | 1 day (sometimes 2-3) | Weeks to months |
| Catalyst required | Yes — that day only | Yes + neglect + multi-week move |
| Neglect needed | No | Yes — added advantage |
| Analogy | Tinder date — "does it matter what her values are?" | eHarmony — "what are her values, what does she do in free time?" |
| Examples today | Meta, Deckers, Amazon, IBM | SMCI (still holding from earlier) |
| How to play | Buy morning, sell same day or next | Position trade, hold for weeks |
| Rarity | Daily opportunity | 2-5 per year |

- IBM SIPs worked for ONE DAY then faded — that's what SIPs do
- SoFi was a "hookup" — next day, got hit with a stick
- Management change SIPs = one day, gone (showed chart)
- **Neglect makes SIPs into EPs** — SMCI had neglect + catalyst = multi-week move
- Meta had no neglect but had massive catalyst = one big day

### The Only Question for SIPs

1. **Is there going to be a party today?** (Is there a catalyst?)
2. **Where is the biggest party?** (Which stock will attract the most day trader attention?)
3. **That's it.** No deeper analysis needed for the day trade.

- Today: Meta was the answer to both questions
- If you can't answer those questions, you're not ready for SIPs

### Deckers: Good SIPs But Execution Issues

- Deckers had good earnings but NYSE stock = OPG problems
- Market makers won't fill your OPG order if they'll lose money
- Solution: for NYSE stocks with lower liquidity, use market order after open, not OPG
- This is an execution skill that must be learned

### NVIDIA Goes Parabolic

- NVDA now in parabolic move (like Qualcomm in dot-com)
- Qualcomm went 2618% — can NVDA do similar? Possible but size limits
- All parabolic moves end in buyer exhaustion — eventually one day gives back massive gains
- SMCI: "I think it will go to 700 or even more" — still holding swing position

### Mind Clarity About Setups

- The #1 problem: people don't understand the difference between SIPs and EP
- "You can put it on Fox News, Instagram, TikTok — people still won't get it"
- Writing your own guide = the way to develop this clarity
- "If your boyfriend/girlfriend can understand your guide, it's clear enough"
- Study last week's SIPs: what worked, what didn't, what was the catalyst, why Meta was the biggest party
