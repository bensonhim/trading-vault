# How Pradeep Read the Top — The London Calling Origin Story, Selling Spreads, and the Indian Skill

July 17 was a teaching masterclass. Pradeep walked through exactly how he identified the market top — the relentless 33% TQQQ rally in two months, the 20% study hitting 248 (highest since COVID), the June 5 breakout failures on NVIDIA and GLW that were the first warning sign, and how that led to the "London Calling" video. He then explained how he found the Google and BB shorts: not through insight or crystal balls, but by running the same EP 9M scan every day and finding setups. The PM session's key lesson was the "Indian skill" — the ability to put your ear to the ground and sense what's coming by scanning the environment, seeing ugly reversals everywhere, and recognizing that selling is spreading from AI/semis to the defensive names where people were hiding.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### The London Calling Origin Story

Pradeep walked through exactly how he identified the top, step by step:

1. **The relentless rally** — 33-34% in TQQQ in two months, no precedent, not even COVID rally had zero pullback days.
2. **20% study at 248** — Highest reading since COVID. Every time it hit 200+, a pullback followed.
3. **June 5 breakout failures** — NVIDIA, GLW, CoreView all had EP 9M breakouts that gapped up next day then crapped. "For the first time in the rally, expected behavior changed."
4. **June 6 London Calling video** — Made on the weekend after seeing the breakout failures.
5. **June 25 SA change** — Consistently said choppy transition since then.

> "I had taken GLW, which also did the same thing — next day gap and then third day gap down. This is where I became [cautious]. I said, now this party is over."

> "I had a six-digit profit which evaporated in the first fifteen minutes, and I ended up selling those positions for small losses."

> "This is not to tell you how good I am at it, but to tell you this is the skill which you need to develop."

### SA Is the Most Important Skill

> "If you want to be in this game, this is the most important skill, which is called situational awareness. The ability to read the environment and accordingly change your tactics, change your risk."

> "It is not easy. But is it impossible skill to develop? No, it is not impossible."

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### The Indian Skill — How to Develop SA

Pradeep's metaphor for situational awareness:

> "Situational awareness is just when you walk out of your house in the morning, and you see three cars in front of your neighbor who never has cars, and you see an ambulance — isn't that situational awareness?"

> "You have to be like the Indian. Not the Patel Indian. Not the Johnny Walker Indian. Not the gas station Indian. Which Indian who could put their ear to the ground and figure out there was a horse coming or a car coming or a cart coming."

> "Constantly scan the environment, and create situational awareness. Otherwise, there is no guide that can teach you this. You have to pay attention."

### Selling Is Spreading

The key PM observation: selling has moved from AI/semis to the defensive names where people were hiding.

> "You see failure of breakouts which had happened in the last two or three days. You see so many of them had these ugly reversals. ... Look at Coca Cola. Have you looked at Coke?"

> "If you run your bearish scan today, you'll see 300 setups like this. ... Ugly reversals after reversals."

> "The selling might spread to the other stocks which are holding up. ... Wherever people are hiding is also not safe now."

### How SA Works in Practice

> "This is why I run both scans even though I'm not going to trade long setups today. I went through everything on the long thing. How do you create situational awareness? ... You see three cars, an ambulance — isn't that situational awareness?"

### Semiconductor Is the Modern Oil

> "There is only one sector which matters to the market — technology, semiconductor. Semiconductor today is the oil equivalent of oil. The economy cannot survive without semiconductor. ... You look at your house, everywhere, in every single thing, there is semiconductor."

### Nothing Actionable

10. **Semiconductor is the modern oil** — The economy cannot survive without it. When semis roll over, everything follows.
