> "Not one person has sent me a message where that person is scared. The only messages are: can I buy SNDK? Do you think it will go to a thousand? Move — why didn't you select move yesterday? That is how life is when you don't have situational awareness."

### Sort by Percentage — Low-Risk Prioritization

Pradeep demonstrated sorting scans by percentage (lowest first) to find low-risk opportunities:

> "The advantage of sorting by percentage at 3 PM is you can prioritize the low-risk opportunities. Something which is down 10-20% is already too late to short."

### Only Viable Shorts: Lennar and FAS

After full scan review:

- [[LEN]] (Lennar) — clean breakdown, earnings $3.19, 2.5% stop might work. Piece of crap stock but the short setup is clean.
- [[FAS]] — financial ETF short, but range-bound market makes it dangerous. Will work well when SPY has range expansion down.

> "I would not recommend shorting anything with this kind of range-bound action. Tomorrow might be a positive day and you'll get stopped out on your shorts."

### Range-Bound Danger

> "90% probability this market is going to break down to the downside soon, but it can take a couple of weeks. If you don't have patience, by that time you'll be down another 10-20%."

> "If it breaks down from here, you'll get so many 9,000,000 breakdowns. But before that, if you try to short, you're going to get chopped."

### Day Traders Market
