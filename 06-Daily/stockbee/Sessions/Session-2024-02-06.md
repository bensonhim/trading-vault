# Choppy Market — Catalyst Creates Charts, Discrete Info Is Fugazi

Day two of choppy continuation. Breadth deteriorating — 5 straight days of more selling than buying. 9M breakdowns now working better than 9M breakouts. Pradeep reinforces yesterday's core teaching: **catalyst creates charts, not the other way around.** If you had to start over, focus on catalyst first. Discrete information (fundamentals, IPO status) is "Fugazi" — it means nothing without a setup. SIPs execution is an art, not a formula. Low-price stocks need 8-10% stops, not 2.5%. The market priced in the rate-cut rally in 6 weeks — now it needs months to consolidate.

### Catalyst Is King — If Starting Over, Focus on Catalyst

> "If I had to start all over, the number one thing I would focus on is catalyst."

- Catalyst creates charts — not the other way around
- A stock does nothing for weeks, catalyst appears, then it takes off
- Study earnings reactions: did it "light up the sky" or fizzle?
- Veronius: perfect setup but past earnings fizzled → low confidence
- Palantir: 2B float, dilution machine, Peter Thiel stock — probability of big move like "me becoming president"

### SIPs Entry: Art Not Formula

- SMCI: Pradeep didn't do OPG because couldn't determine stop level
- Waited 1-2 minutes, entered when it started recurring (bouncing from initial dip)
- "Execution is an art" — no perfect answer, watch the stock till the last minute
- SIPs will never go away — but knowing SIPs doesn't make money without execution skill
- Market context matters: on a day with 90% stocks down, you can get burned on SIPs entry

### Float Question: 2B Float = Not a Rocket

- Palantir: 2B float, $36B market cap — "probability of becoming a rocket is as much as me becoming president"
- Short interest only 1.8 days — no squeeze fuel
- Can it work? Possible, but not probable — size accordingly or avoid
- "If you have to ask someone 'is this a good stock?' — you shouldn't play it" (zero confidence = zero trade)

### Forward Guidance > Backward Earnings

- Market is forward-looking — airlines best bought when profits are in the toilet (cyclical bottom)
- ULCC: 5.1 days short interest = interesting, but no guarantee of follow-through
- Each situation is unique — phase of market cycle determines interpretation

## 11 AM Meeting — Execution Art, Low-Price Stock Stops, Consolidation Breakouts

### Market: Still Choppy, Highly Selective or Sit Out

- Breakouts still not working — "you have to be highly, highly selective or drop to day trading level"
- Some 9M will still work, some breakouts will still work — but broadly, not working
- Nvidia the only real stock in play — Pradeep did a small NVDA trade, closed immediately
- Adobe might follow through but wide bid-ask spread = small size or skip

### SIPs Execution Details

- SMCI entry: waited for initial dip, entered at 1:01 when it started recurring
- Not looking at bid-ask spread — looking at whether the stock was recurring (bouncing)
- "Some SIPs are must-buy (mountainside catalyst) — others are intraday only"
- Must-buy SIPs: adjust size and execution accordingly
- Intraday SIPs: wait for setup on 1-min or 5-min chart, then execute

### Low-Price Stocks Need Wider Stops

- Lower price stocks = inherently higher volatility = wider stops required
- 2.5% stop doesn't work on low-price stocks — need 7-10% stops
- Even half-day stops don't always work on low-price stocks
- Wider stop → smaller position size → more trades needed to move account
- "Everybody attracted to low-price stocks, but they don't realize the math"

### Consolidation Breakouts Appear Late in the Rally Cycle

- MPA (Mark Miner Private) primarily trades consolidation breakouts near 52-week highs
- Continuation setups appear early in a rally; consolidation setups appear late
- When the rally was raging Nov/Dec, MPA had no setups — their members sat idle
- "40-50% of people here were MPA members at some stage"
- Both approaches can make money — but you need SA to know WHEN your setup works

### Breakouts in Choppy = Chinese Girl Scam

- "Perfect breakouts will still show up in choppy markets — that's the beauty of the market"
- Like the Chinese girl scam — always feels like YOU won't get caught
- SA is the filter that prevents you from taking bad breakouts in bad conditions

### ITB (Homebuilders) — Good Consolidation Breakout

- ITB setting up nicely as a consolidation breakout
- But: market conditions not right for breakouts → lower probability
- "If market conditions were right, this would work like charm"
