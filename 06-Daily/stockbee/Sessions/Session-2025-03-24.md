# Counter-Trend Bounce on Tariff Optimism, Companies Guiding Down

Bearish long-term, cautiously bullish for short-term bounce on tariff optimism. T2108 decreased from 28.02 to 24.06. Select breakouts working — mostly technology and biotechnology stocks near lows, not stocks holding up well. Companies continuing to guide down: Micron (guidance fails to signal recovery), FedEx (worrying report, weak industrial economy), Nike (weak outlook). SIPs only, controlled risk taking. No high-confidence setups.

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautiously Bullish Short-Term, Bearish Long-Term

- Essay: Cautiously bullish for short-term bounce on tariff optimism
- Reports Trump may hold back on some April 2 tariffs = positive market reaction
- Remains bearish long-term: choppy, unpredictable, driven by trade policy and recession fears
- 4% indicator had slight red day Friday. Primary/secondary indicators still red
- T2108 decreased from 28.02 to 24.06 (weakness)
- 20% study leans bullish (4:1 ratio). Select breakouts working
- VIX pulled back. Mixed close after tariff developments
- TNA/IWM closed red. Dow/S&P flat, Nasdaq green

### Select Breakouts Working — But From Lows

> "Stocks which have gone up here are stocks which are near low. Most of them."

- Laser, Luminaire, Ouster = technology stocks
- Breakouts working are near their lows, NOT stocks that held up well
- "The biggest bullshit sold to retail investors" is that stocks holding up best will bounce first
- Reality: stocks near lows are bouncing, not the ones that held up

### Companies Guiding Down — Economy Slowing

> "Every company coming out with earnings is saying consumer slowdown, uncertainty, and guiding down."

- **Micron**: sales up but guidance fails to signal meaningful near-term recovery
- **FedEx**: worrying report, weak industrial economy, guidance down
- **Nike**: beat on earnings but weak outlook signals further trouble
- Airlines guided down (Delta, American)
- Trump policies are not stimulative, likely leading to stagflation

### SIPs for the Day

- No strong SIPs identified. Mostly low-priced names (Crowd, Lunar, Boeing mentioned)
- MLGO, ASEC, RGC, TNXP, GLTO, GRR gapping up premarket — low quality
- SIPs on short side working well (Adobe, Costco as examples)

### Key Quote

> "Controlled risk taking. This is not a market where FEE is working."
