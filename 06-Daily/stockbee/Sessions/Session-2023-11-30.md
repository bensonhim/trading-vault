# Rotation Market: Tech Exhausted, Money Flows to New Sectors

> "Bullish long term, short term choppy. Breakouts are expanding — 200+ breakouts yesterday. Story stocks joining the party. But technology stocks are exhausted. Money is rotating into new sectors. SIPs will work when the market is choppy." Back in office after 2 days working from home.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Darnell's SA: Bullish Long Term, Cautious Short Term

> "Breakouts have been fading. Yesterday felt like a PLPD day where lots of things faded. Sitting on hands, waiting for better market for breakouts."

Pradeep's view: "Bullish long term, short term choppy. Breakouts are expanding. Breadth positive two days in a row with 200+ breakouts. Rotation into new stocks and sectors. Large cap tech exhausted — that's where failures are happening."

### T30/T90: Start of a Bull Move

> "We are at the beginning of a bull move lasting months, likely to be a choppier move based on how it started. 100-300 opportunities will show up in 9M. Bucket zero and one will start breaking out at some stage."

### Best Setup Right Now: Delayed Reaction to Earnings

> "Delayed reaction to 9M is a better setup many times than 9M itself. Because on 9M day, if you didn't get in early, you have a very wide stop. Delayed reaction is an excellent setup."

Find these using **sugar babies** scan. Look at everything that made a move in last 50 days. [[ROKU]] and [[LMND]] are examples of delayed reactions that gave better entries.

### SIPs Are the Play in Choppy Markets

> "I would start focusing on SIPs here because SIPs will work when the market is choppy."

### Leg Quality: Short and Explosive

> "The best legs are three to six days. Explosive leg means 15-20%. If it's 50% but took 13-14 days, that's not a good leg."

Short, explosive legs = better continuation setups. Long, drawn-out legs = lower probability.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Bullish long term, choppy short term** — breakouts expanding, but tech fading
2. **Delayed reaction to earnings** — better entry than 9M day itself
3. **SIPs are the play** — they work in choppy markets
4. **Best legs: 3-6 days, 15-20% move** — not 13-day 50% legs
5. **Bucket zero and one** — watching for breakout signals
6. **SIPs = complete trade plan**, not just a list of stocks

## 11 AM Meeting

### Follow-Through: Mixed

A lot of things are following through, but **not in technology**. The tech sector is extended and exhausted. Newer stocks in other sectors are making moves.

### Parabolic Shot Warning: $250K Loss Story

A trader lost ~$250K on a parabolic shot (biotech/short squeeze). Got halted, froze, then doubled down. Pradeep spent an hour counseling him — first stabilize emotionally, then recovery plan.

> "I've seen this happen so many times that it doesn't even bother me anymore. First, make sure they're not going to do something crazy. Then tell them they can recover."

### Procedural Memory Lesson

> "This is a game of procedural memory. Watching somebody do it on live calls builds your bum muscles, not your procedural memory. You have to do it yourself."

Like watching baseball vs. playing baseball. Experts on TV can't hit a curveball.

### Money Rotating into New Sectors

> "Money keeps rotating into new sectors. The stocks which broke out first — they're all extended. The technology party is over for the time being."

### Not Aggressive — Protecting November Profits

> "My SA is not really gung-ho bullish. I'm happy to keep my November profit and not worry too much."

### Key Takeaways

1. **Technology exhausted** — first-movers are extended, money rotating out
2. **Parabolic shots = risk of ruin** — don't touch them, biotech especially
3. **Procedural memory** — must do the work yourself, watching isn't learning
4. **Money rotating into new sectors** — financials, healthcare, consumer
5. **Not aggressive** — protecting November gains, selective entries
6. **Good setups exist** — but choppy market means smaller position sizes
