# Powell Firing Threat Backtracked, "Something Brewing" for Monday, Continuation Setup Teaching

Short week (Good Friday ahead). T2108 dropped to 14.89, five-day ratio collapsed from 6.42 to 1.51. Range expansion down on indexes failed with late rally — classic chop. Trump threatened to fire Powell, then backtracked ("somebody must have threatened to resign behind the scenes"). Market rallied on "stupid news" — nothing fundamental. Deep teaching on why most first legs are NOT tradable and continuation setups are the low-hanging fruit. Goldman Sachs ($512) the most tempting setup but "I'm not desperate." ## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Short-Term Bounce, Overall Bearish

- Essay: Anticipate short-term bounce closing up this short week — overall bearish
- Market Monitor: Red day on 4% study, primary/secondary red with deteriorating margins
- Five-day ratio: pulled back from 6.42 to 1.51 — signaling caution
- T2108: decreased from 16.38 to 14.89 — weakness confirmed
- 20% study: 2:1 bullish (over 100 stocks still, expect pullback)
- Major indices: opened lower on NVIDIA news, attempted bounce, sharply sold off after Powell's outlook
- Range expansion down on indexes, then late rally — sets up for chop
- Plan: slow and deliberate risk taking on A+ quality EP 9M and delayed reaction setups, sell into strength

### NOT How Bull Markets Start — Again Reinforced

- "This is NOT how bull markets start" — emphatic
- Bull markets start with: tech, cyclical stocks, consumer discretionary, financials leading
- What's leading: gold, silver, minerals, mining, defense — NOT bull market leaders
- Technology "in deep funk" since DeepSeek, NVIDIA $5.5B charge confirms
- Biotech: FDA in disarray, drug approvals on hold
- Consumer discretionary: vulnerable, low confidence

### Trump Threatening to Fire Powell — Constitutional Crisis Risk

- Trump publicly discussed firing Fed Chair Powell — would be catastrophic for markets
- Fed role is critical: "Fed is more important than the president"
- Historical context: before Fed (1913), markets had back-to-back bear markets constantly; Fed stabilized that
- Firing Powell would: create constitutional crisis, 6-month court fight, destroy market confidence
- "This is just destroying things for the sake of destroying things"
- Later backtracked: White House said "he didn't mean it" — someone privately told Trump market would crash
- Market rallied on the backtrack — but it was "stupid news" with no fundamental basis

### Deep Teaching: Why Most First Legs Are NOT Tradable

- Extensive 20% study walkthrough showing why first legs are problematic
- Key insight: on the DAY a first leg starts, there's often not enough information to make a buy decision
- "Stocks near the low make the biggest moves" — debunking "stocks that hold up best" myth
- "You should profoundly thank that person for his wisdom — and just not follow that advice"
- Only tradeable first legs start with: reversal bullish, bottom bounce, or obvious start-of-swing with catalyst
- Most first legs: no signal on day 1, no scan trigger, no context for buy decision
- Continuation setup = "the low-hanging fruit of the market" — maximum information available
- "Once you understand this once for all, you'll start looking for continuation setups because that is the only tradable setup"
- Consolidation breakouts tend to FAIL in this environment — almost all failing
- Same logic applies to day trading — not just swing timeframes

### Short Setups Emerging

- Walmart: stopped out for profit on tight stop
- Home Depot, AbbVie: retailers turning down
- UnitedHealth: 18% gap down, may bounce for months (delayed reaction in healthcare)
- Humana: weaker of the two healthcare shorts
- SIPs: UnitedHealth, Humana, LLOY, Lilly, TGL, Hertz, IIN

### Key Quotes

> "This is NOT how bull markets start. Bull markets start with tech, cyclical, consumer discretionary, financials leading."

> "The Fed is more important than the president. That's why the Fed was established — to stabilize the economy."

> "Irrationality cannot be priced in. You cannot predict irrational behavior."

> "Stocks near the low make the biggest moves. The stocks which hold up the best are NOT the ones which make the biggest move."

> "Continuation setup is the low-hanging fruit in the market. Here, you have context, you have information."
