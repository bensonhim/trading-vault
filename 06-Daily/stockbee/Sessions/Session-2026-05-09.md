# SIPs Deep Dive — Stocks in Play for Day Traders

Saturday session dedicated entirely to **SIPs (Stocks in Play)** — Pradeep's framework for day traders seeking explosive one-day moves driven by fresh catalysts. This is not a swing trading strategy; it requires babysitting, lower timeframes, and fast execution. The core argument: *fresh news creates fresh moves*, and this tendency will never change in your lifetime.

---

## 1. What Is SIPs?

**SIPs = Stocks in Play** — stocks with a clear catalyst (earnings, contracts, policy changes, insider buying, hedge fund activity) capable of making 20–100%+ moves in a single day.

> "SIPs is like finding stocks with fresh news. Fresh news leads to fresh moves, and those moves are explosive."

SIPs happen **every day**, regardless of market regime. Earnings season or not, there are always stocks with news making big moves. This is structural — it happened in 1998, 2008, and Livermore's era.

> "If you can develop SIPs as an expertise, it is lifelong. Because no matter what happens in the market, there are always going to be SIPs."

### Key Distinction: SIPs ≠ Swing Trading

| SIPs (Day Trading) | Swing Trading |
|---|---|
| Enter on lower timeframes (1–5 min) | Enter on daily charts |
| Requires babysitting | Put trade on and walk away |
| Multiple trades per stock per day | One entry, hold for days/weeks |
| Catalyst = one-day explosion | Catalyst = multi-day durability |
| "Knife fight" — fast, volatile | "Yogi" — patient, later entry |

> "If you're trying to be like, 'I'm going to have one wife and one sugar baby — I'm going to trade SIPs and swing trade' — it's going to be very difficult. Don't be in two worlds."

## 2. The Barcelona Pickpocket Analogy

The session's signature metaphor: **a pickpocket in Barcelona** — the world capital of pickpocketing, where top pickpockets earn $250K/year — only operates in La Rambla, where all the tourists are.

> "If you want to be a pickpocket in the market, where do you want to be? Do you want to be in a dead stock, saying 'today I'm going to trade GE'? Or do you want to trade Rocket Lab, SNDK, Dell — stocks that had news?"

**Translation**: Day traders must go where the volatility is. Dead stocks = dead money. SIPs = La Rambla for traders.

---

## 3. The 3–5 Candle Rule

A structural insight about how stocks move on **any timeframe**:

> "Stocks move in three to four candles. That is the inherent tendency. When they move, they move in three to four candles. So you sell on the third or fourth candle."

- Applies to 1-minute, 3-minute, 5-minute, daily, weekly — **fractal**
- After 3–5 candles, expect a pullback
- This is not a strategy tweak — it's the **destiny of the market**

> "Three to five day moves are very common. On any time frame. If you look at three-minute or five-minute, you're going to see three candles, and then there's a pullback."

## 5. How to Find SIPs

### Priority Filters

| Priority | Filter | What to Look For |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Premarket volume** | Which stocks are gapping up/down before open |
| 2 | **News** | Earnings, contracts, guidance, policy changes, insider activity |
| 3 | **Short interest** | Higher = better; 5+ days to cover is good. RXT had 22% of float short |
| 4 | **Themes** | When a theme is working (crypto, AI), even Fugazi companies rip 200% |

### Process

1. **After market close**: Scan for after-hours movers and news
2. **Pre-market (4–6 AM)**: Larry F's method — print out all post-4PM news, highlight catalysts
3. **Morning**: Check gapping stocks; build watchlist
4. **During the day**: Trade on lower timeframes (1–5 min) using same setups — breakout, anticipation, reversal

> "Larry F used to get up at 4 AM and print out whatever stock had whatever news came out after 4PM. Then he'd go through it with a highlighter to see what the news was, and come out with candidates."

## 6. Day 2 SIPs

Every SIP has a **second-day move** — up or down. This is structural:

> "Every stock is going to have a second-day move. It's just the way the market operates. Up or down. And if you're day trading, you're agnostic."

- [[RXT]] continued its move on day 2 after the AMD AI contract catalyst
- [[AMD]] earnings created a multi-day SIPs opportunity
- Day 2 can be **easier to trade** for some traders (less opening volatility)

## 9. The "Yogi" Approach to SIPs

JBM and his partner represent the **yogi style** of SIPs trading:

- **No OPG (Opening Range Breakout)** — they never trade the first 30 minutes
- They wait until ~10:00 AM, let the knife fight settle
- Then enter on established patterns
- Built a **database** of all SIPs trades and misses, tracking news, charts, entries, and outcomes
- Have a **morning call at 8:00–8:30 AM** to discuss candidates

> "They don't get into the live fight early in the morning. But there is a lot of money in the knife fight also. Sometimes the real money is in the knife fight only."

## 10. Catalyst Evaluation Skill

Not all catalysts are equal. The critical question: **Is this news capable of a multi-day move, or just a one-day pop?**

> "There is nothing in NXPI which was earth-shattering. But for that particular day, is it going to make a move? Yes. Is it going to lead to a multi-day, multi-month rally? No."

### Examples of Catalyst Durability

| Catalyst Type | Durability | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Earnings beat + guide up | 1–2 days | [[Datadog]] — beats on revenue, guides above consensus |
| AI contract / sector theme | Potentially multi-day | [[RXT]] + AMD AI deal |
| CEO change / insider buying | Variable | Depends on magnitude |
| High short interest squeeze | Day trade only | [[RRGB]], DNUT — terminal businesses, but short interest creates squeeze |
| Earnings miss + guide down | 1–2 days (downside) | Whirlpool — tendency to bounce day 2 |

## 11. SIPs Setup Mechanics on Lower Timeframes

On intraday timeframes, SIPs use the **same three setups** as swing trading:

1. **Breakout** — price clears resistance on volume
2. **Anticipation** — tight consolidation before the move (lower risk, lower conviction)
3. **Reversal** — exhaustion play after extreme move

> "Breakout, anticipation, reversal. These are the basic three setups. There are no other setups than those."

The difference is **timeframe**, not setup type. You trade the same patterns on 1–5 minute charts that you'd trade on daily charts.

## Key Takeaways

1. **SIPs is a day trading strategy** — don't mix it with swing trading; pick one world
2. **Fresh news = fresh moves** — this structural tendency won't change in your lifetime
3. **Go where the volatility is** — the Barcelona pickpocket analogy: trade stocks with news, not dead stocks
4. **Every SIP has a day 2 move** — up or down, agnostic; day 2 can be easier to trade
5. **3–5 candle rule is universal** — fractal across timeframes; sell on the 3rd or 4th candle
6. **Premarket work is non-negotiable** — scan news, gaps, short interest before the open
7. **Short interest amplifies moves** — higher short interest = better squeeze potential
8. **Know your personality** — if you can't babysit a screen, SIPs is not for you
9. **Build your own database** — JBM and Larry F built expertise through daily tracking, not following
10. **Catalyst evaluation is the core skill** — distinguishing one-day pops from durable moves requires reading news and studying history
