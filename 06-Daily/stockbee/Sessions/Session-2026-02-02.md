### SNDK: The Missed Entry Lesson

Multiple members asked when to buy SNDK after missing the delayed reaction entry.

> "What stopped you from entering it in the morning? I told you this stock is a delayed reaction EP on Sunday itself. In the morning meeting we discussed it too. Now you're asking me when to buy it? The best thing you can do is next time be organized for it."

> "Either you understand the setup and buy at the right time, or don't buy it. Miss? What else is the answer? Wait for the next setup."

### Don't Buy Late in the Move

> "I cannot understand the fascination with finding a setup very late in the move, after it has already done multiple legs. Why is that fascinating?"

Epstein Island lesson: **Get them young.** Buy early in the move, not on the 4th or 5th leg.

> "We definitely need you to keep buying on second, third, fourth day. Without you there will be no market. Buying more on the fourth day is the best strategy — for the people who bought on the first day."

### Chop Market = Best Market for Traders

Market in range-bound chop. Not bearish, not breakout — just chop. Best conditions for disciplined traders.

### Process Eliminates FOMO

> "If you have a proper process and setup, there is no need to FOMO. Because there will be a next setup. Write down: what do I want to trade? Why do I want to trade that? Focus on that. No FOMO after that."

### Key Takeaways

1. **SNDK was the delayed reaction EP on Sunday.** If you missed it, wait for the next setup. Don't chase.
2. **Get them young.** Buy early in the move, not on the 4th-5th leg.
3. **Process eliminates FOMO.** Write down what and why you're trading. Focus. No FOMO.
4. **Chop market = best for disciplined traders.** Range-bound action gives setups on both sides.
5. **Don't buy late in multi-leg moves.** If it's already done 4+ legs, you're providing exit liquidity for early buyers.
6. **KNX (trucking sector) doing well.** Sector-based setup.
7. **Biotech anticipation setups exist but gap-down risk is high.**
