# Expect Short-Term Bounce, Qualify Your Setups, and Don't Stand in Front of Elephants

> "If you just keep marking anything as a start of a swing, it doesn't work that way. You have to look at the context and using the context, only select the ones which fit our criteria. What we trade is a qualified start of a swing, which meets certain definitions."

Monday. T2108 at 38.6. 20% study bearish 3:1. VIX surging. DIA and IWM had their largest intraday pullback of the year. Pradeep expects a short-term bounce before more downside. The primary indicator is bearish. Institutions are selling — don't stand in front of elephants. Only trade **qualified setups** with context. The three-legged stool: **setup + situational awareness + process** — all must be equal length or the stool falls.

---

- 20% study leans bearish at **3:1** ratio (36 bullish vs 61 bearish)

### Expecting Short-Term Bounce, Then More Downside

- > "I'm expecting a short term bounce before potential follow through to the downside"
- Any bounce unlikely to stick — unless VIX/UVXI gaps down and collapses
- TQQ had a 9M breakdown — every 9M breakdown on TQQ had 2-3 days follow through

### Stocks That Held Up the Market Now Getting Hit

- Good earnings breakouts fading: Airbnb, Robinhood, Expedia, Shopify, UPST all gave up gains

- > "The market reaction doesn't really look like a positive reaction to that"

### DeepSeek Effect: AI Story Under Suspect

- After DeepSeek, AI stocks never recovered
- Microsoft canceling data center contracts — excess capacity concerns
- > "The AI story is now becoming a suspect"
- OKLO down 40% in one week
- Coin, MicroStrategy breaking down

### Start of a Swing Must Be QUALIFIED

- > "If you just keep marking anything as a start of a swing, then it just doesn't work"
- Qualifying criteria: two links, CV, context, close near high
- A start of a swing on a negative day that gaps down the next day = not tradeable
- Context overrides setup quality in a bad market
- > "Good breakouts will still look good in a bad market. But then when you buy it, what happens is they don't follow through"

### Don't Buy the 4th Leg: HIMS Lesson

- Member bought HIMS on 02/19 after 145% parabolic move — 4th leg entry
- > "These are the kind of trades which are suicidal"
- Ozempic now readily available = HIMS story under pressure
- After parabolic moves, stocks gap down 60-80%
- > "You go long on a fourth leg. What is the probability?"

- New tariffs on Chinese ships entering US ports
- BABA, PDD, JD, FUTU — all gapping down
- Chinese stocks need consolidation before next move
- ETF plays: YANG (long) or FXI/TIGR (selective)

### Don't Look at Premarket If You're a Swing Trader

- Premarket noise yanks you out of positions for no reason
- Only exceptions: catastrophic gap down or 30%+ gap up
- > "If you're a swing trader, don't press stop premarket"

### EP 9M and Momentum Burst Variations

| Setup | Qualifying Criteria |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Momentum burst (continuation)** | Two links |
| **Momentum burst (consolidation)** | C+V (longer consolidation) |
| **Momentum burst (bottom bounce)** | Specific context |
| **Momentum burst (FSP/high-price stock)** | Can start from any level, doesn't need C+V |
| **Momentum burst (plain)** | Only when primary indicator is bearish — just buy range expansion in market direction |
| **Weak structure short** | 3 down days in a row, down 10%, weak bounce 1-3 days, short on range expansion |
| **Strong structure short** | Parabolic short |

- High-price stocks (TRV, DJ30 components) — institutional buying, can start from any level
- Technology stocks — swing can start from any level
- Biotech — different rules, story-driven
- Plain momentum burst ONLY when primary indicator bearish — e.g., short IWM on range expansion

### Buy the Dip = Contrarian Signal

- Every email Pradeep gets: "Should I buy the dip?"
- > "If everybody wants to buy the dip, do you think the dip is going to work?"
- People asking about NVDA, BABA, PLTR — all want to buy
- Contrarian indicator: when everyone wants to buy, it's not the time

### Write Your Own Guide

- > "Write your own guide with clear definitions. I've seen members buy a whiteboard and write their setup criteria in their own language"
- Some members marking everything as start of swing — wrong approach
- Clarify, reclarify, reclarify setup understanding
- Only trade what makes sense to you

### Morning Selling Failed to Follow Through — Bouncing

- Selling stopped midday, no follow through
- Bounce forming — but likely just a counter-trend move

### Start of Swing = First Qualification Criterion

- First check: is it a start of a swing? If no, stop.
- Second: is it first, second, or third leg? (Occasionally third, never fourth)
- Then apply setup-specific criteria (two links, C+V, etc.)
- > "Start of a swing is just one criterion. After that, we look at other criteria"

### Reversal = Anticipation, Not Start of Swing

- > "Reversal is an anticipation. It's not a start of a swing"
- Range expansion AFTER the reversal is what confirms the start of a swing
- Sometimes reversal becomes start of swing, but not guaranteed
- Anticipation setups extremely low probability in this market

### 25% Study: All Big Moves Start with Range Expansion

- Top 25 stocks that made big moves all started with range expansion
- Not VCP, not cup-with-handle, not moving averages — range expansion
- > "If you want to find a stock which is likely to make a big move, what do you have to focus on? Range expansion. Start of a swing"
- Earnings/sales don't predict — momentum gets momentum

### Three-Legged Stool: Setup + Situational Awareness + Process

- Setup alone = stool with missing legs, falls over
- > "All three legs have to be equal length for it to work"
- Setup without situational awareness = buying breakouts in a bear market
- Process without setup = random trading
- Must have all three

### Year Outlook: Range-Bound or Forming a Top

- > "The entire year might be range bound, or this might be a formation of a top"
- Tops take a long time to form
- No euphoria conditions — not seeing 800-1000 AI startup IPOs
- IPO count flat at ~6,116 (vs 7,000+ at 2021 peak)
- Republican second half historically = guardrails removed, bubbles form, bear markets start
- 18 of last recessions started under Republicans, 6 under Democrats
- But currently no euphoria signal
- Day trading and stocks in play = best setups for range-bound markets

### Momentum Burst Will Work When Market Turns

- > "When the market turns around, which setup will work best? Momentum burst. My friend, momentum burst"
- Bottom bounce works for 1-2 days, then momentum burst takes over
- Unfortunately, the setup people don't want to trade is the one that will work

## Key Takeaways

1. **Primary indicator bearish** — T2108 at 38.6, 20% study 3:1 bearish, don't fight it
2. **Expect short-term bounce before more downside** — but any bounce unlikely to stick
3. **Qualify your setups** — start of swing is just the first filter; add context, legs, C+V, two links
4. **Never buy the 4th leg** — HIMS parabolic lesson; probability collapses after 3 legs
5. **Three-legged stool** — setup + situational awareness + process, all must be equal
6. **Reversal ≠ start of swing** — range expansion confirms; reversal is just anticipation
7. **Good breakouts in bad markets don't follow through** — situational awareness overrides setup quality
8. **Weak structure short: 3 down days, 10%+, weak bounce 1-3 days, short on range expansion day**
9. **High-price stocks start swings from any level** — don't need C+V (TRV, DJ30 components)
10. **Momentum burst is the setup that will work when market turns** — bottom bounce for 1-2 days, then momentum burst
11. **Buy the dip sentiment = contrarian signal** — everyone wants to buy, dip unlikely to hold
12. **Range-bound year or top formation** — no euphoria, no IPO flood, day trading best for this environment
13. **Don't stand in front of elephants** — institutions selling, retail buying the dip
