### Qualification Criteria = Probability Stacking (Key Teaching)

> "When you're selecting stocks, it's like dating. She has yellow teeth and is an alcoholic — can it work? Yes. But if you select someone with a stable job who didn't kill her first husband, the probability goes up."

Every filter stacks probability:

| Filter | Increases Probability? |
|--------|----------------------|
| Start of a swing (vs. 2nd/3rd day) | Yes |
| First leg 15%+ with <10 day consolidation | Yes |
| Cap below $10B (vs. above) | Yes |
| IPO in last 10 years (vs. established) | Yes |
| 9M qualification (volume, catalyst) | Yes |
| Magna 53, Cap 10x10 | Yes |

- Each qualification doesn't guarantee 100% win rate
- But substantially increases probability
- Nvidia on 2nd day: can it work? Yes. Probability? Below 50/50.
- Can you build a career on second-day entries? "It is going to hell"

### Capitalization: The #1 Factor Most People Ignore

- Top 50 stocks by 20%+ moves: almost all below $1B cap
- Biggest winners IPO'd in last 10 years, most in last 3-4 years
- "This is what builds edge" — knowing cap, IPO year, sales growth
- Most people focus on VWAP, moving averages — those don't determine big moves
- Capitalization determines how far a stock can go with a given catalyst
