# Process Over Intellect: 9,000,000 Finds SOUN and SOXL/TQQ Short

> In a selective bull market where 47 stocks surged 20% but the trade factory yielded slim pickings, the process delivered SOUN (sugar baby, +43% in 2 days) and SOXL/TQQ shorts (tech breakdown) — the rest was sit on hands.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Regime: Selective, Slow-Grinding Bull

> "It's a selective, slow grinding, selective bull market. Select breakouts are working. Not much money on the short side."

Focus areas: **9,000,000 breakout**, **delayed reaction**, **sugar babies**. If nothing found in those, only then look elsewhere.

### The 20% Study: Buying Came In

The 20% study jumped from 20 (Tuesday) to 47 — buying pressure arrived. Key sectors: **biotechnology**, **solar**, **oil field**. Every breakout started with a **4% breakout or range expansion**.

> "There is a signaling power in 4% breakout. And there is more signaling power in 4% breakout if it is a biotechnology or technology sector."

### Catalyst + Setup: The Two-Legged Stool

> "Catalyst plus setup is the thing."

What drives 50% moves? Earnings, news, stories — then the setup (4% breakout at start of swing) provides the entry framework.

> "What kind of catalyst makes the stock go up 50%? How does the move progress? Where can I find low-risk entries?"

### Build Conviction Through Study, Not Blind Faith

> "Don't just because I say these things, you should not take it as a gospel truth."

Pradeep challenged members to verify every claim:
- Do stocks below $10B cap dominate 50% moves? (Yes — only 5 out of 53 had cap above $10B)
- Do most have IPO within 10 years? (Yes)
- Do stories beat earnings? (Yes)
- Is linearity important? (Verify yourself)

> "Nothing works in the market out of the box. You have to make it work. And to make it work, you have to systematically hack at the problem."

### Dollar Bullish: High-Priced Stock Opportunity

For $20+ moves in 5 days: BlackRock risked $6 to make $10. Not percentage — **risk/reward**. High-priced stocks ($100+) make dollar moves that are tradeable with tight risk.

### Visualization (Same Framework)

Always be controlled. Find and enter early. Sell into strength. Think 3 days / 30 days ahead. BTFD when Fed is active. Plastics (Healthcare, Tech, Consumer Discretionary). Stories > earnings. IPO 10, Built 10, Size, FSS, FHP, BBT, 80/20, Bill.

## 10 AM Meeting (Trade Factory)

### Yesterday's Pick: SOUN Management

SOUN, selected yesterday, was already up 18% at the 10 AM meeting:

> "Most of its moves are one or two days. The past tells you a lot. This move has actually started from here. It's already up around 43%."

**Trade management**: Lock in 90% profit, put a very close stop, let the remainder ride. For a stock that moves 2-3 days, the trade is done.

> "If the last 20% gets stopped out, I have no problem — 20% in a day, I will take it on a stock which has demonstrated that it moves only in two to three days."

### Ford (F): Managing the Liquid Lava

F from yesterday was a follow-through. Strategy: move stop to breakeven. Best case: keeps going, you make more. Worst case: stopped out at breakeven. Zero downside.

### Scanning: Mostly Fading Setups

Most morning breakouts were fading — a hallmark of this selective market:

> "I'm more selective here. Looking for more 9,000,000 breakout kind of thing, something which has a clear catalyst and not be in a hurry to get in."

## 2 PM Meeting

### The Clear Trade: SOXL Short and TQQ Short

Technology was breaking down. Range expansion to the downside across semiconductors and big tech. The trade was obvious from the scan:

> "All the technology stocks breaking down. SPY itself having a range expansion to the downside. TQQ short, SOXL short — that's the trade."

> "Five minutes is all that was needed to make up the mind that the trade of the day is going to be SOXL short, TQQ short."

Why triple ETFs over individual names:

> "Instead of doing individual names, it's much better to go into these things. I go short on the bigger one, the one which is higher priced."

**Key trade**: SOXL short and TQQ short. SQQ for the long side if preferred. Stop at high of the day.

### SOUN: The Completed Trade

> "The SOUN trade is done. Take your money. Run. Write it."

> "5% if you can move your account in one day, is that bad? If you took 25% position and it went up 20%, I think that's a damn good rate."

### SOUN was a sugar baby — magnitude not duration:

> "Home runs are rare. Singles are easier."

### Process Over Intellect

> "What did we do yesterday? Just run the scan, look at 9,000,000, find SOUN, find F, and get into them. Was it something special? It is a process."

> "The people who can follow process, they always make money."

> "Just find a good setup and put your position and see what happens."

### FOMO: The Ice Water Cure

> "FOMO is a beginner trader's problem. Whenever you get FOMO, just drink a glass of water instead of buying or selling something."

> "It's like the UN population conference — what's the best contraceptive? A glass of cold water instead."

### CFO Departure Signal

On Chipotle CFO leaving:

> "Who is the smartest rat in any company? The CFO. He knows all the things. So if he is leaving, why is he leaving now?"

### Market Rotation: Small Caps Getting Asset Allocation

Inflation data showed signs of coming down → asset allocation into small caps. But this doesn't change the process:

> "All that you need to do is run your trade factory and take trades from that."
