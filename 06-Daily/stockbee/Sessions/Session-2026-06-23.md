### The Psychology Core: Discipline Is a Myth

The session's most important teaching — discipline does not change behavior:

> "Discipline is not going to solve your trading problem. It just doesn't work."

> "The story of Livermore — every time he used to make money, he used to give it to his wife to lock it away so that he doesn't lose it. And what ended up happening? Every time he lost money, he would go and take the money from his wife. So how does it help? Artificially trying to be disciplined, I don't think is a solution to any problem in life."

> "I went to a school where there was a big board saying discipline builds character. It might build character, but it doesn't make you successful in life. The people who became successful in my class were all the people who were constantly getting into trouble. And the people who are very good, very disciplined, they are government servants doing some nine to five job."

### What Actually Changes Behavior: Beliefs + Interventions + Qualifiers

**1. Change beliefs, not discipline:**

> "Changing belief, if not discipline, what changes — is changing belief. And changing belief that I should trade with situational awareness. I should always have a stop is a belief."

**2. Physical interventions:**

> "One of the ways to change behavior is by intervention. What is my intervention mechanism? I go to the gym at 11:00. That's an intervention. The moment you have intervention, it's a physical intervention. I'm not physically present in front of the computer, so I'm not going to press buttons."

> "I can try and be very disciplined, sit here, hold my hands, and then you see a trade, and then your hand automatically goes and buys. And there goes your discipline."

**3. Context and qualifiers:**

> "When you have qualifiers in life saying I'm not going to settle for this. I'm not going to just date any number. I have qualifiers for selecting people to hang around with. Or I have qualifiers to go to a restaurant — I'm not going to go to some restaurant which fries food all day. That qualifier itself is going to make sure that I don't eat shitty food."

### T3 / T10 / T30 Thinking Framework

> "Always learn to create a framework — think three days ahead, think ten days ahead, think thirty days ahead — T3, T10, T30. That way you can visualize the scenario. Not all of your scenarios are going to come true, but at least if you are mentally prepared for a scenario, then you have a better thing."

### The Married-Man Analogy (Situational Awareness in Practice)

> "When you're married, every woman going on the street looks more attractive. But then you have to understand your context and qualifier. Otherwise, you'll get into trouble both with that woman and the woman at home also."

### Master One Setup First

> "Start with master one setup. And then go to another one. You cannot master multiple things at the same time. It becomes hell of a lot easy if you do one thing."

## Key Takeaways

1. **"London Calling" was validated** — Pradeep's situational-awareness video from two weeks prior called the market vulnerability. The South Korea shock gap-down confirmed it. Situational awareness is not hindsight — it's forward-looking scenario planning.
2. **Triple 9M EP red candles on TQQQ = confirmation** — One is a freak, two is a pattern, three is confirmation. When they come in a bunch, no good thing happens. This is a simple, observable, mechanical rule.
3. **Discipline is a myth** — Artificial discipline (holding hands, locking money away) does not change behavior. Livermore tried it and failed. Priests try it and fail. The solution is not discipline — it is changing beliefs, physical interventions, and qualifiers.
4. **Beliefs change behavior, not willpower** — "I should always have a stop" is a belief. "Fitness is extremely important in my life" is a belief. Once the belief is real, behavior follows. Without the belief, discipline collapses at the first temptation.
5. **Physical intervention is a real trading tool** — The gym at 11 AM is not a lifestyle choice — it is a deliberate intervention to prevent suboptimal trades taken after the first hour. "I'm not physically present in front of the computer, so I'm not going to press buttons."
6. **Qualifiers filter decisions before temptation** — "I'm not going to go to a restaurant which fries food all day" is a qualifier that removes the decision at the moment of temptation. The same applies to setups: if breakouts are not likely to work, don't look at breakouts.
7. **T3 / T10 / T30 — think in three time horizons** — Three days, ten days, thirty days ahead. Not all scenarios come true, but mental preparation gives you a better response when the scenario arrives.
8. **Breakouts unlikely to work in this regime** — The market is range-bound and choppy. EP 9M with genuine catalysts, SIPs, and short setups are the actionable universe. Breakouts are a trap in this context.
9. **BLZ is the favorite** — $335M/5yr catalyst, highest volume, clear reason to go. Already gapped 41%, so 20% upside remaining. The only stock Pradeep expressed genuine conviction on.
10. **Biotech = size accordingly** — [[CURE]] has a real catalyst and 20–30% potential, but biotech "can take you to the cleaners." One disaster in a lifetime is enough. Small size is non-negotiable.
11. **HP/DELL are tentative, not conviction** — Highest volume in three days, holding, but "not going anywhere." Gap-down recovery is not a momentum burst. Early entry only if you want to be early — Pradeep is not convinced.
12. **Tesla is on the do-not-trade list** — Random things happen. It can gap up and go tomorrow. Stay away.
13. **Never sell conviction is a cycle-repeating trap** — Amazon 1998 (+900%, then -90%), Qualcomm 1999 (+3000%, then -90%). The "if you sell you're the biggest fool" crowd has always been wrong eventually.
14. **Master one setup before moving to the next** — "You cannot master multiple things at the same time. It becomes hell of a lot easy if you do one thing."
15. **The trade factory produces a fresh list daily** — Like fresh French bread. You don't know when the chop ends or which stock goes — only god knows. Your job is to show up every day and run the process.
