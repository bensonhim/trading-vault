# Pullback Likely After Relentless Rally — Selective Market Phase Begins, EP 9M Breakdowns in Focus

January 2 opened the year with Pradeep's clear call: the market was extended after a relentless six-week rally, and short-term pullback was likely. The broad-based rally was transitioning to a selective phase where only the right setups would work. The primary focus: EP 9M breakdowns on the short side, and delayed reaction setups on the long side. Choppy, extended stocks were to be avoided at all costs.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM & 11 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Long-Term Bullish, Short-Term Pullback

> "My situational awareness is short term likely to be a pullback. Because the market is extended. Most of the stocks are extended."

> "In six weeks, market has done what it takes typically eighteen months to do."

The 20% study showed 278 stocks up 25% in a quarter, only 470 down. That kind of excessive strength is not sustainable. The market would correct by price or by time, and the practical result for swing traders would be chop unless they selected the right setups.

### The Second Phase: Selective Rally

> "Now we'll move into the second phase, which is very selective rally. From November 2 onward, it didn't matter whether whatever was, like, and everything was going up. So selective rally is what is likely to happen."

Breakouts would only work in a momentum market. Without aggressive buying by funds, pattern-based breakouts would stall.

### What Works in This Market

| Setup | Works? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| EP 9M breakdown (short) | ✅ | Most likely scenario; extended stocks starting to break |
| Delayed reaction (long) | ✅ | Catalyst-driven, selective entry |
| Consolidation breakouts | ✅ | In second-phase bull markets |
| Pattern-based breakouts | ❌ | No follow-through without momentum |
| Anticipation | ❌ | Choppy market kills tight setups |
| Choppy stocks (XOM, BMY) | ❌ | Taylor Swift volatility, no linearity |

### Key Principles Reinforced

> "If you want to make money in 2024, have a setup. That is the first requirement."

> "Linearity is going to affect the likelihood of your stop getting hit."

> "IPO 10: stocks which are IPOed in the last ten years tend to make the biggest moves. Amazon made 968% in its second year. Tesla made 743% and 348% moves in its first ten years. Zero probability it ever does that again."

> "Stocks with fresh news are more likely to make big moves. If you build your methods around stocks in play, IPO 10, and built-in, you are more likely to make money."

## PM Meeting (2 PM)

### Process Over Guru: Learn the Method

> "If you understand this process once, you can run this on your own, and you can find trades like this on your own without waiting for 11:00 meeting or 02:00 meeting or any meeting."

Pradeep walked through the Trade Factory scan process: start with EP 9M, then combination bullish/bearish, then LTB, then anticipation. The sequence matters.

### Linearity Above All

> "The first thing which you should be looking at whenever you're looking at any setup is linearity. Because linearity will directly affect your stop. If there is no linearity, do you want to be even involved in that particular stock?"

> "The more choppier stock you trade, the more choppier your equity curve is going to be."

> "Almost all good traders make the choice of going to things which are more predictable and less choppy."

### Avoid the North Korea Tickets

> "After watching the 401k video, you came to the conclusion that TQQQ and SQQQ is not the thing to buy? That means I don't know what exactly you're watching."

Repeated theme: buying choppy, extended, low-quality stocks (ExxonMobil, Bristol Myers Squibb, MongoDB on day 2) was a fast track to losses. He called these "North Korea tickets."

### Anticipation: Only One Candidate

After tightening scan criteria to ±0.25% price change, only Mura (Oscar Health's ticker) appeared as a marginal anticipation candidate. Pradeep was not inclined to take anticipation setups in a pullback environment.

### Trade Factory Criteria Tightened

Pradeep tightened his anticipation scan:
- Price percentage change: ±0.25% (down from ±0.4%)
- Net change: less than $0.40
- Added 60+ relative strength filter

> "I'm not interested in having more candidates."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Short-term pullback likely** — Market extended after six-week relentless rally; only three negative days in that period
2. **Selective rally phase beginning** — Broad-based gains are over; only the right setups will work
3. **EP 9M breakdowns are the primary focus** — Most likely scenario in an extended market
4. **Linearity is the #1 filter** — If a stock is choppy, skip it regardless of setup
5. **IPO 10 and built-in remain key** — Stocks under $10B market cap with fresh catalysts make the biggest moves
6. **Cigna was the best setup of the day** — Delayed reaction to catalyst, good linearity, proper entry early
7. **Avoid choppy stocks at all costs** — ExxonMobil, Bristol Myers Squibb, MongoDB: "North Korea tickets"
8. **Don't buy stocks up 13%** — Find and enter early, or don't enter at all
9. **Process over guru** — Learn the scan sequence so you can find trades independently
10. **Anticipation setups don't work in choppy markets** — Tightened criteria and still found almost nothing
11. **Capitalization below $10B** — The biggest moves come from smaller stocks, not mega-caps
12. **Delayed reaction means delayed reaction** — Not buying a week later after the move has already happened
