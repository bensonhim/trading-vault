# Define Your Setup, Stop Diluting: Victoria's Secret vs Walmart Models

> Pradeep's last full day before vacation. Market choppy with intraday fades — good for day traders, difficult for swing. **NVIDIA bought as liquid lava 9M EP, sold for quick profit to recover losses from TGTX and another trade.** Core teaching: **define your setup and don't dilute it.** The "Victoria's Secret model" analogy — start with ideal criteria, then slowly dilute to "Frederick's of Hollywood," then to "Walmart models," and then complain your trading sucks. Delayed reaction requires **clear positive reaction to catalyst** — not any random stock. Start of a swing is non-negotiable. Self-leadership is the #1 determinant of trading success. ### Key Teaching: Find Solutions to Every Problem

> "Every single problem in your life has a process solution."

Blood pressure monitor story: doctor said measure BP 5x/day. Cumbersome. Found a $19.99 smartwatch on Amazon that does it automatically with app tracking. Verified accuracy against traditional cuff — identical readings. Problem solved.

Monitor mount story: multiple monitors taking up 2/3 of office. Found wall mounts for $19.79 each on Amazon. Hired TaskRabbit guy for $450 to mount all 10 monitors. Office freed up.

Trading problems: same approach. Can't get into small stocks because of size? Created scans for 5,000+ fund ownership stocks. Breakouts not working? Created delayed reaction setup. SIPs missing catalysts? Create a process to find them.

### Key Teaching: Yesterday's Trades — 2 Stopped Out, 1 Recovered

Took 4 trades yesterday. 2 stopped out. NVIDIA bought late as 9M EP (liquid lava) to recover losses. It worked — sold for quick profit today. That's what process-oriented traders do: find solutions, not sit and cry.

## 10 AM Meeting

### Key Teaching: Two Distinct Setup Styles

1. **Home runs / mini home runs** — requires fundamental analysis + catalyst/story. 9M EP, EP classic, sugar babies, buckets. These move accounts 10-100%+.
2. **Singles** — pattern-based, requires procedural memory. Combo, LTV. Consistent, smaller increments.

### Key Teaching: Define Your Setup — The Victoria's Secret Model Analogy

> "You start with the ideal setup. Then you don't find Victoria's Secret models. So you start diluting your criteria. Then you start dating Frederick's of Hollywood models. Then Walmart models. Then you say your sex life sucks. Whose fault is it?"

**Delayed reaction is NOT any stock that didn't immediately react.** It requires:
1. Clear positive reaction to catalyst (earnings, news)
2. Stock closes near the high on catalyst day
3. Orderly pullback/consolidation
4. THEN you enter on the breakout

Member asked about a stock that "didn't even attract one buyer on the day of catalyst" — that's NOT a delayed reaction. It's a Walmart model.

### Key Teaching: What Is a Clean Setup?

> "When you see a clean setup, it just stands out. It says 'Hey, please buy me.'"

Clean = orderly, clear range expansion, volume surge, linear price action. Choppy = random, hard to define stops, low probability.

### Key Teaching: Starbucks CEO Change — 99% a One-Day Move

> "99% likelihood this is a one-day move. After this, the stock will pull back."

CEO change = catalyst, but not a catalyst for a 50% move. Starbucks is $85B market cap. Probability of 50% move: very low. Look for delayed reaction setup later, not day-one FOMO buy.

### Key Teaching: Lifeway Foods — Members Missed Free Money

Lifeway: 35¢ vs 21¢ earnings (beat by 67%). 13.4M float. Neglected small stock with catalyst. Free money. Nobody had it in their SIPs because they're "getting into really low price kind of stuff" instead of finding real catalysts.

### Key Teaching: Crocs — 7% First Leg Too Small

7% first leg on Crocs continuation setup. Compare to Dash with 19% first leg. The larger the first leg, the more profit potential on the second leg. If first leg is small, even a successful second leg yields little.

### Key Teaching: Don't Ask About Setups You Don't Trade

> "Is this a setup? Do you understand what setup I trade? If you're trading setups which are not my setups, find a service that trades those setups."

Member asked about Redfin — choppy, no clear setup pattern. Pradeep's answer: "I have no edge trading this setup. Find someone who does."

### Key Teaching: SKE vs FF — Quality Difference Between Similar-Looking Setups

SKE: consolidation breakout, established uptrend, 46% first leg, orderly consolidation. **Quality setup.**
FF: bottom bounce, no established uptrend, no real catalyst. **Lower quality.**

Both can work. But SKE fits the definition; FF doesn't. Define your criteria and stick to it.

### Key Teaching: PSNL — 24% in Less Than 24 Hours

PSNL showed up in scan yesterday. Bought it. Now up 24%. Sold most into strength. That's what a clean setup does.

## 3 PM Meeting

### Key Teaching: If You Can't Define Start of a Swing, Find Another Job

> "If you cannot define what is a start of a swing, you're going to lose money. You're better off doing something else."

The most important determinant of trading success: understanding start of a swing. Without it, every trade is random.

### Key Teaching: Self-Leadership — The #1 Determinant

> "Self-leadership. Without that, nothing happens. Absolutely nothing happens in life until you focus on self-leadership."

Self-efficacy, belief, process orientation, problem-solving. The traders who make it found solutions to their problems. The ones who don't keep asking "what are you thinking?" and never define their setup.

### Key Teaching: How Do Traders Make Money? Singles.

> "Most traders who make money consistently are just doing very simple setup ideas and hitting a lot of singles."

Singles = IAG, Kava, Gold, PSNL. That's it. Every single person Pradeep talks to wants home runs. They start with home run mentality and use home run rules on single setups. Result: losses.

### Key Teaching: 9M Breakout Priority Over Other Setups

> "When you have 9M breakouts, always buy 9M breakout as priority."

Bristol Myers Squibb — 9M breakout, 2,500 funds, clean setup. That's priority over choppy combo breakouts.

### Key Teaching: Small Accounts Need Sure Money Setups Even More

> "If I have a small account, I would be paying all my attention to making sure everything I buy has at least 98% probability of working."

Small account = more selective, not less. Don't buy Tilray at $1 with 9M volume daily. Buy quality setups with catalysts. "It's not the size that matters, it's the motion you create in the ocean."

### Key Teaching: Bid-Ask Spread Matters for Position Sizing

Coca-Cola European Partners: bid-ask spread of 3%. Can't put big size in. That's the compromise — small position, accept the limitation.

### Key Teaching: Shorts — Don't Enter Early

> "I never go into shorts early in the morning. Most shorts tend to have these tails. If they end up trading near the low, probability is higher they'll work."

Shorting on intraday bounces (tails) is dangerous. Wait for confirmation near the low.

### Key Teaching: FOMO Day — Market Up, People Buying Random Things

Market up today = FOMO. People buying setups that don't meet criteria. At least buy something with a valid setup (Bristol Myers, Dash continuation), not random low-price stocks.
