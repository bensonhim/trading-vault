### Setup Template Clarity — Draw It By Hand

- **"Close your computer, take a notebook, draw each setup type by hand"**
- Continuation setup: first leg 15%+ (or $10+ for high-priced), <10 days consolidation, then breakout
- Consolidation setup: long base, breakout with catalyst + volume
- Bottom bounce: huge range expansion from bottom
- **"If you can't draw it by hand, you don't understand it"**
- Take a photo of perfect examples and put them on your wall — "replace your boyfriend/girlfriend photo with setup charts"

### Costco vs Adobe — Understanding Bottom Bounce vs 4-Day Chase

- Costco: clear bottom bounce setup (near lows, bouncing)
- Adobe: 4 days up in a row — NOT the same thing
- "Your wife tells you to buy a camel, you go to the market and bring home a kangaroo"
- **They are completely different setups** — don't confuse them

### 2 Lynch Criteria for Continuation Setups (Repeated)

- **T**: Two days not up in a row (no range expansion in last 2 days)
- **w**: not up more than 2.5% each day (narrow/negative pre-breakout)
- **o**: linearity of first leg (orderly, buying came in)
- **L**: first leg 15%+ (or $10+ for high-priced stocks) — if first leg is 5%, second leg will be even less
- **n**: narrow/negative pre-breakout day
- **c**: consolidation quality (orderly, low volume, low volatility)
- **h**: closing near day's high

### Short Side Framework (Repeated)

1. **Weak structure short (Niagara fall)**: 3 days continuous selling down 10%, weak bounce <3 days <2.5% each day — then short
2. **Momentum burst short**: only when market monitor primary is bearish
3. **Strong structure short (parabolic short)**: stocks that went 200-300% crazy — "hero or zero" — Pradeep doesn't trade these
