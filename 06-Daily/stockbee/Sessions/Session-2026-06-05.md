# Choppy Transition — Second-Day Breakouts Failing, Take an Early Weekend

> After one of the strongest trending periods in market history, the market is transitioning into chop — second-day breakouts are failing, and the skill that matters now is knowing when **not** to trade.

---

## AM Session

**Market Context: Abnormal Move, Now Transitioning to Chop**

The market just came off one of the strongest trending periods Pradeep has ever seen — a 10% move followed by 15% in two months, something he compares to **1929** in terms of rarity. Coming out of that kind of run, the transition to chop is expected but dangerous.

> "This is an abnormal move. How many times you see that kind of a thing where there is a continuous 10%? 1929 is when it happened."

**Second-day breakouts are failing** — this started 3–4 days ago and signals the shift from trending to chop. Yesterday's gap down made people briefly bullish; today's gap down confirmed the chop pattern.

**Viable Setups in This Environment:**

| Setup | Status | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| **SIPs Reversal** | Very few selective | Scarcity itself is a signal |
| **EP 9M** | Focus area | Primary setup in chop |
| **DEP** | Focus area | Needs time to develop — thrives in choppy periods |
| **Shorts / WSS** | Not yet | No big volume breakdowns on leaders yet |
| Regular breakouts | Avoid | "They will look safe as when a breakout happens in a good market environment" — but they fail |

> "Breakouts are going to happen. And they will look safe as when a breakout happens in a good market environment."

**Leaders transitioning to sideways/chop:** The big-cap stocks that made the big move (up 200–300%) will transition into sideways consolidation. Some may take a month or more.

**Watchlist:**
- [[DELL]] — Dell (had breakout yesterday, likely to pull back)
- [[QCOM]] — Qualcomm
- [[APH]] — Amphenol
- [[GLW]] — Corning (had catalyst, but pulled back)
- [[SNOW]] — Snowflake
- [[MCHP]] — Microchip
- [[OKTA]] — Okta
- [[ASTC]] — Astrotech

**The Retail Trader Advantage**

> "That's the great advantage of being a retail trader. You can quickly get back in if things start working. And if things are not working, you can quickly get out."

**Verdict:** Time to take an early weekend. Don't hurry into anything until the market resolves.

## PM Session

**SNDK and the Moving Average Trap**

[[SNDK]] stops are at the 10-day and 20-day moving average. "Lots of money on a high-price stock" — when it comes back to those levels, many will be trapped.

> "People stops are where ten day moving average, twenty day moving average. So if it comes back to that, you're still looking at lot of money on a high price stock."

**TSLA Warning:** Tesla is retail traders' favorite stock — they "come out of the woodwork" immediately. Be careful.

**Pullback Expectations:** [[QCOM]], [[LRCX]] (Lam Research), and other leaders are expected to pull back **15–20% in 3–5 days**.

**IWM Rotation Is a Mirage**

> "IWM rotation into small cap. There's always a mirage."

**Short Candidates:**

| Instrument | Notes |
|------------|-------|
| [[SOXL]] (short) | Second day of swing; but better to short SNDQ first day of swing |
| [[SNDQ]] (short) | First day of swing — preferred short over SOXL |
| [[AMD]] (short) | Almost first day of swing |
| Inverse ETFs | [[TSDD]] (inverse TSLA), [[SQQ]] mentioned for directional shorts |

**EP 9M Breakdown:** An EP 9M breakdown is setting up (4th attempt) — but the stock is already down 22%, so stop placement is difficult.

> "Size is always a function of your own personal risk tolerance."

**Reversal Bullish: Almost Nothing**

Only [[BAP]] qualifies as a reversal bullish candidate. The scarcity of setups is itself informative.

> "There are not many setups — that itself tells you something."

**The "Perfect Indicator" — When Everyone Quits**

> "30 emails in the last one week telling me that they don't want to do any more swing trading. There is no real money in swing trading. Perfect indicator."

Everyone has stops at the 20-day moving average. The pattern: stocks pull back to 20-day MA → people don't sell → it undercuts → then they sell.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Market is in choppy transition** after an historically abnormal trending period (10% + 15% in 2 months, comparable to 1929)
2. **Second-day breakouts are failing** — this started 3–4 days ago and is the clearest sign of regime change
3. **EP 9M and DEP are the focus** — regular breakouts have the same probability as "me getting a six pack in the next six months"
4. **Shorts aren't ready yet** — leaders haven't broken down on volume; they're transitioning to sideways consolidation
5. **Retail traders can move fast** — get in when things work, get out when they don't
6. **When everyone quits swing trading, pay attention** — 30 emails from people giving up is a "perfect indicator"
7. **20-day moving average is the trap** — stocks pull back → don't sell → undercut → then sell
8. **Take an early weekend** — don't hurry into anything until the market resolves
