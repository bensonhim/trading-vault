# Process Problem — 4% Escape Velocity, McDonald's Trading, and Choppy Reality

Friday was a **process orientation day**. Pradeep hammered one message: trading profitability is a process problem, not a setup problem. The setups are known — it's the process that separates profitable traders from losing ones. Meanwhile, the market confirmed what Thursday suggested: slow breadth deterioration masked by mega-cap strength, with 3 positive days vs 7 negative in the last 10. BA short closed for free money. Zoom exited 100% at gap up. 27 bullish vs 237 bearish. Be selective or get chopped.

### 4% Breakout Deep Dive: Escape Velocity

This was the most detailed teaching on 4% breakout so far:

**Why 4%?**
- Studied every stock that made 25%+ move in a month — the only common factor was a 4% breakout day
- 90%+ of 100%+ moves in a year start from lows, not highs
- "There has to be a escape velocity. If it's not there, it doesn't go up for next 3-4 days."

**The "poor person" objection debunked:**
- "By the time it shows 4%, it's already up a lot" — Yes, but it still goes up after that
- Stocks that don't break out 4% never make the move — entering early at 1% doesn't help because those stocks don't go anywhere
- 4% IS the signal — below that threshold, no escape velocity

**ADR vs 4%:**
- Different stocks have different ADRs, so ADR-based entries seem logical
- But 4% works simply — "if I can do this simply with just 4%, why make your life more complicated?"

**Structure of the 20% move:**
- Starts from a consolidation or bottom
- 4% breakout is the ignition
- If it has to work, it works — stop never comes into play
- If it doesn't work, even a 3-day low stop gets hit

> "If you just focus on 4% breakout and do a damn good job of managing the risk — put a very close stop — you will make money. The things which work, the stop never comes into play."

### Two Ways to Move Your Account

| Method | Account Impact | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Liquid lava / high-priced** | 0.5–2% per trade | Booking breakout, any high-priced liquid stock |
| **Catalyst trade** | 5–50%+ per trade | BA short, Mobileye short, genuine EP with news |

Both are valid. The key is knowing which game you're playing.

## 11 AM Meeting

### Process Orientation: The McDonald's Metaphor

This was the centerpiece teaching. Pradeep used three business analogies:

**McDonald's** — Not the quality of food, but obsessive process orientation. Every burger, every order, every franchise = same process.

**Dunkin' Donut** — Minimum wage worker follows a process. Coffee ordered → pull from machine. Breakfast sandwich → microwave for X seconds. All programmed. No thinking required.

**7-Eleven** — A woman running a 7-Eleven was making $20K/month. Not brains — process. Everything specified by corporate.

> "Profit is an outcome of following a good process. You don't need brains to trade momentum burst. You don't need brains to trade weak structure short. The more brains you use, the worse your results."

### The Toyota Quote (Key Takeaway)

> "Trading profitability is a process problem. Toyota did not invent the car. It invented a process to make quality cars and beat the pants of others. What works in the market — momentum bursts, episodic pivots, stocks in play, breakouts, pullbacks, volatility contraction, small-cap shorts — is well known. You don't need to invent the car in trading. What you need is a process like Toyota."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Trading is a process problem** — Setups are generic (breakout, pullback, 9M). What makes money is process discipline, like McDonald's, Dunkin' Donut, 7-Eleven
2. **4% breakout = escape velocity** — It's the signal. Below 4%, stocks lack the force to sustain a move. Study stocks that made 20%+ in a month — they ALL started with 4% breakout
3. **Simple process = executable process** — Remove redundant steps. The more you complicate, the worse you trade
4. **BA short = template complete** — Entered delayed reaction, breakeven stop, exited at gap down for free money. Zero risk, pure process
5. **Zoom = 100% exit at gap up** — When something gaps up 12%, sell. Don't hold and hope
6. **Breadth deterioration masked by mega caps** — 3 up days, 7 down days. Small caps being sold systematically. Indexes look fine — don't be fooled
7. **27 bullish vs 237 bearish** — The scan numbers tell you everything about the environment
8. **Consolidation breakouts = high failure rate** — They look sexy but fail most often, especially without volume confirmation
9. **Don't add complexity to simple ideas** — Sugar babies, 4% breakout — they work because they're simple. Adding filters, ADR, extra conditions = shooting yourself
10. **Front-load your process** — By 10 AM, most of the work is done. Enter, set stops, manage. The rest is just maintaining
11. **Mobileye losing confidence** — When a short doesn't play out despite catalyst, reassess. Don't fight the tape
12. **Process orientation applies to everything** — Dunkin' Donut worker makes coffee by process. 7-Eleven owner makes $20K/month by process. Trading profitability = same thing
13. **"People who argue against 4% breakout have closed minds"** — The data is clear. The only question is whether you're willing to study it objectively
14. **Systematic selling visible in market monitor** — Someone is selling every day. Afternoon buy programs create whipsaws. Be selective or get chopped
15. **Only 18 people doing the work** — Deep Dive submissions reveal who's process-oriented and who's not
