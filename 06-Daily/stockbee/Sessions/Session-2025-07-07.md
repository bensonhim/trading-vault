# Junk Flies, Leaders Stall — Tariff Tantrums, NVX Con Stories, and Setup Identification 101

Pradeep returned from a July 4th holiday where he nearly ended up in hospital from a spider bite, on doxycycline and cold compresses. The market opened with a familiar pattern: junk stocks flying while leaders went nowhere. NVX (QuantumScape's solid-state battery competitor) was the poster child — same repeated story, same CEO insider selling, same 100%+ move on zero revenue. Pradeep delivered a setup identification masterclass, rejecting multiple member picks because they were day-three or day-four setups, not start-of-swing. The PM session was dominated by tariff theater — Kazakhstan, Laos, Myanmar tariffs announced mid-day — and Pradeep concluded there was almost nothing worth buying. The best candidates were Rocket Lab and Circle on the long side, with a possible short-side delayed-reaction setup forming.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautiously Bullish, Extended Market

Judy's essay: cautiously bullish, fourth consecutive 400+ green reading, T2108 up to 69.28, 5:1 bullish-to-bearish ratio. But Pradeep was cautious:

> "We rallied into earnings. NVX rallied into earnings. It's the junkiest stock in the market. The same story which the stock sells has been selling and multiple times it makes this 100% to 100% rally based on the same shitty story."

### Junk Flies While Leaders Stagnate

The defining market characteristic:

> "Select breakouts are working. Junkier the stock, better it is at current market conditions. We are back to the same thing as COVID time — everything which is junk is flying. While most of the bigger stocks are going nowhere."

### Setup Identification — The #1 Problem

Pradeep rejected multiple member picks because they were not start-of-swing:

> "Is this a start of a swing today or was it a start of yesterday? It's not even good from yesterday... The number one problem in trading is setup identification."

> "You want to date a woman — first question: is she available? If she's married, there is no setup there."

### NVX, QuantumScape, and the Dog-Shit Pattern Repeats

All the 100%+ movers shared the same DNA:

> "All of these companies have one thing in common. They have no earnings, no sales, no probability of ever making sales or earnings."

Pradeep connected it to the Theranos analogy:

> "Remember that girl who went to jail? She had that technology that one drop of blood can do everything? How much money did she raise? A billion."

### Health Care Bill Impact

> "The biggest sector which is going to be affected by the big bill is health care. Hospitals and insurance companies."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Tariff Theater — Kazakhstan, Laos, Myanmar

> "25% tariff on Kazakhstan. 25% on Malaysia. I don't even know what we import from there. Two comedians from South Africa — Elon Musk and Trevor Noah."

> "This is all drama. This will all be taken back. By 9:30 I'm telling you, the tariff will be suspended. If the market sells off 5% in the next two hours, the tariff will be pulled by the end of the day."

### Nothing Worth Buying — Extremely Selective

After scanning EP 9M, combination bullish (74 stocks) vs combination bearish (679 stocks), and multiple other scans:

> "I really don't see much to get really, really, really excited about."

### Breakouts from Yesterday — No Follow-Through

> "CRCL gapped down. Lulu gapped down. Apple — small follow-through. Kroger — gapped down. Rocket Lab — hardly making any move. What does this market tell you? There is no real appetite for risk."

### Setup Identification: Bull Flag ≠ Broken Stock

A member asked about Starbucks as a cup-with-handle. Pradeep was blunt:

> "The first rule of cup with handle is it forms within 15% of a 52-week high. How can Starbucks have a cup with handle when it is down very low? Cup with handle is a continuation pattern, not a broken stock."

On Oklo as a "bull flag":

> "You are buying it after the bull flag is breaking. You are late to the party."

### Continuation Setups Failing

> "Very few continuation setups are working. Start-of-swing and delayed-reaction setups are working better than continuation."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Setup identification is the #1 problem** — if you can't identify start-of-swing vs. day-three, you will always be late
2. **Junk flies in extended markets** — COVID-style pattern where garbage stocks double while leaders stagnate
3. **NVX / QuantumScape are con stories** — same repeated narrative, same insider selling, same outcome
4. **Tariff announcements are theater** — nobody takes them seriously; intraday volatility is the real risk
5. **Short-side setups exist but don't short early** — "starting early, you're going to burn your hands"
6. **Continuation setups are failing** — start-of-swing and delayed-reaction are the working setups
7. **74 bullish vs. 679 bearish** — the combo scan told the whole story: money flowing out, not in
