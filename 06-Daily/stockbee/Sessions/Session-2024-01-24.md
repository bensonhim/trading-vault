# Netflix Day — One Trade, Big Move, and the Victoria's Secret Model Test

Wednesday was Netflix day. One clear catalyst, one clear trade. Pradeep bought at $538-541 with a $2 stop, sold 80% into strength at ~$560, and moved his account 5-7% in under an hour. Meanwhile, people were buying MARA, Kura Oncology, and EDU — the Victoria's Secret model test in action: asked to find one, they brought back garbage and then asked what was wrong with their pick. The day reinforced three themes: prioritize ruthlessly, size up on high-confidence SIPs, and treat trades like Tinder dates — when they don't work, move on.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### The Only Game in Town: Netflix

> "I'm bullish. The buying is coming to the market."

- **Bullish SA continues** — market breadth improving after bounce
- **One clear catalyst stock today**: Netflix — massive earnings beat, 20 analyst upgrades
- **SIPs quality crisis continues** — people posting ENS (press release company), UAL (bearish catalyst), Celsius (late to party), Netflix/Meta (no real SIPs catalyst)
- **Netflix: the game** — "I was sitting there like a hungry lion, and the moment I saw my prey, I attacked"

### Netflix Tactics: Position Sizing for Working People

> "If you're a working person, put 2-5% in Netflix with a 20% stop. Worst case = 1% loss."

- **Don't use OPG on $540 stock** — $2 stop on Netflix is noise, 100% guaranteed stop-out
- **Need $10-20 stop** for Netflix-style high-priced stocks
- **Not an EP** — Netflix is a good SIPs, but not a groundbreaking EP like SMCI. Maybe 20% upside max, already gapped 10%
- **Working people**: small position, wider stop, longer hold. Active traders: oversized, sell into strength, done in 30 minutes
- **SMCI**: still holding swing position, "qualitatively A-grade EP"

### EDU: The Suckers' SIPs

> "It's a Chinese stock, it's rallied 194%, it's the 4th consecutive earnings beat. Who's left to buy it except you?"

Why EDU was a bad SIPs:
1. Chinese stock (disaster after disaster)
2. Already rallied 194% into earnings
3. 4th consecutive earnings beat (not neglected)
4. Suckers' game — market isn't waiting for you to buy

### China Stocks: Let the First Leg Go

> "Too many times, China has disappointed. Wait for second/third leg."

- BABA volume not impressive — don't buy on gap
- Let first move go, buy the second or third leg

### Fund Ownership = Neglect = Bigger Moves

- **DRCT**: single-digit fund ownership = massively neglected = huge move
- **SMCI**: 1000+ funds = widely owned, still worked because of EP quality
- **Netflix**: widely owned, not neglected = smaller potential move

### 4% Breakout Thought Experiment (continued from yesterday)

- Range expansion always happens at the start of a swing
- Buy every 4% breakout at the start of a swing in biotech/tech = works

### Delayed Reaction: Becoming #1 Setup

> "Delayed reaction is becoming my favorite setup for day traders."
