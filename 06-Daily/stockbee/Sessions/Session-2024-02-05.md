# Buy Setups, Not Stocks — Discrete Information Is Not a Trading Method

Monday after Meta earnings — but the Meta party is over. Breakouts failing, breadth negative, choppy market continuing. Pradeep delivers the most fundamental teaching of the week: **a setup is a complete end-to-end trading method. Discrete information (institutional ownership, float, chart patterns) is NOT a setup — it's like knowing "avocado is good for you" without having a recipe.** SIPs, EP, 9M, momentum burst — these are setups. SA + Setup = 100% of the equation. Miss either piece and you lose money.

## 11 AM Meeting — Setup vs Discrete Information (Core Teaching)

### ⚡ Situational Awareness Is THE Single Most Important Skill

> "If situational awareness doesn't penetrate your mind, this game is not for you."

- Overwhelming evidence: breakouts not working, breadth negative
- SA = the filter AFTER you find a setup
- "Just because something shows up in a scan doesn't mean anything" — scan is like sitting in front of a casino machine
- Every day: run process → find setup → apply SA filter → only trade if setup matches SA
- "The need to put a trade is greater than the need to understand SA" — this is the problem

### The Setup Framework: Complete End-to-End Trading Method

| Setup | What It Does | Entry | Exit | Duration |
|-------|-------------|-------|------|----------|
| **SIPs** | Catalyst → big move for one day | OPG or first 1-5 min | Sell into strength, peel 20% at a time | Intraday to 1 day |
| **EP** | Neglect + catalyst → multi-week move | 80/20 entry on breakout | Sell 50%, trail rest | Weeks to months |
| **9M** | Low-float + catalyst → explosive move | Breakout entry | Sell into strength | 1-3 days |
| **Momentum Burst** | 3-day phenomena | Entry on day 1 | Exit day 3 by definition | 3 days |
| **Delayed Reaction** | Post-catalyst follow-through | Entry after initial reaction | Sell into next move | 1-5 days |

### Discrete Information Is NOT a Setup

> "Institutional ownership is not a setup. Float is not a setup. Chart patterns are not a setup. They're like saying 'avocado is good for you' — that's not a recipe."

- **Discrete information**: institutional ownership %, float numbers, chart patterns, indicators — all useless without a setup context
- **Setup** = complete formula: scan criteria → identification → entry method → stop level → exit strategy → position sizing
- People yawn during setup discussions but go crazy for random charts and spreadsheets — backwards
- Reading a technical analysis book gives you chart patterns, NOT setups
- A double bottom IS NOT a setup. A setup would be: "This is a double bottom. Here's how you scan for it. Here's where you enter. Here's where you exit. Here's how you sell."

### Institutional Ownership: Number of Funds Matters, Not Percentage

- Microsoft has 98% institutional ownership — but 10,000+ funds own it
- If catalyst hits (EU resolution, CEO departure), price moves regardless of ownership %
- For SIPs: catalyst is all that matters — the stock doesn't care who owns it for that one day
- For EP: number of funds matters more than percentage — more funds = more potential buyers discovering the stock

### SMCI Extension Question — Setup Thinking

- Member asks if SMCI is "overextended" — wrong question
- For day trade: doesn't matter if extended, what's the SETUP?
- Buy the setup (5-min breakout), not the stock
- For swing/position trade: yes, 90%+ in 14 days is extended

### SA + Setup = 100%

> "Setup is 50%. SA is 50%. Both must be in place or it doesn't work."

- Without SA: you trade setups that don't match market conditions → lose money
- Without setup: you trade on discrete information (charts, indicators, float) → lose money
- COVID-era champions who made big money then lost 70%+ — no SA concept

### Process Over Money

- "Do you brush your teeth every day? Same thing — run the process every day"
- "Find a setup. Master it. Trade with 5 shares. Then 50. Then 500. Then 5000."
- Start with momentum burst or 9M — these finance your EP trades
