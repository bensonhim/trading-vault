# Range-Bound Chop — EP 9M Qualification, SOS Context, and the Discipline of Sitting Out

Pradeep opened Monday with a clear situational-awareness call: the market is range-bound and choppy, most breakouts are fading, and the only actionable path is extreme selectivity — focusing on EP 9M with genuine catalysts, continuation setups, and sugar babies, while avoiding the trap of trading every scan hit. By the PM session, nearly everything had faded — [[SNOW]] reversed, [[OKTA]] faded, reversal bullish setups failed — confirming the morning call. The session became a masterclass in why SOS is not a setup by itself, why context and qualifiers matter more than pattern recognition, and why the best trade in a choppy market is often no trade.

### Situational Awareness: Range-Bound, Choppy, Selective

> "I see this as a range bound market. Most breakouts happen, they fade. Breakout suddenly, a breakout spread, or they don't have follow through."

The market is not trending — it's range-bound. Breakouts are failing, follow-through is absent. This means:

- **Primary focus**: EP 9M, DEP, sugar babies, momentum burst
- **Secondary focus**: Reversal (selective), selective anticipation, some shorts
- **Not looking at**: Early entries (FOMO) — early moves are fading
- **Key warning**: Thursday's high volume was distorted by index rebalancing / triple witching — many EP 9M readings from that day are unreliable

> "You have to be highly selective. You select or start with a clear catalyst and a reason to go up or down."

### EP 9M Explained: Two Types of Opportunities

Pradeep clarified a persistent misunderstanding about EP 9M:

| Type | Description | How to Find |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Gap-up EP** | Stock already gapping up on news/catalyst | Found via premarket scans (NTRT/MTRT) |
| **Non-gap EP** | Stock shows 9M+ volume *without* a gap — the volume *is* the signal | Found via EP 9M scan, then research *why* |

> "The idea is not to get in early. Idea is to find something — why is this stock suddenly showing EP 9M? Then you'll investigate, and then you find."

The advantage of non-gap EP: lower risk than gap plays, expand your playbook, and the market is telling you something the premarket missed.

> "It is good that people have so much problem understanding EP 9M because then they will not use it."

### SOS Is Not a Setup — It's a Concept That Needs Qualification

A critical teaching moment on [[SOS]] (Start of Swing):

> "SOS by itself is not an entry signal. SOS in a context — like this is a good SOS. Why is this a very good SOS? Because it is a continuation setup."

| SOS Type | Qualification | Trade? |
|----------|--------------|--------|
| **Continuation SOS** | After a first leg, tight consolidation, breaking out | ✅ Best for beginners |
| **Consolidation SOS** | After an extended move, consolidation with volume | ✅ With catalyst |
| **High-price SOS** (FHP) | $60+ stock showing SOS from any level | ✅ Can work |
| **Random SOS** | In a downtrend, no catalyst, just volume | ❌ Avoid |
| **Weak-structure SOS** | Three negative days before the SOS bar | ❌ Not even a real SOS |

> "Always think in setups. Setups are a set of specific conditions a stock needs to meet. SOS needs to meet specific conditions to be tradable. Otherwise, no."

### Sector Rotation: You're Late by Three Hours

On using sector/industry screens for stock selection:

> "By the time a sector is in the top sector here, most of them would have already made their move. It means they're in the fourth or fifth leg of the move."

Sectors appear in screens *after* the leaders have already moved. Use NTRT/MTRT for premarket discovery, not lagging sector screens.

Pradeep introduced the trade factory metaphor:

| Conveyor Belt | Target | Setups | Basis |
|---------------|--------|--------|-------|
| **Home runs** | 20%+ account move in one trade | EP, EP 9M, DEP, Sugar Babies | Catalyst |
| **Singles** | 0.5%–5% account move | Momentum burst (3–5 day, 8–20%) | Pattern + structure |

> "Granular level understanding is what you need. What is momentum burst? What is the length of momentum burst? If you don't understand momentum burst, it doesn't make sense."

### Catalyst Research: The 10-Minute Filter

On researching EP 9M stocks:

> "It doesn't take more than ten minutes to do. Most of them, there is nothing to research here."

The workflow:
1. Qualify with SOS first — only 3–4 stocks have valid SOS on any given day
2. Check market search / briefings for catalyst
3. For Chinese stocks, use Grok for additional research
4. Ask: "Based on this catalyst, how far is this stock going to go up?" — if you can't answer, skip it

> "Just because names are showing up in your scan, it is like — listen to your wife. And just because your neighbor is calling you, don't go. You're going to be in big trouble."

### Exhaustion Setup Explained

### Context and Qualifier Framework

Pradeep laid out the decision framework explicitly:

1. **Situational awareness** — is the market supportive?
2. **Qualifier** — does the setup meet specific conditions?
3. **Context** — is there a real catalyst with magnitude and duration?
4. **Prioritize** — rank qualified setups, then decide

> "Qualify. Qualify. Prioritize. Prioritize. Once you qualify, prioritize."

> "The best option is to not trade."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Situational awareness overrides everything** — In a choppy, range-bound market, the best trade is often no trade. Most breakouts are fading. Neither longs nor shorts are following through.
2. **SOS is not a setup** — SOS is a concept; continuation SOS, consolidation SOS, and FHP (high-price) SOS are the actual tradable setups. Random SOS in a downtrend is a trap.
3. **EP 9M has two types** — Gap-up EP (found via NTRT/MTRT) and non-gap EP (the volume itself is the signal, then research why). The non-gap type expands your playbook with lower risk.
4. **Catalyst is king in choppy markets** — In a trending market, the quality of the setup alone can carry you. In a choppy market, you need a genuine catalyst to justify risk. PJN was the only stock with a clear reason to go up.
5. **Thursday's volume was distorted** — Index rebalancing and triple witching inflated volume readings. Many breakouts from Thursday's close were last-minute artefacts.
6. **Context and qualifier, not pattern recognition** — Every setup must be qualified (does it meet conditions?) and contextualized (is the market supportive? is there a catalyst?). Then prioritize.
7. **Story stocks are traps** — [[QS]] (QuantumScape), BLDP, REF — companies telling the same story for years while diluting shares. Research the background before buying.
8. **Don't buy on the third day of a swing** — If you understand momentum burst (3–5 day duration), you'll never buy on day three or day seven. Start of swing is the entry.
9. **The best trade on June 22 was sitting out** — Pradeep's only conviction: [[PJN]] with a clear catalyst. Everything else: faded, chopped, or lacked reason to go up.
10. **Granular understanding is non-negotiable** — You cannot skip momentum burst, SOS classification, and setup qualification. No amount of GPA or intelligence substitutes for putting in the work.
