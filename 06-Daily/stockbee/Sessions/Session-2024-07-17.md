> "Most of these stocks, vast majority, are coming out from some correction or from 52-week low or within 20% of 52-week low. Stocks near highs are sitting there — they're not participating."

Key lesson: the conventional wisdom "stocks that hold up best break out" is **wrong** for short-term trading. First legs are forming below 50-day MA. Question assumptions.

### Sugar Babies Over Nice Breakouts

> "Would you prioritize Sugar Baby range expansion over a nice two-leg breakout? Yes. Sugar Babies have proven tendency to make big moves. When they call, you don't ever say no."

SOUN made 20%+ in one–two days. Both sides — long AND short — sugar babies work.

### Wider Stops for 9M/EP, Tight Stops for Momentum Burst

> "When trading 9M EP or Sugar Babies or classical EP, you need wider stops because they are more volatile. Momentum burst — very close stop. If it's going to work, it's going to work."

Singles allow you to finance the wider stops on home-run attempts.

### Thinking 30 Days Ahead

> "Thinking 30 days ahead, we are likely to have this rally last for 6–10 weeks. Even if you didn't manage to make money in the last week, this is just a start."

Look for continuation setups like a hawk — that is the low-hanging fruit.

> "The one single most important thing — buy at the start of the swing. If you can just understand one thing about swing trading: buy at the start of the swing."

Someone asked about MARA (4 days up, 30% already): "After four days the stock is up 30%, and that person might be buying. Beginning of the swing — that is it."

### The Real Catalyst: 39%+ Sales Growth

Extended lesson on catalysts:

> "The only catalyst that matters is 39%+ sales growth back-to-back quarters. NVDA, SMCI, ZS, TMDX — every big move is driven by underlying growth."

Stocks making 52-week highs are NOT a catalyst — they're a **negative catalyst** (everyone waiting to buy at 52-week high gets trapped).

### Singles Finance Home Runs: The Trade Factory Explained

> "I'm willing to take 4% risk on SOXL because I have this trade factory which continuously produces 1–5% trades. I can do this 1,000 times a year."

The full explanation:

1. **Singles** = momentum burst with tight stops (1–2.5% risk), 3–5 day hold
2. **Home runs** = 9M/EP with wider stops (8–10% risk), occasional, life-changing potential
3. **Singles finance home runs** — the 1–5% gains from singles allow you to take the 4–8% risk on home-run attempts
4. **If a home run doesn't work** — scratched or small loss, recovered by 20 singles
5. **If it works** — can make your year

> "They think it's below their dignity to hit singles. Nobody becomes rich by hitting singles. That's the internet message. And it's wrong."

### Don't Short in a Bull Market

> "I just see an opportunity to make 30% on the long side. Why should I spend time finding shorts that might make 8–10%?"

> "Every single email I got this morning says IWM is going to pull back. When everyone waits for pullback, the pullback doesn't come. Overbought markets at the start of a bull move are the best."
