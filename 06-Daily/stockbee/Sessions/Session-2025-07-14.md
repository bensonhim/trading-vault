# Big Winners Top Out, Breakdowns Beat Breakouts — Define Your Problem, Focus on Catalysts, One-Day Wonders Everywhere

A critical day for market observation: big winners (BMNR, SZL, DLTR, TSSI) started topping out after multi-leg runs, and 9M breakdowns were having better follow-through than breakouts. Pradeep's key message: "If you cannot define your trading problem, you don't have a problem" — and the #1 problem is lack of setup. The PM session was stark: "I just don't see anything which stands out." MP was the delayed reaction that worked (third time the charm), and Unity Software was the best continuation setup. The day ended with a powerful teaching on why catalyst matters more than chart pattern — "pretty looking chart + no catalyst = wallet picked."

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Big Winners Topping Out

Pradeep himself presented (Judy absent, Shankar disappeared):

> "Number of big winners showing signs of topping out, pullback in last one week. BMNR, BSDM, MFI, MEI, SZL, Dave, TSSI — they had made 50% plus moves, some 100% plus, and they're all showing signs of topping out."

### 9M Breakdowns Now Working Better Than Breakouts

> "If you see, most of the 9M breakdowns are having a better follow-through in the last two or three days. Most of the breakouts are not really having follow-through."

This was a key tactical shift: the market was signaling that short-side setups were starting to outperform long-side breakouts.

### Rotation Theory — Probably Not Valid

> "The rotation theory sounds good every time the market is about to pull back. But typically, I don't see that as a very valid point at this stage."

> "If Google and Amazon have not participated in this rally to the extent other stocks have, is there a reason? Why would they start rallying now, after everything else has gone up?"

### One-Day Wonders and the Need to Evolve

> "UMAK — everybody was asking, 'Is UMAK an EP?' It's down 18% today. One-day wonders. Even the story stocks are having difficulty getting traction."

> "If we just sit and keep doing the same thing, we'll be dead ducks. The market is going to change. We have to evolve."

### The Pretty Chart Trap — No Catalyst = No Trade

On a member following VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern) with pretty charts but no catalyst:

> "Your VCP pattern is right. But why is it failing? Because there is no reason why this stock should go up. It's not making Bitcoins. It's not doing AI. It's not doing drones. You buy a breakout like this — is it a good VCP pattern? Yes. But there is no reason why the stock should go up."

> "In this market condition, almost everything which is working has some sort of a catalyst. Buy stocks with a reason to go up."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Nothing Compelling — Extreme Selectivity Required

> "I went through this with a fine-tooth comb before this meeting. There is just not much there."

> "I don't see anything which stands out."

> "Sometimes it's better to just be very slow, deliberate, and not jump on anything easily."

### Define Your Trading Problem

> "Ability to define your trading problem — ability to solve your trading problem. Most important. If you cannot solve your trading problem, you can keep on reading books."

> "Most of the problems are — no setup. If there is no setup, everything else after that doesn't make sense."

### Catalyst > Chart Pattern

> "Buy stocks which have a reason to go up. When you see this list, ask yourself: is there a reason why this stock should go up? Rocket Lab? Yes — drones. NBIS? Yes — AI catalyst. Aurora Innovation — is there a catalyst? Otherwise, why should it go up?"

> "Just buying a pretty looking chart — what is going to happen is you're going to get your wallet picked."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Big winners topping out** — BMNR, SZL, TSSI, Dave all showing distribution after multi-leg runs
2. **Breakdowns now outperforming breakouts** — tactical shift; 9M breakdowns having better follow-through
3. **Catalyst > Chart pattern** — "pretty chart + no catalyst = wallet picked"
4. **Define your trading problem** — if you can't state it clearly, you don't have a real problem
5. **One-day wonders everywhere** — UMAK down 18% the day after excitement; most breakouts not following through
6. **Rotation theory is weak** — lagging stocks haven't participated for a reason
7. **Extreme selectivity required** — PM session found almost nothing actionable
8. **Delayed reaction is the go-to strategy** — at least there's a catalyst to justify entry
9. **FEE is still dead** — morning fade pattern continues; later entry is better
