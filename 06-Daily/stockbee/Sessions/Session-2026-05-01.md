# Earnings Season Setups: Sloppy Works When Fuel Is Present

> Sloppy setups are working because people are chasing moves — there is desperation, and if you have missed out, you want to be in it.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Market Monitor: Cautiously Bullish

The market opened with strong breadth — the Market Monitor had a **500+ green day** with good breadth expansion across primary and secondary indicators, signaling a **green light environment**. The **20% Study** leaned bullish at a **2:1 ratio** of bullish to bearish stocks. All major indices rallied to new record highs on broad-based earnings strength.

> "My essay this morning is cautiously bullish as the market continues to show strong resilience in response to corporate earnings."

**Small cap rotation signal**: [[IWM]] and [[TNA]] both rallied, outperforming other indices. Pradeep noted that during periods where money rotates into small caps, [[SPY]] tends to do nothing — a possible "second inning" of the rally where story stocks may come into play.

### Earnings Season: The Fuel Thesis

The dominant theme was blowout earnings and what they mean for tradeable setups:

- **Goldman Sachs reported only 3% of S&P stocks missed earnings** — an extraordinary breadth of corporate profitability
- Companies are cutting employees and reducing headcount while maintaining margins — "they don't give a fuck about you"
- This creates the "fuel in the tank" for sustained moves

> "For stocks to make those kind of explosive, sustainable moves, there has to be some sort of a fuel or a catalyst. [...] That's why they are going up. And there is a reason why they are going up because they have the fuel."

**Key distinction**: Not all earnings-gapped stocks are created equal. Most gap up on earnings day then fade. The real opportunity comes from:

1. **Stocks with genuine catalyst** (sales growth 39%+ this quarter and projected next quarters)
2. **First or second earnings acceleration** — coming out of neglect (Bucket Zero, Bucket One)
3. **Institutional quality** stocks that the market has just discovered

> "Make a list of stocks that gapped up on earnings, research them, find sustainable catalyst, put in a watchlist. They will make 20–50% after digesting the earnings and once early buyers are shaken out."

### Setup Focus: EP 9M and DEP

Pradeep's primary focus for the day:

| Priority | Setup | Rationale |
|----------|-------|-----------|
| **Primary** | EP 9M | Home-run setups with genuine earnings catalyst |
| **Primary** | DEP (Delayed Reaction) | Stocks that reacted positively to earnings, pulled back, now setting up |
| **Secondary** | Continuation | In-trend plays |
| **Secondary** | Reversals | Limited; market is bullish |

> "Focus on EP 9M and DEP. That's the focus. Secondary focus is continuation and then reversals."

### The "Sloppy Setups Are Working" Dynamic

A critical observation: in this market, setups that would normally be rejected are working because of **FOMO and desperation**:

- The normal 5–10 day pause before a delayed reaction entry has compressed to almost nothing
- Stocks are not pulling back — they just keep going
- NBIS was highlighted as a prime example: "sloppy setup" but working because the **AI sector is hot** and it has the "fuel" of blowout earnings

> "Things which normally don't work are working because of the amount of desperation. People are chasing moves. There is desperation — if you have missed out, you want to be in it."

### Oracle ([[ORCL]]): The Setup That Got Away

Oracle was Pradeep's primary watchlist candidate for a **delayed reaction EP**:

- It had a catalyst (earnings), made a 26% move, then had an **orderly pullback**
- **But it gapped up** at the open — and Pradeep doesn't chase gaps
- If it had started from the pullback level without the gap, he would have taken it

> "This is a delayed reaction EP. So it's going to work if it is going to work. The market is bullish. We are in a bull market."

**Warning**: Friday after close is when Trump typically starts geopolitical trouble — so position management matters heading into the weekend.

### Position Management: Stops and Sizing for EP 9M

A detailed explanation of how to manage EP 9M trades:

- **For high-priced stocks**: $5–10 stop; for low-priced stocks: 8–20%
- **Hold period**: 3–5 days targeting 30–40%+
- **Position sizing is the key**: If your stop is 10%, take only 5% of your account — that way a 40% gain moves your account 2%

> "You're looking for a home run or mini home run when you are doing EP 9M. That is the only reason why you enter."

**The Victoria's Secret Model analogy** — on getting 2.5% stops:

> "If you want to date a Victoria's Secret model, there are two ways. One is to go build a body and have a genetic advantage. Or make a shit ton of money — probably need $200M plus. Otherwise, can you play the game which is in your league?"

If you want 2.5% stops, you need to **engineer your entire approach for it** — trade SIPs intraday, or get early entries on DEPs. You can't force 2.5% stops on EP 9M trades.

### Mind Clarity: The Most Important Lesson

> "If you don't have clarity, you're just an ape. You're doing things which you don't right."

The most common problem: people don't have **crystal clear mind clarity** about what setup they're trading. If you're trading EP 9M, you need to know:
- This is a home run setup
- Hold 3–5 days
- Expect 30–50% moves
- Use wide stops (8–10%) and small position size

If you're trading SIPs intraday, you take profit — you don't "give it room" like a swing trade.

> "Clarify, re-clarify, and re-clarify your thinking. Once you have clear thinking, then you might decide: this game is not for me. Or I'm going to focus on SIPs."

### SIPs vs. EP 9M: Different Games

Pradeep emphasized these are **completely different trading styles** requiring different mindsets:

- **SIPs** (Stocks in Play): Intraday, quick in/out, 2.5% stops possible, take profit fast
- **EP 9M**: Multi-day holds, wide stops, home run mentality, patience required
- **SIPs is not for everybody**: If it doesn't work for you, find your own setup

### Reddit ([[RDDT]]): The Neglected Stock Problem

> "The very fact that Reddit's earnings didn't produce volume that's not even the highest volume in a year — isn't it telling you something? There is nothing here which the mutual funds are excited about."

Reddit had excellent earnings but the market simply didn't care. The money is **entirely in AI and semiconductors**. Pradeep still liked it long-term ("it will still go to 200, might double in 6–12 months") but not actionable now.

### The Lululemon ([[LULU]]) Short: Blueprint for Catalyst-Based Shorts

Lululemon was the example of how to properly evaluate a short:

1. **Broken business**: Inventory problems, design issues — not fixable quickly
2. **Clear catalyst**: Earnings miss with fundamental deterioration
3. **Institutional quality stock**: So when it sells off, it's a clean short
4. **Rallied for only 2 days, then started selling off again** — the perfect short entry

> "When you want to short, you have to have a clear reason why you're shorting. In the case of Lululemon, I see a reason."

Contrast with [[HOOD]]: no clear catalyst for sustained downside — just a one-day pullback.

### What Makes a Real DEP vs. Calling Everything DEP

This was a major educational theme. Pradeep drew a sharp distinction:

> "If you call anything and everything DEP, then next you'll say a 400-pound person looks like Brad Pitt."

**Real DEP criteria** (using [[CRL]] and [[MP]] as templates):
1. Stock coming out of **neglect** — long base, sideways for weeks/months
2. **Catalyst** that changes the fundamental picture — not just any earnings beat
3. **Highest volume in years** on the catalyst day — institutional conviction
4. **Not already rallied into earnings** — genuine surprise
5. Then offers an orderly pullback and **delayed reaction entry**

> "How many stocks are going to meet that kind of criteria? Only three or four will meet that kind of thing. So then they will double or triple."

**Fake DEPs**: Stocks already up 84–100% into earnings, no real neglect, no genuine catalyst surprise. [[B]] was cited — up 103% already, "is it really neglected?"

### Buy Setups, Not Stories

A member asked about [[MRVL]] as a Broadcom competitor:

> "It doesn't matter whether Marvel is somebody's competitor, or Marvel is your brother-in-law, or Marvel is your sister-in-law. It doesn't matter. What matters? Setup. There has to be a setup."

> "If you buy at the right point, the probability of making money is high. There is no setup. It doesn't matter what the reason is."

**The time to buy is the start of a swing** — not the third or fourth day. Referencing [[AOI]] and [[TEM]]:

> "The time to buy this was this day. Start of a swing. There is no way to buy it now."

> "Buy setups on the right day. And after that, pray that it goes in your favor."

On holding period: **3–5 days** for EP 9M / DEP trades, then **sell into strength** depending on magnitude of move.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Fuel is everything**: Stocks need genuine earnings catalysts (39%+ sales growth) and institutional buying to sustain moves. Without fuel, earnings-day gaps fade.

2. **EP 9M and DEP are the primary setups** in this market. Continuation and reversals are secondary. Focus on research-based setups with real catalysts, not pattern-only calls.

3. **Sloppy setups are working** in this melt-up environment because of FOMO and desperation — but that doesn't make them your standard. They are context-dependent exceptions.

4. **Mind clarity above all**: Know which setup you're trading (EP 9M vs. SIPs vs. DEP), know your hold period, know your stop, know your position size. Without clarity, you're "just an ape."

5. **Buy at the start of a swing**: Third and fourth day entries have extremely low probability. "The time to marry your sister-in-law was before you married your wife."

6. **Real DEP requires research, not just pattern recognition**: Genuine neglect + genuine catalyst + highest volume in years + orderly pullback. "Only three or four stocks will meet that criteria."

7. **Position sizing adjusts to stop width**: EP 9M with 10% stops = 5% position size. SIPs intraday with 2.5% stops = larger position possible. Engineer your entire approach for your chosen game.

8. **Earnings timing matters**: Don't enter setups with earnings in 2–3 days — not enough time for the move to develop.

9. **Friday geopolitical risk**: Be aware that Trump typically starts geopolitical trouble after Friday close. Manage positions accordingly.

10. **Shorts need clear catalysts**: Lululemon (broken business) and Accenture (lost contracts + AI threat) are proper short candidates. HOOD (no clear catalyst for sustained downside) is not.
