### Process Mapping — Japanese Productivity Applied to Trading

Pradeep draws a powerful analogy from Japanese industrial productivity (Kaizen, Toyota Production System, Taichi Ohno):

> "Create buckets. Bucket one problem, bucket two, bucket three. Then process — which process should be moved? Where?"

Applied to trading, the process map for Anticipation:
1. **Scan** — find candidates
2. **Enter** — execute the trade
3. **Stop** — place the stop
4. **Exit** — manage the position

The Japanese approach asks: *Can we reorder these steps? Can we do entry the night before? Can we pre-place stops?* This is common sense, but most traders don't systematize it.

> "First decide your setup, then decide your process, then decide what tools you want, and then polish those tools. Know your tools inside out."

Pradeep credits a DHL colleague (Dr. Morley, Stanford PhD in Operations Research) who eliminated 70% of problems in 3 months through systematic process engineering.

### The Cluster Pattern — How Parabolic Moves Always End

This is the core lesson of the session. Pradeep explains the repeatable pattern:

1. **Stock goes parabolic** — 50%+ move, often 300%+
2. **First EP 9M appears** — bulls say "just a pullback to 10-day moving average"
3. **Deep buyers step in** — counter-trend bounce
4. **Cluster of 2-3 EP 9M breakdowns** — appears in quick succession
5. **Stock never recovers** — each successive attempt to bounce fails

> "Every single 50% plus winner or 300 plus winner, you'll see the same story plays out. You get first EP 9M. You don't go in the first EP 9M because there are a lot of bulls. But you get a cluster of EP 9M in that sector. And once the cluster has come in, the whole thing can never recover."

**Historical examples cited**: Bitcoin rally, QVST (quantum), oil shortage rally, silver rally, Plug Power, Palantir, Fluence, Lithium Americas

> "The more people say 'this is just a rotation' or 'it's just a pullback to the 10-day moving average' — that makes it more likely it will end the same way. Historically, things such ended."

### The Counter-Trend Bounce Setup for Shorts

- **Don't short the first EP 9M** — wait for counter-trend bounce
- After the bounce fails, short on the **second or third EP 9M**
- This is where conviction meets confirmation

> "I want to see a EP 9M red candle, and I would not short the first one. I would typically see, in this counter-trend. If the counter-trend bounces, then you short it."

### Qualifying Criteria — The Victoria's Secret Legs Analogy

When asked about Workday as a potential setup, Pradeep delivers a memorable explanation of what makes a good setup:

> "You want Victoria's Secret legs. Consistent, persistent buying in the first leg. Or tightness, at least. There is no tightness? Don't get involved."

The qualifying checklist for any setup:
- **First leg**: Consistent, persistent buying? (Victoria's Secret legs)
- **Tightness**: Is price consolidating tightly before breakout?
- **Catalyst**: Is there a genuine, game-changing catalyst?
- **Volume**: Is there aggressive buying volume?

If the answer to all four is no — don't get involved.

## Key Takeaways

1. **20% study at extremes = pullback warning**: When bullish stocks exceed 100 and bearish approach 20, expect sideways or pullback action. The "curse" never fails.
2. **EP 9M cluster pattern signals the top**: After a parabolic move, watch for 2-3 EP 9M breakdowns in quick succession. This cluster marks the end — the stock never fully recovers.
3. **Don't short the first EP 9M**: Wait for the counter-trend bounce, then short the second or third one. The pioneers get shot.
4. **Story stocks have a season**: Currently only AI/semiconductor is working. Story stocks will fly when speculative fever reaches a fever pitch — but not yet.
5. **Qualify setups with four criteria**: Persistent buying in first leg (Victoria's Secret legs), tightness before breakout, genuine game-changing catalyst, aggressive volume. If even one is missing, don't trade it.
6. **Distinguish SIPs from genuine catalysts**: A stock popping on earnings day because of short interest or options flow is not the same as a stock with a real catalyst. Sustainable moves need real catalysts.
7. **Don't chase gaps**: DEP setups need time to develop. Wait for sideways consolidation after the initial move. GLW, Fortinet — good candidates but not on day one.
8. **Process mapping (Kaizen)**: Map your trading process — Scan → Enter → Stop → Exit. Systematize it. Don't ask "what do I do" at 9:24 AM.
9. **Position sizing by experience**: 5% for beginners, 25%+ for experienced traders. Individual risk tolerance drives size.
10. **Gold/oil stocks are difficult**: They have "drunken person's rallies" and unfathomable logic. Avoid unless you specialize in them.
