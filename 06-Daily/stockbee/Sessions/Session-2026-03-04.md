# AI Butler Finds What Judgment Misses — BW Re-Entry Works, AI Basket Outperforms Human Picks

Day 3 of Iran conflict noise. Market gapped up on negotiation news but faded — a classic gap-up trap in volatile conditions. Pradeep was cautious, focused on catalyst-driven setups only. The standout story: Gemini AI identified BW, KANF, SHMD, and SWIM as morning picks, and all four would have worked. Pradeep's human judgment overrode the AI on BRKB (short) and KANF (too gappy) — both would have been the best trades. "The AI knows something I don't know." He re-entered BW after a morning stop-out, took 80% profit on CRCL, and emphasized that anticipation setups fail in choppy markets while catalyst-driven EPs thrive.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Cautious Essay — Anticipating Short-Term Bounce

> "My essay this morning is cautious, anticipating a short term bounce following multiple days of bearishness over the past week."

Market monitor showed 400+ selling day with margin deterioration across all indicators. 20% study was equal weighted (1:1 bullish to bearish). VIX surged. Small caps (TNA, IWM) underperformed. The plan: slow and deliberate risk taking on A+ quality EP 9M and delayed reaction setups, selling into strength.

### Gap Up Is a Trap

> "You can't trust these gaps, gap ups and gap downs currently because of all the noise from the war."

Market gapped up on Iran negotiation news, but Pradeep saw it as a trap. After 3-4 consecutive down days without knockout selling, the market tends to revert to range — not trend. The gap up would likely fade.

### AI Butler Outperforms Human Judgment

> "The AI told me four ideas for today — KANF, BW, SHMD, and SWIM — and just go see, all four of them would have worked."

Pradeep showed how Gemini Pro, given access to his StockBee site and EP framework, identified morning picks that all worked. His human judgment overrode two of the best calls:

- **BRKB short**: AI suggested it three days ago. Pradeep didn't believe it. "That would have been the best trade of this week."
- **KANF**: Gapping up too much for his taste. Would have worked.
- **SHMD**: Up 88%. He wasn't convinced. Would have worked massively.

> "I use my judgment and it screws up. The AI told me BRKB is a short. I use my judgment saying 'it's Berkshire Hathaway, I don't want to short Berkshire Hathaway.'"

His plan going forward: take AI picks as a basket and stop overriding with human judgment.

### BW — The Catalyst That Kept Calling

BW (Babcock & Wilcox) was the primary morning play:

> "BW — that's fired. That's the beauty of this thing. Miss stops never came into play."

He entered BW as a SIPs/catalyst play. Got stopped out on the remaining swing position in the morning, but re-entered later when the catalyst persisted. The 80/20 pill: booked 80% profit on first leg, kept 20% swing with breakeven stop.

### ONDS — Third Time Not the Charm

Third attempt at ONDS. Entered as OPG, but it didn't have the thrust. Sold for small profit, moved on.

### French Butler / AI Methodology Deep Dive

> "I don't want to become a coder. I want to make money."

Pradeep's AI workflow:
1. Load premarket gappers list into Gemini
2. Ask: "Find stocks meeting Episodic Pivot framework using StockBee criteria"
3. Gemini returns ranked picks with catalysts
4. Double-check the top 3-4

> "The butler told me there are these four stocks — SHMD, SWIM, KANF, and BW. Literally all four would have worked."

He uses Gemini Pro (not Claude) because it has access to his StockBee site and subscription products. The key insight: **don't complicate it.** Simple prompts work:

> "Analyze bullish case for BW today" — that's all you need.

### Anticipation Setups Fail in Choppy Markets

> "Anticipation is going to get you the lowest win rate out of all setups. You're starting with a very difficult thing."

Key teaching: in range-bound, volatile markets:
- **Anticipation fails** — gets chopped out repeatedly
- **Catalyst-driven EPs thrive** — BW, IOVA worked because they have reasons to go up
- **Undercut and rally works** — AMD-type reversal bullish setups
- **Buy support, sell resistance** — the playbook for choppy conditions

> "When the market becomes choppy, focus on things which have a clear reason or catalyst to go up."

### NTRT/MTRT for AI Stock Screening

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI Butler outperformed human judgment** — KANF, SHMD, SWIM, BW all worked from AI picks. BRKB short was the trade of the week that Pradeep skipped. Lesson: stop overriding the AI with "I don't want to short Berkshire."
2. **Gap ups are traps in volatile markets** — Iran negotiation news caused gap up, then faded. Don't chase gaps during geopolitical noise.
3. **BW re-entry validated the catalyst thesis** — Stopped out AM, re-entered PM, still worked because the catalyst (defense/energy) persisted.
4. **Anticipation setups = lowest win rate** — In choppy markets, anticipation gets chopped out. Focus on catalyst-driven EPs (BW, IOVA) and undercut-and-rally (AMD-type).
5. **Buy support, sell resistance in ranges** — When the market chops, the playbook is range trading, not trend following.
6. **SNAC "killed by talking about it"** — Mentioning a low-liquidity stock expanded the bid-ask spread. Keep quiet about thin names.
7. **Simple AI prompts work** — "Analyze bullish case for BW today." No need for complex prompts or coding. Just ask.
8. **NTRT/MTRT framework for AI screening** — Load the post, add recent examples, ask AI to find matches. Refine over time.
9. **BarChart + AI = 5 minutes instead of 30** — Pre-market list into AI for catalyst analysis. Massive time savings.
10. **Overnight risk capped at 1% in volatile conditions** — Controlled position sizing matters more than entry timing during headline risk.
11. **CRCL booked at 70%** — 80/20 pill: took 80% profit, kept 20% swing. "Good enough money for 8-10 days."
12. **Process over prediction, simplicity over complexity** — "I'm a simple man looking for dollars."
