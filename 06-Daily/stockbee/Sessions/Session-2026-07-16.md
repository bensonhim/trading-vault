# Stalemate Breaking — Google Shorts, BB Setups, and the One-Eyed King Advantage

July 16 marked a subtle shift: the stalemate that had gripped the market all week started breaking. Selling accelerated in AI-related names — Google got hammered on news of a delayed Gemini launch, and semiconductors continued their slow deterioration. Pradeep took his first trade since returning from Norway: a Google short, based on a clear catalyst and range-expansion breakdown. The AM session was a deep dive into why breakouts fail in range-bound markets, how to find qualified SOS setups (not random ones), and the importance of the "London Calling" video as a framework. The PM session identified two actionable setups — BB (Blackberry) and Google — both with catalysts, and Pradeep emphasized the "one-eyed king" advantage: if you understand setups and SA, you are already ahead of 90% of market participants who have no scans, no setup, and no clue.

### London Calling: The Framework Video

Pradeep pointed to his June 8 "London Calling" video as the warning framework.

> "I made this video on June 8. ... That was the day of the 800+ market breadth day. I did the video on the weekend because that was the time to do it. ... This should not come as a surprise."

### Tight Range = Volatility Contraction

> "Market is in a very tight range-bound phase till a new catalyst arises. ... Whenever tight range forms, there is no guarantee that it will break to the upside. It can break to the downside also. ... Volatility contraction leads to volatility expansion."

### What Works Here

| Setup | Works? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| SIPs (day trading) | ✅ | Higher volatility environment |
| EP 9M with clear catalyst | ✅ | Catalyst overcomes market weakness |
| DEP with catalyst | ✅ | Low-risk entry on quality names |
| Pattern-based breakouts | ❌ | No momentum/follow-through |
| Continuation setups | ❌ | Inherent to momentum; no momentum here |
| Anticipation | ❌ | Same — momentum-dependent |

### 84 Emails of People Not Listening

> "Since morning, 84 emails I have seen, which are asking me either what should I buy or 'I didn't listen to you, and I am down 40% on this position which I bought just before this correction or after the London Calling video.'"

> "You can take a horse to water, but can you make the horse drink water?"

### How Pradeep Learned SA

> "I learned it the hard way. By third time, I ended up giving up the gains which I had made during the bull phase, and I didn't understand why it was not working. I'm buying the best breakout, and it kept on failing. Then I said, okay, I'm gonna stop trading for six weeks or two months till I figure this out."

> "I looked at every single filter that can be used, and I found out that most of the market participants use market breadth as a filter to decide when to trade, not to trade at all. So I created my own solution, which worked for me, which still works for me."

### Day Trading vs Swing Trading Trade-Off

> "If you get used to day trading, then you are only thinking in ten minutes, fifteen minutes, or a few dollars. And your style drifts, and then you can never hold stocks for a bigger swing."

> "Some people are ambidextrous — they can do day trading and swing trading simultaneously. Most of the time, I have seen that you get used to one or the other."

### Qualified SOS vs Random SOS

Pradeep drew a memorable analogy for why you must qualify every SOS:

> "This is like a girlfriend you went on a date with. Your underwear was flying. Next time you went on a date, you had to go to the emergency room to get your private parts stitched back. Third time you tried, your lips were burning. Fourth time, her boyfriend showed up. Fifth time, you realized she has a husband. You want to trade this, be my friend."

> "Just because it's a SOS is not enough. You buy a qualified SOS. ... No context, no trade."

### 1,000 Hours to Trade 1 Hour

> "Can you make 5 million trading one hour a week? Answer is yes. But to get to that level where you can spend one hour trading, you'll need to spend a thousand hours to first have a setup. ... You'll need to spend ten thousand hours to get to that level."
