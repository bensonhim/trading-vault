# Narrative Shift: AI Disruption Panic, Cautiously Bullish Bounce, Singles Focus

> "I trade setups. If there is a setup, I'm going to trade it. It doesn't matter to me what my opinions are. That's all that is true."

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Market Regime: Cautiously Bullish (Short Term)

Cautiously bullish for continuation of the 2-day bounce. Primary indicators green, secondary still red but improving. 20% study equal weight. Big tech earnings being sold despite good results.

> "When there are real turns, institutions fall over themselves to get into the market. You get big bounces, big breadth. So this is in line with a typically dead cat bounce scenario."

### Narrative Shift: AI Disruption

The dominant theme — AI panic spreading across sectors. Insurance (WLTW, brokers), financial data (SPGI, Morningstar), software — all selling on "AI will kill this industry" narrative.

> "AI is going to kill this industry. AI is going to kill that industry. What industry is AI not going to kill? This is the same opposite of what happens in story stocks."

### DP Watchlist

Delayed reaction setups: [[TXN]], [[STX]], [[SNDK]], [[CVS]], [[HUM]] (best looking short). No meeting Wed-Fri. Monday holiday. Boot camp upcoming.

### T: The Anticipation Setup

[[T]] (AT&T) was a clean anticipation setup from yesterday — the standout trade. Pradeep highlighted it as the model setup:

> "If you don't know what you're looking for, you should be able to find the same trades. If you don't know what you're doing, then you will ask 'is this a long or short?'"

### Breakouts Likely to Work? Not Sure

Despite bullish SA, Pradeep was not buying breakouts:

> "I don't see anything which is remotely looks like a breakout which I would get interested in, which meets my definition of what to look for in a setup."

Only [[ITB]] and [[NAIL]] (home builders) looked decent — but sector already up 3 days, not ideal entry.

### Key Takeaways

1. **AI disruption narrative spreading** — insurance, financial data, software all under pressure
2. **[[T]] was the model anticipation setup** — clean, clear, executed
3. **Dead cat bounce likely** — 2-day bounce sustainability questionable
4. **Home builders showing breadth** — [[ITB]], [[NAIL]], [[HD]], [[LOW]], [[MTH]], [[KBH]] — but already extended
5. **Google: 10-20% downside probable** — hundred-year bonds, utility business model transformation
6. **AI hype is unreal** — "I would trust AI when it can put 2.5cm of toothpaste on my toothbrush and hand it to me"

### Google: Short Thesis

[[GOOG]] heading for 10-20% downside. The investment in AI infrastructure ($200B) is a terrible business model shift — from a zero-cost toll collector to a low-margin utility.

> "They were just eating the profit. Now they are going to be becoming like a utility company. Investing $200B in infrastructure — that's a terrible business."

### AI Stocks: The Next Big Short

> "The real money is going to be when AI hype stocks start breaking down. Short will be the money."

[[DAL]] already done — multiple legs, no more upside. All AI build-out stocks priced in 3-5 years of growth.
