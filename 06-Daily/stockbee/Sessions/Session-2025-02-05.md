### Start of a Swing — The #1 Fixable Problem (Continued)

- Member asks about buying a stock on the **third day** of a breakout
- > "Should I send you to Trump deportation? Why is it so difficult to understand that if you're a swing trader, you need to buy a swing at the START of a swing?"
- FNMA was bought on the **first day** of its swing — that's the entry
- LRCX — that's a start of a swing (clear range expansion, first day)
- Second day of a swing = already late. Third day = you missed it.

### Consolidation Quality: Drama Queen vs. Predictable

- Poor consolidation = multiple failed breakouts, 4% breakdowns within the base
- > "You went on a first date, it didn't work out. Second date, didn't work. Third time, your underwear was flying. You think it's going to work?"
- Ideal consolidation: orderly pullback on low volume, compact action, no 4% breakdowns
- Volume signature is critical — low volume during consolidation, high volume on breakout
- Choppy consolidations CAN work sometimes, but probability is much lower
- > "Focus on quality setups only. Your win rate will be higher and you won't spray bullets everywhere"
