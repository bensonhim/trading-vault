# Selling Below the Surface — EP 9M Focus, OPG Discipline, and the Male Drone Ant

Market indices near highs, but beneath the surface, selling is accelerating — stocks down 25% surged from ~45 to 137, EP setups dying on the short side, and only the AI/semiconductor narrative holds the market together. Pradeep zeroes in on EP 9M and DEP as the only valid setups, hammers home OPG as the single entry technique for catalyst stocks, and draws a devastating analogy: most EP moves are male drone ants — they mate, die, and it's over in days.

---

## AM Meeting

### Breadth Deterioration Beneath the Rally

The market shows "strong resilience" on the surface — Nasdaq and S&P at fresh highs — but Pradeep immediately flags the danger underneath:

- **20% study** at 1:1 ratio (equal bullish/bearish stocks) — no clear edge
- **Stocks down 25%** jumped from ~40–50 to 137 — massive selling pressure
- **Primary/secondary indicators** still green, but margins deteriorating for the third consecutive day
- **EP setups on the short side** going straight down — not even giving delayed reactions
- Selling visible in order flow: buying, selling, buying, selling — no sustained blocks of buying

> "Below the surface, there is a lot of selling. And how do you know? Because you just don't see blocks of buying."

### The CPU Shortage Narrative = Egg Shortage

The semiconductor/data center narrative has reached "shortage will never end" territory. Pradeep compares this to the egg shortage cycle:

| Narrative Phase | Egg Crisis | CPU/Semi Now |
|----------------|------------|-------------|
| Shortage declared | "Eggs $14, will never end" | "CPU shortage will never end" |
| Commodity response | Supply found, prices collapsed | Solutions emerge, bloodbath follows |
| Outcome | Narrative collapsed | Pre-market selling in extended semi stocks |

> "It's a commodity industry. If there is a shortage, they find a solution. The very hint of a shortage getting over can be the bloodbath."

### Setup Focus: EP 9M and DEP Only

Pradeep declares his focus unambiguously:

| Setup | Priority | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [[EP]] 9M | **Primary** | Must have genuine catalyst + volume |
| [[DEP]] | Primary (alternate) | Needs orderly pullback + catalyst |
| Continuation | Secondary | If breadth confirms |
| Anticipation | Secondary | Low conviction today — TBLA breaking down |

**Anticipation setups not working**: TBLA, which looked good as an ANT, was actually going down. The setup category is failing in this choppy regime.

### The Neglect Principle

> "For us, something to be a surprise, there has to be a neglect. And if it is not neglected, that the stock has rallied into it. Is there a surprise?"

This cuts directly against people asking "Is Cisco an EP?":

> "If Cisco is an EP, I'm Brad Pitt."

The point: Cisco is already widely owned and discussed. No neglect = no surprise = no EP.

### Tightness as Qualification

Two to three tight days, or volatility compression, before a breakout — this is non-negotiable for entry qualification. Without tightness, you're trading noise.

### ONDS — The Day's EP 9M Trade

[[ONDS]] reported record Q1 with 10-fold revenue increase, raised full-year guidance by 25%. Pradeep entered at $9.99 with a stop at $9.80 — only ~20 cents of risk:

> "The 100 out of 100 times, I'm going to take a trade like ONDS. Whether it works or not, how much did I lose? Just a few cents."

This is the EP 9M methodology in action: minimal risk, massive potential reward.

### Process Over Everything

> "The single most important principle, if you want to be profitable as a trader, is to pre-decide which setups you are going to trade."

He returns to this relentlessly:

- Don't jump from setup to setup depending on the season
- Develop a permanent edge (EP 9M, reversal bullish, etc.)
- Don't chase IPOs, random screeners, or things outside your circle of competence
- "If you created an edge in EP 9M, you created a permanent edge"

The **alien photograph analogy**: Trading with cursory knowledge has the same probability as finding a real photograph of an alien in the Pentagon files — blurry images people claim are spacecraft, but look like birthday balloons or adult diapers.

### Bad Habits Require Intervention

> "To get rid of bad habits, there has to be intervention. All other techniques which tell you to control your mind, discipline — no. It's not going to work."

### The Male Drone Ant Analogy

The most memorable analogy of the session:

> "The male drone ant is born. Within the first four to six weeks, he copulates with the queen. And after that, the bugger is dead. His life is over."

The parallel: Most [[EP]] moves complete in 1–5 days. By the time you figure it out on a weekly chart, the move is already done. Asking "Can I trade EP 9M on a weekly chart?" misses the entire point — the setup is designed for rapid moves.

[[ONDS]] itself made ~30% in one day. [[RKLB]] made 44% in three days. The move happens fast, or it doesn't happen at all.

### Mind Clarity = Foundation

> "If you don't have mind clarity, there is no mind clarity. Confidence comes from mind clarity."

The hierarchy:

1. **Understand the setup** completely (EP 9M = catalyst + volume + neglect + tightness)
2. **Pre-decide entry**: OPG is the method
3. **Know your profit objective**: Singles (8–20%) or Home Runs (50–300%)
4. **Execute without hesitation**: If ONDS has a game-changing catalyst, you hold

### TSM — Not a Quality Setup

This reflects the maturation of his process: fewer trades, higher quality, larger size.

### AI / Semiconductor Is the Only Game

> "There is only one thing which is working currently in the market, and that is the AI thing."

All other sectors are being ignored. [[FLEX]] (Flextronics) is the only anticipation that might work because it's in the right sector.

### Pre-Market Process: NTRT / MTRT

Pradeep explains his methodology:

1. **Wake up early** — this makes money, so he treats it seriously
2. **Briefing.com** — go through everything, not just cursory
3. **Premarket scan** — look at stocks gapping up 4%+
4. **NTRT / MTRT methodology** — systematic scan, sort by NC (number of criteria)
5. **AI tools**: Tried them, not impressed — "hit and miss"

[[ONDS]] was found because it was gapping up with 17M shares traded pre-market. The process works; you just have to do it.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Selling below the surface**: Market indices at highs, but breadth deteriorating — stocks down 25% surging, EP short-side dying, margins thinning for 3rd day.
2. **EP 9M is the primary focus**: Only trade setups with genuine catalyst + neglect + volume + tightness. Everything else is noise.
3. **OPG is the entry method**: For catalyst stocks, opening price gap is the only valid entry. Every day the same question — the answer doesn't change.
4. **Neglect = Surprise**: If a stock has already rallied into the catalyst, there's no surprise. Cisco is not an EP.
5. **Singles vs. Home Runs**: EP 9M targets 8–20% in 3–5 days. Sugar Wave targets 50–300%. Know which you're playing before entry.
6. **Male drone ant lifecycle**: Most EP moves complete in 1–5 days. Weekly chart trading of EP setups misses the entire point.
7. **Mind clarity is non-negotiable**: Pre-decide setups, entries, and profit objectives. Without clarity, you're trading alien photographs.
8. **Institutional quality shift**: Moving away from low-priced, choppy anticipations toward stocks owned by 2,500+ funds — larger size, cleaner patterns.
9. **AI/semiconductor is the only sector working**: All other sectors ignored by the market. Trade where the money flows.
10. **Chinese stocks and story stocks**: Avoid both — untrustworthy and no follow-through.
