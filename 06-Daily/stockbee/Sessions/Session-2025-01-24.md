## 10 AM Meeting — Trade Factory: EP vs Pattern, SA Filter, Deep Dive

### EP 9,000,000 vs Pattern-Based Setups

- **EP** = 90% catalyst + analysis, 10% pattern
- **Pattern-based** (SIPs, Sugar Babies, delayed reactions) = 90% pattern, some other criteria
- **Magna 53 Cap 10 by 10**: EP checklist
  - M = massive acceleration in earnings/sales (prefer sales > earnings)
  - A = gap up on catalyst
  - G = neglected (few funds holding)
  - N = 53: 5%+ short interest, 3+ days of consolidation, 3+ analyst upgrades
  - A = capitalization below 10M
  - Cap = IPOs below 10 years
- **EP produces only 3-5 trades per year** — if you're trading EP every day, it's not EP
- **Singles finance EPs**: 0.5-5% trades happen more frequently, allow you to take larger EP positions
- Without singles, one failed EP = 5% account drawdown (25% position × 20% stop)

### SA Filter = The Edge

- Pattern-based without SA: **~30% win rate** in current market
- Pattern-based WITH SA: **55-60% win rate**
- "Situational awareness is something which beginner traders don't understand the importance of"
- **SA designed to answer ONE question: "Are breakouts likely to work?"**
- KQ uses index filters (above 10/20 day MA), Minervini has his filter, Zanger has his
- Every successful swing trader has some form of SA filter

### NVDAI = Pump and Dump Territory

- Nasdaq compliance notice → stock up 150%
- Previously made 1500-1800% moves in 3 days
- People specialize in these: scan for low-price stocks in premarket
- "These are pump and dumps, chat rooms, management doing pump and dump"
- Not EP, not pattern — pure speculation on low-price stocks

### Curbing Natural Instinct

- **"Italian guy in piazza" analogy** — see a good-looking setup, can't help but want to enter
- **DDD**: has catalyst, looks like good breakout → fading intraday
- **Waystar**: nice breakout → but market conditions don't justify size
- **Cooper Companies**: nice high-priced stock → watching, not acting
- **Berkshire Hathaway**: delayed reaction on Buffett retirement rumors — NOT a buy today
  - "If the young man who is running this company retires, Berkshire will not be the same"
  - It's just imagery around Warren Buffett, not great business strategy

### MDT — Heartbreak Hotel

- Momentum burst setup, but context matters
- **Past history**: one good breakout worked, every subsequent breakout = "heartbreak hotel"
- **Dating analogy**: "based on the dating history of API, how much confidence do you have?" → 50/50
- Always give context to any setup — what happened in the past?

### Life Philosophy: Solve Problems Permanently

- "Find solutions to your problems which are permanent or semi-permanent"
- Setup = solved problem. SA = solved problem. Finding capital = solved.
- Current problem: deploying full capital (liquidity limits on smaller stocks)
- "I have a DDD setup, can I put $1M? No, maybe $100-200K based on volume"
- Problems change as you progress — solve the current ones, don't re-solve old ones
- Day trading vs swing trading = a personal decision, not a universal answer

### Deep Dive Philosophy — The Core Teaching

- **"Deep dive"** = the secret to success in any field
- Story of a young woman at Unilever who became top management by age 35
  - She deep-dived every ad/campaign from all markets — became walking encyclopedia
  - "If you bring something meaningful to any job, people will flock to you"
- **Pattern recognition requires supersaturation**:
  - Study every chart of every stock that made 50%+ moves
  - Print them out, mark where the opportunity was
  - Build a database, keep doing it until patterns become second nature
  - "Once you supersaturate your brain, charts start talking to you"
- **Screen time = deep dive** for day traders
  - Every successful trader (Zanger, Minervini, O'Neil) did obsessive chart study
  - "US Army converts 18-20 year olds into lean fighting machines in 6 months" — through overload
  - Same principle: overload yourself with patterns until they become reflexive
- **Copy what works, then adapt**:
  - "Monies" member found success by adapting Slater886's exhaustion setup
  - But what works for one person may not work for others — it requires unique adaptation
  - "Survive long enough, copy somebody which is working, make it work"
- **"You have to eat trading, sleep trading, breathe trading"** — then suddenly it clicks

### 80/20 Position Management

- Sell 50-80% of position, keep 20-50% with trailing stop
- "Keep moving your stop on the remaining 20%"
- Or: sell 50-60%, keep 40% with tight stop
- This is how you manage in choppy markets
