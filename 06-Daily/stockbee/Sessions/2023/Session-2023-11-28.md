# Buyer Exhaustion: Breakouts Fading, SA Is the Master Skill

> "A lot of stocks are breaking out, but not finding more buyers. Buyer exhaustion. Breakouts are reversing — almost all of them reversed yesterday. I was extremely bullish two weeks ago, now I'm more cautious. SA is the most important skill in trading." Operating from home due to lawyer/divorce matters.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Buyer Exhaustion

> "A lot of stocks are breaking out, but they're not finding more buyers. There is buyer exhaustion setting in. You see silent selling."

The breakout attempt in the overall market hasn't had follow-through. Not that it's failed — just no traction. Negative days on market monitor confirm the exhaustion.

### SA Lesson: Be Bullish at Start, Cautious Now

> "I was extremely bullish at the start of this move. Now I'm more cautious. You have to be bullish at the start and a bit more cautious now."

Anurag's story: was cautious at the start (missed the rally), then became very bullish after Thanksgiving, got thrashed by fading breakouts, went on hiatus from SA.

> "The market always does this. You have to be ahead of confirmation. Confirmation doesn't pay."

### Breakouts Reversing

> "Almost all breakouts reversed yesterday. You can lose a lot of money very quickly if you're not paying attention. This looked like a very nice breakout, and it suddenly reverses on you."

### Not Aggressive — Controlled

> "I can't control the external market. But I can control my reaction to it. That's why I'm not super aggressive here."

### Key Takeaways

1. **Buyer exhaustion** — breakouts not finding more buyers, silent selling
2. **Almost all breakouts reversed yesterday** — confirmation doesn't pay
3. **SA is the most important skill** — be bullish at start, cautious now
4. **Not aggressive** — controlled risk taking, not gung-ho
5. **First legs established** — stocks will form continuation setups, not breaking down
6. **Short side has nothing** — only ~20 stocks with breakdowns vs 200+ on long side
