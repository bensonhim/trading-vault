# Three Days of Bounce on Low Breadth — Looking for the Breath Thrust

> Bearish overall, three days of bounce on deteriorating breadth. Until a large positive breath thrust appears, remain in day trading mode — no fee, wait and watch.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bearish Overall

Pradeep's situational awareness was clear:

> "I'm bearish overall. We've had three days of bounce on low breadth, which is deteriorating. The market is gapping up, which I think we could see some profit taking today."

The key signal he was watching for: **a large positive breath thrust** — the confirmation that turns bearish into bullish.

> "Until we see that large positive breath thrust, I remain in day trading mode — no fee, waiting and watching for low risk opportunities, or sit on hands."

### The Breath Thrust: What Would Change the Regime

> "If there is a breath thrust, I'll be bullish. I'm looking for a breath thrust here. Even a 500, 600 day."

**Breath thrust** = a sudden surge in market breadth (stocks up 4%+) that confirms institutional buying. This is the signal that separates a dead-cat bounce from a genuine reversal.

### Green Shoots in Earnings Reactions

Despite bearish overall stance, Pradeep identified positive signals:

- Some earnings reactions were working ([[AMZN]], [[INTC]])
- 91 stocks showing up in scans (breadth improving from zero)
- "These are green shoots which are showing up"

> "If these form second legs, those are the kind of things which are fixed. The second leg is formed — continuation legs kind of a thing."

### Position Review: MicroStrategy Was the Anticipation Play

A key lesson on **anticipation setups**:

> "MicroStrategy was an anticipation setup yesterday. We discussed it. And it's bullish. Somebody said it's earnings — but I had bought it."

Pradeep bought [[MSTR]] at $14–$15 during the meeting itself, as an **anticipation setup** (buying before the catalyst event). The next morning it opened at $40+ — a massive gap.

> "If you're getting free money in the morning, and the stock opens at $40, and you bought it yesterday at $14... I'm going to take my profit."

**Lesson**: Anticipation setups (ANTS) are bought **before** the catalyst. If you wait until the morning, you've already missed the entry — you're chasing.

### The "Know Your Setups" Lesson

Pradeep challenged members to understand setup types:

> "If you're a day trader, it doesn't make sense. If you're doing it as an earnings breakout, and you feel the stock is gonna go up 50%, then you might as well get in."

> "MicroStrategy was an anticipation setup. Did anyone else notice? Or care?"

**Key distinction**: Anticipation = buy before catalyst. Earnings breakout = buy at open on catalyst day. Continuation = buy on pullback after first leg.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Breath thrust is the confirmation signal** — three days of bounce on low breadth is NOT a reversal. Wait for 500+ day.
2. **Anticipation setups are bought before the catalyst** — MSTR at $14, not $40
3. **80/20 exit rule** — take 80% profit on earnings breakouts, trail 20%
4. **Know your setup type** — anticipation ≠ earnings breakout ≠ continuation
5. **Volume matters** — "higher volume would make this better setup"
6. **Green shoots ≠ reversal** — some earnings working is positive, but not enough to change regime
