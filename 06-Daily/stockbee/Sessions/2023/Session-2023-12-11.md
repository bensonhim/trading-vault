# Fix Your Setup Understanding: 50% Start From the Bottom

> "Fix your setup understanding. It's not psychology which is the problem. It's the setup understanding which is the problem." Pradeep's #1 lesson from boot camp: members don't know their setups. The 50% study proves stocks start from bottoms, not 52-week highs. Most picks are third-leg FOMO or choppy consolidations — not valid setups.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish — New Bull Market Born

T2108 at rest extreme. Rally of months to years possible from this bottom.

> "This is the bottom. This is the birth of a new bull market potentially."

Twitter chorus calling tops = good contrarian signal. They'll FOMO later.

### The 50% Study: Everything Starts From the Bottom

Pradeep shows the 50%+ study (stocks up 50%+ in 40 days). Nearly ALL started from their lows — not from 52-week highs, not near moving averages.

> "Can you please ask for a refund? All of these stocks, which are up 50%, where did they start? They're all starting from bottom."

Books on technical analysis say: only buy near 52-week highs, only buy above 200-day MA. The data says the opposite. Question that assumption.

Every stock with a 50%+ move also had a 4% breakout. "How is that possible?"

### First Leg → Second Leg Is Where the Money Is

Many stocks have made their first leg. They'll set up for second leg. That's the current opportunity — continuation setups, not chasing extended moves.

### Visualization Script (Unchanged)

Multiple examples of choppy consolidations being flagged as "setups" — they're not. If a stock chops around like a girlfriend who kicks you every night, don't keep dating her.

### MongoDB: Bad Setup

Third leg of a move. Choppy consolidation. Not a valid setup. People see it on Twitter and buy randomly.

> "Have a method. Have a setup and operate from a method or a setup."

### ISRG: Good Anticipation

Intuitive Surgical was a good consolidation/anticipation setup — high-priced, first leg was clean. But only buy at market open, not chasing into the second day.

### Key Setups Reviewed

- **High-priced stocks** — don't need volume to work (ADP, LRCX, MA, ACN)
- **Delayed reaction** — only if positive earnings reaction first
- **Choppy consolidation** — skip, even in a bull market
