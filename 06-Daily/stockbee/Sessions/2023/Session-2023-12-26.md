# Develop Your Conviction: Rocket Lab EP and the Universe Study

> "Never join a religion. Start a religion." Post-Christmas low-volume day. Pradeep bullish long-term but cautious short-term — expecting a "checkout day" before New Year in this extended market. The universe study proves stocks making the biggest moves start from 52-week lows, not highs. Rocket Lab EP thesis intact: 51M shares (lifetime record), might double. "It's not a puppy — don't look at it every five minutes." The #1 lesson: develop your own conviction. Secondhand confidence doesn't exist.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Bullish Long-Term, Cautious Short-Term

> "My situational awareness remains bullish longer term. We might get a pullback or range-bound action, but seasonally the last part of the year tends to be bullish. Very low volume expected."

20% study at 40 stocks — not at extremes. Most 9M EP stocks starting moves from 52-week highs, which is typical of an extended rally.

### The Universe Study: Life-Changing Exercise

> "Do this study for yourself — it will change your life. Sort stocks by biggest move during the rally. Where did they start? From 52-week highs or 52-week lows?"

The answer: almost all big movers started from 52-week lows. [[MARA]] was down 50%+ before it moved. Stocks that "held up the best" are NOT where you should focus. Focus on stocks down the most — those are the ones that make the moves.

### Size Matters: Small Caps Make the Biggest Moves

The biggest movers during the rally all had market caps under $1B:
- $298M, $199M, $92M, $415M, $208M, $60M, $720M, $122M, $199M, $571M

> "If you have a smaller account, focus on stocks with $1B-$3B caps. Apple, Microsoft, Google, Tesla. But for big moves, small caps are where the money is."

### Plastics and Key Principles

Reiterated the core framework:
- **Plastics** = healthcare, technology, consumer discretionary always make the biggest moves
- **Stories** = stocks with stories make bigger moves than stocks with earnings and sales
- **Cougar** = younger stocks/younger moves are better
- **IPO 10** = stocks IPO'd in last 10 years tend to make big moves
- **Big bang volume** at the beginning of a big move (80/20 rule)

## 2 PM Meeting

### Trade Factory: Very Thin, Few Setups

9M EP had some good ones: [[NVDA]], Tesla, [[IWM]], AMD, Intel, [[CCC]], Cipher, Bluebird Bio, [[CAT]], Apple, [[COIN]]. But volume was extremely low.

### The Problem: People Choose the Worst Breakouts

> "When you have a choice of Cipher, CCC, BLBD — such nice breakouts — what do you want to do? You want to go look for a Walmart model. We don't like to buy good breakouts. We want to buy breakouts which are likely to fail."

The fundamental problem: members gravitate toward the shittiest setups instead of the obvious good ones.

### Rocket Lab: The EP Thesis Continued

> "If it has to go up, it will go up. It will double. I told you Rocket Lab is going to double. Stop getting ants in your pants. Put in some money, put a stop, and that's the end of the story."

51M volume. $2.1B market cap. Could go 100%, 200%, even 500%. Or could do nothing. But if you don't have conviction, don't take the trade.

> "Nobody can create that conviction for you. You have to create confidence yourself. That's why the #1 thing required to become a good trader is L — not F. If you don't have L, you can never become a trader."

### Anticipation: Watch List Only
