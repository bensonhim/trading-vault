# Sell Into Strength: Bird in Hand vs. Two in the Bush

> "Selling into strength always works. But greed gets in the way." The easy money on the TQQQ/SOXL trade is done. Pradeep is 95% cash, taking his payday. Story stocks with no earnings make the biggest moves. Oil stocks making first legs. Most setups are extended — not much catching his eye today. The 20% study proves again: stocks start from bottom, not near highs. And always check if your "great setup" is actually a buyout.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish Long-Term, Smaller Size Short-Term

> "I'm bullish long term. But in short term, I'm gonna keep my size small. There is a lot of rotation happening."

Oil stocks making first legs — continuation setups coming. Small caps and financials rotating in. Large caps may be exhausted short-term. Last week was one of the best follow-through weeks in this rally.

### Study 20%: Buy After 3-4 Days Up? That's What Most People Are Good At

> "If you do the 20% study, you'll come to the conclusion that the best way to make money is to buy a stock after it has been up three, four, or five days in a row. And this is what most of the people here are very good at."

Sarcastic — the 20% study proves the opposite. Every day people ask about stocks already up 5-9 days. Mark where the move STARTED — that's the range expansion. That's where you buy.

### Story Stocks Make the Biggest Moves

> "Stocks with stories will make bigger moves than stocks without. You can prove this to yourself."

Look at 50% movers — most have no earnings, no sales. Story stocks, sugar babies, and IPOs make the biggest moves. Technology, healthcare, and consumer discretionary are the best-performing sectors over 20 years — not gold, not uranium, not copper.

Two interesting setups: **W** (delayed reaction to 9M) and **CarMax** (potential). Light volume on many setups.

### Sell Into Strength — AMD Lesson

> "AMD had a picture-perfect anticipation setup. Sell into strength. What part of selling into strength is so difficult?"

AMD had primary breakout, then first leg, second leg, third leg move. Those who bought early should be selling into strength — not asking "what happened to AMD?" on day 3.

### 401k: Taking the Money and Running

Pradeep is selling 95% of his 401k positions. TQQQ/SOXL profits taken. Even a mutual fund account is up 50% in 1.5 months — he's taking that too.

> "Bird in hand is worth two in the bush. You never want to overstay a party like this."

This is NOT like 2009 or COVID — those were real washouts with Fed pumping. This rally ran ahead of itself. A 5-10% pullback could wipe out TQQQ/SOXL holders. The 3x leverage works both ways.

> "In July, there was a Fed-induced rally that went crazy. Three days later, you lost 11%. By the time some people got out, it was a 16% haircut."

### When You Get a Big Payday, Take It

> "When you get really a big payday in a short period of time, you take it. It's a ruthless business. Most hedge fund managers, if they go into losses, they just close down the fund."

He gets paid on closed profits, not open P&L. No incentive to stay in drawdown. Controlled risk taking = sitting in cash for months, then ramping up when the signal fires.
