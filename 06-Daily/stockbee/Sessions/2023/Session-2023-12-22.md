# Not a Thinking Game: Rocket Lab Catalyst and Holiday Caution

> "This is not a thinking game. The moment I saw the catalyst — $500M government contract — I said this stock is likely to double. I need to buy this stock. You buy setups, not news. Rocket Lab was the stocks-in-play." Pradeep entered Rocket Lab at open with a standard 40¢ stop. By 2 PM, he predicted it could do 100-200%. Meanwhile, the broad market showed fading momentum — the worm's "Viagra effect" wore off by 10 AM. Very thin day ahead of a 4-day Christmas weekend.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market: Bullish Long-Term, Cautious Short-Term

> "I'm bullish long term, but I'm cautious in the short term. The market might be going through a consolidation period for a few days."

Pullback likely, which may translate into range-bound action for some weeks. 20% study at ~41 — nothing exciting. No 11 AM meeting (Pradeep viewing a house).

### Trade Ideas Platform Walkthrough

Detailed walkthrough of how Pradeep uses Trade Ideas in real-time:

- **Worm**: Shows buying/selling pressure. At open, 76% buying — but by 10 AM, the "Cialis/Viagra effect" wore off
- **Multi-Strategy Window**: Acts as a ticker. Green floods = buying, red = selling. Shows buying/selling coming in before it shows up in TC2000
- **Stocks with Momentum**: The big movers show up here first and dominate for the entire day. [[IREN]] showed up here yesterday
- **Dollar Breakout Scan**: Equivalent to what Trade Ideas shows. Stocks gapping up $1+ pre-market
- **Earnings Window**: Shows which stocks had earnings — never miss an earnings play

> "Everything which is showing up here is basically starting their moves from the lows. If you're looking for 3-5 day moves, most of them start from levels not necessarily near the highs."

### Rocket Lab: The Textbook Stocks-in-Play

Jay sent Pradeep two SIPs at 9:20 AM: Nike (gapping up) and Rocket Lab ($500M government contract).

> "Do you really think Rocket Lab? Let me think. I got the text this morning. I know Rocket Lab $500M, trading at $4.50. That's a real catalyst. Am I going to be thinking, or am I just going to put an order in? That's it."

Standard stop: 40¢ on a $4-5 stock. No thinking required — process over deliberation.

### Buy on Day One of Breakout (Reiterated)

> "If you can develop the skill of identifying where a breakout starts and buying it on that day, that's better than any other skill in trading."

Most moves in 9M EP start from lows, not near highs. Focusing on stocks near highs is a different kind of opportunity.

### 20% Study as Oscillator

> "When it was at 100+, that was extreme bullish. When it is at 20, it's extremely bullish. That means the market has had a correction and new money comes in."

Post-COVID correlations have broken down. 15% is the new 50% for breadth signals. The 20% study used to reliably signal corrections when it hit 20+ — now less reliable.

### Day Trading Lesson: Go Where the Action Is

> "If you're day trading some dead stock with no volume, why do you think it's going to make money? You have to trade stocks in play — that's where the money is."

Day traders should be on Coinbase, Moderna, Google — stocks attracting volume. Not exotic low-volume stocks.

### China Stocks: Do Not Go Zone

> "China has a category where they want to screw their companies. Every three months they decide — now we screw the gaming companies. It's a do not go zone."

Baba, BIDU, all Chinese stocks = avoid entirely.

### Biotech Breakouts

Biotech always has lots of breakouts during any bull market. Not an indicator of market direction specifically.
