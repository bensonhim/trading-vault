# Find and Enter Early: The Only Rule That Matters

> "There are three ways to make a living in this business: be first, be smarter, or cheat. Now I don't cheat. It sure is a hell of a lot easier to just be first." — Margin Call. The market is entering a choppy consolidation phase. Smaller size. Find and enter early. Everything else is a variation of momentum. Setup understanding is the #1 problem — if you can't identify continuation vs consolidation vs first leg, everything looks difficult.

> "I'm bullish long term, but cautious in short term. The markets might be moving into a choppy consolidation phase."

After a fast and furious 1.5-month rally from extremely low T2108, the first phase was broad-based. Now fewer stocks will participate — a discriminating rally. Low holiday volume means exaggerated moves. Keep smaller size.

### Study 20%: 65 Stocks, All Start From Bottom

> "If there is one thing which you should really focus on studying, it is how we start. And that is a fascinating field which will probably change the way you think about the market."

The 20% study shows stocks start from bottom — contrary to Twitter advice to "buy near highs." Every day the study proves it. If your theory doesn't match the study, your theory is wrong.

## 11 AM Meeting

### Trade Factory: Thin Pickings, Two Ideas

Combination bearish 145 vs bullish 50 — more selling than buying.

**Two ideas:** GCT (continuation setup) and Sarah Diagnostic (continuation setup, low price).

> "Setup understanding is the biggest problem. GCT is a continuation setup, not a consolidation breakout. Four days of consolidation/pullback = continuation. Twenty days sideways = consolidation."

### "Be First" — The Margin Call Quote

When asked whether to wait 15-30 minutes and re-run scans:

> "There are three ways to make money in this business: be first, be smarter, or cheat. It sure is a hell of a lot easier to just be first."

GOOGL and META showed up in the first 1-2 minutes — institutional buying right out of the gate. That's who's buying. LTV (low threshold breakout) is the filter to catch things early.

### Being Late Costs Money

If you buy late, your stops are wider, so reduce size accordingly. "The confirmation cost money." Sometimes being early stops you out (Wayfair, 40 cents) — that's the cost of being first.

### Catalyst Analysis for Longer Holds

> "If you want to hold something longer term, you have to do catalyst analysis. And if you're buying an extended stock with a catalyst, it still becomes a pattern-based trade."

Pattern-based = short-term (3-5 days). Catalyst-based = longer holds possible, but buying right is even more important.

### No FOMO = Bullish Sign

> "I'm very bullish on this market because everybody on Twitter says the rally is over. There is no real FOMO in my email indicator."

600-700 back-to-back selling days would be the warning sign. Not happening yet.

## 2 PM Meeting

### 9M EP: Cypher and Kava Stand Out

Cypher = 100% story stock (Bitcoin angle). Kava = bought earlier on 9M breakout, still valid. Dynatrace = low threshold but volume signature weak. GOOGL = delayed reaction fading.

### Don't Buy Extended Stocks

> "If you have a wife and you wish her happy birthday four days after her birthday, what is going to happen?"

Amazon = 4th day of breakout. NIO = 3rd day. LQR = up 54%. Buying these = no edge.

> "56% of Americans now own stocks, up from 37% pre-COVID. There will be no shortage of gullible fools buying things on third and fourth day of breakout. I can buy on the first day and sell to them on the third."

### Only One Genuine Anticipation Setup

Dollar Tree (DLTR) — the only one that looks like a real anticipation setup. Everything else is extended or peaceful (needs more time to develop).

### Ugly Setups Can Work — But Do You Need To?

> "Can ugly setups work? Yes. Do really ugly guys get good girlfriends? Jeff Bezos' girlfriend has plastic oozing out of all five parts. Same way, ugly setups can work also. The question is, do you really need to?"

### Stop Looking at 5-Minute Charts

A member asked about getting stopped out of GCT on a 5-minute drop:

> "Stop looking at five minutes. Give stocks room to run. If you have such a nice setup and you're not giving it room, you'll never make money."

Preferred stop: 2.5%. Beginners: up to 5%. 8% too much. 10%+ = "buy funeral and burial insurance."

**Fix your problems before increasing size.** Size is the easiest problem to fix once you're good at entries and exits.

### The Cold Water Method

> "Whenever you get an urge to buy a breakout on third day, fourth day, or fifth day — try a glass of cold water. You don't drink it. You put it on your head to remind you not to do stupid things."
