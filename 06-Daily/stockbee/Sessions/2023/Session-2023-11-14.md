# Reluctant Bounce: Sell Into Strength, FOMO Is the Enemy

> "This is not really the real deal. In 24 years, a genuine bull market has hundreds of stocks up 20% in a month. We have 22. It's a reluctant bounce — sell into strength aggressively." Anti-FOMO day: everything sold into strength faded by afternoon.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Cautiously Bullish — But Not the Real Deal

> "This is nowhere close to any bull market I've seen in the last 24 years. It looks like a reluctant bounce."

The 20% study tells the story: only 22 stocks making big moves in 5 days. In a genuine bull market, that number climbs to hundreds quickly. This bounce has no spark rate.

### CPI Reaction Was Weak

> "If CPI was a real catalyst, the worm would be in the forties and never come back. The reaction to CPI was less than 9 stocks making new highs."

Catalyst existed but institutional buying didn't follow through. The gap was a surprise reaction, not deep buying.

### Aggressive Peeling — Sell Into Strength

> "I'm going to be aggressively peeling because in this market, not peeling has not been the best strategy. Giving room has not worked."

Key positions being sold into strength:
- [[ROKU]] — largest position, selling in premarket
- [[BKNG]] — still holding after yesterday's hero trade
- [[PLTR]] — carrying

### Buy at Start of Swing Move

> "Buy at the start of a swing move. If you're buying on the second or third day — God help you."

Advice for struggling traders: add this to your visualization script as the FIRST thing. Sugar babies not working either.

### Mark Minervini Comparison

> "Mark's method is all confirmation. He waits, he's not early. He builds positions — first position, then adds. That's different from my approach."

---

## 11 AM Meeting

### Selling Into Strength Was the Right Call

> "Everything I sold into strength is now down from where the new high was in the morning. Selling into strength was still the best strategy for today."

### The Anti-FOMO Message

> "The worst thing in this business is FOMO. My phone has been ringing all morning — neighbors, local people — they see the market and they want to buy. It doesn't work that way."

Pradeep's 401k: invested a couple weeks ago, made no changes. People can't avoid FOMO even with retirement accounts.

### You Make Money by Buying a Setup

> "You make money by NOT understanding the market. You make money by buying a setup. Setup plus risk. That's all."

The decision framework:
1. Is there a continuation setup? → Buy
2. Is there a consolidation setup with catalyst and volume? → Buy
3. No setup? → Wait. Tomorrow or in 2-3 days, you'll find something.

> "Gap is not institutional buying. A gap happens when there is surprising news. I don't have the stomach to buy gaps where risk/reward is not in my favor."

## 2 PM Meeting

### Breadth Improving — But Still Cautious

Breadth at ~600 by afternoon. Better than recent days but still not thrust-level.

> "If this is a genuine move, the trade factory will produce more trades. Is there any doubt the trade factory will have continuation setups?"

### Continuation Setups = Low-Hanging Fruit

> "Everybody who studies the market comes to the same conclusion — continuation setup is the low-hanging fruit. That's the easiest money in the market."

**Consolidation breakouts often fail**: "I can show you hundreds of examples where it looks like a nice long consolidation, perfect breakout... and then it just poops."

- Buy too early → don't know if volume will come
- Buy too late → stop is too wide
- Continuation setups > consolidation breakouts

### Three-Day Hold Period

> "I sold my WTW yesterday. If I'd held it hoping it would keep going up — that just doesn't work."

Sold [[WTW]] yesterday per the 3-day hold discipline. Today it faded.

### Key Takeaways

1. **This is NOT a real bull market** — 22 stocks vs hundreds in genuine thrusts; reluctant bounce
2. **Sell into strength aggressively** — everything sold in the morning faded by afternoon
3. **FOMO is the enemy** — gaps are not institutional buying, don't chase
4. **Setup + risk = all you need** — stop trying to "understand the market"
5. **Continuation setups are low-hanging fruit** — easiest money in the market
6. **Consolidation breakouts often fail** — look nice on breakout day, then "poop"
7. **Buy at start of swing move** — buying day 2 or 3 is asking for trouble
8. **3-day hold discipline** — sold WTW yesterday, would have faded today
9. **CPI reaction was weak** — only 9 stocks made new highs; not a real catalyst
10. **Aggressive peeling over giving room** — in choppy markets, take profits fast
