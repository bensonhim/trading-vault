# Lockout Rally: Continuation Setups Forming as Market Leaves the Station

> Bullish SA with a lockout rally warning — 5-day up streak, breadth thrust from T2108 below 12, and continuation setups proliferating. Story stocks > earnings stocks.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish — Lockout Rally Underway

Cora's SA: bullish with breadth thrust expanding, but approaching overbought after 5 up days. Pradeep diverged:

> "If you have seen the market, when it starts going up, it typically doesn't do that [pull back]. People are left to chase. And that's the significant characteristic of a start of a new bull move."

He called it a **lockout rally** (Mark Minervini term) — the pullback everyone is waiting for never comes, and people get locked out.

### T2108 Breadth Thrust Signal

The T2108 reached ~12.62 at its lowest, then surged to 51% above 40-day moving average in just 5 days — a **powerful breadth thrust**. Pradeep used this as his 401K entry signal.

> "Whenever it reaches extremely bearish level or when T2108 goes below 20, I'm looking to invest in a 401K. But just because it goes below, it's not immediately — I'm looking for some signal that there is a turn."

### Continuation Setups Will Proliferate

> "The continuation setups will start showing up in your HNS15. Stocks which are up 15% and going sideways — those are the stocks from which continuation moves will happen."

### High Short Interest = Squeeze Candidates

Pradeep posted a watchlist of top 50 high short interest stocks. Many already making big moves: [[AFRM]], [[CVNA]], [[AI]], [[WVE]].

> "You buy a stock with high short interest, it is likely to make a big move. That's how you benefit from it — buy breakout on a high short interest stock."

### Story Stocks > Earnings Stocks

> "Stocks which have made the biggest move YTD — do they have earnings or do they have stories? ACIC? Carvana? AI? Those people who say profitability matters should answer why these 50 stocks are up."

### Continuation Setup Masterclass: "Does It Have Legs?"

> "Stock should go up, then it should go sideways, and then it is breaking out. Does this have a leg? That's the first most important criteria."

Pradeep used his daughter's video lesson to illustrate: **no leg = no continuation setup**. One day of buying ≠ persistent buying.

Key examples:
- **SPGI**: aggressive buying for 4 days = has legs ✅
- **EXPO**: one day of buying = no legs ❌

### Anticipation Watchlist Built
