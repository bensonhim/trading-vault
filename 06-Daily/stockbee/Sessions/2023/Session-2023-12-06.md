> "That was such a nice free money. I just laugh at people who are late to the party."

### The Fading Lesson: Situational Awareness

Everything gapped up and faded from the open. The red worm was dancing above the green worm. SPY gapped up and sold off.

> "Don't you people have eyes? There are two things above your nose. Can you not see this? Right from word go, you can see it starts spreading."

The analogy: five cop cars on the corner with flashing lights, and you're asking "how fast should I go?" before speeding into them.

> "Please operate with your eyes open. That's the least thing you can do."

### Don't Buy Extended Breakouts

On SQ (Block): gap first leg, second leg, third leg, now fourth leg — and someone wants to buy here?

> "Severe case of FOMO."

### Stay In Your Lane

> "I don't care about all these people doing levels and buying gap downs. I know that's not what I'm going to be doing. My game is very clear."

### Delayed Reaction Setup on CRM

CRM was highlighted as a delayed reaction setup — some members didn't even recognize what setup it was. Pradeep: "It's written there. In English. Delayed reaction."

### Volume Matters for Consolidation

When asked about buying a consolidation: "Is there volume here?" No volume = no buy. Market is fading, don't force it.

3. **Sugar babies are must-buys** — when Rivian calls, you answer
4. **80/20 quick trade works in fading markets** — CRM profit in minutes
5. **Stay in your lane** — don't trade other people's styles
