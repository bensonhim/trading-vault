# Session 2026-05-11 — Extended Market, DEP Qualification, and Earnings Discipline

The market is in a bull phase but many sectors are **extended**, making early breakouts prone to fading — a day where discipline about catalysts, earnings dates, and position sizing mattered more than finding setups, which were abundant but mostly in already-extended names.

### DEP Qualification — The Victoria's Secret Model Rant

A participant asked about [[IONQ]] as a DEP. Pradeep's response was the most quotable teaching moment of the session — a fierce defense of **what DEP actually means**:

> "I explain this every day. I don't know why people are, like, going into these stocks with no earnings, no sales, and shouting DEP DEP, but you cannot change the world."

> "Every woman is Victoria's Secret model."

> "Every single thing for some people is EP, and every single stock is DEP."

**Pradeep's DEP definition** (stated explicitly):

1. Must have **EP 9M** (Episodic Pivot with 9 million+ volume on catalyst day)
2. Must have a **genuine catalyst or theme** (not just random volume)
3. Should have **gap up** and **massive volume**
4. Ideally the volume is **the highest ever or near-highest**
5. Must be **institutional quality**

> "Definition of DEP for me is that it should be doing EP 9,000,000. Should have real catalyst or a theme. Gap up? Massive volume? As far as possible, volume is the highest in here or ever. Team. Most importantly, on quality."

> "Now if you have a definition of DEP which is different from mine, trade your own setup, then don't show up for these meetings and confuse everybody."

He contrasted [[VIAV]] (highest volume, real catalyst, institutional quality) with [[BW]] (low volume on the day) — VIAV was the superior DEP because volume confirmed conviction.

### Earnings Discipline — The Oklo Incident

A participant kept asking about buying [[OKLO]] (Oklo) before earnings. Pradeep's response was scathing:

> "Please buy Oklo. And buy full account. Borrow money and buy it."

> "Either you'll be a hero or you'll get nuked. Only two options."

> "Earnings is a lottery."

He pointed to [[FSLY]] (Fastly) as a cautionary tale — held into earnings, wiped out 38% in one day. The lesson: **always check earnings dates before entering a trade. Never buy in front of earnings.**

> "Can you not get excited if the earnings is in next day or something. Is that very difficult to understand?"

### Exhaustion Signals

Multiple stocks were printing **exhaustion candles** — extended names rallying parabolically:

> "All these looks like exhaustion on these stocks. All these, this move and all, these are all printing exhaustion candles."

[[RXT]], [[COHR]] (Coherent) were flagged as exhaustion signals. The pattern: extended stocks in AI/data center theme reaching parabolic extremes.

### OLC (Organized Like Crazy)

Pradeep emphasized the daily checklist ritual multiple times:

> "Organized like crazy. 10:00, you start looking at your scans. 10:00, you don't know where the earnings is. You're buying in front of earnings. These are signs of dementia."

The morning checklist:
1. Look for **tight action** (3-4 days sideways minimum)
2. Check **earnings dates** — avoid entries within 3 days
3. Have **scans in place** before market open
4. Only trade setups you **understand**

> "Think like a Japanese. Japanese people do small things right."

### Extended Setups Are Working — But Adjust Size

Pradeep acknowledged an uncomfortable reality: **extended setups are the only ones working** in this market phase. The AI/data center theme kept going.

> "Extended setups have worked very well. So who am I to say not to buy extended setups?"

The solution: **adjust position size** for extended entries. Don't avoid them entirely, but size down.

### Position Sizing on Gaps

When a participant pointed out [[BW]] was up 25%, Pradeep's response was about sizing, not avoidance:

> "If you're telling me BW is up 25%, you adjust your size accordingly if you want to trade."

> "In this market, things gap up fifty, sixty, 70%, and then still make move. [[FSLY]] gapped up 42%. After that, the stock made 138%."

The key: catalyst-driven gaps are the **nature of these moves**. Size accordingly; don't avoid them, but don't go full size either.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Early strength fades in extended markets** — don't chase the open. Wait for breakouts to stick. "If it sticks, it's good" was the repeated refrain.
2. **DEP requires EP 9M + genuine catalyst + institutional quality** — not just any stock with volume. "Every woman is Victoria's Secret model" = every stock is not a DEP.
3. **Never buy in front of earnings** — check earnings dates before entry. 3-day buffer minimum. "These are signs of dementia."
4. **Exhaustion candles on extended names** — be aware of where you are in the swing. Fourth legs are not this methodology.
5. **Adjust size, don't avoid** — extended setups are working, so size down rather than sit out entirely.
6. **Stick to the theme** — AI and data center are the working themes. Non-theme breakouts (Robinhood) have less follow-through.
7. **Anticipation and reversal setups skip in bull phases** — they're for choppy markets. Right now, trend continuation wins.
8. **OLC: do the work before 9:30 AM** — have scans ready, earnings dates checked, setups identified. "Think like a Japanese."
