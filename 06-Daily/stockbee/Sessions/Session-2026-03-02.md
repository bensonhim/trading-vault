# Drone Wars — ONDS Sugar Baby Certified by Market, Iran Conflict Fades, and the Probability Game Holds

March 2 opened with the Iran conflict creating headlines, but the market told a different story. Pradeep's essay was bearish, the 20% study showed bullish readings at 2:1, but the real signal came from the EP 9M scan: ONDS, a drone/defense stock, exploded out of the gate as a sugar baby with volume catalyst. The market barely gapped down, signaling it wasn't taking the geopolitical event seriously. Pradeep bought ONDS at 11.26 average, it ran 10%, then the entire defense sector sold off on a Pentagon policy change and he was stopped out at breakeven. His lesson: nothing wrong with the trade, it is a probability game, and he would take the same trade 100% of the time. The PM session found little actionable beyond AMD as a reversal bullish candidate and SNDK remaining on the delayed reaction watch list.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Bearish Essay, Bullish 20% Study, but Market Says Noise

> "My essay this morning is bearish, anticipating the market to remain choppy and unpredictable amidst the Middle East conflict."

The 20% study leaned bullish (roughly 2:1), but this was an anomaly given three days of selling. The VIX strengthened, small caps underperformed, and mega caps pulled back on AI disruption and valuation concerns. Pradeep's plan: slow, deliberate risk taking on A+ quality, EP 9M and delayed reaction setups only.

### Iran Conflict: Volatility, Not Direction

> "Expect lots of volatility for few days with intraday news and rumors. That will make it difficult for swing, but it will make it easy for day traders because day traders thrive on volatility."

The Iran event surprised on Friday night, but the market's muted reaction told the real story. Gap ups in oil/defense faded intraday. The market was not pricing this as serious.

### EP 9M Certified the Theme: Drones

The EP 9M scan immediately surfaced ONDS (Ondas Holdings) as the first stock to show up with massive volume. The drone theme was unmistakable:

> "Is the market telling you that RTX is going to be the winner, or is the market telling you ONDS is going to be the winner? It's clearly shouting at you saying, 'ONDS. ONDS.'"

ONDS came out of the gate at 09:31:33, a clear sugar baby with volume as catalyst. Pradeep's entry was 11.18 first fill, 11.35 on the add. The setup had everything: defense theme, volume, early entry, sugar baby confirmation.

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### ONDS Stopped Out at Breakeven

The defense sector sold off after a Pentagon policy change news hit. ONDS reversed from its highs. Pradeep had moved his stop to breakeven:

> "I had breakeven stop. No damage done to the account. And when was this, the, if you see how quickly that showed up on the scan and how quickly I was in it."

> "It's a probability game. Sometimes it works, sometimes it doesn't work. All that you have to do is controlled risk taking."

Nothing wrong with the trade. The entry was correct, the setup was correct, the catalyst was real. The entire sector reversed on policy news. He would take the same trade 100% of the time.

### The Market Dismissed the Iran Event

> "Today's action is telling you, the market recovered. It is not even something which the market is really serious bothered about. It's just noise."

Oil and defense stocks faded. The market was pricing in that this would force the Fed to lower rates, which was bullish. Geopolitical noise, not signal.

### Toby Crabel on Early Entry

Pradeep read from Toby Crabel's work:

> "In general, the earlier in the session the entry is taken, the better the chances for success. In fact, the ideal is an entry within the first 10 minutes of the session."

> "The objective of this entry technique is to establish a position for one to three day run, but is successful only if a substantial profit is realized by the end of the first session."

He contrasted this with members who always want to enter on day 2 or day 3, when probability has already declined.

## Key Takeaways

1. **EP 9M is the primary setup idea** — It tells you what the story for the day is. ONDS showed up first and told the drone story.
2. **Volume itself is a catalyst** — You don't need to know what ONDS does. If it shows up in EP 9M with volume, that is the catalyst.
3. **The market dismissed Iran** — Gap downs were minimal, oil/defense faded intraday. The market was pricing in rate cuts, not war escalation.
4. **ONDS trade was correct, stopped at breakeven** — Entry at 11.18/11.35, stock ran 10%, then reversed on sector-wide Pentagon policy news. Probability game. No damage to account.
5. **Sugar baby + catalyst + early entry = the trade** — When a sugar baby with a theme-specific catalyst appears in the first 10 minutes, take it. Move stop to breakeven quickly.
6. **Prioritize the #1 setup** — Don't dilute with second-tier names like PL when ONDS is calling. 40-50% upside potential vs. marginal setups.
7. **Don't chase late entries** — By PM, most good entries were gone. "I want early entries. The first few minutes for a breakout. That is the best one."
8. **Toby Crabel confirms early entry** — "The more time that passes between open and trade entry, the lower the probability of success."
9. **Controlled risk taking is the only defense** — "Sometimes you ride the market, sometimes the market rides you. Make sure the market doesn't ride you by doing controlled risk taking."
10. **SNDK remains the #1 delayed reaction idea** — Carried over from prior days, still on watch.
11. **Bitcoin is a graveyard** — Not a play. Wait for continuation setup.
12. **Boot camp in New Jersey, March** — Not on a cruise ship.
