# Breadth Thrust Day: Themes Are Back, Train Your Procedural Memory

> Big 600+ breadth thrust to start the year — AI, drones, uranium all flying — but Pradeep's focus was on training procedural memory on clean setups, not chasing dirty patterns.

---

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautiously Bullish

600+ breadth thrust yesterday. All indicators green with improving margins. T2108 up to 54.78. Small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) outperforming. Dow at all-time highs. Breakouts likely to work.

### Themes Are Back: AI, Drones, Nuclear, Uranium

Pradeep identified the clear themes by looking at what's moved in the last 5 days:

> "Data center AI, uranium, lunar, NVTS semiconductor, robotics, aerospace defense, drones — these are the stocks which have gone up. This is where the money flowed in. Themes are back, baby."

### The Procedural Memory Lesson

This was the dominant teaching of the morning session — what you train your brain on determines your results:

> "If you train your procedural memory on the wrong thing — dirty setups, second-day breakouts — you'll spend your entire life trying to find shitty setups. Train your procedural memory on clean start-of-swing setups like TTD."

> "This trade is all about training your procedural memory. You want to overwrite your procedural memory, not undertrain it."

### Tesla: Institutional Waterfall Decline

> "Some institution is selling Tesla. It tries to break out, one day bounce, and the seller is back. Retail investors are all sold on Tesla — who is selling? People smarter than you."

## PM Session (3 PM)

### Momentum Fading: Nothing Compelling

By afternoon, the morning's gap-ups were fading. SOXL gave up all gains. Many setups were now 3rd day of swing — not ideal.

> "NVIDIA had good news yesterday, nothing happens on NVIDIA. SOXL was supposed to be good news, today it's given up all the gains."

### Don't Buy Second or Third Day

> "So many three-day setups now. Some will transition into anticipation setups. Some into continuation setups. But you don't want to be buying second or third day of a swing."

### Tesla Short Discussion

> "Tesla is the story here. Retail investors are all sold on it. I have not met a retail investor who's not bullish on Tesla. So who is selling? People smarter than you."

### Breakouts Working, But Selectively

Breakouts were working, but only selectively. Pradeep scanned through the bullish work list:

- Start-of-swing setups ([[GAP]], [[PLTR]], [[HD]], Plastic) — valid but already day 2-3
- Many "dirty setups" that members were asking about
- Clean breakouts: [[BFS]], [[TTD]], [[MELE]]

> "On a day when you have so many good breakouts, if you're going and buying things like this [dirty setup], I cannot help you."

### Key Takeaways

1. **Themes are back.** AI infrastructure, drones, uranium, nuclear — money flowing into these sectors.
2. **Train your procedural memory on clean setups.** TTD is the model — clean start of swing with volatility decrease. Not dirty 3rd-day breakouts.
3. **Don't buy the second or third day of a swing.** Many setups were fading by PM.
4. **Tesla is a waterfall decline.** Institutions are selling. Don't fight institutional selling.
5. **SOXL gave up all morning gains by PM.** Gap-ups don't always hold.
6. **Selective breakouts work.** But you need clean entries, not dirty setups.
