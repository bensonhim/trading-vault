# The Superpower: Buy the Start of the Swing — Nothing Else Matters

> In a choppy 2012-style market where very few setups pass the filter, the single superpower that separates traders from the crowd is buying at the start of a swing — and 95% of people never learn this.

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### 2012 All Over Again: Choppy, Selective, Up

> "This year reminds me of what happened in 2012 — ultimately an up year, but choppy, periods of selling. No real big buying, but still going up."

The regime: selective market, not broad-based. You must:
- Be quick taking profits
- Be very selective in what you buy
- Have lower profit expectations

### Range Expansion: The Unchanging Signal

The 20% study remained unchanged — stocks continue to move in the direction of range expansion:

> "Any range expansion, which has a good context, a known base, is what you want to focus on."

Key rules:
- Range expansion at the **start** of a swing — not the 3rd or 4th day
- Healthcare and Technology dominate the working breakouts
- 4% breakout continues as the unit that starts moves

> "Why does it continue to do the same thing? The stock did this same thing earlier also. Why the moves only last three to five days?"

### Three Sectors, Stories Over Earnings

The consistent structural edge:
- **Healthcare, Technology, Consumer Cyclical** — these three sectors make the biggest moves
- **Stories > real earnings** — stocks with narratives outperform stocks with fundamentals

## 10 AM Meeting (Trade Factory)

### Long Side: Mostly Gold, Mostly Choppy

Combination scan: 43 bullish, 58 bearish — bearish dominant.

Gold stocks dominated the bullish scan (AEM, Assanti Gold, nugget names), but Pradeep was uninterested:

> "Gold stocks — they are too volatile for my taste, so I stay away from them."

> "The Nugget has choppy track record — many times it has these kind of records and next day it just gaps down."

Only marginal possibilities were gold-related consolidation breakouts. Nothing to get excited about on the long side.

### Short Side: Low Volume, No Liquidity

Bearish scan yielded stocks with insufficient volume — Lyft, CRM, PayPal, Crowdstrike, Roku — none with enough liquidity to short:

> "Not enough liquidity to get excited about any of these on the short side."

### Anticipation: Nothing Remotely Close

> "There is nothing here which remotely looks like an anticipation setup. You need to see a series of narrow range days, something which is setting up nicely."

## 2 PM Meeting

### The One Superpower

The session's core teaching — repeated with increasing intensity:

> "There is one superpower in trading. You know what that is? To buy the move at the start of the move. That's a superpower. If you can do that, you'll be like Spider-Man."

> "That one thing is most difficult for people to understand. And it's amazing."

> "95% of trading people don't understand that one simple thing or they struggle with that all the time."

The principle: **Buy at the start of a swing, not after 3-4 days of movement.** This is the single most important differentiator.

### 9,000,000 With Story: The Most Powerful Setup

> "The most powerful setup is 9,000,000 with a story or with a theme. Because they work better than even the stocks which have actual real earnings."

> "That's the paradox of the market. Stories run bigger than stocks with real earnings and real sales."

> "Isn't it amazing that stocks with real earnings and sales are not even leading in this bull market?"

### Biotech Exception

> "Anytime there is a biotechnology breakout near start of a swing — that's an exception."

Biotech range expansions are treated as start-of-swing by default because capital raises create forced buying.

### Weak Structure Short Explained

> "Weak structure short — you need to have three candles in a row down, then one bounce, then it goes down. No waterfall."

A waterfall decline is the opposite — not a weak structure short.

### Sugar Baby Rule: Magnitude, Not Duration

> "You hold a sugar baby for a magnitude, not duration. You hold it for 40-50% more, not for longer. That's another superpower — understanding the difference between magnitude moves and duration moves."

> "It's like asking, should you hold your sugar babies longer than three to five days? It's like asking, should you marry your sugar baby?"

## Key Takeaways

1. **Buy at the start of the swing** — this is the one superpower that 95% of traders never learn
2. **Choppy 2012-style market** — selective, not broad-based; take profits fast, lower expectations
3. **Range expansion + start of swing** is the universal signal — 4% breakout remains the unit
4. **Gold stocks are too volatile** — Pradeep stays away from them entirely
5. **9,000,000 with a story > 9,000,000 with real earnings** — the paradox of the market
6. **Biotech breakouts get an exception** — any range expansion near start of swing is valid
7. **Sugar babies are held for magnitude (40-50%), not duration** — don't marry them
8. **Most scans turn up nothing** — in this market, doing nothing is better than forcing trades
9. **Weak structure short = 3 down candles + 1 bounce + continuation down** — not a waterfall
10. **If it looks too good to be true, check for a buyout** — Spirit AeroSystems lesson
