# Market Timing Is the Game: T2108 at 130 Signals Caution

> "T2108 was at 130 this morning. When readings are that extreme, the probability of a pullback is very high. Stocks breaking out are fading — a subtle distribution is happening behind the scenes. Pradeep is 100% cash in all 401k accounts. The easy money is done. Market timing is not impossible — it's a learnable skill. And the 20% study proves again: first legs from bottom, not third legs from the top."

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish but Fading — T2108 at 130

> "I'm short-term cautious. T2108 is close to 80, and the 20% study readings hit 130. Most breakouts near 52-week highs are failing. The ones working are starting from bottom — first legs."

The rally is broadening, but the broadening is coming from stocks near lows. Stocks near highs are stalling. Google breakout has no traction. Amazon tried and got hammered back. This is not the environment for extended stocks.

### Study 20%: Everything Starts From Bottom

> "Where are these stocks coming from? They're all coming from NOT near high. They're starting from correction."

The 20% study, especially H15 (15% movers in 10 days), proves that recent first-leg breakouts are where the money is. Relative strength stocks have already had third legs by now — buying them is for "fools."

> "Next year, I'm going to spend more time finding first legs rather than continuation setups. That's where the money is."

### 401k: 100% Cash Across All Accounts

All 401k accounts are now in cash. Invested since November 2 — one and a half months of extraordinary returns. "Too good to be true" money. He gets paid on closed profits, not open P&L. Take the payday and run.

> "In my entire career, I haven't seen this kind of return in 1.5 months. Not even in 2009. When it's too good to be true, you take it and run."

The 401k tracker spreadsheet is causing confusion — people see "100% invested" and jump in on December 1, completely missing the November 2 signal. 33 emails about 401k today, all from people who didn't follow the methodology.

### AMD: The Anticipation Setup That Worked

> "If you bought AMD at 3:58 PM (anticipation), and it went up $4 from your entry — with $1 risk, you made $4. That's not even a hold. Just 15-20 minutes."

But if you bought AMD the next morning, you're doing something else entirely. Context matters — 80/20 at 4:00 hour is different from 1-hour hold. And it was already a third leg — the picture-perfect setup was the anticipation entry at 3:58.

### Reflect on Your Mistakes

> "I learned so much about life by reflecting on 2023. It's been an epic year for me personally. If you don't reflect, you're not going to fix your problems in trading."

## 11 AM Meeting

### Trade Factory: Nothing Actionable

9M EP shows mostly extended stocks — NVDA, AMZN, GOOGL, AAPL, TSLA — all extended. Nothing actionable in the scans.

> "The fact that you don't see many setups in the scans itself is the indicator."

Three-four days ago, the market had two days of selling, then so many breakouts appeared — and they all worked (Sarah, CIPHER, etc.). Now, breakouts are fading. It's a self-correcting system. Have faith in your system.

### A Subtle Distribution Is Happening

> "Things are fading. There is a very subtle distribution going on behind the scenes. Stocks are breaking out and stalling out, or they are fading."

This is why Pradeep is not aggressive. When T2108 readings are at 130, probability of pullback is very high. Even though the market showed buying in the morning, there's no guarantee it holds.

### The Guy With 200 Stocks Down 70-90%

A local guy came to Pradeep's office with a portfolio of ~200 stocks, all bought at the top of the COVID bull market. Average stock down 70%, many down 90%. 26 pages of holdings. Mogo, Motors Holding, every COVID story stock.

> "Amazing skill to pick all the losers. He entered when the bull market was getting over. Every single stock bought at the wrong time."

Advice: sell everything, take the loss like a man, start over. But he can't bring himself to do it — "what if they come back?"

### Market Timing Is the Game

> "Market timing is very, very important to making money. Nothing was working until November 2. Everything started working from November 2. Same traders, same methodology, same everything."

The tools: T2108, Market Monitor, and 20% study. These are learnable skills. When SA says breakouts won't work, they won't work. When T2108 was below 20, that was the signal. Now at 130, it's time to be cautious.

> "Can you walk on a rope between two buildings in Manhattan? A common person cannot. Are there people who can? Yes. Market timing is learnable."

### First Leg Should Be 15%+

> "First leg should be explosive. 15% minimum. If the first leg is 15%, the second leg might be 8%. If the first leg is 8%, you make 4% with a 4% stop — that doesn't make sense."

Victoria's Secret model analogy — "have you seen a Victoria's Secret model with short legs?" First legs need long legs. Two Lynch needs long legs.

### Don't Trade Cup and Handle on SPY After 14% Rally

Someone pointed out a cup and handle on SPY:

> "Cup and handle on SPY after the market has made a 14% move up? That's the setup you want to trade? The simplest way to trade indexes is always buy weakness."
