## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### Market Context: Cautiously Bullish with Deteriorating Margins

The [[05-Wiki/StockBee/Market Monitor|market monitor]] had a 300+ red day yesterday. Primary and secondary indicators remained green but with **deteriorating margins** — a signal that the bullish regime is intact but fraying at the edges.

The [[05-Wiki/StockBee/20% Study|20% Study]] leaned bullish at roughly 2:1 ratio of bullish to bearish stocks. Major indices pulled back from record territory on renewed US-Iran hostilities and a surge in oil prices. Small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) underperformed. Crypto stocks closed green as [[BTC]] pushed above $80,000.

Pradeep's plan of action: **slow and deliberate risk-taking on A+ quality [[05-Wiki/StockBee/EP|EP 9M]] and [[05-Wiki/StockBee/DEP|DEP]] setups, selling into strength and peeling to account for anticipated volatility from headline risk.**

### Setup is Everything — The Foundational Argument

This was the dominant teaching of the morning. Pradeep stated it plainly:

> "90% of the people will blow up their account before they have a setup because that's something which takes time for people to understand that you cannot be a successful trader if you don't have a setup."

> "Without a setup, having situational awareness itself is not enough."

He had done **five one-on-one meetings** the previous day, and in every case the problem was the same: no setup.

> "It's like saying I want to find a girl, but I don't want to have a membership to Bumble or Hinge or I don't want to go into a Lululemon store. It's just not going to work."

> "I want to make $8,000 a day. I want all those hotties from Met Gala parading in front of me. But for that, I need a $10 billion Jeff Bezos body."

The point: **desire without a defined, repeatable setup is fantasy.** The process hierarchy is: **Situational Awareness → Setup → Process → Sell → Beliefs**. Setup is the starting point. Everything flows from it.

### Linearity: A Critical Setup Filter

Pradeep introduced **linearity** as a key quality to look for in setups:

> "Linearity — that's such an important concept. Look for more linear trends."

He contrasted [[FTNT]] (Fortinet) with smoother-trending names:

> "Buying stocks like Fortinet is like dating a Latina. You're going to have a lot of drama."

The principle: **stocks with linear, smooth uptrends are more tradeable than stocks with choppy, drama-filled charts.** If the chart looks like a roller coaster, it probably trades like one.

### The 20% Study: Pattern Recognition on the Market Level

Pradeep walked through the [[05-Wiki/StockBee/20% Study|20% Study]]:

> "After this big push, there was a small pullback. Or stalling action. This is consistent behavior of the market."

> "How many of them start with [[05-Wiki/StockBee/Momentum Burst|4% breakout]]? If they continue to start with 4% breakout, then you continue to focus on 4% breakout. If they tend to rally after earnings, then you focus on earnings or [[05-Wiki/StockBee/EP 9M|EP 9M]]."

The lesson: **use the 20% Study diagnostically** — not just as a sentiment indicator, but as a pattern-recognition tool that tells you which setup type is working *right now*.

### Anticipation Setups: If They Work, They Work Immediately

A key teaching on [[05-Wiki/StockBee/Anticipation|anticipation]] setups:

> "Anticipation — if they are going to go, they go immediately. Otherwise, they just sit there."

> "This is like Tinder dating. When you go on a Tinder date and if the kissing and petting doesn't start in 10-15 minutes, there is no point sitting in that Tinder date."

He reinforced with the [[CNC]] and [[SDLD]] examples — both worked immediately upon entry. When [[BKR]] didn't fire in the first 15-20 minutes, the setup was questionable.

> "If they don't fire in the first 15, 20 minutes, I'm out of them."

### What Is NOT a DEP

A member showed a chart claiming it was a DEP. Pradeep was emphatic:

> "That is not a DEP by any definition. It is a start of a swing, a high-price start of a swing, but it is not a DEP."

> "A DEP is a specific setup. There is a positive catalyst. Stock has pulled and consolidated."

The lesson: **calling something a DEP doesn't make it one.** A setup must meet specific criteria — catalyst, consolidation, volume confirmation. Without those, it's just a random chart pattern.

### Earnings: A Binary Event

When asked about holding through earnings:

> "Even if you promise me 100 of those Met Gala women, I would not do it. It's a binary event. It can go either way."

> "Even Goldman Sachs doesn't know what the reaction to the earnings will be."

He cited [[MU]] (Micron) — good earnings, stock tanked 31%. Now the same stock is being rewarded. **The market reaction to earnings is unpredictable. Never hold through.**

### The Fertility Ant: Life Lessons from Biology

In a characteristic analogy about risk/reward:

> "The male ant has a total lifespan of a few weeks. In that time, he's born, he meets the queen, he dies, and he never gets to see millions of his kids. Your only role in life is to screw the queen and die."

> "In a way, it's good because there is no child support. Imagine if you had to give child support. All day, he'll be working for DoorDash."

The point: **trading positions are like the male ant's mission — get in, do your job, get out. Don't overstay.**

### Fidelity: The Necessary Evil

Pradeep vented about trading through Fidelity:

> "Trading through Fidelity is like you're in a different world. It's not even funny."

> "Five times a day, it will log you out for inactivity. And when you want to put your order, you have to log back in."

> "It's good in a sense that you don't do crazy things. You're forced to be careful."

The silver lining: **friction in execution can prevent overtrading.** But it cost him on [[GOOG]] — he had a $20,000 profit that faded to breakeven because he couldn't execute fast enough.

### The Tight Pants Rule

To a member showing choppy, non-linear charts:

> "I'll send you a tight pant, and I want you to start wearing the tight pant every day. That way you'll remember what to look for. Tightness."

The metaphor: **look for tight price action before breakouts.** A loose, choppy consolidation is not a valid setup base. Tightness = low volatility before expansion = higher probability breakout.

> "If you're going to trade momentum burst or any setup, buy the tightest pants possible. So tight that you can't even zip it."

### The Cinderella Strategy

When asked about shorting stocks with negative earnings:

> "Find a post called 'The Cinderella Strategy.' Understand that — that is how the market operates."

> "Stocks top when their earnings are very good, not when their earnings are bad. By the time you research a -39% earnings drop, the stock is already down a lot because the market already knows."

> "You need to find a stock which has a very good earnings. That's when you short — at some stage, all these stocks making crazy moves are going to be short. When they start missing."

The principle: **stocks top on good news, not bad news.** The short side requires identifying "broken businesses" — companies where the fundamental deterioration is unfixable, not just a temporary miss.

### The 98-99 Parallel

Pradeep drew a striking parallel between the current AI/semi rally and the 1998-99 dot-com bubble:

> "These are all going into parabolic now. Go back to the market in '98, '99 and see what happened. This is the exact replica."

> "The only difference is: it's happening in weeks, not in months."

Stocks like [[COSO]] (cosmo.com) — making $300 moves in a day — echo the pet.com, Qualcomm era. The same pattern: **straight-up parabolic moves that eventually end in disaster.**

## PM Session (3 PM)

### One Movie in Town: AI/Semi is the Only Play

The afternoon reinforced the morning's theme with even more clarity:

> "There's only one movie running currently. You go to whichever theater you go, there's only one movie. That movie is semiconductor AI. I just don't see any other movie running."

> "Everything else is on a completely holiday."

[[AVGO]] ([[05-Wiki/StockBee/EP 9M|EP 9M]]), [[MRVL]], extended semi names — these were the only things working. Everything else: biotech, oil, financials — dead in the water.

> "The only thing the market likes currently is AI. Everything else is dead in water."

This is **situational awareness at its most distilled**: when only one sector is absorbing all capital, that's where you look. Period.

### Setup Definition: Non-Negotiable

The PM session doubled down on the morning's core message. A member asked about [[AAPL]]:

> "If Apple is a DEP, I'm telling you, I'm Brad Pitt. You can call anything a DEP. But doesn't make it so."

He then showed what a real DEP looks like — [[GLW]] — with a clear catalyst and high-volume breakout, contrasting it with AAPL which has no catalyst.

> "No setup understanding — that is the persistent, consistent problem."

He told the story of a member who lost money twice and pleaded for help:

> "I asked him, what are you doing? I was doing day trading. What is your setup? And then, after day trading didn't work, so I started doing swing trading. What is the setup? And then, hey, you know, when I started swing trading, my girlfriend went to Puerto Rico and met a guy. That's not setup."

> "If you cannot fix your setup problem, obviously your girlfriend is going to leave for some guy in Puerto Rico."

The message: **without a clearly defined setup, nothing else matters — not your indicators, not your TradingView scans, not your AI models.**

### Tightness: The Objective Criterion for Anticipation

Pradeep provided a **quantifiable definition** for [[05-Wiki/StockBee/Anticipation|anticipation]] setups:

> "The stock should be up or down less than 0.4%. That's tightness for me."

He demonstrated on [[CNC]]:
- Day 1: less than 1%
- Day 2: 0.16%
- Day 3: tight consolidation

Contrasted with a member's candidate:
- Day 1: 5%
- Day 2: 2.5%

> "So tight that two people are required to unzip it. That's when the setup works."

**Anticipation requires genuine tightness.** A 3.89% range day is not tight. A 0.4% range day is.

### DEP Catalyst Requirements: Reading X-Rays

For [[05-Wiki/StockBee/DEP|DEP]] on the short side, Pradeep stressed **catalyst analysis** using earnings X-rays:

> "For a short to work, you really need a catalyst. Because the market is a bullish Ponzi scheme."

He walked through examples:

| Ticker | Catalyst Quality | DEP Short Viable? |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| [[LULU]] | Missed earnings + broken business model | ✅ Yes — real catalyst |
| [[KMX]] | Catastrophic guidance (-24%, -26%) | ✅ Yes — unfixable |
| [[GIS]] | Negative growth rate | ✅ Yes — can't fix quickly |
| [[META]] | Investment in AI = negative reaction, but not a broken business | ❌ No |
| [[WGS]] | Growth slowdown, not catastrophic | ❌ Marginal — needs more time |
| [[SHOP]] | Softer Q2 guidance, not game-changing | ❌ No |

The principle for DEP shorts: **you need a broken business or a catastrophic catalyst.** "Softer guidance" or "market expectations were high" are not enough in a bull market. The market will forgive minor misses.

> "Look at hundreds of examples of X-rays like this, then you will see. When you see something like this, negative growth rate for four quarters — is that stock likely to go up? Or is it a broken business? It's going to take time to fix that."

### P/E Ratios: The Worst Way to Select Stocks

> "P/E is the worst way to look at stocks. If you make your decision on P/E for getting into [[05-Wiki/StockBee/EP 9M|EP 9M]], 90% of them you'll not get into. Because all growth stocks will have P/E in the stratosphere."

> "How would you trade something like [[OKLO]]? There are no earnings."

The lesson: **growth stock trading and value investing are different games.** Don't apply value metrics to momentum setups.

### Cutting Losses: The Gangrene Principle

A member asked about giving stocks "more room":

> "Small loss becomes big loss because I'm going to give it more room. That's what leads to doom. It's painful to take losses, but that first cut is better than a deep cut. Later, you have to cut your leg or gangrene inside."

> "They make money for one, two, three, four weeks, and then they get into a trade, it goes against them, and they say 'I'll give it more room.' Next, they end up giving up all the gains they made in the last three months."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Setup is the non-negotiable starting point.** Without a clearly defined setup — catalyst, volume, pattern, institutional quality — nothing else matters. 90% of traders blow up before they even have one.
2. **One movie in town.** When only one sector (AI/semi) is absorbing all capital, that's where you look. Don't fight it.
3. **Anticipation requires extreme tightness.** Pradeep's definition: stock up or down less than 0.4%. If it's choppy and loose, it's not an anticipation setup.
4. **DEP requires a game-changing catalyst.** "Softer guidance" or "high expectations" don't qualify. You need a broken business (short side) or outlier earnings (long side).
5. **Linearity matters.** Avoid stocks with choppy, drama-filled charts. Look for smooth, linear uptrends.
6. **The 20% Study is diagnostic.** It tells you which setup type is working right now — use it to focus, not just as a sentiment gauge.
7. **Earnings are binary events.** Never hold through earnings. Even Goldman Sachs doesn't know the market's reaction.
8. **The first cut is better than gangrene.** Cutting losses small prevents the cascade that wipes out months of gains.
9. **Stocks top on good news, not bad news.** The Cinderella Strategy: the best time to short is when a stock has had extraordinary earnings and the narrative is about to shift.
10. **P/E is the worst metric for growth stock setups.** Growth stocks will always look expensive. If you filter by P/E, you'll miss 90% of [[05-Wiki/StockBee/EP 9M|EP 9M]] signals.
11. **If they don't work immediately, they're probably not going to.** Especially for anticipation setups — Tinder dating rule.
12. **The 98-99 parallel is real.** Parabolic moves in weeks, not months. They end in disaster, but they can go further than you think.
