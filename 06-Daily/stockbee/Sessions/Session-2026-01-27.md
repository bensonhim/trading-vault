# Breakouts Fading, SIPs and Intraday Focus, and Organize Your Process

> "Most breakouts are not having traction. Buy a breakout, it goes up two days, then no third-day follow-through. When the market is challenging, you need a catalyst. Organize yourself — the headlines are free. Find the ideas on the front page."

## AM Session (9:15 AM)

### SA: Bullish but Choppy — Breakouts Fading

Nasty breakout fades yesterday. APLD faded. Many 9M breakdowns in the themes that dominated the New Year (space, rare earth). Breakouts not having traction — two days up, then no follow-through.

> "Considering this kind of action: no SIPs. Focus on SIPs and intraday. If there's a breakout, focus on 9M EP or DEP. Look for stock with reason to go up. When market overall is going up, any stock can go up. Now it's challenging — you need catalyst."

### Process: How to Find SIPs

Pradeep laid out his morning process:
1. **Check earnings reports** — stocks with earnings (after-hours releases, pre-market)
2. **Check briefing.com** — catalysts, upgrades, downgrades
3. **Check finance page headlines** — Bloomberg, CNBC front pages (free)
4. **Check SEC MRX** — analyst upgrades/downgrades
5. **Organize your watchlist** — put key stocks on your TV screen

> "You cannot miss a big move if you have a process. The headlines are free. You just need the tickers."

### Four Sugar Babies Maximum

> "The number of sugar babies you should have is four. If you start getting into eight, you're going to have a problem. There are only seven days in a week. Too hard to manage."

### Don't Trade Biotech as SIPs on Earnings Day

> "I decided not to trade biotech as SIPs on the day of earnings. If I had to look at NTLA, I'll look at it as a delayed reaction."
