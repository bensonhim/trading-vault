### Deep Teaching: 50% Moves and Persistent Selling

- Studied stocks that made 50% moves in 25 days
- **Key insight**: 50% up moves typically happen in 1-2 legs, rarely 3+
- "Three legs is a unicorn — two legs is the max"
- Range expansion ALWAYS at the start of a move — "not going to change"
- **50% down moves**: almost always show persistent selling at the beginning
- Persistent selling = 3-5 consecutive down days near highs = "something is wrong with this stock"
- Institutional selling visible through persistent selling pattern
- Weak structure short = variation to look for on downside
- "Currently I'm trading weak structure short"
- Study the 20% study daily to understand where moves start and how they develop
- Bottom bounce example: Hudson Chemical — good context, range expansion at start of swing
