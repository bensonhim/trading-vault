# Don't Arrive at the Party at 12:30 for the Cleaning

Tuesday reinforced the SIPs-first mindset with a flood of live examples — HP (short squeeze), Sanmina (AI magic word + short interest), BA short (catalyst independent of market direction), Cisco (neglected SIPs). The eureka moment theme continued: **creativity and insight matter more than discipline for developing traders.** Pradeep introduced Kevin Marder's "liquid glamour" concept (1000+ funds = institutional quality), and the number of funds matters more than percentage owned — consensus buying is what drives multi-month moves.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### Essay Critique: Being Bullish After the Fact Is Useless

> "You become bullish after the fact. Now if you become bullish, probably you're coming to the party to wash the dishes."

- AG's essay: "bullish but cautious" — same every day, reactive not proactive
- The 20% study told you to be aggressively bullish when breadth came in
- **Essay must reflect current conditions, not lag them**
- Breakouts worked because money came into the market (breadth thrust), not because the setups were great
- A shitty setup also works when money flows in — it's the market, not the setup

### 20% Study: Still Bullish, No Selling Pressure

- Only ~3 stocks down 20%+ (bearish side tiny)
- 425+ stocks up 50%+ (bullish side massive)
- 20% study is your "prayer" — read it daily

### SMCI: Don't Arrive Late to the Party

> "If the party starts at 9:00, you arrive at 12:30 for the cleaning purpose."

- SMCI already had 5-6 days of fund buying — the trade was ON the catalyst day
- Now it's just day trade territory until it sets up again
- Believes SMCI goes to $700-$1000 long-term, but **must wait for a setup to form**
- All the fund buying happened early — by the time it's reported, it's too late

### Sofi: The Dilution Trap (Continued)

- 950M+ share float — how? Constant insider dilution
- Management owns only 1% — distributed to retail "fools"
- Twitter hype = management wants to dump shares
- Good for day trade only, terrible for swing

### FOMC Wednesday

- Not going to say anything different, but can cause volatility and stop hits
- Be cautious ahead of FOMC

### BA Short: SIPs Works Independent of Market Direction

> "SIPs is a strategy which works independent of the market direction."

- BA withdrew FAA clearance — more plane problems = bearish catalyst
- Market was going up, BA went down — **catalyst overrides market direction**
- Institutions not panicking (didn't drop $10 as expected) = institutional support
- Category 2 SIPs — nobody paying attention, no fistfight to get in

### SIPs Categories: Crowded, Secondary, Tertiary

| Category | Risk | Return | Example |
|----------|------|--------|---------|
| Crowded | High (fistfight) | 25-50%+ in one day | SMCI, Sanmina |
| Secondary | Medium | 5-10% | BA, Bank of America |
| Tertiary | Low | 2-5% | Cisco, CVX |

- "Green is green" — $2K without stress > $20K with blood pressure meds
- Find your own area of the SIPs well — don't fight for crowded trades

### Short Interest: The Higher the Better

- 5% minimum to look at, but higher is better
- 100% short interest = "sell your wife, kids, everything"
- Days to cover also matters (HP = 8.6 days)
- But beware: market makers know high short interest stocks and can flip them

### News Sources for SIPs

- **Briefing.com** = quality SIPs (institutional grade)
- **The Fly** = finds small caps like PXLW that Briefing.com misses
- **Seeking Alpha** = conference call transcripts (PK recommends)
- **FinViz** = free, has earnings info
- The information is freely available — it's the methodology that matters

### Info Sources: Free vs Paid

> "The information about earnings is freely available everywhere. It's the methodology and how to convert that into money — that's the important part."

- Don't need $10K subscriptions
- Creativity and hustle matter more than tools
- The methodology is what separates profitable traders from information collectors

## PM Meeting — Institutional Quality + Delayed Reaction + Anticipation

### ⚡ Kevin Marder's "Liquid Glamour" Concept (1000+ Funds)

> "If you want to see a stock which is really going to have a multi-month, multi-year kind of move, look for a stock which surpasses from below 1,000 funds to 1,000+ funds."

- **Kevin Marder** (deceased) — IBD trader, member of StockBee, ran Marder Investment Advisory
- His insight: **number of funds matters more than percentage owned**
- One institution holding 40-50% = nothing (maybe IPO allocation)
- Multiple funds buying = **consensus buying** = rocket fuel for the stock
- SMCI: funds jumped from 597 → 767 → 1000+ — that's where the move came from
- The data is expensive (WONDA = Bloomberg-level cost) and lagging, but when you see it, it's gold

### High-Priced Stocks: Hidden Alpha

- AutoZone, Chipotle, O'Reilly — small position, big dollar moves
- $72 on 50 shares of AutoZone = real money
- "Nobody wants these" = less competition = edge
- **O'Reilly**: $7.50 → $14 in one day
- High-priced stocks = another SIPs well to draw from

### Delayed Reaction: Proactive vs Reactive

> "The best bang for buck on delayed reaction is by being more proactive."

- Reactive delayed reaction = still profitable but lower risk/reward
- Proactive = identifying setups before they trigger
- BA = perfect example: catalyst identified before market open, entered proactively
- 2-3 delayed reaction trades per week is enough — low risk, good reward
- Fits with FFM philosophy

### Cisco: The Neglected SIPs

- Earnings not glamorous but not bad
- Neglected for 2 months before the move
- 4 days short interest = potential squeeze
- Buyback announced = catalyst
- Category 3 SIPs — slow mover, low risk, "nobody pays attention"

### BA Short: Context Matters for Shorts Too

- Withdrew FAA clearance = negative catalyst
- Even in a bullish market, BA went down
- SIPs works both directions — catalyst determines direction, not market
- But institutions didn't panic (only down $5.50, not $10) = institutional support underneath
- Closed the trade before earnings — don't hold shorts over catalyst events

### FOMC Caution

- No anticipation setups ahead of FOMC
- "Unless you have something screaming, be careful buying in front of the handsome man's guitar show"
- Swing positions kept with stops, no new big positions

### HP SIPs Breakdown (Repeated for Emphasis)
