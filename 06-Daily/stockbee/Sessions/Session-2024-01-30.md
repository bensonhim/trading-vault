# Late to the Party, SIPs Only Working, and the Creativity vs. Discipline Debate

> "If you become bullish after the fact, you're coming to the party at 12:30 to wash the dishes. The 20% study told you to be bullish when it went down — that was the time to be aggressive."

Tuesday. Market bullish but extended after 1.5 weeks of gains. SIPs is the only thing working — classical breakouts failing, shorts not working. Pradeep challenges a member's essay for being late to bullish. The day features a deep debate on creativity vs. discipline, the Kevin Marder liquid glamour concept, and BA as a textbook short SIPs play.

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Essay Critique: Late to Bullish

- Member's essay: "bullish long-term but cautious short-term" — same thing every day
- Pradeep: "you become bullish after the fact" — 20% study signaled bullish when it went down
- Being bullish now means "coming to the party at 12:30 to wash the dishes"
- SA should drive sizing: aggressive when signals align, cautious when they don't

### Setup vs. Market Context: Which Drove the Trades?

- Yesterday's reversal trades worked because money came into the market, not because setups were great
- Take the exact same setup without the breadth thrust → they fail
- "Is it the setup, or is it the breadth thrust which animated the setup?"
- SA tells you WHERE to deploy energy, not just WHETHER to be bullish

### Bullish Thesis Remains, But Selective

- Bullish based on data: selling never had urgency, mid-cycle correction in bull market
- Small caps getting buying now
- FOMC tomorrow — volatile, stops may get hit then market goes up
- Range-bound action likely through July/August, then Santa Claus rally

### SMCI: Wait for Setup, Don't Chase

- Six days of fund buying already done — setup was earlier, not now
- SMCI likely to go to $700-$1000 eventually, but MUST wait for setup to form
- "Buy a setup, wait for a setup to form"

### 4% Breakout Study

- New setup idea: 4% breakout where no 4% breakout happened 10 days before
- Keen observation of these setups — creativity, not just blind study

### Creativity vs. Discipline: The Fundamental Debate

> "If you dedicate your life to discipline, you're going to have the most boring life and never make it big. Almost every trader who made it big found his own unique setup."

- Newbies need discipline, but what they really need is **creativity**
- Mark Minervini didn't become successful through discipline alone — he was creative and found VCP pattern
- Self-appointed experts saying "discipline is key" are wrong for this stage
- The SIPs focus this year is meant to trigger Eureka moments

### SIPs: The Only Thing Working

- SIPs producing one-day moves on earnings catalysts
- Most breakouts: hit and miss, sitting there after breakout
- Shorts: still not working even with catalysts
- SIPs with catalyst = the strategy for this market

## 2 PM Meeting — Kevin Marder's Liquid Glamour, Hidden Alpha in High-Priced Stocks

### Kevin Marder's 1000+ Funds Concept

> "When a stock surpasses from below 1000 funds to 1000+, that means the market has discovered something. That's rocket fuel."

- Kevin Marder (IBD trader, StockBee member, died last year) developed this concept
- **Number of funds matters more than percentage owned**
- One institution holding 50% = IPO allocation, not conviction
- Multiple funds buying = **consensus buying** = institutional quality
- [[SMCI]]: funds jumped from 597 to 1000+ — that's where the fuel comes from
- Wonda database (expensive) has this data; MarketSmith shows it partially
