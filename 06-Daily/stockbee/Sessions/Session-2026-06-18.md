# Situational Awareness & Setup Families — Choppy Market Discipline

A choppy, "dead by thousand cuts" market demands radical selectivity — only trade setups with absolute conviction, avoid extended third- and fourth-leg moves, and let situational awareness (the OODA loop from aviation) dictate every decision. Pradeep introduced the full framework of setup families (EP, DEP, Sugar Babies, Anticipation, Reversal) and explained why EP 9M is specifically for non-gap stocks, why high-priced anticipation has the best risk/reward, and why reversal bullish works in any market regime.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness — The OODA Loop

Pradeep opened with an extended analogy from aviation. Situational awareness (SA) originated in the US Air Force during the Vietnam War, when pilots were losing planes because they lost awareness of their environment. The solution was the **OODA loop**: Observe → Orient → Decide → Act. At FedEx, every flight began with a dispatch briefing — weather, cargo, alternate airports. As a trader, losing SA is the equivalent of crashing a plane:

> "The moment you lose your situational awareness, what happens? Your plane crashes. And crashing a plane is a very painful experience."

SA is **setup-specific** — long-term traders don't need it, but the more short-term you are, the higher-order SA skill you need. Most traders who blow up lost SA first, then kept trading when the market changed.

### Market Classification: Dead by Thousand Cuts

The market had transitioned from a smooth uptrend into a choppy, wide range. Gap ups are tempting but become trapped:

> "Breakouts are going to look very attractive. You buy them. They're going to fade by the end of the day."

Pradeep classified the current environment as a **"dead by thousand cuts"** market — where breakout after breakout fails, slowly bleeding your account. The prescription:

1. **Be highly selective** — only trade setups with an absolute reason to go up
2. **Avoid extended moves** at all costs
3. **If you can't answer with absolute certainty, don't trade** — go to SIPs or sit on hands

### The Cougar Metaphor — Chasing Extended Moves

> "You want to be a cougar? You want to be like Madonna? You're trading or going after old extended moves. Then you're complaining that this doesn't work, that doesn't work. If you're buying third, fourth leg of a move and getting stopped out, it is not the problem of the setup. It is the problem of you being addicted."

He compared chasing extended stocks to watching "Canadian videos" (adult content) — the excitement isn't real, and in actual trading life, it doesn't work.

### Reversal Bullish — Works in Any Market

In a choppy environment, **reversal bullish** is the one setup that works across conditions:

> "Reversal bullish works in any market condition. Please write it down."

It's a small, short-term trade — buy at ~$3.58 near the close (the "golden time" of 3:50 PM), sell on the next morning's gap up. Most reversal bullish trades don't make swing moves; they're pre-money trades. Take 80% off the table immediately, keep 10-20% as a runner.

### Intel Trade — Discretionary Execution

Pradeep took a small [[INTC]] position on Trump rumor catalyst. He managed it with aggressive stop movement:

> "If it has to work, it should not come down below $1.33. If it comes down below $1.33, it is the end."

He moved his stop tighter as the trade worked, taking incremental profits — a pure discretionary approach. He emphasized he's not a systematic trader: "Don't make too many rules."

### Shorting Dangers — The BTRT Crowd

Pradeep warned about shorting in this environment. When you short early, the **BTRT (Buy The Rip) crowd** comes in rapidly, causing violent squeezes:

> "The buying comes in so rapidly that you just get steamrolled. You don't even know what hit you. Before that, you'll have an $8,000–$3,000 loss. And stops don't work the same way they work on the long side."

Friday reversals are especially furious for shorts.

### Newsletter Front-Running

An edge: understand what popular newsletter writers scan for overnight, and position before their followers buy in pre-market. The reversal bullish setup exploits this — you buy before the newsletter crowd arrives, and sell to them at the open.

### Smith & Wesson — Profit Management

A member ([[SWBI]]) had a 22-26% gain on an earnings play. Pradeep's advice: take 80% off immediately. Don't wait for a swing when the market is choppy. Keep 10-20% as a runner with a stop.

### Don't Need 26 Exit Rules

> "The very fact that you need 26 rules for exiting a trade means you don't understand trading."

Keep it simple. Discretionary trading requires judgment, not rulebooks.

### Market Scan Results — Nothing Compelling

After scanning combination bullish, EP 9M, and other scans, Pradeep found very few actionable setups. Most were continuation setups or SOS entries in a market where breakouts are failing. Only [[INTC]] (catalyst-based) and [[MRVL]] (EP with catalyst) were mildly interesting, but even those were questionable given the choppy SA.

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### Setup Families — The Coca-Cola Framework

Pradeep laid out his complete setup architecture, comparing it to Coca-Cola having multiple flavors:

| Setup Family | Purpose | Variants |
|---|---|---|
| **EP** | Home runs, mini home runs | EP 9M, Sugar Babies |
| **DEP** (Delayed EP) | Post-catalyst pullback | Institutional quality required |
| **ANT** (Anticipation) | Tight days before breakout | ANT 15, ANT 60+, ANT bullish |
| **Reversal** | Exhaustion plays | Reversal bullish, reversal bearish |
| **SOS** (Start of Swing) | Breakout on volume | Foundation of all setups |

Three pillars underpin everything: **SOS** (know what it is), **Linearity** (trade smooth trends, not choppy messes), and **Magnitude over Duration** (seek big moves, not long holds).

> "Magnitude versus duration — this is very, very important. If you understand this, you can say a lot of things in your life."

### Linearity — The Dating Analogy

> "It's like dating people who are well put together, whose hair is in place. San Francisco techies have such a hard time finding girlfriends — have you looked at any techie? They have money to buy tokens on Cloud, but their hair is somewhere, some t-shirt going like this. Who's going to date you if you have zero linearity in your life?"

Same with stocks — prefer smooth, linear trends over choppy, unpredictable ones.

### EP 9M — Specifically for Non-Gap Stocks

A critical nuance Pradeep emphasized: **EP 9M is designed to find non-gap EPs**. If a stock is gapping up on a known catalyst, you should have already bought it via NTRT/MTRT (morning news scans). EP 9M catches what you missed:

> "The whole idea of EP 9M is to focus on non-gap stocks. If something has a catalyst in the morning and which is already known to you, you'd have bought based on that catalyst. If you're buying based on EP 9M now, you're late."

[[BFLY]] (BlueFly) was a perfect example — it was an EP with a clear AI catalyst, not an EP 9M. Buying it via EP 9M scan means you missed the morning catalyst entry.

> "It's the difference between genuine Italian food and food you get at Olive Garden."

### DEP — Institutional Quality Required

[[OCC]] was not in the DEP watchlist because it doesn't meet the **institutional quality** criterion. DEP requires fund-quality, institutional-grade stocks — not speculative or low-quality names.

### Catalyst Freshness — The Marriage Analogy

> "In the first one or two years of marriage, there's a lot of romance, a lot of sex, a lot of electricity. After five or ten years? You're making Costco runs and the damn kids have come in."

Catalysts work the same way — **if a catalyst is going to work, it works immediately**. The longer it takes, the less urgency there is. [[OKTA]] was highlighted as taking too long — "What is taking you so long to come?" means there's no urgency to buy.

### High-Priced Anticipation — Best Risk/Reward

Within the ANT family, **high-priced stocks ($60+) have the best risk/reward** for anticipation setups. A 30¢ risk on a $300 stock can yield a $30+ move — that's a 100:1 potential reward. Pradeep's deep dive confirmed: all stocks making 50%+ moves trade >1M shares in volume. No need to fish in illiquid waters.

### Don't Buy at the 3 PM Meeting

> "If you're coming into the 3 PM meeting and buying breakouts, you are doing it wrong. Most breakouts happen in the morning. The 3 PM meeting is for review, not for buying."

Front-load your effort in the first hour. Breakouts that happen early give you a buffer by end of day.

### Clarity — The Single Most Important Thing

> "The single most important thing in trading is mind clarity. If you see a continuation setup and instantly know 'this is what I should do,' you're home. Strive every day for clarity."

But clarity takes time and repetition. Pradeep emphasized that it takes 32 hearings to start paying attention, and another 32 to start comprehending. If you keep changing styles, every new approach requires 64 attempts to master.

### SA Remains Choppy

Pradeep's SA didn't change from morning to afternoon — market is still choppy, breakouts still likely to fail. Only semiconductor/AI themes have conviction due to catalyst power.

> "My SA remains the same. Unless something is compelling — the semiconductors, AI-related — I'm not really going for just normal breakouts. They may or may not work."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Situational awareness is non-negotiable** — OODA loop (Observe, Orient, Decide, Act) applies to every trade decision. Losing SA = plane crash.
2. **Choppy markets require radical selectivity** — "dead by thousand cuts" means breakouts look attractive but fade. Only trade setups with absolute conviction.
3. **Never chase extended moves** — third, fourth, fifth legs are cougars. Buy the first or second leg, not the exhausted ones.
4. **Reversal bullish works in any market** — buy near 3:50 PM, sell the morning gap up. Take 80% off the table immediately.
5. **EP 9M is for non-gap stocks only** — if a stock is gapping on a known catalyst, buy it on the catalyst, not the EP 9M scan.
6. **DEP requires institutional quality** — not every pullback is a DEP. Fund quality matters.
7. **Catalysts decay like marriages** — if it's going to work, it works immediately. No urgency = no trade.
8. **High-priced anticipation has the best risk/reward** — 30¢ risk on a $300 stock can yield $30+. Deep dive proves all 50%+ movers trade >1M shares.
9. **Clarity is the single most important thing** — know your setups instantly. It takes 64 attempts (32 to notice + 32 to comprehend) to internalize a concept.
10. **Don't buy at the 3 PM meeting** — front-load your work in the first hour. 3 PM is for review.
11. **Shorting in choppy markets is dangerous** — BTRT crowd creates violent squeezes. Stops don't work the same on the short side.
12. **Keep it simple** — 26 exit rules means you don't understand trading. Discretionary judgment > rulebook.
13. **Liquidity filter** — stocks making 50%+ moves all trade >1M shares. Don't waste time on illiquid names.
14. **Front-run newsletter writers** — understand what they scan for, buy before their followers arrive at the open.
