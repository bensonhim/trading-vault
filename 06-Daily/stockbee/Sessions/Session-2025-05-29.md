### Key Teaching: EP Is a Tactic, Not a Setup

- "Be first, be smart, or cheat — it's a hell of a lot easier to be first"
- EP (Early Price) = tactic for low-risk entry, NOT a setup itself
- If breakouts aren't working, no reason to enter just because EP looks good
- EP = like getting good seats at a stadium by arriving early (no assigned seats)
- EP = like getting first pick at a buffet
- "If you can't get EP, enter at 11AM, 12PM, whenever — as long as there's a setup"
- Setup is the foundation; EP is just the timing tactic

### Key Teaching: Delayed Reaction Requires Real Catalyst

- **Don't do delayed reaction on random stocks without catalyst**
- Abercrombie & Fitch: no catalyst, guided down, 4% growth — NOT a delayed reaction candidate
- Advance Auto Parts: earnings "good" but no real sales growth catalyst — what catalyst?
- NVTS worked as delayed reaction because: (1) Nvidia catalyst, (2) stock reacted positively on catalyst day, (3) highest volume
- Netflix delayed reaction: clear earnings catalyst
- HIMS delayed reaction: mountainside catalyst tied to NVIDIA
- SMR delayed reaction: bought because it reacted positively to earnings catalyst
- "Just because a stock goes up on earnings day doesn't mean I'm interested. I want a clear catalyst."
- "If there's no catalyst, you're just doing pattern-based trading."

### Key Teaching: Late-Stage Breakouts = Liquidation Events

- SPY made range expansion yesterday → already had 2 legs up
- **Breakout becomes a reason for dumping positions** — institutions sell into strength
- Late-stage breakouts (phase 2 moves) fail because the stock already made its move
- "Those late legs look the most attractive. That's the trap."
- Advance Auto Parts: example of late-stage breakout used for liquidation
- CRWV: parabolic move, already 196% up, $60B cap — **last buyer problem**
- "Why do these things work? The last buyer buys, and then there's nobody left to buy."

### Key Teaching: Market Conditions Dictate Profit-Taking

- **In this market: take profit on day 2** (not day 3-5)
- In bull market: take profit on day 3-5
- Sometimes take profit on day 1 (like NVTS 80% move)
- BULL example: had profit on day 2, didn't take it, **got stopped out on day 3**
- "Momentum burst: if not moving by day 3, it's likely going nowhere."
- "Tinder dating analogy: if the action doesn't start in 30 minutes, you're wasting your time."

### BULL: Stopped Out
