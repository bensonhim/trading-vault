# Choppy Markets Demand Framework Thinking — Then Range Expansion Changes Everything

**One-sentence overview**: The morning framed a choppy, transition market where breakouts lack edge and only EP 9M breakdowns and SIPs work — but by afternoon, a geopolitical catalyst (Iran/oil) triggered range expansion across indexes, printing EP 9M setups in airlines, cruises, and financials, shifting Pradeep's stance from cautious to cautiously bullish.

---

## AM Session — Framework Thinking & the Choppy Regime

### 1. Market Condition: Transitioning to Chop

Breadth has been deteriorating for 10+ consecutive days — net selling dominates. The 20% study leans bearish (2:1 bearish-to-bullish), and the quarterly 25% and 13% indicators have turned negative.

> "Inherently, there is a small edge in breakout trading. When you buy a breakout, most of the time, there is a small edge. But under the right condition, it magnifies dramatically."

Pradeep's framework identifies **five market regimes** and requires you to know which one you're in:

| Regime | What Works | What Doesn't |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Trending** | Breakouts, SOS, continuation | Shorts, mean reversion |
| **Choppy (current AM)** | EP 9M breakdowns, SIPs, reversal bullish (anticipation only) | Standard breakouts, SOS |
| **Tight range continuation** | ANT, tight-range breakouts | Extended breakouts |
| **Topping** | WSS, shorts | Long breakouts |
| **Extreme oversold** | Breadth thrust buys (no confirmation needed) | — |

> "Trick is only trade when the edge works. And this took me first three years after finding a set of idea of breakouts to figure this out."

### 2. Breakouts Have No Edge Right Now

The 20% study shows EP 9M **breakdowns** having follow-through, not breakouts. Even good-looking breakouts appear in a choppy market but fail.

> "Even in a bad market, even in a choppy condition, a good breakout is going to look like a good breakout. But then the ability to understand — that means hindsight is something which you develop as you age."

Key observation: After a strong trending run, the market needs consolidation. Buying breakouts "gun blazing" during a choppy transition guarantees drawdowns.

### 3. What Works: SIPs, EP 9M Breakdowns, Shots (Shorts)

> "Shots is the game here."

- **SIPs** — Day-trading strategy that works independent of market conditions.
- **EP 9M breakdowns** — Tesla's EP 9M breakdown worked well for three days.
- **Reversal bullish** — Only if bought on the day (anticipation), NOT next day.

### 4. Reversal Bullish: The Trade Was Yesterday, Not Today

Tesla's reversal bullish on the prior day gapped up this morning. The edge in reversal bullish is **anticipation entry** — buying on the day of the reversal with the intention it gaps up next day.

> "If I have to do this, I have to buy it on that day, like, in anticipation, not next day. There is just no edge buying today."

> "The whole idea of buying that reversal bullish is with the intention that it will gap up."

Practical constraint: If your job prevents intraday trading, **don't trade reversal bullish at all** — find setups that fit your constraints.

### 5. Albert Bandura, Social Learning, and Audacity

Pradeep references **Albert Bandura's social learning theory** (Bobo doll experiment): you don't need to reinvent every lesson — learn by observing others.

> "Don't reinvent your own setup. Don't reinvent your own situational awareness."

But Bandura's theory of success adds two more elements: **self-efficacy belief** and **chance encounter**. Pradeep adds a third:

> "The most important virtue in life is audacity. If you don't have audacity, nothing happens in life."

Examples of concentrated audacity:

| Trader | Concentration | Outcome |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| [[Warren Buffett]] | ~70% in American Express after scandal | Career-defining win |
| [[Mark Minervini]] | 140-150% in one restaurant stock | Changed career trajectory |
| [[Dan Zanger]] | 150-200% in small caps | Legendary returns |
| [[Ron Baron]] | ~80% in Tesla (Baron Capital) | Massive long-term gains |

> "Now he says you should not put more than 8% in one trade. But if you put only 8% in one trade, can you become the next Mark Minervini?"

### 6. SMCI: Valid Setup, Personal Pass

SMCI has a valid EP 9M/undercut-and-rally setup, but Pradeep avoids it due to fraud history and external overhang. A setup can be valid and still not worth trading based on integrity/overhang concerns.

### 7. AMD: The Only Viable Long Setup

[[AMD]] is the only EP 9M worth considering — clear catalyst (earnings), liquid lava (institutional quality), but with a major wildcard: NVIDIA earnings after the close could make it "hero or zero."

> "It's a liquid lava kind of setup."

### 8. Manish, Slater Nine, and Evolution of Style

Day trader Manish's style evolved through social learning — he originally traded biotechs, adopted [[Slater Nine]]'s pitchfork/Fibonacci approach, then refined it into his own mean-reversion system. Pradeep cautions: Manish's style is **intuition-driven and difficult to replicate** because much of it is implicit stock selection from consuming 500+ posts daily.

> "By the time you are getting into it, he's taking profit. He's trimming."

## PM Session — Range Expansion Changes the Game

### 9. Cautiously Bullish: Range Expansion Day

By the afternoon, the picture shifted dramatically. Indexes showed **range expansion**: [[IWM]], [[TNA]], [[SPY]], [[QQQ]] all expanding range. The 20% study showed **1,000+ bullish breakouts**, breadth turning positive.

> "We are back in business."

> "My situational awareness now is cautiously bullish because I see range expansion on the indexes."

But: this may be a **2-3 day rally**, not necessarily a trend resumption. NVIDIA earnings after close remains a wildcard.

### 11. Sector Rotation: Semis Step Aside

The critical signal: EP 9M setups are **not dominated by semiconductors**. Money is rotating into airlines, cruises, financials, and energy. This broadening of participation is bullish.

Semiconductor stocks may go range-bound around NVIDIA earnings, but the **non-semi sectors are where the opportunity is**.

### 12. Housing Deregulation: No EP 9M = No Real Catalyst

Pradeep dismisses housing deregulation plays (DR Horton, Lennar, etc.) — housing's problem isn't regulation, it's **affordability and mortgage rates**. Without EP 9M volume, it's not a game-changing catalyst.

> "If there was a game-changing catalyst, you'll get EP 9M today. These stocks are having EP 9M on the policy change. The market is telling you this policy change, the market is really excited about."

### 13. Reversal Bullish: Nothing Compelling

Most reversal bullish setups from the prior day have already broken out. Nothing compelling like Tesla's yesterday. Snap, Target, TTD, Carvana — all unconvincing.

### 14. Position Sizing & Conviction

Different traders have different approaches:

| Style | Position Size | Timeframe | Example |
|-------|--------------|-----------|---------|
| Investor | 1% per position | 3+ years | Ron Baron (Tesla) |
| Swing trader (concentrated) | 70-80% per position | Days-weeks | Pradeep |

> "If I want to get into AMD, I want to put 70, 80, 100% of my account."

Key principle: **your position size must match your objective and risk tolerance**.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Framework thinking is non-negotiable** — You must know which market regime you're in (trending, choppy, tight range, topping, extreme oversold) before selecting setups.
2. **The edge in breakout trading is inherently small** — It only magnifies under the right conditions. In a choppy regime, breakouts fail; EP 9M breakdowns and SIPs are the play.
3. **Reversal bullish = anticipation trade only** — The edge is buying on the day, not next day. If you can't trade intraday, skip this setup.
4. **Situational awareness shifts intraday** — Morning was choppy/cautious; afternoon brought range expansion and EP 9M setups. Adapt in real-time.
5. **Sector rotation is the tell** — When EP 9M setups are dominated by non-semi sectors (airlines, cruises, financials), money is rotating. That's bullish breadth.
6. **Government policy change = EP 9M catalyst** — Iran/oil policy drove airlines, cruises, and energy. Look for volume confirmation (9M+) as proof.
7. **Audacity + self-efficacy + chance encounter = success** — Bandura's theory plus Pradeep's addition. All great traders made concentrated bets at key moments.
8. **Don't reinvent setups** — Learn from the community (SIPs, EP 9M, DEP are developed frameworks). Social learning works.
9. **NVIDIA earnings = binary wildcard** — Any semi-related position (AMD, SMCI) carries event risk. Size accordingly.
10. **Housing deregulation without EP 9M = noise** — If the catalyst were truly game-changing, volume would confirm it. It didn't.
