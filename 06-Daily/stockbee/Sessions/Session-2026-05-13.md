# Extended Market, New Merchandise Needed

The market is extended after a marathon run — most AI stocks are overextended and pulling back, the 20% study shows over 100 bullish stocks (overheating), and fresh setups are scarce. Pradeep's stance: **sit on hands, wait for new merchandise, focus only on EP 9M and DEP setups with genuine catalysts.** NBIS emerges as the single stock with a real catalyst and growth projections worth watching.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Market Condition: Cautiously Bullish but Extended

Market monitor had a second red day. Primary and secondary indicators remain green but with deteriorating margins. The 20% study shows overheating with over 100 bullish stocks. Major indices pulled back from all-time highs on oil-driven inflation concerns and rising yields after the April CPI data. Small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) underperformed. Crypto closed red with [[BTC]] below $80K.

> "My plan of action remains slow and deliberate risk taking on A+ quality EP 9M and delayed reaction setups, selling into strength and peeling to account for anticipated volatility from headline risk."

The picture will become clear in the next 2–3 days. Most AI stocks are extended — either they pull back or go sideways. Every professional trader is expecting a pullback or consolidation.

### Focus: Fresh, Non-Extended Moves Only

> "Unless you find something which is not extended, which is starting a fresh move, like, you don't have anything right there."

> "New merchandise is needed. There's a lot of extended merchandise, but new merchandise is needed."

Primary focus: **EP 9M**. Secondary focus: **continuation**. SIPs are a fallback but carry danger — they can become a habit. SIPs is primarily a data-reading strategy.

### Earnings Methodology: 39%+ Revenue Growth

The single number Pradeep cares about on the earnings table: **39%+ year-over-year sales growth AND projected sales growth of 39%+.** Anything below that threshold can make moves, but you don't want to go there.

> "If you're asking at 10:00 in the morning, I have a problem. That means you are not really in a setup for NTRT, MTRT. You're just winging it, or you're dependent on somebody else to point out things for you."

### Three Things the Market Rewards

Pradeep identified three categories that drive the biggest moves:

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Growth** | Real revenue acceleration | [[NBIS]], [[UEC]] |
| **Stories** | Themes that excite the market, even if you don't understand the tech | Space (Rocket Lab), Quantum, AI |
| **Turnarounds** | Companies reversing from deep declines | Where biggest moves come from |

> "The biggest winners are going to come from stories. Completely stories."

The analogy: if a company called BEER launches a beer using AI with quantum health benefits and Elon Musk asked them to brew on Mars — that stock doubles or triples. The story is what attracts capital.

### Neglect = Explosiveness

> "Neglect is so important for our explosiveness. This was neglected — sideways for almost 3.5 years. That's why it came out of the gate 44–45% in two days."

Stocks that have been neglected and sideways for extended periods are the ones that explode when a catalyst arrives. Extended stocks that already ran don't offer the same opportunity.

### Sellers in Control

By mid-morning, sellers had taken control of the tape. Gap-ups were fading, semis under pressure, and the market tone was deteriorating. Pradeep's situational awareness said: **wait and watch.**

> "Resisting the urge is the most difficult part of this game."

> "A restless soul wants to do something."

> "I don't have itchy fingers."

### Process: Organize Like Crazy

Morning routine:
1. Look at the **earnings table** — focus on 39%+ revenue growth and projections
2. Bake tea, go through **every single stock** gapping up
3. Research catalysts — understand the story behind each name
4. Use **NTRT / MTRT** scans to find EP candidates pre-market
5. Update the **long-term file** with markers (like ★★★★★ for NBIS)

> "If you have a process, you can find things. If you don't have a process, you're always going to get surprised."

### Catalyst, Timing, and Art

Even with perfect catalyst identification, timing is everything. [[AMD]] is a case study: catalyst was clear, but three consecutive stop-outs before the move. [[SNDK]] had a valid catalyst that did nothing for two months before suddenly exploding.

> "It's an art also. And it's not a perfect science. If it were just perfect science, AI would be doing all these stocks and minting money."

Research stocks, keep a watchlist, try multiple times, and stay on top of them. Mark important catalysts so when they appear in scans, you already know the story.

## PM Meeting (3:00 PM)

### NBIS: The Only Real Catalyst

By the afternoon, Pradeep had taken a **small position in NBIS** but not a large one. The conviction was there on catalyst, but the market extension tempered the sizing.

> "NBIS is the only thing which has a catalyst, and then potential to make 40–50% more. But I would have had a higher confidence if I had seen a volume which was greater than this."

On NBIS being 19% up: the gap was 13%, so the 19% is not really relevant. If you're buying on EP 9M, the gap-up is expected — what matters is what happens after.

### Delayed Reaction: Both Ways

> "Sometimes you wait for a delayed reaction and they just go. So it's both ways."

The risk of waiting for a DEP entry: the stock may never pull back and you miss the move entirely. The risk of buying immediately: you may be buying an extended move that fades.

### Cat, Dog, or Liquid Lava?

When an EP 9M fires, the first question is classification:

| Category | Meaning | Action |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Cat** | Institutional quality, widely held, stable | Can trade with large size |
| **Dog** | Speculative, low quality, risky | Avoid or tiny size |
| **Liquid Lava** | Mega-cap, highly liquid, slow-moving | Good for large size but smaller moves |

> "Just because it's EP 9M, it's not a buy. There has to be a reason, some qualification which you need to use in order to decide. A lot of these EP 9Ms are just going to be one day."

Every EP 9M candidate must pass catalyst qualification: what is the story? Is it a game-changer or just noise? Did the stock already make a big move in anticipation?

### Random Questions = Random Results

Pradeep pushed back hard on members asking about random stocks without doing their own research:

| Random Question | Pradeep's Analogy |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| "What do you think of Mobileye?" | "What do you think of Kim Kardashian's attempt at becoming a lawyer?" |
| Asking about unqualified setups | "Who was the president who died after eating cherries?" (Zachary Taylor) |

The core message: **have a setup with clear qualifiers, or you're just gambling.**

> "If you're just doing random things, then random things will happen. And I have no idea why you people get excited about certain stocks."

### Insider Buying Is Not a Signal

[[TTD]]: $148M insider buy → stock down 35% since.
[[COUP]]: $100M insider buy → stock down 21%.
Asana's founder has been buying since way up → stock down 93%.

> "Insider buying is not really reliable. It's not even something which is a reliable setup idea."

### Position Sizing Philosophy

Two approaches to trading:

| Approach | Position Size | Stop Width | Typical Outcome |
|----------|--------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Large size, tight stop** | 40–60% of account | Tight (low of day, just below pivot) | Quick 20–40% gains on few trades |
| **Small size, wide stop** | 5% of account | Wide (technical support levels) | Slow gains; needs double to matter |

> "If I want to swing, I want to put in 30, 40, 50, 60, 100% of my account on swings. I don't want to do small."

> "If you put in 40–50–60% and it goes 26%, you're achieving the same goal as someone with 5% who needs a double."

**Maximum positions: 3–5.** Not 10. "Do you really want to swing with 10 people, or do you want to swing with 3?"

### Why DEP Allows Larger Size

When you enter a DEP (delayed reaction EP), the stock has already pulled back from the initial gap. This tighter entry allows a much larger position with a smaller stop. That's the structural advantage of DEP over chasing EP entries.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Market extension demands patience.** After a marathon, the market needs to rest. Don't force trades when setups are extended and breadth is overheating.

2. **The 39% rule.** Only look at stocks with 39%+ current revenue growth AND 39%+ projected revenue growth. Below that threshold is noise.

3. **Growth, Stories, Turnarounds — in that order of magnitude.** The biggest winners come from stories (space, quantum, AI), but growth provides the fundamental backbone.

4. **Neglect = explosiveness.** Stocks sideways for years that suddenly get a catalyst explode. Already-extended stocks have limited upside.

5. **Every EP 9M must be classified: Cat, Dog, or Liquid Lava.** Just because volume is 9M+ doesn't mean it's a buy. Know the catalyst. Know the story.

6. **Process over randomness.** Organize like crazy. NTRT/MTRT scans pre-market. Go through every stock on the earnings table. Mark important catalysts. Don't ask random questions about stocks you haven't researched.

7. **DEP allows larger sizing than chasing EP entries.** The pullback gives a tighter entry, which means you can put on more size with less risk.

8. **Insider buying is not a reliable signal.** Multiple examples of massive insider buys followed by 20–90% declines.

9. **Large size + tight stop vs. small size + wide stop.** Pick your approach. Pradeep's preference: 3–5 concentrated positions at 40–60% of account.

10. **SIPs can become a crutch.** They're a data-reading strategy, but if you're not experienced, you'll get into the habit of just doing SIPs instead of waiting for high-conviction EP setups.
