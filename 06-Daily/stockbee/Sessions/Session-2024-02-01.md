# Don't Call Everything EP — Red Flags, Process Mastery, and Drinking Cold Water

Day after FOMC. Market choppy, breakouts unlikely. Pradeep spends the entire day dismantling a dangerous myth: calling every gapping stock an EP. NXT and SoFi are case studies in red flags — institutional shareholders with free shares from spinoffs/IPOs ready to dump. The real teaching: **process orientation over money fixation.** Fix your process, reflect, repeat. The setups that work in choppy markets? Delayed reactions and shorts — the very strategies that require patience, not impulsiveness.

---

## AM Meeting (9 AM)

### NXT Is NOT an EP — The Red Flag Autopsy

> "Before sending me an email claiming this is a Victoria's Secret model, just have one look at that girl."

- Members excited about NXT (Flex spinoff) — Pradeep demolishes it
- **Float discrepancy**: TCM says 23M, actual float 121M — 87M shares distributed to Flex shareholders on Dec 31
- Those 87M shares went to **institutional holders**, not individuals
- Institutions got shares for FREE on Dec 31 — now up 25-30% in one month = obvious selling pressure
- This is NOT dilution (company didn't issue new shares) but a **spinoff distribution** — same effect: massive selling overhang
- "If I jump off a cliff and don't die, it doesn't mean my decision to jump was correct"

### SoFi: Same Red Flag Pattern

- Float went from 110M at IPO to 927M — same institutional overhang
- Top-line numbers look great but the **context kills the trade**
- EP requires analytical skills — peeling layers, not just checking boxes

### EP vs SIPs — Critical Distinction (Repeated)

- EP is **extremely rare** — 2-5 genuine ideas per year
- If you call everything EP, you'll have a 25% win rate (at best) and get destroyed
- "Every single Walmart model is not a Victoria's Secret model"
- NXT had good numbers — but the **context** (87M free shares) was the dealbreaker
- SIPs = daily opportunity, smaller but consistent — know the difference

### Situational Awareness: Choppy, Range-Bound

- Correction of smaller magnitude likely, or range-bound action
- Market digesting yesterday's FOMC (Powell said rates likely peaking, cuts coming)
- Long-term bullish — "who's your daddy?" (Fed dovish)
- **What's working**: SIPs, delayed reaction, FHP (high-priced stocks)
- **What's NOT working**: Classical sugar babies, regular breakouts in choppy market

### Align: Good Setup, Bad Risk

- Good continuation setup but $4 spread = guaranteed pain on stop
- "I want to find somewhere I can put less than half a percent risk"
- If the risk isn't there, wait — don't force it

## PM Meeting — Process Orientation: The Only Path to Profitability

### ⚡ The Process Loop (Core Teaching of the Day)

> "If something is not going right in your trading, fix the process."

Process mastery consists of:
1. **Knowing the setups** — EP, SIPs, 9M, delayed reaction, sugar babies, continuation
2. **Knowing the scans** — how to find them efficiently
3. **Quality parameters** — Magna, 53, cap 10x10, short interest 5%+
4. **Execution skills** — when to enter, when to exit, how to size

All four are **fixable**. All four are **under your control.**

### Process Goals > Money Goals

- "Can I make 5% a month?" → wrong question
- "I must follow the process when SA is bearish (no trade) and when SA is bullish (take trade)" → right question
- Fix the process → money follows as outcome
- Process obsession = incremental daily improvement
- Money obsession = frustration, anger, externalization

### SIPs Quality Control Checklist

| Criterion | Threshold | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Short interest | 5%+ (or 12+ days to cover) | Auto-qualify — short squeeze fuel |
| Earnings growth | 100%+ | Significant catalyst |
| Float | <10M shares | Low supply = big moves |
| Catalyst clarity | Obvious to anyone | No research needed for entry |
| Market context | SA aligned | Don't fight the tape |

- If any of float/catalyst/short interest qualify strongly → consider the trade
- Start with 5-10 shares to prove your process works, then scale

### Delayed Reaction: The Best Anticipation Strategy

- You **know the catalyst** ahead of time → can size with conviction
- BA (Boeing door fell off) = perfect example — catalyst obvious, just wait for setup
- Mobileye (missed earnings badly) = another — short on negative open
- Powell (after 50% move) = potential future delayed reaction buy on pullback
- "What's a good delayed reaction? The best ones happen quickly — if your girlfriend takes a month to return your kiss, it's not working out"

### Choppy Market Playbook

- Good setups exist but probability of follow-through is low
- **Drink cold water** — don't trade just because setups look good
- If you must trade: smaller size, tighter stops, hit singles not home runs
- Focus on delayed reactions and shorts — they work regardless of market direction
- Several good delayed reactions identified: NKTX, ARQT, Fastenal, KTX, Colgate, Wix

### NXT/SoFi Post-Mortem: Why They Faded

- NXT faded after initial gap — 87M institutional shares overhang
- Members argued "but it's still going up!" — Pradeep: "I have no regret"
- Peloton also fading — members called it EP, it's not
- These are **negative SIPs** (negative catalyst + institutional selling) → fade

### Reflection Builds Mind Clarity

> "The best thing you can do to become a good trader is spend time reflecting."

- Reflect → fix process → come back → repeat
- 24-48 hours to fix a problem — take that mentality
- Activities that foster reflection: walking, hiking, biking, kayaking — **not** watching football or drinking with friends
- "Don't drink whiskey during trading day" — focus during market hours
- Exercise, sleep, have other hobbies — personal life mess = trading mess

### Write Your Own Trading Book

- Marios (USIC champion) interview: "The book you write is the most important thing"
- Don't ask for a guide — **write your own guide**
- Every time you think "there's no guide for this" → write one
- SIPs guide, delayed reaction examples, quality criteria — create your own reference

### Watch List: 2-3 Stocks Maximum

- If you have 30-50 stocks on your watch list, you're doing it wrong
- 2-3 stocks, laser focused
- Better to do a damn good job of 2-3 setups than a bad job of 600

## Key Takeaways

1. **NXT is NOT an EP** — 87M institutional shares from spinoff = massive selling overhang, despite good top-line numbers
2. **EP requires analytical depth** — peel layers, check float discrepancies, check who holds shares, check context
3. **Don't call everything EP** — genuine EPs are 2-5 per year; calling everything EP = guaranteed losses
4. **Confirmation costs money** — on low threshold breakouts, buy when the scan shows it, don't wait for ORH
5. **Situational awareness trumps setup quality** — Fastenal was an excellent delayed reaction but wrong market = drink cold water
6. **Variable sizing by conviction** — NXT = zero, SMCI = size up, BA short = size up
7. **Process orientation > money orientation** — fix the process, money follows as outcome
8. **Reflect, fix, reflect, fix** — 24-48 hour problem-solving cycle
9. **Write your own trading book** — don't ask for guides, create your own reference
10. **Watch list = 2-3 stocks** — laser focus, not 30-50
11. **Delayed reaction is the best anticipation strategy** — you know the catalyst, you can size with conviction
12. **Short interest 5%+ or 12+ days to cover** = auto-qualify for consideration
13. **Best delayed reactions happen quickly** — if it takes a month to react, the catalyst wasn't that strong
14. **Market choppy = focus on delayed reactions and shorts** — breakouts unlikely to work
15. **Activities that foster reflection** — walking, hiking, kayaking — not partying or screen-staring
16. **Don't join the bitching gang** — focus on process, not on criticizing others
17. **SIPs quality control** — short interest, earnings growth, low float, clear catalyst, market context aligned
