## 10 AM Meeting — Trade Factory Framework Deep Dive

### Trade Factory: Process, Process, Process

- **"Setup ideas are dime a dozen. What matters is process and trade factory."**
- If two people run the same trade factory with the same process, they should get the same trades
- Problem: people jump from tip to tip (Twitter, TraderLion, weekly charts, FTD indicator)
- **Chinese reverse-engineering analogy**: they studied American cars, then made better ones. Same with Tesla — they made better EVs. Reverse-engineer a setup idea, create a process
- McDonald's analogy: same burger everywhere because of process. Your trading should be the same

### Three Types of EP 9 Million Setups

1. **Cats** — quality setups with clear catalyst
2. **Dogs** — speculative, story-driven (DATS, Trump stocks)
3. **Liquid lava** — high-volume breakouts with momentum

### Three Types of Momentum Burst Setups (Long Side)

1. **Continuation setup** — first leg established, <10 days consolidation, then breakout
   - **2 Lynch criteria**: Two days not up in a row (no range expansion in last 2 days), **L**inearity (orderly first leg), **n**arrow/negative pre-breakout day, **c**onsolidation quality (orderly, low volume), **h**igh closing near day's high
   - First leg should be 15%+ (or $10+ for high-priced stocks) — if first leg is only 5%, second leg will be even less
   - Example: 17% first leg, 7 days consolidation, then breakout — textbook

2. **Consolidation setup** — big leg then months of sideways
   - **Consolidation breakouts fail ~75% of the time** — they go back into range
   - Why? Consolidation = institutions selling, breakout = they sell into the liquidity
   - **Must have C+V: catalyst + volume** to increase probability
   - Without catalyst and volume, probability is very low

3. **Bottom bounce** — stock bouncing from bottom after major decline
   - Best in biotech and tech sectors
   - Needs huge range expansion from bottom
   - Most common when market corrects

### Setup Qualification — "You Need a Template"

- **"You have to have a clear example, a template"**
- Continuation setup template: first leg 25%, <10 days consolidation, then breakout
- If you can't identify the setup instantly, you're not ready to trade it
- Develop **procedural memory** — each setup hammered into your mind through repetition
- **"The more clarity you have, the faster you'll enter setups"**

### Short Side Framework — Three Types

1. **Weak structure short (continuation short)** — Pradeep's primary short strategy
   - **Niagara fall**: 3 days continuous selling, down 10%+ in those 3 days
   - Then weak bounce of <3 days, each day up no more than 2.5%
   - Short on the weak bounce
   - Best candidates: stocks that went up a lot then turned — institutions selling
   - **First leg is best for shorts too** — 2nd and 3rd legs work, 5th+ legs are too late
   - SJM example: 4 days down ~9.6%, weak bounce, then continuation short

2. **Momentum burst short** — short at start of a swing
   - Only when market monitor primary indicator is bearish
   - Can take in any market condition if setup is clean

3. **Strong structure short (parabolic short)** — Pradeep doesn't trade these
   - Stock that went 200-300%+ crazy, then short it
   - "Either you'll be a hero or zero"
   - Too risky — can blow up account
   - FUV example: someone made 33% shorting it
   - Quantum stocks (QBTS, IONQ, etc.) = potential strong structure shorts

### "Explain It to Machine Gun Kelly" — Dating Analogies

- Continuation setup: go on 3 dates, continue on 4th and 5th, take a 2-week break, but don't chase another woman
- Weak structure short: something went wrong (institution selling), 3 days of waterfall decline, then weak bounce (Patrick buying support), then it continues down
- Taylor Swift boyfriend analogy: know the history — consolidation breakouts fail like Taylor Swift's relationships (8-10 months average)
- DiCaprio: only dates women under 27, ditches them when they hit 27 — know the pattern
