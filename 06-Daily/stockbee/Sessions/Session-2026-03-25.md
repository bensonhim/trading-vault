### Procedural Memory — Don't Stop Scanning

> "If you stop doing this, your procedural memory is gone. You come back after three weeks and you've forgotten everything."

Extended discussion about traders who become profitable, go on holiday, and can never get back to the same level. The skill is **fungible** — if you don't use it, you lose it. Keep scanning, keep building watchlists, keep the process going even when there's nothing to buy.

### Livermore's Real Story

Extended biography of Jesse Livermore:
- Made first millions through scalping/day trading in bucket shops, NOT position trading
- The famous "sit and make money" quote is misleading — he was a scalper first
- Personal life was a disaster: crazy spending, sugar babies, bad real estate investments
- Married multiple times, wives spent his money on furniture they then cut the legs off
- Gave money to his wife "to keep safe," then took it back when he lost in the market
- His son sold the story rights — the family fell apart after Jesse died
- His granddaughter is a porn star
- The book "Amazing Life of Jesse Livermore" by Richard Smitten tells the real story (not the cult classic)
- Filed for bankruptcy, had clinical depression, was a complete mess personally

> "He's become a cult favorite. But Livermore was a scalper. Livermore was day trading."

### Break — Don't Take Holidays When You're Hot

> "People who are successful go on a holiday because money goes to their head. They come back and can never get back to trading."

When you're hot, freeze the skill. Introspect. Document why you're making money. Don't take breaks during profitable streaks.
