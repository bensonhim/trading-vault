# Controlled Risk in Choppy Waters — ONDS Re-Entry Fades, AMD Stopped Out, and the War the Market Ignored

Day 2 of the Iran conflict. Pradeep was cautious, and the market confirmed his suspicion: the geopolitical event was noise. The 20% study was overheating (100+ bullish, ~20 bearish), signaling a pullback was near. Pradeep re-entered ONDS as an OPG play, but the defense sector faded and he was out for a 3 cent loss. AMD reversal bullish also stopped out. The PM session found almost nothing actionable. Key themes: controlled risk taking when setups don't work, the importance of process over prediction, and a lengthy digression on gold, international markets, and using AI to find SIPs.

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Cautious Essay, Overheating 20% Study

> "My essay this morning is cautious, anticipating further bearishness as the market continues to trade in a choppy fashion amidst escalating geopolitical tensions."

The 20% study showed over 100 bullish stocks approaching 20 bearish, an overheating signal. Pradeep expected a pullback or sideways action. The VIX surged above 20. Small caps (TNA, IWM) outperformed. Energy and defense gained; mega caps were mixed.

### The War Narrative and Drones as a Secular Theme

> "Out of that war, what theme has come out? Drones. The drone as a theme is going to be big because it's a secular theme."

Pradeep drew parallels to Afghanistan and Iraq wars creating new technology stocks. He highlighted FRPT (Force Protection), which went up 1200% on Humvee orders. The current secular theme: drones and defense.

### ONDS Re-Entry and AMD Reversal Bullish

Pradeep bought ONDS again as an OPG order at market open, believing the drone catalyst would persist:

> "I bought ONDS again as an OPG today morning. I think it might be another kind of a thing which might work. Because there is a genuine catalyst."

He also entered AMD as a reversal bullish:

> "AMD kind of a setup, 100 out of 100 times, I will take it. Because it has everything lined up for it in terms of the kind of reversal which you look for. The money is kind of this thing. It's institutional quality. If it doesn't work, that's part of the game."

### Don't Follow Trades

> "You cannot make money by following somebody's trade, especially if you're day trading and if you're swing trading. Process. You have to create the process and follow the process."

### Finding SIPs with AI (French Butler)

Pradeep demonstrated using AI (ChatGPT and Gemini) to pre-screen SIPs and EP candidates:

> "I just ask questions: 'Does this stock meet Magna 53 Cap 10 by 10 criteria?' The French butler does the work for you and tells you."

He showed how Contour Brands (KTV) was found via pre-market earnings screening and then validated through AI-assisted fundamental analysis. The AI also suggested shorting BRKB before market open, which he didn't take but turned out to be correct.

### Gold Bashing

> "When somebody is asking you should [you] should never buy gold. Gold, you buy when nobody wants to buy gold. You don't buy gold when it has made all time high and made a parabolic move."

> "Gold reprice versus stock market, hundred year chart. If you see the chart and you still got arguing, certainly there is no this thing."

> "S&P 10.67% versus 7.96% for gold over 30 years."

### International Markets Discussion

Pradeep gave a macro view on investing: China, Canada, Eurozone, and Japan are at early stages of secular bull trends. He specifically recommended avoiding India (bubble post-IPO flood) and Brazil (late cycle).

## PM Meeting (3 PM)

### Nothing to Get Excited About

> "I don't see much here to get excited about because of the follow through in the last two days."

The market churned sideways. Defense stocks from yesterday faded. Oil and gas faded. The market clearly didn't believe the Iran conflict was serious.

### ONDS Stopped Out, AMD Stopped Out

ONDS was closed for a 3 cent loss. AMD hit its stop. Pradeep's rule after consecutive losses:

> "Two or three trades in a row don't work, then I take a break. ONDS didn't work. Setup was good. 100% of the time I'll take it. AMD didn't work. That's not something which is based on this thing or that."

### Setup Deterioration: SNDK

> "Yesterday SNDK was looking very good. Now what has happened to it? It's deteriorated. It's no more a good setup."

A key lesson: setups on watch lists can degrade. What was a category setup yesterday is no setup today. This is why you need to constantly re-evaluate and not just trade off stale watch lists.

### SIPs as Day Trade Strategy

> "If you are really wanting to make money, SIPs as a day trade strategy, that is what I would focus on."

### DEP Quality: Hold Gains, Don't Give Them Back

Pradeep differentiated between a good DEP and a suspect one:

> "Anything which ends up giving up gains to me is suspect. You want to see a DEP which is really tight, which just sits there after the move and doesn't give up much of the gains."

Good examples: CRCL (held gains), XYZ (held gains). Bad example: CAVA (gave up gains).

## Key Takeaways

1. **Market dismissed the Iran conflict again** — No real traction. Defense and oil stocks faded. The market priced in rate cuts, not war escalation.
2. **ONDS re-entry stopped out for 3 cent loss** — Correct setup, genuine catalyst, sector reversed. "100% of the time I will take that trade."
3. **AMD reversal bullish stopped out** — Institutional quality, perfect pattern, didn't work. Part of the probability game.
4. **Consecutive losses = take a break** — Pradeep's personal rule: after 2-3 consecutive losing trades, step back. The market may be telling you something.
5. **Drones are a secular theme** — Not a one-day event. Will persist as long as geopolitical uncertainty continues.
6. **Use AI to pre-screen SIPs and EPs** — ChatGPT and Gemini can dramatically speed up fundamental analysis. "Train your dragon."
7. **French Butler methodology** — Pre-market gainers list + earnings filter + AI fundamental check = process for finding stocks like KTV and OUST before the open.
8. **Gold is a cult** — "S&P 10.67% vs 7.96% for gold over 30 years." Buy gold when nobody wants it, not at all-time highs.
9. **International markets: China, Canada, Eurozone, Japan** — Early stages of 8-10 year secular trends. India is a bubble. Brazil is late cycle.
10. **Setups deteriorate** — SNDK went from category setup to no setup in one day. Re-evaluate constantly, don't trade off stale watch lists.
11. **DEP quality = holding gains** — A good DEP sits tight and doesn't give back gains. CAVA gave back gains = suspect.
12. **SIPs for day trading in choppy markets** — When the market chops, SIPs (Stocks in Play) with earnings catalysts are the viable strategy.
