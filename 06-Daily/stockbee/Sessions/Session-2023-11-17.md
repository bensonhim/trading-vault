# Choppy Rally: 4% Breakout Is the Magic Indicator, Patience Required

> "This bull move doesn't have the same thrust as past bull moves. You want institutional FOMO — back-to-back 800-900 days — not retail FOMO. The 4% breakout is the magic indicator. At the beginning of every 20% move, you'll find a 4% breakout. That's the lowest-hanging fruit."

---

## AM Meeting (9:00 AM)

### Market Regime: Bullish but Choppy

> "This particular bull move just doesn't have the same thrust as past bull moves. You don't want to see the primary indicator going back and forth."

After the 1,500-day breadth thrust, you want 800-900 follow-through days immediately. Not happening yet — choppy instead.

**Institutional FOMO vs. Retail FOMO:**

> "Institutional FOMO is what creates back-to-back buying days — they want to get in at any cost. That's the single biggest characteristic of the start of a new big bull boom. We don't really see that currently."

### Belief: Start of New Bull Move

> "I believe this is the start of a new bull move which will last for a couple of months at least. We're going to get around 300 continuation setups and around 10 9M setups."

Focus: **9M EP, sugar babies, delayed reaction to earnings, continuation (two-linch)**.

### 20% Study Insight: Capitalization Matters

> "If you're looking for explosive moves, capitalization matters. The probability of big returns decreases as the capitalization increases. Big moves happen below $10B market cap."

Key teaching: do the 20% study daily. You'll learn that sub-$10B stocks produce the explosive moves.

### The 4% Breakout: Nature's Miracle

> "Whenever there's a 20% move in a stock, that move tends to start with a 4% breakout. It's a miracle. At the beginning of a big move, there is going to be a 4% breakout."

> "I see all of them have 4% breakout. 90-95% of these stocks — 98% will have 4% breakout. What should you be paying attention to?"

Simple math: if a stock makes 20% in 5 days, the average daily move is 4%. The 4% breakout is the genesis of every big move.

> "In the first 10 years of my trading, I had just 4% breakout, and I did very well. Tell me one book on technical analysis which has 4% breakout as an indicator."
