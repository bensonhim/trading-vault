# Gap and Crap — Institutional Selling on Gap Up, Shorts Still Working, Situational Awareness Is Your Religion

Market gapped up on Russia-Ukraine war resolution news and semiconductor tariff exemptions — but **71% of stocks made new lows intraday**. The gap was used aggressively by institutions to reduce positioning. Pradeep's morning verdict: **"Are breakouts likely to work? Some select breakouts always work. But broadly, no."** By PM: **"I remain steadfast — breakouts are not likely to work."** Everything that gapped up faded. Duolingo and NBIS were the only profitable SIPs trades (bought after-hours, sold at open). APLD stopped out on the gap. Shorts from prior days (JCI, AMAT, CRM, QCOM) all following through. Key message: **situational awareness is your religion — if you believe, you use it; if not, you get chopped.** Jesse Livermore: "You see an impending train coming — do you stand there or run away?"

---

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Cautious — Gap Will Fade

Market gapping up on Russia-Ukraine war news + semiconductor tariff exemptions. Pradeep's immediate reaction:

> "The gap may fade. That's how the trade may be today. Breakouts are not likely to work. Some select breakouts always work. Breakdowns are likely to work — much better follow-through on breakdowns."

> "We are reaching the same stage where T2108 readings are going to go above 100 very soon. I still remain of the view that more likely we get into choppy territory before some sort of pullback happens."

### No "Get Me Out" Selling — Slow Deterioration

> "What you want to see is 'get me out at any cost' selling. Which you did not see. What you have is slow deterioration — one pillar falling, then the second, then the third."

> "It can chop around for a couple of weeks. Most likely scenario. At some stage, one day it will just dip and keep going down."

### Duolingo SIPs Trade — After-Hours Entry

> "I bought Duolingo in after-hours yesterday at $394. The earnings were very good. The stock had already given up gains from $500. This was a neglected stock with a surprise. I thought it would go to $450-500."

> "Duolingo worked out very well because it was such a nice setup. Free money is better than holding. I saw it fading right from the word go."

### NBIS SIPs Trade

> "NBIS — I would definitely look at it as a delayed reaction for a position trade. But for SIPs, I bought at the open and sold quickly. It went to $70 so fast I couldn't even sell 80% at $70."

### Gap and Crap Pattern

> "This is typical behavior — stocks make a one-day move and then give up all the gains next day. Gap and crap."

> "All the stocks which had good catalysts are also getting hit today, which tells you there is big asset allocation selling going on."

### Shorts Have Follow-Through (Again)

> "AMAT has follow-through. JCI has follow-through. CRM has follow-through. QCOM has follow-through. NXPI has follow-through. Good follow-through on the bearish side, much better than the bullish side."

### Situational Awareness = Religion

> "If your belief is you should trade with situational awareness, then you will use it. If your belief is situational awareness is for fools, you will buy any random breakout anytime."

> "When I tripled my account and then gave it all back, I said: 'Never going to happen again.' I found a workable solution. If you don't believe in that, you're going to have a thing."

### Belief System and Trading Psychology

> "I was so devastated when that happened. I had this much money in my account and then I just over-traded and ended up pissing up all that money. Next time this is not going to happen."

> "The market has to kick you in your nuts for you to appreciate these kinds of things."

## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### 71% of Stocks Making New Lows — Distribution Day

> "71% of the stocks are making new low for the day. There are no buyers. The gap was used aggressively to reduce positioning."

> "This is institutional selling. Somebody is systematically sitting there."

> "Everything which showed strength in the morning has faded."

### Breakouts NOT Working — Don't Buy

> "Even if I find a setup, I'm not likely to buy. Breakouts are not likely to work here. I don't buy."

> "This is not a market for breakouts. Which I said even before the market opened."

### Shorts Will Still Work — Need Deep Buyers to Get Rejected

> "Shorts will still work. You just need a couple of times the deep buyers getting rejected. And then at some stage, one day, it will be open season."

### Gap and Crap Confirmed

> "Shopify ended up giving up all the gains from yesterday. Stocks that went up on earnings are giving up all the gains next day."

> "This is what is happening to a lot of breakouts. Nice breakout, next day they take it to the chopping block."

### APLD Stopped Out

> "APLD — I got stopped out. I had put my stop and it got stopped out. At profit, but not a major profit."

### SOXL — Considering Re-Short

> "I'm tempted to put my SOXL short back. This is a market which is weakening. But SOXL, for whatever reason, is still up today. So I'm not going there."

### Best Shorts for Today

> "ENVX, AI, TXN, DIA, Roku, SPXL, Disney. I would take out Murphy. DIA is better because it hasn't been up and down like SPXL."

### Stop Management on Shorts

> "If I'm going to short something, I always put my stop at the high of the day. By the end of the day, I would move it. By next day, I definitely want to do it at half of the day's range."

### Jesse Livermore Quote

> "As Jesse Livermore said, you are standing on a rail track and you see an impending train coming. So you should sit there and start drinking water or whiskey, or you just run away."
