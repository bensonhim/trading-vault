# Extended and Selective — Tariff Volatility Kills Intraday, Setup Clarity Is Everything, and Don't Date RxRx

The market opened to tariff chaos — Trump announced reciprocal tariffs on select countries, and the intraday volatility was the real risk, not the tariffs themselves. Pradeep's stance was clear: wait and watch, extremely selective. The AM session was a masterclass on setup identification — rejecting member picks (Starbucks cup-with-handle? "It's down 50%, how can it have a cup with handle?"), explaining why continuation setups are failing while start-of-swing works, and delivering the "don't date every girl in your town" trading philosophy. RxRx got the sugar-baby treatment: "you went on four dates, every time she kicked you or burned your $800 Italian shoes, and you want to try a fifth time?" The PM session confirmed the selective posture — nothing screaming buy, only Carvana and General Dynamics as mild possibilities, with the market showing slow deterioration signs.

## AM Meeting (9:15 AM)

### Situational Awareness: Wait and Watch, Extended Market

Judy's essay: cautiously bullish, but the T2108 pulled back from 69.28 to 62.78 — a significant deterioration. Tariff news dominated.

> "I'm more wait and watch mode, highly selective. Lot of stocks are extended — three, four legs. More tariff news is expected today and the market is extended, way extended in the short run. Some sort of consolidation, pullback, or sideways action is likely."

### Tariff Theater = Intraday Volatility

> "The problem is intraday volatility. It comes in suddenly at 10 AM when the market is going up. Your stops get hit. And that is what happens — the algos read the news and buy or sell in milliseconds. By the time retail figures out the news, the market is up or down 20 points."

> "BTFD is the only thing retail knows. Buy the fucking dip."

### Setup Identification: Start of Swing or Nothing

A member asked about Oklo as a "bull flag":

> "This is the second day after the bull flag is breaking. You are late to the party. The idea of buying at the start of a swing — it has to make intellectual sense."

> "Setup understanding and mind clarity about setup — that is the first building block of becoming a profitable trader. If you are struggling to do that, may God help you."

On the first rule of cup-with-handle:

> "Cup with handle forms within 15% of a 52-week high. How can Starbucks have a cup with handle when it is down very low? Cup with handle is a continuation pattern, not a broken stock."

### Continuation Setups Failing

> "Very few continuation setups are working in the 20% study. Start-of-swing and delayed-reaction setups are working better there."

### RxRx — The Four-Date Disaster

> "RxRx — 9M breakout, disaster. Got stopped out. 9M breakout, got stopped out. 9M breakout, got stopped out. You went on four dates and every time she kicked your balls or burned your $800 Italian shoes, and you want to try a fifth time?"

## Key Takeaways

1. **Setup clarity is the first building block** — if you can't identify start-of-swing vs. day-three, nothing else matters
2. **Extended market = extreme selectivity** — three and four-leg stocks are dangerous; wait for start-of-swing
3. **Tariff volatility kills intraday** — algos react in milliseconds; your stops get hit before you know the news
4. **RxRx = stop dating the abusive partner** — four failed breakouts = leave it alone
5. **Cup-with-handle requires near-high base** — broken stocks don't form valid cup-with-handle patterns
6. **Continuation setups failing** — start-of-swing and delayed-reaction are the working methods
7. **Slow deterioration is the tell** — leaders try to break out, fail, and that's the first sign of pullback
8. **DraftKings has a hidden catalyst** — the gambling loss cap provision benefits the house
