## PM Meeting (2:00 PM)

### Powell Speech — Rate Cuts Likely, Market Explodes

> "Relentless deep buying. 1,500 stocks showing breakouts."

### WOLF — Home Run Trade

> "WOLF — I bought at $9.01, 5,000 shares. Sold at $9.83 bulk of it. Remaining moved stop, got stopped out at $9.33. In another account, bought at $9.06, sold at $9.83. All in all, very successful trade."

> "Based on the catalyst, which is there for WOLF, how much is it going to move? Three days, the stock made 88%. The catalyst is already priced in. 88% move in 3 days."

> "That's why on the third day, I sold all my position — because it had already made 60% move based on the catalyst, and it gapped up another 28%."

### Fast Moves Fade — Core Lesson

> "Whenever there are fast moves, what happens to fast moves? Fast moves fade. Slow deliberate moves persist."

> "When WOLF came out of the gate gung ho, I said I need to take 80% and move my stop on remaining. As soon as it went to $10 and started fading, I sold."

> "The whole move of 60% happened in less than 10 bars. What used to take 6 weeks now happens in 3-5 days. Realistic expectation is really important."

### Zoom — SHIPS Play, Clear Catalyst

> "Zoom — earnings beat + revenue guide. Triple B setup. $10-12 range, free money day trade."

> "Based on the catalyst, I thought this stock will make a 10-12% move. It just made that 12% move in one day. How much is left in the tank? Not much."

### Immediate Follow-Through — Key Rule for Shorts

> "If shorts are going to work, they will have immediate follow-through. If you short something and it doesn't have follow-through in the next half hour to one hour after open, you're better off just getting out of it and retrying it."

> "Most of the shots I've got — immediately, no follow-through. Just get out."

### Delayed Reaction EP Week Concludes

> "This marks the end of one week of delayed reaction EP. Hopefully some of you understood how to do it, and rest of you, you're on your own."

> "The whole point of doing delayed reaction EP is to get in with very tight entries. Not use it as a list."

> "My expectation in delayed reaction is that it should immediately start going in my favor."

### Reversal Bullish — Next Week's Focus

> "Starting next week, I'm going to focus on reversal bullish. The meeting will be at 3PM, not 2PM, and only for vetted members."

> "Reversal bullish is an anticipation setup. You enter on the day of the reversal candle, at 3:45-3:58 PM, expecting it to go up the next day or in the next two days."

> "If you buy it now, you're just chasing a move. The ideal entry is at the end of the day."

### Pattern vs Setup

> "Pattern is not a setup. Pattern by itself is not a setup. A qualified pattern with rules — how you enter, how you exit, what is your profit expectancy — that is a setup."

> "Once you understand a pattern, you can see it everywhere. You don't need a scan. You can just visualize it. That's the key to becoming a good trader — train on pattern recognition."

### Target — Stopped Out

> "I got stopped out on my Target short. The expectation on delayed reaction is if there is a negative candle, it should keep going down. You don't want it to keep coming back. But that's happening a lot in this market."

### Singles + Home Runs (Reiterated)

> "All those base hits, those singles, they all add up. That's why Lance Bernstein became a successful trader."

> "600 customers for chimney cleaning. One apartment complex is 200 apartments. Small trades — if you can do 600 small reversal bullish or delayed reaction, they can add up to the same thing."
