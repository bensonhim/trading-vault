# Singles Over Home Runs: 20% Study Deep Dive, Biotech Burst, and the FFM Game

> Sugar babies are back, biotechs are bursting, and the 20% study reveals: range expansion starts it, 3–5 days is the window, young story stocks dominate — so hit singles relentlessly and let the free money compound.

### 20% Study: How 20% Moves Start

Detailed lesson on the anatomy of 20% moves:

> "How do 20% moves start? They start with range expansion. 4% is the magnitude of that range expansion."

Key findings from the study:
- **Biotech** can start from anywhere — 52-week low, any price level
- **Technology** can also start from low bases, but less frequently
- Most 20% moves last **3–5 days**
- If you know the move lasts 3–5 days, buy on day 3–4 when confirmed, not day 1
- Stories matter more than fundamentals: of 108 stocks making 20% moves, only 13–14% had real earnings/sales driving them

> "For short term trading, earnings and sales and all that crap doesn't matter. But if you're holding for three months, six months, it matters."

### IPO10: Young Stocks Dominate

> "The stocks which are showing up are dominated by stocks which IPO'd in the last ten years."

IPO10 filter is a critical edge: when you have 8–9 scan results, go for the younger stock. Old large-caps rarely make 20% moves.

### Size and FFM

> "If you put 25% of your account, you're going to move your account by 5%. If you can put in 50% and it goes up 20%, it can make your month."

FFM = Free Money concept: buy at start of swing, move stop to breakeven, worst case = scratch, best case = 20%+ move. No risk after breakeven.

> "Size matters. And FSS. If you cannot do size, do lot many trades."
