# Process Over Setup: 9M EP Hierarchy, Delayed Reaction Mastery, BA Short Case Study

> "Trading is a process problem, not a setup problem. The setups are known. If you fix your process, profit is an outcome." ### BA Short: Masterclass in Delayed Reaction

The defining trade of the session. Pradeep walked through the entire logic:

1. **Catalyst**: Boeing had a massive quality/safety failure
2. **Volume signal**: 47M shares traded — highest in a long time. Institutions were selling
3. **Observation**: After the initial 9M breakdown, BA faded the next day (didn't bounce)
4. **Entry**: Shorted first thing in the morning when it started printing negative
5. **Risk**: Initially $1 stop, now breakeven
6. **Reward potential**: $30-40+ if catalyst is genuine

> "Take a $1 risk to probably make $30-40. If the catalyst is valid, it will go down a lot. If not, I get stopped out for a dollar."

**Mobileye (MBLY) Short**: Same pattern — 43M shares (highest since IPO), missed earnings badly, delayed reaction entry. Now breakeven stop, free money trade.

> "What is my risk on Mobileye currently? Zero. What is my risk on BA? Zero. Can it gap up tomorrow? Possible. But even if it gaps up, it's not going to gap up 20%."

### 9,000,000 Volume = Catalyst

### Fixed Position Sizing

> "Why do experienced traders become fixed position size traders? It's convenient. You don't have to calculate. You just know: 5,000 shares, done."

### Anticipation: Lowest Win Rate

> "Out of all the methods I trade, anticipation has the lowest win rate. So it is critical to trade anticipation only when probability is overwhelmingly in your favor."

> "Everyone is enamored by anticipation. That's the setup with the very lowest win rate."

### Delayed Reaction: Becoming Favorite Setup

> "Delayed reaction is becoming my more favorite setup because it gives you low risk entry points, and you can plan the trade."

BA and Mobileye are textbook examples:
1. Massive volume on catalyst day (institutions selling/buying)
2. Wait for the first reaction (dip buyers step in on shorts, bounce on longs)
3. Enter when the reaction fails (shorts: when it starts printing negative again; longs: when it starts following through)
4. Tight stop → breakeven → free money

### Market Outlook: Range-Bound Chop

> "What happened after the COVID bubble? 18 months of chop. What happened after this too-good-to-be-true rally? Chop."
