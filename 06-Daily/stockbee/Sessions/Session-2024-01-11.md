# Process Over Pattern — Fix Your Process, Hit and Run, Delayed Reaction Deep Dive

Thursday was the most process-heavy day so far. Pradeep hammered one theme: **trading is a process problem, not a setup problem**. The setups are known — it's the process that's broken. Meanwhile, the market confirmed choppy: combo bullish only 21 stocks at 11 AM, combo bearish 377. BA and Mobileye emerged as the best trades of the day — both delayed reactions to 9M breakdowns with genuine catalysts. Both now have zero risk (breakeven stops). This is the template.

> "If a Sugar Baby shows up in a scan, do you really need to ask any more questions? It's an automatic buy."

### Process Over Pattern

> "Trading is a process problem. The setups are known. If you fix your process, the profit is an outcome of following the process."

The daily process:
1. Situational awareness essay
2. Run scans (9M, combo LTV, always by volume)
3. Find setups within your priority hierarchy
4. Execute with proper risk management
5. Think 3 days ahead

> "Most questions on the timeline are from people who haven't thought through the entire process. They buy random breakouts and then ask what to do."

### 20% Study: Understanding Momentum

Key observations from the 34 stocks:
- Most moves are from **bottoming patterns**, not continuation breakouts
- **Below $10B market cap** and **below $25 price** dominate
- **Earnings-driven** stocks with news catalysts make the biggest moves
- Continuation moves from earnings breakouts are the pattern to study
- Many of these stocks **won't show up in combo scan** (3.47% isn't enough) — they show up in LTV bullish

> "If it takes you so much time to answer 'which scan will this show up in,' you are not even ready."

### Liquid Lava — The Most Underrated Setup

> "Can you look at a stock which does 9 million every day? That's my favorite. I love those stocks."

Liquid lava stocks (consistently high volume) allow:
- **Full position size** because of liquidity
- **Low risk** — tight spreads, easy to enter/exit
- **Account-moving gains** — can move your account 3-5% with one trade
- The volume IS the catalyst — you don't need external news

### Mind Clarity: The Missing Ingredient

> "The most elusive thing in life is mind clarity. That is the one missing ingredient in a lot of people's life."

- People ask "what's the scan?" instead of understanding WHY the setup works
- The first question on Twitter/Youtube is always "what scan for this?" — not "why does this work?"
- **Once you have mind clarity on a setup, you can do it many ways** — 9M, 7M, 5M — it's the IDEA that matters, not the exact parameter
- "I invented these setups. I know what I'm doing. You have secondhand information and you're not going deeper."

### Setup Quality Framework

> "Do you want a 50% probability? 60%? 70%? 80%? 90%?"

In a choppy market with:
- Red worm dominating green
- Stocks up 3-4 days in a row
- Breakouts failing
- Distribution in the market

→ The probability of a 4th-leg breakout working is LOW. Don't buy it just because it's breaking out.

### Delayed Reaction: The Simplest Setup

> "It's a very simple idea. And that's why people struggle. Anything simple is too good to be true."

The delayed reaction template:
1. Stock has a 9M volume day (breakout or breakdown)
2. First day: overreaction — don't trade it
3. Wait for the reaction (bounce or pullback)
4. Enter when the reaction starts fading in the direction of the 9M
5. Stop is tight because you're entering after the reaction
6. If catalyst is genuine, the stock continues — if not, you're stopped out for minimal loss

### Anticipation Has the Lowest Win Rate

> "Anticipation has the lowest win rate. Trade it only when probability is overwhelmingly in your favor."

Anticipation setups look tempting but underperform. Pradeep listed several anticipation candidates (Arvin, KPTX, SECD, Maxon, Emerson, Rockwell Automation) but classified them all as "watch list only" — not taking trades today.

### Not a Bear Market, Just Choppy

> "I don't think we're in a bearish market. We're just going to chop around for some months or weeks. In between, there'll be tradable rallies."

The Nov-Dec rally was "too good to be true" — a once-in-decades event. After such a rally, a choppy digestion phase is normal. The market needs time to absorb the $5 trillion that came in during 6 weeks.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Process > Setup** — The setups are known. Fix your process: SA → scans → priority hierarchy → execute → manage

4. **Delayed reaction = low risk entry** — Wait for pullback after 9M day, then enter in the direction of the catalyst. Zero risk once breakeven stop hit
5. **Liquid lava = full size** — Stocks doing 9M+ daily allow maximum position size with minimum risk
6. **Anticipation = lowest win rate** — Only trade when probability is overwhelming
7. **Hit and run in chop** — Tight stops, quick exits, don't hold through volatility
8. **21 bullish vs 377 bearish** — The scan numbers tell you everything about the environment
9. **BA short = template trade** — Genuine catalyst (737 Max), 9M volume (47M), delayed entry, $1 stop, now free money
10. **Mobileye short = same template** — Missed earnings, 43M volume, highest since IPO, delayed entry, now breakeven
11. **Death by thousand cuts** — In choppy markets, small losses accumulate if you're not disciplined
12. **Mind clarity is the missing ingredient** — People ask "what scan?" instead of "why does this work?" Understand the idea, not the parameter
13. **Observation = trading skill** — Empty parking lot = no money. Red worm dominating = selling pressure. Learn to see
14. **Nov-Dec was unicorn rare** — Don't expect it to continue; expect chop, and be grateful for tradable rallies
15. **Profits buy real things** — Van Cleef & Arpels, not Fed opinions
