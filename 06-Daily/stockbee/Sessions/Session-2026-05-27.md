## AM Session — Cautiously Bullish, Four-Day Breadth Thrust

### Market Phase: Trending

Pradeep introduces a **market phase framework** borrowed from quant methodology: first determine whether the market is trending, choppy, in a tight range, topping, or in an extreme reversal. The current verdict is clear:

> "We are in a trending market currently."

The essay remains **bullish** — four consecutive breadth thrust days (including Friday). The 20% study is elevated (~100+ bullish stocks vs ~20 bearish), which Pradeep frames not as an immediate danger but as a **yellow light**:

> "It can remain like that for three, four days, and then it gives you a sense of false confidence. And then suddenly it hits. It's not immediately — but this is a yellow light."

### The Consolidation Breakout Trap

Pradeep delivers an extended critique of vanilla consolidation breakouts:

> "If you focus your energy on just consolidation breakout, which is what most books on trading tell you to do — the consolidation breakout which works and breaks out with high volume can at times go and double. But the majority of consolidation breakouts, if you continue to trade, you'll spend a lot of time in heartbreak hotels."

The antidote: **consolidation breakout must have a catalyst (CV)**. Without catalyst + volume, these are traps. [[AVGO]]'s recent failure is cited as example.

### SugarMamas List — Capping Low-Cap Dominance

Pradeep announces a structural change to his watchlist methodology:

- **Problem**: SugarBabies list dominated by low-capitalization stocks
- **Solution**: Created a "SugarMamas" universe — stocks with market cap > $10B
- Took 30 from SugarMamas and added them to the SugarBabies list
- List expanded to ~143 names, now more balanced across cap sizes

> "On a $10B stock, you don't need $3. You need position sizing appropriate for the instrument."

### Extended Stocks = First Casualties of 20% Study Rotation

When the 20% study overheats, it's not every stock that gets hit — it's **extended stocks**:

> "The stocks which are extended are the ones which get hit. Not every stock. The market is a rotating mechanism. It doesn't mean there is a market top. It just means if you're holding a stock which has gone up 60% in one month, those are where people aggressively take profit."

[[SNDK]], [[AVGO]], [[MU]] (down 18% during correction) cited as examples.

### Livermore on Selling Into Strength

A significant teaching moment referencing Jesse Livermore's *How to Trade in Stocks*:

> "Livermore himself says he bought a stock, it went up 20 points, and he sold some into strength. He was getting out after a 20-point move."

Pradeep contrasts this with the common retail advice to never sell:

> "All these people who say 'you should not take profit' — if you've traded for some years, you know how the market operates. Most stocks, 20% is their max capacity."

### Story Stocks Not Flying — Real Earnings Are

An important observation about the current rally:

> "In this particular rally, the story stocks are not making big moves. [[RKLB]], [[LUNR]], [[FLYW]] have actual earnings. They are not zero-revenue, negative-growth-rate stocks making moves."

The distinction: **cat/liquid lava stocks with real earnings** are working; pure narrative plays are not.

### Insider Buying ≠ Bullish Signal

Using [[APPS]] (Digital Turbine) as example — CEO has been buying continuously while stock is down 95%+:

> "Don't go by insider buying. He keeps buying. I don't know why he's buying. During the IPO he must have sold — he's made his money. He's not buying with all his money."

## PM Session — EP 9M Magnitude, Meta Catalyst, and Process Discipline

### Meta = EP 9M Textbook

[[META]] dominates the PM session. Zuckerberg announces an Instagram subscription model, triggering aggressive buying:

> "It's a big mountain. It's not something you can just easily [push around]."

> "I saw Meta showing up on trade ideas. By the time I could put an order, that thing was flying — $6, $26, $99."

Meta qualifies as a **clear EP 9M signal** — high volume, genuine catalyst, institutional-quality stock. Pradeep wishes for even more volume:

> "You want to see at least volume comparable to the prior day's volume to really go in with guns blazing."

### The EP 9M Magnitude Analogy

Pradeep delivers the session's defining explanation of why EP 9M works:

> "EP 9M is a higher magnitude signal. Somebody saying 'I love you' — that's EP 9M. It's telling you, can you look and pay attention to this stock? This is going up."

> "In the last five days, everything which made a big move was EP 9M. Amazon — for so long nothing happened, then EP 9M, and it went 23%."

> "Everything makes sense if you know the context. Everything looks nonsense if you don't know the context."

### NVIDIA — The Only A-Class Reversal Bullish

While Meta gets the EP 9M spotlight, [[NVDA]] is the only stock qualifying as a **true reversal bullish** setup:

> "Nvidia is the thing. That is exactly what you want to look at for the kind of setup."

Four days down after good earnings → orderly pullback → potential reversal entry. This aligns with the DEP/reversal playbook discussed in the AM session.

### 20% Study Revenge = Rotation, Not Market Top

The PM session reframes the AM's yellow-light warning:

> "The 20% study revenge — when it gets overheated, stocks which have made big moves, those are the ones which get hit most. Not necessarily [a market top]. It's a rotation."

> "As long as you're not taking a second or third leg, and you're not sitting and waiting for your 30% profit to be wiped out — take it. It's a rotation."

Money is rotating into [[AMZN]], [[GOOGL]], and other lagging large-caps. Extended semis are bleeding but not reversing.

### The DEP Process — No Fixed Day Count

A member asks about delayed reaction timing. Pradeep is emphatic:

> "I look for an orderly pullback. I cannot say 'okay, third day I'm going to look at it.' It can happen second day, it can happen third day. You look at them every day, and each one is going to look like a different key case."

Key DEP criteria:
1. **Valid catalyst** (earnings, news)
2. **Orderly pullback** (not just sideways — volume must decline)
3. **Breakout from the pullback zone** with volume
4. **No fixed timeline** — watch daily, act on setup formation

### Process Over Tips

> "All this will make sense only if you start running the scan yourself. Start looking at delayed reaction. Start looking at SugarBabies list. See which one is setting up."

> "That Irish-born investor wasn't born with SIPs as a skill. He started doing SIPs. Through 100 iterations, he found a way to find SIPs and rate them."

> "If you're waiting for somebody to tell you what to do, that person who is telling you becomes good, and you're just going to be continuously chasing things."

### CrowdStrike Short Rejected — Extended ≠ Short Setup

A member asks about shorting [[CRWD]]. Pradeep dismisses it:

> "Just because a stock is extended doesn't make it a short. It can just transition into a chop. There is no range expansion. There is no short setup."

The 20% study doesn't support shorts — few breakdown setups working, most action on the long side with catalysts.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Four-day breadth thrust = bullish essay**. No change in directional bias. Trending market confirmed.
2. **20% study at yellow light** signals rotation, not reversal. Avoid extended stocks; rotate into new setups.
3. **EP 9M is a magnitude signal**, not just a volume filter. It screams "pay attention" — context determines whether you act.
4. **Consolidation breakouts without catalyst = heartbreak hotel**. Always require CV (catalyst + volume).
5. **Selling into strength is disciplined, not timid**. Livermore sold after 20-point moves. Most stocks' max capacity is ~20%.
6. **DEP has no fixed day count**. Watch for orderly pullback, volume decline, then breakout — not a calendar-based entry.
7. **Process > tips**. Run your own scans, research your own catalysts, iterate. Nobody can hand you readiness.
8. **Extended ≠ short setup**. CrowdStrike is extended but has no short setup — no range expansion, no breakdown pattern.
9. **Money rotates, doesn't disappear**. 20% study overheating means rotation from extended into lagging names, not a market top.
10. **Story stocks are not leading this rally**. Real-earnings stocks ([[RKLB]], [[LUNR]], [[FLYW]]) are outperforming narrative plays.
