# Breakouts Failing, Pilot Fish Warning, and the Setup Is Everything

Yesterday's perfect breakouts got hammered today — [[NVIDIA]], [[CRWV]], [[CoreWeave]] all faded from the gate — and Pradeep's morning thesis was clear: **when your best setups fail, that's the market telling you something is wrong**. The PM session added nuance: the selling was real but not yet serious — no EP 9M breakdowns to confirm a top. Cautious, process-driven, and light on actionable trades.

---

## AM Meeting

### Market Monitor: Cautiously Bullish but Warning Signs Flashing

Pradeep opened with his morning situational awareness:

> "My essay this morning is cautiously bullish as we remain in a trending market showing strong resilience despite continued geopolitical unrest."

But the qualifiers came fast:

- Market monitor had a **300+ red day** yesterday
- Primary and secondary indicators still green, but **deteriorating margins** signaling slower buying
- The **20% study remains at extreme levels** — signaling pullback or sideways action near term
- Major indices captured new record highs on semiconductors and AI strength, but small caps ([[TNA]], [[IWM]]) outperformed — a rotational signal

> "So it's a choppy one, which is a transition market."

### The Pilot Fish: Yesterday's Breakouts Failed Today

This was the session's central observation. Pradeep identified yesterday's failed breakouts as the **pilot fish** — the early warning sign:

> "The way you know there is something changing is breakouts happen and perfect breakouts start fading or they just fail, and that's how it starts."

Specific examples of breakouts that failed:

| Ticker | What Happened | Signal |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| [[NVIDIA]] | Gapped up, then sold off all day | Perfect setup, no follow-through |
| [[CoreWeave]] | Consolidation breakout with catalyst, hammered back into range | Catalyst + pattern = nothing |
| [[GLW]] | Only stock holding green for a while | Isolated strength, not broad |
| [[Oklo]] | Gapped down directly | Setup destroyed at open |
| [[SNDK]] | Fading | Sector weakness |
| [[Marvel]] | Gapped up, started fading | Story stock exhaustion |
| [[SMCI]] | Nearly parabolic, then fading | Extended danger zone |

> "Most of the things which broke out yesterday are getting hit again. And that's like a warning sign."

### The Two-Trade Rule

Pradeep shared his personal rule for detecting market shifts:

> "I have a rule when my setups which look very good, they don't work — two in a row, then there is something wrong in the market."

He closed all positions yesterday when [[CRWV]] and [[NVIDIA]] both failed. That was the signal.

> "Yesterday itself, when that started refading, I closed all things because that action was suspect."

### EP 9M Breakdowns: The Three-Count Rule

A key methodological teaching on when breakouts signal a true regime change:

> "Three EP 9M typically is when… shorting is not the play… It just doesn't fall apart like that."

> "When you have such a strong rally, it takes three EP 9M for it to transition into either choppy or writing."

The pattern: one EP 9M breakdown = warning. Two = confirming. Three = regime shift likely. He noted Intel had two EP 9M breakdowns already, but not three — not yet the full signal.

### Extended Stocks: Lottery Tickets and Career-Terminating Shorts

Pradeep was blunt about the danger zone stocks had entered:

> "Large cap AI stocks are playing like lottery tickets. That's like, typically, what you don't want to see."

On parabolic shorting specifically:

> "Parabolic shorting is like — if you get it right intraday, you can be a hero. And if it doesn't work, then you are zero. And then maybe it will be career-terminating."

> "Just because the stock is overextended. Right? And on this day, the person who shorted it, he wiped out his account."

### Third and Fourth Leg: The Addiction Warning

A sharp observation about members' behavior:

> "This is almost like a compulsive addiction many of you have to buying stocks which are third or fourth leg."

> "Every stock people ask about during the meetings is something which is third or fourth leg."

Continuation setups work on **first or second leg**. By third or fourth leg, the move is extended and probability drops dramatically. Most of the current market leaders — [[NVIDIA]], [[AMD]], [[Marvel]], [[SMCI]] — are in extended territory.

### Meta: Dead Money and Easy Money

On [[Meta]]:

> "I think Meta is dead money. Meta doesn't have any AI model which is useful. They are just spending money like crazy."

> "Facebook, easy money. Instagram and all, people make content, he makes money."

He drew a parallel to corporate governance — Meta's dual-class structure means Zuckerberg can't be forced out, so he "just wastes money."

### The Lag Effect

Pradeep explained why the 20% study extreme doesn't trigger an immediate crash:

> "There is a lag effect. Right? There is always a lag effect."

> "It accelerated before correcting, giving you a small sense of, here also it took, and then there's a three-day bath."

The market can accelerate into overbought territory before the correction arrives — the extreme reading is the warning, not the trigger.

### Organized Like Crazy: Setup Discipline or Get Chopped

A pointed exchange about a member asking if a stock was a sugar baby at 9:28 AM:

> "At 9:28 you are asking, 'Is it a sugar baby?' I cannot even imagine. Organize like crazy. Do your work. Set up the scan. Write your guide. Otherwise you are going to be, again and again, getting into trouble for no reason."

> "This is focused time. This is where you should be like an egg — focus like crazy."

### Process Over Opinions

> "Opinions are like assholes. Everyone has one and they stink. I just go by setups."

> "If there is a setup, then you trade. If there is no setup, you don't trade. There is nothing beyond that."

> "Our system is self-corrected."

### OPG Orders and the 50% Study

Two technical teachings:

1. **OPG (Opening Price Guarantee)**: Cannot attach a stop loss to an OPG order. You don't know where it will execute. It's riskier than limit orders.

2. **50% Study**: Pradeep challenged members to study how small caps make 50% moves:

> "Even nano caps, small caps, which are going to go up, they're going to start with a bang. Bang. And then go up."

> "Study EP 9M. One or two may not start like that, but majority start with 9 million volume."

Every major move — even in tiny stocks — begins with a volume explosion. This is why EP 9M is the primary scan.

### Margin Call Reference

A brief exchange about someone putting 150% of their account in BTC on margin:

> "There are three ways to make money in the market: Be first, cheat, or be smart. Right?"

> "He might become hero or he might become zero. And that's the only two things."

## PM Meeting

The PM session was notably short (~13 minutes) and thin on content. Pradeep summarized:

### Selling but Not Serious

> "It is selling, but it's not like really serious selling."

> "Most of it is just little bit of ticklish kind of selling. No real selling kind of a thing."

No EP 9M high-volume breakdowns appeared. The breadth was around 100 on the short side — not enough to confirm a top or make shorting compelling.

### GLW: The Only Bright Spot

> "GLW from yesterday still might work because it is still looking good."

[[GLW]] was the only name that retained any bullish appeal across both sessions.

### Shorts: Not Compelling Yet

> "You have to see a good 500-600 down day for shorts to get interesting."

> "Otherwise it can transition into a choppy zone."

On shorting extended stocks like [[PayPal]]:

> "PayPal is like a dead horse. One of the problems is some company decides to buy out PayPal. You might get a surprise."

Shorting beaten-down stocks carries buyout risk. Better to short near the top on fresh breakdowns.

### Nothing Exciting

> "I don't really see anything which I am really excited about as a short."

> "I don't really see any setup which I would get excited about currently."

The session ended with reversal bullish scans showing nothing actionable — maybe [[Arms]] at best. The overall verdict: **minor pullback, not a top. Wait for confirmation.**

## Key Takeaways

1. **When your best setups fail, the market is warning you** — Pradeep's two-trade rule: if two good setups fail in a row, reduce risk immediately.
2. **The pilot fish pattern** — perfect breakouts fading on the open is the earliest signal that the market regime is shifting.
3. **Three EP 9M breakdowns = regime change** — one or two EP 9M on the downside are warnings; three in a row after a strong rally signals transition to choppy or bearish.
4. **20% study extremes have a lag effect** — the market can keep running for days after the indicator hits extreme levels. The extreme is a warning, not a timing signal.
5. **Third and fourth leg stocks are a compulsion trap** — most questions about setups are on extended names. Continuation setups work on first or second leg.
6. **Parabolic shorting is hero or zero** — career-terminating risk if timed wrong. The person who shorted SMCI at the wrong time wiped their account.
7. **Process over opinions** — "Opinions are like assholes. Everyone has one." Go by setups only.
8. **Organized Like Crazy (OLC)** — set up your scans before the open, not during trading hours. 9:28 AM is not the time to ask "Is this a sugar baby?"
9. **Even nano caps start with a bang** — EP 9M volume threshold applies to all market caps. Study the 50% movers to confirm.
10. **Minor pullback ≠ top** — the PM session confirmed selling was not yet serious. No EP 9M breakdowns, breadth around 100 short. Wait for confirmation.
