# Bearish Trading

> *"Short side is not a mirror image of the long side. Intuitively, it might look like that, but it doesn't work like that."*

Bearish Trading is Pradeep's **short-side framework** — a set of setups that exploit institutional selling, broken structures, and sector collapse. It is **not** simply "inverting" bullish setups. The dynamics, timing, and risk management are fundamentally different. Shorting requires **higher conviction, tighter risk control, and faster execution** because downside moves are rapid and unforgiving.

---

## Definition

**Bearish Trading** encompasses four distinct short-side setups, each triggered by different market conditions:

| Setup | Trigger | Timing | Conviction |
|-------|---------|--------|------------|
| **DEP Short** | Post-catalyst failure — stock had a catalyst, broke down, then rallied back to resistance | Delayed (like bullish DEP, inverted) | Standard |
| **Weak Structure Short (WSS)** | Stock has broken support, no buyers at logical levels | Immediate after breakdown | High |
| **Momentum Burst Bearish (MB Bearish)** | 4% breakdown on volume — the inverse of bullish momentum burst | Day 1–2 of breakdown | Standard |
| **Broken Business** | Fundamental deterioration — the business itself is failing | Any time; long duration | Variable |

Key attributes:
- **Max overnight risk**: 6% of total capital (all stops hit simultaneously)
- **Speed**: "It might take six months to make a 100% move, and three days for that 100% to be given back"
- **Sector focus**: "Go to sectors which are weak. Weak sectors get even weaker."
- **Illiquidity filter**: Don't short anything with <1M shares/day average volume

---

## Key Principles

### 1. Short Side Is NOT the Mirror Image of the Long Side

> *"Short side is not a mirror image of the long side. Intuitively, it might look like that, but it doesn't work like that."*

The most dangerous mistake in bearish trading is assuming that if you flip your bullish playbook, you'll profit on the short side. Shorting has different dynamics: moves are faster, halts are more frequent, borrow costs vary, and the psychology is fundamentally different.

### 2. Downside Moves Are Rapid — Speed Kills

> *"It might take a stock six months to make a 100% move, and it might take three days for that 100% move to be over."*

Bearish moves compress time. A stock that took months to climb can collapse in days. This means:
- **Entry timing is tighter** — you can't be late
- **Profit-taking must be faster** — the move can reverse quickly
- **Halts are more frequent** — short squeezes are real

### 3. Sector Focus Over Stock Picking

> *"Go to sectors which are weak. Weak sectors get even weaker."*

Don't short a strong stock in a weak sector, and don't short a weak stock in a strong sector. The best shorts are **weak stocks in weak sectors** — the selling cascade is structural, not temporary.

### 4. Use High-of-Day Stops on Shorts — But With Caveats

> *"High-of-day stop on shorts is terrible risk/reward, guaranteed loss. Use low of day or half of day instead."*

A common mistake is using the high-of-day as a stop on a short position. This gives enormous risk because intraday spikes can be violent. Instead, use **structural stops** — below the low of day, below a support level, or below half of day's range.

### 5. Broken Structures = Short Signal

When the EP 9M scan fills with **bearish EP 9Ms** (stocks breaking down on 9M+ volume with no catalyst), it signals that institutional selling is accelerating. This is one of Pradeep's most reliable market health signals.

> *"Your EP 9M scan is full of broken structures."*

When multiple stocks simultaneously show EP 9M breakdowns, the market is unhealthy beneath the surface — even if the index looks fine.

### 6. Don't Short Into a Waterfall

> *"Never short into a waterfall. Wait for a counter-trend bounce, then short, essentially using the same anticipation logic as reversal bullish, but inverted."*

The inverse of "don't buy into a waterfall decline" is "don't short into a waterfall decline either." Wait for a bounce, then short into strength. The best short entries come on **rally days**, not on big red days.

### 7. Position Size Is Smaller on Shorts

Because short moves are faster and more volatile, position sizes should be **smaller than equivalent bullish positions**. The maximum overnight risk should not exceed 6% of total capital even if all stops are hit simultaneously.

## Contradictions & Gaps

1. **"Not a mirror image" vs. DEP Short**: DEP Short is explicitly described as the "inverse of bullish DEP" — post-catalyst failure, then rally back to resistance. This is structurally very similar to bullish DEP, just inverted. The distinction is that the **timing and speed** are different, not the structure.
2. **Low mention count (15 mentions) vs. importance**: Bearish Trading has fewer mentions than EP 9M (91) or FHP (236), partly because the period covered (May–June 2026) was predominantly bullish/choppy. Bearish setups are more relevant during corrections and bear markets.
3. **"Don't short into a waterfall" vs. WSS**: WSS requires entering immediately after a breakdown — which looks like shorting into weakness. The resolution: WSS enters after the **initial breakdown is confirmed**, not during a waterfall decline. The breakdown provides structural confirmation.
4. **FHP shorting**: Pradeep mentions "I like shorting high priced stocks" — high-priced stocks can also be short candidates because of their institutional dominance and linear moves (in reverse). This is not fully developed in the current source material.
