# Catalyst Lifespan

How long a catalyst remains tradeable. A catalyst's lifespan determines your hold period, position sizing, and exit strategy. Match the trade to the lifespan or get stopped out holding a dead catalyst.

## Definition

**Catalyst Lifespan** is the concept that every catalyst — the news, event, or theme driving a stock's move — has a finite tradeable window. After that window closes, the move exhausts itself regardless of how strong it initially looked. Pradeep categorizes catalyst lifespans into four tiers, from secular (months) down to hot story (hours).

> "Hard story themes are going to have a lifespan of anywhere between one day to three to four weeks."

## Key Principles

1. **Secular themes last months** — AI, crypto, cloud computing — these are multi-month narratives that support repeated [[05-Wiki/setups/Episodic Pivots|EP]] entries and position trades. Each individual trade within a secular theme may be 3–5 days, but the theme itself persists.
   > "Typically, the lifespan of technology companies is around ten years, fifteen years." — (on secular durability)

2. **Cyclical themes last weeks** — Earnings beats, FDA approvals, sector rotations. These drive 1–4 week moves. After the initial reaction fades, the catalyst is exhausted.
   > "But most bottom bounce also are going to have, like, only a lifespan of just a month."

3. **Stock-specific catalysts last days** — A single company's earnings surprise, contract win, or management change. These are the bread and butter of momentum burst trades (3–5 day holds).
   > The typical momentum burst: enter on the catalyst day, peel by Day 3, time stop by Day 5.

4. **Hot stories last hours** — A tweet, a rumor, a short-lived headline. These create intraday spikes that fade quickly. Day-trading territory only.
   > "Hard story themes are going to have a lifespan of anywhere between one day to three to four weeks." — This range covers both hot stories (hours) and stock-specific catalysts (days).

5. **Match your hold period to the catalyst lifespan** — This is the critical error most traders make: holding a stock-specific catalyst for weeks (mistaking it for a secular theme) or trying to day-trade a secular theme (missing the multi-week move).
   > If the catalyst is stock-specific → 3–5 day momentum burst. If secular → can hold for weeks via [[05-Wiki/setups/Episodic Pivots|EP]] or position trading.

6. **Catalyst lifespan determines time stops** — A stock-specific catalyst should be time-stopped by Day 3–5. A secular theme can be trailed longer. Using the wrong time stop for the catalyst type is a systematic error.
   > "Hold momentum burst past day 5 — probability of reversal increases." — This rule applies to stock-specific catalysts, not secular themes.

7. **EP9M identifies stock-specific catalysts** — The [[05-Wiki/setups/Episodic Pivots|EP9M]] scan (9M+ volume on earnings/catalyst day) finds stocks with catalyst-driven moves. These are typically 3–5 day holds unless the catalyst proves to be the start of a secular theme.

8. **DEP is a delayed catalyst reaction** — [[05-Wiki/setups/Episodic Pivots|Delayed Episodic Pivot (DEP)]] is a catalyst where the market initially underreacts, then catches up over days. The lifespan is still days, not weeks.

## Catalyst Lifespan Tiers

| Tier | Lifespan | Examples | Trade Style | Hold Period |
|------|----------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Secular** | Months | AI theme, crypto bull run, cloud adoption | Position trade, EP on pullbacks | Weeks–months |
| **Cyclical** | Weeks | Earnings season, FDA cycle, sector rotation | Swing trade, EP | 1–4 weeks |
| **Stock-specific** | Days | Single earnings beat, contract win, management change | Momentum burst | 3–5 days |
| **Hot story** | Hours | Tweet, rumor, intraday headline | Day trade only | Hours |

## Contradictions & Gaps

- **Only 5 mentions in source material**: This concept is underdeveloped in the wiki. The four-tier framework (secular/cyclical/stock-specific/hot story) is inferred from scattered quotes rather than a dedicated guide. A full Catalyst Guide would strengthen this page significantly.
- **"Technology lifespan = 10–15 years" vs. "hard story = 1 day to 3–4 weeks"**: These refer to different things — the former is the secular durability of the sector, the latter is the tradeable catalyst window. Not contradictory, but the distinction is implicit rather than explicit.
- **No precise time stop by catalyst tier**: The guide gives general "Day 3–5 for momentum burst" but doesn't explicitly map each catalyst tier to a specific time stop. This is a gap — the reader must infer.
