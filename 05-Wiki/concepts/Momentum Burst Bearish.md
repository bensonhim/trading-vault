# Momentum Burst Bearish

The short-side mirror of [[Bullish Momentum Burst]]. Breakdowns on stocks with weak structure, timed with bearish [[Start of a Swing|Situational Awareness]]. Five setups, but Pradeep trades only two.

## Definition

**Momentum Burst Bearish** is the collective term for short-side setups that capture 3–5 day downside moves. A stock breaks down from a weak structure on volume, and you short at the [[Start of a Swing]] to the downside. The concept is the same engine as bullish momentum burst — range expansion at the start of a swing — but applied to the short side in a bearish market context.

> "The combination bearish scan generates a list of stocks showing bearish momentum."

## Key Principles

1. **Short-side momentum burst is the same concept as long-side** — Range expansion at the start of a swing works in both directions. The setup logic, entry timing, and position sizing are identical; only the direction changes.
   > "While Pradeep does not cover position sizing or stop-placement mechanics for bearish setups in this guide, the logic follows from the bullish momentum burst framework."

2. **Five bearish setups exist, but only two are traded** — Pradeep trades only [[05-Wiki/concepts/Weak Structure Short|Weak Structure Shorts]] (universal, works in all markets) and SPY-sync momentum bursts (bear-market only). The other three — parabolic shorts, low-float/nano-cap shorts, and bottom bounces — are either logistics nightmares or low-probability.
   > "But primarily out of this, I only trade weak structure short."
   > "Parabolic shorts — NO — logistics nightmare (halts, borrow costs)."

3. **SA filter is critical on the short side** — In bullish markets, most shorts fail. Only trade momentum burst bearish when the primary indicator is bearish or when a stock-specific catalyst is overwhelming (institutional liquidation in WSS).
   > "You can have a roaring bull market and a weak structure short will still work because something specifically has gone wrong with that stock."

4. **WSS works in ALL market conditions** — Unlike SPY-sync shorts, [[05-Wiki/concepts/Weak Structure Short|Weak Structure Short]] is stock-specific. The stock's own weakness (institutional selling) provides the edge regardless of the broad market.
   > "Weak structure short is my favorite setup on the short side because it works in all categories, in all kind of market conditions."

5. **SPY-sync shorts require bearish market** — Shorting breakdowns that sync with SPY selling only works when the market is in a downtrend. In a bull market, these shorts get squeezed.
   > "During that particular time period, if you take a straight breakdown from a consolidation, then it is likely to work."

6. **Entry is still at the start of a swing** — The same [[05-Wiki/concepts/Start of a Swing|SOS]] concept applies: you short on Day 1 of the downside swing. Buying Day 2 or 3 of a downside move is as bad as buying Day 3 of an upside move.
   > "The setups on this list will still be start of a swing, anticipation, or reversal."

7. **Stop placement mirrors the long side** — Hard stops above the high of the entry day (or half-bar for aggressive entries). 2.5–8% average stop distance. Time stops by Day 3–5.

## The Five Bearish Setups

| # | Setup | Market Condition | Pradeep Trades It? | Notes |
|---|-------|-----------------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 | **Parabolic shorts** | Any | **NO** | Logistics nightmare — halts, borrow costs 70%+, impossible to size |
| 2 | **SPY-sync momentum burst** | Bearish only | **YES** | Short breakdowns that sync with index selling |
| 3 | **[[Weak Structure Short]]** | Any (all markets) | **YES** — favorite | Waterfall decline + weak bounce + resumed selling. Stock-specific. |
| 4 | **Bottom bounce short** | Bearish | No (rare) | Fade dead-cat bounces in weak stocks |
| 5 | **Low-float / nano-cap shorts** | Any | **NO** | Borrow fees 70%+, frequent halts, dangerous |

## Timeline (Bearish Momentum Burst)

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Day 1** | Short on breakdown at SOS to downside. Place hard stop above entry day high. |
| **Day 2** | If opens negative → move stop to breakeven. If bounces → exit if stop hit. |
| **Day 3** | Peel 80% by midday if follow-through continues. Trail remaining 20%. |
| **Day 4–5** | Time stop if no further downside. Trail on runners. |

## Contradictions & Gaps

- **"Works in all markets" vs. "bear-market only"**: WSS works in all markets because it's stock-specific (institutional liquidation). SPY-sync shorts only work in bearish SA. These are two different setups — the contradiction resolves by separating them.
- **Position sizing underspecified**: Pradeep explicitly states he doesn't cover position sizing for bearish setups in the Bearish Guide. The logic transfers from the long side, but exact sizing tables for shorts are a gap.
- **Borrow cost reality check**: Parabolic and low-float shorts are theoretically profitable but practically impossible due to borrow costs and halts. The guide lists them but effectively disqualifies them.
