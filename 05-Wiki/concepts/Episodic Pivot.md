> [[../../03-Guides/stockbee/19. Deep Dive Guide.md|19. Deep Dive Guide]]: ### What is a Deep Dive / X-Ray?

> [[../../03-Guides/stockbee/19. Deep Dive Guide.md|19. Deep Dive Guide]]: An **X‑ray** is the fundamental financial profile of a company — primarily sales growth, earnings growth, and forward estimates — displayed in MarketSmith, William O'Neil database, or similar tools. When Pradeep looks at an X‑ray, he is asking one question: *Is there a dramatic acceleration or turnaround that is strong enough to drive a multi‑day or multi‑week move?*

> [[../../03-Guides/stockbee/19. Deep Dive Guide.md|19. Deep Dive Guide]]: A **deep dive** is the process of empirically studying a large sample of stocks that exhibited the exact behavior you want to trade (e.g., a 20% move in five days, a 50% move in a month, a 300% annual winner) and cataloguing their common traits. Pradeep printed 40,000 charts by hand when he started. Today he does a "mini deep dive" every single morning.

> [[../../03-Guides/stockbee/19. Deep Dive Guide.md|19. Deep Dive Guide]]: ### Why Deep Dives matter

> [[../../03-Guides/stockbee/19. Deep Dive Guide.md|19. Deep Dive Guide]]: > *"Burn all the books on trading and start with a deep dive. You are going to learn in a deep dive more than you'll learn by reading 50,000 books on trading."*

> [[../../03-Guides/stockbee/19. Deep Dive Guide.md|19. Deep Dive Guide]]: Deep dives also answer the granular questions no book can: Where do I put the stop? How long does the move last? Do I buy on day one or wait for a pullback? Should I only trade above the 10‑day MA? The answers come from *your* data, not from a forum post.
