# Bullish Momentum Burst

The core 3–5 day swing setup in the StockBee system. A 4% breakout with volume burst, traded at the [[Start of a Swing]], with tight stops and aggressive position sizing. The "engine inside SOS" — every [[05-Wiki/concepts/Start of a Swing|SOS]] is a momentum burst at its core.

## Definition

A **Bullish Momentum Burst** is a short-term swing trade setup that captures a 3–5 day move of 8–20% (or 40–50% on [[05-Wiki/concepts/Peeling|Sugar Babies]]). It triggers when a stock breaks out 4%+ on above-average volume at the [[Start of a Swing]]. The setup works best in bullish [[05-Wiki/concepts/Start of a Swing|Situational Awareness (SA)]] and is distinguished from [[05-Wiki/setups/Episodic Pivots|EP]] by its shorter hold period and different exit logic.

> "Make momentum burst work. You're going to waste a lot of time on that." — Process Guide

## Key Principles

1. **4% breakout is the trigger** — The momentum burst fires when a stock moves 4%+ from its prior close on volume significantly above average. This is the scan that finds candidates.
   > "If he finds a 4% breakout and if it is start of a swing, he just puts all his account."

2. **3–5 day hold, then time stop kills it** — Momentum bursts are short-lived by design. If the move hasn't played out by midday Day 3, exit. By Day 5, probability of reversal increases sharply.
   > "Day 3 midday for momentum burst; day 4–5 for MB."

3. **Massive position sizing is the edge** — The setup uses 50–80% of account per trade with stops of 0.5–2%. Wider stops mean smaller size; tighter stops allow bigger positions.
   > "I'm putting in 60–80% of my account on a swing trade. And then I'm taking 80% off on a second day or a third day."

4. **Buy at the start of a swing, nowhere else** — The entry must be on Day 1 of the swing move. Buying Day 2 or 3 destroys the risk/reward because the stock is already extended.
   > "If you cannot define what is the start of a swing, then do not trade swing trading setups. It's just a matter of time before you'll blow up."

5. **Hard stops, market hours only** — No mental stops. Stops are active only during 9:30 AM–4:00 PM. No pre-market or after-hours entries or stop logic.
   > "I am not a fan of mental stop." — Bullish MB Section 4

6. **Move stop to breakeven within 1 hour** — If the trade opens positive on Day 2, move the stop to breakeven quickly. By Day 3–4, trail at 2–3%.
   > "To breakeven if opens positive; trailing 2–3% by day 3–4."

7. **SA filter is mandatory** — Only trade bullish momentum bursts when the primary market indicator is bullish. In bearish or neutral SA, this setup degrades rapidly.
   > "Only trade SOS when primary indicator is bullish." — Market Monitor Guide

8. **Different from EP** — EP ([[05-Wiki/setups/Episodic Pivots|Episodic Pivots]]) holds for multi-week moves with different exit logic. Momentum burst is a faster, shorter setup — enter aggressive, peel 80%, trail 20%.
   > "Hold momentum burst past day 5 — probability of reversal increases."

## Timeline

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Day 1** | Enter on 4% breakout at SOS. Place hard stop (0.5–2% risk). Size aggressively. |
| **Day 2** | If opens positive → move stop to breakeven within 1 hour. If weak → consider exit. |
| **Day 3** | Peel 80% of position by midday if follow-through is strong. Trail remaining 20%. |
| **Day 4–5** | Trail stop on remaining 20% runner. Time stop: exit if no further follow-through. |
| **Day 5+** | Exceptional cases only. Probability of reversal increases. |

## Peeling (80/20 Rule)

> "Peeling a position, doing 80/20, is a very advanced trading technique."

For experienced traders only: take a larger-than-normal position (60–80% of account), sell 80% on Day 2–3 to lock profit, keep 20% as a runner. See [[05-Wiki/concepts/Peeling|Peeling]].

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Large position (50%+ account)** | Peel 80% on Day 2–3, trail 20% |
| **Normal position** | Hold full size, move stop to breakeven Day 2 |
| **No follow-through by Day 3 midday** | Kill entire trade regardless of P/L |

## Contradictions & Gaps

- **Peeling vs. home runs**: Pradeep warns "you'll never hit a home run if you're peeling" yet also advocates 80/20 on Sugar Babies. The resolution: peel on ordinary SOS (8–20%), let runners ride on Sugar Baby SOS (40–50%+).
- **Day 3 time stop vs. let-it-ride**: The rule says "kill by midday Day 3 if no follow-through" but also says "trail 2–3% by Day 3–4." These aren't contradictory — the time stop applies only if the stock is stalled; trailing applies when the move is still in progress.
- **"No mental stops" vs. "trail 2–3%"**: Trailing is a systematic stop rule, not a mental one. The prohibition is against discretionary "I'll get out when it feels right" stops.
- **Volume requirement stated loosely**: The raw mentions reference "volume burst" but never give a precise volume multiplier. The 4% scan typically uses EP9M (9M+) as the volume threshold, but exact volume criteria for non-EP momentum bursts is underspecified.
