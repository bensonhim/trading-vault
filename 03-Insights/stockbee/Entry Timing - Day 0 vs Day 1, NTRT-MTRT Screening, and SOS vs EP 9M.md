# Deep Dive — Entry Timing: Day 0 vs Day 1, NTRT/MTRT Screening, and SOS vs EP 9M

Your question has three parts:
1. Should I enter on Day 0 or Day 1?
2. How do I screen potential stocks on Day 0 (before the 9M volume confirms)?
3. For SOS setups, when do I enter?

---

## Part 1: Day 0 vs Day 1 — The Two Entry Paths

There are **two distinct entry paths** in Pradeep's trade factory, and which one you use depends on the stock's behavior:

### Path A: Direct Entry (Day 0 = the day of the EP)

This is for **small-cap, neglected stocks with earth-shattering catalysts** where the stock is going to explode immediately and you can't afford to wait.

> "If the capitalization is small, there is lot of neglect, and your analysis shows that this stock is going to double, triple, then I will just do a PG [opening gap]. ... I can't wait for a delayed reaction EP. You just go and buy it as an OPG because this news is so earth shattering that it is just going to go."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

**When to use Path A:**
- Small capitalization stock
- Heavily neglected (nobody knows about it)
- Mind-blowing catalyst (triple-digit revenue growth, FDA approval, game-changing product)
- The stock is going to gap up and keep going — if you wait, you miss it

**How Pradeep found his life-changing Facebook trade:**
> "I bought 50,000 shares of Facebook in premarket at around $32 or 33, and my target was $70. ... I bought it in premarket, and I put a substantial amount of money."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

### Path B: Delayed Reaction Entry (Day 1 or Day 2)

This is for **larger-cap, well-known stocks** where the initial gap is too big and you need to wait for a pullback setup.

> "If the stock has a higher capitalization, it is a big capitalization stock and it is something good, then you know that it is going to gap up. That gap up is something like classic example of yesterday, APLD. ... People know about this. They're already positioned. Some of the people might have bought it here, so they take profit. They use this, and then the stock gives you a delayed reaction kind of an entry."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

> "For the larger stocks, the capitalization being larger, float being larger, that is what will be something which I would look for a delayed reaction. ... I might trade it for that 20, 30, 50%. Sometimes they make 50% moves in one day. And then after that, you wait for a delayed reaction EP."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

**When to use Path B:**
- Large-cap or well-known stock
- Stock gaps up too much on Day 0 (e.g., 70%+ gap — "no matter what, catalyst is there, is it worth 70%? It got priced in in the premarket itself")
- You expect the initial enthusiasm to fade, then set up again
- The float is large, so the move takes longer to develop

### The ELVA Example (July 16)

Pradeep gave a live example of the delayed reaction approach:

> "ELVA from yesterday, it might work as a delayed reaction. Yesterday, it gapped up too much, but keep it on your watch list. It might work."  
> — July 16 AM

ELVA gapped up 70% on Day 0 — too much to enter. Pradeep put it on his watch list and waited for it to set up again on Day 1 or Day 2.

### Which Path Should You Use?

| Factor | Path A (Direct, Day 0) | Path B (Delayed Reaction, Day 1+) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------------------|
| Market cap | Small / micro | Large / well-known |
| Neglect level | Heavily neglected | Well-known |
| Gap size on Day 0 | Moderate (buyable) | Too big (70%+ = skip) |
| Catalyst strength | Earth-shattering | Good but not unknown |
| Your experience | Advanced (fast decisions) | Beginner-friendly (more time) |
| Risk | Higher (might fade) | Lower (wait for setup) |

## Part 2: How to Screen Potential Stocks on Day 0

This is the **NTRT/MTRT process** — Pradeep's daily routine for finding EP candidates before the 9M volume confirms.

### NTRT = Nighttime Is Right Time

After the market closes (4:00 PM+), earnings are released. The stock will gap in the aftermarket.

**What to do:**
1. After 4:00 PM, scan for stocks that released earnings
2. Look at aftermarket scans — stocks gapping up or down
3. Note the gap direction and size
4. This is your preliminary watch list for tomorrow

> "When the market after 4:00, you start looking at which stock has released earnings. Now the stock, when it releases earnings, the market is going to react to a shock by having a gap. So there'll be a gap in the aftermarket itself."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

### MTRT = Morning Time Is Right Time

Before the market opens (premarket), you check which stocks are gapping up or down with news.

**What to do:**
1. Before 9:30 AM, scan for stocks gapping 4%+ on 100K+ volume (after hours or premarket)
2. For each stock, find the catalyst — use briefing.com, Benzinga, catalyst trackers, or AI (Grok/Gemini)
3. Classify the catalyst: Is it a real catalyst (earnings, product launch) or a story/fugazi?
4. Assess: Is the stock neglected? Does it have triple-digit revenue growth?
5. Decide: Direct entry (Path A) or wait for delayed reaction (Path B)?

> "I look at all stocks which are gapped up 4% plus on 100k volume, either after hours or premarket. And then I go and look for what was the catalyst."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

### The Daily Process

```
NTRT (After 4 PM):
├── Scan aftermarket earnings releases
├── Note stocks gapping up/down significantly
└── Preliminary watch list for tomorrow

MTRT (Before 9:30 AM):
├── Scan premarket gaps (4%+ on 100K+ volume)
├── For each stock:
│   ├── Find the catalyst (briefing.com, Benzinga, AI)
│   ├── Classify: real catalyst or story/fugazi?
│   ├── Check: is it neglected? Revenue growth?
│   ├── Check: how big is the gap? (70%+ = too big for direct entry)
│   └── Decide: Path A (direct entry at open) or Path B (wait for delayed reaction)
└── Final watch list with entry plan

Market Open (9:30 AM):
├── Path A stocks: enter at open (OPG) if catalyst is earth-shattering
├── Path B stocks: watch for pullback and setup
└── Run EP 9M scan during the day to confirm volume building
```

### What to Look For in the Catalyst

From the Episodic Pivots video:

> "You're looking for something which is heavily neglected. And which has a mind-blowing catalyst in terms of earnings and sales. ... Revenue growth is what I'm looking at. Typically, I'm looking at triple-digit kind of a revenue growth, but triple-digit is not necessary. Even something which is like a 39% plus sales growth, depending on what is the total sales is enough. 39% sales growth, two quarters in a row is what I would be looking at."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

**Two types of earnings EP:**
1. **Turnaround** — Company was losing money/sales, then turns around (new CEO, new product). Example: Abercrombie.
2. **New growth discovery** — Market discovers a new growth story. Example: TransMedics, Duolingo.

> "Profits are a luxury which companies can have currently. But what is most important is revenue growth."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

### The End-of-Day Study (for learning)

> "Go at the end of the day, see which stock made the biggest move for today, and then by dollar or percentage term and go and see what was the news on it premarket. Was there a news on this premarket? How would that news — what time was the news released? And put a database of things."  
> — Mind Clarity SIP video (Jan 6, 2026)

This is how you build the pattern recognition over 1-2 earnings seasons:
1. End of day: find the biggest movers
2. Check: what was the premarket news?
3. What time was the news released?
4. How did the stock react?
5. Build a database of patterns

> "If you do this religiously every day starting Monday, and if you do it for this entire earning season, your first earning season, you may not capture many of them because you don't have enough experience. But it's going to change your life."  
> — Episodic Pivots video (Jan 9, 2026)

## Part 3: SOS vs EP 9M — When to Enter

### The Core Rule: First Day of a Swing

> "If you want to be a trader and if you cannot understand the concept of buying on a start of a swing on the first day of a move, I think the game is over. ... That is absolutely unbreakable law of trading. You buy it on the first day of a swing."  
> — Start Doing This video (Jan 2, 2026)

> "Anything which is second day, third day — if you buy a second day of a swing, you should not be doing swing trading. ... Why are you buying on the second day? Is there any reason why you should be buying on the second day?"  
> — Start Doing This video (Jan 2, 2026)

### SOS Supersedes Everything — But Only on Day 1

> "SOS supersedes every single thing. You buy a SOS if you want to make money."  
> — Start Doing This video (Jan 2, 2026)

But this means SOS on **Day 1** (the first day of the swing). If you're on Day 2 or Day 3, you're chasing, not trading a setup.

### What Is SOS?

SOS = Stock Open Strong (or "Stock Open Signal"). It's a **day trading setup** where a stock opens with unusual strength and you trade the intraday momentum.

SOS is related to but different from EP 9M:
- **EP 9M** = swing trading setup (hold 3-5 days)
- **SOS** = day trading setup (hold minutes to hours)
- **SIPs** = Stock in Play (day trading, any stock with a catalyst and volume)

From the Mind Clarity SIP video:

> "Many of the people who focus on SIPs, they call this EP. ... They are actually trading SIPs, but they call it EP because EP sounds more sexy than SIPs."  
> — Mind Clarity SIP video (Jan 6, 2026)

### When to Use SOS Entry

SOS is for **day traders** who are watching the screen and can make fast decisions:

> "You need to be sitting in front of a computer. You need to have multiple screens. You need to be watching it. You need to babysit your position on SIPs. ... It's a job."  
> — Mind Clarity SIP video (Jan 6, 2026)

> "Everything happens in ten to fifteen minutes also. Sometimes you're going to get a $14, $15, $16, $17, or $60 move happen in ten or fifteen minutes. And if you blink and if you didn't sell, you are going to be [stuck]."  
> — Mind Clarity SIP video (Jan 6, 2026)

### The Google Short Example (SOS on the Short Side)

Pradeep's Google short on July 16 was essentially a **SOS-style entry on the short side**:

1. He saw the Gemini delay news during lunch (MTRT — though midday, not premarket)
2. He ran his scan — Google showed up as EP 9M to the downside
3. He saw it was breaking down near the high (range expansion)
4. He entered the short
5. Next day, moved stop to breakeven

This was a **Day 0 entry** — he entered on the day the setup appeared, not the next day. The 9M volume confirmed during the day as selling accelerated.

### Can You Enter on Day 1 for EP 9M?

**Yes — this is the "delayed reaction EP" path.** If a stock had an EP 9M on Day 0 but gapped up too much to enter, you wait for Day 1 or Day 2 when it pulls back and sets up again.

From July 16 AM:

> "ELVA from yesterday, it might work as a delayed reaction. Yesterday, it gapped up too much, but keep it on your watch list. It might work."

From July 17 AM:

> "Big move down? Highest volume traded in years. That was a clear signal. You just wait for a delayed reaction EP and short."

### The Decision Tree

```
DAY 0 (Premarket / Market Open):
├── NTRT/MTRT screening finds stock with catalyst
├── Stock gaps up/down significantly
├── Decision:
│   ├── Gap is moderate + catalyst is earth-shattering + small cap
│   │   └── PATH A: Enter at open (OPG) — Day 0 entry
│   │
│   ├── Gap is too big (70%+) + large cap + well-known
│   │   └── PATH B: Add to watch list, wait for delayed reaction
│   │
│   └── You're a day trader with fast skills
│       └── SOS/SIPs: Trade intraday momentum — Day 0 entry
│
DAY 1 (Next Day):
├── Stock had EP 9M on Day 0
├── Stock gapped too much on Day 0
├── Stock pulls back and sets up on Day 1
├── Decision:
│   ├── Setup qualifies (catalyst still valid, stock setting up)
│   │   └── Enter delayed reaction EP — Day 1 entry
│   │
│   └── Setup does not qualify (catalyst faded, no setup)
│       └── Skip
│
DAY 2+:
├── Stock had EP 9M on Day 0
├── This might be a DEP if:
│   ├── It's a fund-quality stock (1000+ rating)
│   ├── The EP 9M happened in the last 25 days
│   └── It's setting up again now
└── Otherwise: you're chasing. Don't.
```

## Part 4: Summary — Your Specific Questions Answered

### "Should I enter on Day 0 or Day 1?"

**It depends on the stock:**
- **Small-cap, neglected, earth-shattering catalyst** → Day 0 (enter at open or premarket)
- **Large-cap, well-known, big gap** → Day 1+ (wait for delayed reaction)
- **Day trader with fast skills** → Day 0 (SOS/SIPs intraday)
- **Swing trader** → Day 0 if gap is buyable, Day 1 if gap is too big

### "I scanned stocks on Day 1 — should I enter?"

If the stock had its EP 9M on Day 0 and you're looking at it on Day 1:
- **Yes**, if it's setting up as a delayed reaction EP (pulled back from the initial gap, now setting up again)
- **No**, if it's just continuing up from Day 0 without a pullback — you'd be chasing on the second day of a swing

> "Why are you buying on the second day? Is there any reason why you should be buying on the second day?"  
> — Start Doing This video (Jan 2, 2026)

### "I don't know how to screen potential stocks on Day 0"

Use the **NTRT/MTRT process**:
1. **NTRT (after 4 PM)**: Scan aftermarket earnings releases, note stocks gapping significantly
2. **MTRT (before 9:30 AM)**: Scan premarket gaps (4%+ on 100K+ volume), find the catalyst for each, classify (real vs story), assess neglect and revenue growth
3. **At open**: Enter Path A stocks directly, add Path B stocks to watch list
4. **During the day**: Run EP 9M scan to confirm volume is building

### "For SOS, when do I enter?"

SOS is a **Day 0 intraday entry**. You enter when the stock opens strong and you see the momentum intraday. This requires:
- Sitting in front of the screen
- Fast decision-making
- Tight stop management
- Multiple screens
- Babysitting the position

If you can't do all of that, don't trade SOS. Use the delayed reaction EP path instead.
