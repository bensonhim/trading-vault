## Part 2: Understanding EP 9M — Is 9M Volume Really That Important?

### Your Confusion Is Common

You said: "When I look at the stocks, I found so many of them are not having 9M when need to entry, but it got 9M after the market."

This is a fundamental misunderstanding of what EP 9M is. Let me clarify.

### What EP 9M Actually Is

EP 9M is **not** a "volume must be 9 million at the moment of entry." It is a **scan condition** that identifies stocks where significant volume happened on a specific day — the day of the episodic pivot.

The scan condition is:
- **Stock traded 9 million+ shares on that day** (the day of the setup)
- **Stock moved 4%+ on that day** (the episodic pivot)
- **This is the start of a swing** (not a continuation)

The 9M is a **filter**, not an entry trigger. It filters the universe of 12,366 tradeable instruments down to the ~2% where money is actually flowing that day.

From the January 22, 2026 video "EP9 Million Bullish Primary Setup":

You are isolating the top 2% stocks by volume traded. ... These are the stocks which are aggressively being bought or aggressively being sold on this particular day."

### Why 9M Matters

| Without 9M filter | With 9M filter |
|---|---|
| 12,366 instruments to scan | ~250-300 stocks to scan |
| No idea where money is flowing | Clear signal: money is here today |
| Random breakouts look the same | Only high-volume breakouts show up |
| You might buy a low-volume breakout that has no institutional backing | You only buy breakouts where institutions are clearly involved |

### The "9M After the Market" Confusion

You said stocks "got 9M after the market." This can happen in two ways:

**Case 1: The volume builds during the day.**
EP 9M is a daily scan condition. The 9M is measured at the end of the day (or intraday as volume accumulates). You don't need 9M at 9:30 AM. You need 9M by the close. The stock may have 2M shares by 10 AM, 5M by noon, and 9M+ by 3 PM. The setup is valid if it closes with 9M+.

**Case 2: You're looking at the wrong day.**
The EP 9M happens on Day 1 (the day of the episodic pivot). You may enter on Day 1 (if you see it intraday) or Day 2 (delayed reaction EP). If you're checking a stock on Day 3 and it doesn't have 9M, that's expected — the 9M was on Day 1, not Day 3.

### The Three Types of EP 9M

| Type | What It Is | When to Enter |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **EP 9M (standard)** | Stock has 9M+ volume and 4%+ move on Day 1 | Day 1 (intraday) or Day 2 |
| **Delayed Reaction EP 9M** | Stock had EP 9M on Day 1, gapped up too much, then sets up on Day 2 or 3 | After the gap fades and it sets up |
| **DEP** | EP 9M that happened in the last 25 days on a fund-quality (1000+) stock, now setting up again | When the DEP setup triggers |

### Why 9M and Not 5M or 19M?

From the April 24 video:

> "If you think 9M is too much, you can bump this up to 19M. If you had one [more zero], what you can do is you'll drop this to even a smaller amount, which will be around 130, which is the top 1% of the stocks trading in the market."

The 9M number is a **practical threshold** that isolates the top ~2% of stocks by volume. It's not magic — it's a filter. You could use 19M (top 1%) for even more selective scans, or lower it for more candidates. Pradeep uses 9M because it gives ~250-300 stocks per day, which is manageable to scan manually.

### The Qualification: Cat, Dog, or Liquid Lava

9M volume alone is NOT enough. Every EP 9M must be classified:

| Classification | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Cat** | Real catalyst (earnings, product launch, big order) | Tradeable — size based on catalyst strength |
| **Dog** | Story/fugazi catalyst (crypto juice, AI rebrand) | Tradeable but smaller size — story may fade |
| **Liquid Lava** | No catalyst, just volume | Skip — no reason to go up |

From July 17 AM:

> "For EP 9M, where does tight candle come in? There is an EP 9M. Cats, dogs, liquid lava. ... Where does tight candle come in? I have no idea."

The point: EP 9M is a volume signal. The qualification (cat/dog/liquid lava) is the catalyst assessment. Both are needed.

### For Mega-Cap Stocks

From July 17 AM (the last question of the day):

> "Is 9M volume relevant for mega-cap stocks?"

Pradeep has addressed this in multiple videos. For mega-caps like NVDA, AAPL, GOOG, MSFT — which routinely trade 10M+ shares daily — the 9M filter is less useful because they always have high volume. For these stocks, the key is the **4% move + catalyst** component of the EP 9M scan. The volume is always there; the question is whether there's a catalyst driving a 4%+ move.

For Google specifically, the short worked because:
1. It had a clear negative catalyst (Gemini delay)
2. It was a range expansion breakdown (4%+ move)
3. Volume was high (as always for GOOG)
4. Funds were hiding there → cascading stops

### Summary: Is 9M Important?

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Is 9M volume important? | Yes — it isolates the top 2% of stocks where money is flowing |
| Do you need 9M at entry? | No — 9M is a daily scan condition, measured by close |
| What if a stock gets 9M after the market? | The 9M is for the day of the EP, not the day of your entry |
| Can you trade without 9M? | You can, but you lose the volume filter that isolates institutional activity |
| Is 9M enough by itself? | No — you also need catalyst (cat/dog/liquid lava) + SA + 4%+ move |
| What about mega-caps? | They always have volume; focus on the 4% move + catalyst component |
