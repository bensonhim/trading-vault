# Institutional Quality and 20% Study — Definitions and Usage

Pradeep filters the trading universe by *who owns the stock* (fund ownership tiers) and *where the broad market stands* (20% study / T2108) — two orthogonal gates that together decide which setups get sized up and which get suppressed entirely.

---

## Institutional Quality Tiers

Pradeep adapted Kevin Marder's "500+ funds = Liquid Glamour" idea and raised the bar to four tiers based on the number of mutual funds holding a stock.

| Tier | Fund Count | Character | Sizing Posture |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Liquid Glamour** | 5,000+ funds | Must-buy for every mutual fund (e.g. [[LLY]], [[GOOGL]], [[JPM]], [[V]], [[MA]], [[NVDA]], [[AAPL]], [[AMZN]], [[META]], [[AVGO]], [[MSFT]]) | Largest size — up to 70–100% of account on a clean swing |
| **Institutional Quality** | 2,500+ funds | Serious institutional commitment; ~166 stocks qualify | Large size; primary swing universe |
| **Gaining Interest** | 1,000+ funds | Newer IPOs / spin-offs attracting institutional money (e.g. [[GEV]], [[CEG]], [[PLTR]], [[RDDT]], [[ASTL]], [[VLTO]]) | Medium size; watchlist for next-generation leaders |
| **Speculative Glamour** | < 1,000 funds | Story stocks, micro-caps, lottery tickets | Small size only; never core position |

> "I primarily focus on these two — 5,000 funds and 2,500 funds." — Pradeep, *Stock Selection Guide: Fund Ownership*

### How fund ownership is used for sizing

- 5,000+ funds → "Can I go 70% on that idea? With capital even? I can put like Nvidia as a clean setup. Can I put in all my capital? Yeah."
- 2,500+ funds → large swings, "more predictable or swings on these are not going to have these crazy 20% kind of gaps"
- < 1,000 funds → at most $100k–$200k on a $3–$5 stock; "one disaster away from taking a 20% hit"
- Reversal Bullish is *only* traded on 1,000+ fund stocks — "on a random stock, the probability of it working is low, because there is not many people sitting there and waiting for it to come down"

### Fund ownership changes as information

A stock *joining* or *leaving* a tier is a signal, not noise.

| Event | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Stock joins 5,000+ funds | Bullish — institutions committing | [[JNJ]] approaching 5,000+ ownership |
| Stock drops from 5,000+ funds | Bearish — something went wrong | [[ADBE]] dropped → stock down 40%; [[CRM]] dropped → declined |
| Stock drops from 2,500+ funds | Fundamental deterioration | [[DOW]] went from 2,500+ to 1,453 funds (negative growth rate) |
| New IPO attracting 1,000+ funds | Next-generation leader candidate | [[GEV]], [[CEG]], [[PLTR]], [[RDDT]] |

> "There is an information in this, right? And when stocks are dropped by funds… the stock is down 40%." — Pradeep, *Stock Selection Guide: Fund Ownership*

## DEP Definition (Delayed Reaction EP)

DEP is the "yogi" version of [[EP 9M]] — a planned, deliberate first-pullback entry *after* the market has already flagged a stock via a 4% breakout on 9M+ volume.

**Scan criteria:**
- Volume ≥ 8,900,000 shares (top 2% by volume that day)
- Price ≥ $3
- Volume today > Volume yesterday (volume spike)
- Close today / Close yesterday ≥ 1.04 (4% breakout, long or short)
- Occurred within last 25 days

**Hard requirements for a valid DEP candidate:**

1. **1,000+ fund ownership** — institutional quality floor
2. **$10B+ market cap** — large caps digest catalysts; small caps just run
3. **Genuine catalyst** — earnings, big order, game-changing contract (not a story stock with zero sales like [[OKLO]] or [[QS]])
4. **Institutional quality** — orderly pullback, low-volume digestion, not choppy

> "The best money in DEP is always in institutional quality stocks, stocks which are owned by thousand plus funds kind of a thing." — Pradeep, *Mind Clarity DEP*

> "Not every stock is going to give you a delayed reaction entry… stocks with lower capitalization, you just have to buy them on the EP 9M day." — Pradeep, *Mind Clarity DEP*

### DEP process

1. Run scan after close / before open (not intraday)
2. Sort by max volume 25-day — prioritize highest-volume triggers
3. Research catalyst for each unfamiliar stock (X-ray, ChatGPT, Gemini)
4. Reject story stocks with no sales/earnings
5. Add genuine-catalyst stocks to watchlist
6. Wait for orderly low-volume pullback (3–10 days for longs, longer for shorts)
7. Enter on SOS hint with close stop (2–3%)
8. Try up to 3 times before giving up on a high-conviction idea

## 20% Study Definition

The **20% study** (Pradeep's primary Market Monitor indicator) counts the number of stocks in the universe that are up **25%+ in the last 65 trading days**.

| Reading | Market Regime | Action |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **Above 600–1,000** | Excessive bullishness | Trade bullish breakouts; most will work |
| **Around 200** | Neutral / transitional | Selective; calibrate risk |
| **Below 200** | Bearish — primary indicator extreme | Prepare for turn; don't stay bearish |
| **Below 20 (T2108)** | Solid bearish market | Suppress long setups |
| **Single digits (T2108)** | Capitulation / major bottom | Bull markets start here; get ready to buy |

> "If you just trade breakouts when the primary indicator is green, you will find most of the time, most of the breakouts work during that time." — Pradeep, *Market Monitor Guide Part 8*

> "If you use excessive bullishness to get out, that will be the greatest error you'll make." — Pradeep, *Market Monitor Guide Part 8*

### How 20% study should be used in the Trade Ideas system

- **Primary indicator green (>200 stocks up 25% in 65 days)** → generate all setup types (EP 9M, DEP, ANT, Reversal, WSS)
- **Primary indicator red (<200)** → suppress long-side EP 9M / DEP / ANT; favor shorts
- **Primary indicator + T2108 both extreme bearish** → *do not* stay bearish — prepare for bottom; this is where 3–5 year bull markets start
- **20% study below 20** → suppress DEP and ANT generation entirely; market is distributing, breakouts will fail

## T2108 Definition

**T2108** (Worden indicator) = percentage of stocks trading above their **40-day moving average**.

| Reading | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| > 90% | Start of a new bull market (not a top) — bullishness persists for years |
| 20–90% | Normal range |
| **Below 20%** | Bearish market — "you are in a solid bearish market" |
| **Below 10%** | Capitulation — "bull market start from such levels" |
| **Single digits** | Major bottom — 2008 bottom hit 1.6%; COVID bottom also single digit |

> "Capitulation kind of selling is required for the market to bottom." — Pradeep, *Market Monitor Guide Part 8*

**Confluence signal (most powerful):** Primary indicator below 200 **AND** T2108 in single digits = bottom that lasts 3–5 years.

> "Bottoms which are formed at that stage tend to last for many years." — Pradeep, *Market Monitor Guide Part 8*

T2108 is **not** standalone — used in conjunction with the primary 20% study indicator. Excellent for finding **bottoms**, poor for finding **tops** (tops form by diminishing participation, not excessive breadth).

> "There are smart funds and there are dumb funds… there is no point in following Vanguard funds." — Pradeep, *Stock Selection Guide: Fund Ownership*

**Cluster signal:** When multiple smart funds buy the same stock at the same time (e.g. [[ELF]] — Fidelity + WhaleRock + Oppenheimer + T. Rowe all entered simultaneously), that is a very powerful signal.

**Lag caveat:** 13F filings lag 6 weeks to 2 months — by the time you see the filing, the fund may have bought months ago. Use fund holdings as *idea generation*, then wait for a setup.

## EP 9M — Cat / Dog / Liquid Lava Classification

Within [[EP 9M]], Pradeep classifies candidates by catalyst quality to decide sizing:

| Category | Definition | Sizing |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| **Cat** | Clear catalyst (earnings, big order, contract) — e.g. [[PNL]] | **Large size** — up to 25–30% of account |
| **Dog** | Story stock, no revenue/earnings — e.g. [[QS]], [[OKLO]] | **Small size** — < 5% of account |
| **Liquid Lava** | Trades 9M+ volume *daily* (real business) — e.g. [[INTC]], [[MRVL]], [[AAPL]], [[DELL]], [[DOW]], [[C]], [[BAC]], [[WFC]], [[NVDA]], [[SNDK]] | **Large size** — can size up because real business + liquidity |

> "Can I look and I there is a lot of difference in 5,000 plus fund ownership kind of a thing? I am willing to put eighty, ninety, 100% of my account on a good swing on something like Amazon, Microsoft, Apple. Will I put 100% of my account on just a liquid lava like Oklo? Tell me." — Pradeep, *Stock Selection Guide: Fund Ownership*

> "You can just do EP nine million on institutional quality… are there enough opportunities in that? Tell me. Plus effort also. And you'll catch all the big moves." — Pradeep, *How to 9 Million EP*

**Liquidity ≠ fund ownership** — these are "two mutually exclusive factors." A stock can be liquid lava without institutional backing ([[OKLO]], [[MARA]]). Fund ownership is the *quality* gate; liquidity is the *capacity* gate.

## Changes Needed to Trade Ideas System

1. **Filter DEP candidates to 1,000+ fund ownership only.** Any DEP candidate with < 1,000 funds is a story stock / speculative glamour — reject at scan stage. This alone will cut false DEP generation dramatically.

2. **Suppress DEP and ANT generation when 20% study is below 20.** In a bearish/distributing market, breakouts fail. Gate setup generation on the primary breadth reading; emit a "market regime: suppress longs" flag instead of flooding the radar with doomed setups.

3. **Add fund ownership tier to each ticker detail.** Each ticker in the Trade Ideas output should display its tier (5,000+ / 2,500+ / 1,000+ / < 1,000) so the user can size accordingly without a separate lookup.

4. **Add 20% study reading to the market regime section.** Surface the current 20% study count and T2108 reading at the top of every daily Trade Ideas file, with a regime label (bullish / neutral / bearish / capitulation).

5. **Use T2108 as a secondary indicator for regime detection.** When T2108 drops below 20, flip the regime to "bearish — suppress longs." When T2108 hits single digits *and* primary indicator is below 200, flip to "capitulation — prepare for bottom" and *reactivate* long setup generation with elevated conviction.

6. **Fix Trade Ideas file naming convention.** Files are currently named after the *data* date; they should be named after the *target/trading* date (the day the ideas are actionable). A file generated from July 21 close data, actionable July 22, should be named `Trade-Ideas-2026-07-22.md`.

7. **Flag tier transitions as events.** When a stock joins or leaves the 5,000+ or 2,500+ tier, emit an alert in the Trade Ideas file — these are bullish/bearish signals in themselves (Adobe, CRM examples).

8. **Classify EP 9M candidates as Cat / Dog / Liquid Lava.** Add a catalyst-quality field to each EP 9M entry so sizing guidance is explicit: Cat = large size, Dog = small size only, Liquid Lava = large size permitted.
