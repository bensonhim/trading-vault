# Lesson 8: Trade Management — When to Sell

> **Core Question:** When do I sell, and how do I manage the trade after entry?
> **Answer:** 80/20 rule by default. Move to breakeven fast. Time stop momentum bursts by day 5. Let winners run on DEP.

---

## The 80-20 Rule (Default Exit)

> *"The exit is selling to strength, eighty twenty per trail. Basically, 99% of the time, it is eighty twenty, and then on the 20% trail."*

| Stage | Action | Trigger | Source |
|-------|--------|---------|--------|
| **20% gap up** | Sell 80%, trail 20% | Morning gap up 20% | [[21. Process Guide]] |
| **100% move** | Sell 80%, keep 20% with trailing stop | Stock doubles | [[16. DEP Guide]] |
| **40–50% move** | Tighten stop aggressively (don't peel yet) | Stock up 40–50% | [[16. DEP Guide]] |
| **10% in one day** | Take 80-20 | Reversal setup, 1-day 10% | [[22. Reversals Guide]] |

### Why 80/20?

- You lock in 80% of the profit at a good price
- You let the remaining 20% run for a potential home run
- If the stock reverses, you've already banked most of the gain
- If the stock continues, the trailing 20% captures the big move

### When NOT to Peel

> *"You'll never hit a home run if you're peeling it."*

- On a **genuine Catalyst DEP** with life-changing potential (300%+), holding the full position may be appropriate
- Peel only when the move is **speculative** or when you're **over-sized**

## Breakeven Management

**The #1 rule of trade management:** Move to breakeven as soon as the stock opens positive the next morning.

| Setup | When to Move to Breakeven | Buffer | Source |
|-------|--------------------------|--------|--------|
| **All setups** | First thing in morning if stock opens positive | Breakeven or breakeven+ | [[22. Reversals Guide]] |
| **DEP** | As soon as stock shows life | Breakeven + $1 | [[16. DEP Guide]] |
| **ANTS** | Next morning if breakout; or 4–5% in 30 min | Breakeven | [[08. Anticipation (ANTS) Guide]] |
| **Sugar Baby** | Within 1 hour after entry | Breakeven | [[14. Sugar Babies Guide]] |
| **FHP** | After initial move | Breakeven + $1 | [[15. Start of a Swing Guide]] |

> *"The first thing in the morning, if a stock opens positive, move your stop to breakeven."*

## Time-Based Exits

Every setup has a maximum hold time. If the trade hasn't worked by then, **exit regardless of P/L.**

| Setup | Max Hold | Time Stop Rule | Source |
|-------|----------|---------------|--------|
| **Momentum Burst** | 3–5 days | Kill by midday day 3 if no follow-through | [[Bullish Momentum Burst Guide-Section 1]] |
| **Reversal Bullish** | 3–5 days | Exit if doesn't work next day | [[22. Reversals Guide]] |
| **Sugar Baby** | 2–3 days | Exit when momentum fades | [[14. Sugar Babies Guide]] |
| **DEP (long side)** | Until stop hit or 100%+ | Tighten at 50%; sell 80% at 100% | [[16. DEP Guide]] |
| **EP (life-changing)** | Weeks to months | 80/20 at major targets; let 20% ride | [[13. Episodic Pivots Guide]] |

> *"Most of the 50% move happens in five to fifteen days. Even for a hundred, two hundred percent move, and that's all."*

## Peeling (Advanced Technique)

> *"Peeling a position, doing eighty twenty is a very advanced trading technique... I'm putting in 60–80% of my account on a swing trade. And then I'm taking 80% off on a second day or a third day."*

| Scenario | Action | Source |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **Large position (50%+ account)** | Peel 80% on day 2–3, trail 20% | [[Bullish Momentum Burst Guide-Section 1]] |
| **Small position (10% account)** | Peeling doesn't make sense — just hold or exit | [[Bullish Momentum Burst Guide-Section 1]] |
| **Home run hunting** | Don't peel — hold full position for 300%+ | [[16. DEP Guide]] |
| **Liquid lava (large cap)** | Slow to peel — day 3–4 | [[21. Process Guide]] |
| **NVIDIA 5% gap** | Peel immediately | [[21. Process Guide]] |

## Overnight Risk Management

| Rule | Limit | Source |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Max overnight risk** | 6% of total capital (all stops hit simultaneously) | [[23. Bearish Trading Guide]] |
| **No pre-market / after-hours entries** | Stops active market hours only | [[Bullish Momentum Burst Guide-Section 1]] |
| **Gap down at open** | Exit immediately, no questions | [[22. Reversals Guide]] |

The 6% overnight rule: if you have 6 positions, each risking 1%, that's 6% total overnight risk. If all stop out simultaneously at open, you lose 6%. This is your maximum scenario.

## What NOT to Do

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| Let winner turn into loser | Move to breakeven quickly | Winners become losers when not managed |
| Hold momentum burst past day 5 | Time stop: exit day 3–5 | Probability of reversal increases |
| Not peeling at 100% | Sell 80%, trail 20% | Gives back massive gains |
| Peeling too early (home runs) | Hold full position if seeking 300%+ | Never catches the big move |
| Mental stops / no hard stops | Hard stops, market hours only | "Mental stop means at some point you won't take it" |
| Adding to losers | Exit at stop; redeploy capital | "Never add to a loser" |

## The Math of 80/20

```
Example: You buy 1000 shares at $10
Stock goes to $20 (100% move)

80/20 exit:
  Sell 800 shares at $20 = $16,000 profit (locked in)
  Hold 200 shares with trailing stop

Worst case: trailing 200 shares stop out at $15
  Additional profit: 200 × $5 = $1,000
  Total: $17,000 profit

Best case: stock goes to $40 (home run)
  200 shares × $30 = $6,000 more
  Total: $22,000 profit

You banked $16,000 with zero risk on the 80%.
The 20% either adds more profit or costs nothing extra.
```
