# Lesson 5: Position Sizing — How Much to Buy

> **Core Question:** How much of my account should I put in this trade?
> **Answer:** Position size = Account × Risk% / Stop Width. This is math, not feeling.

---

## The Core Formula

```
Position Size = (Account × Risk Per Trade) / Stop Width

Example:
Account: $100,000
Risk: 1% ($1,000)
Stop: 2%

Position Size = $1,000 / 0.02 = $50,000 (50% of account)
```

**This is the single most important formula in the entire system.**

- **Closer stop** → **bigger position** (DEP, ANTS)
- **Wider stop** → **smaller position** (EP Day 1, story stocks)
- **Higher conviction** → **bigger position** (DEP with catalyst)
- **Lower conviction** → **smaller position** (speculative, no catalyst)

---

## The Fundamental Relationship

> *"How much you can put in a trade is a function of your stop."* — [[15. Start of a Swing Guide|SOS Part 9]]

| Stop Width | With 1% Risk | With 0.5% Risk | Setup |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| **<1%** (ANTS) | 100% of account | 50% of account | Anticipation — tightest stop |
| **0.5–2.5%** (DEP) | 40–100% of account | 20–50% of account | DEP — closest stop = biggest size |
| **2–5%** (SOS) | 20–50% of account | 10–25% of account | Standard breakout |
| **8–20%** (EP Day 1) | 5–12.5% of account | 2.5–6.25% | Catalyst day — widest stop = smallest size |
| **10%+** (story stock) | 10% of account max | 5% of account max | Speculative |

**This is why DEP is so powerful.** Not because the setup is better than EP — it's the SAME catalyst. But the stop is 10× tighter, so you can put 10× more money in.

## Position Size by Setup

| Setup | Position Size | Risk Per Trade | Why This Size |
|-------|--------------|----------------|---------------|
| **DEP** | 25–50%+ of account | <1% or 1% | Tightest stop enables massive size |
| **EP Day 1** | 10–20% of account | 1–2% | Wide stop limits size |
| **SOS** | 3K–5K shares (per $1M account) | 0.5–1% | Standard flat size |
| **ANTS** | 10–20% of account | <1% | Very tight stop |
| **Reversal** | 5K–20K shares | 0.5–1% | Flat share count; tight stop |
| **Sugar Baby** | 25% of account | Varies | SB stocks move 40%+ in 2–3 days |
| **FHP** | Can be 50%+ of account | 1% | <2% stop on high-priced stocks |
| **Story stock** | 5–10% of account max | 1% or less | High volatility = small size |

## Conviction Sizing

Not all trades are equal. **Catalyst quality determines conviction, and conviction determines size.**

| Catalyst Quality | Size | Example |
|----------------|------|---------|
| **A+** (real catalyst + institutional + SB) | Full size (25–50%+) | SMCI earnings beat + 1000 funds + on SB list |
| **B** (clear catalyst) | Standard (10–20%) | Standard DEP setup with identifiable catalyst |
| **C** (speculative) | Small (5–10%) | Story stock with hot theme but no fundamentals |
| **D** (no catalyst) | Skip | Random breakout without identifiable catalyst |

> *"The couple who is very successful doing DEP, their focus list is never more than five."* — Small watchlist = high conviction per trade.

---

## The Druckenmiller Model

Stanley Druckenmiller (from *Market Wizards*): 65 positions, 0.5–2% risk per position, constantly reshuffling.

The key insight: **It's not about "what stock" but "how much risk."** If each trade risks 1%, you can be wrong 50 times before you've lost half your account. But one 10× winner on a 50% DEP position pays for all the losses.

## Account Risk Levels

| Risk Level | Account % at Risk | Who Should Use This | Stop Width |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| **Conservative (new trader)** | 0.25–0.5% | Beginners; still learning setups | Any |
| **Standard** | 0.5–1% | Most traders | 2–5% |
| **Aggressive (expert)** | 1–2% | Confident in setup + catalyst | <1% (DEP/ANTS) |

> *"I have recommended that you should risk no more than 0.5 to 1% on any one trade."*

## Adding to Winners vs. Adding to Losers

| Action | Allowed? | When | Why |
|--------|---------|------|-----|
| Add to a **winner** (DEP breakout) | ✅ Yes | When stock breaks out from entry area | Position is validated |
| Add to a **momentum burst** (3–5 day) | ❌ NO | N/A — 3–5 day hold, no time to add | Move is too fast |
| Add on **trend trade** | ✅ Yes | Within first 1/3 of the trend | Trend is established |
| Add to a **loser** | ❌ NEVER | N/A — exit at stop | Compounding losses |

> *"I never add to a position, but I was so convinced about NBIS... I added to the position, which is the reason why I took a dump on this one."* — Lesson: even when it works once, adding to losers is a losing strategy.

## The FHP Advantage

High-priced stocks ($100+) have a unique advantage: **the stop is very tight in percentage terms**, enabling very large positions.

```
Example: Stock at $150
Stop at $148 (low of day) = $2 = 1.33% risk

Account: $100,000, Risk: 1%
Position = $1,000 / 0.0133 = $75,000 (75% of account)
Shares = $75,000 / $150 = 500 shares

Same risk as 10% of account on a $10 stock, but the stop is 10× tighter.
```

> *"Close stop enables ~50% account position"* — This is why Pradeep loves FHP (Full High-Priced) stocks.
