# Lesson 3: Entries — The Five Ways In

> **Core Question:** How do I enter a trade?
> **Answer:** Five entry methods, each with different timing, stop width, and position size. Choose based on catalyst, market conditions, and conviction.

---

## The Five Entry Methods

| # | Entry | When to Enter | Stop Width | Position Size | Key Rule |
|---|-------|--------------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| **1** | **EP 9M** | First 15 min on catalyst day | 8–20% (low of day) | 10–20% account | Seconds to decide; requires catalyst |
| **2** | **SOS** | First 30 min; first day of breakout | 2–5% (low of day) | 3–5K shares | Buy day 1, NEVER day 2 or 3 |
| **3** | **DEP** | Days 3–10 after catalyst | 0.5–2.5% (below consolidation) | 25–50%+ account | Closest stop = biggest size |
| **4** | **ANTS** | 2:58 PM day BEFORE breakout | <1% (below tight days) | 10–20% account | Buy BEFORE breakout; BSLO order |
| **5** | **Reversal** | 3:58–4:00 PM (last 2 min) | 0.5–2.5% (below open/prev close) | 5–20K shares | NEVER before 3:58 PM |

---

## The Decision Tree

```
Is there a catalyst TODAY?
├─ YES → Is it clear and genuine?
│   ├─ YES → Can I enter in first 15 min?
│   │   ├─ YES → EP 9M / OPG entry (Entry #1)
│   │   └─ NO → DEP candidate; add to watchlist (Entry #3)
│   └─ NO → Skip
└─ NO → Is the stock in tight consolidation with momentum?
    ├─ YES → ANTS entry at 2:58 PM day before (Entry #4)
    └─ NO → Is it 3:30 PM with exhaustion pattern?
        ├─ YES → Reversal entry at 3:58 PM (Entry #5)
        └─ NO → No trade today
```

## Entry #1: EP 9M (Episodic Pivot)

**What:** A stock gaps up 4%+ on 9M+ volume on a catalyst day (earnings, news, analyst action).

**Why it works:** The catalyst forces a structural revaluation. Institutional money flows in. The gap is the start of a multi-week or multi-month move.

**How to enter:**
- **OPG (Opening Price Guarantee):** Market-on-open order before 9:28. Stop 2.5–5% below open price.
- **First 10–15 minutes:** Buy as soon as you see range expansion + volume. Stop at low of day.

**The Magna 53 formula** (for life-changing EPs):
- **M** = Massive earnings acceleration (100%+, ideally 500%+)
- **A** = Acceleration of sales (39%+ for 2 quarters)
- **G** = Gap up 4%+
- **N** = Neglect (no rally into earnings, low fund ownership)
- **Cap 10×10** = Market cap <$10B, IPO'd within 10 years

**Stop:** 8–20% (low of day). Wide because catalyst days are volatile.

**Size:** 10–20% of account. Small relative to DEP because of the wide stop.

> *"You are not looking for episodic pivot for a small-time swing trade. The whole idea of trading episodic pivot is to change your life in one or two trades."*

**Key nuance:** Only 2–3 life-changing EPs per year. Most EP 9M stocks are good for 20–50%, not 300–800%.

## Entry #2: SOS (Start of a Swing)

**What:** The first day a stock breaks out with range expansion. This is the "grammar of the market" — every explosive move starts with a day-1 range expansion.

**Four SOS types:**
1. **Ground Zero** — no first leg; breaks from flat base. Highest risk/reward.
2. **Continuation** — first leg → 3–5 day consolidation → second leg. **Most tradable. Start here.**
3. **Consolidation** — extended sideways base (10+ days) → breakout. Moderate risk.
4. **Exhaustion / Third Leg** — after two completed legs. **High failure rate; avoid.**

**How to enter:**
- Buy within first 30 minutes on range expansion (4%+ or $0.90+)
- Stop at **low of the day** (preferred) or half of the day
- If the stock opens positive next day → move stop to **breakeven**

**Stop:** 2–5% (low of day). Tighter than EP because you've seen the start of the swing.

**Key rule:** Buy ONLY day 1. Never day 2 or 3.

> *"You make money in swing trading by buying at the start of a swing. If you cannot define what is the start of a swing, then do not trade swing trading setups."*

**Dollar Breakout (FHP variant):** For stocks >$60, use `C - O ≥ $0.90` with 100K+ volume. Institutional stocks move in dollars, not percentages.

## Entry #3: DEP (Delayed Entry After Pullback)

**What:** Wait 3–10 days after the catalyst for the stock to pull back, then enter with an ultra-tight stop (0.5–2.5%).

**Why it's powerful:** Same catalyst as EP, but instead of an 18–20% stop (EP day 1), you get a 0.5–2.5% stop. This enables **3–4× larger position size**.

```
EP 9M (Day 1):    Stock $10 → gapped to $12. Stop at $10.20 → $1.80 = 15% stop
DEP (Day 3–5):    Stock $11.50 → pulled back. Stop at $11.20 → $0.30 = 2.6% stop

With 1% risk:
- EP 9M:   1% / 15% = 6.7% of account
- DEP:     1% / 2.6% = 38% of account
```

**Two post-catalyst behaviors:**
- **Gap and Go** (small cap, neglected) — NEVER pulls back. Not a DEP candidate.
- **Gap, Pullback, Shakeout, Go** (institutional, 1000+ funds) — THIS is the DEP candidate.

**How to enter:**
- After catalyst, wait for 3T/2T tight days or orderly pullback
- Enter when stock "shows life" — up $0.10–$0.30 from consolidation
- Stop below tight consolidation (NOT low of day — that defeats the purpose)

**Critical rules:**
- **First valid setup ONLY.** Second/third leg is NOT DEP.
- **No confirmation needed.** If it goes up $0.30, you should be in.
- **Up to 3 retries** on genuine catalyst before abandoning.

> *"I like to wear the tightest pant. Tight pants. Not bell bottoms."*

**Key nuance:** DEP is specifically designed for "working people" who can only trade 9:30–11:00 AM. You have hours to decide, not seconds.

## Entry #4: ANTS (Anticipation)

**What:** Buy BEFORE the formal 4% breakout, entering during tight consolidation with a Buy Stop Limit Order (BSLO) at 2:58 PM.

**Prerequisite:** Master breakout trading first. If you jump to anticipation before mastering breakouts, you will lose money.

**How to enter:**
1. Run M20/TI65/MDT scans → ~415 stocks
2. Filter for 3T (3 tight days, daily change <1%) → ~19 stocks
3. Place BSLO at close+1%, limit at trigger+0.25% at 2:58 PM
4. If stock gaps up past limit → order not activated (natural protection)
5. Next morning: if positive → breakeven fast (4–5% in 30 min → breakeven now)

**Stop:** Below tight consolidation (<1%, ideally 40¢–$1). The tightest stop in the system.

**Key rules:**
- **ONLY trade ANTS when Market Monitor is confirmed bullish** — choppy market = "death by a thousand cuts"
- **Rising wedge = bad ANTS** — flat or slightly declining is ideal
- **Long sideways consolidation = trap** — wait for proper high-volume breakout instead
- **Consolidation must be 3–7 days** — >10 days means buying and selling have equalized

> *"The only time you should be trading anticipation setup is when the market is in a very confirmed and in a wild bullish conditions."*

> *"If you wait for a stock to breakout 4% and then buy, you wasted all your effort because then why did you create this watchlist?"*

---

## Entry #5: Reversal Bullish

**What:** Buy in the last 2 minutes of the trading day on exhaustion patterns after 3–5+ days of selling.

**How to enter:**
1. At 3:30 PM, scan for stocks down 3–5+ days, showing exhaustion
2. Identify the exhaustion pattern (doji, hammer, small body)
3. Enter at **3:58–4:00 PM** — NOT before
4. Set stop below open price or prior day close (0.5–2.5%)
5. Next morning: if opens positive → breakeven+

**Key rules:**
- **Never before 3:58 PM** — win rate is much lower if you front-run
- **Best in oversold markets** (Market Monitor deeply red, T2108 < 20%)
- **Time stop:** If it doesn't work next day, exit
- **"Fallen chicks"** metaphor — pick up stocks that have "fallen on the floor"

## What NOT to Do

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct | Why |
|-----------|----------|-----|
| Buy day 2 or 3 | Buy day 1 (SOS) or wait for DEP | Move is 3–5 days; mostly gone by day 2–3 |
| Wait for confirmation / ORB | Enter early with tiny stop | By ORB, stop is 8–10%; you've missed the move |
| Enter ANTS in choppy market | Only in confirmed bullish | "Death by thousand cuts" |
| Enter reversal at 2 PM | Enter at 3:58 PM | Win rate much lower earlier |
| Use market orders on FHP stocks | Use mid-price or limit order | Slippage kills tight-stop edge |
| Enter pre-market / after-hours | OPG or first 10 min regular session | Low liquidity blows out stops |
