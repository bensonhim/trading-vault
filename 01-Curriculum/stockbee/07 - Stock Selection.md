# Lesson 7: Stock Selection — Which Stocks to Trade

> **Core Question:** Which stocks make the best trading vehicles?
> **Answer:** Institutional-quality stocks with 39%+ sales growth, owned by 1000+ funds. These pull back after catalysts (creating DEPs) and follow through reliably (creating SOSes).

---

## The Core Principle

> *"Sales growth is what is the most important part which drives the stock's growth. Earnings growth is nice to have, but it is not essential."*

The market rewards **sales acceleration**, not profitability. A company can be unprofitable but have 99%+ sales growth, and the stock will soar. A profitable company with declining sales will languish.

---

## Fund Ownership Tiers

| Tier | Fund Count | What It Means | Best For |
|------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| **Ultra-Institutional** | 2,500+ | Must-own stocks; funds cannot live without them | Large size; predictable |
| **Institutional** | 1,000+ | Well-covered, liquid, orderly pullbacks | **DEP** — best tier |
| **Emerging** | 500–1,000 | Growing institutional interest | Swing trades; potential |
| **Neglected** | < 500 | Potential for surprise; higher risk | Gap-and-go (NOT DEP) |

> *"DEP is more suitable for institutional quality. Institutional quality means something owned by thousand plus funds or 2,500 funds."*

---

## Why Institutional Quality Matters for DEP

**Institutional stocks pull back after catalysts.** This is the key insight:

| Stock Type | After Catalyst | DEP Opportunity? |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Institutional (1000+ funds)** | Gaps up, then pulls back 3–10 days | ✅ YES — this is the DEP |
| **Neglected + hot theme** | Gaps up and keeps going (no pullback) | ❌ NO — gap and go |
| **Story stock** | Gaps up, extreme volatility | ⚠️ Maybe — smaller size, wider stop |
| **Biotech** | Gaps up, one-night stand | ❌ NO — "certified male drones" |

**The discovery gap is what makes institutional stocks powerful:**

When GRWG was found, <60 funds owned it. By end of one quarter, 253 funds were holding it. **The fund rush IS the move.** Institutional discovery drives follow-through.

### The 39% Rule

> *"If a company has sales growth of 39%+ back-to-back for two quarters, that stock can double, triple, go up 500%, 600%, 800%."*

**Why 39%?**
- 39% compounding for 4 quarters ≈ 100% annual growth
- This is the rate where analysts start upgrading aggressively
- It separates "growing" from "accelerating"

---

## The Magna 53 + Cap 10×10 Formula (For EP)

For **life-changing** episodic pivots (not every EP, just the 2–3 per year):

| Component | What It Means | Threshold |
|-----------|--------------|-----------|
| **M** | Massive earnings acceleration | 100%+, ideally 500%+ |
| **A** | Acceleration of sales | 39%+ for 2 quarters |
| **G** | Gap up | 4%+ on 100K+ volume |
| **N** | Neglect | No rally into earnings, <100 funds |
| **5** | Short interest ratio | 5+ days (optional, nice-to-have) |
| **3** | Analysts raise targets | 3+ analysts (optional, nice-to-have) |

**Cap 10×10:**
- Market cap **below $10B** (sub-$1B = best)
- IPO'd **within 10 years** (under 5 = best)

Both Magna 53 + Cap 10×10 = likely **300–800%+ moves**.

---

## Capitalization Math

| Cap Range | Multiplier | Likelihood | Examples |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| $100M → $1B | 10× | Common | Early-stage growth |
| $1B → $10B | 10× | Possible | Mid-stage growth |
| $10B → $50B | 5× | Rare | Large-cap growth |
| $100B → $500B | 5× | Nearly impossible | Mature companies |

**Smaller cap = larger potential move.** That's why Bucket 0 (IPO'd, <$10B, 39%+ sales growth) is the sweet spot for life-changing EPs.

## The Sugar Baby Universe

Stock selection isn't just about filters — **the Sugar Baby list IS your pre-selected universe.**

The Sugar Baby process:
1. Run Count Through for 504/252/126/63/21/10/5 days
2. Sort descending by count per timeframe
3. Select top 20–30 per timeframe
4. Flag all symbols → master list of ~90 stocks
5. **Trade SOS/DEP/ANTS exclusively on this list when possible**

> *"If you held the ordinary stock as a SOS for three days, it might make 8 to 20%. But the Sugar Baby will make in the same three days, 40% or more sometimes."*
