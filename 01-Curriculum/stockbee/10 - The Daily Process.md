# Lesson 10: The Daily Process — How It All Connects

> **Core Question:** How do I put it all together in a single trading day?
> **Answer:** Setup is 10% of success; process is 90%. Organize like crazy, execute mechanically, review relentlessly.

---

## The Core Principle

> *"Setup is ~10% of success; process is ~90%."*

> *"You don't need discipline. You need process orientation."*

The setups are public. The edges are public. There are no secrets. **The difference between profitable traders and unprofitable ones is process.**

A process is not motivation. It's not willpower. It's a **predefined checklist** that eliminates cognitive load. When you see a setup, you don't think — you execute, because all decisions were made months ago.

## The Daily Routine

### Before Market (8:00–9:25 AM)

| Time | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| **8:00** | Review Market Monitor | Green/red/yellow classification |
| **8:15** | Situational Awareness study (20% of one stock) | Build pattern recognition |
| **8:30** | Review after-hours movers | Which stocks gapped up/down overnight |
| **8:45** | Check NTRT/MTRT scans | Today's earnings, catalysts |
| **9:00** | Review DEP/Sugar Baby watchlist | Pre-selected stocks for the day |
| **9:25** | Final check — OPG orders ready? | All decisions made BEFORE bell |

> *"Organize Like Crazy (OLC): Every tool, scan, entry type, stop rule, and exit rule must be decided BEFORE the market opens. If you are asking 'Where do I find this?' at 9:30 AM, you are not ready."*

### Morning Session (9:30–10:30 AM)

| Time | Activity | Rule |
|------|----------|------|
| **9:30** | Run EP 9M scan | 4%+ breakout + 9M+ volume |
| **9:30–9:45** | Find 1–2 SOS setups | Out of 10–20 EP stocks, find 1–2 with SOS |
| **9:45–10:00** | Enter with stop at low of day | Place hard stop immediately |
| **10:00+** | Classify: cat, dog, or liquid lava | Decide sizing based on catalyst quality |

**"Cat / Dog / Liquid Lava" Classification:**

| Category | What | Size | Source |
|----------|------|------|--------|
| **Cat** | Clear catalyst (earnings, sales, real contract) | Full size | [[21. Process Guide]] |
| **Dog** | Story/speculative (biotech pivoting to AI, etc.) | Small size (5–10%) | [[21. Process Guide]] |
| **Liquid Lava** | Theme stocks (semis, AI chips) | Medium size; they move every day | [[21. Process Guide]] |

### Midday (10:30 AM–2:00 PM)

| Time | Activity | Rule |
|------|----------|------|
| **10:30–14:00** | **Dead zone** — go to the gym, do R&D | Do NOT sit in front of screen |

> *"All that work of anticipation setup, reversal bullish happens after 2 PM."*

### Afternoon Session (2:00–4:00 PM)

| Time | Activity | Rule |
|------|----------|------|
| **14:00–15:00** | Run Sugar Baby + reversal scans | Prepare for afternoon entries |
| **15:00–15:30** | Prepare reversal bullish list | Identify exhaustion patterns |
| **15:30–15:50** | Run ANTS scan for tomorrow | 3T/2T filter on momentum stocks |
| **15:50–15:58** | Place BSLO orders for anticipation | Enter at 2:58 PM or 3:58 PM |
| **15:58–16:00** | Reversal bullish entries | Last 2 minutes only |
| **16:00** | Market close | All positions accounted for |

### Post-Market

| Activity | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| Update DEP watchlist | Sort by Max V25 for tomorrow |
| Review today's trades | What worked, what didn't |
| Deep dive / R&D | Study one stock or setup in depth |
| Prepare for tomorrow | NTRT/MTRT, catalysts, watchlist ready |

## The Process Mindset

### Self-Efficacy (Bandura's 5 Sources)

| Source | What It Means | Trading Application |
|--------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Mastery experience** | Doing it successfully builds confidence | Deep dive 5,000+ examples of one setup |
| **Social persuasion** | Others telling you it works | Trading community, bootcamp |
| **Vicarious experience** | Seeing others succeed | Watching Pradeep's live trades |
| **Psychosomatic factors** | Physical state affects performance | Rest, exercise, nutrition |
| **Visualization** | Imagining success creates neural pathways | "Always be in control" — process-driven scripts |

### The 48-Hour Deep Work Method

1. **Pick ONE setup** to master (EP 9M, DEP, reversal, etc.)
2. **Lock yourself away** for 24–48 hours
3. **Study 5,000+ examples** of that setup
4. **Document for each:** pre-move price, catalyst, sector, cap, volume, legs, duration
5. **Do 3+ passes** — question quality improves each round
6. **Result:** Your brain permanently alters; you "unsee" false setups and instantly recognize valid ones

> *"My number one recommended book for traders is a book called Deep Work by Cal Newport."*

### Overcoming FOMO

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **FOMO** | Cognitive dissonance; need for action | Have a process with checklist; only trade setups |
| **Chasing** | No defined process; reacting to noise | Build watchlist so setups come to you |
| **Relaxing conditions** | Seeing others make money | DEP is designed as "non-FOMO way to trade" |

> *"If your problem is FOMO, break that by having a process where you have a checklist."*

## The Checklist (Before Every Trade)

Before entering any trade, ask:

- [ ] Is Market Monitor green? (For longs) / red? (For shorts)
- [ ] Is there a clear catalyst I can explain in 10 minutes?
- [ ] Is the stock on my Sugar Baby list or institutional quality (1000+ funds)?
- [ ] Is this the FIRST valid setup? (Not second/third leg)
- [ ] Is my stop defined and placed?
- [ ] Is my position size calculated (risk / stop width)?
- [ ] Am I entering in the right time window? (Morning = breakouts; afternoon = ANTS/reversals)
- [ ] Am I buying day 1? (Not day 2 or 3)
- [ ] Does this setup have confluence? (Multiple edges aligned)
- [ ] Am I prepared to move to breakeven tomorrow if positive?

**If you can't check all boxes, don't trade.**

## Common Process Mistakes

| Mistake | Why Wrong | Fix |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| No process at all | FOMO, fumbling, inconsistency | Define daily process; stick to checklist |
| Too many stocks on watchlist | Overwhelmed; scattered effort | Focus list: max 5 stocks |
| Buying second/third day | Move is mostly gone | Buy day 1 (SOS) or wait for DEP |
| Sitting at screen all day | Cognitive fatigue; revenge trading | Take gym break 10:30–14:00 |
| No post-market review | Repeating same mistakes | Daily review: what worked, what didn't |
| Trading without catalyst | Random; no edge | If you can't identify catalyst in 10 min, skip |
| Overtrading in choppy market | Death by thousand cuts | Only ANTS in confirmed bullish |

## Final Homework: The Deep Work Exercise

1. **Pick ONE setup** to master (recommend starting with SOS or DEP)
2. **Lock yourself away** for 24–48 hours
3. **Study 5,000+ examples** using TC2000 or your screener
4. **Document each example:** pre-move price, catalyst, sector, cap, volume, legs, duration
5. **Do 3 passes** — your question quality improves each round
6. **Result:** You should be able to recognize valid setups instantly and reject invalid ones without thinking

This is the single most impactful exercise you can do as a trader. After 5,000 examples, your brain will have **procedural memory** — you see the setup and you execute without thinking.

> *"Once you learn a process, the brain puts that process into procedural memory. Procedural memory is stored as a total sequence, not one sequence after another."*
