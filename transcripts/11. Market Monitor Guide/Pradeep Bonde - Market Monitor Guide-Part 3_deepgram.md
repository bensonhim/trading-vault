## Summary

Part 3 covers the derived ratio columns — 5-day and 10-day ratios. Key points:

- **5-day ratio**: Sum of 5 days of 4% breakouts ÷ Sum of 5 days of 4% breakdowns
- **10-day ratio**: Same calculation over 10 days — smoother, slower to turn
- **Ratio interpretation**: >1.0 = more buying than selling; ~2.0 = "very aggressive buying"; 4–5.0 = "super aggressive buying"; <0.5 = more selling than buying
- **5-day is reactive/faster**; 10-day is smoother/lagging — both turn green quickly during strong rallies
- **Color coding**: ratio >1 = green cell; ratio <1 = red cell
- **Pradeep's candid admission**: "Do I really look at that ratio? No." — the raw 4% numbers already convey the same information
- **Ratios are secondary indicators** added per member requests — they add qualitative shortcuts but are redundant if you read the raw numbers
- Example shown: ratio hitting 3.0 during strong buying periods
- **Core takeaway**: Ratios are useful shortcuts but not essential — the primary indicators (next video) are what matter
