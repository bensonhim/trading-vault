## Summary

The simplest exercise to learn SOS: mark start of a swing on 25 stocks. After that, you'll see it everywhere. Critical distinction: every stock has SOS events, but not every SOS is tradable. Tradable SOS requires specific conditions (consolidation, catalyst, context) — those qualified conditions turn random events into setups. Start with 100 scan ideas, narrow to 3–5 high-probability SOS candidates. Clean consolidation breakouts and continuation setups are the most tradable. Only trade in good market conditions. DOMH example: 5.93% range expansion after 4.26% down day = clear SOS. Goldman Sachs (GS) and Vista Oil shown as examples. The essence: "understanding SOS is the grammar of swing trading — if you cannot get this concept right, don't trade."
