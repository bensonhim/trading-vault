## Summary

- **What is M20**: A momentum-based method to find stocks that have started rallying after putting in a bottom (or declining after a top). Acts like a red light/green light — tells when to go long vs. short.
- **Anchored momentum**: Calculates momentum from a low point (bullish) or high point (bearish) — not a fixed moving average.
- **Best after corrections**: When the market comes out of a correction, M20 finds hundreds of stocks that were beaten down and just turned.
- **Two sides**: Bullish M20 finds stocks turning from down to up (continuation setups); bearish M20 finds stocks turning from up to down (short candidates).
- **Stock examples**: Coherence (CHRW) — stock going down, M20 turns bullish → continuation setup; ChargePoint — M20 shows where momentum turned positive; many stocks in the scan showing positive momentum change after bottom.
