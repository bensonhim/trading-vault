## The Core Idea

Think of your trading like a **Tesla factory**. You decide which models to build (which setups to trade), then you set up production lines with every tool, scan, and calculation pre-positioned. When the shift starts, you just execute.

> "You are a Tesla car factory. You decide you're going to make Tesla Y model, Tesla dump truck, and Tesla battery. You have three production lines. There is a set process. If there is a shift starting at 8:00, there is a set of things you have to do."

> "Decide once for all what setups you want to trade, then everything for it has to be in place like a factory has."

The same applies to a restaurant — you don't ask "where's the menu?" when the first customer walks in. Everything is prepped before opening.

## Pradeep's Setups (The Production Lines)

| Setup | Type | When to Prioritize |
|---|---|---|
| **EP** | Episodic Pivot (4%+ move, high volume) | Always — the core |
| **EP 9M** | EP with 9M+ shares traded | Always — primary setup |
| **DEP** | Daily EP (EP 9M from last 25 days, setting up again) | Always — on fund-quality stocks |
| **Sugar Baby** | Neglected stock with mind-blowing catalyst | Always — home-run potential |
| **SIPs** | Stock in Play (day trading) | Only when SA says breakouts won't work |
| **Momentum Burst (MB)** | 3-5 day swing | When breakouts are working |
| **Anticipation** | Buy before a breakout | Selectively; PM only (moved from AM) |
| **Reversal Bullish** | Buy a bottom reversal | Selectively; entered at 3:58 PM |

**Key rule on SIPs:**
> "If breakouts are going to work, I don't even bother going into SIPs because then it becomes a distraction. When market conditions are good, the main business of this trade factory is swing trading."

## The Daily Process Flow

### Phase 1: Morning Arrival (Before 9:30 AM)

**Step 1 — Situational Awareness (SA)**

Pradeep has a plaque on his desk: *"Are breakouts likely to work?"*

> "First thing in the morning, I ask this question to myself: Are breakouts likely to work? Are breakdowns likely to work?"

If breakouts won't work → focus on bearish side and SIPs. If breakouts will work → focus on swing trading.

**Step 2 — 20% Study**

> "As soon as I come, I'm going to do that because that gives you information. That tells you what is working, what is not working, and it allows you to create your SA."

Readings:
- **>100 on bullish side** = caution zone, extended
- **Very low readings** = bounce likely (mean reversion)
- **Very high bearish readings (>100)** = good bounce setup
- **Middling readings (15-46)** = weak bounce, chop continues

**Step 3 — Write SA on index cards**

> "I buy a box of cards like this and write down my SA. As soon as the SA is clear, rest of the factory is going to run according to the SA."

**Step 4 — News & Market Scan (MTRT)**

Open the same set of pages every morning (they auto-open):

| Tool | What It's For |
|---|---|
| **Briefing.com** | Primary catalyst source — earnings, news |
| **Market Search** | X-ray scans, fund ownership data |
| **Trade Ideas** | Premarket gaps, real-time scanning |
| **TC2000** | Scans, charting, fund ownership (2500+ funds) |
| **Finviz** | Backup screener |
| **Barchart** | Premarket trading page (auto-opens) |
| **X (Twitter)** | Crowd-sourced catalyst info — "Twitter is full of human ants" |
| **Wall Street Journal** (Market & Finance page) | "Heard on the Street" column — 5-6 stocks with market news |
| **Bloomberg** | Big news highlights — front page = important news only |

> "Front page of a newspaper or a site is a very valuable commodity. They're not going to put any small news. If it makes the front page, it's important."

> "I don't look at the market after 4:00 PM. Post market — nothing."

**Step 5 — EP & SIPs Analysis**

From MTRT screening, create watch list:
- Bucket 0 (automatic catalyst — earnings, product launch)
- Bucket 1, Bucket 2 (fund quality tiers)
- IBD 250 stocks

> "If I see an EP 9M and it's from bucket zero, bucket one, bucket two, or IBD 250, it is automatic cat. I don't have to think about it."

**Step 6 — Pre-load Orders**

> "If I found something in premarket, I'll be putting in OPG entries, stops, and position sizing. All the thinking is done."

---

### Phase 2: Market Open (9:30 AM – 10:30 AM)

**Step 7 — Run Scans Continuously**

| Scan | Purpose |
|---|---|
| EP 9M | Find stocks with 9M+ volume + 4%+ move |
| Combination scans | Various EP variants |
| Sugar Wave | Sugar baby candidates |

> "By 10:30, if from the scans, if there is a trade which I find and it meets my criteria, I enter the trade, set stop, exit trade."

**Step 8 — Manage Open Positions**

For each open position:
- What day is this? (Day 1? Day 3? Day 5?)
- Is it working?
- Should I take profit?
- Adjust stops

> "Goldman Sachs, that day I came in. I said this thing is three days. It hasn't done much. I'm going to take a call on this today because it doesn't look like it wants to go. I'll give it some room in the morning. If it doesn't go, I'm going to free up that money and use it somewhere."

### Phase 3: Midday Break (11:00 AM – 1:30 PM)

**Step 9 — Gym (11:00 – 12:00)**

> "Eleven to twelve, I spend with my trainer, trying to develop six packs."

**Step 10 — Lunch & Reset (12:00 – 1:30 PM)**

### Phase 4: Afternoon Session (1:30 PM – 4:00 PM)

**Step 11 — Run Scans Again (1:30 PM)**

Same scans as morning. By 1:30 PM, most stocks have 800K-1M+ shares.

> "Nowadays, I made a decision that I'm not going to trade anything which trades less than a million shares. If it doesn't trade a million shares or around 800,000 shares by that time, I don't even go and look at it."

**Step 12 — Manage Positions**

Adjust stops, exit trades, enter new trades from afternoon scan.

**Step 13 — 3:00 PM Meeting**

StockBee PM meeting with members.

**Step 14 — Reversal Bullish Entry (3:58 PM)**

> "3:45 to 4:00, if I have an anticipation or reversal idea, then I'm going to enter that trade just around 3:58."

---

### Phase 5: After Hours (4:00 PM+)

**Step 15 — Stop. No Trading.**

> "After 4 PM — nothing. Not ready. No trading. No market related thing I will look at."

> "I don't look at the market after 4:00 PM."

## Pre-Decided Decisions (No "To Be or Not to Be")

The factory runs because decisions are made **once**, not re-litigated every day:

| Decision | Pradeep's Rule |
|---|---|
| **Entry method for EP/SIPs** | OPG (Opening Order) — always |
| **Stop for EP 9M** | Low of the day OR half of the day — pre-decided |
| **Swing trade duration** | 3-5 days — by definition |
| **Anticipation entry time** | PM only (moved from AM to avoid interference) |
| **Reversal bullish entry** | 3:58 PM |
| **Stocks below 1M shares** | Don't trade — skip |
| **Undercut and rally** | Don't use — not his setup |
| **Position trading** | Don't do — "only positions in Kamasutra" |

> "There is no 'to be or not to be.' I'm going to do OPG. If it works, doesn't work — there is always ability to reenter. But I'm not going to keep arguing whether to do OPG or opening range breakout or undercut and rally."

## Fund Ownership for Sizing

Pradeep tracks which stocks are owned by 2,500+ and 5,000+ funds in TC2000/Market Search:

| Fund Ownership | What It Means | Sizing Implication |
|---|---|---|
| **5,000+ funds** (e.g., AVGO) | Maximum institutional quality | Can size bigger than normal |
| **2,500+ funds** (e.g., AMD, Cisco) | High institutional quality | Normal to larger size |
| **Below 2,500** | Lower institutional interest | Smaller size, more caution |

> "When Cisco showed up that day, I said okay, this is it. Cisco — I can go big. Cisco was not a 5,000 fund stock, but now it has gone into 5,000. So things are changing."

> "I saw Adobe dropping from 5,000 funds. When it dropped, every setup where it showed up as a short was a short. CRM — for many years, owned by 5,000+ funds. When it dropped out, the stock never rallied. It went down."

Fund ownership changes are **information**:
- Stock joining 5,000+ → institutions are buying → bullish
- Stock dropping from 5,000+ → institutions are selling → bearish

## The Marie Kondo Principle

> "It has to be organized in a way in which Marie Kondo will be proud of. All your t-shirts should be sorted by color. If you're gonna be wearing gym pants, sort your pants by tightness."

Everything in its place:
- Scans are in place (don't ask "what's the scan?" at 9:30)
- Stops are calculated and ready
- Position sizing tool is accessible
- Fund ownership data is on the chart
- Earnings dates are visible
- Float data is visible
- Capitalization data is visible
- Trend data is visible

> "You're like in an operating theater. You have all the tools. You have a patient. You need tools to open the chest, tools to clamp, tools to stabilize the heart. Everything is laid out in a sequence."

## No Cognitive Load

> "Once you have a trade factory, you have no cognitive load. You have pre-decided what you're going to do. You are in control."

Benefits:
1. **FOMO disappears** — you're not scrambling, you're generating your own trades
2. **No impulse decisions** — everything is pre-decided
3. **Improvement is possible** — when you have a set process, you can see bottlenecks and fix them
4. **Consistency** — same thing, same sequence, every day

## How to Build Your Own Trade Factory

### Step 1: Decide Your Setups

> "First decide your setup. For each setup, be very clear about what are the qualifiers."

You don't have to copy Pradeep exactly. If you only trade from a watch list, your factory looks different — but it's still a factory.

### Step 2: Resolve Your Dilemmas

> "Many of you are not really sure whether you are gay, straight, or bisexual. One day you want to be gay, then you want to be bisexual, then you want to be straight. You're vacillating between day trading, swing trading, position trading, from setup to setup."

Decide: Am I a day trader? Swing trader? Both? Which setups? Then build the factory around that.

### Step 3: Build the Process Flow

For each setup, have:
- The scan that finds it
- The qualifier that validates it (cat/dog/liquid lava for EP 9M)
- The entry method (OPG, limit order, etc.)
- The stop rule (low of day, half of day, fixed %, etc.)
- The exit rule (3-5 days for swing, specific profit target, etc.)
- The position sizing tool

### Step 4: Iterate

> "First iteration of process flow will be chunky. It may not be the final one. Then it will go through iteration. It will be like the Windows version — the first version was command line. Now it's version 27. Same way, your process will get refined."

Pradeep moved anticipation from AM to PM because it was interfering with morning breakout entries. That kind of refinement comes from running the process and seeing the bottlenecks.

### Step 5: No R&D During the Day

> "In case I see during the morning that there is something which I need to change in order to be successful, I'll note it down. I'm not going to do R&D during the day. Because the moment you get into R&D during the day, you lost it."
