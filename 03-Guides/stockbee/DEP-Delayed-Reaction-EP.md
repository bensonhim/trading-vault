# DEP — Delayed Reaction EP

## Overview

DEP (Delayed Reaction EP) is a **scan + watchlist-based setup** that finds stocks with a recent EP9M catalyst (within 25 days) that have pulled back after the initial move. It allows **large position sizing with close stops** — the whole objective.

> "DEP is not really EP with a DEP. It's a DEP EP9M. The whole objective of DEP is to get a stop of 2.5% or less. The name of the game in trading is sizing. If you want big returns, you have to find opportunities where you can size big." — 2026-05-29

> "DEP is equally important to me as EP9M. It's not even number two." — 2026-06-11

## Key Concepts

### Why DEP Exists

- EP9M entries require 8–10% stops — limits position size
- DEP entries offer **2.5% or less stops** — allows much larger positions
- After EP9M, stocks often shake out early buyers before continuing the move
- DEP captures the **first valid SOS after pullback** — the key word is FIRST
- You already know the catalyst is valid because the market confirmed it

### Two Post-Catalyst Paths

1. **Gap-and-go** — Stock gaps up on catalyst; DEP captures the first pullback after the initial move completes
2. **Delayed entry** — Stock doesn't gap up much; DEP captures the first SOS after the catalyst becomes apparent (often discovered via EP9M scan, not premarket)

> "If you buy it on the catalyst day and put your stop at low of the day, you would have got stopped out. You could buy it here and put a much bigger position size. Your stop can be much closer." — 2026-06-11 (GLW example)

### DEP vs. EP9M — Key Differences

| Aspect | EP9M | DEP |
|--------|------|-----|
| Timing | Same day as volume spike | 1–25 days after EP9M |
| Stop size | 8–10% | **2.5% or less** |
| Position size | Limited by stop | **Large** (whole point) |
| Catalyst knowledge | Find after scan | Already known (researched on EP9M day) |
| Entry | OPG, opening range | First few minutes from open, from watch list |
| Skill level | Medium | Medium-low (more time to research) |
| Best for | Daily opportunities | Institutional/fund-quality stocks |

### Who DEP Is For

> "For those who have time commitment issues, you don't want to do only one hour — this is the setup which is complementary to EP9M." — 2026-05-29

- Working people with limited screen time
- Those who want **controlled risk with large size**
- Those who want to become "catalyst experts" over time
- Anyone who wants to move their account in bigger increments

## Setup Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Scan | EP9M with volume filter, last 25 days, plus fund/bucket filters |
| Watch list | Created daily from scan; prioritized by catalyst strength |
| Universe priority | 1) Fund quality (1000+ fund ownership), 2) Buckets (0, 1, 2, IBD 250), 3) Rest |
| Qualifier 1 | **SOS** — must be start of swing (first valid buy opportunity) |
| Qualifier 2 | Real catalyst (earnings, product, theme) — NOT just chart pattern |
| Qualifier 3 | Must have reacted positively to catalyst |
| Entry | First 10–15 minutes from open; **no end-of-day entries** |
| Stop target | **2.5% or less** — this is the whole objective |
| Holding | 3–15 days (same as EP9M) |
| Exit | Catalyst-based target, first 4% breakdown, or sell into strength |
| SA relevance | **None** — DEP moves based on catalyst, independent of market |
| Max window | 25 days from EP9M; if no breakout by then, no longer valid |

### DEP Daily Process

1. **Night/morning**: Run EP9M scan filtered for fund quality (1000+ fund ownership)
2. **Sort by** max volume 25 (highest volume first = highest priority)
3. **Research catalyst** for each candidate — must be real, not fugazi
4. **Check**: Must have reacted positively to catalyst (not 24% breakdown)
5. **Eliminate**: Stocks already broken out (no longer DEP)
6. **Prioritize**: 2–3 ideas max, ranked by catalyst strength
7. **Set orders**: Pre-place market orders in broker; if stock starts going up, press button
8. **Morning**: Check premarket news on watch list stocks; enter in first 10–15 minutes

> "The whole game of doing this is sizing. If I can get into that stock in the first few minutes, and then as soon as I get in, it just blasts — I don't have to even worry about the stock. Stop can be moved to breakeven. It's free money." — 2026-06-11

### Fund Quality vs. Buckets Priority

Both are **equal priority** (#1):

| Filter | Description | Advantage |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Fund 1000+** | Owned by 1000+ funds | Largest size possible, closest stops |
| **Bucket 0/1/2** | IBD growth stocks with real catalyst | Clear catalyst, institutional buying |
| **Rest of EP9M** | Everything else | Lower priority, smaller sizing |

> "If you want to do DEP, start with fund quality only. You're going to get very less number of stocks and focused institutional quality stock." — 2026-06-13

## Exit Strategies

1. **Catalyst-based price target** — Know how far the stock should go based on catalyst understanding
2. **First 4% breakdown after entry** — Sell when 4% breakdown occurs
3. **Sell 80% into strength, 20% trailing stop** — For rapid moves
4. **Move stop daily** — If stock goes in your favor, move stop to protect profit

> "Most of these stocks, even if they make 100% or 70% or 300% move, they will make that move in 5–20 days max." — 2026-05-29

## Catalyst Research — Becoming a Catalyst Expert

> "Understanding catalyst — that is the thing. In order to become good at this, you should be completely good at understanding catalyst. Every day, you have to get obsessed with catalyst." — 2026-05-29

### Catalyst Priority for DEP

| Priority | Type | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| 1 | **Earnings/Sales** | Massive acceleration (SNDK: 61, 644, 7900%, 11000%) |
| 2 | **Real product** | New product launch (NViz: 81% in 5 days) |
| 3 | **Theme** | AI, semiconductor cycle, government policy |
| 4 | **Management change** | New CEO, restructuring |

### What to Avoid

- **24%+ gap stocks** — If stock gapped 32%, probability of stop-out is very high
- **Victoria's Secret-type catalysts** — Gap up 49% means catalyst is priced in
- **Biotech** — "99% of biotech stocks are male drones" — one move then done
- **Stocks that already made their move** — If it already broke out, it's no longer DEP
- **Story stocks without real catalyst** — For DEP, focus on fund quality with real catalyst

## Key Takeaways

1. **DEP's whole objective is 2.5% or less stops** — allowing large position sizing
2. **DEP is equally important as EP9M** — not a secondary setup
3. **Focus on fund quality stocks** — allows controlled risk with maximum size
4. **All work done premarket** — you know the watch list before open
5. **First 10–15 minutes only** — no end-of-day DEP entries
6. **Catalyst must be real** — DEP on fund quality with real catalyst, not story stocks
7. **Maximum 2–3 ideas per day** — prioritize by catalyst strength
8. **25-day window** — if no breakout in 25 days from EP9M, no longer valid
9. **Pre-place orders** — have them ready in broker; press button when stock starts moving
10. **SA doesn't matter for DEP** — catalyst-driven stocks move independent of market
11. **Works on both long and short** — Walmart DEP short example given
