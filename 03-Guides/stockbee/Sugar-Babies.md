# Sugar Babies — Stock Selection Strategy

## Overview

Sugar Babies is a **stock selection strategy**, NOT a setup. It identifies stocks the market is "madly in love with" — stocks with frequent EP9M episodes that make large, repeated swings. You trade setups (SOS/breakout, anticipation, reversal bullish) ON Sugar Babies stocks; Sugar Babies itself is the universe filter.

> "Sugar Babies is a different philosophy. Sugar Babies is stocks which are being aggressively bought repeatedly. Making big moves. Stocks which have history of being proven winners." — 2026-05-29

## Key Concepts

### What Makes a Sugar Baby

- Stocks **aggressively bought repeatedly** with 40–50% swings in 3–5 days (vs. normal stocks' 10–20%)
- Identified by scan: stocks with **frequent EP9M episodes** (e.g., TSLA: 106 EP9M in 1450 days; IREN: multiple EP9M in current year)
- Sector bias: **software, semiconductor, computer systems** — technology themes dominate
- The list boils down to ~100–150 stocks; top 25–40 are the actionable watch list

### How It Relates to Other Setups

> "All the other qualifiers are for sizing purpose. For sizing purpose, for your own confidence. Nobody is stopping you from putting all your account on this stock." — 2026-05-29

- **SOS is required** for everything in this field — it's the universal qualifier
- Setups traded ON Sugar Babies: SOS/breakout, anticipation, reversal bullish
- Sugar Babies does NOT need EP9M to work — if it has SOS, that's enough
- You operate from a **watch list** (created weekly, refined daily)
- ~4 tradable setups per Sugar Baby per year

### The "Coke" Analogy

> "EP is like Coke. EP9M is like Cherry Coke. Sugar Babies is like plant-based Coke. SIPs is a Coke mini can. But it's all Coke." — 2026-05-29

All members of the EP family share the same core logic: **catalyst + large moves**. Sugar Babies is the "spice up your sex life" recommendation — more action, more frequent opportunities.

### Sugar Babies vs. Other Setups

| Aspect | Sugar Babies | EP9M | DEP |
|--------|-------------|------|-----|
| Type | Stock selection | Scan-based setup | Scan + watchlist |
| Frequency | ~4 per SB per year | 300–1000/yr | Daily |
| Skill needed | Low (just need SOS) | High (catalyst analysis) | Medium |
| Size | Large possible | Medium | Large (close stops) |
| Entry | SOS on watchlist | After scan confirms volume | First SOS after pullback |

## Setup Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Scan type | Sorted by `nby5`, `nby10`, `nby20` with minimum count of 3 |
| Universe | Top 25–30 stocks by frequency of EP9M episodes |
| Update frequency | Weekly (add recent 5/10/20/50-day new entries) |
| Qualifier | Must have SOS (start of swing) |
| Tightness | ±0.4% or less preferred |
| Entry | From watch list — anticipate breakout OR buy every SOS long/short |
| Exit targets | 30–40% per swing (then sell 80%, keep 20% with trailing stop) |
| Holding period | 3–5 days per swing |
| Institutional quality | Optional — for sizing, not for selection |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Sugar Babies is stock selection, not a setup.** You still need SOS to enter
2. **SOS is the universal qualifier** — never buy without SOS
3. **Every swing on a Sugar Baby is 3–5 days** — sell into strength (80% position, 20% trailing stop)
4. **Operate from a watch list** — pre-decide which stocks you'll buy, then just wait for SOS
5. **Institutional quality is for sizing**, not selection — the catalyst is what matters
6. **Don't chase extended stocks** — wait for SOS on pullback or tight days
7. **Move stops rapidly** on fast-movers; use structural stops on slow movers
8. **Exit methods**: (a) Catalyst-based price target, (b) First 4% breakdown after entry, (c) Sell 80% into strength, keep 20% with trailing stop
