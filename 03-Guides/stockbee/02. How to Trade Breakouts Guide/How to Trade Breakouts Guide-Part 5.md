## Summary

Part 5 is the first of three sessions on **vehicle selection** — choosing WHAT stocks to trade breakouts on. Pradeep covers two major approaches: (1) the classic **IBD/CANSLIM momentum method** (RS rating ≥85, price within 15% of 52-week high, EPS ≥85), which is excellent for consolidation breakouts but misses continuation setups and bottom bounces; and (2) his own **revenue momentum method** — "Bucket Zero" and "Bucket One" — which selects stocks based on **sales growth** (not earnings), market cap, and IPO age. The core thesis, backed by Pradeep's research and Morgan Stanley studies: **sales growth, not earnings growth, is what drives multi-year explosive moves.** Shopify, MongoDB, and Celsius Holdings all made life-changing moves while unprofitable. The session includes specific scan criteria, the logic behind the 39% threshold, and Pradeep's daily workflow of monitoring bucket stocks for breakout setups.

## 1. Setup vs. Vehicle: Two Parts of the Equation

The breakout setup (momentum burst) is only half the battle. **Vehicle selection** is equally critical:

> "You want to select vehicles where there is a very high probability that the move will be explosive."

A perfect consolidation breakout on a slow-moving utility stock is pointless. A mediocre breakout on a high-growth small-cap can be life-changing.

---

## 2. Method 1: IBD Momentum Approach (Growth + Momentum)

The classic IBD / William O'Neil method — widely used by retail traders.

### 2.1 Criteria

| Filter | Value | Purpose |
|--------|-------|---------|
| RS Rating | ≥ 85 | Top 15% by 6-month relative strength |
| Price vs 52-Week High | ≥ 85% | Within 15% of highs — avoids falling knives |
| Price | > $15 | Liquidity and stability |
| EPS Rating | ≥ 85 | Earnings momentum (though Pradeep argues this is the wrong metric) |
| Volume | ≥ 1.5 × avg(V50.1) | Confirms institutional participation |

### 2.2 The "85-85-85" List

IBD publishes a computer-generated list requiring:
- Price > $15
- Within 15% of 52-week high
- EPS ≥ 85 **and** RS ≥ 85
- Avg daily volume ≥ 10,000 shares

This list used to be published in the newspaper on a specific day; now it's published on weekends.

### 2.3 What It Finds Well
- Consolidation breakouts (cup-with-handle, flat base, double bottom)
- Growth-momentum leaders near highs

### 2.4 What It Misses

> "By choice they are selecting not to trade certain kind of bottom bounces."

- **Bottom bounces** — no stock near highs meets this criteria
- **Continuation setups** — a stock like CHRS in mid-move won't be within 15% of its 52-week high
- **Early-stage growth** — many explosive stocks haven't earned profits yet

Pradeep's assessment: "It's a very valid way. It's a very good way of looking at only stocks with momentum." But it deliberately narrows the universe to consolidation breakouts only.

---

## 3. Method 2: Pradeep's "Bucket Zero & Bucket One" (Revenue Momentum)

This is Pradeep's primary vehicle-selection method for longer-term holds and bigger positions.

### 3.1 The Core Insight: Sales > Earnings

Multiple research sources (including Morgan Stanley and IBD's own evolving analysis) confirm:

> "What makes the stock go up is not really the earnings momentum — it's the sales momentum."

**Examples:**
- **Shopify**: Made massive moves while unprofitable; sales growth was phenomenal
- **MongoDB**: $34 → $500+ without consistent profit; driven by sales. "Till 2023, this is the first time it is making a profit. And actually, when it started making profit, the price actually went down."
- **Celsius Holdings**: Explosive growth driven by phenomenal sales, not profitability. "Look at the sales growth, right? I mean, the sales growth is phenomenal. Profitability doesn't matter."
- **Digital Turbine (APPS)**: ~6000%+ move from ~$250M market cap, fueled by sales

### 3.2 Bucket Zero (Highest Priority)

| Criterion | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Sales Growth | 2 consecutive quarters ≥ 39% | Revenue acceleration = institutional interest |
| Market Cap | < $10B | Room for explosive expansion |
| IPO Age | ≤ 10 years | Maximum growth happens in first decade |
| Annual Sales | > $25M | Avoid micro-revenue companies |
| Liquidity | Sufficient daily volume | Tradeable without slippage |

**Why Bucket Zero works:**
- Young companies grow fastest — "once they become older, it's difficult for them to make big moves"
- Small cap stocks can 10× their market cap; large caps cannot
- Turnaround situations naturally appear here (sales turnaround → >39% growth after negative quarters)
- Best risk-adjusted returns: "If you find something at < $500M–$1B cap + 39% sales growth, that's explosive"

### 3.3 Bucket One (Secondary Priority)

| Criterion | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Sales Growth | 2 consecutive quarters ≥ 39% | Same revenue acceleration |
| Market Cap | Any (can be > $10B) | Lower growth ceiling but still valid |
| IPO Age | > 10 years | Older companies with renewed growth |

Bucket One catches companies like **DraftKings** ($16B cap, 81% sales growth) that still move but with lower explosive potential.

### 3.4 Turnaround Capture

> "When there is a turnaround EP, is it going to be captured by your bucket zero and bucket one? The answer is yes."

A true turnaround = sales turnaround (not cost-cutting). Negative quarters → positive quarters = >39% comparative growth = automatic bucket inclusion.

### 3.5 Why 39%? (The Number Is Arbitrary)

- **29%** used to be Pradeep's threshold → gave too many stocks
- **39%** reduces universe to manageable handful
- **49%+** captures fewer — some industries (retail, restaurants) rarely grow that fast
- The exact number is less important than the principle: **prioritize revenue momentum + small cap + youth**

> "If you use 29% plus sales growth in a quarter, two quarters in a row, which is what I used to use, it gives you more stocks. You want lesser number of stocks. So is 39% sales growth."

## 4. The Practical Workflow

1. **Daily bucket updates** outsourced (Pradeep's sister-in-law generates cleaned lists)
2. **Import into MarketSmith** (or equivalent tool)
3. **Apply breakout scans** (bottom bounce / continuation / consolidation) to bucket-filtered universe
4. **Monitor intraday** for setups on bucket-zero stocks
5. **Position size up** when conviction is high (5–10% of account)

> "Your probability of missing a big turnaround is very low."

### 4.1 MarketSmith Weekly View

Pradeep notes the weekly view in MarketSmith shows more information than daily — including 8 quarters of fund ownership data, industry/sector rankings, and stock rank within industry group.

---

## 5. Key Distinction: Revenue Momentum ≠ CANSLIM

| | CANSLIM/IBD | Pradeep's Buckets |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary driver** | EPS momentum | Sales momentum |
| **Secondary driver** | RS rating (momentum) | Age + cap (structural) |
| **Best for** | Consolidation breakouts near highs | All three breakout types |
| **Profitable required?** | Yes (EPS ≥85) | No (sales can be phenomenal while unprofitable) |
| **Typical hold** | Weeks to months | Days to months (depends on setup) |

> "Sales growth is the real story behind these bigger moves of stocks... Short-term doubling/tripling can happen for no reason. But if a stock is going to go up for multiple years, it will be because of two reasons: valuation was very small, and it was growing rapidly."

## Key Takeaways

1. **Setup is only half the equation.** Vehicle selection — what stocks you trade — determines whether you make 25% or triple digits on the same breakout setup.

2. **IBD 85-85-85 is valid but narrow.** Excellent for consolidation breakouts. Deliberately misses bottom bounces and continuation setups. EPS-focused.

3. **Sales growth, not earnings, drives multi-year moves.** Shopify, MongoDB, Celsius, Digital Turbine all made life-changing moves while unprofitable.

4. **Bucket Zero = highest conviction.** 2 quarters ≥39% sales growth + cap < $10B + IPO ≤ 10 years + sales > $25M. This is where the next MongoDB lives.

5. **Bucket One = older/larger companies with renewed growth.** DraftKings at $16B cap with 81% sales growth still moves. Lower explosive potential but still valid.

6. **The 39% threshold is arbitrary.** 29% worked but gave too many stocks. 39% is a manageable filter. The principle matters more than the number.

7. **Turnarounds are automatically captured.** Negative quarters → positive quarters = >39% comparative growth = bucket inclusion. No special "turnaround" scan needed.

8. **Combine with volume filter (Part 4) for working people.** Bucket Zero + highest volume in 252 days = very few stocks, very high conviction, hold longer.

9. **Daily workflow: monitor bucket stocks for breakout setups.** The breakout setup is unchanged (anticipation, reversal, or breakout). What changes is the universe you scan.

10. **Research once, know forever.** "Once you research, do you need to research it again? Answer is no." Young companies that have already moved = known, done research, no need to repeat.
