## Summary

Part 6 is Pradeep's brutal reality check on day trading. The core message: **all day trading setups are the same 3 swing setups at 10× speed, PLUS a few additional ones made possible by intraday structure.** Fade, SGU/BGD, news plays, parabolic move fades, weak structure shorts, order flow reading — these are day-trading-specific. But the biggest revelation? **The day traders making $2M–$15M/year aren't using special setups. Their secret is SIZE.** They're trading the same methods but with 100,000–2,000,000 share positions. Day trading takes 3–5 years minimum (vs 6 weeks for swing trading), demands physical stamina, costs ~$500/month in tools, and some personality types will never handle the PNL variance. Start with swing trading first.

### 1. Swing Trading Setups Still Work — Just at 10× Speed

> "Every setup which works in swing trading also works in day trading. It's like watching a YouTube movie at 10 times the speed."

The three setups (breakout, anticipation, pullback) from Part 5 still apply. But the intraday timeframe creates additional phenomena:
- **Breakouts** — same setup, intraday
- **Pullbacks** — common on 5-min, 3-min, 1-min timeframes
- **Anticipation** — even more critical. By the time breakout confirms, you are too late.

---

### 2. The Extra Day Trading Setups — Intraday-Only

These setups work because of the unique intraday market structure (opening print, premarket, gaps, order flow):

| Setup | What It Is | Swing Trading Equivalent? |
|---|---|---|
| **Fade** | Buy weakness / sell strength at intraday extremes | Yes (pullback) |
| **SGU** | Short Gap Up — stock gapping up wildly, fade it intraday | No — gap-dependent |
| **BGD** | Buy Gap Down — stock gapping down on news, buy opening print | No — gap-dependent |
| **News plays** | Trading whatever stock is in the news that day | No — news-dependent |
| **Order flow / tape reading** | Reading Level II/III, aggressive bid stacking | No — requires real-time order book |
| **Parabolic move fade** | Stock spikes parabolically intraday, fade the reversal | Yes (extreme pullback) |
| **Weak structure short** | Short stocks with weak intraday structures breaking down | Yes (breakdown) |

**Fade is the most common intraday setup.** Stocks fade constantly. "Stocks will have several fades." Zoom is called the "king of fades" — it fades in both directions all day.

**Gap plays are highly profitable but dangerous:**
- Example of BGD: a stock gapped down 70–80%, established low in first 5 minutes, then rallied +38%—a "fantastic trade"
- Pradeep himself faded UnitedHealth gapping up aggressively
- But for the SGU/BGD trader: "If you are waiting for a scanner to tell you... your best option is ZipRecruiter. Keep your job." You need to know the gaps before the market opens.

### 3. The Secret of Big Day Traders — It's Size, Not a Better Setup

> "What setups do they do? They're all looking at pullbacks. They're trading anticipation. They're looking at breakouts, SGU and BGD. Exactly same thing."

> "The secret is size. They're putting in 500,000 shares positions. They're putting in 100,000 shares positions. So if they put in that kind of size, then that's when they end up making $500K in a day or $1M in a day."

Pradeep spent time with a famous day trader (making $2M–$5M/year) who approached him to learn swing trading:

> "There is nothing different. The only thing is his ability... It ultimately boils down to your ability to do size. If you can't do size, then you are not going to do real numbers."

**The scale:**
- A $3 stock, trader makes $500K in a day → position size was likely 1,000,000–2,000,000 shares (~$3M–$6M in capital)
- Example shown: PNL statements with -$1.2M in one day, +$2.64M another day, +$1.5M another
- PNL swings: up $600K, down $1.2M, up $1.5M — wildly volatile

> "If you see 30,000, it loses 1,200,000 in one day. 264,000. These are people doing real size."

### 4. Day Trading Takes 3–5 Years Minimum vs. 6 Weeks for Swing

> "Can you learn swing trading in six weeks? The answer is reasonably yes if you have a template. Even given a template and you sit next to the best day trader in the world — how much time will it take you to become a good day trader? Probably three to five years."

SMB Capital's own research: **3 years to become a good day trader** even at a top prop firm with training, mentorship, and the best technology.

**During those 3 years: be willing to lose substantially.**

---

### 5. Some People Will Never Make It

> "Some people will never be able to make it. I would never be able to trade like that with that much variability in my thing."

Madaswani and other big PNL posters show extreme variability: +$75K, -$1.2M, +$120K, -$264K. Pradeep explicitly says he cannot trade like this.

> "That's not my thing. You have to know your own personality."

---

### 6. Physical Fitness Is Required

> "You need to be physically fit to do day trading. That's another thing."

The successful day traders Pradeep observes focus heavily on fitness. Why?
- You are at the computer from premarket (~6 AM) through the close
- You need stamina, alertness, quick reactions
- By 4 PM, "you'll be completely exhausted"
- He can tell who will fail by looking at them on Zoom: "I look at their body language. I look at how they're dressed. I know they're not going to make it."

---

### 7. The Butler / Toothpaste Analogy — Self-Leadership

> "You cannot even find information. You cannot go through YouTube videos, find information. Close the chapter. Look for a job."

Pradeep drops the Prince Charles reference:

> "Prince Charles has a person who is in charge of squeezing the toothpaste on his toothbrush. You are that kind of person."

Meaning: some beginners expect everything handed to them. A full-time trader is an independent professional. You must find information, study methods, search videos on the site, figure out tools. If you wait for someone to bring you answers on a silver platter, you are in the wrong profession.

> "The butler should bring it on a silver thing, open the thing, put it in a glass... You just can't open this bottle and drink also."

### 8. Tools and Monthly Costs

To day trade professionally, you need approximately **$500–$600/month in tools:**

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Trade Ideas** | Scanner for gaps, premarket movers, alerts |
| **DAS Trader** | Fast execution platform, level II |
| **Benzinga Pro** | Breaking news (news is "the lifeblood") |
| **Bloomberg terminals** | For prop firms |
| **Custom scanners** | At prop firms, or in IB (Interactive Brokers) |

For BGD/SGU specifically: you can't wait for a scanner alert. You need to know before the opening print which stocks are gapping. This requires premarket preparation.

### 9. Recommended Day Trading Path for Beginners

If you decide to day trade, especially as a beginner, Pradeep's recommended order:

1. **Anticipation setups** — see the setup developing, enter before confirmation
2. **Pullbacks** — easier than chasing breakouts (you are always late on breakouts in day trading)
3. **Weak structure short** — on the short side

**NOT recommended for day trading beginners:** Chasing breakouts.

> "If you think of buying breakouts in day trading, you're going to be very late. Sometimes the breakout is just one candle. You buy it, and every time you get chopped."

> "If you wait for confirmation, you're guaranteed to become a mainsmith." (You get your positions "main-smithed" / destroyed.)

**The 3×300 method:** Stock up $3+, on 300K+ volume in the first 15 minutes. Likely to make a big move. There are many videos on the site about this method.

### 10. The Vacuum Research Method for Day Trading

Same method as Part 4 (backward engineering), now applied specifically to day traders:

1. **Identify successful day traders** — on Twitter, YouTube, Twitch, prop firms
2. **Buy the most powerful vacuum** — go through every post, every video, every comment, every account they follow
3. **80% is noise** — reject comments on other people, personal life, general talk
4. **20% is meat** — find the trades, the setups, the tools, the processes
5. **Study who they follow** — iterate outward through their network
6. **Ask them** — if you have good questions, they may tell you directly

> "I will go through every post... That's what I did with Dan Zanger and all of these people. Like, you go through everything."

**But be aware of scams:** Many successful day traders stop responding to DMs because they get burned out by constant requests for money, help, and mentorship.

---

### 11. News Is the Lifeblood of Day Trading

> "News plays because whatever is in news for a day, that is going to make a big move."

> "News is their life blood. If a break news breaks during intraday, the day traders will be off that stock within seconds."

Knowing the news before the market opens gives a structural edge. Example: AVID had big news premarket. Anyone who knew that had an edge.

Knowing news is not optional:

> "You should be not asking, 'hey, what's the news on that?' You should be knowing. Right? You should be knowing."

If you don't know the news, you are not going to make it as a day trader.

---

### 12. The Recommended Path: Swing Trade First, Then Day Trade

> "Become a good swing trader. Then later it is easier to become a day trader because then you already know the mechanics of trade, stop, and you've done it at a slower pace. And then you run at a faster five times, six times. You're going to make it right."

Trying to day trade as your first entry point is the **hardest possible path**. You need:
- Mechanics mastered (entry, stop, exit)
- Understanding of setups
- Physical stamina
- ~$500/month in tools
- 3–5 years of runway with substantial losses
- The ability to handle PNL swings like -$1.2M in a single day

## Key Takeaways

1. **Day trading = swing setups at 10× speed** — breakout, anticipation, pullback still work, just faster timeframe.
2. **Additional day-trading-specific setups** — fade, SGU/BGD, news plays, order flow/tape reading, parabolic move fade, weak structure short. All made possible by intraday structure: gaps, opening prints, news, level II.
3. **The secret of big day traders is SIZE** — not a better setup. $2M–$15M/year traders do the same setups with 100K–2M+ share positions. PNL volatility is extreme.
4. **3–5 years minimum** to become good at day trading. Even at a top prop firm with training, mentorship, and best tech. During those years, expect substantial losses.
5. **Some people will never make it** because they cannot handle the PNL variance or the physical demands.
6. **Physical fitness matters** — 6 AM to 4 PM screen time, quick reactions, stamina.
7. **Tools cost ~$500/month** — Trade Ideas, DAS Trader, Benzinga Pro, custom scanners. News access is essential.
8. **Self-leadership is non-negotiable** — find information yourself, study YouTube, figure out tools. If you expect to be spoon-fed, "look for a job."
9. **Best beginner day trading setups:** anticipation, pullbacks, weak structure short. **Worst:** breakout chasing (always late, always chopped).
10. **Swing trade first, then day trade** — master mechanics at slower speed. Then speed up.
