# How to Get Started In Trading Guide – Part 1

## Summary

**Speaker:** Pradeep Bonde (StockBee) — introductory session for the StockBee community on the fundamental questions every trader must answer *before* starting. This is Part 1 of a multi-week series covering mindset and foundations, not strategy specifics.

### 1. Why Are You Trading? (Define Your Objective)

Most people stumble into trading without asking why. Your objective determines *everything* — your strategy, time commitment, and whether you should even be trading. Be honest:

- **Freedom/Lifestyle?** Trading won't give you freedom — it's a full-time job. Most successful traders work intensely all day.
- **Grow a retirement account?** Just DCA into an ETF. Don't waste time learning to trade.
- **Escape a job you hate?** Trading is probably not the solution. Look at other options first.
- **Make serious money ($10M+)?** Then your path is clear but extremely demanding.

If you can't answer "what am I in it for?" with conviction, you're likely wasting time and money.

### 2. Profit Expectations Dictate Your Path

Your profit target determines your trading style, not the other way around:

| Goal | Realistic Path | Capital Needed |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| $1K/day | Day trading only | Varies, but requires full-time commitment |
| $100K/month | Swing trading with large account | ~$300K+ with elite skill |
| $10M+ (wealth building) | OPM (Other People's Money — hedge funds) | Track record + sales skills |
| Tax-efficient growth | Long-term holding (3–5 years) | Research-oriented, established businesses |
| Escape a job | Find a different job or side hustle | N/A |

**Key insight on OPM:** Every single billionaire trader (Warren Buffett, Paul Tudor Jones, Jim Simons, Ray Dalio, Ken Griffin, David Tepper, George Soros) built their wealth through OPM — managing other people's money at leverage — not through personal account trading. If you want to be a billionaire in trading, OPM is the only proven path.

### 3. Capital Requirements — Be Realistic

- **$300K minimum** to trade for a living, preferably **$500K+**
- Even at 30% returns, $300K gives you ~$90K after 45% taxes = barely enough to live on
- Below $300K, you're constantly on a treadmill and will likely deplete capital
- Pradeep sees people sold dreams at $25K–$30K accounts — "it's not going to work"
- If you only have $10K–$15K, **find a job first**, don't depend on trading for living expenses
- 401(k) money is not accessible without penalties and taxes — you become "asset rich, cash poor." Even if you make money in a 401(k), you can't take it out for living expenses without paying both income tax **and** a 10% early withdrawal penalty. Pradeep himself has much of his money trapped in restricted accounts — he's "asset rich" but faces cash flow challenges because withdrawing triggers tax + 10% penalty. You can borrow against it or buy real estate through it, but you still owe the mortgage. **Don't plan to make a living based on growing your 401(k).**
- Even Christian Kahl (KQ) kept his job until he made his first million day trading

### 4. Time Commitment — Superhuman Effort for 2–5 Years

- Every single successful trader Pradeep knows put in **2–5 years of superhuman effort** before becoming consistently profitable
- Mark Minervini worked as a nightclub bouncer so he could trade during the day — it took him 5–6 years
- Dan Zenger didn't quit his swimming pool business until he made $17M
- Christian Kahl spent **$10K+ on education**: $5K for Minervini's course, $1,500/$200mo for Dan Zenger's site, ~$300mo for Investors Underground, plus Access Trading and Embark Winner — he invested heavily in learning before discovering what worked for him
- "Superhuman effort" does **not** mean sending 17 DMs asking for info that's already on the site — it means doing your own research, building your own scans, and internalizing the methodology
- StockBee members typically attend 4–5 bootcamps before they truly "get it"
- People see EP setups on the timeline and still don't buy them — "they don't even understand what the scan is doing" because "none of this they made their own"

### 5. The Learning Curve Costs Money

- Learning period = inevitable losses + educational investment
- You can learn by losing money (tuition of the market) or by spending money on education (courses, tools, mentorship)
- "There are no free lunches in this business"
- If you can't find information on the stockbee site using the search bar, you're not going to make it — basic research skills are a prerequisite
- KQ found trades using the same combination scan that community members claim "finds setups too late to enter" — the problem isn't the scan, it's **you**

### 6. Common Mistakes That Guarantee Failure

- **Taking $30–$40 profits while taking $300–$400 losses** — this equation can never work
- **17 trades per day** — are there really 17 quality setups in a day? No.
- **Refusing to use recommended tools** — insisting on TradingView or Thinkorswim when you can't create your own scans, instead of just getting TC2000 and having all scans in place within 24 hours
- **Jumping between time frames** — spending 6 months on one style, 3 months on another, never committing. Pradeep admits he did this himself and it cost him years
- **Not making things "your own"** — running other people's scans without understanding the underlying market structure

### 7. The Most Important Decision: Time Frame & Frequency (Tomorrow's Topic)

- Choosing your time frame (day trading, swing trading, position trading, long-term investing) is the **single biggest decision** that will save you 3+ years
- Pradeep admits that if he had committed to swing trading from day one, "I would have saved so much money and effort and I could have gone to sleep at 9 PM, not 2 AM"
- He dithered between swing, position trading, and other styles for his first 3 years — don't make the same mistake
- Once you commit to a time frame, "very quickly things started to happen"

### 8. The Six-Week Window

- If you follow the template exactly — use the same software, same scans, same process — it should take **6–10 weeks** to be functional
- If you insist on experimenting on your own or can't follow instructions, it will take **6 months or 6 years**
- "6 weeks if you follow the template, 6 months if you experiment, 6 years if you're stubborn"

### Bottom Line

Trading is not an escape hatch, not a shortcut, and not "something to do." It is a demanding career that requires $300K+ capital, 2–5 years of superhuman effort, and the humility to follow proven processes before innovating. If you can't commit to that, index funds and a second job will serve you far better.
