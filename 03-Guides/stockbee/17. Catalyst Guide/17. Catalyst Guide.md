## The Core Truth

**Stocks move based on catalysts — not chart patterns.**

> *"Most of the major moves in any stocks are started by a catalyst. If you don't have any understanding of catalyst, then you are just a village idiot."*

Every big move has a catalyst behind it. The chart pattern is merely the *footprint* of that catalyst. If you only study charts, you are studying footprints without understanding what animal made them.

---

## The Three Dimensions of Every Catalyst

| Dimension | Question | Example |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Catalyst** | What is the event/news/driver? | Earnings beat, FDA approval, AI theme, new CEO |
| **Magnitude** | How far will the stock go? | 10%? 50%? 300%? 1200%? |
| **Duration** | How long will the move last? | 30 minutes? 3 days? 3 years? |

If you know all three, you know:
- Whether to trade it at all
- How large a position to put on
- How long to hold
- Where to place your stop

> *"Your ability to understand which catalyst is going to make a big move — that is the edge."*

## Catalyst Lifespan: Three Categories

Every catalyst falls into one of three lifespan categories. Misclassify it, and you will hold too long or sell too early.

### 1. Secular Catalyst (10–30+ years)

**Definition:** Fundamental structural change in industry, society, politics, or technology. Driven by forces much larger than any single company or government can control.

**Characteristics:**
- Persistence — doesn't fade in weeks or months
- Creates multi-year bull markets in affected sectors
- Multiple companies benefit simultaneously

**Examples:**

| Secular Trend | Duration | Key Stocks |
|---------------|----------|------------|
| **AI / Machine Learning** | 2023–2033+ | Nvidia, AMD, SMCI, AVGO |
| **Digital Payments** | 2015–2035 | PayPal, Square, Adyen |
| **E-commerce** | 1995–2025 | Amazon, Shopify |
| **Clean Energy / Solar** | 2005–2035 | Enphase, SolarEdge |
| **EV / Electrification** | 2015–2035 | Tesla, BYD |
| **Social Media** | 2005–2025 | Meta, TikTok |
| **Aging Population** | 2020–2050 | Healthcare, biotech |
| **Space Exploration** | 2020–2050 | SpaceX (private), Virgin Galactic |
| **Robotics / Automation** | 2020–2040 | Automation companies |
| **Death of Physical Retail** | 2010–2040 | Amazon, Costco, dollar stores |

**How to identify:** Travel outside the US. If a trend is already dominant in Asia or Europe, it is likely secular and coming to the US next.

> *"If you traveled outside the US five years ago, you'd see digital payment and tap-to-pay everywhere. Now it's coming to the US. Travel outside the US to find secular themes."*

---

### 2. Cyclical Catalyst (Quarters to 2–3 years)

**Definition:** Driven by business cycles, management efficiency, seasonality, or industry rotation. Under the control of the company or the economy.

**Characteristics:**
- Shorter duration than secular
- Often tied to earnings/sales cycles
- Management turnaround is a classic cyclical catalyst

**Examples:**
- Earnings acceleration for 3–6 quarters
- Management cost-cutting / turnaround (FedEx, Flextronics)
- Chemical industry cycle (up/down based on commodity prices)
- Retail rotation (ANF comeback for 14–18 months)

**How to identify:** Look for companies with improving earnings/sales over 2–4 quarters. Check if management has changed and is executing.

---

### 3. Hot Story Catalyst (1 day to 3–4 weeks)

**Definition:** Temporary excitement, news event, or market fad. Often absurd upon logical inspection, but the market runs with it anyway.

**Characteristics:**
- Shortest duration
- Highest frequency (300+ stocks/year)
- Can produce the biggest single moves (50–2000%+)
- Post-COVID market is dominated by stories

**Examples:**
- Biotech "12 patients took our drug, 8 didn't die" → 2600% move
- Vaping company becomes "crypto treasury company" → 548% move
- Company declares bankruptcy → retail squeezes shorts → 661% move
- Algorithm confuses private company with public one → 1600% move

**How to identify:** Run the 9M EP scan daily. When you see a stock with crazy volume (100M+ shares) and no obvious earnings catalyst, dig for the story.

> *"Story EP is the biggest opportunity in the market, because stories is what the market really, really, really, really loves."*

## Types of Catalysts

### Earnings & Sales Growth
- **Most common driver** of stock moves
- Includes forward guidance (sometimes more important than current quarter)
- Sales growth > profitability for market reaction

> *"Profitability doesn't matter. The market gives a shit about profitability. Sales growth is what the market reacts to."*

### Analyst Actions
- Upgrades, downgrades, price target changes
- Influential analysts can move stocks significantly
- Must have *valid reasoning* behind the call to be meaningful

### Government Policy
- Major, long-duration catalyst
- Examples: solar subsidies, Obamacare, HSA expansion, tariffs
- Can create entire bull markets in affected sectors

### Management Change
- New CEO/CFO = future direction signal
- CFO leaving = potential trouble signal
- Classic examples: FedEx turnaround, Flextronics restructuring

### Product Launch
- Tesla announcements, ChatGPT launch, Ozempic (Eli Lilly / Novo Nordisk)
- Must be *genuinely new*, not just an iteration

### Theme / Technology
- AI, cloud computing, cybersecurity, EV, drones, nuclear, quantum
- Hotter the theme, more stocks benefit
- Some themes fade (EV is "done" as a major theme); others persist (AI)

### Biotech / Drug News
- Evergreen category
- Can produce 100% to 1200%+ moves
- FDA approval, trial results, patent wins

### Fed / Central Bank
- "Mother of all catalysts"
- Can start and end major market moves
- Rate changes, QE/QT, forward guidance

> *"Fed is your daddy... Fed is the mother of all catalysts."*

### Political / Geopolitical
- Elections (Trump tax cuts), war, natural disasters
- Usually shorter duration unless structural policy change

## Story EPs: The Biggest Opportunity

### The Numbers

| Metric | Story Stocks | Real Earnings Stocks |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| 50–200%+ moves/year | **300+** | ~15–20 |
| Doubled in <2 months (recent scan) | **354** | ~3 (>$40B cap) |
| Top 25–50 winners (any period) | 90%+ are stories | <10% have real earnings |
| Frequency | Dime a dozen | Rare |

**Conclusion:** If you only trade stocks with real earnings, you miss 90%+ of the biggest opportunities.

### Anatomy of a Story Stock

1. **Plausible narrative** — sounds possible, even if ridiculous
2. **Hot theme** — fits current market obsession
3. **Influencer / Promoter** — someone with credibility or following
4. **Low float + High short interest** — explosive squeeze potential
5. **Little/no real earnings** — often the more absurd, the bigger the move
6. **Massive premarket volume** — signals retail interest

### Story Stock Examples

| Stock | Story | Move | Reality |
|-------|-------|------|---------|
| **Oklo** | Small nuclear reactor (Sam Altman-backed) | 2674% ($6→$200) | No permit (denied). $100K revenue |
| **QuantumScape** | Solid-state battery | 50–200% repeatedly | No revenue. No applicable financials |
| **FuelCell** | Hydrogen fuel cell | 154%, 830%, 133% repeatedly | Never made money. Same story since 2021 |
| **RGC** | Herbal medicine "cured ADHD" in 6 patients | 1500% | Chinese company. Reverse split 38:1 |
| **Vaping→Crypto Co** | Converted to crypto treasury | 548% (833% intraday) | Traded 14.3M vs. prior 4K volume |
| **Beyond Meat** | Declared bankruptcy | 661% | Shareholders should get nothing. Retail squeezed shorts |
| **NovaBay (NBY)** | Algorithm confused with Tata Chemicals | 1600% | Mistaken identity |
| **Joby / Archer** | Vertical takeoff planes | 50–300% repeatedly | Unproven technology. No revenue |
| **Virgin Galactic** | Space travel | 463%, 170%, 95% | Repeatedly makes moves on same story |

> *"If you are too logical and skeptical a person, you'll never be able to make money from story stocks because every story is so ridiculous."*

### The Signal: Premarket Volume

The single best indicator that a story has legs:

| Premarket Volume | Interpretation |
|-----------------|----------------|
| 5M–9M+ shares | "Enough fools are lined up here. I might as well line up." |
| 100M+ shares | Retail is frothing. This will be explosive. |
| Normal volume | No story. Skip it. |

### How to Find Story EPs

1. Run **9M EP scan daily**
2. Focus on stocks with **crazy volume** (100M+ shares)
3. Check **premarket volume** — 5M+ = signal
4. Identify the **theme** — semiconductor? Drone? Space? Nuclear? Crypto?
5. Check for **influencers** — search X/Reddit: "Why is [ticker] going up?"
6. Check **float and short interest** — low float + high short interest = max explosiveness
7. Use AI/Google — "Why is [ticker] stock going up?"
8. Check for **bankruptcy / reverse merger / activist** — these ignite stories

### Shorting Story Stocks

**Rule #1: Don't short while they're going up.**

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Wait for story to run its course |
| 2 | Watch for **9M breakdown** |
| 3 | Don't catch first 20–50% of drop |
| 4 | Wait for first leg down, then short the next leg |

Examples: Oklo, SMR, Quantum stocks, MicroStrategy — all had 9M breakdowns after the story soured.

## How to Validate Any Catalyst

### The Volume Test

Volume is the market's voting machine on catalyst quality:

| Volume Signal | Interpretation |
|-------------|----------------|
| Unusual volume on normally quiet stock | Significant catalyst |
| 9M+ on catalyst day | Market cares. Valid catalyst. |
| 100M+ on story stock | Retail frenzy. Story has legs. |
| Normal volume on "news" day | Market doesn't care. Fake catalyst. |

> *"Volume tells you how good the catalyst is. Volume tells you whether this is a significant catalyst or not."*

### The Delayed Reaction Test

If a catalyst is valid and powerful, the stock won't give up gains immediately. It will sit there and continue higher. If it collapses back to pre-catalyst levels, the catalyst was weak or fake.

### The Daily Study Process

**Morning routine (10 minutes):**
1. Look at stocks up 50%+ this week
2. Ask: **"What is the catalyst?"**
3. Document: catalyst type, magnitude, duration
4. Check if you missed it — study the setup for next time

**Weekend routine (30 minutes):**
1. Look at YTD, 3-month, 5-year winners
2. Trace back: what catalyst started each move?
3. Classify: secular / cyclical / hot story
4. Build your mental database

> *"Study catalyst. Weekly study catalyst. The moment you see a big move, your first reaction should be: What's the catalyst?"*

### The Database Method (JBM's Approach)

1. Document **every SIPs** you see
2. Document **every missed move**
3. Screenshot the chart, save the news
4. Do this for **2 years**
5. Your skills will be "thousands of miles ahead" of others

> *"There is no book which is going to teach you this... 98% of trading books are about support/resistance. Very few focus on catalyst-based trading."*

## Entry Strategies by Catalyst Type

| Catalyst Type | Best Entry | Stop | Hold Time |
|---------------|-----------|------|-----------|
| **Earnings EP** | OPG (open) or ORB | 2.5–5% | 2–5 days |
| **Secular theme** | DEP (delayed reaction) or ORB | 2.5% | Weeks to months |
| **Story EP** | OPG premarket / first 10 min | Low of day | 1–5 days |
| **Biotech FDA** | OPG or premarket | 5–10% | 1–3 days |
| **Cyclical turnaround** | DEP after first pullback | 2.5% | 2–8 weeks |
| **Analyst action** | OPG or intraday | 2.5% | 1–2 days |

**Key principle:** The stronger your catalyst conviction, the earlier you enter. If you truly understand the catalyst, you don't need confirmation.

> *"If you understand catalyst, your job is to read the news, understand the catalyst, and enter it immediately. Otherwise, what is the point?"*
