# EP 9 Million — Scan-Based Catalyst Setup

## Overview

EP 9 Million (EP9M) is Pradeep Bonde's **#1 recommended setup** — a scan-based method that identifies stocks in the top 1–2% of daily volume. The scan finds the signal (volume); you then identify the catalyst and trade accordingly.

> "EP 9M is my most recommended setup for anybody. This is the only thing which I get up in the morning for." — 2026-05-29

> "EP9M gives you daily opportunities. In a year, probably at best 5–10 [for pure EP]. While this can give you anywhere from 300 to 1000 opportunities." — 2026-05-29

### What EP9M Tells You

- A stock appearing in EP9M scan has volume in the **top 1–2%** of all traded instruments that day
- Out of ~12,554 total issues (5,450 stocks + 6,416 ETFs + ~400 ADRs), only ~270 show up on a typical day
- Volume itself tells you "there is something important here" — then you find the catalyst
- EP9M can be **catalyst-based** (real news) or **story/fugazi** (hype) — both are tradeable if volume confirms

### EP vs. EP9M — Critical Distinction

| Aspect | EP (Pure) | EP 9M |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Discovery | Premarket (NTRT/MTRT) | Post-market scan |
| Catalyst | Known before entry | Found after scan confirms volume |
| Frequency | 3–5/year | 300–1000/year |
| Skill needed | High | Medium |
| Gap | Large gap common | Often non-gap (closer stops) |
| Examples | GLW, CRCL (after IPO neglect) | Sweetgreen, NFViz, Oracle |

### LiquidLava

- Stocks trading $9M+ volume daily (e.g., Wells Fargo)
- Allows **large position sizing** due to high liquidity
- 10% swing with 150% position size = 15% account move
- Same risk, bigger size = bigger returns

## Setup Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Scan | EP9M — top 1–2% volume (c-o ≥ $0.90, vol ≥ 100K) |
| Universe | ~12,554 issues; ~270 qualify on typical day |
| Qualifier 1 | **SOS** (start of swing) — non-negotiable |
| Qualifier 2 | Catalyst classification (cat/story/liquidlava) |
| Entry | OPG, opening range breakout, or first SOS pullback |
| Stop | 8–10% for EP9M entries; tighter for LiquidLava |
| Target | 50–500% in <1 quarter; most explosive moves happen in 5–20 days |
| Holding | 3–15 days max for explosive moves |
| Exit | Catalyst-based target, first 4% breakdown, or sell 80% into strength |
| Daily process | Start scans at 9:30 AM, run combo bullish/bearish sorted by volume |
| Classification | Cat (real catalyst), Dog (fugazi/story), LiquidLava (high-volume quality) |

## NTRT/MTRT — Daily Process for Pure EP

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| Night (NTRT) | After market close, scan for stocks gapping 4%+ on 100K+ volume |
| Morning (MTRT) | Pre-market, check same scans, qualify with Magna 53 Cap 10 by 10 |
| Premarket | Identify catalyst using Seeking Alpha, Finviz, Grok, ChatGPT |
| Entry | OPG at open or first few minutes |

## Key Takeaways

1. **EP9M is the #1 recommended setup** — most opportunities, daily availability
2. **Volume confirms importance; catalyst determines magnitude** — find the catalyst after volume signal
3. **SOS is non-negotiable** — never enter without start of swing
4. **Themes are the #1 catalyst** — AI, drones, nuclear energy, etc. produce the biggest moves
5. **Biotech = male drone** — 99% make one move then die; focus on technology stocks instead
6. **LiquidLava allows large sizing** — $9M+ daily volume stocks for big position sizes
7. **All explosive moves happen in 5–20 days** — this is not a long-term method
8. **Understand catalyst strength** — fugazi catalysts still work if volume confirms, but real catalysts allow bigger sizing
9. **EP9M requires more skill than Sugar Babies** but less than pure EP
10. **NTRT/MTRT is the daily process** — nighttime and morning right time to find big winners
