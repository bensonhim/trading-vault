# SOS / Dollar Breakout / 4% Breakout

## Overview

SOS (Start of Swing) is the **universal entry qualifier** across all StockBee setups. It's not a standalone strategy — it's the trigger mechanism. Two scan-based variants exist:

- **Dollar Breakout (SOS)**: close minus open ≥ $0.90, volume ≥ 100K — finds low-risk intraday entries
- **4% Breakout**: stocks making 4%+ moves (or 4%+ range expansion) — verified across 40 years of data; works both directions

> "SOS is required for everything in this field. That is a requirement." — 2026-05-29

## Key Concepts

### SOS — Start of Swing

- **First day** of a new directional move — never buy 2nd, 3rd, 4th day of an existing swing
- SOS is both a **context** (where are we in the move?) and a **qualifier** (is this a valid entry?)
- Without SOS, no setup works — EP9M, DEP, Sugar Babies all require SOS
- "If you cannot understand SOS, you cannot trade" — foundational concept

### Dollar Breakout (SOS-425 variant)

- Close minus open ≥ $0.90
- Volume ≥ 100,000
- Identifies stocks where aggressive buying/selling happened on day 1
- Works on both long and short side

### 4% Breakout

- Stock makes 4%+ move OR 4%+ range expansion
- Tends to start 25%+ moves — verified across 40 years of data
- Works both directions (long and short)
- FHP variant: Focus on High-Priced stocks (> $60) — same $0.90 c-o filter but high-priced = low volatility, more linear/longer moves, institution-dominated

### Tightness

> "My definition of tightness for my personal trading is plus or minus 0.4%. Ideally, I like to see tightness." — 2026-06-11

- Tightness = price action ±0.4% or less before breakout
- Two tight days followed by a breakout = ideal entry
- Visual pattern — you see it or you don't; no need to measure with a ruler
- Tightness before SOS = higher probability setup

## Setup Parameters

| Parameter | Dollar Breakout | 4% Breakout | FHP Breakout |
|-----------|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| Close − Open | ≥ $0.90 | ≥ 4% move | ≥ $0.90 |
| Volume | ≥ 100K | — | ≥ 100K |
| Price filter | None | None | > $60 |
| Direction | Both | Both | Both |
| Entry | OPG, opening range breakout, or pullback | At breakout | At breakout |
| Stop | Low of day or 4% breakdown | 4% breakdown | Close stop (larger size possible) |
| Holding | 3–5 days typical | 3–5 days | 3–5 days |

## Entry Methods

1. **OPG (Opening Print Gap)** — Buy at the opening print for EP-style entries
2. **Opening Range Breakout** — Buy when stock breaks above first 5–15 min range
3. **Pullback on day** — Buy on intraday pullback after SOS confirmed
4. **Anticipation** — Buy before breakout when tightness + catalyst present (from watch list)
5. **Market orders** — Use when you want the whole setup; enter early in first 10–15 minutes

## Exit Methods

1. **Catalyst-based price target** — If you understand the catalyst, set a % target (e.g., 50% for EP9M, 30–40% for Sugar Babies)
2. **First 4% breakdown after entry** — Sell on first 4% breakdown from entry
3. **Sell 80% into strength, keep 20% with trailing stop** — For fast movers
4. **Structural stops** — For slow-moving stocks: move stop to low of breakout day, then advance on each new breakout

## Key Takeaways

1. **SOS is the universal qualifier** — never enter without it, regardless of setup
2. **Never buy extended stocks** — 2nd, 3rd, 4th day of a swing is NOT SOS
3. **Tightness before SOS increases probability** — look for ±0.4% or less
4. **4% breakout is empirically validated** across 40+ years of data
5. **FHP (High-Priced) variant allows larger position sizing** with closer stops
6. **All EP family setups require SOS** — it's the non-negotiable entry condition
7. **Enter early** — first 10–15 minutes is ideal; pre-decide from watch list
8. **Move stops aggressively on fast movers** — 3–5 day moves mean you must protect profits quickly
