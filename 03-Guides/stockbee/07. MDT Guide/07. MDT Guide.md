# MDT Guide — Modified Double Trouble (6-Month Momentum Ranking)

> **MDT** is a **6-month momentum ranking mechanism** that mimics IBD's Relative Strength Rating. It answers two questions: (1) Which stocks are the strongest right now? (2) Which stocks were strong a month or two ago and are now consolidating near their highs? MDT is specifically designed for **working professionals** who cannot trade 3–5 day momentum bursts but want to hold for multi-week or multi-month moves.

### 1. What Is MDT?

MDT (Modified Double Trouble) is a more advanced version of the [[06. DT Guide|Double Trouble]] method. While DT looks at 52-week lows, MDT looks at the **126-day moving average** — giving you a **6-month momentum perspective**.

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Formula** | `C / AVGC126` |
| **What it measures** | How far the stock is from its 126-day moving average |
| **Timeframe** | 6 months (126 trading days) |
| **Primary use** | Ranking stocks by relative strength; finding longer-term consolidation breakouts |
| **Best for** | Working professionals who want multi-week to multi-month holds |
| **Equivalent to** | IBD Relative Strength Rating (RS 99 = top ranked) |

**The logic:** A stock far above its 126-day MA has been in a strong uptrend for 6 months. The higher the MDT value, the stronger the stock.

> "MDT is essentially mimics, or it is going to get you equivalent of finding a stock which has a relative strength, high relative strength. It will be very similar to the IBD ranking."

**Example:** TURN ranked #1 by MDT would have an IBD RS rating of ~99.

### 2. Two Ways to Use MDT

#### 2.1 MDT as a Ranking Tool (Relative Strength)

Add MDT as a **column** in your TC2000 scan results:

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Insert column → Value column |
| 2 | Name: `MDT` |
| 3 | Formula: `C / AVGC126` |
| 4 | Sort descending to find strongest stocks |

**What you get:** A ranked list of the strongest stocks in the market. Top 50–100 are your focus universe.

**Use case:**
- Focus your money only on the strongest stocks
- Look for low-risk entry points on top-ranked stocks
- For shorting: rank by MDT ascending to find the weakest stocks

> "If you want to focus your money only on the strongest stock, then MDT is going to help you. Rank stock and isolate the top 100 stocks by MDT, or top 50 stocks by MDT, and look for low-risk entry points on them."

#### 2.2 MDT as Absolute Momentum Indicator

Instead of ranking, use MDT as a **boolean momentum indicator**:

| Condition | Formula | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **Bullish MDT** | `C / AVGC126 >= 1.18` | Stock is 18%+ above its 126-day MA |
| **Bearish MDT** | `C / AVGC126 <= 0.81` | Stock is 19%+ below its 126-day MA |

**Displayed as area plot on chart:** Green when MDT >= 1.18, showing the exact day momentum turned positive.

**Use case:**
- Find the start and end of longer-term momentum
- Identify when a stock entered a 6-month uptrend
- Find pullbacks within established 6-month trends

> "MDT 126 is useful for those people who are looking for longer consolidation breakout. If a stock is breaking out from a longer consolidation, you're getting in at the first leg of the move. So you can hold it for a second leg, and you can also hold it for third leg."

### 3. The Power of Offset MDT: MDT 25 and MDT 50

This is the most important concept in MDT. The problem with plain MDT is it shows you stocks that are strong **right now**. But the best setups are stocks that were strong **a month or two ago** and have since gone sideways.

#### MDT 25 — Strong 1 Month Ago

| Column | Formula | What It Finds |
|---|---|---|
| `MDT25` | `C25 / AVGC126.25` | MDT ranking **25 days ago** (1 month) |

**Why this matters:** A stock ranked #1 by MDT 25 days ago may have since gone sideways. That sideways consolidation is your setup.

**Two advantages:**
1. **Long setups:** Stock was strong, now consolidating near highs → breakout candidate
2. **Short setups:** Stock was strong, now breaking down → short candidate (e.g., Ciga)

> "Stocks which are very strong a month ago, when they go sideways and set up for a possible breakout, MDT 25 is going to allow you to find stocks like this."

#### MDT 50 — Strong 2 Months Ago

| Column | Formula | What It Finds |
|---|---|---|
| `MDT50` | `C50 / AVGC126.50` | MDT ranking **50 days ago** (2 months) |

**Why this matters:** 50 days = 2 months of trading. Stocks that were strong 2 months ago and are still holding near highs have formed even **longer, higher-quality bases**.

**Patterns you'll find:**
- Cup-with-handle
- VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern)
- Flat bases
- Longer-term consolidations

> "You want to find a stock which two months ago was ranked very highly by MDT, and you want to find out if they are setting up nicely. This is where you're going to get these patterns like cup with handle or VCP patterns or flat bases."

**Example:** Madrigal (MDGL) was ranked #1 by MDT 50 days ago. It then went sideways. Any breakout from that consolidation would be a good longer-term entry.

### 4. The Complete Workflow: From 410 Stocks to 10–15 Setups

This is Pradeep's recommended workflow for working professionals.

#### Step 1: Build the MDT Universe (~410 stocks)

1. Create a scan or use a watchlist of all US stocks
2. **Sort by MDT** → select top 200 → flag symbols
3. **Sort by MDT25** → select top 200 → flag symbols
4. **Sort by MDT50** → select top 200 → flag symbols
5. Copy all flagged symbols to a new personal watchlist (e.g., "MDT Universe")

**Result:** ~400–410 stocks total.

> "Select top 200, flag all symbols. Sort it by MDT 25, look for top 200. And then go to MDT 50 and sort by top 200. You need to look at 200 to 250, 300 in order to find good candidates. So you get total of 410 stocks. Copy flagged to a new personal watchlist."

#### Step 2: Eliminate Stocks That Reversed

Add **TI65** as a column and sort by TI65 ascending within your MDT Universe:

- Stocks with **very low TI65** have given up all their gains → **eliminate**
- These are not forming sideways bases; they reversed

> "If something has given up all the gains, you can eliminate these stocks very rapidly. These stocks are not forming a sideways base. They are reversed."

#### Step 3: Eliminate Biotechnology (Optional but Recommended)

For longer-term holds, Pradeep strongly recommends avoiding biotech:

- Insert **industry/sector** column
- Remove all biotechnology stocks
- From 410 → ~289 stocks

> "If you want to retain your money and also not really get into a poor house, it's much better to not trade biotech for longer term hold."

#### Step 4: Additional Filters (Optional)

Narrow further before visual review:

| Filter | How | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **IPO age < 5 years** | Add IPO date or use TC2000 filter | "The first few years of a stock's life is where the fun is" — young stocks make the biggest moves |
| **Price > $8–9** | Sort by price, eliminate low-price stocks | Reduces candidates; low-price stocks are noisier and riskier |
| **Earnings date** | Check earnings calendar before entry | Don't enter just before earnings — avoid holding through unpredictable gaps |

> "I only do this on stocks which have appeared in the last five years. This is where you get good bang for your buck. After five years, it's all like the first few years of marriage — that's where the fun is. After that, it's all washing the dishes and drudgery."

#### Step 5: Visually Review Remaining Stocks

Go through the remaining stocks on a **weekly chart** (easier to see bases):

- Remove stocks that are not forming clean sideways consolidations
- Remove stocks that are too extended (already broken out)
- **Always check for buyouts** — if a stock looks too good, verify it's not an acquisition target already
- Keep stocks with 2–3 month bases near highs

> "When you see something going sideways like this, the first thing is to check if there is a buyout. If there is a buyout, that's not a setup — that's a done deal."

**Result:** **10–15 stocks** setting up nicely per week.

> "400 stocks — if you do it on the weekend, you'll get a watch list of around 10 to 15 stocks which are setting up very nicely. And during the weeks, one or two might break out from that."

### 5. What to Look For: The Ideal Setup

The perfect MDT setup has these characteristics:

| Characteristic | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| **Big first leg** | Stock made a significant move up |
| **2–3 month consolidation** | Longer base = longer potential move |
| **Near the high** | Stock didn't give up gains |
| **Sideways, not reversed** | Flat or gently declining, not crashing |
| **High volume on breakout** | Confirms institutional interest |
| **Non-biotech** | Lower risk for longer holds |
| **First leg only** | Don't chase second or third leg |

### 6. Entry and Volume Requirements

#### The Breakout Rule

Any consolidation breakout you buy **must have high volume**:

> "You need a high volume breakout. You need real volume. You just don't need small volume. Any consolidation breakout you want to buy, there has to be tall volume."

| Volume Type | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| **High volume on breakout** | Institutional participation — valid entry |
| **Low volume on breakout** | Weak breakout — likely to fail, skip |

#### Entry Timing

| Style | Entry Point |
|---|---|
| **Aggressive** | Buy on the breakout day itself |
| **Conservative** | Wait 1–2 days for confirmation that the breakout has worked |

> "Some people will buy this as a breakout. Our friend Rahul will buy it another day, two more days, and he'll be confirmed that the breakout has worked, then he'll buy it. But if you are aggressive, you'll buy it here. But even if you buy it here, it still is okay because you're looking at holding for longer term."

### 7. Market Phase Dependency

MDT works best in specific market conditions:

| Market Phase | MDT Effectiveness |
|---|---|
| **Established bull market** | Best — multi-month moves are common |
| **Early recovery from correction** | Less effective — choppy, breakouts may fail |
| **Choppy/sideways market** | Poor — consolidations break down instead of up |
| **Bear market** | Avoid — bases fail |

> "These kind of methods work well once the market starts going up and enters multi-month bull market. In the beginning, they don't work well."

---

### 8. Who Is This For?

MDT is specifically designed for a particular type of trader:

| Profile | Why MDT Fits |
|---|---|
| **Working professionals** | Can't watch markets all day; weekend prep is sufficient |
| **Longer-term holders** | Want to hold for 2–3 legs, not just 3–5 days |
| **Larger accounts** | Position sizing works better with longer holds |
| **People with other commitments** | "Unfortunately decided they're going to spend their time bootlegging, and they just still want to make money" |

> "Those of you who keep asking me that they cannot trade this small two, three days — this is the method for you."

**Not for:** Beginners or people who haven't mastered shorter-term momentum burst trading first.

> "If you're a beginner, don't try all these things. You're better off just learning how to do simple things. The more they get exposed, the more they're going to get more confused."

### 9. Key Principles

#### Principle 1: Longer Consolidation = Longer Move
> "If there is a longer consolidation, the breakout which happens from it is going to be going up for a longer duration. The more consolidation is there, the more likely the move is going to be bigger magnitude move."

#### Principle 2: Get In at the First Leg
> "Best setup is going to be something which forms first leg. I don't look at second, third leg. If the first time it forms a base like this is when you want to be getting in."

#### Principle 3: Six Months Is the Sweet Spot
> "Most of the big moves will be less than six months. If you use too long a momentum, you're going to be buying very extended stocks. Six months is ideal."

#### Principle 4: MDT, MDT25, MDT50 Are a Family
All three work together:
- **MDT** = currently strong
- **MDT25** = strong 1 month ago (1-month consolidation setups)
- **MDT50** = strong 2 months ago (2-month consolidation setups)

#### Principle 5: Always Check for Buyouts
> "When you see something going sideways, always check if there is a buyout. If there is a buyout, that is not a trade opportunity."

#### Principle 6: Weekend Work, Weekday Execution
> "If you do it on the weekend, you'll get a watch list. During the weeks, one or two might break out from that."

### Key Takeaways

1. **MDT = 6-month momentum ranking** — equivalent to IBD Relative Strength
2. **Two uses:** (a) Rank stocks by strength, (b) Absolute momentum indicator (1.18 threshold)
3. **MDT25 and MDT50 are the secret weapons** — find stocks that were strong 1–2 months ago and are now consolidating
4. **Longer consolidation = longer move** — 2–3 month bases produce multi-week to multi-month moves
5. **The 410 → 10–15 funnel** — Top 200 MDT + MDT25 + MDT50, then eliminate reversed stocks, biotech, and extended stocks
6. **High volume breakout is mandatory** — low volume breakouts fail
7. **Designed for working professionals** — weekend prep, weekday execution, multi-week holds
8. **First leg only** — don't chase second or third leg
9. **Always check for buyouts** — if a stock looks too perfectly sideways, verify it's not already an acquisition target
10. **Young stocks perform best** — focus on stocks that IPO'd within the last 5 years for biggest moves
11. **Not for beginners** — master shorter-term trading first before attempting MDT
