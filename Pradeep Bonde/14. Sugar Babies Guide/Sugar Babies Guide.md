# Sugar Babies Guide

> **Speaker:** Pradeep Bonde (StockBee)  
> **Series:** Sugar Babies Guide (12 parts, including 3 Working People sub-series)  
> **Transcripts:** [[../../transcripts/14. Sugar Babies Guide/|Transcripts]]

## Summary

**Sugar Babies is a stock selection strategy, not a setup.** It identifies stocks that the market is "madly in love with" — stocks that repeatedly demonstrate the ability to make explosive moves. The core concept: find stocks that have had the most **4% breakouts with 9M+ volume** over a long lookback period (ideally 1,450 days in TC2000). These stocks are the market's "celebrities" — when they move, they swing 40–50% in 3–5 days instead of the typical 8–20% from ordinary stocks. Once identified, you trade standard setups on them (SOS, anticipation, reversal, EP) but with dramatically higher payoff. Sugar Babies is essentially a **refined version of the EP 9-million concept** applied to stock selection rather than catalyst timing. A typical Sugar Baby list contains 60–150 names; each produces ~4 tradable setups per year, giving 240+ high-probability trades annually from a focused, manageable watchlist.

## 1. What Is a Sugar Baby?

### 1.1 Core Definition

A **Sugar Baby** is a stock the market has "fallen in love with" — a stock that repeatedly produces **4% breakouts on 9M+ volume**. These are not ordinary stocks. They are the market's persistently popular names that, for whatever reason, attract sustained speculative interest.

> "Sugar Babies is a stock selection strategy. It is not a setup. The setups on this list will still be start of a swing, anticipation, or reversal." — *Part 5*

> "These are stocks which the market loves. These are stocks which repeatedly get the market falls in love with them." — *Part 9*

### 1.2 The "9 Million" Discovery

Pradeep discovered that explosive EP moves almost always start with a **9-million share volume breakout** on the first day. He then asked: *which stocks do this repeatedly?*

> "When I discovered EP as a strategy, the original strategy was to look at only earnings breakout. Then I extended it to look at anything which is a catalyst. And Sugar Baby is just a very refined version of EP." — *Part 1*

> "Most of the good EPs, the one which make very explosive moves... at the time of their breakout, they have 9M volume. MARA had 38M. CEI had 11M. That was basically 38M. And it was one of the best performing stocks in 2021, making 5,762% move." — *Part 1*

### 1.3 Why Sugar Babies Outperform

| Factor | Ordinary Stock | Sugar Baby |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| 3-day SOS move | 8–20% | 40–50%+ |
| Market interest | Sporadic | Persistent, "in love" |
| Volume on breakout | Variable | Consistently high (9M+) |
| Number of setups/year | 1–2 | 4+ |
| Predictability | Low | High (historical pattern) |

> "If you held the ordinary stock as a SOS for three days, it might make 8 to 20%. But the Sugar Baby will make in the same three days, 40% or more sometimes." — *Part 5*

## 2. The TC2000 Scan: How to Find Sugar Babies

### 2.1 Core Parameters

The scan is simple but requires TC2000's **Count Through** functionality:

| Parameter | Value | Why |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| **Price move** | C ≥ C1 × 1.04 | 4% breakout |
| **Volume** | V ≥ V1 AND V ≥ 8,900,000 | Volume expansion + 9M threshold |
| **Lookback** | 1,450 days (max in TC2000) | Long-term persistence |
| **Universe** | All US stocks | Broad search |
| **Sort by** | Count (descending) | Most frequent breakouts first |

> "You're looking for how many times in last 1,400 days this stock had 4% breakout with 9M volume. When you select stocks, select the top 20, 25, or 30." — *Part 6*

### 2.2 Building the Scan in TC2000

1. **Create a new scan** in TC2000
2. **Add condition:** `C / C1 >= 1.04 AND V >= V1 AND V >= 8900000`
3. **Add Count Through column:** Count how many times this condition was true in last 1,450 days
4. **Sort by Count Through** descending
5. **Top 20–30** = your weekly Sugar Baby list

> "How many times in last five days this stock had 4% breakout? You'll see these stocks had three 9M breakouts in the last five days. Now when I go to this particular thing and I do this only on a weekend." — *Part 6*

### 2.3 Frequency of Scanning

- **Weekly scan** (weekend) — refresh the full list
- **Daily monitoring** — track which Sugar Babies are setting up
- **No mid-week full rescans** unless market changes dramatically

## 3. Trading Sugar Babies: The Setup Layer

### 3.1 Sugar Babies Is NOT the Setup

This is the most common misunderstanding. Sugar Babies tells you **which stocks to watch**. The actual entry is still a standard StockBee setup:

| Setup | When to Use on Sugar Baby |
|-------|--------------------------|
| **SOS (Start of a Swing)** | Primary setup — 3–5 day momentum burst |
| **Reversal** | After pullback/consolidation within Sugar Baby trend |
| **Anticipation** | Pre-breakout tight action on a known Sugar Baby |
| **EP** | Earnings/catalyst surprise on a Sugar Baby |

> "Sugar Baby is essentially a stock selection strategy. The setup is not Sugar Baby because it's a stock selection strategy on which we trade setups like SOS, anticipation, and reversals." — *Part 6*

### 3.2 Position Sizing and Stop Management

Sugar Babies are **more volatile** than ordinary stocks:

- **Wider stops required** — they chop around during the move
- **Smaller position size** — compensate for volatility
- **Move stop to breakeven quickly** — but not too quickly (give room)

> "You have to have wide stop. You have to get good entries and wider stop because during the move, they will make 40, 50% move, but they'll be very choppy also sometimes." — *Part 9*

### 3.3 Prioritization Rule

When multiple setups appear simultaneously, **always prioritize Sugar Babies**:

> "When you have to prioritize a lot of trading ideas, you always take the Sugar Babies first because they have shown historically the ability to make big moves." — *Part 5*

## 4. Working People and Sugar Babies

### 4.1 Part-Time Trader Adaptation

The Working People sub-series (Parts 2–4) adapts Sugar Babies for traders with day jobs:

- **Scan once per week** (weekend) — 30 minutes
- **Set alerts** — Trade Ideas or TC2000 alerts on your Sugar Baby list
- **Focus on swing entries** — Not intraday; hold 3–5 days
- **End-of-day review** — 15 minutes after market close

### 4.2 Timeframe Selection

For working people, Sugar Babies are ideal because:
- They produce **swing moves** (3–5 days) — no need to watch screens all day
- **Higher predictability** — less time spent analyzing
- **Alerts handle discovery** — you don't need to scan intraday

## 5. Scaling: From 20 to 150 Sugar Babies

### 5.1 The Core List (20–30)

The most focused approach. Only the top 20–30 stocks by count-through frequency.

### 5.2 The Expanded List (150)

As shown in Part 12, you can scale to 150 Sugar Babies by:
- Lowering the count-through threshold slightly
- Including stocks with consistent (but less frequent) 9M breakouts
- Adding Trade Ideas alerts on the expanded list for automated discovery

> "If you decide to just focus on your Sugar Babies and get rid of everything else, you still have between 60 or 70 Sugar Babies multiplied by 4. You're looking at potentially 240 trades. Some of those swings will be 100% plus." — *Part 5*

## 6. Integration with Other Methods

### 6.1 Sugar Babies + EP

The highest-potential combination: an EP catalyst on a stock that is *already* a Sugar Baby. This is the "EP Sugar Baby" concept from Part 8.

### 6.2 Sugar Babies + SOS

The standard combination: trade SOS setups exclusively on your Sugar Baby list. This is Pradeep's primary trading approach.

### 6.3 Sugar Babies + Market Monitor

Only trade Sugar Baby setups when the Market Monitor is bullish (primary indicator above 200, ideally above 300).

## Key Takeaways

1. **Sugar Babies is a stock selection strategy, not a setup.** It tells you *which stocks* to trade. You still need SOS, reversal, anticipation, or EP for actual entry.
2. **The core metric:** 4% breakout + 9M volume, counted over 1,450 days. Top 20–30 stocks by frequency = your watchlist.
3. **Sugar Babies swing 40–50% in 3–5 days.** Ordinary stocks do 8–20%. Same setups, dramatically higher payoff.
4. **The market "falls in love" with certain stocks.** These stocks attract persistent speculative interest. You cannot always explain why — but you can measure it by counting breakouts.
5. **Create the list once per week.** Weekend scan in TC2000. Daily, just watch which Sugar Babies are setting up.
6. **Wider stops, smaller positions.** Sugar Babies are choppy. They will shake you out if your stop is too tight.
7. **Prioritize Sugar Babies over ordinary stocks.** When you have 100 scan results, the Sugar Babies on that list are the highest-probability trades.
8. **60–70 Sugar Babies × 4 setups/year = 240 trades.** Even a focused list provides ample opportunity. You don't need to scan the entire market.
9. **Sugar Babies originated from the EP 9-million discovery.** Pradeep noticed that the biggest EP moves started with 9M volume breakouts. He then asked: which stocks do this repeatedly?
10. **Working people can trade Sugar Babies effectively.** Weekly scan, alerts, end-of-day review. No need to watch screens all day.
11. **Each Sugar Baby gives ~4 tradable setups per year** (long or short). The list refreshes but core names persist for months or years.
12. **Sugar Babies can be integrated with Trade Ideas.** Create a watchlist of your Sugar Babies in TI, then add momentum alerts (e.g., "New High of Day") to catch breakouts automatically.
