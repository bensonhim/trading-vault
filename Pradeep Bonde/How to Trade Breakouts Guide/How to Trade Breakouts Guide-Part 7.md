# How to Trade Breakouts Guide — Part 7

### The Theme: Stock Selection by Youth

The breakout setup is fixed (anticipation, reversal, or breakout). What varies is **the stock list** you apply it to.

Part 7 introduces a third vehicle-selection criterion after fund ownership (Part 6) and revenue momentum (Part 5):

> **Youth — IPO age within the last 5 years.**

---

### The Core Thesis: Big Moves Happen Early

> "Maybe the stock might never again make the move again."

Historical evidence:
- **Amazon** — biggest move in first 4–5 years after IPO
- **Qualcomm** — monster move within first 5–6 years
- **Shopify** — moved 4,300% in first 5 years; after that, sideways
- **Upstart (UPST)** — 1,400% out of the gate; now likely never repeats
- **MongoDB** — massive run from $34 to $500+ in first few years
- **Shockwave** — 400–500% in first few years

The logic:

> "Once a company becomes older, it's difficult for them to make big moves."

Young companies have **vitality** — new products, rapid growth, undiscovered stories. Old companies are "done, married, had kids, and out of the market."

### The IPO Youth Screen

#### MarketSmith Version
| Filter | Value |
|---|---|
| IPO Date | 2018 → 2023 (5-year window) |
| Exclude | ETFs/CLOs |
| Liquidity | Volume > 300,000 |
| Price | Any (most will be < $50 naturally) |

**Result:** ~1,017 stocks → after liquidity filters: **only ~307 stocks**

> "Is it very difficult to keep an eye on 307 stocks? Answer is no."

#### TC2000 Version (No MarketSmith Needed)

Add a column called **Price Growth Rate 5 Years**:
- Stocks with **no value** = IPO within last 5 years
- Sort by this column; the blanks at bottom are your young stocks
- Apply standard liquidity filter to eliminate thin names

---

### How to Use the Youth List

#### 1. Run Standard Breakout Scan on It
Apply the same 3 setups:
- Bottom bounce
- Continuation setup
- Consolidation breakout

> "Only 300 stocks. Any one of those sectors, whether there is a bottom bounce or a continuation setup or consolidation breakout."

#### 2. Build an Anticipation List

Not every youth stock is breaking out every day. Create anticipation lists for those setting up:

- Sort by YTD % positive — identify which young stocks are already leading
- These are the ones with real momentum

#### 3. Research the Story (Once)

When a young stock makes an abnormal move, **research it once**:
- What is the business?
- Is it a real product/service or a fad?
- Is it in a growth sector (e.g., energy storage, SaaS, fintech)?
- Does it have the potential for a multi-year move?

> "Once you research, do you need to research it again? Answer is no."

**Example — Fluent Energy:**
- Provides energy storage for renewables
- Real business, real sales (~$400M)
- Growth sector
- Worth keeping an eye on for an EP (earnings-driven) breakout

**Counter-example — Gato Silver:**
- A metal/mining stock
- No explosive capacity for a multi-year move
- Not a priority

#### 4. Set "Sugar Babies"

Young stocks that pass research become your **sugar babies** — long-term watchlist items you monitor for repeated entries:

> "Can we find one of them early in its move and ride that trend?"

### Priority Framework

Pradeep's current morning priority:

| Priority | List | Purpose |

| **#1** | 5,000+ funds (9 stocks) | **Size** — large position, low anxiety |
| **#2** | Youth IPO < 5 years (~307 stocks) | **New discoveries** — explosive potential, story stocks |
| **#3** | Bucket zero / Bucket one | **Real growth** — sales + momentum validated companies |

These three lists cover different risk profiles:
- **Fund ownership** list = stability, weighted by institutional consensus
- **Youth list** = vitality, story-driven, higher explosive potential
- **Bucket list** = revenue momentum, real business growth

---

### The Key Distinction: Youth ≠ Bucket Zero

| | Youth | Bucket Zero |
|---|---|---|
| **Filter** | IPO < 5 years | Sales growth > 39% |
| **Includes** | IPOs with no sales yet | Companies with proven revenue |
| **Best for** | Story stocks, early-stage discovery | Real business momentum |
| **Risk** | Higher (many fail) | Lower (have revenue track record) |

> "Here the factor is youth. Here the factor is sales. These are two separate factors."

**Recommendation:** Keep both. Youth finds MongoDB before it has revenue. Bucket zero confirms it after sales appear.

---

### Reducing Workload

> "There is no need to jump on every single breakout."

If you operate only from the **youth list** (~307) instead of all stocks (~6,000):

- **Anticipation lists** become smaller and higher-quality
- **Daily review** goes from thousands → hundreds
- **Research** stays fresh because young stocks are constantly entering
- The 9 stocks in the fund-ownership list are trivial to monitor

---

### Key Principle: Research Once

> "If I research these stocks every week, then over a period of time, you know every single stock."

- Young companies that have already moved = known, done research, no need to repeat
- New names that just IPO'd or just broke out = research immediately
- Focus energy on the **new new kid on the block**, not yesterday's story

---

### Practical Workflow

1. **Weekly:** Update youth list (outsourced sister-in-law, or manual Excel export)
2. **Daily:** Sort by YTD positive; identify which are setting up
3. **On breakout:** Check if already researched; if not, research once
4. **Mark charts:** Pradeep manually labels "2500" or "5000" on TC2000 charts so he knows instantly which list a stock belongs to
5. **Position size:** larger on widely held, smaller on neglected young stocks

---

### Key Insight

> "Let's catch them young."

The market's biggest winners — Shopify, MongoDB, Qualcomm, Upstart — all made their life-changing moves **within the first 5 years of going public**. After that, probability of a repeat is near zero. The youth list ensures you never miss the next young stock riding a new trend.
