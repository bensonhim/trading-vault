# How to Trade Breakouts Guide — Part 5

### Setup vs. Vehicle: Two Parts of the Equation

The breakout setup (momentum burst) is only half the battle. **Vehicle selection** is equally critical:

> "You want to select vehicles where there is a very high probability that the move will be explosive."

A perfect consolidation breakout on a slow-moving utility stock is pointless. A mediocre breakout on a high-growth small-cap can be life-changing.

---

### Method 1: IBD Momentum Approach (Growth + Momentum)

The classic IBD / William O'Neil method — widely used by retail traders.

#### Criteria
| Filter | Value | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| RS Rating | ≥ 85 | Top 15% by 6-month relative strength |
| Price vs 52-Week High | ≥ 85% | Within 15% of highs — avoids falling knives |
| Price | > $15 | Liquidity and stability |
| EPS Rating | ≥ 85 | Earnings momentum (though Pradeep argues this is the wrong metric) |
| Volume | ≥ 1.5 × avg(V50.1) | Confirms institutional participation |

#### The "85-85-85" List
IBD publishes a computer-generated list requiring:
- Price > $15
- Within 15% of 52-week high
- EPS ≥ 85 **and** RS ≥ 85
- Avg daily volume ≥ 10,000 shares

#### What It Finds Well
- Consolidation breakouts (cup-with-handle, flat base, double bottom)
- Growth-momentum leaders near highs

#### What It Misses
- **Bottom bounces** — no stock near highs meets this criteria
- **Continuation setups** — a stock like CHRS in mid-move won't be within 15% of its 52-week high
- **Early-stage growth** — many explosive stocks haven't earned profits yet

> "By choice they are selecting not to trade certain kind of bottom bounces."

---

### Method 2: Pradeep's "Bucket Zero & Bucket One" (Revenue Momentum)

This is Pradeep's primary vehicle-selection method for longer-term holds and bigger positions.

#### The Core Insight: Sales > Earnings

Multiple research sources (including Morgan Stanley and IBD's own evolving analysis) confirm:

> "What makes the stock go up is not really the earnings momentum — it's the sales momentum."

**Examples:**
- **Shopify**: Made massive moves while unprofitable; sales growth was phenomenal
- **MongoDB**: $34 → $500+ without consistent profit; driven by sales
- **Celsius Holdings**: Explosive growth driven by phenomenal sales, not profitability
- **Digital Turbine (APPS)**: ~6000%+ move from ~$250M market cap, fueled by sales

#### Bucket Zero (Highest Priority)

| Criterion | Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Sales Growth | 2 consecutive quarters ≥ 39% | Revenue acceleration = institutional interest |
| Market Cap | < $10B | Room for explosive expansion |
| IPO Age | ≤ 10 years | Maximum growth happens in first decade |
| Annual Sales | > $25M | Avoid micro-revenue companies |
| Liquidity | Sufficient daily volume | Tradeable without slippage |

**Why Bucket Zero works:**
- Young companies grow fastest — "once they become older, it's difficult for them to make big moves"
- Small cap stocks can 10× their market cap; large caps cannot
- Turnaround situations naturally appear here (sales turnaround → >39% growth after negative quarters)
- Best risk-adjusted returns: "If you find something at < $500M–$1B cap + 39% sales growth, that's explosive"

#### Bucket One (Secondary Priority)

| Criterion | Value | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Sales Growth | 2 consecutive quarters ≥ 39% | Same revenue acceleration |
| Market Cap | Any (can be > $10B) | Lower growth ceiling but still valid |
| IPO Age | > 10 years | Older companies with renewed growth |

Bucket One catches companies like DraftKings ($16B cap, 81% sales growth) that still move but with lower explosive potential.

#### Turnaround Capture

> "When there is a turnaround EP, is it going to be captured by your bucket zero and bucket one? The answer is yes."

A true turnaround = sales turnaround (not cost-cutting). Negative quarters → positive quarters = >39% comparative growth = automatic bucket inclusion.

---

### The Practical Workflow

1. **Daily bucket updates** outsourced (Pradeep's sister-in-law generates cleaned lists)
2. **Import into MarketSmith** (or equivalent tool)
3. **Apply breakout scans** (bottom bounce / continuation / consolidation) to bucket-filtered universe
4. **Monitor intraday** for setups on bucket-zero stocks
5. **Position size up** when conviction is high (5–10% of account)

> "Your probability of missing a big turnaround is very low."

---

### Why 39%? (The Number Is Arbitrary)

- **29%** used to be Pradeep's threshold → gave too many stocks
- **39%** reduces universe to manageable handful
- **49%+** captures fewer — some industries (retail, restaurants) rarely grow that fast
- The exact number is less important than the principle: **prioritize revenue momentum + small cap + youth**

---

### Key Takeaway

> "Sales growth is the real story behind these bigger moves of stocks... Short-term doubling/tripling can happen for no reason. But if a stock is going to go up for multiple years, it will be because of two reasons: valuation was very small, and it was growing rapidly."

The breakout setup tells you WHEN to enter. Bucket zero tells you WHAT to enter. Together they create trades with both timing AND explosive potential.
