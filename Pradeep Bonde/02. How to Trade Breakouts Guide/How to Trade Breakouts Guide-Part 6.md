# How to Trade Breakouts Guide — Part 6

### Setup vs. Vehicle: Fund Ownership as a Filter

The breakout setup (price action + volume) tells you **when** to buy. **Fund ownership** tells you **what** to buy — and how much conviction to have.

> "You want to select vehicles where there is a very high probability that the move will be explosive."

Pradeep introduces **five buckets** based on mutual fund ownership, each with different risk/reward profiles and suitable for different account sizes.

---

## Five Fund-Ownership Buckets

### Bucket A: 5,000+ Funds (The "Sleep Well at Night" List)

| Trait | Value |
|---|---|
| Count | Only **9 stocks** |
| Character | Mega-cap, ultra-stable, liquid |
| Purpose | Large position sizing, low overnight anxiety |
| Examples | Microsoft (9,213), Nvidia, Google, Apple, Amazon, UnitedHealth, JNJ, Visa, Mastercard |

**Why this works:**
- More funds = more stability — "if more funds own a stock, it's more stable"
- Breakouts here are backed by institutional conviction, not speculative noise
- Can hold through volatility without panic
- Also acts as a **market health indicator** — when all 9 correct simultaneously, the market itself is correcting

> "Can you put a bigger position in this kind of a stock and hold it and not worry too much about it? The answer is yes."

**Trade-off:** Lower explosive potential. These are not 10-baggers. But with size, a 10% move on a $1M position = $100K.

---

### Bucket B: 2,500+ Funds (The Widely Held Expansion)

| Trait | Value |
|---|---|
| Count | **~125 stocks** (includes the 9 above) |
| Character | Large-cap, institutional favorites |
| Purpose | More opportunities while retaining stability |
| Examples | Adobe, Cisco, JPMorgan, Meta, Pfizer, Merck |

**Key insight:** Only 125 out of ~500 S&P 500 stocks are widely held by 2,500+ funds. This itself is a filter — if funds consider these the "most important," they deserve attention.

This list changes slowly — weekly/monthly updates are sufficient.

---

### Bucket C: 1,000+ Funds + IPO < 5 Years (Kevin Marder's "Liquid Glamour" Twist)

**Inspired by Kevin Marder** (recently deceased), who found that stocks with 1,000+ fund ownership make persistent, multi-year moves.

Pradeep adds a **youth filter**:

| Trait | Value |
|---|---|
| Count | Only **29 stocks** |
| Filters | IPO < 5 years AND 1,000+ funds |
| Character | Young companies validated by institutional due diligence |
| Examples | Uber, Airbnb, DoorDash, Moderna |

**Why add the IPO filter?**
- A young stock that already has 1,000+ funds must have something special
- Funds don't pile into young stocks by accident — "they would have done some due diligence"
- These are growth ideas with institutional backing
- Allows **larger position sizing** with higher confidence: "I know I'm not alone. The funds are also liking this."

---

### Bucket D: 1,000+ Funds + Ownership Up 9%+ (The Aggressive Accumulation List)

| Trait | Value |
|---|---|
| Count | Only **24 stocks** |
| Filter | 1,000+ funds AND fund count increased by 9%+ since last report |
| Character | Institutions are actively buying, not just holding |
| Examples | First Solar, Schlumberger, Enphase, Airbnb, Booking |

This captures the **inflection point**: when funds already holding a stock start adding more.

> "When this happens and if they break down, then you know a lot of these funds are going to get out of it."

**Trade example:** First Solar appeared on this list before its major move — "one of the reasons why I recommended First Solar for the working people."

---

### Bucket E: < 100 Funds + 39% Sales Growth (The Explosive Neglect List)

For smaller accounts seeking maximum explosive potential.

| Trait | Value |
|---|---|
| Count | ~50 stocks (after filters) |
| Filters | Funds < 100 + sales growth ≥ 39% + annual sales > $25M + liquidity > 100K volume + price > $3 |
| Character | Neglected, under-owned, high-growth |
| Example | AEHR: 55 funds → 114 funds after catalyst → explosive move |

**Why this works:**
- Big moves happen when **neglected stocks** get discovered
- Low fund ownership = room for 100→1,000 funds to pile in
- The shift from 55 → 114 funds is what drives the explosive move
- Combines with Bucket Zero/One sales-growth criteria from Part 5

> "These explosive moves are always going to be on stocks which are very low fund ownership... Once something comes in, those stocks make a big move."

**Trade example:** TSCS — found via this bucket, bought because it's a neglected sales-star with a bottom-bounce setup.

## The Spectrum: Stability vs. Explosiveness

| Bucket | Funds | Count | Stability | Explosive Potential | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 5,000+ | 9 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐ | Large accounts, low anxiety |
| B | 2,500+ | ~125 | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐ | Balanced |
| C | 1,000+ + <5yr IPO | 29 | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐ | Growth + stability |
| D | 1,000+ + ↑9% | 24 | ⭐⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Active accumulation plays |
| E | <100 + sales growth | ~50 | ⭐⭐ | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Small accounts, max upside |

**The rule:**
- **More funds owned** = can put larger size, sleep better, lower explosive potential
- **Fewer funds owned** = smaller size only, higher anxiety, higher explosive potential

## Implementation: Same Setup, Different Conviction

> "What setup do you trade on this? Same setup. I trade breakouts and pullbacks."

The breakout setup (bottom bounce / continuation / consolidation) is **unchanged**. What changes is:
1. **Position size** — larger on widely held, smaller on neglected
2. **Hold time** — longer on widely held, shorter on neglected
3. **Conviction level** — higher when funds are actively accumulating

**Daily workflow:**
1. Import bucket lists (outsourced — sister-in-law generates them)
2. Apply standard breakout scan (combination bullish)
3. When a stock shows up, check which bucket it's in
4. Size the position based on bucket stability
5. Mark charts with fund count (e.g., "5000", "2500", "<100") so you know instantly

---

## Key Insight: Fund Ownership as a Sentiment Filter

> "I have a higher confidence level because I know I'm not alone. The funds are also liking this particular thing."

Fund ownership is not just a stock selection filter — it's a **conviction amplifier**:
- A breakout on a stock with 5,000+ funds is backed by massive institutional consensus
- A breakout on a stock with <100 funds is a speculative bet with higher payoff
- A breakout on a stock where funds just increased by 9% is backed by **momentum in institutional sentiment**

The exact number thresholds (5,000 / 2,500 / 1,000 / <100) are arbitrary — what matters is the principle: **use fund concentration as a proxy for stability and institutional validation**.
