# How to Get Started In Trading Guide – Part 3

**Source:** https://stockbee-videos.b-cdn.net/2021-10-13-how-to-get-started-in-trading/2021-10-13-how-to-get-started-in-trading.mp4

## Summary

Part 3 picks up where Part 2 left off. You've decided your time frame — now what? **Figure out what everybody on that road is actually doing, then master ONE of those things.** Pradeep's central claim: every successful trader in each category is doing the same 2–3 generic setups. They just give them fancy names. Don't chase exotic techniques — perfect the common ones first.

---

### 1. Performance Sport — No Participation Trophies

Trading is not school. There are no marks for "knowing things." You either make money or you don't. Karate schools give everyone a black belt and a photo — trading doesn't. It's a 100% performance sport. You have to build a skill and build an edge.

---

### 2. The Generic Setups for Each Category

Once you've chosen your time frame (Part 2), find the **most commonly used setups** for that category. This is not hard today — YouTube, Twitter, blogs, Twitch. When Pradeep started in 1998–99, there was only Silicon Investor forums and Yahoo boards. Now the problem isn't finding information — it's filtering the junk.

| Category | What Everyone Is Actually Doing | Same Thing, Different Name |
|----------|------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **Day Trading** | News plays (stocks in play), shorting parabolic/pump moves, breakouts, pullbacks | "stocks in play", "fading", "pump & dump shorts", "support/resistance" |
| **Swing Trading** | **Breakouts**, **anticipation**, **pullbacks** | VCP (Minervini), TBA (Zanger), "Trader's Action Zone", "slingshot" (Redler), "consolidation buy", "EP/PEG" (earnings breakout) |
| **Position Trading** | Growth stocks, value stocks, dividend aristocrats | "hypergrowth", "IBD-style", "biotech focus", "cyclical value" |

**What differentiates returns is NOT the setup — it's size and trade count.** Oliver Kell does 400–500 trades per year and calls himself a "position trader," but his average profit per trade is 8–20% — same as swing trading. He just puts more size on.

**Specific trader breakdowns Pradeep gives from 20 years of study:**

| Trader | What They Actually Do | Not What They Claim |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Dan Zanger** | Buys breakouts of $40+ stocks with dollar breakouts; used to buy low-float breakouts | Calls it "Technical Buy Area" (TBA) |
| **Mark Minervini** | Anticipation (VCP), breakouts, pullbacks | Calls it "Volatility Contraction Pattern" |
| **Scott Redler** (StockBee alum) | Pullbacks | Calls it "slingshot" — it's a pullback |
| **Patent Profit** | Earnings breakouts (PEG) | Same as Pradeep's EP, but enters late — after the price gap, not at the OPG |
| **Various day traders** (Madas, etc.) | News plays, stocks in play, fading | All chasing the same stocks each morning |
| **Almost every swing trader on YouTube** | Breakouts or pullbacks on growth stocks | Different names, same core |

**"A rose by any other name is a rose."** They all wear their trousers one leg at a time.

---

### 3. Don't Pollute Your Mind With Other Time Frames

Pradeep is explicit: **if you're a day trader, why pollute your mind with swing trading concepts like "situational awareness" and "momentum burst"?** If you're a position trader, why study day trading setups?

Next week he'll dive deep into each category separately — day trading, swing trading, position trading. He doesn't want position traders sitting through day trading sessions or vice versa.

---

### 4. Dividend Aristocrats — A Quick Primer

Pradeep briefly covers dividend stocks for position traders who want income:
- **Dividend aristocrats** = stocks that have paid increasing dividends for 20–25+ years
- **Examples:** Unilever (dividend ~$2.02/share, 100+ year old company), Phillips 66, Snap-on, Illinois Tool Works, General Dynamics
- **Not recommended:** AT&T (dying business), stocks that just started paying dividends
- **Key metric:** Look at dividend yield as a percentage, not raw dollar amount (BlackRock pays $4/share but the stock is $867 — relatively low yield)
- **Use case:** Building a portfolio that generates $3,000–$5,000/month in income
- **Pradeep's take:** He researched this years ago when someone paid him to develop a dividend strategy. It's a valid rabbit hole for position traders, but he personally wouldn't chase it — growth stocks are the "easy, low-hanging fruit" for position trading.

---

### 5. The Core Decision Tree for Swing Traders

Within swing trading, there are only 3 generic setups:
1. **Breakouts** (buying new highs)
2. **Anticipation** (buying in consolidation before the breakout)
3. **Pullbacks / Mean Reversion** (buying after a pullback in an established uptrend)

**The decision framework:**
- **Can you buy breakouts?** If yes → master breakouts first. Everything else flows from there.
- **Can't stomach buying stocks up 4%+?** → master pullbacks.
- **Need a watchlist, can't react to scans?** → master anticipation.

**One thing at a time.** Make ONE setup work. Then add others. If you can make breakouts work, pullbacks and anticipation will naturally make sense later. They're all connected.

**The weight-loss analogy:** There are only 3 ways to lose weight — eat less, exercise more, or do both. Everything else (supplements, vinegar drinks, liposuction) is exotic nonsense. Trading setups are the same — only 3 per category. Master the basics.

---

### 6. Personality Determines Your Setup — Not the Other Way Around

Some people will **never** be able to buy breakouts. They see a stock up $9 (like Roku) and think "it's too late." That's personality, not skill. For them, pullbacks or anticipation is the path.

Conversely, people who say "you should never buy a breakout, always use a watchlist" are really saying: **"My personality can't react to a scan and buy something I haven't researched."** That's fine — but don't universalize your personality constraint as a trading law.

Pradeep points to specific traders whose public advice reflects their personality, not universal truth:
- **LB** — advises "only use a watchlist, never chase" because that's what works for his personality (anticipation)
- **Mark Minervini** — advocates VCP (anticipation within a consolidation) because that's his comfort zone

---

### 7. Don't Chase Exotic Rabbits

Pradeep tried everything in his early years. The list of things that wasted his time and money:

- **Elliott Wave** — couldn't make it work
- **NeuroShell / NeuroCell** — neural network software from a full-page ad in Stocks & Commodities magazine. Cost $650 + data feed. His wife helped him try to figure it out. It just printed arrows on charts. **"None of that works."**
- **Ed Seykota** — wanted $25,000 for one-on-one mentorship. Pradeep contacted him but didn't pay. Ironically, Seykota was in the middle of his worst drawdown, a divorce, and IRS problems at the time. Pradeep ended up learning from Seykota's ex-girlfriend instead — she was also a trader and told him everything. The StockBee 25 and StockBee 50 concepts are similar to Seykota's approach.
- **Options trading academies, gold newsletters, value investing courses** — he subscribed to all of them

**Why exotic doesn't work:** Common techniques have abundant free information. Exotic techniques have one gatekeeper. If that source disappears, or was a fluke, or only works for that person's personality, you're stuck. **If you can't make the most commonly used technique work, you won't make an exotic one work either.**

---

### 8. The "Vacuum Out" Research Method

Pradeep's learning method for any subject:

1. **Pick ONE thing** (e.g., "swing trading breakouts")
2. **Spend 1 month vacuuming out the Internet** — YouTube, Twitter, blogs, forums, every source
3. **Go through the top 200 results** on your search term
4. **Hack through the material** — 90% will be junk, but you'll find the 10% that matters
5. **Build your method from that 10%**

He uses this for everything: trading, cooking, business ideas, software (learned Podia for the boot camp this way). It takes him ~15 days to become competent on a new subject.

**The Dan Zanger case study:** When Pradeep studied Zanger (who had made $1.6M with a 1,644% return):
- Collected every interview
- Watched every recording, no matter how bad the quality
- Signed up for his site
- Read every post and newsletter
- **Conclusion:** Dan buys breakouts of stocks with low float, or $40+ stocks making dollar breakouts. That's it. Nothing exotic.

**Podia case study:** He didn't know how to use Podia for the boot camp. Searched YouTube, watched videos, spent a weekend. Figured it out. "It's not rocket science."

---

### 9. Copy First, Improvise Later

The fastest path to profitability:

1. **Find someone who has mastered your exact time frame and style**
2. **Copy everything** — their software (TeleChart because Pradeep uses TeleChart), their scans, their process, their rules. Even their green T-shirt. (He's joking, but the point is serious: copy the entire system, not just the idea.)
3. **Make it work first**
4. **Then improvise and add your own nuances**

Pradeep is blunt: "I made a video on copy also." He studied successful traders obsessively — "I vacuumed out every single piece of information." His Dan Zanger deep-dive is a perfect example: he went as far as figuring out "what color is Dan Zanger's underwear" (joking) — that's how thorough the vacuum process is.

**His formula saves 5–10 years.** If you pick swing trading, 3–5 days, 8–20% — that's his expertise. You can ask him anything about that niche. But he says explicitly: "Can I tell you about position trading? No. That's not my expertise."

---

### 10. Commit and Stop Chasing — His $500K Education

Pradeep estimates he spent **~$500,000** on books, courses, seminars, software, and mentorship programs before he was confident in his method. He tried everything:

- NeuroShell neural network software ($650 + data feed)
- $1,000 courses from "some guy who sells total courses"
- Options trading academies
- Gold newsletter subscriptions
- Value investing courses
- Even contacted Ed Seykota for $25K mentorship

Even **after** he was profitable, he kept thinking "somebody has got something else" and kept spending money looking for the secret — **there is no secret.** Every successful trader is doing breakouts, pullbacks, or anticipation.

**The biggest time-waster he sees in his community:**
- Members try swing trading for 6 months → switch to day trading → someone tells them to put all money in gold stocks → 6 months later: "It's down 30%, I didn't tell my wife, what should I do?"
- Signing up for $500/month, $1,000/month guru services and jumping from guru to guru

**The anti-pattern:** Trying Elliott Wave, then "total trader" courses, then value investing, then gold newsletters. All before making even ONE setup work.

**The truth:** "Simple thing works. I bought Roku, and Roku is up already $2 from my entry. That's it."

---

### 11. The Formula in Full

Decide **time frame** → decide **specific setup within that time frame** → vacuum out all information on that ONE thing for 1 month → make it work → then add others. Don't change your hairstyle halfway through.

**The narrowing process:**
1. Day trading? Swing trading? Position trading? → Pick one road (Part 2)
2. Within that road, what are the 3 generic setups? → Pick one
3. Vacuum out all information on that one setup → Master it
4. Personality check: can you actually trade this setup? If not, pick the next one
5. Copy someone who already does it successfully → Make it work → Then improvise

**8:30 AM is not for asking "What is this scan?" Show up with the intention of making $10,000 for the day.**
