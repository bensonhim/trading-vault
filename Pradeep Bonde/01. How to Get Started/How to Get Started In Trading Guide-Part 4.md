### 1. Start Trading NOW — Stop Waiting for "Readiness"

The most common mistake Pradeep sees: people who say **"I'll learn everything for 6 months, then start trading."**

His response: "Why are you asking me now? Ask me after 6 months."

**The swimming pool analogy:** You can sit next to the pool for 6 months watching how to swim. You won't become a swimmer. You have to get in the water. Paper trading is like moving your arms on dry land and calling yourself a swimmer.

**"There is never going to be a perfect market."** People say "I'll start trading when the market is better." There is no better market. No perfect conditions. Nothing falls into place by just watching charts and videos.

**The path:** Select a setup → Start trading it → Perfect it through practice, practice, practice → Become good at it. But you must start with real money. Even 5 shares.

---

### 2. Shamelessly Copy — There Is No Prize for Originality

Pradeep's #2 advice for compressing profitability: **shamelessly copy what has worked for profitable traders.**

"Don't try to be original. Okay? Don't try to be original."

**Originality is for advertising, design, interior decorating.** In those fields, if you copy, your ads look like everyone else's. But trading is about making money. Who cares whether you copied someone's method and made money?

**The backward engineering analogy:**
- **China:** Tears apart iPhones. Makes copies at 1/10 the price. Now graduates to making products better than the originals (TikTok > Instagram).
- **Japan:** Backward engineered most US inventions. Now they innovate products of their own.
- **You:** Find a profitable trader. Tear apart their process. Study. Copy. Then simplify and make it your own.

**The Kim Kardashian analogy:** "If you want to be the next Kim Kardashian, you do shamelessly what Kim Kardashian is doing. All our sisters are doing exactly that. And that's why they're also becoming a billionaire." Get a big butt first. Copy same-as. Replicate everything.

**The simplification cycle:** Copy first. Make it work. Then after 6 months of success, simplify and innovate.

Pradeep's own path: He copied the method from *The Hedge Fund Edge* book. The trader had back-to-back 300–400% annual returns. He shamelessly copied everything — the scans, the process, the entries, the stops, the exits. **Then** over the next year, he simplified it from "running 5 different scans, ranking stocks, calculating scores" to "one scan, finding setups in the first 5 minutes." You simplify after you've made it work, not before.

### 3. Copy the Process and Scans — NOT the Stock Picks

**The #1 mistake people make when copying:** They hunt for **stock picks**, not **process**.

"What are people most hunting for? They're hunting for what stocks. That is going to get you not far."

**What you actually need to copy:**
- What does that person do in the morning?
- What are their scans?
- What software do they use?
- What is their process?
- How do they find the stock?
- How do they enter?
- How do they put stops?
- How do they exit?

**The "how to" part is everything.** If you know the scans and process, you can replicate results yourself.

**Guru sites don't share their real scans:** Pradeep warns that most paid services give stock picks, not processes. Even Mark Minervini: "does he tell you exactly how he scans for this? You ask his assistant Bob. Mark has three other scans which he has not shared with anybody." Dan Zanger's site: "does it tell you how you find this stock?"

**Pradeep's contrast:** "I don't think anybody should subscribe to my site for more than one year. If he's smart, he should be able to learn all these things in the first two months."

### 4. Paper Trading Is Useless — Trade Real Money, Even Tiny

"I don't see any reason for paper trading."

**Why paper trading fails:**
- No emotion involved
- No money on the line
- You can "swim" on paper by moving your arms — doesn't mean you can swim in water

**The alternative:** Trade with 5 shares, 10 shares. Real money. Real emotions. Start small. Every profitable trader Pradeep has taught will tell you the same: "Start with making it work for 10 shares, then increase to 100 shares, then 1,000, then 2,000."

**In order to copy, you need to understand things.** Blind copying doesn't work. But understanding comes from executing real trades, not from studying on paper.

---

### 5. But First — Decide Your Time Frame (Part 2 Connection)

Pradeep makes a critical connection back to Part 2: **you can't copy someone until you've decided your time frame.**

"In order to do this also, you first need to go through the process which I told you, which is to decide what is your time frame. Because if you don't decide the time frame, then you're going to try and copy so many people's different styles."

**You need ONE person and ONE style.** Not five different gurus with five different time frames.

### 6. The Corporate Analogy — How Pradeep Rose to Head of Marketing

Before trading, Pradeep was a corporate marketing executive. The story:

- He looked up and saw 17 layers of management above him
- After two double promotions, there were still 17 layers (corporations just create more layers)
- But at the top (level 2, below CEO), there were 8–10 people. Most were in their 50s and 57s with diabetes, heart surgery, pills.
- **One lady was 35 years old.**
- Pradeep had lunch with her and asked what made her successful.
- He shamelessly copied her technique.
- **Within 3 years, at age 32, he was head of marketing**, sitting next to the same people who discussed blood pressure before meetings.

He also appeared on the cover of business magazines in India and was voted among the top 3 most successful marketing managers that year. This isn't just a trading principle — it's a life principle.

### 7. The 6-Week Rule — Put Pressure on Yourself

**"Six weeks. You should aim to become profitable in six weeks."**

**Why 6 weeks?**
- If you have a ready-made template (someone's scans, process, entries, exits, stops)
- You're not developing from scratch
- Everything is already laid out
- You just need to execute

**The weight-loss analogy:** If you put pressure on yourself to lose 20 pounds by Christmas, you'll go to the gym twice a day. You'll do everything. If you don't put pressure, "nothing is going to happen."

**If your plan is 6 months or 1 year:** "I don't think you're going to make it." Without time pressure, you drift. With it, you compress years into weeks.

---

### 8. Start With ONE Setup — Momentum Burst

Pradeep's specific advice for swing traders: **start with momentum burst.**

- "Make momentum burst work. Don't worry about anticipation, reversal, this and that."
- "I myself make 90% of my money on just trading momentum burst."
- "Anticipation, all is gravy. That's an additional thing."

**The principle:** Make ONE thing work first. Only then add others. If you try to learn breakouts, anticipation, pullbacks, and EPs all at once, you'll master none.

---

### 9. The Market Doesn't Matter for Your Method

"Don't get too scared about market direction. You're going to waste a lot of time on that."

"The market doesn't really matter." For the kind of methods they trade (swing, short-term), market direction is less relevant. Position traders are already taking volatility risk, so market direction is even less important for them.

---

### 10. Social Media Is Your Research Lab — With Humility

Pradeep sees social media as a massive advantage for modern traders:
- You can find profitable traders
- You can talk to them
- You can see them in action (live trading, Twitter, YouTube)

**Approach them with humility and some "buttering up."** "There is no harm in buttering them up. Get what you want from them and use that."

This approach reduces "3 years, 5 years of struggle to just a couple of months of struggle."

---

### 11. Use the Same Tools as the Person You're Copying

Common mistake: trying to copy a trader's method but using different software.

People send Pradeep DMs asking: "Can you please tell me how to make this work in MetaStock?" His response: If you want to copy, **copy shamelessly. Use the same tools.** Use TeleChart because he uses TeleChart. Use the same scans.

Only after you've mastered the method should you innovate with your own tools.
