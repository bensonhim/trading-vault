# Episodic Pivots (EP) Guide

> **Speaker:** Pradeep Bonde (StockBee)  
> **Series:** Episodic Pivots Guide (6 parts)  
> **Transcripts:** [[../../transcripts/13. Episodic Pivots Guide/|Transcripts]]

## Summary

The Episodic Pivot (EP) is a **catalyst-driven, life-changing trade methodology** designed to find stocks that will make 100–800%+ moves within weeks or months — not small 20% swing trades. The core idea: find a stock that has been **neglected** by the market for months or years (low volume, no price movement, minimal fund ownership, no analyst coverage), then identify a **major catalyst** (earnings, sales, product launch, policy change) so significant that it completely changes the stock's trajectory. The setup is captured by the acronym **"Magna 53 + Cap 10×10"** — massive earnings/sales acceleration, gap up of 4%+ on 100K+ volume, neglect across price/volume/fund ownership/analyst coverage, sales acceleration (back-to-back quarters of 39%+), short interest ratio of 5+ days, and 3+ analysts raising price targets — all on a stock with **market cap below $10B** and **IPO'd within 10 years**. EP is an **information-intensive, research-driven** method requiring pre-market and after-hours diligence using tools like Briefing.com, The Fly, Earnings Whisper, Zacks, and MarketSmith. The goal is not a quick scalp — it is to identify 2–3 candidates per year, size up aggressively, and hold for multi-hundred-percent moves.

## 1. What Is an Episodic Pivot?

### 1.1 Core Definition

An Episodic Pivot is a stock that experiences a **life-changing catalyst** — an event so significant that it fundamentally alters the company's trajectory and causes the stock to reprice dramatically upward.

> "The idea behind episodic pivot is to look for a catalyst which is going to make a stock go up 50, 100, 200, 300, 500, 800%. You are not looking for episodic pivot for a small-time swing trade." — *Part 1*

**Examples of catalysts:**
- Earnings surprise (900%+ EPS growth)
- Sales acceleration (doubling revenue quarter-over-quarter)
- New product launch
- Government policy change
- CEO change
- Large order win
- Natural disaster/disease creating demand (COVID → work-from-home stocks)

### 1.2 Origin Story: The USLB Trade

Pradeep developed the EP method after reading a book about stocks with triple-digit earnings growth. The next morning, he found **USLB** (smart meter company) with **2,600% earnings growth**. The stock gapped up and went from $5 to $14 in 11 weeks. This single trade taught him that **neglected + catalyst = explosive move**.

> "I saw this particular stock called USLB at that time... the earnings was 900% or 1,000% or 2,000%, 2,600%... the stock gapped up, and from there the stock went on within eleven weeks from around $5 all the way to $14. That was the first idea behind episodic pivot." — *Part 1*

### 1.3 Why It Works

The market systematically **neglects** small, obscure companies. When these companies suddenly report transformative results:
- **No one is positioned** (no funds, no analysts, no retail interest)
- **The gap is massive** between current price and fair value
- **Funds rush in** after the catalyst, creating a sustained uptrend
- **Shorts get squeezed** if short interest is high

> "When I found GRWG, there are less than 60 funds owning GRWG. By the end of one quarter, 253 funds were holding GRWG. That is what created this massive move." — *Part 2*

## 2. The Selection Criteria: Magna 53 + Cap 10×10

### 2.1 NTRT / MTRT — The Discovery Timing

**NTRT = Night Time is the Right Time**
**MTRT = Morning Time is the Right Time**

Most companies announce earnings after hours (4 PM+) or pre-market (before 9:30 AM). You must scan during these windows to find candidates **before** the market opens.

> "99% of companies announce earnings either during the night or in the morning." — *Part 2*

### 2.2 Magna 53 — The Essential Criteria

| Letter | Stands For | Requirement | Essential? |
|--------|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **M** | Massive | Massive earnings acceleration (100%+, ideally 500%+) OR massive sales acceleration (doubling QoQ) OR massive earnings surprise (beats analyst estimate by huge margin) | **Yes** |
| **A** | Acceleration of sales | Back-to-back quarters of 39%+ sales growth; earnings alone can be manipulated (cost-cutting), but sales acceleration proves real demand | **Yes** |
| **G** | Gap up | Stock gaps up 4%+ in after-hours or pre-market on 100,000+ volume; proves the market recognizes the catalyst | **Yes** |
| **N** | Neglect | No rally into earnings (ideally flat/down for months/years), low daily volume, low fund ownership (<100 funds), no analyst coverage, no news flow | **Yes** |
| **5** | 5-day short interest | Short interest ratio of 5+ days; shorts forced to cover adds fuel to the rally | Optional (nice-to-have) |
| **3** | 3+ analysts raise target | 3+ analysts raise price target after earnings; signals Wall Street conviction | Optional (nice-to-have) |

> "Magna 53 essentially means that the stock had massive earnings acceleration... or massive sales acceleration... or a massive earning surprise." — *Part 2*

> "Neglect means the stock is not rallying big into earnings. The best stocks on episodic pivots are stocks which have no rally into earnings for one year, two years, three years." — *Part 2*

> "Sales acceleration is even more important than a company being profitable. Many newer companies may not be profitable. But if they have very high sales growth, that high sales growth is what is going to lead the stock to go up big." — *Part 2*

**The 39% sales rule:** If a company has sales growth of 39%+ back-to-back for two quarters, "that stock can double, triple, go up 500%, 600%, 800%."

### 2.3 Cap 10×10 — The Magnitude Multiplier

For **truly explosive** moves (300–800%+), the stock must also meet:

| Criterion | Requirement | Why It Matters |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Cap** | Market capitalization **below $10B** | $100M → $1B is easy; $10B → $50B is hard; $100B → $500B is nearly impossible |
| **10** | **Below $10B** cap | Even within the Cap rule, sub-$1B stocks make the biggest moves |
| **×10** | IPO'd **within 10 years** | Younger stocks have more runway; older stocks that never grew have structural problems |

> "If a company has a capitalization of $100B... it's very difficult for that stock to make 300, 400, 500% move. But if you have a stock which has a capitalization of $100M... it is likely to fly. It is likely to go up 300, 400%." — *Part 2*

> "The older the stock, if the stock has been around for twenty years and it has never grown, there is something drastically wrong with their business model. The big moves in the stock market happen in stocks which are IPO'd below ten years." — *Part 2*

### 2.4 Why Both Matter

| Magna 53 | Cap 10×10 | Expected Move |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| ✓ | ✗ | 50–100% move possible |
| ✓ | ✓ | 300–800%+ move likely |

> "If a stock had just Magna 53 criteria, it can make a 50% move, 100% move. But if in addition to Magna 53, it meets Cap 10×10, that is the stock which is likely to make 300%, 400, 500, 800% move." — *Part 2*

## 3. Information Sources and Workflow

| **Wall Street Journal** | Free | Post-market movers list |

> "If you have Interactive Broker, you can create a scan for post market or pre market. If you have Trade Ideas software, you can create this." — *Part 3*

### 3.2 Fundamental Data: Earnings & Sales

| Tool | Type | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| **Briefing.com** | Paid | Best for earnings color, analysis, and news filter; "Upgrades / Downgrades" tab shows analyst target raises |
| **The Fly** | Paid | Real-time earnings data, analyst price target changes, detailed figures; free tier gives headlines only |
| **Earnings Whisper** | Free (+ paid tier) | EPS/sales surprise, growth rates; **free email digest** every night and morning with all earnings summary |
| **Zacks** | Free | EPS surprise, sales surprise, chart overlays; some ads on free tier |
| **MarketBeat** | Free | Earnings calendar, analyst ratings |

> "The Fly also allows you to find how many analysts raise the price target... Earnings Whisper is free... if you just sign in and opt in, it is going to send you a summary of all the earnings which have been released after hours, every night." — *Part 3*

### 3.3 Deep Research: Fundamentals & Ownership

| Tool | Type | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| **MarketSmith ($150/mo)** | Paid | **Best** source for sales growth, EPS data, fund ownership history (8–9 quarters), short interest, float, IPO date |
| **Finviz** | Free | Float, short ratio, analyst coverage; press Ctrl+F & search "raised" to find analyst target raises |
| **Compustat (via Fidelity)** | Free (with Fidelity account) | Deep fundamental database — revenue, earnings, detailed financials |

> "MarketSmith is the most reliable and best source of information for earnings data... I use it because it pays off in one or two trades." — *Part 3*

> "On Finviz, press Ctrl+F and put 'raised.' It will show you all the stocks for which the analyst raised their price target." — *Part 3*

### 3.4 Daily Workflow

1. **Pre-market (~8 AM):** Check Earnings Whisper email for overnight earnings
2. **Pre/Post-market scan:** Run TC2000/IB scan for stocks up 4%+
3. **Filter with Magna 53:** Check The Fly/Zacks for EPS/sales surprise & growth
4. **Check Cap 10×10:** Confirm market cap <$10B and IPO <10 years (MarketSmith or monthly chart)
5. **Deep dive:** Briefing.com for earnings color; MarketSmith for fund ownership trend and short interest
6. **Price target estimate:** Based on catalyst magnitude (Part 4)
7. **Decision:** Size position proportional to conviction and target

## 4. Price Target Estimation: How Big Will It Move?

### 4.1 Why Target Estimation Is Critical

The entire EP methodology is built around **life-changing returns**, not 20% scalps. You must estimate the magnitude before entering so you know:
- How much capital to deploy
- How much volatility to tolerate
- When to exit (not at +20%, but at +200%)

> "If you predetermine that this stock is capable of making 300, 400%, then you are not going to take your profit at only 10%, 20%." — *Part 4*

> "The whole idea of trading episodic pivot is to change your life in one or two trades." — *Part 4*

### 4.2 Factors That Determine Move Magnitude

| Factor | Small Move (20–50%) | Big Move (100–300%) | Life-Changing (300–800%+) |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| **Earnings growth** | 100–300% | 500–1,000% | 1,000%+ |
| **Sales growth** | Moderate | 39%+ back-to-back | Explosive, consumer-facing |
| **Neglect** | Partial | Strong | Complete (zero volume pre-catalyst) |
| **Fund ownership** | >200 funds | 50–100 funds | <50 funds |
| **Market cap** | >$10B | $1B–$10B | <$1B (ideally <$250M) |
| **IPO age** | >10 years | 5–10 years | <5 years |
| **Short interest** | Low | Moderate | High (5+ days) |
| **Analyst upgrades** | 1–2 | 3–5 | 6+ |
| **Industry** | Industrial, slow-cycle | B2B tech, healthcare | **Consumer discretionary** (fastest) |

### 4.3 Sector Matters

**Consumer discretionary** stocks make the most explosive moves:

> "Technology stocks, healthcare stocks, and consumer discretionary stocks — these three categories are going to grow very rapidly, especially consumer discretionary... When Crocs came in, Crocs are not costing arm and legs. They're costing around $18–19. And there are 250M consumers in US who wants Crocs. So it can quickly grow very rapidly." — *Part 4*

> "Lululemon, Celsius Holdings — because the product is such that it can catch fire. It's a cold drink. It has caught fire, so it can grow earnings and sales very fast." — *Part 4*

### 4.4 Capitalization Math

- $100M → $1B = **10× move** (common)
- $1B → $10B = **10× move** (possible)
- $10B → $50B = **5× move** (rare)
- $100B → $500B = **5× move** (nearly impossible in one catalyst)

> "If the capitalization is small, then the stock is going to make bigger move. That's the most important thing." — *Part 4*

### 4.5 Facebook Example: Sizing for Life-Changing Returns

Pradeep bought **50,000 shares of Facebook** at ~$33 on earnings day, expecting it to reach at least $70. He used options for additional leverage. The stock doubled.

> "I bought 50,000 shares of Facebook on the day of the earnings, and that went and doubled the account... I knew that $70 at least the stock will go to based on the underlying figures. So I held it till 70." — *Part 4*

## 5. Live Example: JAKK (JAKKS Pacific) — A "Meh" EP

### 5.1 What It Had

- **Massive earnings surprise:** Expected loss of $0.73; reported profit of $1.98
- **Sales growth:** 96% revenue growth
- **Fund neglect:** Only 44 funds owned it
- **Gap up:** Strong pre-market gap
- **Positive:** Turnaround situation (company was losing money 2017–2020)

### 5.2 What It Lacked (Why It Wasn't Life-Changing)

| Missing Factor | Issue |
|---------------|-------|
| **Cap 10** | Market cap was $163M (good, but...) |
| **×10** | IPO'd in 1997 — **25+ years old**, not under 10 |
| **Neglect (price)** | Stock had rallied ~50% into earnings — not neglected enough |
| **Short interest** | Only 2.3 days — not 5+ |
| **Analyst upgrades** | Zero — no analysts followed it |
| **Pre-catalyst rally** | Already moved before the gap |

### 5.3 The Outcome

The stock gapped up **~50%** at the open. There was no additional room for a second leg. Expected target was 50%+; the gap consumed almost all of it.

> "It did not meet all Magna 53 criteria. It had massive acceleration, but it had neglect on funds, but it didn't have neglect in price because it was rallying to an extent of around 50% into the earnings... It gapped up almost 50%. And as a result, there's not much left in this particular stock for a further move." — *Part 5*

**Lesson:** Pre-catalyst rally destroys the setup. True EPs gap up **from a base of complete neglect**.

## 6. Live Example: ENPH (Enphase Energy) — A Mature EP

### 6.1 What It Had

- **Strong growth:** 154% earnings growth, 67% revenue growth
- **Back-to-back sales growth:** Yes
- **Neglect (price):** Stock had been going sideways for 6–7 months
- **Analyst upgrades:** 9 analysts raised price targets
- **Gap up:** Significant

### 6.2 What It Lacked (Why It Capped at 30–40%)

| Missing Factor | Issue |
|---------------|-------|
| **Neglect (funds)** | 1,759 funds owned it — **heavily owned**, not neglected |
| **Cap 10** | Market cap was **$29.3B** — way above $10B |
| **×10** | IPO'd 2012 — just barely under 10 years (aged out) |
| **EPS surprise** | Only 17% — not massive |
| **Revenue surprise** | Only 5.2% — not massive |
| **Well-known stock** | Already a major holding, already discovered |

### 6.3 The Outcome

Stock made **~30–40%** post-earnings and then stalled. Good for a trade, not life-changing.

> "This is not going to be life changing. I know immediately... Is it a good tradable stock? Yes. Is it a life changing EP? Answer is no." — *Part 6*

**Lesson:** When a stock is already owned by 1,700+ funds and has a $30B market cap, the **discovery gap** is gone. EP works best on **undiscovered** stocks.

## 7. Key Rules and Mindset

### 7.1 Not a Swing Trade Method

EP is explicitly **not** for 20% scalps. If you want small, frequent gains, use momentum burst or anticipation.

> "In order to find a stock which is going to make a 20% move, there are better methods. You're looking for a stock which is likely to go up 200, 300, 400%." — *Part 2*

### 7.2 Size Proportionally to Conviction

The more boxes a stock checks, the bigger the position:
- **JAKK** (partial fit) → small position, short-term trade
- **Facebook** (perfect fit) → 50,000 shares + options leverage, hold to target

### 7.3 Number of EPs Per Year

Pradeep aims for **2–3 truly life-changing EPs per year**. Not 50 trades. Not every week.

> "If you can find in a year two or three candidates like this, and if you can risk sufficiently, then your life is going to change." — *Part 2*

### 7.4 Catalyst Must Change the Trajectory

The catalyst must be so significant that it **redefines** the company's future:
- Turnaround from years of losses to profitability
- From zero sales to explosive demand
- From obscurity to market leadership

> "You're looking for a catalyst which is such a major catalyst that it is completely going to change the trajectory of that particular stock." — *Part 1*

## Key Takeaways

1. **EP is a catalyst-driven, life-changing trade method.** Target: 100–800%+ moves in weeks or months. Not 20% scalps.
2. **The core setup: Neglected stock + massive catalyst.** The stock must have been ignored by the market for months/years (low volume, no price movement, minimal fund ownership).
3. **Selection framework: "Magna 53 + Cap 10×10."** Massive earnings/sales acceleration, gap up 4%+ on 100K+ volume, neglect (price/volume/fund ownership/analyst), back-to-back 39%+ sales growth, short interest 5+ (optional), 3+ analyst upgrades (optional), cap <$10B, IPO <10 years.
4. **Sales acceleration > earnings manipulation.** Companies can fake earnings by cutting costs, but sales growth proves real demand. Back-to-back quarters of 39%+ sales growth is the strongest single predictor.
5. **Capitalization is the biggest magnitude driver.** Sub-$1B stocks can 10×. $30B stocks can barely double. Always check market cap before deciding conviction level.
6. **IPO age matters.** Stocks under 5 years old make the most explosive moves. Stocks that have been around 20+ years and never grew have structural problems.
7. **Pre-catalyst rally destroys the setup.** If the stock has already moved 50% into earnings, the EP is ruined. True EPs gap from a flat/sideways base.
8. **Research-intensive method.** Requires The Fly, Briefing.com, MarketSmith, Earnings Whisper, Zacks, Finviz. Not for passive traders.
9. **Price target estimation separates good from life-changing.** Before entering, estimate the magnitude: Is this a 20% trade, a 50% trade, or a 300% trade? Size and hold time depend entirely on this estimate.
10. **Consumer discretionary produces the most explosive moves.** Products that catch consumer imagination (Tesla, Crocs, Lululemon, Celsius) can grow sales fast and drive 10× stock moves.
11. **Only 2–3 life-changing EPs per year.** You don't need many. You need conviction and size. One perfect EP per year can double or triple an account.
12. **The JAKK lesson:** A stock can have great earnings and still fail as an EP if it rallied into the catalyst, is too old, lacks short interest, and has no analyst coverage.
13. **The ENPH lesson:** A mature, already-discovered, heavily-owned $30B stock with good earnings can make 30–40%. It cannot make 300–400%. The discovery gap is closed.
14. **Work the discovery window.** NTRT/MTRT means scanning pre-market and after-hours. The edge comes from identifying the candidate before the crowd.
15. **EP is a patience game.** Most nights there are no candidates. When a perfect EP appears, you must recognize it and act with size.
