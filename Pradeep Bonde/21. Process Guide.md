# Guide 21 - Process / Daily Routine

## Overview

This guide is the capstone of the Stockbee methodology. It argues that **setup is only 10% of the game; process is 90%**. Pradeep Bonde dedicates eight sessions to hammering a single thesis: once you know *what* to trade, your only job is to build, refine, and execute a repeatable process so ruthlessly that your brain stops thinking and starts acting via **procedural memory**.

The series weaves together three threads:
1. **Mental**: accepting that trading requires a brutal, med-school-level effort and the willingness to destroy old mental models.
2. **Organizational**: every tool, scan, entry type, stop rule, and exit rule must be decided *before* the market opens.
3. **Procedural**: the day is sliced into time blocks, each assigned a specific process loop, so that by 10:30 AM the bulk of the day's money-making work is already done.

## Key Principles

### 1. Brutal Effort

> "If you want to make it, you will have to be putting in effort which you have never done before in your life. Because you're just not really or never in your life you've done a kind of activity which needs you to do finances."

Pradeep compares trading to med school and top-tier MBA programs. The learning curve is non-negotiable. In the beginning you will chase rabbit holes, ask questions that have been answered a hundred times, and hold onto broken mental models (e.g., "just buy low, sell high" or trend-line crossing strategies). The only way through is **mastery experience**: doing 20, 50, or 300 deep dives on a single setup until your confidence becomes self-efficacy.

**Kelvin Wang's example**: He attended six bootcamps, documented everything, researched big moves, created his own EP criteria, and in 2009 put $1M into the right EP because he had *researched conviction*. The result: $1M became $70M. He then left the site because he was tired of answering the same questions from people unwilling to do the work.

**Self-efficacy is built five ways** (Bandura):
- **Mastery experience**: doing the deep dives yourself.
- **Social persuasion**: seeing others succeed with the same setup.
- **Vicarious experience**: watching successful traders execute.
- **Psychosomatic factors**: being rested and focused.
- **Visualization**: process-driven, positive scripts (e.g., "Always be in control," "Controlled risk taking," "Find and enter early").

### 2. Organize Like Crazy (OLC)

> "This is as serious a business as being a surgeon, and you need to take it that seriously."

Once you pick a setup (e.g., SIPs, EP9M, momentum burst), you have **48 hours** to lock your tools, scans, broker, and workspace into place. Changing tools every month is a form of procrastination.

**Pradeep's fixed toolkit**:
- **Scanning**: TC2000 + Trade Ideas
- **News/Research**: Briefing.com, The Fly, MarketSmith, Seeking Alpha
- **Broker**: Interactive Brokers (primary), Fidelity (retirement/long-term)
- **Entry types**: OPG, market order, limit order — nothing exotic
- **Sizing**: Fixed share counts (multiples of 1,000 or 5,000) for speed; mental math tells you the risk instantly
- **Stops**: 2.5% or less on early entries; half-of-day or low-of-day otherwise
- **Exits**: 80/20 sell into strength; 3-5 day hold for momentum burst

**Organize before the bell**:
- Pre-commit to SIPs or EP candidates the night before.
- Rank ideas in your broker platform so the #1 idea is at the top, #2 next, etc.
- Pre-stage limit orders at multiple prices.
- If you are asking "Where do I find this?" at 9:30 AM, you are not ready to trade that setup.

### 3. Develop a Process Edge

> "Process orientation decreases cognitive load. Because you're not taking the decision now. You are following a process. So there is no cognitive load."

Two traders with the exact same setup can produce 5-10x different results because of **process leakage**. Process edge comes from:
- **Timing**: Running reversal bullish scans at 3 PM, not at 6 AM. Running EP9M at 9:30 AM, not pre-market.
- **Quality control checklist**: Is it a cat, dog, or liquid lava? Is it continuation, consolidation, or start-of-a-swing?
- **Entry detail**: OPG by 9:28 AM, or limit order at 9:35 AM after a 2-minute shakeout?
- **Stop detail**: Low of the day for early entries; half of the day for later entries.
- **Exit detail**: Sell 80% into strength, trail 20%. No exceptions.

This is not discipline; this is **process orientation**. Discipline is nebulous. A process is concrete and fixable.

### 4. Daily Routine

Pradeep's day is a **process factory**:

**Pre-market (~8:00 - 9:25 AM)**
1. Situational Awareness: answer the only question that matters — "Are breakouts likely to work?" 
   - Inputs: your own recent trade success rate, 20% study, Market Monitor.
   - If answer is *yes* → go with size.
   - If *maybe* → selective size only on clear catalyst.
   - If *no* → don't trade; check if breakdowns are likely instead.
2. NTRT / MTRT scan → EP or SIP candidates.
3. DEP and Sugar Baby watch list creation.
4. If EP/SIPs found: stage OPG orders before 9:28 AM.

**9:30 - 10:30 AM: The Execution Window**
- Laser focus. Most breakouts happen in the first 15-20 minutes.
- Toggle between **Combination Bullish** and **EP9M** scans, sorted by volume.
- If no EP/SIPs: hunt momentum bursts or delayed reaction EPs.
- Enter → immediately place stop.
- Priority selection if multiple setups: (1) highest volume, (2) lowest capitalization.

**10:30 AM - 2:00 PM: The Dead Zone**
- Pradeep does not sit in front of the screen.
- He goes to the gym, does cardio, or handles non-trading life.
- **Process fix**: shifting non-trading tasks into the dead zone prevents overtrading.

**2:00 - 3:00 PM: Afternoon Scan Block**
- 2:00 PM alarm: check Sugar Babies and 2,500 fund-ownership list.
- Begin reviewing reversal bullish and anticipation setups.

**3:00 - 4:00 PM: Second Execution Window**
- Entries for reversal bullish, anticipation, and short setups.
- Typically enter between 3:50 PM and 3:58 PM.
- End of day: close positions if needed, move stops to breakeven+.

**Post-market**
- NTRT / MTRT again.
- Update delayed reaction EP watch list.
- Do deep dive / R&D work.

## Why Process Is an Edge

### Procedural Memory

> "Once you learn a process, the brain puts that process into procedural memory. Procedural memory is stored as a total sequence, not one sequence after another... You see a setup. The moment you see a setup, it triggers execution."

Conscious decision-making burns glucose and breeds hesitation (cognitive dissonance). Procedural memory makes a five-course French meal feel as automatic as tying your shoes. This is why Formula 1 pit crews change tires in under three seconds: roles, sequence, and zero thinking.

### Cognitive Load Reduction

When you are process-oriented:
- You don't wonder where to put a stop. The stop was decided months ago.
- You don't ask if you should take profit. The 80/20 rule decides for you.
- You don't FOMO-chase at noon because your process tells you the dead zone is for cardio.

### Time & Motion Thinking

Pradeep borrows from his DHL process-engineering days. The key insight from logistics: **batch processing** is fragile. If you wait for all information before acting, you either get 100% on the plane or 0%. The solution is **sweep vehicles** — partial, staged actions. This maps directly to the 80/20 exit: sell 80% into strength, let 20% trail. You guarantee capture of profit rather than hoping for a perfect exit.

## Workflows and Checklists

### EP9M Process Loop

```
09:30 AM → Run EP9M scan + Combo Bullish (sorted by volume)
    ↓
Apply SOS filter (Start-of-a-Swing)
    ↓
Classify for SIZING ONLY:
   ├─ Cats (clear catalyst: earnings, sales, real contract)
   ├─ Dogs (story/speculative: biotech pivot to AI, etc.)
   └─ Liquid Lava (theme stocks: semis, AI chips)
    ↓
Determine position size based on conviction + stop width
    ↓
Buy → Stop at low of day (early) or half of day (later)
    ↓
Hold 5-15 days
    ↓
Exit: Sell into strength (80/20) or move stop to breakeven+ after gap
```

### Situational Awareness Checklist (Morning)

- [ ] 20% study: what % of stocks made 20%+ move yesterday?
- [ ] Market Monitor: breadth, stage?
- [ ] Own recent trades: last 3-4 breakouts worked or failed?
- [ ] Answer: "Are breakouts likely to work?" → Yes / Maybe / No
- [ ] If Yes → full size
- [ ] If Maybe → selective, catalyst-only, smaller size
- [ ] If No → stand aside; check if shorts working

### Pre-Trade Organization Checklist

- [ ] Software decided and paid for (TC2000 / Trade Ideas / IB)
- [ ] Scans written and tested (Combination Bullish, EP9M, Reversal Bullish, etc.)
- [ ] News sources bookmarked (Briefing.com, The Fly, MarketSmith)
- [ ] Broker layout: priority list pre-ranked, orders pre-staged
- [ ] Entry method chosen: OPG / limit / market
- [ ] Stop rule: 2.5% / low of day / half of day (decided before open)
- [ ] Exit rule: 80/20 or full sell on day 3-5
- [ ] Position size: fixed share count (1K / 5K / 10K multiples)

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Happens | Process Fix |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Asking "Where do I find...?" at 9:30 AM | Not organized | Spend one Saturday setting everything up |
| No stop in place after entry | No process for stops | Pre-decide stop rule; attach it the moment you buy |
| Taking profits too late / not at all | No exit process | 80/20 is the process. Sell 80% into strength |
| Trading the dead zone (12-2 PM) | Sitting in front of screen with nothing to do | Shift gym/cardio/sauna into 12-2 PM |
| Chasing 40%+ gap-ups | No classification filter | EP9M + SOS first; if already up 70%+ in a day, catalyst is used up |
| Too many setups / "diversifying" | Lack of focus | Pick 1-2 setups. Obsess over their process. Focus list < 5 |
| FOMO on random breakouts outside watch list | Watching scans instead of a curated list | Operate from watch list; accept missing random movers |
| Over-intellectualizing formulas | Mental model mismatch with start-of-swing | Do 100 iterations of deep dives until concept clicks |
| Changing tools every month | Tool-chasing as procrastination | Pick one. Learn it inside out. Never change for 6 months |
| Not believing in the setup | Belief gap | Do it for 6 weeks. Conviction is a muscle built by doing |

## Bottom Line

**Setup gives you the target. Process pulls the trigger.**

Pradeep's entire trading life is governed by a small number of tightly defined process loops executed at specific times of day. The pre-market is for preparation, the first 30 minutes are for execution, the middle of the day is for *living*, and the last hour is for end-of-day entries. Every question — where to enter, where to stop, how much to buy, when to sell — has been answered in advance so that the brain is free to recognize patterns, not make decisions.

If you are struggling, the answer is almost never "I need a better setup." It is: **you need a written-down, tested, and followed process.** Fix the process, and profit becomes the by-product.
