## What is DEP?

**Delayed Reaction EP** is a method for entering a stock *after* its catalyst day — not on the day of the news — to achieve a dramatically closer stop and therefore much larger position sizing.

> *"DEP is an entry plus sizing tactic. The whole objective is to enter with a 0.5%–2.5% stop so you can put a boatload of money into the idea."*

Instead of buying on the catalyst day with an 18–20% stop and 10% position size, DEP lets you buy days later with a 2.5% stop and 50–100% position size on the same idea.

---

## Why Delay?

Three reasons to delay entry:

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Better Risk/Reward** | Closer stop = smaller risk per share = larger position size |
| **More Time to Analyze** | Not everyone can judge a catalyst in real-time at 9:30 AM |
| **Working People Schedule** | Ideal for those who can only trade 9:30–11:00 or do research at night |

> *"There are only three ways to make money in the market: be first, be smart, or cheat. So if you're going to wait for confirmation, good luck. Make sure you have a good job or a rich wife."*

## The Two Post-Catalyst Behaviors

After a catalyst, a stock does one of two things. DEP only works on the second type.

### 1. Gap and Go (NOT a DEP Candidate)
- **Profile:** Small cap (<$10B market cap), huge neglect, massive surprise, hot theme
- **Behavior:** Gaps up and never looks back; straight-line move
- **Why no DEP:** No pullback, no consolidation, no low-risk entry point
- **Examples:** Rocket Lab (+76% straight up), small-cap biotechs with FDA news

### 2. Gap, Pullback, Shakeout, Go (DEP Candidate)
- **Profile:** Institutional quality (1000+ funds), well-covered stock, less "surprise" because the market already knows the company
- **Behavior:** Gaps up on catalyst, then pulls back, consolidates, or shakes out weak holders for 3–10 days before the real move begins
- **Why DEP works:** The pullback creates a tight, low-risk entry with a stop just below the consolidation
- **Examples:** TXN (+19% gap, 4-day pullback, then continued), GLW, HIMS, eBay

> *"Gap and go vs. gap, then pull back, shake out, and go."*

## What Counts as a "Delayed Reaction"?

### The Critical Rule: FIRST Valid Setup Only

DEP is **not** any entry after a catalyst. It is the **first** valid low-risk setup after the catalyst move.

| Invalid DEP | Valid DEP |
|-------------|-----------|
| Second or third leg continuation | First pullback or first tight consolidation |
| Stock already up 50%+ from catalyst day | Stock pulled back or consolidated near catalyst-day levels |
| High-tide flag 20 days later | First SOS or anticipation setup within 3–10 days |
| Buying a breakout after a 40% move | Buying the first tight action after a 20% move |

> *"If the stock already made a 50% move from the catalyst, buying a continuation setup is NOT DEP."*

### Timing Window
- **Long side:** Best within 3–10 days of catalyst. If it doesn't set up in 10 days, probability drops.
- **Short side:** Can take 8–20 days or even a month for the bounce to fail.

## The DEP Scan

### Primary Scan (TC2000 / PCF)
```
4% breakout or breakdown
+ 9,000,000+ volume
+ Within last 25 days
+ Price $3+
```

**Variations:**
- Sort by **max volume 25** (highest volume day in last 25 bars) to surface the most significant participation
- Add `V > V1` (volume > previous day) to reduce ~10 stocks and improve quality
- For busy traders: add 20M+ volume filter to reduce to ~85 stocks
- For long side only: reduce to last 5–10 days (genuine DEPs usually break out quickly)
- Institutional filter: run on 1000+ funds list (~181 stocks) for simpler, higher-quality DEPs

**Current output:** ~470–553 stocks depending on market conditions.

## Catalyst: The Non-Negotiable Requirement

DEP is **not** a pattern trade. Without a genuine catalyst, DEP does not exist.

### What Counts as a Catalyst?

| Catalyst Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| **Earnings surprise** | Revenue/earnings beat, raised guidance |
| **Sales acceleration** | 50–300% revenue growth |
| **New product/theme** | AI, robotics, EV, new drug |
| **Government policy** | Regulation change, subsidy, tariff |
| **Large order/contract** | $10B+ government contract |
| **Turnaround** | New CEO, restructuring, spin-off |
| **M&A / strategic investment** | *(Note: skip these — nothing happens after the news)* |
| **Story / secular trend** | Multi-year industry shift |

### How to Research Catalysts

1. **Briefing.com** — primary source for earnings summaries
2. **MarketSmith** — earnings/sales data, EPS rating
3. **AI tools** (Gemini/Grok) — spell out ticker names carefully, ask for "why did this stock move"
4. **Stock timeline / Twitter** — member discussions
5. **Bucket system** — bucket zero (best earnings/sales) > bucket one > bucket two

> *"Profitability doesn't matter. The market gives a shit about profitability. Sales growth is what the market reacts to."*

### What to Skip

| Skip | Reason |
|------|--------|
| **Biotechs** | One-night stands. After 1–2 days, nothing happens. Exception: fund-quality biotechs (Eli Lilly, BIIB) |
| **Mergers/Acquisitions/Strategic Investments** | Nothing happens after the news. No follow-through |
| **CEO resignations** | Doesn't immediately change the business — not a valid catalyst for DEP |
| **No identifiable catalyst** | If you can't explain why the stock moved, skip it |
| **Already extended 50%+** | Second/third leg, not first setup |

### DEP on the Short Side: "Broken Business" Rule

For DEP shorts, Pradeep only shorts stocks with a **broken business** — confirmed by X-ray showing declining sales/losses. A negative earnings catalyst alone isn't enough; the underlying business must be deteriorating.

- **CarMax**: Negative growth + missed earnings = broken business → DEP short, -33%
- **Dow**: Continuous losses, declining business → shorted twice as DEP
- **Target**: Clear fundamental deterioration → high-conviction short

> *Shorts must have "urgency to sell" — if there's no urgency, the bounce can last too long and stop you out.*

**Short side timing:** Shorts take longer to set up than longs. DEP shorts may require 8–20 days (vs 3–10 days for longs). But the best shorts also happen fast when there's urgency to sell.

> *"Biotechs are like certified male drones... one night stand. There is no relationship after one night stand."*

## The DEP Process

### Weekend Process (Primary)

1. **Run DEP scan** (5 min)
2. **Sort by max V25**
3. **Go through list top-down**, research catalyst for each high-volume stock
4. **Prioritize:** Bucket zero/one first → institutional quality (2500+ funds) → everything else
5. **Mark projected earnings/sales** in notes
6. **Remove stocks with no real catalyst** or where the move already happened
7. **End with 1–2 high-conviction ideas** for the week

### Daily Process (Alternative)

1. Create watchlist (evening or morning)
2. Boil down to 5–6 ranked candidates, then to 2–3 priorities
3. Transfer to broker; keep orders ready
4. In the morning, watch only those stocks
5. Enter immediately if they open positive
6. Run this loop every day; weekend work often converts into 2–4 trades the next week

### Entry Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **When to enter** | First 10–15 minutes if stock opens positive |
| **How to enter** | Buy as soon as stock shows life (up $0.10–$0.30) |
| **Entry structures** | Anticipation OR Start of a Swing (SOS) — both are valid DEP entries |
| **Volatility contraction** | 3 tight days, 2 tight days, or orderly pullback = ideal setup pattern |
| **What NOT to do** | Wait for confirmation, ORB, previous-day high, or 4% breakout |
| **Stop** | 0.5%–2.5% maximum |
| **Position size** | Large — 3–4x what you'd use on catalyst day |
| **Retries** | Up to 3 attempts on a genuine catalyst before abandoning |

> *"If it goes up $0.30, you should be in. Don't wait for confirmation."*

### Stop Management

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| **Entry day** | Stop at low of day or breakeven plus |
| **Stock moves in your favor** | Move stop to breakeven quickly |
| **Stock makes 40–50%** | Tighten stop aggressively |
| **Stock makes 100%** | Sell 80%, keep 20% with trailing stop |
| **Gapped up too much premarket** | Skip it — widened stop kills DEP edge |

## DEP vs. Other Setups

| Aspect | EP 9M (Day 1) | DEP (Delayed) |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| Stop size | 8–20% | 0.5–2.5% |
| Position size | Small (10–20%) | Large (50–100%) |
| Time to decide | Seconds/minutes | Hours/days |
| Research window | Pre-market only | Evening, weekend |
| Conviction required | Very high | High |
| Best for | Fast, experienced traders | Working people, analytical traders |
| FOMO level | High | Low |

## DEP and Sugar Babies

DEP and Sugar Babies are natural companions:

- **Sugar Babies** = stocks capable of 30–40% explosive moves in 2–3 days
- **DEP** = the entry method to get into those Sugar Babies with a close stop

**Combined workflow:**
1. Run Sugar Baby selection (count 9M breakouts over 504/252/126/63/21/10/5 days)
2. Run DEP scan
3. Create combined watchlist
4. Narrow to **max 5 stocks total** for the morning
5. Prioritize 1–2 must-buys before the open

> *"If everybody understood and everybody had sugar babies, the world will come to an end."*

## Developing DEP Expertise ("Deep Work")

### The #1 Book for DEP Mastery

**Deep Work** by Cal Newport — the methodology for mastering any complex skill through intense, focused study.

### The 5,000-Example Study Plan

To master DEP (or any setup), study **5,000+ examples** over 3+ years in a compressed timeframe:

1. **Lock yourself away for 48 hours**
2. Go through every EP 9M in last 1,200+ days (~1,800 examples)
3. For each stock, document:
   - Pre-move price
   - Catalyst type
   - Sector
   - Capitalization
   - Volume on trigger day
   - Number of legs
   - How many days the move lasted
   - Where stops should go
4. **Do 3+ passes** — question quality improves each round
5. **Daily maintenance:** study 20%+ moves, 50%+ moves, high-price movers every day

> *"If you do this, your probability of becoming a millionaire will go up by 90%. Because 90–95% of people attempting to make money are never going to do this."*

### Mental Database Effect

After studying thousands of patterns, your brain permanently alters. You begin to "unsee" false setups and instantly recognize valid ones.

> *"How do you build confidence? You build confidence through expertise. The expertise builds comfort confidence."*

## DEP for Working People

DEP is specifically designed for traders who cannot watch the market all day:

- Research at night or weekend
- Create a 2–3 name watchlist
- Enter in first 10–15 minutes if stock opens positive
- Stop is tight (2.5%), so no need to watch all day
- Size is large, so fewer trades needed per year

**Real example:** Harish and his wife, a couple with two small kids, can only trade 9:30–11:00. They do DEP research at night, enter in the morning, and doubled a large account two years in a row using only institutional-quality DEPs.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong |
|---------|----------------|
| **Calling any late entry "DEP"** | DEP is first valid setup only, not any entry after the catalyst |
| **Trading DEP without a catalyst** | DEP is not a pattern; catalyst is mandatory |
| **Waiting for confirmation/breakout** | Defeats the purpose; by then stop is 6–8% |
| **Using ORB on DEP stocks** | First candle is already 6–8%; you're buying after the move |
| **Chasing biotechs** | One-night stands; no follow-through |
| **Trading M&A/news events** | Nothing happens after day 1 |
| **Too many stocks on watchlist** | Max 5 (DEP + Sugar Babies combined); 2–3 is ideal |
| **6%+ stop on DEP** | "Why did you waste your weekend?" |
| **Not moving stop to breakeven** | Let winners run, but protect capital first |
