# Guide 19 - Deep Dive / X-Ray

## Overview

This guide teaches you two inseparable skills that separate hobbyists from professionals: **reading the X‑ray** (the financial DNA of a stock) and **doing the deep dive** (the deliberate, high‑volume study that embeds setups into your subconscious). Pradeep Bonde argues that every chart is merely the *footprint* of fundamentals. If you cannot read an X‑ray, you are flying blind on catalyst quality. And if you have not done deep dives, you are trading second‑hand opinions instead of hard‑won expertise. The guide covers what to look for in growth stocks, turnarounds, and story stocks; how to run your own empirical studies; and why compressed, obsessive study changes your brain permanently.

## Core Concept

### What is a Deep Dive / X-Ray?

An **X‑ray** is the fundamental financial profile of a company — primarily sales growth, earnings growth, and forward estimates — displayed in MarketSmith, William O'Neil database, or similar tools. When Pradeep looks at an X‑ray, he is asking one question: *Is there a dramatic acceleration or turnaround that is strong enough to drive a multi‑day or multi‑week move?*

> *"One look at the X-ray, and it tells you that hey, this stock is likely to go up a lot… If somebody tells you Tesla is going to double and triple, you just look at the X-ray. And you know why it is down."*  
> — Part 1

A **deep dive** is the process of empirically studying a large sample of stocks that exhibited the exact behavior you want to trade (e.g., a 20% move in five days, a 50% move in a month, a 300% annual winner) and cataloguing their common traits. Pradeep printed 40,000 charts by hand when he started. Today he does a "mini deep dive" every single morning.

### Why Deep Dives matter

Books, videos, and AI research summaries are **second‑hand information**. Only by seeing thousands of examples with your own eyes does your brain develop what psychologists call **encapsulation** — the automatic pattern recognition that lets you spot a start of a swing or a reversal bullish without conscious thought.

> *"Burn all the books on trading and start with a deep dive. You are going to learn in a deep dive more than you'll learn by reading 50,000 books on trading."*  
> — Part 3

Deep dives also answer the granular questions no book can: Where do I put the stop? How long does the move last? Do I buy on day one or wait for a pullback? Should I only trade above the 10‑day MA? The answers come from *your* data, not from a forum post.

## Key Principles

### 1. The X‑ray Is the Truth Teller
Management press releases are biased. CEO's never say their quarter was mediocre. The X‑ray reveals the truth in seconds.

> *"Press releases are like what you say about your kids... My son, he's so this thing fucking son goes and smokes crack... They always say nice thing about themselves. Just look at the numbers. Look at the X-ray, and you'll know."*  
> — Part 1

When Pradeep sees a stock gap up, he does not read the headline first. He opens MarketSmith, glance at the sales and EPS growth rates, and within five seconds classifies the stock as **growth**, **turnaround**, or **story**. If the numbers are deaccelerating — e.g., Lululemon dropping from 29% sales growth to 16% to 10% — he knows the stock is going down regardless of how many yoga pants his wife buys.

### 2. Sales Growth Is the Primary Driver
Profitability is optional for technology stocks. Sales growth is mandatory.

> *"Market really doesn't care about profit, especially if it is a technology company... What is going to make a stock go up a thousand percent is sales acceleration and triple‑digit sales."*  
> — Part 5

Pradeep's hierarchy when reading an X‑ray:
1. **Sales growth** — is it accelerating? Is it triple‑digit?
2. **EPS growth** — confirming the sales story.
3. **Forward estimates** — anything below the line is the market's future expectation.
4. **Margin / short interest / fund ownership** — toppings, not the pizza.

If a retailer shows 300% sales growth but no profit, that means they are giving away free chicken tikka masala. For restaurants and cosmetics, profit matters. For software, semiconductors, and biotech, sales growth is what funds chase.

### 3. The Three Financial Signatures
Every stock that moves big falls into one of three X‑ray buckets:

| Type | X‑Ray Signature | Example |
|------|----------------|---------|
| **Growth stock** | Triple‑digit or sharply accelerating sales/EPS; forward estimates rising | **CRDO**: 275% EPS, 89% sales, projected 70%+. **SMCI**: 71% EPS, 103% sales. **HIMS**: 287% sales growth. |
| **Turnaround** | From deeply negative earnings/sales to sudden positive acceleration; projections spike | **ANF**: from $0.25 EPS to $6.28; 2,443% earnings growth projected. **Love**: from -30% EPS to +340% and sales acceleration to 7%, 12%, 14%. |
| **Story stock** | "Not applicable" sales; zero revenue; losses forever; a plausible futuristic narrative | **BNAI**: 2,857% move, no sales. **Oklo**: 762% move, PhD students with a mini nuclear reactor idea. **QuantumScape**: 900% move, no applicable revenue. |

### 4. Start with a Blank Chart, Then Build Rules
For a first deep dive, Pradeep insists on **naked charts** — no indicators, no moving averages, no scans overlaid.

> *"Do it with a blank mind. Don't presume anything... The moment you do it without any indicator, it will show you where the swing started."*  
> — Part 4

He captures each chart (using Snagit or the Snipping Tool), marks the suspected start of the swing with an arrow, and asks: *Which scan would have found this on that exact day?* Was it a **4% breakout**? A **9M EP**? A **dollar breakout**? A **combination scan**? After marking 40–50 charts, the brain stops guessing and starts knowing.

### 5. Momentum Bursts Are 3–5 Day Phenomena
Pradeep's most significant discovery from years of manual database building:

> *"If a stock makes a 25% move in a month, it didn't do it linearly... it happened in three to four days."*  
> — Part 7

This means the *momentum burst* is the core tradable event. Swings that deliver 20% do not dribble out 1% per day; they explode and are largely done within three to five days. That empirical fact answers hold‑time questions automatically: if you are still holding on day six, you are no longer trading the burst — you are hoping.

### 6. Encapsulation Requires Compressed, High‑Volume Work
Deep dives should be done in **48 hours**, not 48 days.

> *"When you do a large volume of work in a compressed time frame, your brain becomes super efficient... If your life depended on doing something in the next forty‑eight hours, you will put all your effort."*  
> — Part 3

Pradeep compares this to Navy SEAL Hell Week. Intense pressure forces the brain to prioritize and internalize. Spreading the same work over six months creates shallow, forgettable study. A single weekend of 4,000 charts changes you permanently.

### 7. Story Stocks Are the Biggest Post‑COVID Opportunity
Since COVID, the market's biggest winners are almost exclusively **story stocks** with no earnings and no sales.

> *"After COVID, if you want to find the biggest winner in the market for a given period of time, it is going to be stories... Story stocks is where big money is."*  
> — Part 6

You do not need MarketSmith to trade them. You need a **9M EP scan** and the ability to judge whether the narrative is hot (AI, quantum computing, nuclear, rare earth, vertical takeoff aircraft, robotics). Buy the 9M breakout, keep moving your stop, and hold until a 9M breakdown. Many of these moves are 200%–2,000%.

### 8. Reflection Is What Turns Data into Edge
Anybody can look at 100 charts. Few reflect on *why* the pattern exists.

> *"Reflection builds insight and knowledge... The person who reflects is the person who discovers that doctors need to wash their hands."*  
> — Part 4

After each deep dive session, Pradeep writes down: What did I notice that I missed the first time? What was the common denominator? Reflection converts raw observation into **age** (edge), and edge translates directly into profit.

## Deep Dive Process

**Step 1 — Define your objective.**  
Are you a swing trader (20% moves), a day trader (liquid $10+ range movers), or a home‑run hunter (300%+ annual winners)? Pick **one** track. Do not mix them on the first dive.

**Step 2 — Pull the universe.**  
In TC2000:
- **Swing:** run the built‑in *20% study* (stocks up 20%+ in last 5 days).
- **Home‑run:** add a YTD column, sort by 300%+ gainers.
- **Day trader:** write a custom scan:
  - `c by c 1 > 1.2` (20%+ move today) with `volume >= 1,000,000` and `c >= 10`, or
  - `abs(c - c1) >= 10` ($10+ daily range).

**Step 3 — Chart capture (naked first).**  
Go through each stock one by one. Hide all indicators. Mark the day the move started with an arrow. Ask:
- Was it a 4% breakout?
- Was it a 9M EP?
- Was there a volume surge?
- Did it close near the high of the day?
- What was the percentage gain on day 1? Days 2–5?

**Step 4 — Document in a Google Doc or folder.**  
Save the annotated image. Write 1–3 bullet observations per chart. Example: *"Start of swing = 4% breakout. Combo scan would have caught it. Below 50‑day MA. Neglected 3 months."*

**Step 5 — Run the same list a second and third time.**  
Take a break, then revisit. You will see things you missed: tightness before breakout, a prior failed breakout, sector context, float size.

**Step 6 — Reflect and write rules.**  
After 40–50 charts, synthesize:
- 90% of successful swings started with a 4% breakout.
- Most close near the high on day 1.
- Moves last 3–5 days.
- Below $10B cap and below $10 price appear frequently.
These become *your* trading rules, not Pradeep's.

**Step 7 — Add layers.**  
On the next iteration, overlay your scans (combo, 9M EP) to confirm which signal fired and which did not. Refine.

**For big‑move hunters:**  
Use Briefing.com or similar to find the exact catalyst (Phase 3 biotech, FDA approval, new contract). You will discover that most 300%+ winners are **neglected, under $10B, owned by <100 funds**, and often have **no earnings at all** (biotech / story).

## Deep Work Framework

Pradeep's study methodology is a direct transplant of how he became an expert in advertising before he ever traded:

| Layer | Frequency | Task |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Morning mini dive** | Daily (first 15 min) | Review *20% study* and *50% study* in TC2000. Count how many started with 4% breakout. Note any new pattern. |
| **Weekend deep dive** | Weekly | Re‑scan 20% and 50% movers for the week. Annotate 20–30 charts. Look for repeated behaviour. |
| **Quarterly thematic dive** | Quarterly | Pick one setup (e.g., reversal bullish, delayed reaction EP, story stocks) and study 500+ examples exclusively for 48 hours. |
| **Catalyst verification** | As needed | When a stock gaps up, open the X‑ray before reading the press release. Confirm acceleration. |

> *"The first deep dive might be just to establish baseline understanding of market. Second deep dive might be more specific... Do that 20 times."*  
> — Part 7

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Reading the press release before the X‑ray.** The press release is marketing. The X‑ray is medical imaging.
- **Chasing growth that is already priced in.** If the stock has already tripled and the forward estimates are now deaccelerating, the train has left the station.
- **Using AI to write your deep dive.** Pradeep calls AI‑generated research *"300 pages of unadulterated bullshit"* because the whole point is the *process* of looking, not the output.
- **Studying too many setups at once.** Do one setup until it is automatic.
- **Endless deep diving without trading.** Some members study for years and never implement. Deep dives exist to solve a *trading problem*, not to become a PhD thesis.
- **Starting with indicators.** Put a naked chart first. Indicators are a crutch that hides the pattern.
- **Focusing only on SIPs and missing the big picture.** SIPs are one‑day fun; they do not teach you to hold a 300% winner.
- **Ignoring capitalization and price.** 80%+ of 50%+ movers are below $10B and below $60. If you only scan large‑cap names, you miss the explosion.
- **Waiting for the second day on a story stock.** Story EPs with 9M volume often never give a second chance. The entry is day one.

## Bottom Line

If you learn only one thing from this guide, let it be this: **the numbers on the X‑ray precede the move on the chart.** Sales acceleration, dramatic turnarounds, and hot narratives are all visible before the breakout. Your job is to study so many examples that your brain flags them automatically. Do not outsource this to AI, to another member's deep dive, or to a $5,000 course. Print the charts, mark the swings, reflect on the commonalities, and compress the work into intense 48‑hour bursts. That empirical obsession — whether it is growth X‑rays, turnaround X‑rays, or story‑stock 9M EPs — is what builds the **age** that turns into profit. There is no shortcut, and there is no substitute.
