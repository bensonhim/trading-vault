# Bullish Momentum Burst Guide — Section 3

### Two Lynch Examples: What to Look For

This section is a **visual deep-dive** into high-quality Two Lynch setups from real charts (TC2000).

---

### Ideal Two Lynch Template

| Feature | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **First leg** | Strong, persistent buying; stock not up two days in a row prior |
| **Linearity** | Candle bodies progressively higher; no big selling candles |
| **Consolidation** | Compact, orderly, 3–5 days; low/no volume during pause |
| **No 4% breakdown** | No violent selling during consolidation |
| **Narrow day prior** | Day before breakout: narrow range or slightly negative |
| **Breakout day** | Clean range expansion; stock closes near the high |
| **Youngness** | First or second leg of move; NOT fourth/fifth leg |

> "So two Lynch: young, narrow or negative day prior to consolidation, consolidation quality good, closing near high. All boxes it ticks."

---

### Example Pattern Walkthrough

#### Example 1: Clean First-Leg Setup
- First leg: stock goes up 8–10%, closes strong
- Consolidation: 4 days of sideways, very low volume
- Narrow/negative day prior to breakout
- Breakout: clean 6%+ candle, closes near high
- Result: 20%+ move in 3–5 days

#### Example 2: Slightly Extended Consolidation
- Consolidation: 10–12 days (longer than ideal)
- BUT: first leg shows persistent buying
- During consolidation: no 4% breakdown, no high volume
- Breakout still works because underlying buying was strong

#### Example 3: Minor Pullback Variation
- After first leg, stock pulls back slightly
- During pullback: no volume increase (no selling)
- Then consolidates briefly and breaks out
- This pullback-flavor is more aggressive but valid

> "Sometimes the consolidation is just three days. Sometimes it can be longer, and still they meet your Two Lynch criteria."

---

### What to Avoid (Failed Patterns)

| Bad Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| **Choppy consolidation** | High volatility during pause = sellers active |
| **4% breakdown during consolidation** | Weakens the setup significantly |
| **Up two days in a row prior** | Stock already extended; limited fuel left |
| **Late leg (4th/5th)** | Public awareness has already caught up; profit-taking hits |
| **Stock closes far from high** | Momentum is already fading on breakout day |
| **Big volume on breakout but no follow-through** | Could be distribution/exit by insiders |

> "The more it becomes extended and more it becomes an apparent setup is when you will see that they tend to fail."

---

### Practical Application

1. **Build a mental image library** — collect 300–500+ historical examples
2. **Study variations** — market correction vs. bull market produce different-looking consolidations
3. **Look at sector context** — when a sector bottoms simultaneously, the breakouts from that group have higher probability
4. **Document nuances** — differences between tight pullbacks vs. flat bases vs. V-bottoms

---

### Key Principle

> "The more examples of Two Lynch you study, the better you become in this game."

Pattern recognition from studying historical examples is the single greatest skill in momentum burst trading. You must internalize what a high-probability setup LOOKS like — then trust your eyes in real time.
