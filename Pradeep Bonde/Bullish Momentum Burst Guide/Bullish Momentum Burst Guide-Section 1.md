# Bullish Momentum Burst Guide — Section 1

### The Central Tendency of Stock Moves

The momentum burst is the **dominant pattern** of stock movement — most significant moves do not unfold over months, but rather burst in **3–5 day windows**.

> "Momentum burst is the central tendency of stocks moves."

---

### The Core Pattern

| Attribute | Value |
|---|---|
| **Duration** | 3–5 days |
| **Typical move** | 8% to 40% |
| **Higher-priced stocks** | $50 to $300 in 3–5 days |
| **Examples** | 278% (Beyond Meat), 121%, 104%, 60% |

This holds true across any sector, any price level, any stock — even on the downside (negative momentum bursts).

---

### What Starts a Momentum Burst?

The single event that triggers the burst is **range expansion on Day 1**:

- Before the burst: stock is trading sideways, pulling back, or in a low-volatility range
- Day 1: a **large breakout candle** — 7%, 13%, or more — far exceeding prior days' range
- After Day 1: the 3–5 day momentum burst follows

> "What happens at the start of a momentum burst is that there is a range expansion or there is a burst of a momentum, or there is a big candle at the start of a move."

**Key: The first day's magnitude matters.**

Without that large Day 1 candle, there is no burst.

---

### Anticipation vs. Reactivity: The Critical Distinction

| | Momentum Burst Method | Anticipation Method |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Reactive — wait for range expansion to confirm | Predictive — enter before confirmation |
| **Effectiveness** | ~80% of bursts can't be anticipated | Works on ~20% of setups |
| **Best for** | Sudden, explosive moves | Steady/visible accumulation |

> "80% of the momentum burst will not be possible to anticipate."

The explosive ones — where a stock suddenly gains 40% in 3–5 days — cannot be foreseen. They start with a sudden range expansion that no indicator predicts.

---

### Key Principles

1. **Momentum burst is reactive, not anticipatory** — you trade it *after* Day 1 confirms the burst
2. **The first day's magnitude matters** — a small 2% candle won't seed a larger burst; it needs to be a true range expansion
3. **It happens everywhere** — in cheap stocks, expensive stocks (e.g., AutoZone $100+, Tesla), and in both directions (bullish and bearish)
4. **It repeats** — it is not a rare anomaly; once you know what to look for, you see it continuously

---

### The Method Implication

To profit from momentum bursts:

1. **Identify range expansion candles** (Day 1) — when a stock suddenly breaks out of a low-volatility range with a large candle
2. **Enter reactively** — after the first day's move confirms the burst
3. **Hold for 3–5 days** — the burst typically lasts this duration before exhaustion
4. **Don't try to anticipate every burst** — anticipate only setups where accumulation is visible; for most bursts, wait for confirmation

---

### Key Quote

> "If your idea is to just try and build all your trading around just anticipating momentum suddenly, momentum came in, and stock went up 40%... It's not possible."
