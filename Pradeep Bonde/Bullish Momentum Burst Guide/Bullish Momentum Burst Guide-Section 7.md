# Bullish Momentum Burst Guide — Section 7

### Dollar Breakout Examples and Risk Math

This section is about **seeing** dollar breakouts in action and understanding the **capital efficiency** they enable.

---

### Example 1: Booking.com

- Breakout: up $4.46, but only **2.05%**
- First leg orderly, tight consolidation
- Result: **$9 move** in 3 days
- 4% scan? **Not shown** — only shows up in dollar breakout

---

### Example 2: O'Reilly (ORLY)

- Very orderly pullback after first leg
- Breakout: up $2.60, 2.68%
- Result: **$25 move** in 3+ days

**Position sizing math:**
- Entry: ~$220.46
- Half-range stop: ~$213.12
- Risk: ~$7.34 per share (~3.3%)
- With 1% account risk: Can put **30% of account** into this single setup

> "That's what really happens with these kind of setups where you can put in a lot more."

---

### Example 3: Zebra Technologies (ZBRA)

- Breakout: up $1.60, but only **0.49%**
- First leg established, very orderly consolidation
- Result: **30%+ move** in 3–4 days

This is Pradeep's **template for ideal dollar breakout** — so low-profile it barely gaps, so early virtually nobody notices.

> "These are the kind of setups which can make your week or they can make your days."

---

### Capital Efficiency: The Dollar Breakout Advantage

| | 4% Breakout | Dollar Breakout |
|---|---|---|
| **Typical breakout** | 6–8% | 1–3% |
| **Half-range stop** | 3–4% | **1–1.5%** |
| **# of positions with 3% total risk** | 1 | 3–4 |
| **Account utilization** | 10–15% | 30–40%+ |

> "You can have four setups like this open, and your risk cumulatively on all four positions still can be less than 3%."

**Why it matters:**
- More capital working = faster account growth
- Tighter stops = less anxiety, more confidence
- Multiple positions = diversification within same method
- Still only risking 3–4% total account

---

### Longer Duration Advantage

| Method | Hold Period | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 4% Breakout | 3–5 days | Must hit target quickly |
| Dollar Breakout | **8–12+ days** | Slower, more deliberate; easier to manage |

> "Many times dollar breakouts will move for $8, 10, 12 days. They may not move very rapidly. They'll move very slowly, but they'll move for eight to ten days."

**Example: Auto Zone**
- 2% breakout
- Moves very slowly but **$120+ total move** over 7–8 days
- Not exciting enough for retail traders → less competition

---

### Timing: Still Needs Favorable Market Phase

Even dollar breakouts require the Market Monitor to be bullish:
- Money flowing INTO the market
- Primary indicator green
- Confirmed bullish phase

> "If you have to trade the dollar breakout, you still need to trade them during favorable time periods, which will be when the market monitor is in a confirmed bullish mode."

---

### What Scares People (That Shouldn't)

| Fear | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Higher price = more risk" | Actually **less** volatile; tighter stops possible |
| "$200 stock with 2% breakout" | That 2% = $4 move; stop at $2 = only 1% risk |
| "I need more shares to make profit" | Wrong — you need bigger *position size*, not more shares |

> "Anything which is higher priced scares people."

---

### Practical Workflow

1. **Morning**: Run both 4% AND dollar breakout scans
2. **Filter**: Eliminate 4% breakouts from dollar list to see pure dollar candidates
3. **Select**: Apply Two Lynch criteria to both
4. **Prioritize**: Choose dollar breakouts from lower-cap, biotech/technology/consumer discretionary
5. **Size**: Use half-range stop to maximize position size while keeping total risk at 3–4%
6. **Hold**: 8–12 days; don't rush exit at Day 4
