# Bullish Momentum Burst Guide — Section 2

### Universal Applicability

Momentum burst works in every liquid market worldwide. The only exceptions are markets with **price filters** (e.g., circuit breakers that halt trading after small moves), which are rare.

### Why Deep Dive?

> "Deep dive of at least 3,000 examples is the starting point for you getting a deeper understanding of momentum burst."

Before trading:
1. Study **2,000–3,000 historical momentum bursts** across all stocks
2. Mark the first leg, consolidation, breakout, and Day 3 result
3. Observe: Do they work near 52-week highs? Lows? In bear markets? How does ADR affect them?
4. Only after this immersion should you start trading

This builds **pattern recognition**, not just theory.

---

### Core Framework: Two Lynch Setup Selection

For **4% breakout + dollar breakout**, use the SAME selection framework:

| Letter | Meaning | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| **T** | Not up **two** days in a row prior | Only one day of lead-up; avoid overstretched stocks |
| **U** | **Unbroken** upward line / linearity | Strong, persistent buying in the first leg; no big selling candles |
| **L** | **Linearity** confirmed | The first leg shows clean, orderly price action |
| **I** | **Industry** momentum / Young setup | First or second leg of a move; NOT fourth, fifth leg |
| **N** | Narrow or negative day prior to consolidation | Ideally a narrow/low-volume day before breakout |
| **C** | **Consolidation** quality | Compact, orderly, low-volatility; no 4% breakdowns during consolidation |
| **H** | Close near the **high** on breakout day | Stock does not give up gains |

> "The most important thing in trading this setup is your ability to select a setup which meets all the criteria."

**Skill = avoiding choppy, extended, or late setups.** The good ones look "obvious" in hindsight — that's the pattern recognition from deep dive.

### Methods Under the Momentum Burst Umbrella

| Method | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **4% breakout** | Stock up 4%+ with volume > yesterday AND volume > 100,000 | Lower-priced stocks, broad scanning |
| **Dollar breakout** | Stock up $0.90+ with volume > 100,000 | Higher-priced stocks, tighter stops |
| **Combination scan** | 4% OR dollar breakout together | Maximum coverage |
| **Low threshold breakout** | Similar logic with lower thresholds | Beginners |

> "4% breakout is good for finding lower priced stocks… Dollar breakout is for higher priced stocks that won't show up in 4% scan."

### Filters to Improve Win Rate

**TI-65 (Trend Intensity)**:
```
avg(C, 7) / avg(C, 65) >= 1.05
```
Shows established momentum. Can reduce scan results from 73 → 24.

**M20 (Absolute Momentum)**:
```
C - min(C, 20) >= 1.20  // OR dollar equivalent
```
Finds **younger setups** — stocks up 20% from recent low, often early in trend. Won't show in TI-65 until later.

> "Use M20 when market is coming out of correction. Use TI-65 in established bull market."

**Top 25 by TI-65**: Sort your scan by TI-65 and only focus on the top 25 ranked. Good for overwhelmed beginners.

---

### Key Principles

1. **Deep dive first** — pattern recognition from historical examples beats theory
2. **Selectivity > frequency** — only trade high-quality Lynch setups
3. **Young setups win** — first/second leg has highest probability; avoid fourth/fifth leg
4. **Filter choice matters** — TI-65 for established trend, M20 for early/young setups
