### 1. Dollar Breakout Example: Clean Setup with $9 Follow-Through

**Chart walkthrough:**
- First leg: strong, orderly buying
- Consolidation: compact, 3–4 day pause
- Breakout day: up **$4.46** but only **2.05%**
- Result: **$9 move** in 3 days
- 4% scan? **Would not have shown up** — only 2.05%

**Key point:** This stock would be invisible to traders only using 4% breakout. Dollar breakout catches what 4% misses.

### 2. Dollar Breakout Example: Orly — Low Risk, Big Reward

**Position sizing math:**
- Entry: ~$220.46
- Half-range stop: ~$213.12
- Risk per share: ~$7.34 (~3.3%)
- With 1% account risk: **Can put 30% of account** into this single setup
- With half-range stop (~$5): **Can put 40–50% of account**

The stock went on to make **$15–25** more.

> "The velocity with which you can move your account by using these kind of moves — unfortunately or fortunately, most traders are not going to focus on because anything which is higher priced scares people."

### 5. Capital Efficiency: The Dollar Breakout Advantage in Numbers

| | 4% Breakout | Dollar Breakout |
|---|---|---|
| **Typical breakout magnitude** | 4–8% | 1–3% |
| **Half-range stop** | 2–4% | **0.5–1.5%** |
| **Low-of-day stop** | 5–10% | 2–5% |
| **% of account per position (1% risk)** | 10–25% | **30–60%** |
| **Max positions at 3–4% total risk** | 1–2 | 3–4 |
| **Hold period** | 3–5 days | 8–12+ days |
| **Next-day entry** | Not recommended | Viable |

**The math:** If your half-range stop is 1.25% and you risk 1% of account, you can put 80% of your account in ONE trade. In practice, 0.25% risk per trade → 30–40% position size → 3–4 positions → total account risk of only 3–4%.

> "You can have four setups like this open, and your risk cumulatively on all four positions still can be less than 3%."

**This is why dollar breakout is Pradeep's personal favorite** — maximum capital deployment with minimum total risk.

---

### 6. Dollar Breakout Catches What 4% Misses

Two additional advantages of dollar breakout over 4% breakout:

1. **Stocks that just miss 4%** — A stock up 3.99% doesn't show up in 4% scan, but it has a valid Two Lynch setup. Dollar breakout catches it.
2. **Stocks that show up earlier** — Some stocks appear in dollar breakout before they reach 4%. You get in earlier.

> "Many times, this stock will show up in your dollar breakout scan even before it shows up in 4% breakout scan."

---

### 7. Longer Duration: 8–12 Days

Higher-priced stocks trend more persistently:

| Stock | Breakout | Duration | Move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AutoZone** | 2% breakout | 7–8 days | ~$120 |
| **O'Reilly** | Small % breakout | 10–14 days | $50–60 |
| **MELI** | 1.5% breakout | 5–6 days | $46 |

> "They'll move very slowly, but they'll move for eight to ten days. Some of these stocks, like AutoZone, will make $120 move over multiple days."

**Implication:** You don't need to rush out on Day 3. Dollar breakouts give you room to hold for longer and still capture the full move.

---

### 8. Market Phase Still Matters

Dollar breakout is NOT immune to bearish market conditions:

- Only trade dollar breakouts when **Market Monitor is in confirmed bullish mode**
- Primary indicator should be green
- Breakouts fail during distribution periods just like 4% breakouts

> "If you have to trade the dollar breakout, you still need to trade them during favorable time periods, which will be when the market monitor is in a confirmed bullish mode."

**Practical tip:** When breakouts that worked for weeks suddenly start failing consecutively, that itself is a signal to reduce exposure — even before the distribution indicator turns fully red.

### 9. What Scares People (That Shouldn't)

| Fear | Reality |
|---|---|
| "Higher price = more risk" | Actually **less** volatile; tighter stops possible |
| "$200 stock with 2% breakout" | That 2% = $4 move; stop at $2 = only 1% risk |
| "I need more shares to make profit" | Wrong — you need bigger *position size*, not more shares |
| "I can't afford a $200 stock" | With 0.25% risk, you only need $500–$2,000 to participate |

### 10. Pradeep's Personal Approach

> "I, for my personal trading, focus exclusively on dollar breakout, these lower risk kind of setups, because that allows me to put in a lot of money to work and make money with low risk."

**His daily workflow:**
1. Run dollar breakout bullish/scan bearish scan
2. Apply Two Lynch to candidates
3. Focus on setups with 1–3% breakouts in higher-priced stocks
4. Enter with half-range stops (1–2.5% risk)
5. Position size aggressively (30–60% per trade at 0.25–1% risk)
6. Hold for 8–12 days
7. Only trade when Market Monitor confirms bullish phase

### Key Takeaways

1. **Dollar breakout captures what 4% misses** — 1–3% breakouts on higher-priced stocks
2. **Stops are dramatically tighter** — 0.5–2.5% vs. 5–10%
3. **3–4 positions with 3–4% total risk** — fully invested with minimal total risk
4. **Duration 8–12+ days** — don't rush to exit on Day 3
5. **Same stock can set up multiple times** — AutoZone, O'Reilly, Copart
6. **Market phase still matters** — only trade during bullish Market Monitor
7. **This is Pradeep's personal focus** — low risk + high position size = fastest account growth
8. **4% breakout catches what dollar misses and vice versa** — use both for maximum coverage
