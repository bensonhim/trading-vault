### 1. The Central Tendency: Momentum Burst Defined

The most important sentence in the entire guide:

> "When we use the term breakout, it means: percentage change today is greater than percentage change in last two to five days."

This is NOT about:
- Breaking above resistance
- Breaking above a moving average
- Breaking out of a chart pattern

It IS about: **a stock that was barely moving (2–5 days of minor pullback) suddenly has a day where the price move exceeds any of those prior days.** That day's candle is the "range expansion" that starts the burst.

**Visual pattern:** Before the burst, you see 2–5 small candles. Then a large candle appears. That large candle vs. the prior small candles is what defines the burst.

---

### 2. Duration: How Long Does It Last?

The answer determines your entire trade management:

| Price Level | Typical Hold | What Happens After |
|---|---|---|
| **Below $40** | 3–5 days | Most common; overstaying gives back profits |
| **$40–$100** | 5–7 days | Slightly longer; higher-priced stocks move slower percentage-wise |
| **Above $100** | 6–10+ days | Slow movers in % terms but significant in $ terms |
| **$500+** | 8–12 days | Very slow percentage, but $50–$300 dollar moves |

> "Three to five days is the golden period for trading a momentum burst."

**The critical implication:** If the move lasts only 3 days and you enter on Day 2, you've missed 33% of the move. If it lasts 5 days and you enter on Day 2, there's still room, but if you enter Day 3, the move is mostly over.

> "Not entering on the first day is a cardinal sin in a momentum burst kind of trading."

---

### 3. Profit Potential by Price Level

Expectations must be set before you trade:

| Stock Price | Expected Move (3–5 days) | Practical Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Below $10** | 20–40% | Most explosive; biotech/penny stocks can do 100%+ |
| **$10–$40** | 8–20% | Sweet spot for percentage traders |
| **$40–$100** | 5–15% | Moderate moves; better in dollar terms |
| **$100+** | $10–$50 per share | Think in dollars, not %; 3–8% typical |
| **$300+** | $30–$100 per share | Slow percentage, massive dollar moves |
| **$500+** | $50–$300 per share | 8–12 day holds; AutoZone, Booking can do $100+ in 3 days |

**Key insight:** Lower price = higher percentage but smaller dollars. Higher price = lower percentage but larger dollars. Know which game you're playing.

> "Lower the price, you're going to get 20 to 40%. Higher the price, you're going to get, like, in dollar terms, not in percentage terms."

### 4. Range Expansion: What Starts the Burst

The single event that triggers every momentum burst:

1. **Before burst:** Stock is going sideways, pulling back mildly, or in a low-volatility range
2. **Day 1:** A large candle appears — the "range expansion" — significantly bigger than the prior 2–5 days
3. **After Day 1:** Stock continues in the direction of the burst for 3–5 (or 8–12) days

**Examples from TC2000 walk-through:**
- Beyond Meat: stock down 3–4 days, then **7% range expansion** → 28% follow-through
- Beyond Meat again: minor pullback, then **13% range expansion** → 10% follow-through
- 42% range expansion day → 102% move in 7 days
- 4.66% expansion → continued follow-through

> "Not every range expansion is going to have a follow through, but enough number of them in a year have a follow through."

The same pattern appears on the downside. Every negative momentum burst (crash) also starts with a large range expansion candle to the downside.

### 5. Myth-Busting: What You DON'T Need

This section systematically destroys common trader objections:

#### Myth 1: "Breakouts need to be from near 52-week highs"

**Reality:** Momentum bursts from 52-week lows work equally well, and sometimes better.

> "Stocks near 52[-week] low as a momentum burst work equally well as a stock near fifty two week high. And many cases, they work better."

Example: Stock near 52-week lows had a burst and made **200%**. Stocks coming off market corrections often have their best bursts from the lows.

#### Myth 2: "Breakouts need to be above the 200-day moving average"

**Reality:** Many of the best bursts happen BELOW the 200-day MA.

> "Many good momentum burst actually happen below 200 day moving average."

When stocks are emerging from a correction, they can have 2–3 bursts before ever crossing the 200-day. Skipping those means leaving a lot of money on the table.

#### Myth 3: "The stock needs good fundamentals (earnings, sales)"

**Reality:** Some of the most profitable bursts happen on stocks with ZERO earnings and sales.

> "Some of the most profitable trades which you'll do in momentum burst will be on stocks which are extremely poor fundamentals."

Biotech stocks with no revenue regularly make 50–100% moves. Pradeep's TC2000 shows AXSM and comparable biotechs as prime examples.

#### Myth 4: "The stock needs high ADR (Average Daily Range)"

**Reality:** ADR is backward-looking. Before a burst, a stock has LOW ADR. The burst CAUSES the high ADR.

> "As soon as the momentum burst happened, the reality of the stock changed, and it will have a high ADR."

If you filter by ADR before the burst, you'll miss the very stocks about to burst.

#### Myth 5: "The stock needs to have a low float"

**Reality:** Momentum bursts happen on stocks with 18M, 50M, 100M, and even 482M share floats. Float is not a prerequisite.

> "Trade momentum burst as an isolated phenomena irrespective of low float."

### 6. Can You Anticipate a Momentum Burst?

**Partial** anticipation is possible, but only for ~20% of bursts.

- **First burst in a series:** Hard to anticipate — it's a sudden event
- **Second or subsequent burst:** Sometimes possible if you see a consolidation pattern forming
- **The truly explosive bursts (40–100%+):** Almost impossible to anticipate

> "80% of the momentum burst will not be possible to anticipate."

**The implication:** Momentum burst trading is fundamentally a **reactive** method. You respond to the range expansion after it happens. Anticipation methods and momentum burst methods serve different purposes.

---

### 7. Applicability Across Markets

Momentum burst works in every liquid stock market (US, UK, Europe, Korea, China, Singapore, Malaysia). The only exception: markets with **price filters** (circuit breakers that halt trading after 5% moves) — these are rare, found in only 1–2 countries.

> "Momentum burst is gonna work the same way in your stock market."

Adapt the percentage thresholds to local market conditions, but the core phenomenon is universal.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Definition:** Momentum burst = today's percentage move > last 2–5 days' percentage moves
2. **Duration:** 3–5 days for low-priced stocks, 6–12+ days for high-priced stocks
3. **Profit magnitude:** 8–40% for stocks under $40; $10–$300 for stocks above $100
4. **Range expansion candle is the trigger** — it's the only signal you need
5. **Day 1 entry is critical** — overdue = overstay = give back profits
6. **None of the common prerequisites are required:** no 52-week high, no 200-day MA, no fundamentals, no ADR, no low float
7. **80% of bursts cannot be anticipated** — this is a reactive method
