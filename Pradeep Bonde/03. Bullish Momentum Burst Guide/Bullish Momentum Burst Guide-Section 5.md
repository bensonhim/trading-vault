# Bullish Momentum Burst Guide — Section 5

### Average Stop by Price Point

| Price Range | Typical Breakout Size | Conservative Stop (Low of Day) | Aggressive Stop (Half Range) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Below $5** | 10–20% | ~10–20% | ~5–10% |
| **$5–$40** | 4–10% | 5–10% | 2.5–5% |
| **Above $40** | 4–6% | 4–6% | 2–3% |

To get stops below 2.5%:
- Combine **lower-priced breakouts** (smaller percentage)
- Use **dollar breakout** for higher-priced stocks
- Enter **early** (half-day-range stop works best with timely entry)

---

### Win Rate: Market Phase Matters

**Trade breakouts ONLY when Market Monitor is bullish:**
- Primary indicator (25%+ movers in quarter) is **green**
- No 300+ down days on 4% breadth
- Money is flowing INTO the market, not distribution

**Red light = stop trading breakouts:**
- Primary indicator turns red
- Distribution days accumulate
- Same high-quality setups suddenly start failing — this IS the market telling you to reduce exposure

> "Breakout methods work very well when there is a bull market. Breakouts work when breadth is improving."

---

### M20 Filter for Early Detection

| Reading | Action |
|---|---|
| **M20 low + breakout** | Better time to buy |
| **M20 at 200 (extreme bullish)** | Peak reached; reduce exposure |

When M20 readings are high (200+ stocks up 20%+ in 5 days), most stocks have already moved; breakouts start failing because everything has gone up.

---

### Capitalization: Single Biggest Move Predictor

| Cap | Move Potential |
|---|---|
| **Below $1B** | Most explosive — many 10–40%+ moves |
| **$1B–$10B** | Big movers — many 15–30%+ moves |
| **Above $10B** | Slower — mostly 5–10% moves |

> "Lower the capitalization, more explosive the move."

When multiple setups compete, ALWAYS pick the **lower capitalization** one for larger profit potential.

---

### Sector Selection

**Top 3 sectors for momentum burst:**
1. **Healthcare / Biotechnology** — largest, most explosive movers
2. **Technology** — consistent follow-through
3. **Consumer Discretionary / Fashion / Luxury** — emotional buying drives big moves

**Avoid:**
- Utilities
- Defense
- Staples (Procter & Gamble, Colgate)
- Extremely large-caps ($100B+)

> "Healthcare, especially biotechnology, technology, and consumer discretionary… these three sectors make the biggest moves any given normal year."

### Position Sizing & Risk per Trade

| Account Size | Risk per Trade | Stop Size | % Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Aggressive** | 1% of account | 5% stop | Can only hold few positions |
| **Pradeep's approach** | Combined risk across ALL positions = 3–4% | 2–3% per setup | Holds 3–4+ positions simultaneously |

> "You can have four setups like this open, and your risk on that setup cumulatively on all four positions still can be less than 3%."

**The dollar breakout advantage:**
- 2% breakout with half-range stop = ~1% risk
- Allows 30%+ of account in one position
- Multiple positions with cumulative risk of only 3–4%
- Moves account faster with less risk

### Realistic Trade Frequency

| Year Type | Estimated Trades |
|---|---|
| **Normal year** | 150–250 trades |
| **Strong bull market** | 250–300+ trades |
| **Bear/correcting market** | 50–100 trades |

> "Realistically, aim for 150 to 200 trades using 4% breakout."
