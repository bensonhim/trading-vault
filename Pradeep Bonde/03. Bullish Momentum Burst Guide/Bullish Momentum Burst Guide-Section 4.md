# Bullish Momentum Burst Guide — Section 4

### Young-ness and Failure Rate

> "Younger the setup, more is the probability that you're gonna make money."

| Leg | Success Probability |
|---|---|
| **First leg** | Highest — fresh trend, undiscovered |
| **Second leg** | Very good |
| **Third leg** | Moderate |
| **Fourth/Fifth leg** | Declining — obvious to everyone, profit-taking |

> "Older the setup becomes, more is the rate of failure."

After a stock has made a big move (e.g., Beyond Meat 20%+ already), late-stage breakouts attract profit-taking and fail.

---

### Entry Timing: The Earlier, The Better

| Entry Time | Risk-Reward |
|---|---|
| **9:30–10:00 AM** | Best — tightest stop, highest follow-through probability |
| **11:00–1:00 PM** | Good — still early |
| **After 2:00 PM** | Declining — wider stop, less day left for momentum |
| **After hours** | Avoid — illiquid, wide spreads |
| **Next day** | **DO NOT** — momentum burst is 3–5 days; late entry = small profit window |

> "The earlier you identify a breakout and enter, the better is the risk reward."

**Why next-day entries fail:**
- A good breakout will often gap up the next day
- You miss the first day's momentum
- Your stop becomes 2–3x wider
- Less follow-through left

---

### Stop Placement

#### Conservative: Low of the Day
```
Stop = Low of breakout day
Risk: Wide (often 5–10%)
```

#### Aggressive: Half of Day's Range
```
If stock opens at $30, closes at $34 ($4 gain)
Stop at $32 (half of $4 = $2 below close)
```

> "If this stock ends up giving up half of these days gain, that breakout is no more valid. There is no more momentum burst."

**Pradeep's personal approach:**
- **Started:** 3-day low as stop
- **Later:** Low of the day
- **Now:** Half of day's range or slightly above half

---

### Stop Implementation

| | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Type** | **Hard stop** (real stop in system) |
| **Mental stop?** | No — too easy to rationalize and not take the loss |
| **Pre/after hours?** | No — only active during trading hours |
| **Trailing stop?** | Yes, ratchet up intraday as stock moves in your favor |

> "I put in hard stops. My stops are only active during the trading hours."


---

### Key Principles

1. **Enter early, exit fast** — first 30–60 minutes of breakout is the sweet spot
2. **Stop half-day-range** is best for experienced traders (after deep dive)
3. **Hard stops during market hours only** — no mental stops, no after-hours exposure
4. **Move stops up intraday** as the stock advances in your favor
