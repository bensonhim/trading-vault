## Summary

Not every SOS is tradable — this distinction is what separates profitable traders from losing ones. Tradable SOS must be preceded by a low-volatility period, a negative/narrow day, a reversal, or exertion prior to the breakout. If you have to ask someone "is this a start of a swing?" it isn't tradable — it should be obvious from half a mile away. The first step for success: have a working guide with tradable SOS continuation + 5 examples. Start with continuation only, then gradually add consolidation. Skip stocks with persistent failed breakouts ("broken stocks") — they'll never give you a tradable SOS. MU, APLD, IBM shown as examples of tradable vs. non-tradable SOS. "Essence of trading is timing, entry, and exit." If you focus only on buying tradable SOS, you put probability in your favor. This concept should take no more than 48 hours to understand.
