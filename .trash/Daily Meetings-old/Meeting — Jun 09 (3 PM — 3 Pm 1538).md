# Meeting — Jun 09 (3 PM)

> Choppy market = patience required. Good breakouts still look tempting in bad markets — that's the trap. SA (situational awareness) separates survivors from victims during "death by a thousand cuts" periods.

---

## 1. Market Regime: Choppy — "London Weather"

The market opened with selling, then buying came in — classic chop. Pradeep's SA assessment:

> "This is going to be choppy. If it kept on selling today, at least it becomes a trend or correction, but more likely, we are going to be choppy."

The dominant regime is **choppy with risk of further deterioration**. Not yet a clean downtrend (which would at least give short-side confidence), but not trending up either. This is the worst environment for swing traders — breakouts look good intraday, then fade.

> "They will use a swing to put you in the ground."

The semiconductor party is over for now:

> "The party for the time being is over on the semiconductor side. People don't want to believe that the party is over."

---

## 3. EP 9M Proliferating to the Downside

A critical breadth signal: **EP 9M setups are appearing on the short side**, not just the long side. This means institutional selling is accelerating.

> "You will see that EP 9M to the downsides are proliferating, and then they will, one day, just start going down in a smooth downturn."

[[GLW]] had **3–4 EP 9M breakdowns** to the downside. When stocks that previously exhibited bullish EP 9M start showing bearish EP 9M, it confirms weakening breadth beneath the surface.

**Implication**: The next leg down may come as a swift, coordinated move — not a gradual drift.

---

## 7. The SA Problem: Good Breakouts Look Good in Bad Markets

A recurring theme across meetings, reinforced here:

> "If you see something in your scans, in a bad market also, good breakouts are going to look good only. That's the thing."

This is the **core trap** of choppy markets. Your scan shows attractive setups. They look perfect on the chart. But in a choppy environment, follow-through fails. The skill is not in identifying the setup — it's in recognizing that **SA says "don't trade" even when setups look good**.

> "These periods are designed to separate the people who respect SA from people who want to trade."

---

## 8. Setup Understanding Gap

Pradeep calls out a fundamental misunderstanding in the community:

> "The level of setup understanding for some people is so problematic that last time I posted the list saying bullish and bearish together, assuming that people will know — look, this is not a bullish setup. And I got email after email saying, 'I do not understand what is bullish/bearish.'"

People are going long on short setups. This is dangerous in any market, but especially lethal in a choppy one where both directions can punish you.

> "People just want to get into something."

---

## 9. Why the Market Is Going Down

A brief geopolitical discussion about Iran negotiations leads to Pradeep's real thesis:

> "The market is going down because the market made a 30% move in less than two months. It just ran out of buyers now. That is the reason."

Not geopolitics. Not Iran. Not the Fed. **Simple supply/demand exhaustion** — after a 30% move in under two months, there are no buyers left at these levels.
